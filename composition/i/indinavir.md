# Indinavir

**Generic Name: Indinavir**

**Usage:** Indinavir is an antiviral medication primarily used in the treatment of Human Immunodeficiency Virus (HIV) infection.  It's a protease inhibitor, meaning it works by blocking the activity of an enzyme called HIV protease. This enzyme is crucial for HIV to replicate and produce new viruses. By inhibiting protease, indinavir slows down the progression of HIV infection and reduces the viral load in the body.  While its primary use is in HIV treatment, it's not typically used as a standalone medication anymore due to the availability of more effective and better-tolerated regimens.  Instead, it might be considered in combination therapy with other antiretroviral drugs for individuals who haven't responded well to other treatment options.

**Dosage:**  Dosage of indinavir is highly individualized and depends on factors like the patient's overall health, other medications being taken, and the severity of the HIV infection.  It's crucial to follow a doctor's precise instructions.  Generally, for adults, it's administered as a capsule or oral solution (although the oral solution is less common now).  Specific dosage will be determined by a healthcare professional and might involve multiple doses throughout the day.  There is limited data on pediatric use, and the dosage and suitability for children are determined on a case-by-case basis by a specialist.  There are no injectable forms of indinavir.


**Side Effects:**  Indinavir can cause various side effects, some common and others less frequent but more serious.

* **Common Side Effects:** Nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, abdominal pain, headache, fatigue, dizziness, rash, kidney stones (nephrolithiasis).
* **Less Common but Serious Side Effects:**  Hyperglycemia (high blood sugar), pancreatitis (inflammation of the pancreas), liver problems (hepatotoxicity),  blood disorders,  increased risk of certain infections (opportunistic infections).


**Note:**  This is not an exhaustive list. Any new or worsening symptoms should be reported immediately to a healthcare provider.

**How it Works:**  Indinavir works by inhibiting HIV protease. HIV protease is an enzyme the virus uses to cut up long protein chains into smaller pieces, which are then assembled to form new infectious HIV particles.  By blocking this enzyme's activity, indinavir prevents the formation of new viruses, reducing the viral load and slowing the progression of the disease.

**Popular Brand Names (India):**  The specific brand names available in India can vary and may not always be consistently marketed.  It's best to check with a local pharmacy or healthcare professional for the currently available brand names of indinavir.


**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** Indinavir is contraindicated in patients with known hypersensitivity (allergy) to the drug.
* **Drug Interactions:**  Indinavir interacts with many other medications. It's crucial to inform a healthcare provider about all medications (including over-the-counter drugs, supplements, and herbal remedies) being taken before starting indinavir to avoid potentially dangerous interactions.  Certain medications can increase or decrease indinavir's effectiveness or increase the risk of side effects.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  The use of indinavir during pregnancy and breastfeeding should be carefully considered and managed by a specialist due to the potential risks to the mother and child.
* **Older Adults and Children:**  Specific dosage adjustments and close monitoring are necessary for both older adults and children. Kidney and liver function should be closely evaluated before and during treatment.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long do I need to take indinavir?**  A: The duration of treatment is determined by a doctor based on individual circumstances and usually involves long-term management of HIV.
* **Q: Can I stop taking indinavir if I feel better?** A: No.  Stopping treatment prematurely can lead to drug resistance and disease progression.  Always follow your doctor’s prescribed regimen.
* **Q: How should I store indinavir?** A:  Store indinavir as directed on the label, usually at room temperature, away from moisture and heat.
* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: If you miss a dose, take it as soon as you remember, unless it is almost time for the next dose.  Do not take a double dose.  Consult your doctor or pharmacist if you have questions.
* **Q: Are there any long-term effects of taking indinavir?** A: Long-term use can have potential risks such as kidney stones and other side effects. Regular monitoring by a healthcare professional is important to assess and manage potential complications.
