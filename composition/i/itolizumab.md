# Itolizumab

**Generic Name: Itolizumab**

**Usage:** Itolizumab is a monoclonal antibody primarily used in the treatment of moderate to severe cases of **acutely worsening moderate to severe chronic plaque psoriasis** that hasn't responded adequately to other systemic treatments.  It works by targeting specific immune cells involved in the inflammatory process that underlies this skin condition. While its primary use is in psoriasis, it has also shown some promise in treating other inflammatory conditions, although further research is needed to establish its efficacy and safety in these contexts.  These potential secondary uses are still under investigation and not currently widely approved.

**Dosage:** Itolizumab is administered intravenously (IV) by a healthcare professional.  Dosage and frequency vary depending on the specific condition being treated and the patient's response.  There isn't a standard pediatric dosage established, as its use in children is currently limited.  Precise dosing instructions are determined by a physician based on individual patient needs and should be followed meticulously.  It's not available in tablet or syrup form.

**Side Effects:**  Common side effects can include:

* Infusion-related reactions (e.g., fever, chills, rash, itching)
* Headache
* Nausea
* Fatigue
* Infection

Less common, but serious, side effects may include:

* Serious allergic reactions (anaphylaxis)
* Heart problems
*  Blood disorders

It's crucial to report any adverse effects to your doctor immediately.  Discontinue use and seek immediate medical attention if you experience severe symptoms.

**How it Works:** Itolizumab is a humanized monoclonal antibody.  It selectively binds to a protein called CD6, found on the surface of certain immune cells (T cells).  By binding to CD6, itolizumab helps to modulate or regulate the activity of these T cells, reducing their contribution to the inflammatory process characteristic of psoriasis.  This, in turn, helps to lessen the symptoms of the disease.

**Popular Brand Names (India):**  Alzumab is a well-known brand name for itolizumab commonly available in India.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:**  Itolizumab should not be used in individuals with known hypersensitivity (allergy) to the drug or its components.
* **Drug Interactions:** While specific drug interactions aren't extensively documented, it's essential to inform your doctor about all medications, supplements, and herbal remedies you are taking.  Potential interactions may exist.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  The safety of itolizumab during pregnancy and breastfeeding hasn't been fully established.  It should only be used in these situations if the potential benefits clearly outweigh the potential risks.  Consult your doctor.
* **Elderly and Children:**  Limited data is available on the use of itolizumab in elderly patients and children.  Close monitoring is essential.

**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long does it take to see results?** A:  The response to itolizumab varies among individuals.  Improvement in psoriasis symptoms may be observed within weeks, but the full therapeutic effect might take longer.
* **Q: How is itolizumab stored?** A:  Storage instructions should be followed as per the product information leaflet provided with the medication. Generally, it needs to be stored under refrigeration.
* **Q: Can I stop taking itolizumab suddenly?** A: No, you should not stop taking itolizumab without consulting your doctor.  Stopping abruptly may lead to a recurrence of symptoms or other complications.
* **Q: Is itolizumab a cure for psoriasis?** A: No, itolizumab is not a cure for psoriasis.  It manages the symptoms and reduces inflammation.
* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Consult your doctor or healthcare provider immediately to determine the appropriate course of action.
