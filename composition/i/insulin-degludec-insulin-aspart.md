# Insulin Degludec + Insulin Aspart

**Generic Name:** Insulin Degludec + Insulin Aspart

**Usage:**

Insulin Degludec + Insulin Aspart is a combination of two types of insulin used to manage blood sugar levels in people with diabetes mellitus.  It's a particularly useful option for people with type 1 diabetes and some individuals with type 2 diabetes who require both a long-acting and a rapid-acting insulin.

* **Primary Use:**  The primary purpose is to control blood glucose levels throughout the day and night. Insulin degludec provides a long-acting basal insulin, supplying a steady background level of insulin to prevent high blood sugar (hyperglycemia). Insulin aspart acts as a rapid-acting bolus insulin, quickly lowering blood glucose levels after meals.  This combination simplifies the treatment regimen for many patients, requiring only two injections daily.

* **Secondary Uses:** While its primary use is focused on blood glucose control, this combination may be used in certain circumstances to manage diabetic ketoacidosis (DKA), a serious complication of diabetes.  However, this would only be under strict medical supervision.


**Dosage:**

Dosage is highly individualized and determined by a healthcare professional based on factors such as blood glucose levels, diet, activity level, and overall health. There's no single "standard" dose.

* **Form:** This medication is administered via subcutaneous injection (under the skin), typically using an insulin pen or syringe.  It is *not* available as a tablet or syrup.

* **Adults:**  The starting dose and subsequent adjustments are tailored to each patient's needs. The healthcare professional will monitor blood glucose levels closely and adjust the dose accordingly.  This may involve adjusting both the basal (degludec) and bolus (aspart) components.

* **Children:** Dosage in children is also individualized and requires careful monitoring.  Pediatric endocrinologists typically manage this medication in children.

* **Administration:**  Always follow the instructions provided by your healthcare professional and the medication packaging carefully.  Proper injection technique is crucial to ensure effective absorption and minimize the risk of complications.


**Side Effects:**

* **Common:** Hypoglycemia (low blood sugar), injection site reactions (redness, swelling, itching), weight gain.

* **Less Common but Serious:** Severe hypoglycemia (leading to unconsciousness or seizures), allergic reactions (rash, hives, difficulty breathing), lipohypertrophy (thickening of fatty tissue at the injection site).

* **Important Note:**  If you experience any unusual or concerning side effects, contact your healthcare provider immediately.


**How it Works:**

Insulin Degludec + Insulin Aspart works by mimicking the body's natural insulin.  Insulin degludec, the long-acting component, binds to receptors in fat tissue and slowly releases insulin into the bloodstream, providing a stable, basal level of insulin for many hours.  Insulin aspart, the rapid-acting component, quickly lowers blood glucose after meals by promoting glucose uptake by cells and inhibiting glucose production by the liver. This combined action helps maintain blood glucose levels within a target range.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:**  This medication is contraindicated in patients with severe hypoglycemia or known allergy to insulin degludec or aspart.

* **Drug Interactions:**  Many medications can interact with insulin, potentially affecting blood glucose levels.  Inform your doctor about all medications, supplements, and herbal remedies you are taking.

* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  Insulin use during pregnancy and breastfeeding requires careful monitoring by a healthcare professional.

* **Elderly:**  Older adults may be more susceptible to hypoglycemia and require closer monitoring.

* **Renal/Hepatic Impairment:**  Patients with kidney or liver disease may require dose adjustments.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: How should I store Insulin Degludec + Insulin Aspart?** A:  Store unopened vials in the refrigerator. Once opened, store at room temperature and discard after the expiration date.

* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A:  Consult your healthcare provider for guidance.  Don't double the dose to make up for a missed dose.

* **Q: Can I mix this insulin with other insulins?** A:  Mixing insulins requires careful instruction from your healthcare professional and is not always recommended.

* **Q: What are the symptoms of hypoglycemia?** A:  Symptoms include shakiness, sweating, dizziness, hunger, confusion, and rapid heartbeat.  Treatment involves consuming fast-acting carbohydrates.

* **Q:  How often should I check my blood sugar?** A:  Your healthcare provider will determine the frequency of blood glucose monitoring based on your individual needs.
