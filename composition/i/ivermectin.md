# Ivermectin

**Generic Name: Ivermectin**

**Usage:**

Ivermectin is an antiparasitic medication primarily used to treat infections caused by various parasites.  Its most significant use is in combating parasitic worms (helminths) that infect the intestines, skin, and eyes.  This includes conditions like strongyloidiasis (threadworm infection), onchocerciasis (river blindness), lymphatic filariasis (elephantiasis), and scabies (a skin infestation caused by mites).  Ivermectin is also effective against certain types of head lice and some internal parasites in animals.  While it has been studied in other contexts,  its efficacy outside of parasitic infections is not consistently supported by robust scientific evidence.  Any off-label use should only be undertaken under strict medical supervision.

**Dosage:**

Dosage varies significantly depending on the specific parasite being treated, the patient's age and weight, and the form of the medication.  It's crucial to follow a doctor's prescription exactly.  Ivermectin is available in several forms, including tablets, oral solutions (syrups), and injectable solutions.  A doctor will determine the appropriate dosage and administration method.  Self-medication is strongly discouraged.  Providing specific dosage examples without professional guidance is irresponsible and potentially dangerous.


**Side Effects:**

Common side effects are generally mild and may include:

* Nausea
* Vomiting
* Diarrhea
* Dizziness
* Headache
* Stomach pain
* Rash


Less common but serious side effects can occur and require immediate medical attention.  These include:

* Severe allergic reactions (anaphylaxis)
* Seizures
* Liver damage
* Coma


If you experience any unusual or concerning side effects, contact your doctor or seek immediate medical help.

**How it Works:**

Ivermectin works by binding to glutamate-gated chloride channels and chloride channels activated by the neurotransmitter γ-aminobutyric acid (GABA) in the parasite's nervous system. This causes paralysis and ultimately kills the parasite.  It is important to note that this mechanism is specific to parasites; it has a limited impact on human chloride channels.

**Precautions:**

* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  Ivermectin should be used with caution during pregnancy and breastfeeding. A doctor should assess the risks and benefits.
* **Liver and Kidney Disease:** Patients with liver or kidney impairment may require dose adjustments.
* **Children:**  Dosage should be carefully calculated based on weight and the specific infection.
* **Drug Interactions:** Ivermectin can interact with certain medications.  It's crucial to inform your doctor about all medications, supplements, and herbal remedies being taken.
* **Hypersensitivity:** Individuals with a known hypersensitivity to ivermectin should avoid using it.
* **Overdose:** Ivermectin overdose can be serious.  Seek immediate medical attention if an overdose is suspected.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: Can I buy ivermectin over-the-counter?** A:  Not for most uses. Ivermectin is typically only available with a prescription.
* **Q: How should I store ivermectin?** A: Store ivermectin as directed on the label, usually at room temperature away from moisture and heat.
* **Q: Is ivermectin safe for everyone?** A:  Not necessarily.  Individuals with certain health conditions or those taking specific medications should consult a doctor before using ivermectin.
* **Q: Is ivermectin effective against COVID-19?** A:  Extensive research has shown that ivermectin is not effective in preventing or treating COVID-19.  Its use for this purpose is not supported by scientific evidence.
* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Contact your doctor for guidance; do not double the dose.
