# Ibuprofen

**Generic Name: Ibuprofen**

**Usage:**

Ibuprofen is a nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drug (NSAID) primarily used to relieve pain and reduce fever.  Its main uses include treating mild to moderate pain associated with headaches, menstrual cramps, muscle aches, back pain, toothaches, and the common cold.  It's also effective in reducing inflammation (swelling and redness) caused by various conditions, such as sprains, strains, and arthritis.  While not its primary purpose, ibuprofen can sometimes be used to reduce inflammation associated with certain types of arthritis.  Always consult a doctor to determine the appropriate use of ibuprofen for your specific condition.

**Dosage:**

Dosage varies significantly depending on age, weight, and the specific condition being treated.  It's crucial to follow the instructions on the packaging or as prescribed by a healthcare professional.  

* **Adults:**  The typical adult dose is 200-400 mg every 4-6 hours, not exceeding the maximum daily dose as indicated on the packaging.  This can be in tablet, capsule, or liquid form.
* **Children:**  Dosage for children is strictly weight-based and should always be determined by a doctor or pharmacist.  Liquid formulations are generally preferred for children.  Never give children ibuprofen without first consulting a medical professional.

Ibuprofen is available in various forms including tablets, capsules, liquid suspensions (syrups), and even injectable forms (used primarily in hospitals).  Always follow the provided instructions for the specific form you are using.


**Side Effects:**

* **Common:**  Nausea, heartburn, indigestion, stomach upset, diarrhea, constipation.
* **Less Common but Serious:**  Allergic reactions (rash, swelling, difficulty breathing), stomach ulcers, bleeding, kidney problems, liver problems, increased risk of heart attack or stroke (especially with long-term use or high doses).

If you experience any adverse effects, particularly severe or persistent ones, stop taking ibuprofen and seek medical attention immediately.

**How it Works:**

Ibuprofen works by inhibiting the production of prostaglandins, chemicals in the body that trigger inflammation, pain, and fever. By reducing prostaglandin levels, ibuprofen helps to alleviate pain, reduce swelling, and lower body temperature.


**Popular Brand Names (India):**

Many brands market ibuprofen in India.  Specific brand names vary regionally and are subject to change; always check the active ingredient to ensure it's ibuprofen.


**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:**  Individuals with known allergies to ibuprofen or other NSAIDs should avoid it.  It should also be avoided by people with certain stomach conditions, bleeding disorders, or severe liver or kidney problems.
* **Drug Interactions:**  Ibuprofen can interact with various medications, including blood thinners, certain heart medications, and some antidepressants.  Always inform your doctor or pharmacist of all medications you are taking.
* **Pregnancy & Breastfeeding:**  Use during pregnancy or breastfeeding should only be under strict medical supervision due to potential risks.
* **Children & Elderly:**  Dosage adjustments and careful monitoring are crucial for these groups.
* **Alcohol:**  Combining ibuprofen with alcohol can increase the risk of stomach irritation and bleeding.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long does it take to work?** A:  The onset of pain relief typically occurs within 30-60 minutes.
* **Q: Can I take it with other pain relievers?** A:  Generally not recommended without consulting a doctor.  Combining NSAIDs or mixing them with other pain relievers can lead to adverse effects.
* **Q: How should I store it?** A: Store at room temperature, away from moisture and direct sunlight. Keep out of reach of children.
* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: If you miss a dose, take it as soon as you remember unless it's almost time for your next dose. Don't double up on doses.
* **Q: Is it safe for long-term use?** A:  Long-term use can increase the risk of serious side effects. Consult a doctor before using ibuprofen for extended periods.
