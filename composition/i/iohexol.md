# Iohexol

**Generic Name: Iohexol**

**Usage:**

Iohexol is a non-ionic contrast media used primarily in diagnostic imaging procedures, specifically X-ray examinations.  Its primary use is to enhance the visibility of internal organs and structures during procedures like computed tomography (CT) scans, magnetic resonance imaging (MRI), and angiography (blood vessel imaging).  This enhancement allows radiologists to better visualize abnormalities, such as tumors, blockages, or injuries.  While its primary function is diagnostic, it plays a crucial role in guiding minimally invasive procedures, allowing doctors to precisely target treatment areas.  Secondary uses may involve myelography (imaging of the spinal cord) or other specialized imaging techniques requiring contrast enhancement.

**Dosage:**

Iohexol dosage is highly individualized and determined by the physician based on factors such as the type of imaging procedure, patient age, weight, renal function (kidney health), and the specific area being examined.  It's administered intravenously (through a vein) or intra-arterially (directly into an artery) depending on the procedure.  There's no standard dosage applicable to all situations;  it's crucial to follow a doctor’s explicit instructions.  Pediatric dosages are carefully calculated based on weight and the specific imaging needs. The medication isn't usually available in oral or other forms such as tablets or syrup.


**Side Effects:**

Common side effects of Iohexol are generally mild and temporary. These can include:

*   Warmth or flushing sensation
*   Nausea
*   Vomiting
*   Headache
*   Metallic taste in the mouth

Less common, but more serious, side effects can occur, including:

*   Allergic reactions (ranging from mild skin rash to severe anaphylaxis requiring immediate medical attention)
*   Kidney problems (especially in patients with pre-existing kidney disease)
*   Seizures
*   Cardiac arrhythmias (irregular heartbeat)

**If you experience any adverse effects, especially serious ones, seek immediate medical attention.**

**How it Works:**

Iohexol is a non-ionic contrast medium, meaning it doesn't carry an electrical charge. This is important because it reduces the likelihood of triggering adverse reactions compared to ionic contrast agents.  Iohexol works by increasing the radiodensity of tissues and fluids. When injected, it temporarily increases the difference in X-ray absorption between different tissues, making the structures being imaged stand out more clearly on the scans. This enhanced contrast allows for better visualization of internal anatomy and helps in the detection of diseases and anomalies.

**Precautions:**

*   **Allergy:**  Inform your doctor about any known allergies, particularly to contrast media.
*   **Kidney function:**  Iohexol is mainly excreted by the kidneys. Patients with impaired kidney function require careful monitoring and may need adjusted dosage or alternative imaging techniques.
*   **Pregnancy and breastfeeding:**  The use of Iohexol during pregnancy should be carefully weighed against the benefits of the imaging procedure.  Consult your doctor.  Information regarding excretion in breast milk is limited, and a doctor should be consulted.
*   **Other medications:**  Inform your doctor about all medications you are taking, as Iohexol may interact with certain drugs.
*   **Metformin:**  There is a potential interaction between Iohexol and Metformin (a diabetes medication).  Your doctor may temporarily discontinue metformin before and after the procedure.
