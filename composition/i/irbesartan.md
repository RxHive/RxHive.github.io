# Irbesartan

**Generic Name: Irbesartan**

**Usage:**

Irbesartan is an angiotensin II receptor blocker (ARB).  Its primary use is in the treatment of high blood pressure (hypertension).  Lowering blood pressure helps reduce the risk of serious complications like stroke, heart attack, and kidney disease.  Irbesartan is also used to protect the kidneys in people with type 2 diabetes and high blood pressure. This protective effect is particularly important in slowing the progression of diabetic kidney disease.  In some cases, it may be prescribed to reduce the risk of cardiovascular events (such as heart attack or stroke) in high-risk patients, even if their blood pressure is already well-controlled.


**Dosage:**

Dosage varies depending on the condition being treated and the individual patient. A doctor will determine the appropriate dose.  Generally, for adults, the starting dose is typically 150 mg once daily.  This may be increased to 300 mg daily, depending on the response to treatment.  Irbesartan is usually available as tablets.  There is no syrup or injectable form of irbesartan.  Children's dosing is determined on a case-by-case basis by a pediatrician and is not standardized like adult dosing. It's crucial to follow the prescribed dosage and not adjust it without consulting a doctor.


**Side Effects:**

* **Common:** Dizziness, lightheadedness, headache, fatigue, nausea, diarrhea, and muscle cramps.  These are usually mild and temporary.
* **Less Common but Serious:**  Angioedema (swelling of the face, lips, tongue, or throat), which can be life-threatening and requires immediate medical attention.  Low blood pressure (hypotension), especially when starting treatment or increasing the dose.  Kidney problems.  Changes in blood potassium levels.  Cough (less common with ARBs than with ACE inhibitors).
* **Important Note:** If you experience any side effects, especially those listed as serious, contact your doctor immediately.


**How it Works:**

Irbesartan works by blocking the action of angiotensin II, a hormone that narrows blood vessels, raising blood pressure. By blocking angiotensin II, irbesartan allows blood vessels to relax and widen, leading to a decrease in blood pressure.  In patients with diabetes, it also helps protect the kidneys by reducing the pressure within the kidney's filtering system.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** Irbesartan should not be used by people who are allergic to it or other ARBs.
* **Interactions:** Irbesartan can interact with other medications, including potassium supplements, diuretics (water pills), and some pain relievers.  Inform your doctor of all medications you are taking.
* **Pregnancy:**  Irbesartan should not be taken during pregnancy, as it can cause harm to the developing fetus.
* **Children:**  Use in children should only be guided by a pediatrician.
* **Older Adults:** Older adults may be more sensitive to the side effects of irbesartan, requiring careful monitoring.
* **Kidney and Liver Disease:**  Patients with kidney or liver disease may require dose adjustments.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: Can I stop taking irbesartan suddenly?** A: No.  Stopping irbesartan abruptly can cause a sudden increase in blood pressure.  Always follow your doctor's instructions for discontinuing the medication.
* **Q: How should I store irbesartan?** A: Store irbesartan tablets in a cool, dry place, away from direct sunlight and moisture.
* **Q:  Can I drink alcohol while taking irbesartan?** A:  Moderate alcohol consumption is generally acceptable, but excessive alcohol use can lower blood pressure further and increase the risk of side effects.  Discuss alcohol consumption with your doctor.
* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it is almost time for your next dose.  Do not take a double dose.
* **Q: Is irbesartan addictive?** A: No, irbesartan is not addictive.
