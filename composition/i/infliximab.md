# Infliximab

**Generic Name:** Infliximab

**Usage:**

Infliximab is a powerful medication primarily used to treat various autoimmune diseases.  These are conditions where the body's immune system mistakenly attacks its own tissues. Its main application lies in managing moderate to severe inflammatory conditions.  Specifically, it's a cornerstone treatment for:

* **Rheumatoid arthritis (RA):** A chronic inflammatory disorder affecting the joints. Infliximab helps reduce joint pain, swelling, and stiffness.
* **Crohn's disease:** A type of inflammatory bowel disease (IBD) causing inflammation in the digestive tract. Infliximab helps to manage symptoms like abdominal pain, diarrhea, and weight loss.
* **Ulcerative colitis:** Another IBD affecting the large intestine, characterized by inflammation and ulceration. Infliximab helps reduce inflammation and improve symptoms.
* **Ankylosing spondylitis:** A type of arthritis primarily affecting the spine, causing pain and stiffness. Infliximab can help improve mobility and reduce inflammation.
* **Psoriatic arthritis:**  Arthritis associated with psoriasis, a skin condition. Infliximab targets both the joint inflammation and the skin lesions.
* **Psoriasis:**  In severe cases where other treatments have failed, infliximab may be used to manage the skin lesions characteristic of psoriasis.
* **Certain types of uveitis:** Inflammation of the middle layer of the eye.
* **Hidradenitis suppurativa:** A chronic inflammatory skin condition affecting areas with sweat glands.


While these are the primary uses, research continues to explore its potential in other inflammatory conditions.

**Dosage:**

Infliximab is administered intravenously (IV) by a healthcare professional. Dosage varies greatly depending on the specific condition being treated, the patient's weight, and their response to the medication.  There is no standard pediatric dosage as it is highly individualized and based on the specific disease and severity.  A physician will determine the appropriate dose and frequency.  It is *not* available in tablet or syrup form.

**Side Effects:**

* **Common:**  Injection site reactions (redness, swelling, pain), headache, nausea, fatigue, fever, and infections (upper respiratory infections are common).
* **Less Common but Serious:**  Increased risk of infections (including tuberculosis and other opportunistic infections), heart failure, allergic reactions (including anaphylaxis, a severe and potentially life-threatening allergic reaction), blood disorders, nervous system problems (such as numbness or tingling), and liver problems.

If you experience any unusual or concerning side effects, it's crucial to contact your doctor immediately.


**How it Works:**

Infliximab is a type of medication called a "tumor necrosis factor (TNF) inhibitor." TNF is a protein involved in inflammation.  Infliximab binds to TNF, preventing it from triggering inflammation in the body. By blocking TNF's action, infliximab helps to reduce inflammation and improve symptoms in the conditions listed above.

The specific brand names available in India can vary, so always consult a local pharmacist or your doctor for the most up-to-date information.  However, some commonly known brands containing infliximab might include Remicade (although its availability in India may vary).

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** Infliximab should not be used in individuals with active tuberculosis, severe heart failure, or known hypersensitivity (allergic reaction) to the medication.
* **Interactions:** Infliximab can interact with other medications, particularly those that suppress the immune system.  It's essential to inform your doctor of all medications you are taking.
* **Vulnerable Groups:**  Pregnant and breastfeeding women, children, and older adults should be monitored closely while on infliximab due to potential increased risk of side effects.  The benefits and risks need to be carefully weighed by a physician.
* **Safety Measures:** Regular blood tests are often required to monitor for side effects and to ensure the medication is working effectively.

**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long does it take to work?** A:  The onset of effect varies depending on the condition, but it can take several weeks to see significant improvement.
* **Q: How long is the treatment?** A: Treatment duration varies and is determined by your doctor based on your response and the severity of your condition.
* **Q: Can I stop taking it suddenly?** A: No, you should never stop taking infliximab suddenly without consulting your doctor.  Stopping abruptly can lead to a flare-up of symptoms.
* **Q: How is it stored?** A:  Storage requirements vary depending on the specific formulation. Always follow the instructions provided with the medication.  Generally, refrigeration is required.
* **Q: What if I miss a dose?** A: Contact your doctor immediately to discuss how to proceed.
