# Iloperidone

**Generic Name: Iloperidone**

**Usage:**

Iloperidone is an antipsychotic medication primarily used to treat schizophrenia and bipolar I disorder (manic or mixed episodes).  Its primary role is to manage the positive symptoms of schizophrenia, such as hallucinations and delusions, and to help stabilize mood swings in bipolar disorder.  While not its primary use, iloperidone may also be considered as an adjunct treatment for depressive episodes in bipolar disorder, often used in combination with other medications.  The effectiveness and suitability of iloperidone for a given individual depend on several factors, which a doctor will consider during treatment planning.


**Dosage:**

Dosage varies depending on the condition being treated and the individual's response to the medication.  It's crucial to follow the doctor's instructions precisely.  Generally, iloperidone is administered orally in tablet form.  

* **Adults (Schizophrenia & Bipolar I Disorder):**  Starting doses are typically low and gradually increased under medical supervision.  A common starting dose is 1-2 mg daily, increasing to an optimal dose of 6-12 mg per day, usually taken once daily or in divided doses.

* **Children:** Iloperidone is not generally recommended for use in children due to a lack of sufficient clinical data supporting its safety and efficacy in this population.

Dosage adjustments may be necessary based on factors such as age, liver or kidney function, and individual response.  The doctor will monitor the patient closely and make appropriate adjustments as needed.


**Side Effects:**

Common side effects can include:

* Dizziness
* Drowsiness
* Weight gain
* Nausea
* Constipation
* Dry mouth
* Tremors
* Sleepiness

Less common but serious side effects include:

* **Neuroleptic Malignant Syndrome (NMS):** A rare but life-threatening condition characterized by high fever, muscle rigidity, altered mental status, and autonomic instability.  Seek immediate medical attention if these symptoms occur.
* **Tardive dyskinesia:**  A movement disorder that can be irreversible.
* **Prolonged QT interval:** A heart rhythm abnormality that can increase the risk of serious heart problems.
* **Increased risk of suicide:** While uncommon, an increased risk of suicidal ideation and behaviour has been reported with antipsychotic medications, including iloperidone. Close monitoring is vital, especially during the initial stages of treatment.

If any side effects, whether common or serious, occur, it is important to contact a healthcare provider immediately.


**How it Works:**

Iloperidone is a dopamine D2 and serotonin 5-HT2A receptor antagonist.  In simpler terms, it works by blocking the activity of certain neurotransmitters in the brain.  These neurotransmitters (dopamine and serotonin) are involved in regulating mood, thoughts, and behaviors. By affecting the balance of these neurotransmitters, iloperidone helps to reduce the symptoms of schizophrenia and bipolar disorder.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** Iloperidone should not be used in individuals with known hypersensitivity to the drug or its components.  Caution is necessary in patients with a history of heart problems, seizures, or liver/kidney disease.
* **Drug Interactions:** Iloperidone can interact with several other drugs, including some antidepressants, antihistamines, and certain heart medications.  It is crucial to inform the doctor about all medications, supplements, and herbal remedies being taken.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:** The use of iloperidone during pregnancy or breastfeeding should be carefully considered, weighing the potential benefits against the risks. The drug may pass into breast milk and potentially affect the infant.  A doctor must guide this decision.
* **Elderly Patients:** Older adults may be more sensitive to the side effects of iloperidone, requiring closer monitoring and potentially lower dosages.

**FAQs:**

* **Q: Can I stop taking iloperidone suddenly?** A: No, abruptly stopping iloperidone can lead to withdrawal symptoms.  The dosage should be gradually reduced under the guidance of a doctor.

* **Q: How should I store iloperidone?** A: Store iloperidone tablets in a cool, dry place, away from direct sunlight and moisture, as directed on the product label.

* **Q: Can I drive while taking iloperidone?** A: Iloperidone can cause drowsiness, so it’s best to avoid driving or operating heavy machinery until you know how the medication affects you.

* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: If you miss a dose, take it as soon as you remember, unless it is almost time for the next dose.  Do not double the dose.

* **Q:  Is iloperidone addictive?** A: Iloperidone is not considered physically addictive in the same way as opioids. However, stopping treatment abruptly can lead to withdrawal symptoms.  Always follow your doctor's instructions regarding medication management.
