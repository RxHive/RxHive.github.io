# Insulin Degludec

**Generic Name: Insulin Degludec**

**Usage:**

Insulin degludec is a long-acting insulin analog used primarily to treat type 1 and type 2 diabetes mellitus in adults and children.  Its primary use is to control blood sugar levels by mimicking the body's natural insulin production.  Unlike some faster-acting insulins, degludec provides a very prolonged effect, offering consistent glucose control over a 24-hour period or even longer.  This makes it particularly useful for managing blood sugar levels throughout the day and night, reducing the risk of overnight hypoglycemia (low blood sugar). While its primary application is in managing diabetes, its prolonged action makes it a useful component in various treatment regimens, including those incorporating other insulin types for optimal blood glucose control.

**Dosage:**

Insulin degludec is administered only via subcutaneous injection (under the skin).  It's not available in tablet or syrup form.  Dosage is highly individualized and determined by a healthcare professional based on factors including the patient's blood glucose levels, overall health, other medications being taken, and individual response to treatment.  There's no universal "standard" dosage.  For adults and children, the starting dose is typically low and gradually increased to achieve optimal glycemic control.  Adjustments are made based on regular blood glucose monitoring.  A healthcare provider will carefully tailor the dosage to each patient's specific needs.

**Side Effects:**

Common side effects can include:

* Hypoglycemia (low blood sugar): This is a significant risk with all insulins, and symptoms can include dizziness, sweating, shaking, and confusion.
* Injection site reactions:  These can range from redness and swelling to pain or itching at the injection site.
* Weight gain:  Insulin can lead to weight gain in some individuals.
* Lipohypertrophy (fatty lumps) at the injection site: This is a less common, but potentially serious, side effect.


Less common, but potentially serious side effects, include severe allergic reactions (anaphylaxis).  Any unusual or concerning symptoms should be reported immediately to a healthcare provider.

**How it Works:**

Insulin degludec works by binding to insulin receptors on cells throughout the body. This binding triggers a cascade of events that allow glucose (sugar) from the bloodstream to enter cells.  By facilitating glucose uptake into cells, it lowers blood glucose levels, thereby helping to manage diabetes.  The long-acting nature of degludec is due to its unique molecular structure, which forms soluble multihexamers that slowly release insulin into the bloodstream, ensuring a consistent effect over an extended duration.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:**  Insulin degludec is contraindicated in individuals with known hypersensitivity (allergy) to insulin degludec or any of its components.
* **Drug Interactions:**  Many medications can interact with insulin, potentially affecting blood glucose levels.  It's crucial to inform your healthcare provider of all medications, including over-the-counter drugs and herbal supplements, you are taking.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  Use during pregnancy and breastfeeding requires careful monitoring by a healthcare professional to ensure both maternal and fetal/infant well-being.  Dosage adjustments may be necessary.
* **Elderly Patients:**  Elderly individuals may be more susceptible to hypoglycemia, requiring close monitoring and potential dosage adjustments.
* **Renal and Hepatic Impairment:**  Individuals with kidney or liver disease may require modified dosing regimens.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long does insulin degludec last?** A: Its effect typically lasts for 24 hours or longer, depending on individual factors.
* **Q: Can I mix insulin degludec with other insulins?** A:  Generally, mixing is not recommended unless specifically instructed by your healthcare provider.
* **Q: How should I store insulin degludec?** A: Store it in the refrigerator, but do not freeze.  Once in use, it can be kept at room temperature for up to 42 days. Always check the manufacturer's instructions.
* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Contact your healthcare provider for guidance.  Never double up on a dose.
* **Q: What are the symptoms of low blood sugar?** A: Symptoms include sweating, shakiness, dizziness, confusion, and rapid heartbeat.  Treatment usually involves consuming fast-acting carbohydrates.
