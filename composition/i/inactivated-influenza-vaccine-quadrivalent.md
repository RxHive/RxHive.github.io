# Inactivated influenza vaccine Quadrivalent

**Generic Name:** Inactivated influenza vaccine Quadrivalent

**Usage:**

The inactivated influenza vaccine quadrivalent is primarily used to prevent influenza (flu).  Influenza is a viral respiratory illness that can cause a range of symptoms, from mild discomfort to severe complications, especially in vulnerable populations like the elderly, young children, and individuals with underlying health conditions. This vaccine protects against four strains of the influenza virus—two influenza A strains and two influenza B strains—that are predicted to be most prevalent during a particular flu season.  While its primary use is prevention,  it indirectly helps reduce the severity and duration of illness in those who still contract the flu after vaccination.  There aren't "secondary uses" in the sense of treating other medical conditions; its sole purpose is influenza prevention.

**Dosage:**

The inactivated influenza vaccine quadrivalent is administered as an intramuscular injection (a shot).  The dosage is typically a single dose for both adults and children aged 6 months and older.  Specific dosage amounts may vary slightly depending on the manufacturer and the child's age, so always follow your healthcare provider's instructions and the information provided with the vaccine.  There's no dosage adjustment based on weight or severity of any pre-existing condition; the recommended single dose is the standard for all recipients.


**Side Effects:**

Common side effects are generally mild and temporary, and may include:

* Pain, redness, or swelling at the injection site
* Headache
* Muscle aches
* Fatigue
* Fever
* Nausea

Less common, but more serious side effects, though rare, can include allergic reactions (such as anaphylaxis, a severe, life-threatening allergic reaction).  

**Important:**  Seek immediate medical attention if you experience any severe or unusual reaction after receiving the influenza vaccine.


**How it Works:**

The inactivated influenza vaccine contains inactivated (killed) viruses.  When administered, this vaccine introduces these inactivated viruses to the body's immune system.  The immune system recognizes these viruses as foreign invaders and produces antibodies against them.  These antibodies provide protection against future infection by the strains of influenza virus included in the vaccine.  If you are subsequently exposed to these flu strains, your immune system will be ready to fight them off, preventing or reducing the severity of the illness.

**Precautions:**

* **Allergies:** Individuals with a known history of severe allergic reactions (anaphylaxis) to any component of the influenza vaccine, including eggs (as the vaccine is often produced using eggs), should not receive it.
* **Guillain-Barré Syndrome:**  A history of Guillain-Barré syndrome (a rare neurological disorder) following a previous influenza vaccination is a contraindication.
* **Illness:**  Individuals with a severe acute illness should generally wait until they recover before receiving the vaccine.
* **Pregnancy:**  While generally considered safe during pregnancy, it's crucial to discuss the risks and benefits with a healthcare professional.
* **Children:**  Children under 6 months of age are not typically given this vaccine.
* **Other medications:** While interactions are rare, it's best to inform your doctor of all medications you are taking.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long does the vaccine last?** A:  The protection offered by the influenza vaccine typically lasts for a single flu season (approximately 6-12 months). Annual vaccination is recommended.
* **Q: Is the vaccine safe?** A:  The inactivated influenza vaccine has a long history of safe and effective use. However, like any medication, there are potential side effects, which are generally mild.
* **Q: Can I get the flu from the vaccine?** A: No.  The vaccine contains inactivated (killed) viruses and cannot cause influenza.
* **Q: How should I store the vaccine?** A:  Always follow the storage instructions provided by the manufacturer, usually refrigerated at a specified temperature.
* **Q: What if I missed my vaccination?** A: Get vaccinated as soon as possible.  It's still beneficial to get vaccinated later in the flu season.
