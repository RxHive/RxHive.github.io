# Isosorbide Dinitrate + Hydralazine

**Generic Name:** Isosorbide Dinitrate + Hydralazine

**Usage:**

Isosorbide dinitrate + hydralazine is primarily used to treat severe, persistent high blood pressure (hypertension) that hasn't responded well to other medications. This combination is particularly beneficial for patients with heart failure, especially those with symptoms like shortness of breath and fluid retention.  The drug combination works synergistically; isosorbide dinitrate dilates (widens) blood vessels, reducing the workload on the heart, while hydralazine relaxes and widens arteries, lowering blood pressure. While not its primary use, it might be considered in certain cases of severe hypertension in pregnancy (pre-eclampsia or eclampsia) under strict medical supervision due to the potential benefits and risks involved.  Always consult with a healthcare professional to determine if this medication is appropriate for your specific condition.


**Dosage:**

Dosage is highly individualized and determined by a physician based on the patient's condition, response to treatment, and overall health.  There isn't a universally standardized dosage for children due to the risks associated with using this drug in young populations. This medication is generally not recommended for children. 

For adults, the usual starting dose might be a low dose, gradually increased over time to find the optimal balance of blood pressure control and side effects. The medication is typically available as an oral tablet.  Dosage adjustments are often made based on blood pressure readings and the patient's tolerance.  Specific instructions on dosage and administration must come directly from a prescribing physician.  Never adjust your dosage without consulting your doctor.


**Side Effects:**

Common side effects can include:

* Headache
* Dizziness
* Nausea
* Flushing (redness of the face)
* Lightheadedness
* Fatigue
* Palpitations (rapid or irregular heartbeat)

Less common but serious side effects may include:

* Swelling in ankles, feet, or legs (edema)
* Rapid weight gain
* Chest pain
* Shortness of breath
* Allergic reactions (rash, hives, difficulty breathing)
* Lupus-like syndrome (a rare but serious autoimmune condition)


If you experience any of these side effects, especially the serious ones, seek immediate medical attention.


**How it Works:**

Isosorbide dinitrate is a nitrate that works by relaxing blood vessels, reducing the resistance to blood flow.  This leads to lower blood pressure and reduced workload on the heart. Hydralazine is a vasodilator that primarily affects the arteries, causing them to relax and widen, thus lowering blood pressure. The combination of these two drugs provides a more potent and comprehensive blood pressure-lowering effect than either drug alone.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** This medication is contraindicated in patients with certain conditions, such as severe anemia, severe coronary artery disease, and hypersensitivity to either isosorbide dinitrate or hydralazine.
* **Drug Interactions:**  Isosorbide dinitrate + hydralazine can interact with various other medications, including other blood pressure medications, antidepressants, and erectile dysfunction drugs.  Inform your doctor of all medications, supplements, and herbal remedies you are taking.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:** This medication is generally avoided during pregnancy unless the benefits outweigh the potential risks.  Its use during breastfeeding requires careful consideration and monitoring.
* **Older Adults:**  Older adults are generally more sensitive to the side effects of this medication.  Careful dose adjustment and monitoring are often necessary.
* **Patients with Liver or Kidney Disease:** Dose adjustments may be needed for patients with impaired liver or kidney function.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long does it take to work?** A: The onset of action varies, but blood pressure lowering effects are usually seen within a short time after taking the medication.  However, the full therapeutic effect may take several days or weeks.

* **Q: Can I stop taking it suddenly?** A: No, abruptly stopping this medication can lead to rebound hypertension (a sudden increase in blood pressure).  Always follow your doctor's instructions for tapering off the medication.

* **Q: How should I store it?** A: Store the medication in a cool, dry place, away from direct sunlight and moisture. Keep it out of reach of children.

* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it's almost time for the next dose.  Do not double the dose.

* **Q: Can I drink alcohol while taking this medication?** A:  Alcohol can potentially worsen the side effects of this medication, so it's advisable to limit alcohol consumption while taking it.  Consult your physician for specific advice.
