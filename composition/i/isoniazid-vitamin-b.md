# Isoniazid + Vitamin B

**Generic Name:** Isoniazid + Vitamin B6 (Pyridoxine)


**Usage:**

This combination medication is primarily used to treat tuberculosis (TB), a serious bacterial infection affecting the lungs.  Isoniazid is the main active ingredient, a powerful antibiotic specifically targeting the *Mycobacterium tuberculosis* bacteria responsible for TB.  Vitamin B6 (pyridoxine) is included because isoniazid can interfere with the body's use of vitamin B6, potentially leading to side effects like peripheral neuropathy (nerve damage).  Adding vitamin B6 helps mitigate this risk. While the primary use is for TB treatment,  it might be prescribed as part of a broader regimen for latent TB infection (LTBI), where the bacteria are present but not causing active disease.  The goal in LTBI is to prevent the infection from developing into active TB.


**Dosage:**

Dosage varies greatly depending on the individual's age, weight, overall health, and the specific type of TB being treated (active or latent). A healthcare professional will determine the appropriate dose.  It's crucial to follow their instructions precisely.  The medication is typically available in tablet form, but other formulations (e.g., syrup for children) might exist.  Dosage may be given once daily or divided into multiple doses throughout the day.  Self-adjusting the dosage is strictly prohibited.


**Side Effects:**

* **Common:** Nausea, vomiting, dizziness, loss of appetite, stomach upset, headache.
* **Less Common but Serious:**
    * Peripheral neuropathy (numbness, tingling, pain in the extremities)
    * Liver damage (jaundice, dark urine, fatigue)
    * Allergic reactions (rash, itching, swelling)
    * Seizures
    * Blood disorders

**If you experience any of these side effects, especially the serious ones, contact your doctor immediately.**


**How it Works:**

Isoniazid works by interfering with the synthesis of mycolic acids, essential components of the *Mycobacterium tuberculosis* cell wall.  This disruption prevents the bacteria from building and maintaining their protective outer layer, ultimately leading to their death.  Vitamin B6 plays a crucial supporting role by ensuring the body has sufficient levels of this vitamin, thus reducing the risk of isoniazid-induced peripheral neuropathy.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** Isoniazid should not be used by individuals with known hypersensitivity (allergy) to isoniazid or any of its components.
* **Drug Interactions:** Isoniazid can interact with various medications, including certain antidepressants, anticonvulsants, and other drugs metabolized by the liver.  Always inform your healthcare provider about all medications, supplements, and herbal remedies you are taking.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  The use of isoniazid during pregnancy and breastfeeding requires careful monitoring by a doctor.  The risks and benefits must be carefully weighed.
* **Children and Older Adults:**  Dosage adjustments may be necessary for children and older adults due to differences in metabolism and potential increased sensitivity to side effects.
* **Liver Function:**  Regular monitoring of liver function is often recommended, particularly during long-term treatment.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long do I need to take this medication?**  A: The duration of treatment depends on the severity and type of TB infection and is determined by your doctor.  It can range from several months to over a year.
* **Q: Can I stop taking the medication if I feel better?** A:  No, stopping treatment prematurely can lead to the development of drug-resistant TB.  Complete the entire course of treatment as prescribed.
* **Q: How should I store the medication?** A: Store the medication in a cool, dry place, away from direct sunlight and moisture. Keep it out of reach of children.
* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it is almost time for your next dose.  Do not double the dose.
