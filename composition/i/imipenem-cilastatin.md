# Imipenem + Cilastatin

**Generic Name: Imipenem + Cilastatin**

**Usage:**

Imipenem + cilastatin is a powerful antibiotic combination used to treat severe bacterial infections.  Its primary use lies in tackling infections caused by bacteria resistant to many other antibiotics.  These infections can affect various parts of the body, including:

* **Serious lung infections (pneumonia):** Particularly those that are difficult to treat with other antibiotics.
* **Severe abdominal infections (intra-abdominal infections):**  Such as peritonitis (infection of the lining of the abdomen) or infections following abdominal surgery.
* **Infections of the urinary tract (UTIs):**  Especially complicated UTIs that haven't responded to simpler treatments.
* **Bacterial infections of the skin and soft tissues:** Including cellulitis and abscesses.
* **Sepsis:** A life-threatening condition caused by the body's overwhelming response to an infection.
* **Bacterial meningitis:** A serious infection of the membranes surrounding the brain and spinal cord.  (Note: Use is usually reserved for severe cases and often requires hospitalization).

While primarily used for severe infections, the physician might consider it for other serious bacterial infections based on their assessment of the individual's specific condition and the bacteria involved.


**Dosage:**

Dosage varies greatly depending on the severity of the infection, the patient's age, weight, and kidney function.  It's crucial to follow the physician's instructions precisely.  Imipenem + cilastatin is typically administered intravenously (IV) or intramuscularly (IM).  Oral formulations are not generally available.

* **Adults:** The usual dose is 500mg to 1g every 6-8 hours, depending on the severity of the infection.
* **Children:** Dosage is determined by weight and the specific infection. A physician will calculate the appropriate dose.  

The administration method (IV or IM) and the exact frequency are determined by the physician and depend heavily on the individual patient's clinical needs and response to treatment.


**Side Effects:**

* **Common:** Nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, rash, injection site pain or inflammation.
* **Less Common but Serious:** Seizures (especially in patients with pre-existing seizure disorders or kidney problems), allergic reactions (including anaphylaxis – a life-threatening allergic reaction), changes in blood cell counts, increased liver enzymes.

**Important Note:** If you experience any unusual symptoms or side effects, consult your doctor immediately.


**How it Works:**

Imipenem is a beta-lactam antibiotic, meaning it works by inhibiting the formation of bacterial cell walls.  This weakens the bacteria, ultimately leading to their death.  Cilastatin is added to protect imipenem from breakdown by an enzyme in the kidneys (dehydropeptidase I), allowing more of the imipenem to reach the bloodstream and effectively fight the infection.

The specific brand names available in India can vary. It's best to consult with a pharmacist or physician for the currently available options.


**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** Imipenem + cilastatin should not be used in individuals with known allergies to carbapenems (a class of antibiotics including imipenem).
* **Drug Interactions:**  This medication can interact with certain other drugs, so it's essential to inform your physician of all medications you're taking.
* **Pregnant/Breastfeeding Women:** Use should be carefully considered and only prescribed by a physician when the potential benefit outweighs the potential risks.
* **Elderly and Children:** Dosage adjustments may be necessary based on kidney function. Close monitoring is often required.
* **Kidney Problems:**  Imipenem is primarily eliminated through the kidneys.  Individuals with reduced kidney function require careful dose adjustments to prevent accumulation and potential toxicity.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: Can I stop taking the medication once I feel better?**  A: No.  Continue the prescribed course even if symptoms improve to prevent the infection from returning or becoming resistant.
* **Q: How should I store Imipenem + Cilastatin?** A:  Store as directed by your pharmacist, usually at room temperature and away from moisture and light.
* **Q: Is it safe to drink alcohol while on this medication?** A:  It's best to avoid alcohol while taking this antibiotic, as it can potentially worsen side effects or interact negatively.  Always check with your physician.
* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A:  Contact your physician or pharmacist for advice; do not attempt to double up on the next dose.
