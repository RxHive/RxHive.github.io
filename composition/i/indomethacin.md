# Indomethacin

**Generic Name: Indomethacin**

**Usage:**

Indomethacin is a nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drug (NSAID) primarily used to reduce pain, fever, and inflammation.  Its primary use is in managing moderate to severe pain, particularly associated with musculoskeletal conditions like rheumatoid arthritis, osteoarthritis, and ankylosing spondylitis.  It's also effective in treating acute gouty arthritis, reducing the pain and swelling associated with a gout attack.  Secondary uses include the treatment of bursitis and tendonitis, and sometimes post-surgical or post-traumatic inflammation.  In certain specific situations, under strict medical supervision, it might be used for other inflammatory conditions.  However,  its use in these secondary applications is often superseded by newer, safer NSAIDs.  It's crucial to understand that indomethacin is a powerful medication and should only be used as prescribed by a doctor.

**Dosage:**

Dosage varies greatly depending on the condition being treated, the patient's age, weight, and overall health.  A physician will determine the appropriate dose.  It's generally available in tablet, capsule, and sometimes suppository forms.  

* **Adults:**  The starting dose is typically low and gradually increased as needed.  This can range from 25-75 mg per day, divided into multiple doses.  Higher doses may be used in certain conditions, but these are usually under close medical supervision due to the increased risk of side effects.

* **Children:**  Indomethacin is generally not recommended for children, except under very specific circumstances and with careful monitoring by a pediatrician. The dosage would be determined based on the child’s weight and the specific condition being treated.  

* **Injection:** Injectable forms exist but are typically reserved for specific hospital settings and managed by medical professionals.

It's vital to follow the prescribed dosage and administration instructions exactly as directed by your doctor or pharmacist.  Never alter the dose or frequency without consulting your physician.


**Side Effects:**

Common side effects can include:

* Nausea
* Vomiting
* Heartburn
* Indigestion
* Headache
* Dizziness
* Diarrhea
* Constipation
* Abdominal pain


Less common, but potentially serious, side effects include:

* Stomach ulcers
* Bleeding or perforation of the gastrointestinal tract
* Liver damage
* Kidney problems
* Increased risk of heart attack or stroke
* Allergic reactions (rash, itching, swelling)


If you experience any side effects, particularly those listed as serious, consult your doctor immediately.


**How it Works:**

Indomethacin works by inhibiting the production of prostaglandins, chemical messengers in the body that contribute to pain, fever, and inflammation. By reducing prostaglandin production, indomethacin helps alleviate the symptoms of various inflammatory conditions.


**Popular Brand Names (India):**

Brand names vary across regions.  A doctor or pharmacist in India can provide information on locally available brands.  It's always best to refer to your prescription for the specific brand name prescribed to you.


**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** Indomethacin should not be used by individuals with known allergies to indomethacin or other NSAIDs, severe heart failure, active peptic ulcers, or certain bleeding disorders.

* **Drug Interactions:**  Indomethacin can interact with many other medications, including anticoagulants (blood thinners), lithium, and certain diuretics.  Always inform your doctor of all medications you are taking, including over-the-counter drugs and herbal supplements.

* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:** Indomethacin should generally be avoided during pregnancy, especially during the later stages, due to potential risks to the developing fetus.  It's also generally avoided during breastfeeding, as it can pass into breast milk.  A doctor should be consulted to weigh the risks and benefits.

* **Older Adults and Children:**  Older adults and children are more susceptible to the side effects of indomethacin.  Dosage adjustments and careful monitoring are often necessary.

* **Alcohol Consumption:**  Combining indomethacin with alcohol can increase the risk of stomach bleeding and other side effects.  It's advisable to avoid alcohol while taking this medication.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long does it take for indomethacin to work?**  A: The onset of pain relief varies, but it can take several days for full therapeutic effect.

* **Q: Can I stop taking indomethacin suddenly?** A: No, it's crucial to follow your doctor's instructions regarding stopping the medication.  Sudden cessation may lead to withdrawal symptoms or worsening of the underlying condition.

* **Q: How should I store indomethacin?** A: Store it in a cool, dry place, away from direct sunlight and moisture. Keep it out of reach of children.

* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it's almost time for the next dose.  Don't double up on doses.

* **Q: Is indomethacin addictive?** A: Indomethacin is not considered an addictive drug, but it’s crucial to only use it as prescribed and not to exceed the recommended dosage.
