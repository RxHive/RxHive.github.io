# Indacaterol

**Generic Name: Indacaterol**

**Usage:**

Indacaterol is a long-acting beta2-adrenergic agonist (LABA).  Its primary use is in the long-term, maintenance treatment of chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD), including chronic bronchitis and emphysema.  This means it helps manage symptoms like shortness of breath and wheezing in people with COPD, making it easier for them to breathe.  While its primary use is in COPD, it's sometimes used as part of a combination therapy for asthma, though it's not a first-line treatment for this condition.  The decision to use indacaterol in asthma will be made by a physician considering individual patient circumstances and other treatments.


**Dosage:**

Indacaterol is typically inhaled, usually once daily.  The standard adult dose is one inhalation of 150 mcg (micrograms) using a metered-dose inhaler or dry powder inhaler.  Dosage adjustments are rarely made based on age or weight in adults, but should always be determined by a doctor.  Indacaterol is not generally recommended for children due to a lack of sufficient data on its safety and effectiveness in this population.  The specific inhaler device and instructions should be carefully followed as prescribed by a healthcare professional.


**Side Effects:**

Common side effects of indacaterol may include:

* Headache
* Upper respiratory tract infections
* Throat irritation
* Cough
* Nausea
* Muscle cramps
* Dizziness
* Tremor

Less common, but more serious side effects, which require immediate medical attention, include:

* Severe allergic reactions (anaphylaxis), including hives, swelling of the face, lips, tongue, or throat, difficulty breathing or swallowing
* Irregular heartbeat (arrhythmias)
* Increased blood pressure
* Worsening of existing heart conditions


**How it Works:**

Indacaterol works by relaxing the muscles in the airways of the lungs. It achieves this by binding to beta2-adrenergic receptors in the lungs.  This relaxes the smooth muscles surrounding the airways, resulting in wider airways and improved airflow. This eases breathing, particularly beneficial for those whose airways are narrowed due to COPD or asthma.  The long-acting nature of indacaterol provides sustained relief over a 24-hour period.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** Indacaterol should not be used by individuals with known hypersensitivity (allergy) to indacaterol or any of its components.
* **Drug Interactions:**  While not exhaustive, interacting drugs include other medications that stimulate beta2-adrenergic receptors. Interactions with other medications should be discussed with a doctor or pharmacist.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:** The use of indacaterol during pregnancy and breastfeeding should be carefully considered by a healthcare professional.  Benefits and risks should be carefully weighed.
* **Older Adults:**  While dose adjustments are typically not necessary, close monitoring for side effects is often recommended in older adults.
* **Cardiovascular Conditions:** Patients with pre-existing cardiovascular conditions should be closely monitored for any changes in heart rate or blood pressure.
