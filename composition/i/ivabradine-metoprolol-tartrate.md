# Ivabradine + Metoprolol Tartrate

**Generic Name:** Ivabradine + Metoprolol Tartrate

**Usage:**

Ivabradine and Metoprolol Tartrate is a combination medication used primarily to treat chronic stable angina (chest pain) in adults who have already been treated with metoprolol tartrate alone but still experience symptoms.  It's particularly beneficial for patients whose heart rate is relatively high and who aren't adequately controlled with metoprolol alone. The combination works by targeting two aspects of the heart's function: reducing heart rate (Ivabradine) and lowering blood pressure (Metoprolol).  While its primary use is for stable angina, it may be considered under a doctor’s supervision in specific situations involving heart failure or other heart conditions where both rate and pressure control are needed.  However, this combination is not suitable for all patients with heart conditions and its use must be determined by a physician.

**Dosage:**

The dosage of Ivabradine + Metoprolol Tartrate is determined by a doctor based on the individual's condition, overall health, and response to treatment.  There isn't a standard, universally applied dosage.  The medication is typically available in tablet form.  The components (Ivabradine and Metoprolol Tartrate) are usually titrated separately to achieve the desired effect.  The doctor will carefully monitor heart rate and blood pressure while adjusting the dosages.  This combination drug is not typically used in children.  Precise dosage instructions will always be provided by a healthcare professional and should be followed meticulously.


**Side Effects:**

* **Common:** Dizziness, lightheadedness, headache, nausea, fatigue, bradycardia (slow heart rate), and visual disturbances (such as blurred vision or seeing halos).
* **Less Common but Serious:**  Severe bradycardia, heart block, hypotension (low blood pressure), shortness of breath, worsening heart failure, allergic reactions (rash, itching, swelling), and liver problems.

*If you experience any of these side effects, especially those listed as serious, contact your doctor immediately.*


**How it Works:**

Ivabradine selectively inhibits the If current in the sinoatrial node of the heart. This slows down the heart rate, reducing the heart's workload and oxygen demand. Metoprolol Tartrate is a beta-blocker, which works by blocking the effects of adrenaline and noradrenaline on the heart. This leads to a decrease in heart rate and blood pressure, further reducing the oxygen demand of the heart. By combining these two mechanisms, the combination therapy helps control angina symptoms more effectively than either drug alone in appropriate patients.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:**  Ivabradine + Metoprolol Tartrate is contraindicated in patients with certain heart conditions (e.g., sick sinus syndrome, second- or third-degree atrioventricular block, bradycardia, severe hypotension), severe liver or kidney disease, and hypersensitivity to either component.
* **Drug Interactions:**  This combination can interact with other medications, including certain heart medications, calcium channel blockers, and antidepressants. Always inform your doctor of all medications you are taking.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  The use of this medication during pregnancy and breastfeeding should be carefully evaluated by a physician, weighing potential benefits against risks.
* **Older Adults:**  Older adults are more sensitive to the side effects of this medication and require careful monitoring.
* **Children:**  This combination is generally not recommended for children.
