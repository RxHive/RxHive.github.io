# Fructo Oligosaccharide + Lactobacillus crispatus + Lactobacillus gasseri + Lactobacillus jensenii + Lactobacillus rhamnosus

**Generic Name:** Fructo Oligosaccharide + *Lactobacillus crispatus* + *Lactobacillus gasseri* + *Lactobacillus jensenii* + *Lactobacillus rhamnosus*


**Usage:** This combination of fructooligosaccharides (FOS) and specific *Lactobacillus* strains is primarily used to support gut health and potentially alleviate symptoms associated with various gastrointestinal conditions.  Its main use focuses on restoring the balance of beneficial bacteria (microbiota) in the intestines.  This can be helpful in managing conditions like:

* **Inflammatory bowel syndrome (IBS):**  By promoting a healthy gut environment, it may help reduce symptoms such as bloating, abdominal pain, and irregular bowel movements.
* **Infectious diarrhea:**  The *Lactobacillus* strains can compete with harmful bacteria, potentially shortening the duration and severity of diarrhea.  This is particularly relevant for diarrhea caused by antibiotic use (antibiotic-associated diarrhea).
* **Constipation:** FOS act as prebiotics, feeding the beneficial bacteria and promoting regular bowel movements.
* **Vaginal infections (certain types):**  While not a primary treatment, some research suggests that specific *Lactobacillus* strains, like those included here, may help restore a healthy vaginal microbiota and potentially prevent or reduce recurrent infections (though further research is needed to solidify these claims).  It is crucial to always consult a healthcare professional for diagnosis and treatment of vaginal infections.

**How it Works:**

This combination product works through a synergistic mechanism:

* **Fructooligosaccharides (FOS):** These are prebiotics—non-digestible carbohydrates that serve as food for beneficial bacteria like *Lactobacillus* strains. By increasing the number of these good bacteria, FOS help to improve the balance of the gut microbiota.  This improved balance helps reduce inflammation, improves digestion, and enhances the immune response in the gut.
* **Lactobacillus Strains (*crispatus*, *gasseri*, *jensenii*, *rhamnosus*):** These are probiotics—live microorganisms that, when administered in adequate amounts, confer a health benefit on the host.  They colonize the gut, competing with harmful bacteria for resources and space. Some *Lactobacillus* strains also produce substances that inhibit the growth of pathogens, helping restore a healthy gut ecosystem. They may also exert positive effects in other parts of the body, such as the vagina.
