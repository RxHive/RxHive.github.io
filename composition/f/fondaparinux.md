# Fondaparinux

**Generic Name: Fondaparinux**

**Usage:**

Fondaparinux is a blood thinner (anticoagulant) primarily used to prevent blood clots (thrombosis) in hospitalized patients who are at high risk of developing these clots.  Its main application is in the prevention of deep vein thrombosis (DVT) and pulmonary embolism (PE) – serious conditions where blood clots form in the deep veins of the legs or lungs, respectively.  This is particularly important after major surgery, such as hip or knee replacement, where patients are at significantly increased risk.  Fondaparinux may also be used to treat DVT and PE in certain situations, although other anticoagulants are often preferred for treatment.  Its use in treatment is typically reserved for situations where other options are not suitable.


**Dosage:**

Fondaparinux is administered as a subcutaneous (under-the-skin) injection.  Dosage varies depending on the patient's weight, medical condition, and the reason for its use.  A healthcare professional will determine the appropriate dose.  There is no standard pediatric dosage readily available in published guidelines – use in children is limited and usually only considered in specific situations under close medical supervision.  The typical adult dose for prevention of DVT and PE post-surgery is usually a single daily injection.  The specific dosage and duration of treatment will be determined by a physician and will vary significantly depending on individual circumstances.


**Side Effects:**

Common side effects of fondaparinux include:

* Bleeding (ranging from minor bruising to more serious bleeding)
* Anemia
* Thrombocytopenia (low platelet count)
* Injection site reactions (pain, redness, swelling)

Less common but more serious side effects include:

* Spinal or epidural hematoma (bleeding around the spinal cord or epidural space)
* Severe allergic reactions
* Hepatic impairment (liver damage)


**It is crucial to contact a healthcare provider immediately if any adverse effects occur.**


**How it Works:**

Fondaparinux works by selectively inhibiting factor Xa, a key protein in the blood clotting cascade.  By inhibiting factor Xa, fondaparinux prevents the formation of blood clots.  This is different from how warfarin works, which affects vitamin K-dependent clotting factors.  The selective action on factor Xa is thought to reduce the risk of certain bleeding complications compared to some other anticoagulants.


**Popular Brand Names (India):**

Specific brand names for fondaparinux available in India can vary.  It's best to consult a pharmacist or check with the relevant regulatory authorities for a current list of available brands.


**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** Fondaparinux is contraindicated in patients with active clinically significant bleeding, severe hepatic impairment, or known hypersensitivity to the drug
