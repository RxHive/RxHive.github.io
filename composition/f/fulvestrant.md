# Fulvestrant

**Generic Name:** Fulvestrant

**Usage:**

Fulvestrant is a medication used primarily in the treatment of hormone-receptor-positive (HR+), human epidermal growth factor receptor 2-negative (HER2-) advanced breast cancer in women.  This means it's effective against breast cancers whose growth is fueled by hormones like estrogen, and which don't overexpress the HER2 protein.  It's specifically indicated for women who have already received endocrine therapy (hormone-blocking treatment) for their metastatic disease.  In simpler terms, it's a treatment option *after* other hormone therapies haven't been as effective.  While its primary use is in advanced breast cancer, research is ongoing to explore its potential in other hormone-dependent cancers.

**Dosage:**

Fulvestrant is administered as an intramuscular injection.  There is no oral or tablet form.

* **Adults:** The standard dose is 500 mg given as a single injection every month.  The initial dose may be administered as two 250 mg injections given one month apart; however, it's usually a single 500mg injection thereafter.  Dosage adjustments based on age or weight are generally not necessary. The physician determines the precise injection schedule and duration of treatment based on the patient's individual response and condition.

* **Children:** Fulvestrant is not approved for use in children.


**Side Effects:**

* **Common Side Effects:**  Injection site reactions (pain, redness, swelling), hot flashes, fatigue, nausea, and vomiting.
* **Less Common, but Serious Side Effects:** Thromboembolic events (blood clots),  liver problems (elevated liver enzymes), and allergic reactions.

**Important Note:**  If you experience any side effects, particularly concerning ones like severe allergic reactions, blood clots, or significant liver problems, seek immediate medical attention.  Your doctor should be informed about any side effects you experience.


**How it Works:**

Fulvestrant is a selective estrogen receptor downregulator (SERD).  Estrogen, a female hormone, plays a significant role in the growth of certain breast cancers.  Fulvestrant works by binding to the estrogen receptors within cancer cells, preventing estrogen from binding.  This effectively blocks the estrogen's stimulating effect on cancer cell growth, leading to a decrease in tumor size or slowing of its progression. Unlike some other hormone therapies, fulvestrant doesn't just block estrogen; it actually degrades the estrogen receptors themselves, making it a potent anti-estrogen agent.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** Fulvestrant should not be used in individuals with known hypersensitivity (allergic reaction) to the drug.
* **Drug Interactions:**  Potential interactions with other medications exist; therefore, it is crucial to inform your doctor of all medications you are currently taking, including over-the-counter drugs and herbal supplements.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:** Fulvestrant is contraindicated during pregnancy and breastfeeding due to its potential harmful effects on the developing fetus and infant. Effective contraception is necessary during treatment and for a period after.
* **Older Adults:** Older adults may be at increased risk for some side effects. Close monitoring is often advised.
* **Liver Function:** Liver function should be assessed before starting treatment and regularly monitored during treatment.

* **Q: How is Fulvestrant stored?** A:  Store as directed by your pharmacist or physician; typically refrigerated.
