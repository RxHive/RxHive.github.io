# Fluphenazine

**Generic Name: Fluphenazine**

**Usage:**

Fluphenazine is a potent antipsychotic medication primarily used to treat schizophrenia and other psychotic disorders.  Its primary function is to manage the positive symptoms of psychosis, such as hallucinations (seeing or hearing things that aren't there), delusions (fixed, false beliefs), and disorganized thinking.  This makes it a valuable tool in helping individuals experiencing these debilitating symptoms to regain a better quality of life.  Fluphenazine can also be used to treat severe anxiety and agitation, particularly in situations where other treatments haven't been effective.  While less common, it may sometimes be used to treat severe Tourette's syndrome.  However, its use in these secondary conditions is usually considered only after other treatments have been tried and found insufficient.

**Dosage:**

Dosage of fluphenazine varies greatly depending on the individual's condition, response to treatment, and the specific formulation used.  It's crucial to follow the prescribed dosage exactly as directed by a healthcare professional.  There isn't a standardized "children's dose" as its use in children is limited and requires careful consideration by a specialist.  

* **Oral formulations (tablets, capsules):**  Dosages typically start low and are gradually increased under medical supervision.  This may range from a few milligrams per day to several times that amount, depending on the individual's needs.
* **Injectable formulations (decanoate):** This long-acting form is often given as a depot injection, providing medication over several weeks. The frequency and dosage are determined by a psychiatrist, based on factors such as the patient's response and stability.  

It is critical to understand that adjusting the dose of fluphenazine should *only* be done under the direct guidance of a doctor.  Never change your dosage independently.

**Side Effects:**

Fluphenazine, like many medications, can have side effects.  These vary in severity and frequency from person to person.

* **Common Side Effects:** Drowsiness, dizziness, dry mouth, constipation, blurred vision, weight gain, and tremors.
* **Less Common, but Serious Side Effects:**  Neuroleptic malignant syndrome (NMS – a rare but life-threatening condition), tardive dyskinesia (TD – involuntary movements, often of the face and mouth, that can be permanent),  low blood pressure, and seizures.  
* **Extrapyramidal Symptoms (EPS):** These include muscle stiffness, rigidity, restlessness (akathisia), and Parkinson-like symptoms.  These are relatively common and often managed with additional medication.

**It's essential to contact your doctor immediately if you experience any concerning side effects.**

**How it Works:**

Fluphenazine is a dopamine receptor antagonist.  This means it blocks the action of dopamine, a neurotransmitter in the brain that plays a significant role in regulating mood, movement, and thought processes.  In people with schizophrenia, there's an imbalance of dopamine activity.  By blocking dopamine receptors, fluphenazine helps to reduce the severity of psychotic symptoms.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** Fluphenazine should not be used by individuals with known hypersensitivity to the drug or other phenothiazines.
* **Drug Interactions:**  It can interact with several other medications, including some antidepressants and sedatives.  It's crucial to inform your doctor about all medications, supplements, and herbal remedies you are taking.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:** Fluphenazine should only be used during pregnancy or breastfeeding if the potential benefits significantly outweigh the risks.  A healthcare provider must carefully weigh these risks.
* **Elderly and Children:**  Fluphenazine requires careful monitoring in elderly patients due to increased sensitivity to its side effects.  Its use in children is generally limited to severe cases under close specialist supervision.
* **Driving and Operating Machinery:** Fluphenazine can cause drowsiness and dizziness, so avoid driving or operating machinery until you know how it affects you.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long does it take to work?** A: The effects of fluphenazine can vary, but noticeable improvements in symptoms may take several days or weeks.
* **Q: Can I stop taking it suddenly?** A: No, abruptly stopping fluphenazine can lead to withdrawal symptoms.  Always reduce the dosage gradually under medical supervision.
* **Q: How should I store it?** A: Store fluphenazine as directed on the label, usually at room temperature, away from moisture and heat.
* **Q: Is it addictive?** A: Fluphenazine itself is not considered addictive in the same way as opioids, but stopping abruptly after long-term use can lead to withdrawal.  This is why gradual tapering under medical guidance is essential.
* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: If you miss a dose, take it as soon as you remember unless it's almost time for your next dose. Don't take a double dose.
