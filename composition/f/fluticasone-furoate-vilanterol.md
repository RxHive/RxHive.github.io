# Fluticasone Furoate + Vilanterol

**Generic Name:** Fluticasone Furoate + Vilanterol

**Usage:**

Fluticasone furoate and vilanterol is a combination inhaler used primarily to treat and prevent symptoms of chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD), including chronic bronchitis and emphysema.  It's also approved for the maintenance treatment of asthma in adults and adolescents aged 12 years and older.  This combination medication works by targeting two different aspects of airway inflammation and obstruction.  The fluticasone furoate component is a corticosteroid that reduces inflammation in the airways, while vilanterol, a long-acting beta2-agonist (LABA), relaxes the muscles surrounding the airways, making breathing easier.  While its primary use is in COPD and asthma, it's crucial to remember that this medication should only be used as prescribed by a doctor and not for other respiratory conditions.


**Dosage:**

Dosage varies depending on the individual's condition, age, and response to treatment. A doctor will determine the appropriate dosage.  It's typically administered via an inhaler, with a specific number of inhalations prescribed daily.  There are no oral, injectable, or syrup forms of this combination medication available.   Children under 12 generally shouldn't use this specific combination; alternative treatments are usually more appropriate. Adult dosages commonly range from one to two inhalations twice daily.  Precise instructions will be provided on the inhaler's packaging and by your doctor or pharmacist.  It's vital to follow these instructions precisely.


**Side Effects:**

* **Common:** Headache, throat irritation, hoarseness, cough, and upper respiratory tract infections.
* **Less Common but Serious:**  Increased risk of pneumonia (especially in patients with COPD), increased risk of thrush (oral fungal infection), paradoxical bronchospasm (worsening of breathing difficulties), and abnormal heart rhythm.
* **Rare but potentially severe:**  Serious allergic reactions (anaphylaxis).

It is important to note that this is not an exhaustive list. If you experience any unexpected or concerning side effects, seek immediate medical attention.


**How it Works:**

Fluticasone furoate, a corticosteroid, works by reducing inflammation in the lungs' airways. This reduces swelling and irritation, making breathing easier.  Vilanterol, a long-acting beta2-agonist (LABA), works by relaxing the muscles around the airways, widening them and improving airflow.  The combination of these two medications provides both anti-inflammatory and bronchodilating effects, effectively managing the symptoms of COPD and asthma.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:**  This medication is contraindicated in patients with known hypersensitivity to either fluticasone furoate or vilanterol or any of the inhaler's excipients.
* **Drug Interactions:**  Certain medications can interact with fluticasone furoate + vilanterol.  Inform your doctor of all medications you are currently taking, including over-the-counter drugs and herbal supplements.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:** The use of this medication during pregnancy and breastfeeding should be carefully considered and discussed with your doctor to weigh the benefits against potential risks.
* **Elderly and Children:**  Specific dosage adjustments may be necessary for elderly individuals or children over 12 (as prescribed by a doctor).  Children under 12 should generally not use this particular combination.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long does it take to work?** A:  You may notice some improvement in breathing within minutes of using the inhaler, but the full benefit typically takes several days or weeks.
* **Q: Can I stop taking it suddenly?** A: No, always follow your doctor's instructions regarding stopping the medication.  Sudden cessation can lead to a worsening of symptoms.
* **Q: How should I store it?** A: Store the inhaler at room temperature, away from direct sunlight and moisture.
* **Q: What if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember unless it is almost time for the next dose. Don't take a double dose.
* **Q: Is it safe to use with other inhalers?** A:  Discuss any other inhalers or medications you are using with your doctor. They will advise on appropriate concurrent use.
