# Fexofenadine

**Generic Name: Fexofenadine**

**Usage:**

Fexofenadine is primarily used to relieve symptoms associated with allergies.  Its main application is in treating allergic rhinitis (hay fever), which causes symptoms like sneezing, runny nose, itchy eyes, and nasal congestion. It's also effective in managing urticaria (hives), a skin condition characterized by itchy welts.  While not its primary use, fexofenadine can sometimes be used to help control allergy symptoms related to other conditions as directed by a doctor.  The exact application may vary depending on the individual's needs and a healthcare professional's assessment.


**Dosage:**

Dosage varies depending on the individual's age and the specific formulation (tablet, capsule, or syrup).  Always follow your doctor's instructions carefully.  Generally, for adults, a common dose is 180mg once daily, although some situations might necessitate a different schedule. Children's dosages are significantly lower and are strictly weight-dependent, typically determined by a physician based on the child’s age and weight.  It's crucial to consult a doctor or pharmacist for the correct dosage for children.  Fexofenadine is usually taken orally, with or without food.


**Side Effects:**

*   Headache
*   Drowsiness (though less common than with some other antihistamines)
*   Dizziness
*   Nausea
*   Dry mouth
*   Fatigue
*   Stomach upset

**Serious but less common side effects:**  While rare, some individuals may experience more serious reactions such as a fast or irregular heartbeat, difficulty breathing, or severe allergic reactions (anaphylaxis).  **Seek immediate medical attention if any severe side effects occur.**


**How it Works:**

Fexofenadine is a non-sedating antihistamine.  Histamine is a substance released by the body during an allergic reaction. It triggers the symptoms you experience, like sneezing and itching.  Fexofenadine works by blocking histamine from binding to its receptors in the body. By preventing histamine from interacting with these receptors, it effectively reduces or prevents the allergic symptoms.

**Precautions:**

*   **Kidney problems:** Individuals with kidney problems may require a dosage adjustment.  Always inform your doctor about any kidney issues.
*   **Interactions with other medications:** Fexofenadine can interact with certain medications, particularly those metabolized by the liver.  It's important to disclose all medications, including over-the-counter drugs and supplements, to your doctor or pharmacist.
*   **Pregnancy and breastfeeding:**  Discuss the use of fexofenadine with your doctor if you are pregnant, breastfeeding, or planning to become pregnant.
*   **Children:**  Fexofenadine should only be given to children under strict medical supervision, with the dosage carefully adjusted to their weight and age.
*   **Older adults:**  Older adults may be more sensitive to the effects of fexofenadine.  A doctor should assess their suitability for this medication.



**FAQs:**

*   **Q: Can I take fexofenadine with alcohol?** A:  While not a strict contraindication, combining fexofenadine with alcohol can increase the risk of drowsiness. It’s best to avoid alcohol while taking this medication.
*   **Q: How should I store fexofenadine?** A: Store in a cool, dry place, away from direct sunlight and moisture. Keep it out of reach of children.
*   **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it is nearly time for your next dose. Do not take a double dose to make up for a missed one.
*   **Q:  Is fexofenadine habit-forming?** A: No, fexofenadine is not considered habit-forming.
*   **Q: Can I stop taking fexofenadine suddenly?** A:  Generally, you can stop taking fexofenadine when your symptoms are under control, but it's always best to consult your doctor before stopping any medication.
