# Felbinac

**Generic Name: Felbinac**

**Usage:**

Felbinac is a non-steroidal anti-inflammatory drug (NSAID) primarily used to relieve pain and reduce inflammation. Its main application is in the management of musculoskeletal disorders characterized by pain and inflammation. This includes conditions such as osteoarthritis, rheumatoid arthritis, and other forms of inflammatory joint pain.  It may also be used to alleviate pain associated with soft tissue injuries, such as sprains and strains.  While not its primary use, Felbinac might be employed for the short-term management of post-operative pain or menstrual cramps in some instances, although other, more specific medications are typically preferred for these conditions.  The use of Felbinac should always be guided by a healthcare professional.


**Dosage:**

Dosage of Felbinac varies depending on the specific formulation (tablet, capsule, gel, etc.), the individual's age, weight, and the severity of the condition.  It's crucial to follow the prescribed dosage instructions exactly as directed by a doctor or pharmacist.  Generally, adult dosages for oral forms range from 50mg to 150mg per day, often divided into multiple doses.  Pediatric dosages are significantly lower and should only be determined by a pediatrician. Topical formulations (gels or creams) have different application instructions and dosages that will be provided with the product.   Never exceed the recommended dose.  


**Side Effects:**

Common side effects of Felbinac can include:

* Upset stomach
* Nausea
* Heartburn
* Diarrhea
* Dizziness
* Headache
* Skin rash

Less common, but more serious side effects include:

* Allergic reactions (including swelling of the face, lips, or tongue)
* Severe stomach pain
* Gastrointestinal bleeding
* Kidney problems
* Liver problems
* Increased risk of cardiovascular events (heart attack, stroke)


If you experience any serious or concerning side effects, seek immediate medical attention.


**How it Works:**

Felbinac works by inhibiting the production of prostaglandins, chemicals in the body that contribute to inflammation, pain, and fever.  By reducing prostaglandin levels, Felbinac helps alleviate pain and decrease inflammation at the site of injury or disease. This mechanism of action is common to many NSAIDs.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** Felbinac should not be used by individuals with known allergies to Felbinac or other NSAIDs, those with active peptic ulcers, or those with severe heart, liver, or kidney disease.
* **Drug Interactions:** Felbinac may interact with several other medications, including blood thinners, diuretics, and other NSAIDs. It's essential to inform your doctor or pharmacist about all medications, supplements, and herbal remedies you are taking.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  The use of Felbinac during pregnancy or breastfeeding requires careful evaluation by a healthcare professional as it may carry risks to both mother and child.
* **Elderly:** Older adults are generally more susceptible to the side effects of NSAIDs, including gastrointestinal problems and kidney issues.  Dosage adjustments may be necessary.
* **Children:** Felbinac should only be administered to children under strict medical supervision due to potential side effects.

* **Q: Can I take Felbinac with alcohol?** A:  Combining Felbinac with alcohol can increase the risk of stomach upset and bleeding. It's best to avoid alcohol while taking Felbinac.

* **Q: How should I store Felbinac?** A: Store Felbinac as directed on the product label, typically in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight and heat.

* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it's almost time for the next dose. Never take a double dose to make up for a missed one.

* **Q: Can I stop taking Felbinac suddenly?** A:  Generally, it's not advised to stop taking Felbinac abruptly. Consult your doctor before discontinuing treatment to prevent potential withdrawal or rebound effects.
