# Flurbiprofen

**Generic Name:** Flurbiprofen

**Usage:**

Flurbiprofen is a nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drug (NSAID) primarily used to relieve pain and reduce inflammation.  Its primary use is in managing mild to moderate pain, such as that associated with headaches, menstrual cramps, toothaches, and musculoskeletal pain (like arthritis).  It's also used to reduce inflammation and pain in conditions such as osteoarthritis and rheumatoid arthritis.  While not its primary indication, Flurbiprofen is sometimes used to treat inflammation in the eye following surgery or injury.  The specific condition treated, and the most appropriate formulation (e.g., eye drops versus oral tablets), will be determined by a healthcare professional.

**Dosage:**

Dosage varies greatly depending on the condition being treated, the individual's age, weight, and overall health, as well as the specific formulation of the medication.  It's crucial to follow your doctor's prescription exactly.  Generally, oral formulations for adults might range from 50mg to 100mg several times a day, but this can vary significantly.  Children's dosages are considerably lower and strictly determined by a pediatrician based on weight and condition.  Opthalmic (eye drop) formulations have their own distinct dosage instructions.  Never alter the prescribed dosage without consulting your doctor.  Different forms exist: tablets, capsules, and eye drops are common, but other forms might be available.


**Side Effects:**

Common side effects can include:

* Upset stomach
* Nausea
* Heartburn
* Diarrhea
* Dizziness
* Headache
* Skin rash

Less common, but more serious side effects can include:

* Allergic reactions (such as swelling of the face, lips, tongue, or throat)
* Stomach ulcers or bleeding
* Liver problems
* Kidney problems
* Increased risk of heart attack or stroke (especially with long-term use or high doses)


If you experience any of these side effects, especially the more serious ones, seek immediate medical attention.


**How it Works:**

Flurbiprofen works by inhibiting the production of prostaglandins.  Prostaglandins are substances in the body that trigger inflammation, pain, and fever. By reducing prostaglandin production, Flurbiprofen helps to decrease inflammation and relieve pain. This effect is seen both systemically (throughout the body) when taken orally and locally in the eye when applied as eye drops.

**Precautions:**

* **Allergy:** Do not use Flurbiprofen if you are allergic to it or other NSAIDs.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:** Use during pregnancy and breastfeeding should only be considered after careful evaluation of the risks and benefits by a healthcare professional.
* **Children:**  Use in children should only be directed and supervised by a pediatrician.
* **Older Adults:**  Older adults might be more susceptible to side effects.  Close monitoring may be needed.
* **Liver or Kidney Disease:**  Patients with liver or kidney disease should use Flurbiprofen with caution and under close medical supervision.
* **Other Medications:**  Flurbiprofen can interact with several other medications, including blood thinners, diuretics, and some antidepressants.  Always inform your doctor or pharmacist about all medications you are taking.
* **Alcohol:** Avoid alcohol consumption while taking Flurbiprofen, as it can increase the risk of stomach upset and bleeding.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long does it take for Flurbiprofen to work?**  A: The onset of effect varies depending on the formulation and the condition being treated.  Pain relief might be felt within 30 minutes to an hour for some formulations, while others may take longer.
* **Q: Can I take Flurbiprofen with other pain relievers?**  A:  It’s essential to consult a doctor before combining Flurbiprofen with other pain relievers or medications.  Interactions can occur.
* **Q: How should I store Flurbiprofen?** A: Store as directed on the product label, usually at room temperature, away from moisture and direct sunlight. Keep out of reach of children.
* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: If you miss a dose, take it as soon as you remember, unless it's almost time for your next dose. Don't double the dose.
* **Q: Can I stop taking Flurbiprofen suddenly?** A: Generally, it's recommended to gradually reduce the dose under a doctor's guidance rather than stopping abruptly.
