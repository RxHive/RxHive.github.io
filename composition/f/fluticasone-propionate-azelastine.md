# Fluticasone Propionate + Azelastine

**Generic Name:** Fluticasone Propionate + Azelastine

**Usage:**

Fluticasone propionate and azelastine is a combination nasal spray used primarily to treat the symptoms of seasonal allergic rhinitis (hay fever) in adults and children aged 5 years and older.  This means it helps manage symptoms like sneezing, runny nose, itchy nose, and nasal congestion caused by pollen and other allergens.  While its primary use is for allergic rhinitis, it can also provide relief from the nasal symptoms of non-allergic rhinitis in some cases, although this is considered an off-label use and should be discussed with a doctor.  It's important to remember this medication treats symptoms, not the underlying cause of the allergy.

**Dosage:**

The usual dose for adults and children 12 years and older is two sprays in each nostril once daily.  Children aged 5 to 11 years old typically receive one spray in each nostril once daily.  The dosage should always be as prescribed by a doctor, and it's crucial to follow the instructions precisely.  This medication is available as a nasal spray; other forms like tablets or syrups are not available for this combination.  Before using, gently blow your nose to clear nasal passages.  Prime the pump as instructed on the package the first time you use it, or if the spray hasn't been used for several days.


**Side Effects:**

Common side effects can include:

* Nosebleeds
* Headache
* Burning or stinging sensation in the nose
* Nasal dryness
* Bad taste in the mouth
* Cough
* Sore throat

Less common, but more serious, side effects are possible, though rare.  These could include:  

* Severe allergic reactions (difficulty breathing, swelling of the face, lips, or tongue)
* Increased risk of infections in the nose and throat

If you experience any side effects, especially severe ones, contact your doctor immediately.


**How it Works:**

Fluticasone propionate is a corticosteroid that works by reducing inflammation in the nasal passages.  It decreases swelling and the production of substances that contribute to allergic symptoms.  Azelastine is an antihistamine that blocks the effects of histamine, a chemical released by the body during an allergic reaction. By combining these two active ingredients, the medication provides both anti-inflammatory and antihistaminic effects, offering broader and more effective symptom relief.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:**  This medication should not be used if you are allergic to fluticasone propionate, azelastine, or any of its ingredients.
* **Drug Interactions:**  Inform your doctor about all other medications you are taking, including over-the-counter drugs and herbal remedies, as some may interact with fluticasone propionate and azelastine.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  Consult your doctor before using this medication if you are pregnant, breastfeeding, or planning to become pregnant.
* **Children:** Use in children under 5 years old is generally not recommended.
* **Elderly:**  The elderly may be more susceptible to side effects.  Careful monitoring is important.

**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long does it take to work?** A:  You might start to feel relief within a few hours, but it usually takes a few days of regular use to achieve optimal symptom control.
* **Q: Can I use this medication if I have a cold?** A:  It's best to consult your doctor. While it may help with nasal congestion, the underlying cause of the cold is different from allergies.
* **Q: How should I store this medication?** A: Store it at room temperature away from direct sunlight and moisture.  Keep it out of reach of children.
* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it's almost time for the next dose. Don't double the dose to make up for a missed one.
