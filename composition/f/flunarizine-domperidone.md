# Flunarizine + Domperidone

**Generic Name: Flunarizine + Domperidone**

**Usage:**

Flunarizine + Domperidone is a combination medication primarily used to treat symptoms associated with **vertigo** (a sensation of spinning or dizziness) and **nausea** and **vomiting**, particularly when these symptoms occur together.  The flunarizine component helps manage vertigo by influencing calcium channels in the brain, reducing the excitability of certain nerve cells involved in balance control.  Domperidone, on the other hand, works by blocking dopamine receptors in the gut, which helps to relieve nausea and vomiting.  This combination is often prescribed for conditions like Ménière's disease, labyrinthitis, and vestibular neuronitis, where dizziness and nausea are prominent features. While not its primary indication, it might be used in some cases to help manage migraine-associated nausea and vomiting.  Always consult a doctor to determine if this medication is appropriate for your specific condition.

**Dosage:**

Dosage varies depending on the individual's condition and the doctor's prescription.  Generally, for adults, the typical dose is one tablet or capsule once or twice daily.  There's no established pediatric dosage for this specific combination; therefore, it is generally not prescribed for children.   The medication usually comes in tablet form.  It's crucial to follow the prescribed dosage and administration instructions carefully, as provided by the prescribing physician.  Never adjust the dosage on your own.

**Side Effects:**

Common side effects can include:

* Drowsiness
* Fatigue
* Weight gain
* Depression
* Restlessness
* Dry mouth
* Headache
* Constipation
* Galactorrhea (breast milk production in non-lactating women)
* Amenorrhea (absence of menstruation)
* Gynecomastia (breast enlargement in men)


Less common, but serious side effects require immediate medical attention and include:

* Severe allergic reactions (e.g., skin rash, swelling, difficulty breathing)
* Parkinsonism (tremor, rigidity, slow movement)
* Tardive dyskinesia (involuntary movements)
*  Extrapyramidal symptoms (involuntary muscle movements)

If you experience any concerning side effects, contact your healthcare provider immediately.

**How it Works:**

Flunarizine acts as a calcium channel blocker in the brain, specifically targeting the vestibular system, which is responsible for balance and spatial orientation. By reducing the influx of calcium ions into nerve cells, flunarizine stabilizes the electrical activity and decreases the symptoms of vertigo.  Domperidone acts as a dopamine antagonist in the gastrointestinal tract. By blocking dopamine receptors, domperidone prevents the stimulation of the chemoreceptor trigger zone (CTZ) in the brainstem, which is a crucial area involved in the vomiting reflex. This helps alleviate nausea and vomiting.  The combined effect provides relief from both the dizziness and gastrointestinal distress often associated with vestibular disorders.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** This medication is contraindicated in patients with known hypersensitivity to either flunarizine or domperidone.  It should be avoided in individuals with Parkinson's disease, prolactinomas (tumors of the pituitary gland that produce prolactin), or severe liver impairment.
* **Drug Interactions:** Flunarizine and domperidone can interact with other medications.  Always inform your doctor or pharmacist about all medications, vitamins, or herbal supplements you are taking.  This includes over-the-counter drugs.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:** The safety of this combination during pregnancy and breastfeeding hasn't been fully established.  Consult your doctor before using this medication if you are pregnant, planning to become pregnant, or breastfeeding.
* **Elderly Patients:** Elderly individuals may be more susceptible to side effects, particularly drowsiness and sedation.  Careful monitoring is necessary.
* **Driving and Operating Machinery:**  Drowsiness is a common side effect; therefore, avoid driving or operating machinery until you know how this medication affects you.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long does it take to work?** A:  The onset of effect varies, but relief from vertigo and nausea symptoms might begin within a few days of starting treatment.  However, the full therapeutic effect might take several weeks.

* **Q: Can I stop taking it suddenly?** A: No, it's crucial to gradually reduce the dosage under medical supervision to avoid withdrawal symptoms.  Never stop taking the medication abruptly without consulting your doctor.

* **Q: How should I store it?** A: Store in a cool, dry place, away from direct sunlight and moisture, as directed on the product label.  Keep out of reach of children.

* **Q:  Is it habit-forming?** A: No, Flunarizine + Domperidone is not considered habit-forming.

* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it is almost time for the next dose.  Do not double the dose.
