# Furazolidone

**Generic Name: Furazolidone**

**Usage:**

Furazolidone is an antibiotic primarily used to treat certain types of bacterial infections, particularly those affecting the gastrointestinal tract. Its main use is in combating infections caused by bacteria susceptible to its action, often including *Escherichia coli*, *Shigella*, and *Salmonella*.  It’s sometimes prescribed for specific diarrheal illnesses, particularly those believed to be bacterial in origin.  However, it's important to note that the increasing prevalence of antibiotic resistance means its use is becoming more restricted.  It's not a first-line treatment for most bacterial infections and is often used only when other antibiotics haven't been effective or are unsuitable.  Some older medical literature may mention its use for other conditions, but contemporary practice significantly limits its applications due to efficacy and safety concerns.

**Dosage:**

Dosage varies greatly depending on the specific infection, the patient's age and weight, and the severity of the illness.  A healthcare professional will determine the appropriate dose.  Generally, information available suggests that for adults, the typical dose is 100mg every four to six hours. Pediatric dosing is considerably more complex and should only be determined by a doctor, as it’s often weight-based. Furazolidone is typically available as oral tablets or liquid suspensions (syrups). Intravenous administration is not typically done.  The medication should be taken as directed and the full course of treatment should be completed, even if symptoms improve before the prescribed time, to prevent recurrence and the development of antibiotic resistance.

**Side Effects:**

*   Nausea and vomiting
*   Diarrhea
*   Headache
*   Dizziness
*   Loss of appetite
*   Allergic reactions (rash, itching, swelling)
*   Peripheral neuropathy (numbness or tingling in extremities - a rare but serious side effect)
*   Hemolytic anemia (in individuals with glucose-6-phosphate dehydrogenase (G6PD) deficiency - a serious side effect)


**Note:** This is not an exhaustive list.  If you experience any unusual or concerning side effects, immediately contact a doctor or other healthcare provider.


**How it Works:**

Furazolidone is a nitrofuran antibiotic. It works by interfering with the growth and reproduction of susceptible bacteria.  It achieves this by inhibiting bacterial enzymes necessary for their metabolism and survival. This disruption in the bacterial cell's function ultimately leads to its death or inability to multiply, allowing the body's immune system to effectively clear the infection.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:**  Furazolidone is contraindicated in individuals with known allergies to nitrofuran antibiotics. It should be avoided in patients with G6PD deficiency due to the risk of hemolytic anemia.
* **Drug Interactions:**  Furazolidone can interact with several medications, including MAO inhibitors (monoamine oxidase inhibitors).  This interaction can lead to a dangerous increase in blood pressure (hypertensive crisis). It’s crucial to inform your doctor of all medications you are taking, including over-the-counter drugs and supplements.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  The safety of furazolidone during pregnancy and breastfeeding has not been fully established. Use should only be considered after careful evaluation of the risks and benefits by a healthcare professional.  It’s generally avoided if possible during these periods.
* **Older Adults and Children:**  Older adults and children may be more susceptible to side effects.  Dosage adjustments may be necessary and close monitoring is crucial.

**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long does it take for furazolidone to work?**  A: Improvement in symptoms may be seen within a few days, but the full course of treatment should be completed as prescribed.
* **Q: Can I drink alcohol while taking furazolidone?** A:  Alcohol can exacerbate some side effects. It's best to avoid alcohol while on this medication.
* **Q: How should I store furazolidone?** A: Store at room temperature, away from moisture and direct sunlight. Keep out of reach of children.
* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it’s close to the time for your next dose. Do not double the dose.
* **Q: Is furazolidone effective against all types of diarrhea?** A: No.  It is primarily effective against bacterial diarrhea and not viral or parasitic causes. A doctor needs to determine the cause of the diarrhea before prescribing medication.
