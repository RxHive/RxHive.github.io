# Flurbiprofen + Hydroxypropylmethylcellulose + Phenyl Mercuric Nitrate

**Generic Name:** Flurbiprofen + Hydroxypropylmethylcellulose + Phenyl Mercuric Nitrate

**Usage:**

This combination of medications is primarily used as an ophthalmic solution, meaning it's applied directly to the eyes.  Its main purpose is to treat the pain and inflammation associated with various eye conditions.  The flurbiprofen component acts as a nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drug (NSAID), effectively reducing inflammation and pain. Hydroxypropylmethylcellulose serves as a viscosity-increasing agent, helping the medication stay in contact with the eye for a longer duration, improving its effectiveness. Phenyl mercuric nitrate acts as a preservative, preventing microbial contamination of the solution.  While primarily used for post-operative inflammation and pain following eye surgery (e.g., cataract surgery), it might also find limited use in managing other inflammatory eye conditions under a doctor's strict supervision. It's crucial to remember that this is not a first-line treatment for most eye conditions and should only be used as directed by an ophthalmologist.

**Dosage:**

Dosage instructions are highly specific to the individual patient and the particular formulation.  A doctor will prescribe the appropriate dosage and frequency based on the patient's condition, age, and overall health.  There is no standard dosage that can be provided here. Typically, the medication is administered as eye drops, with a prescribed number of drops instilled into the affected eye(s) at specific intervals.  Children, pregnant women, and older adults may require adjusted dosages or alternative treatments.  Always follow the prescribing ophthalmologist's instructions precisely.

**Side Effects:**

* **Common:** Burning or stinging sensation upon application, temporary blurred vision, slight eye irritation.
* **Less Common but Serious:** Allergic reactions (ranging from mild skin rash to severe anaphylaxis), increased eye pressure, corneal damage (rare).


**If you experience any unusual or persistent side effects, immediately consult your doctor or ophthalmologist.**


**How it Works:**

Flurbiprofen, the NSAID component, inhibits the production of prostaglandins, substances in the body that contribute to inflammation and pain.  By reducing prostaglandin production, it alleviates the pain and inflammation in the eye. Hydroxypropylmethylcellulose enhances the drug's residence time in the eye, prolonging its effect. Phenyl mercuric nitrate, a preservative, prevents bacterial or fungal growth in the solution, maintaining its sterility.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:**  This medication should not be used by individuals with known allergies to any of its components (flurbiprofen, hydroxypropylmethylcellulose, or phenyl mercuric nitrate).  Those with certain eye conditions might also be advised against its use.
* **Drug Interactions:**  Inform your doctor about all other medications you are taking, including over-the-counter drugs, herbal remedies, and supplements.  Some medications might interact with flurbiprofen.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:** Use during pregnancy and breastfeeding should only be considered after careful assessment of risk-benefit by a doctor.
* **Children and Older Adults:** Dosage adjustments might be necessary for these groups.
* **Contact Lens Wearers:**  Check with your doctor regarding the safe use of this medication with contact lenses.
