# Flutamide

**Generic Name: Flutamide**

**Usage:**

Flutamide is primarily used in the treatment of prostate cancer.  Specifically, it's an anti-androgen medication. This means it works by blocking the effects of androgens, male hormones like testosterone, which can fuel the growth of prostate cancer cells.  While its primary use is in advanced prostate cancer, it's sometimes used in combination with other therapies, such as luteinizing hormone-releasing hormone (LHRH) agonists, to manage the disease.  In some cases, it might be used to treat other androgen-dependent conditions, but this is less common and should only be done under the strict supervision of a medical professional.


**Dosage:**

Dosage varies greatly depending on the individual's condition, overall health, and response to treatment.  A doctor will determine the appropriate dosage and schedule.  Generally, flutamide is administered orally in tablet form.  There is no pediatric formulation, and its use in children is not established.  Common adult dosages range from 250 mg to 500 mg taken two to three times daily.   However, it is crucial to strictly adhere to the prescribed dosage and not alter it without consulting with a doctor.  Adjustments might be necessary based on individual responses and potential side effects.


**Side Effects:**

Common side effects can include:

* Gynecomastia (breast enlargement in men)
* Hot flashes
* Decreased libido
* Impotence (erectile dysfunction)
* Nausea
* Vomiting
* Diarrhea
* Liver problems (rare but serious)
* Fatigue

Less common, but serious side effects may include allergic reactions (rash, hives, difficulty breathing),  jaundice (yellowing of skin and eyes), and changes in liver function tests.  If any concerning side effects occur, it's crucial to seek immediate medical attention.


**How it Works:**

Flutamide works by competitively binding to androgen receptors in cells.  These receptors are typically activated by androgens like testosterone, stimulating cell growth. By occupying these receptors, flutamide prevents testosterone from binding and thus inhibits the growth and spread of androgen-dependent prostate cancer cells.  This mechanism effectively reduces the influence of androgens on the cancerous tissue.

**Precautions:**

* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:** Flutamide is contraindicated in women who are pregnant or breastfeeding.
* **Liver Disease:** Individuals with pre-existing liver problems should use flutamide with extreme caution, as it can potentially worsen liver function. Regular monitoring of liver enzymes is often necessary.
* **Drug Interactions:** Flutamide can interact with other medications. It's crucial to inform your doctor of all medications, supplements, and herbal remedies you are taking.
* **Older Adults:** Older adults might be more susceptible to side effects. Dosage adjustments might be necessary.
* **Driving and Operating Machinery:** Flutamide may cause drowsiness or dizziness in some individuals; caution is advised when driving or operating machinery.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long does it take to see results?** A:  The effectiveness of flutamide varies.  Significant improvement may not be noticeable immediately and requires ongoing monitoring by a healthcare professional.
* **Q: Can I stop taking Flutamide if I feel better?** A: No, abruptly stopping Flutamide without consulting a doctor can be detrimental.  Treatment should follow the prescribed schedule.
* **Q: How should I store Flutamide?** A: Store Flutamide at room temperature, away from moisture and direct sunlight. Keep it out of reach of children.
* **Q: Is Flutamide addictive?** A:  Flutamide is not considered an addictive substance.
* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it is almost time for the next dose.  Do not double the dose.
