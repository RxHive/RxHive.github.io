# Flunarizine

**Generic Name: Flunarizine**

**Usage:**

Flunarizine is primarily used to prevent migraine headaches.  It's particularly helpful for those experiencing frequent or severe migraines, offering a preventative measure rather than immediate relief during an attack.  While its main use is migraine prophylaxis, flunarizine also shows some efficacy in managing certain types of vertigo (dizziness) associated with inner ear problems, specifically Ménière's disease.  It's important to note that flunarizine isn't a treatment for the acute symptoms of either migraines or vertigo; it aims to reduce the frequency and intensity of these conditions over time.


**Dosage:**

Dosage varies depending on the specific condition being treated and the individual's response.  A doctor will determine the appropriate dosage. Typically, for adults, it starts with a low dose and gradually increases.  There is no established pediatric dose as its use in children is limited due to potential side effects.  Flunarizine is generally available as a tablet for oral administration.  Precise instructions on dosage and frequency will always be provided by a prescribing physician.  Self-adjusting the dosage is strongly discouraged.


**Side Effects:**

Common side effects can include:

*   Drowsiness or fatigue
*   Weight gain
*   Depression
*   Dry mouth
*   Constipation
*   Restlessness or nervousness

Less common, but potentially serious side effects include:

*   Extrapyramidal symptoms (movement disorders like tremors or rigidity)
*   Jaundice (yellowing of the skin and eyes)
*   Severe allergic reactions

If any concerning side effects occur, immediately consult a healthcare professional.


**How it Works:**

Flunarizine belongs to a class of drugs called calcium channel blockers.  However, unlike many calcium channel blockers that primarily target the heart, flunarizine's main action is on the brain. It works by blocking calcium channels in the brain's blood vessels, leading to vasodilation (widening of blood vessels). This improved blood flow is thought to help prevent migraine attacks and reduce the symptoms of vertigo by stabilizing blood flow in the inner ear.

**Precautions:**

*   **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  Flunarizine's use during pregnancy and breastfeeding should be carefully considered and only used if the potential benefits outweigh the risks. Consult a doctor.
*   **Elderly Patients:**  Older adults may be more sensitive to flunarizine's side effects, particularly drowsiness and sedation. Careful dose adjustment is often necessary.
*   **Liver and Kidney Disease:**  Patients with liver or kidney impairment may require dose adjustments or closer monitoring.
*   **Drug Interactions:** Flunarizine can interact with other medications, particularly those affecting the central nervous system.  It's crucial to inform your doctor about all medications you are taking.
*   **Driving and Operating Machinery:**  Due to the risk of drowsiness, avoid driving or operating machinery until you know how flunarizine affects you.


**FAQs:**

*   **Q: How long does it take to work?**  A:  Flunarizine's preventative effects on migraines usually aren't seen immediately. It often takes several weeks of regular use before a noticeable reduction in migraine frequency or severity is observed.
*   **Q: Can I stop taking it suddenly?** A: No, abruptly stopping flunarizine can cause withdrawal symptoms.  Always consult your doctor before stopping the medication.
*   **Q: How should I store it?** A: Store flunarizine tablets in a cool, dry place, away from direct sunlight and moisture, as directed on the packaging.
*   **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: If you miss a dose, take it as soon as you remember, unless it's almost time for your next dose. Don't double the dose to catch up.
