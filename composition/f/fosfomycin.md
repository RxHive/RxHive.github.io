# Fosfomycin

**Generic Name: Fosfomycin**

**Usage:**

Fosfomycin is an antibiotic used primarily to treat urinary tract infections (UTIs).  This includes both uncomplicated UTIs (cystitis, affecting the bladder) and more complicated infections that may involve the kidneys (pyelonephritis).  It's particularly effective against common UTI-causing bacteria like *Escherichia coli* and *Enterococcus faecalis*.  While its primary use is in UTIs, fosfomycin may sometimes be used to treat other bacterial infections, depending on the specific bacteria involved and the doctor's assessment.  However, it's not a first-line treatment for most infections outside the urinary tract. Its use in these cases is often guided by factors like antibiotic resistance patterns in a specific region.

**Dosage:**

Dosage varies greatly depending on the type of infection, the patient's age and weight, and the specific formulation of fosfomycin.  There's no single "standard" dosage.  A doctor always determines the appropriate dose.  

Commonly, for adults with UTIs, a single dose of 3 grams of oral fosfomycin may be prescribed.  However, some treatment regimens might involve a longer course or a different dosage. Pediatric use requires careful consideration and adjustment based on the child's weight and age, and this is always determined by a healthcare professional.  Fosfomycin comes in oral forms (tablets, granules for suspension) and occasionally as intravenous injections, though the latter is less common for UTIs.  Specific administration instructions will always be provided by a doctor or pharmacist.


**Side Effects:**

Common side effects can include:

* Nausea
* Vomiting
* Diarrhea
* Headache
* Vaginal yeast infection (candidiasis)


Less common but potentially more serious side effects may include allergic reactions (rash, itching, swelling, difficulty breathing).  If any adverse reaction occurs, stop taking the medication and seek immediate medical advice.


**How it Works:**

Fosfomycin works by inhibiting bacterial cell wall synthesis.  Essentially, it interferes with a crucial step in how bacteria build their protective outer layers. This disruption prevents bacterial growth and ultimately kills the bacteria.  Its unique mechanism of action makes it effective even against some bacteria that have developed resistance to other antibiotics.

**Precautions:**

* **Allergy:**  Individuals with known allergies to fosfomycin should avoid using it.
* **Kidney disease:** Patients with impaired kidney function may require dose adjustments or alternative treatment.  
* **Pregnancy and breastfeeding:**  Use during pregnancy and breastfeeding should only be considered when the benefits outweigh the potential risks; a doctor should be consulted.
* **Drug interactions:** Fosfomycin may interact with certain medications. Always inform your doctor about all other medications, supplements, or herbal remedies you are taking.
* **Older adults and children:** Dosage adjustments may be necessary for these populations.
* **Severe dehydration:** This condition may affect the efficacy of the medication.

**FAQs:**

* **Q: Can I stop taking fosfomycin if I feel better?** A: No, complete the prescribed course even if symptoms improve to prevent recurrence of infection and antibiotic resistance.
* **Q: How should I store fosfomycin?** A: Follow the storage instructions on the medication label.  Generally, it's best kept at room temperature, away from moisture and direct sunlight.
* **Q: Is fosfomycin safe for long-term use?** A: It’s not intended for long-term use.  Its primary application is short-term treatment of acute infections.
* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Consult your doctor or pharmacist for advice. Do not double the dose.
* **Q: Can I take fosfomycin with alcohol?** A:  It’s best to avoid alcohol while on antibiotics unless explicitly advised otherwise by your doctor.
