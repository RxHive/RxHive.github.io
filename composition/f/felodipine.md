# Felodipine

**Generic Name: Felodipine**

**Usage:**

Felodipine is a medication primarily used to treat hypertension (high blood pressure).  Its main role is to help lower blood pressure, reducing the strain on the heart and blood vessels.  This, in turn, helps prevent serious complications like heart attack, stroke, and kidney disease.  While its primary use is in hypertension, felodipine may also be used, sometimes off-label, to help manage angina (chest pain) by relaxing the blood vessels supplying the heart muscle, improving blood flow.  It's important to note that off-label use should only be undertaken under the strict supervision of a doctor.


**Dosage:**

Dosage for felodipine is highly individualized and determined by a healthcare professional based on several factors, including the patient's age, weight, overall health, and the severity of their condition.  Generally, felodipine is available in tablet form for oral administration.  There is no standard pediatric dosage, and use in children is usually not recommended.  A typical adult starting dose might be 5mg once daily, but this can be adjusted upward or downward depending on individual response and blood pressure readings.  The doctor will closely monitor the patient's blood pressure and adjust the dose as necessary.  It's crucial to follow the prescribed dosage and regimen exactly as directed by your doctor. Never alter the dosage without consulting your physician.


**Side Effects:**

Common side effects of felodipine can include:

* Headache
* Flushing
* Dizziness
* Swelling in ankles or feet (edema)
* Fatigue
* Nausea
* Palpitations

Less common but more serious side effects include:

* Severe allergic reactions (anaphylaxis)
* Irregular heartbeat
* Chest pain
* Swelling of the face, lips, tongue, or throat
* Severe dizziness or fainting

If you experience any serious side effects, seek immediate medical attention.  Even seemingly minor side effects should be reported to your doctor to ensure the appropriate management of your treatment.


**How it Works:**

Felodipine is a calcium channel blocker.  Calcium plays a vital role in the contraction of heart muscle and blood vessel walls.  By blocking the influx of calcium into these cells, felodipine causes relaxation of blood vessels, leading to vasodilation (widening of blood vessels). This vasodilation lowers blood pressure and reduces the workload on the heart, thereby relieving symptoms of hypertension and angina.

**Precautions:**

* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  The use of felodipine during pregnancy and breastfeeding should be carefully considered and only undertaken under strict medical supervision, as potential risks to the fetus or infant may exist.
* **Liver and Kidney Disease:**  Individuals with impaired liver or kidney function may require dosage adjustments due to altered drug metabolism and excretion.
* **Grapefruit Juice Interaction:** Grapefruit juice can significantly interact with felodipine, leading to increased blood levels of the drug and potentially dangerous side effects.  Avoid grapefruit juice while taking felodipine.
* **Other Medications:** Felodipine can interact with certain other medications, including other blood pressure medications, and certain heart medications.  Always inform your doctor of all medications you are taking, including over-the-counter drugs and herbal supplements.
* **Elderly Patients:**  Older adults may be more sensitive to the side effects of felodipine, requiring careful dose monitoring and adjustment.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: Can I stop taking felodipine suddenly?** A: No.  Suddenly stopping felodipine can lead to a rebound increase in blood pressure, potentially causing serious health problems.  Always consult your doctor before stopping or changing your dosage.
* **Q: How should I store felodipine?** A: Store felodipine at room temperature, away from moisture and direct sunlight. Keep it out of reach of children.
* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it is almost time for your next dose. Do not take two doses at once.
* **Q: Is felodipine habit-forming?** A: No, felodipine is not habit-forming.
