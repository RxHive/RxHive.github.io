# Finasteride

**Generic Name: Finasteride**

**Usage:**

Finasteride is primarily used to treat benign prostatic hyperplasia (BPH), also known as an enlarged prostate.  BPH is a common condition in older men, causing urinary problems like frequent urination, weak urine stream, and difficulty starting urination.  Finasteride works by reducing the size of the prostate gland, thus alleviating these symptoms.

Beyond BPH, finasteride is also approved for the treatment of male pattern baldness (androgenetic alopecia).  In this case, it works by inhibiting the conversion of testosterone to dihydrotestosterone (DHT), a hormone that plays a significant role in hair loss.  It's important to note that finasteride only slows or prevents further hair loss; it doesn't necessarily regrow already lost hair.


**Dosage:**

Dosage varies depending on the condition being treated:

* **BPH:** The typical adult dose is 5 mg once daily.  Dosage adjustments are generally not necessary based on age or weight, but a doctor should always be consulted.  It's available as an oral tablet.
* **Male Pattern Baldness:**  The usual adult dose is 1 mg once daily.  Again, dosage adjustments are typically unnecessary based on age or weight, and it comes as an oral tablet.

Finasteride is not generally recommended for children, and there's no pediatric formulation.  The information above pertains to adult use only.


**Side Effects:**

Common side effects can include:

* Decreased libido (sex drive)
* Erectile dysfunction
* Ejaculation disorders (e.g., decreased volume of ejaculate)
* Breast tenderness or enlargement (gynecomastia)
* Dizziness
* Headache

Less common, but serious side effects can include allergic reactions.  If any significant side effects occur, it's crucial to seek medical attention immediately.  Prolonged use may also lead to a slight increase in the risk of certain cancers, though this risk is generally considered low.


**How it Works:**

Finasteride is a 5α-reductase inhibitor.  This means it blocks the enzyme 5α-reductase, which converts testosterone into dihydrotestosterone (DHT).  DHT is a potent androgen that plays a crucial role in the growth of the prostate gland and in the development of male pattern baldness. By reducing DHT levels, finasteride shrinks the prostate in men with BPH and helps to slow hair loss in men with androgenetic alopecia.

**Precautions:**

* **Pregnancy:** Finasteride is contraindicated in women who are or may become pregnant.  It can cause birth defects in male fetuses.  Women should avoid handling crushed or broken tablets.
* **Children:**  Finasteride is not typically recommended for children.
* **Liver/Kidney Disease:** Individuals with severe liver or kidney disease should use caution and may require dosage adjustments under medical supervision.
* **Drug Interactions:**  Finasteride may interact with other medications.  Always inform your doctor of all medications and supplements you are taking.
* **Allergic Reactions:**  If an allergic reaction occurs (e.g., rash, swelling, difficulty breathing), stop taking finasteride and seek immediate medical help.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long does it take to see results?** A:  For BPH, symptom improvement may take several months. For hair loss, results may be visible after 3-6 months of consistent use.
* **Q: Can I stop taking finasteride anytime?** A: It's best to discuss stopping finasteride with your doctor. Stopping suddenly may lead to a return of symptoms.
* **Q: How should I store finasteride?** A: Store finasteride at room temperature, away from moisture and heat.
* **Q: Is finasteride addictive?** A:  No, finasteride is not considered addictive.
* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it's almost time for the next dose.  Do not double the dose.
