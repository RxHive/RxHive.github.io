# Fluticasone Furoate + Azelastine

**Generic Name: Fluticasone Furoate + Azelastine**

**Usage:**

Fluticasone furoate + azelastine is a combination nasal spray used primarily to treat seasonal allergic rhinitis (hay fever) in adults and children aged 12 years and older.  Seasonal allergic rhinitis is characterized by symptoms like sneezing, runny nose, itchy nose and eyes, and nasal congestion, caused by an allergic reaction to pollen or other airborne allergens.  While its primary use is for seasonal allergies, it may also provide relief from symptoms of perennial allergic rhinitis (allergies present year-round, often triggered by dust mites or pet dander).  It's important to note that this medication treats symptoms; it doesn't cure the underlying allergy.

**Dosage:**

Fluticasone furoate + azelastine is typically administered as a nasal spray.  The usual dosage for adults and children 12 years and older is one spray in each nostril once daily.  Dosage adjustments based on age, weight, or severity are generally not necessary, but always follow the prescription instructions provided by your doctor.  This medication is not available in tablet, syrup, or injection form.  Proper technique is crucial for effective delivery; the patient should gently blow their nose before administering the spray and follow the instructions provided with the specific product.


**Side Effects:**

Common side effects may include:

*   Nasal dryness
*   Nosebleeds
*   Headache
*   Sore throat
*   Bad taste in the mouth

Less common but more serious side effects are rare but should be reported to a doctor immediately.  These could include:

*   Increased risk of infection (especially fungal infections in the nose)
*   Severe allergic reactions (anaphylaxis)

If you experience any concerning side effects, contact your healthcare provider immediately.


**How it Works:**

This medication is a combination of two active ingredients that work in different ways to alleviate allergy symptoms:

*   **Fluticasone furoate:** This is a corticosteroid. It reduces inflammation in the nasal passages by suppressing the release of inflammatory substances.  This helps to reduce swelling, congestion, and itching.
*   **Azelastine:** This is an antihistamine. It blocks the action of histamine, a chemical released by the body during an allergic reaction.  Histamine is largely responsible for the symptoms of sneezing, itching, and runny nose.

By working together, these two ingredients provide comprehensive relief from the symptoms of allergic rhinitis.

**Precautions:**

*   **Contraindications:**  This medication should not be used by individuals with known hypersensitivity (allergy) to fluticasone furoate, azelastine, or any of the inactive ingredients.
*   **Drug Interactions:**  Inform your doctor about all medications you are currently taking, including over-the-counter drugs and herbal supplements.  Some medications may interact with fluticasone furoate + azelastine.
*   **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  Consult your doctor before using this medication if you are pregnant, breastfeeding, or planning to become pregnant.
*   **Children:**  This medication is generally not recommended for children under 12 years of age.
*   **Older Adults:**  Older adults may be more susceptible to side effects.  Close monitoring may be necessary.
*   **Other Precautions:** Avoid spraying the medication in your eyes. If you develop a nasal infection, contact your doctor.


**FAQs:**

*   **Q: How long does it take to work?** A:  Relief from symptoms typically begins within a few days of starting treatment, but full benefit may take a week or more.
*   **Q: Can I use this medication if I have a cold?** A:  It's best to consult your doctor. While it can help manage allergy symptoms, it might not be the best choice for a cold.
*   **Q: How should I store this medication?** A: Store at room temperature, away from moisture and direct sunlight.  Keep out of reach of children.
*   **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A:  Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it is almost time for the next dose.  Do not double the dose.
*   **Q: Can I use this medication long-term?** A:  Long-term use should be discussed with your doctor.  The decision will depend on the severity and persistence of your allergies.
