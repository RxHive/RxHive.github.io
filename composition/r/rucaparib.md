# Rucaparib

**Generic Name: Rucaparib**

**Usage:**

Rucaparib is a type of cancer medicine called a PARP inhibitor.  Its primary use is in treating certain types of ovarian cancer.  Specifically, it's approved for women with recurrent ovarian cancer who have a specific genetic mutation known as a BRCA mutation (or other homologous recombination deficiency, HRD). This means the cancer cells have a defect in a gene that normally repairs damaged DNA.  Rucaparib exploits this defect, making it harder for cancer cells to grow and spread.  

While ovarian cancer is its primary application, research is ongoing to explore its potential use in other cancers with similar genetic characteristics, such as certain types of breast and prostate cancers. However, these are still under investigation and not currently standard treatment options.  It's crucial to discuss any potential use beyond ovarian cancer with your healthcare provider.


**Dosage:**

Dosage of rucaparib varies depending on the individual and the specific type of cancer being treated. It's typically administered orally as a tablet. Your doctor will determine the appropriate dosage based on factors like your overall health, the stage and severity of your cancer, and any other medications you're taking.  There are no established dosages for children; rucaparib is primarily used in adult patients. Detailed instructions on how and when to take the medication will be provided by your prescribing physician or pharmacist.  It is crucial to follow these instructions precisely.


**Side Effects:**

Rucaparib, like many cancer medications, can cause side effects.  Common side effects often include:

*   Nausea
*   Vomiting
*   Fatigue
*   Diarrhea
*   Loss of appetite
*   Headache
*   Anemia (low red blood cell count)
*   Low white blood cell count (neutropenia)
*   Thrombocytopenia (low platelet count)


Less common, but serious side effects may include:

*   Severe allergic reactions
*   Kidney problems
*   Liver problems
*   Myelosuppression (suppression of bone marrow function)
*   Increased risk of bleeding


If you experience any concerning side effects, particularly those listed above, contact your doctor immediately. Early intervention can help manage these complications.


**How it Works:**

Rucaparib works by targeting a protein called PARP (poly ADP-ribose polymerase).  PARP is involved in repairing damaged DNA in cells.  Cancer cells with BRCA mutations or other HRD defects already have trouble repairing their DNA.  Rucaparib further inhibits PARP's ability to repair this DNA damage, leading to cell death in cancer cells while sparing healthy cells to a greater extent. This targeted approach is what makes it a more selective cancer treatment compared to traditional chemotherapy which damages both cancerous and healthy cells.


**Precautions:**

*   **Contraindications:** Rucaparib is contraindicated in individuals with known hypersensitivity (allergy) to the drug or its components.
*   **Drug Interactions:** Rucaparib can interact with other medications.  It's essential to inform your doctor about all medications, supplements, and herbal remedies you are taking.
*   **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:** Rucaparib is not recommended for use during pregnancy or breastfeeding due to the potential risk to the developing fetus or infant.  Effective contraception is necessary during treatment.
*   **Elderly Patients:** Older adults may be more sensitive to the side effects of rucaparib, requiring careful monitoring.
*   **Liver and Kidney Impairment:**  Individuals with impaired liver or kidney function may require dose adjustments.


**FAQs:**

*   **Q: How long do I need to take rucaparib?** A: The duration of treatment depends on your individual response to the medication and the overall treatment plan determined by your oncologist.
*   **Q: How should I store rucaparib?** A: Store rucaparib tablets as directed on the label, typically at room temperature, away from moisture and heat.
*   **Q: Can I drink alcohol while taking rucaparib?** A:  It's best to discuss alcohol consumption with your doctor, as it can potentially interact with the medication or worsen side effects.
*   **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Follow your doctor's or pharmacist’s instructions on what to do if you miss a dose. Generally, don't take a double dose to make up for a missed one.
*   **Q: Is rucaparib a cure for cancer?** A: Rucaparib is a cancer treatment aimed at slowing or stopping the growth of cancer cells, but it is not a cure for all cancers.  The effectiveness varies depending on the individual and the type of cancer.
