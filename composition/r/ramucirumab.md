# Ramucirumab

**Generic Name:** Ramucirumab

**Usage:**

Ramucirumab is a targeted cancer therapy, a type of medicine that specifically targets cancer cells while minimizing harm to healthy cells.  Its primary use is in treating advanced or metastatic cancers where it's shown efficacy.  Specifically, it's approved for use in:

* **Advanced Gastric Cancer:** Ramucirumab is often used in combination with other chemotherapy drugs to treat advanced gastric cancer (cancer of the stomach) that has progressed after initial treatment.  It's particularly effective in patients whose cancer expresses high levels of a protein called VEGF.

* **Advanced Non-Small Cell Lung Cancer (NSCLC):** Ramucirumab is also approved for use in patients with advanced NSCLC whose disease has progressed after treatment with platinum-based chemotherapy.  Again, its effectiveness often relates to VEGF expression levels.

* **Hepatocellular Carcinoma (HCC):**  Ramucirumab is utilized for treating advanced hepatocellular carcinoma (liver cancer) that has progressed following treatment with sorafenib.


The exact use of ramucirumab will depend on the specific type and stage of cancer, as well as the patient's overall health.  A healthcare professional will determine the most appropriate treatment plan.

**Dosage:**

Ramucirumab is administered intravenously (IV) – meaning it’s injected into a vein.  Dosage is determined by a physician based on several factors, including the patient's body weight, overall health, and the specific cancer being treated.  There is no standard dosage applicable across all situations.  Typical dosage ranges are established per treatment protocol, and adjustments may be necessary based on individual response and tolerance. The medication is not available in tablet or syrup form. It’s crucial to follow the physician's instructions meticulously regarding dosage and administration schedule.  The drug is not currently approved for pediatric use.

**Side Effects:**

Ramucirumab, like many cancer treatments, can cause side effects.  These vary in severity and frequency among individuals.  Common side effects include:

* Fatigue
* High blood pressure (hypertension)
* Bleeding (nosebleeds, gastrointestinal bleeding)
* Hand-foot syndrome (pain, swelling, redness of hands and feet)
* Low platelet count (thrombocytopenia)
* Anemia (low red blood cell count)
* Nausea
* Vomiting
* Diarrhea
* Abdominal pain

Less common, but more serious side effects may include:  blood clots, heart failure, and protein in the urine.  

**It is crucial to report any new or worsening symptoms to a healthcare provider immediately.**

**How it Works:**

Ramucirumab is a monoclonal antibody.  This means it's a laboratory-made protein designed to target a specific substance in the body. In this case, it targets vascular endothelial growth factor (VEGF).  VEGF is a protein that plays a crucial role in the formation of new blood vessels, a process essential for tumor growth. By binding to VEGF, ramucirumab blocks its activity, hindering the formation of new blood vessels that supply tumors with oxygen and nutrients. This ultimately slows or stops tumor growth.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** Ramucirumab should not be used in individuals with a known hypersensitivity (allergic reaction) to the drug.

* **Drug Interactions:**  Ramucirumab may interact with other medications. It's essential to inform your doctor of all medications, supplements, and herbal remedies you are taking.

* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:** Ramucirumab should not be used during pregnancy unless the potential benefit clearly outweighs the potential risk to the fetus.  Women should avoid becoming pregnant during treatment and should use effective contraception.  Its use during breastfeeding is not recommended.

* **Older Adults and Children:**  As mentioned, ramucirumab is not approved for use in children.  Older adults may be at increased risk for some side effects, requiring careful monitoring.

**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long will I need to take ramucirumab?**  A: The duration of treatment depends on your individual response and is determined by your oncologist.

* **Q: How is ramucirumab stored?** A: Store ramucirumab as directed by your pharmacist or healthcare provider.  Specific storage conditions may vary.

* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Contact your doctor immediately if you miss a dose to discuss the appropriate next steps. Do not double the dose without instruction.

* **Q:  Are there long-term effects from ramucirumab?** A: While long-term effects are possible, this varies greatly by individual and requires close monitoring by healthcare professionals.  Discuss any long-term concerns with your oncologist.
