# Recombinant Human parathyroid hormone

**Generic Name:** Recombinant Human Parathyroid Hormone (rhPTH)

**Usage:**

Recombinant human parathyroid hormone (rhPTH), most commonly known by brand names like Forteo, is primarily used to treat osteoporosis in adults who are at high risk of fracture.  This includes individuals with postmenopausal osteoporosis who haven't responded well to other treatments, and those with osteoporosis caused by long-term use of corticosteroids (like prednisone).  

Its primary mechanism focuses on stimulating bone formation, making bones stronger and less prone to fractures.  While primarily used for osteoporosis, it might also be considered in cases of hypoparathyroidism (a rare condition where the parathyroid glands don't produce enough parathyroid hormone), though this is less common.  The use in hypoparathyroidism requires careful monitoring and is often considered only when other treatments fail.  It's crucial to understand that rhPTH is not a first-line treatment for osteoporosis; other medications are typically tried first.

**Dosage:**

rhPTH is administered via daily subcutaneous injection (under the skin).  Dosage is determined by a doctor and is individualized based on the patient's specific condition and response to treatment.  A common starting dose for adults with osteoporosis is 20 mcg daily.  There isn't a standardized pediatric dosage as its use in children is very limited and usually confined to specific clinical trials or exceptional circumstances under close medical supervision.  The medication should be injected at roughly the same time each day.


**Side Effects:**

* **Common:** Nausea, dizziness, headache, muscle pain, and pain at the injection site.  These are usually mild and temporary.
* **Less Common but Serious:**  Hypercalcemia (high levels of calcium in the blood), which can cause symptoms like nausea, vomiting, constipation, and weakness.  Rarely, severe hypercalcemia can lead to kidney problems.  Bone pain, although sometimes experienced, should be discussed with a physician as it might indicate a problem.
* **Note:**  Any new or worsening symptoms should be reported to a doctor immediately.


**How it Works:**

Parathyroid hormone (PTH) is a naturally occurring hormone that regulates calcium and phosphate levels in the body and plays a critical role in bone metabolism.  rhPTH mimics the action of natural PTH, binding to receptors on bone cells. This stimulates bone formation (the creation of new bone tissue) by increasing the activity of bone-forming cells (osteoblasts).  The increased bone formation, over time, leads to increased bone density and strength, reducing the risk of fractures.  Importantly, it's a cyclical process: prolonged exposure to PTH can have a catabolic effect (breaking down bone).  The daily injection regime is designed to optimize the anabolic (bone-building) effects while limiting the catabolic ones.


**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** rhPTH should not be used in patients with certain types of bone cancer (such as Paget's disease of bone) or those with high levels of calcium in their blood (hypercalcemia).
* **Drug Interactions:**  Some medications can interact with rhPTH, so it's essential to inform your doctor of all medications, supplements, and herbal remedies being taken.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  The use of rhPTH during pregnancy and breastfeeding should be discussed with a doctor as the potential risks and benefits must be carefully weighed.
* **Older Adults:**  Older adults may be more susceptible to side effects, particularly hypercalcemia. Close monitoring is necessary.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long will I need to take rhPTH?** A:  The duration of treatment varies depending on individual response and the doctor's assessment.  It’s usually a long-term treatment for osteoporosis.

* **Q: Can I stop taking rhPTH suddenly?** A: No, it's crucial to follow your doctor's instructions regarding stopping treatment.  Stopping abruptly could negatively impact bone health.

* **Q: What if I miss a dose?** A:  If you miss a dose, take it as soon as you remember, unless it is almost time for your next dose. Do not double the dose.

* **Q: How should I store rhPTH?** A:  Store rhPTH as directed by your pharmacist or doctor. Usually, this involves refrigeration.

* **Q: Is rhPTH safe?** A:  Like all medications, rhPTH carries potential risks.  However,  when used appropriately under medical supervision, the benefits often outweigh the risks for individuals at high risk of fractures.
