# Racecadotril

**Generic Name: Racecadotril**

**Usage:**

Racecadotril is primarily used to treat acute diarrhea in adults and children.  Its main function is to reduce the frequency and volume of loose stools, thereby alleviating the symptoms associated with this condition. While its primary use targets acute diarrhea, it's sometimes considered for other gastrointestinal issues involving increased intestinal motility, although this is less common and often requires further medical evaluation.  It's crucial to understand that Racecadotril doesn't treat the underlying cause of the diarrhea; it merely manages the symptoms.  If the diarrhea is severe, persistent, or accompanied by other concerning symptoms (e.g., fever, bloody stools, severe dehydration), immediate medical attention is necessary.


**Dosage:**

Dosage varies depending on age and the severity of the diarrhea.  Prescribing information should always be followed carefully.  Generally, for adults, the usual dose is one or two capsules (100 mg or 200mg), once or twice daily. Children's dosages are significantly lower and always determined by a physician based on weight and age; usually a specific liquid formulation is used.  Racecadotril is typically available as capsules or oral suspension (liquid).  It's essential to take the medication exactly as prescribed and not to exceed the recommended dose.  Always follow your doctor's or pharmacist's instructions.


**Side Effects:**

* **Common:**  Constipation, nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain or discomfort.  These are usually mild and temporary.
* **Less Common but Serious:**  Allergic reactions (rash, itching, swelling, difficulty breathing), severe abdominal pain, changes in bowel habits persisting for an extended time.
* **Important Note:**  If you experience any side effects, particularly those listed above as less common but serious, discontinue use and seek medical advice immediately.  Prompt attention to adverse reactions is crucial to ensure your safety and wellbeing.


**How it Works:**

Racecadotril works by inhibiting an enzyme called enkephalinase. This enzyme normally breaks down enkephalins, which are natural substances in the body that reduce intestinal motility (the movement of the intestines).  By inhibiting enkephalinase, Racecadotril allows enkephalins to remain active for longer, thus slowing down the movement of the intestines and reducing the frequency of bowel movements.  This mechanism directly targets the symptom of diarrhea by reducing the speed of intestinal transit.
