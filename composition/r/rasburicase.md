# Rasburicase

**Generic Name:** Rasburicase

**Usage:**

Rasburicase is a medication primarily used to prevent or treat hyperuricemia, a condition characterized by excessively high levels of uric acid in the blood.  This high uric acid can lead to serious complications, most notably tumor lysis syndrome (TLS).  TLS occurs when cancer cells break down rapidly, releasing large amounts of uric acid into the bloodstream. This can cause kidney damage, kidney stones, and other serious problems. Rasburicase is often administered before, during, and after chemotherapy or radiation therapy in patients with certain types of cancer, particularly those at high risk of developing TLS.  While its primary use is in oncology, it may also be considered in other situations of severe hyperuricemia, though this is less common.  The decision to use rasburicase will always be made by a physician based on individual patient circumstances.

**Dosage:**

Rasburicase is administered intravenously (IV) – meaning it's injected directly into a vein.  Dosage varies significantly depending on the patient's weight, age, and the severity of their hyperuricemia. A physician will determine the appropriate dose and schedule.  There is no standard pediatric or adult dose as it is always individualized.  The medication is not available in tablet or syrup form.  The infusion rate is also carefully controlled by healthcare professionals to minimize the risk of side effects.

**Side Effects:**

* **Common Side Effects:**  Nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, headache, flushing, and low blood pressure.
* **Serious but Less Common Side Effects:**  Methemoglobinemia (a dangerous decrease in the blood's ability to carry oxygen), allergic reactions (including anaphylaxis – a severe and potentially life-threatening allergic reaction), and hemolytic anemia (destruction of red blood cells).  These require immediate medical attention.

*It is crucial to report any side effects, even seemingly minor ones, to your healthcare provider immediately.*


**How it Works:**

Rasburicase is a recombinant urate oxidase enzyme.  Urate oxidase is naturally present in many animals, but not in humans.  Its function is to break down uric acid into a more soluble compound called allantoin, which is then easily excreted by the kidneys. By introducing rasburicase, the body gains the ability to efficiently process and remove the excess uric acid, thus preventing or treating hyperuricemia and mitigating the risks associated with high uric acid levels, like TLS.


**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:**  Rasburicase should not be used in individuals with a known history of hypersensitivity (allergy) to the drug or its components.
* **Drug Interactions:**  Rasburicase may interact with other medications.  It's essential to inform your doctor of all medications, supplements, and herbal remedies you are currently taking.
* **Specific Populations:**  Pregnant women, breastfeeding mothers, and individuals with pre-existing kidney or liver problems should be carefully monitored while using rasburicase.  The potential risks and benefits must be carefully weighed by a healthcare professional in these cases. The use in children requires particular attention to dosing and monitoring.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long does it take for rasburicase to work?**  A: The effect of rasburicase is usually seen within a few hours of administration, leading to a reduction in serum uric acid levels.

* **Q: How is rasburicase stored?** A:  Storage instructions will be provided by your pharmacist; typically, it involves refrigeration.  Always adhere to the provided guidelines.

* **Q:  Is rasburicase safe?** A: Like all medications, rasburicase carries the potential for side effects.  However, the benefits usually outweigh the risks when used appropriately under a doctor's supervision for managing high uric acid levels, particularly in the context of tumor lysis syndrome.

* **Q: What should I do if I experience a side effect?** A: Contact your doctor or healthcare provider immediately if you experience any side effects, particularly those listed as serious.
