# Milk of Magnesia

**Generic Name:** Milk of Magnesia (Magnesium Hydroxide)

**Usage:**

Milk of Magnesia is primarily used as a laxative to relieve occasional constipation.  It works by drawing water into the intestines, softening the stool and making it easier to pass. This makes it effective for treating infrequent constipation and preparing the bowel for medical procedures like colonoscopies.  While its primary use is as a laxative, it also has a secondary, less common use in neutralizing stomach acid, offering temporary relief from heartburn and acid indigestion.  However, it's important to note that for persistent heartburn or acid indigestion, ongoing treatment with a more appropriate medication should be discussed with a doctor.  Milk of Magnesia should not be considered a long-term solution for either constipation or heartburn.

**Dosage:**

Dosage varies depending on the individual and the reason for taking it. Always follow the directions on the product label or consult your doctor or pharmacist.  

* **For constipation relief in adults:** The typical dose is 1 to 2 tablespoons (15 to 30 ml) as needed.
* **For heartburn relief in adults:** The typical dose is 1 to 2 teaspoons (5 to 10 ml) as needed.
* **Children's dosage:**  Children's dosages are significantly lower and should always be determined by a doctor or pharmacist. Never give Milk of Magnesia to a child without professional medical advice.
* **Forms:** Milk of Magnesia is commonly available as a liquid suspension (the most common form), and less frequently as chewable tablets.  It is not available as an injection.

**Side Effects:**

* **Common Side Effects:** Diarrhea, stomach cramps, nausea.  These are usually mild and temporary.
* **Less Common, but Serious Side Effects:**  Prolonged diarrhea can lead to dehydration and electrolyte imbalances.  High doses can cause magnesium toxicity, which can manifest as muscle weakness, confusion, and slowed breathing.  If you experience any of these serious side effects, seek immediate medical attention.

**How it Works:**

Milk of Magnesia contains magnesium hydroxide, which is an antacid and laxative. As an antacid, it neutralizes stomach acid by reacting with it, providing relief from heartburn.  As a laxative, it draws water into the intestines, increasing the bulk and softness of the stool and stimulating bowel movements.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** Milk of Magnesia should not be used by individuals with kidney problems, bowel obstruction, or known allergies to magnesium.
* **Drug Interactions:** Milk of Magnesia can interact with certain medications, particularly those containing tetracyclines (a type of antibiotic) or other drugs that are absorbed in the gut.  Always inform your doctor or pharmacist of all medications you are taking.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  Use during pregnancy and breastfeeding should only occur under the guidance of a healthcare professional.
* **Older Adults and Children:** Older adults and children are particularly vulnerable to side effects and should use Milk of Magnesia with caution and under medical supervision.  The correct dosage is crucial in these groups.

**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long can I use Milk of Magnesia?** A: For constipation, use only as needed for a few days.  For persistent constipation, consult a doctor.  For heartburn, it's best used only for occasional relief.  Don't use it for prolonged periods without medical advice.
* **Q: Can I take Milk of Magnesia with other medications?** A: It's vital to inform your doctor or pharmacist about all your medications before using Milk of Magnesia, as it can interact with some drugs.
* **Q: How should I store Milk of Magnesia?** A: Store at room temperature, away from heat and moisture.
* **Q: What should I do if I have an adverse reaction?** A: Stop taking Milk of Magnesia and contact your doctor or pharmacist immediately.
