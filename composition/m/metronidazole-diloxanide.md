# Metronidazole + Diloxanide

**Generic Name:** Metronidazole + Diloxanide

**Usage:**

Metronidazole and diloxanide furoate combination is primarily used to treat intestinal infections caused by amoebae, specifically *Entamoeba histolytica*. This parasite can cause amoebiasis, a disease ranging from mild intestinal discomfort to severe, potentially life-threatening complications like amoebic dysentery or liver abscesses.  The combination therapy leverages the strengths of both drugs: metronidazole effectively targets the trophozoites (actively feeding and multiplying forms) of the parasite, while diloxanide furoate is highly effective against the cyst stage (dormant, resistant form) that allows the parasite to survive outside the body and spread infection.  While the primary use is for amoebiasis, some formulations might be used off-label by doctors for other intestinal parasitic infections, but this should always be under strict medical supervision.

**Dosage:**

Dosage varies depending on the specific formulation, age, weight, and the severity of the infection.  A healthcare professional will determine the appropriate dose.  Generally, for adults, a common regimen involves taking specific numbers of tablets daily for a certain duration, typically 7-10 days.  Children’s dosages are always lower and are carefully calculated based on their weight and age.  It's crucial to follow the prescribed dosage and duration meticulously as advised by your doctor.  Different formulations may exist – tablets being the most common.  Never adjust the dosage without consulting a doctor.

**Side Effects:**

* **Common:** Nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, metallic taste in the mouth, headache, dizziness.
* **Less Common but Serious:**  Severe abdominal pain, dark urine, jaundice (yellowing of skin and eyes), allergic reactions (rash, itching, swelling), seizures (rare but possible).
* **Note:** If you experience any of these side effects, particularly the serious ones, stop taking the medication and seek immediate medical attention.

**How it Works:**

Metronidazole works by interfering with the DNA of *Entamoeba histolytica*, preventing its replication and ultimately killing the parasite. Diloxanide furoate acts differently; it inhibits the development of the parasite’s cyst form, preventing its spread and persistence in the body.  By combining these two drugs, the treatment effectively tackles both the active and dormant stages of the parasite's life cycle, leading to a higher cure rate.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** This medication should not be used by individuals with known hypersensitivity to metronidazole or diloxanide furoate.  Pregnant and breastfeeding women should exercise extreme caution; use only if strictly necessary and under close medical supervision.
* **Drug Interactions:** This medication can interact with certain other drugs, particularly those metabolized by the liver.  Inform your doctor about all medications, supplements, and herbal remedies you are taking.
* **Alcohol Consumption:** Avoid alcohol consumption during treatment and for several days afterward, as it can cause severe adverse reactions.
* **Liver and Kidney Function:** Patients with impaired liver or kidney function require careful monitoring and possibly dosage adjustments.
* **Elderly:** Older adults might be more susceptible to side effects and may require careful monitoring.

**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long does it take to work?**  A: Improvement in symptoms usually starts within a few days, but the full course of treatment is essential for a complete cure.
* **Q: Can I take this medication with food?** A:  Generally, it's recommended to take this medication with food to minimize gastrointestinal side effects. Check with your doctor or pharmacist for specific guidance on your chosen brand.
* **Q: How should I store this medication?** A: Store in a cool, dry place, away from direct sunlight and moisture, and out of reach of children.
* **Q: What if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it is almost time for the next dose. Do not double up on doses.
* **Q: Is this medication safe for pregnant women?** A: No, it’s not recommended during pregnancy unless deemed absolutely necessary by a doctor who will closely monitor the situation.
