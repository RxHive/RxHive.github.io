# Montelukast

**Generic Name: Montelukast**

**Usage:**

Montelukast is a medication primarily used to prevent and control symptoms of asthma and allergies.  Its main application is in the long-term management of asthma, helping to reduce inflammation in the airways and preventing asthma attacks.  It's particularly effective in preventing symptoms triggered by exercise or allergens.  Montelukast is also used to relieve symptoms of allergic rhinitis (hay fever), such as sneezing, runny nose, and itchy eyes. While not a first-line treatment for these conditions, it can provide significant relief when other treatments are insufficient.  In some cases, it might be used alongside other asthma or allergy medications.


**Dosage:**

Dosage varies depending on the individual, their age, weight, and the specific condition being treated.  Always follow your doctor's prescription instructions carefully.  Generally:

* **Adults and Adolescents (15 years and older):**  The usual dose is one 10mg tablet once daily, usually in the evening.
* **Children (6-14 years):** Dosage depends on weight.  A doctor will prescribe the appropriate chewable tablet or oral granule formulation.
* **Children (2-5 years):**  A lower dose chewable tablet or oral granule is available.
* **Children under 2 years:**  Montelukast is not typically recommended for children under two years of age.

The medication is usually taken once daily, and the timing (morning or evening) may be adjusted based on individual needs and doctor's recommendations.  Different formulations (tablets, chewable tablets, oral granules) are available to suit various age groups.  Never change your dose or stop taking the medication without consulting your doctor.


**Side Effects:**

* **Common:** Headache, nausea, diarrhea, vomiting, abdominal pain.  These are usually mild and temporary.
* **Less Common but Important:**  Increased risk of infections (like colds), sleep disturbances, dizziness,  mood changes (irritability, anxiety, depression, rarely suicidal thoughts).
* **Serious (Rare):** Allergic reactions (rash, hives, swelling), severe liver problems,  serious mental health issues (including suicidal thoughts or actions).

If you experience any concerning side effects, stop taking the medication and contact your doctor immediately.


**How it Works:**

Montelukast works by blocking leukotrienes. Leukotrienes are substances in the body that contribute to inflammation and tightening of the airways, causing symptoms of asthma and allergies. By preventing leukotrienes from working, montelukast helps reduce inflammation and improve airflow, leading to relief of symptoms.  It’s important to note that montelukast is a preventative medication, meaning it works best when taken regularly to prevent symptoms rather than relieving them once they have already started.


**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** Montelukast should not be used by individuals with known allergies to the medication.
* **Drug Interactions:**  Inform your doctor of all medications you are taking, including over-the-counter drugs and herbal supplements, as some medications may interact with montelukast.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  Consult your doctor before taking montelukast if you are pregnant, breastfeeding, or planning to become pregnant.
* **Children:**  Follow the specific dosage recommendations for children based on their age and weight.
* **Older Adults:**  Older adults may require dose adjustments based on their overall health and other medications they are taking.
* **Liver and Kidney Disease:**  Patients with severe liver or kidney disease should use montelukast with caution, potentially requiring dose adjustments.

**FAQs:**

* **Q: Can I take montelukast as needed, or should I take it every day?**  A: Montelukast is most effective when taken daily, even if you don't have symptoms, as it prevents the onset of symptoms.
* **Q: How should I store montelukast?** A: Store montelukast in a cool, dry place away from moisture and direct sunlight.  Follow the specific storage instructions on the label.
* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it's almost time for your next dose.  Do not double the dose to make up for a missed one.
* **Q: Can I stop taking montelukast suddenly?** A: No, always talk to your doctor before stopping montelukast.  Sudden cessation could potentially worsen your condition.
* **Q: Is montelukast addictive?** A: No, montelukast is not considered an addictive medication.
