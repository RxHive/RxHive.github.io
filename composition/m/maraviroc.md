# Maraviroc

**Generic Name: Maraviroc**

**Usage:**

Maraviroc is an antiretroviral medication primarily used to treat HIV-1 infection in adults.  Its main role is as part of a combination antiretroviral therapy (cART) regimen.  This means it's always used with other HIV medicines, not on its own.  Maraviroc specifically targets a protein on the surface of HIV-1 cells called CCR5, preventing the virus from entering healthy CD4 cells (a type of white blood cell crucial for the immune system).  By blocking this entry point, maraviroc helps to reduce the viral load and improve the CD4 cell count, thus slowing the progression of HIV and improving the overall health of the individual.  It's important to note that maraviroc is only effective against HIV-1 strains that use CCR5 to enter cells; it doesn't work against HIV-2 or strains that use a different protein (CXCR4).  Therefore, a person's HIV strain needs to be tested before Maraviroc can be prescribed.  Secondary uses or research applications are still being explored, but it is not currently approved for conditions other than HIV-1.


**Dosage:**

Dosage of maraviroc varies depending on the individual and the specific treatment plan established by their healthcare provider.  It is typically administered orally as a tablet.  There is no specific dosage for children, as its use is primarily restricted to adults. The standard adult dosage usually involves taking one tablet (150 mg or 300 mg, depending on the specific formulation and the individual's circumstances) twice daily with food.  Dosage adjustments may be needed based on factors like kidney or liver function.  The specific dosage and schedule should always be determined by a physician and followed precisely as instructed.


**Side Effects:**

Common side effects of maraviroc can include:

* Nausea
* Diarrhea
* Headache
* Fatigue
* Dizziness
* Rash
* Upper respiratory infections


Less common, but more serious side effects can include allergic reactions (such as severe skin reactions), liver problems (hepatitis), and depression.  **It's crucial to contact a healthcare provider immediately if any concerning or unusual side effects occur.**


**How it Works:**

Maraviroc works by selectively blocking the CCR5 receptor on the surface of CD4 cells.  HIV-1 needs this receptor to enter and infect these cells.  By preventing the virus from attaching to and entering the CD4 cells, maraviroc effectively reduces the amount of virus in the body and gives the immune system a better chance to fight the infection. This helps to slow the progression of HIV and improve a patient's overall health.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** Maraviroc should not be used in individuals with known hypersensitivity (allergy) to the drug.  It is also not effective against HIV-1 strains that use CXCR4 for entry.
* **Drug Interactions:** Maraviroc can interact with other medications, particularly those metabolized by the liver. It’s essential to disclose all medications, including over-the-counter drugs and herbal supplements, to the healthcare provider before starting maraviroc.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:** The use of maraviroc during pregnancy and breastfeeding should be carefully considered and discussed with a healthcare provider, weighing the benefits against potential risks.
* **Liver and Kidney Impairment:** Dosage adjustments may be necessary for individuals with impaired liver or kidney function.
* **Older Adults:** Older adults might be more susceptible to side effects.  Careful monitoring is advisable.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: Can I stop taking maraviroc if I feel better?**  A: No, stopping maraviroc prematurely can lead to drug resistance and disease progression.  It must be taken consistently as prescribed.
* **Q: How should I store maraviroc?** A: Store maraviroc at room temperature, away from moisture and direct sunlight.  Follow the storage instructions on the product label.
* **Q: Is maraviroc a cure for HIV?** A: No, maraviroc is not a cure for HIV.  It is part of a combination therapy to manage the infection and improve the quality of life for those with HIV-1.
* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it is almost time for the next dose.  Do not take two doses at once.  Contact your healthcare provider if you have questions about missed doses.
