**Usage:**

The primary use of meclizine is to treat vertigo (dizziness) and nausea associated with motion sickness, inner ear problems (labyrinthitis), or other conditions causing dizziness. The addition of Vitamin B vitamins is often intended to support overall health and potentially address nutrient deficiencies that could contribute to or exacerbate symptoms in some individuals. **Side Effects:**

Common side effects of meclizine can include:

* Drowsiness
* Dry mouth
* Blurred vision
* Constipation
* Dizziness
* Headache

Less common, but more serious side effects are possible and require immediate medical attention. These may include:

* Allergic reactions (rash, hives, difficulty breathing)
* Fast heartbeat
* Seizures
* Severe drowsiness or confusion

Vitamin B side effects are generally mild, usually related to gastrointestinal upset (e.g., mild diarrhea), but individual vitamins in a given formulation might have their specific side effects, which will be listed on the product packaging.

**How it Works:**

Meclizine is an antihistamine that works by blocking the action of histamine in the central nervous system.  Histamine can contribute to dizziness and nausea. By reducing histamine activity, meclizine helps alleviate these symptoms. Vitamin B's mechanism of action depends on the specific vitamin(s) included. In general, they serve as essential coenzymes in various metabolic processes, supporting nerve function and overall health.  The synergistic effect of the combination is not fully established scientifically and needs specific evidence for any claimed effect.
