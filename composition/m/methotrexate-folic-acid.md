# Methotrexate + Folic Acid

**Generic Name:** Methotrexate + Folic Acid

**Usage:**

Methotrexate, combined with folic acid, is primarily used to treat rheumatoid arthritis (RA), a chronic inflammatory disease affecting the joints.  It helps reduce pain, swelling, and stiffness associated with RA.  This combination therapy is particularly effective because while methotrexate is the primary disease-modifying antirheumatic drug (DMARD), the added folic acid helps mitigate some of methotrexate's common side effects, particularly those related to gastrointestinal upset and blood cell counts.  

Beyond RA, this combination may also be used in certain cases of psoriatic arthritis (arthritis related to psoriasis), and sometimes in other inflammatory conditions, though this is less common and generally determined on a case-by-case basis by a rheumatologist or other specialist. It’s important to remember that this combination is not a cure, but rather a treatment aimed at managing symptoms and slowing disease progression.

**Dosage:**

Dosage of methotrexate + folic acid is highly individualized and depends on various factors including the specific condition being treated, the patient's age, weight, overall health, and response to the medication.  A physician will carefully determine the appropriate dose.  

* **Adults:**  Methotrexate doses typically range from 7.5mg to 25mg weekly, often administered as a single oral dose or injection. Folic acid supplementation is usually concurrent, with a common dose being 1-5mg per day.
* **Children:** Pediatric dosing is significantly different and carefully calculated based on weight and condition. It's crucial to follow a doctor's precise instructions for children.

The medication may be available as tablets, injections (subcutaneous or intramuscular), or occasionally, as a liquid.  Always follow the prescribed route and frequency of administration as directed by the healthcare professional.

**Side Effects:**

* **Common:** Nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, mouth sores, fatigue, headache, abdominal pain.
* **Less Common but Serious:** Liver damage, kidney damage, bone marrow suppression (leading to decreased blood cell counts, increasing risk of infection and bleeding), lung problems, severe allergic reactions.

**Important Note:** If you experience any unusual or concerning side effects, contact your doctor immediately.

**How it Works:**

Methotrexate is a disease-modifying antirheumatic drug (DMARD) that works by interfering with several processes that contribute to inflammation in the body. It inhibits the production of certain immune cells involved in the inflammatory response, thereby reducing joint inflammation and pain.  Folic acid, a B vitamin, helps counteract some of methotrexate's side effects by supporting cellular processes affected by the drug.  The combination aims for optimal disease control with minimized side effects.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** Methotrexate is contraindicated in individuals with severe liver or kidney disease, bone marrow suppression, active infections, or known hypersensitivity to methotrexate. Pregnancy and breastfeeding are significant contraindications.
* **Drug Interactions:** Methotrexate can interact with many medications, including nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drugs (NSAIDs), antibiotics, and certain vaccines. It's vital to inform your physician about all medications, supplements, and herbal remedies you are taking.
* **Vulnerable Groups:** Special caution is required for pregnant or breastfeeding women, children, and older adults due to increased risk of adverse effects.  Pregnancy testing is usually mandatory before starting treatment.

**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long does it take to work?** A:  The onset of benefit varies, but noticeable improvement in symptoms may take several weeks or months.
* **Q: Can I stop taking it if I feel better?** A: No.  Stopping treatment prematurely can lead to a relapse of symptoms.  Always consult your doctor before making any changes to your medication regimen.
* **Q: How should I store it?** A: Store as directed on the label, typically in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight and moisture.
* **Q: Are there any long-term risks?** A:  Long-term use of methotrexate carries potential risks, including liver and kidney damage. Regular monitoring by a healthcare professional is essential.
