# Mercaptopurine

**Generic Name: Mercaptopurine**

**Usage:**

Mercaptopurine is a powerful medication primarily used in the treatment of certain types of cancer and inflammatory bowel diseases.  Its main application lies in oncology, where it acts as a chemotherapy agent.  It's frequently used in the treatment of acute lymphoblastic leukemia (ALL), a common childhood cancer, and other types of leukemia.  Mercaptopurine also plays a role in managing inflammatory bowel diseases like Crohn's disease and ulcerative colitis, particularly when other treatments haven't been fully effective or are unsuitable. In these conditions, it helps to reduce inflammation and control symptoms. The specific use and dosage will depend heavily on the individual's condition, response to treatment, and overall health.

**Dosage:**

Dosage for mercaptopurine is highly individualized and determined by a physician based on factors like age, weight, disease severity, and the patient's response to the drug. It’s crucial to follow the prescribed dosage precisely.  There's no single "standard" dosage.

* **Oral Forms:**  Mercaptopurine is usually available as tablets or a suspension (liquid).  Dosage is often calculated based on body surface area (BSA) or body weight, and adjusted according to the patient's response and any side effects.

* **Children:** Dosage is meticulously calculated for children and is significantly lower than for adults, adjusted for their weight and specific condition.

* **Adults:** Adult dosages vary widely depending on the disease being treated.

**It's absolutely essential to consult a doctor or pharmacist for precise dosage instructions.**  Never adjust the dosage without professional medical guidance.


**Side Effects:**

Mercaptopurine, like many medications, can cause side effects.  These can range from mild to severe.  It's crucial to report any concerning side effects to your healthcare provider immediately.

* **Common Side Effects:** Nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, loss of appetite, abdominal cramps, mouth sores, hair loss.

* **Less Common but Serious Side Effects:**  Low blood counts (leukopenia, thrombocytopenia, anemia), liver damage (hepatotoxicity), pancreatitis, increased risk of infection.  These require immediate medical attention.

* **Allergic Reactions:** While rare, allergic reactions, such as rash, itching, or swelling, can occur.  Stop taking the medication and seek immediate medical attention if you experience an allergic reaction.


**How it Works:**

Mercaptopurine is an antimetabolite chemotherapy drug. It interferes with the production of DNA and RNA, essential components for cell growth and replication.  Cancer cells divide rapidly, making them particularly vulnerable to this interference.  In inflammatory bowel disease, the mechanism is less clearly defined but is thought to involve its impact on the immune system, reducing inflammation and easing symptoms.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** Mercaptopurine should not be used by individuals with severe liver or bone marrow problems.

* **Drug Interactions:**  Several medications can interact with mercaptopurine, potentially affecting its effectiveness or increasing the risk of side effects.  Inform your doctor about all medications, including over-the-counter drugs and herbal supplements, you are taking.

* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  Mercaptopurine should generally be avoided during pregnancy and breastfeeding due to potential harm to the developing fetus or infant.

* **Children and Older Adults:** Careful monitoring is crucial for children and older adults due to their potentially increased sensitivity to side effects.  Dosage adjustments are frequently necessary.

* **Liver Function Monitoring:** Regular liver function tests are typically required during mercaptopurine treatment to detect any signs of liver damage.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long do I need to take mercaptopurine?**  A: The duration of treatment varies greatly depending on the condition and response to the medication.  Your doctor will determine the appropriate treatment duration.

* **Q: Can I stop taking mercaptopurine suddenly?**  A: No, abruptly stopping mercaptopurine can be harmful.  Always follow your doctor's instructions regarding tapering off the medication.

* **Q: How should I store mercaptopurine?**  A: Store mercaptopurine as directed on the label, typically in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight and moisture. Keep it out of reach of children.

* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: If you miss a dose, take it as soon as you remember unless it is almost time for the next dose. Do not double the dose to make up for a missed one.
