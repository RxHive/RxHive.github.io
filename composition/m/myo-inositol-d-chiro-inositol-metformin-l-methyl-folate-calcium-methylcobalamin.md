# Myo-Inositol + D-Chiro Inositol + Metformin + L-Methyl Folate Calcium + Methylcobalamin

**Generic Name:** Myo-Inositol + D-Chiro-Inositol + Metformin + L-Methylfolate Calcium + Methylcobalamin

**Usage:** This combination of ingredients is not a standard, pre-formulated medication found in pharmacies.  Instead, it represents a combination of supplements and a prescription drug (metformin) often used together *off-label* by some healthcare practitioners, primarily to address aspects of polycystic ovary syndrome (PCOS).  Let's break down each component's role:

* **Myo-inositol and D-chiro-inositol:** These are isomers (similar molecules with slightly different structures) of inositol, a vitamin-like substance. In PCOS, they may improve insulin sensitivity, ovulation, and hormone balance.  They're often used to address irregular periods, infertility, and some PCOS-related metabolic issues.

* **Metformin:** This is a prescription medication commonly used to manage type 2 diabetes.  In PCOS, it helps improve insulin sensitivity and can aid in weight management and ovulation.

* **L-Methylfolate Calcium:** This is an active form of folate (vitamin B9).  Folate plays a crucial role in cell growth and function, and its use here is often linked to improving overall metabolic health and supporting reproductive function.

* **Methylcobalamin:** This is an active form of vitamin B12.  B12 is vital for nerve function and red blood cell production.  Its inclusion is likely intended to support overall health and potentially mitigate any potential side effects related to metformin or the metabolic imbalances seen in PCOS.


**Dosage:**  There is no standardized dosage for this combined regimen because it's not an FDA-approved combination. Dosages of each component are determined individually by a healthcare professional, tailored to the patient's specific needs, condition severity, and response to treatment.  This would involve careful monitoring of blood glucose levels, hormone levels, and overall health parameters.  Different brands of supplements may also have varying strengths, necessitating precise instructions from a doctor or registered dietitian.  Metformin, in particular, needs careful titration (gradual increase in dosage) to minimize side effects.


**Side Effects:**

* **Metformin:**  The most common side effects include nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, abdominal bloating, and metallic taste.  Less common but more serious side effects include lactic acidosis (a rare but potentially fatal condition characterized by a buildup of lactic acid in the blood), vitamin B12 deficiency, and liver problems.

* **Myo-inositol & D-chiro-inositol:** Generally well-tolerated but may cause gastrointestinal upset (e.g., gas, bloating, diarrhea).

* **L-Methylfolate Calcium & Methylcobalamin:** Generally considered safe, but allergic reactions are possible, though rare.

**Note:** This is not an exhaustive list.  Any new or worsening symptoms should be reported immediately to a healthcare provider.


**How it Works:** This combination works synergistically. Metformin primarily improves insulin sensitivity, while myo-inositol and D-chiro-inositol may enhance this effect and potentially directly improve ovarian function.  L-methylfolate and methylcobalamin support overall metabolic health and cellular function, contributing to better overall response to treatment.


**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** Metformin is contraindicated in individuals with severe kidney or liver disease, heart failure, or a history of lactic acidosis.
* **Drug Interactions:** Metformin can interact with several medications, including certain diuretics, and some antibiotics.  Always inform your doctor of all medications and supplements you're taking.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  The use of this combination during pregnancy and breastfeeding requires careful consideration and close monitoring by a healthcare professional. Metformin, in particular, may not be recommended during pregnancy.
* **Older Adults and Children:** Dosage adjustments may be necessary for older adults and children due to variations in metabolism and renal function.
