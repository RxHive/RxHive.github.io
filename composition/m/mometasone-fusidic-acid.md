# Mometasone + Fusidic Acid

**Generic Name: Mometasone + Fusidic Acid**

**Usage:**

Mometasone furoate and fusidic acid is a combination medication used primarily to treat skin conditions involving both inflammation and bacterial infection.  Its main application is in the treatment of eczema (specifically, eczema complicated by bacterial infection), psoriasis (again, when a secondary bacterial infection is present), and other inflammatory skin conditions where bacteria are suspected to be contributing to the problem.  The combination is beneficial because mometasone, a corticosteroid, reduces inflammation and itching, while fusidic acid, an antibiotic, targets the bacterial infection.  It's crucial to understand that this combination is not a first-line treatment for uncomplicated eczema or psoriasis; it's reserved for cases where a bacterial infection complicates the primary condition.  The doctor will determine if this combination therapy is appropriate based on a proper diagnosis.


**Dosage:**

Dosage varies significantly depending on the specific formulation (cream, ointment), the age of the patient, and the severity of the infection.  A healthcare professional will always prescribe the appropriate dose.  Generally, the cream or ointment is applied thinly to the affected area a few times a day, following the instructions provided on the prescription label.  **It is vital to never exceed the prescribed dosage or frequency.**  There is no standard dosage information that can be safely generalized here; always follow your doctor's instructions.  This medication is generally not used in children under the age of one, and usage in children and elderly patients should be carefully monitored by a doctor.


**Side Effects:**

* **Common:** Burning, stinging, or itching at the application site; skin thinning; skin discoloration; stretch marks (striae); acne-like rash.
* **Less Common but Serious:** Allergic reactions (rash, swelling, difficulty breathing); fungal or viral skin infections (opportunistic infections, which can arise when the bacterial infection is suppressed); increased intraocular pressure (especially with prolonged use around the eyes);  worsening of the underlying skin condition.


If you experience any of these side effects, especially the serious ones, contact your doctor immediately.


**How it Works:**

Mometasone furoate is a corticosteroid. It works by reducing inflammation and suppressing the immune response in the skin, thus alleviating itching, redness, and swelling. Fusidic acid is an antibiotic that works by preventing the bacteria from producing essential proteins necessary for their survival and replication. By targeting both inflammation and infection, this combination helps control the symptoms and promotes healing in skin conditions involving both.


**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** This medication is contraindicated (should not be used) in individuals with known hypersensitivity (allergy) to either mometasone furoate or fusidic acid.
* **Drug Interactions:**  While rare, interactions are possible. Inform your doctor about all medications you are currently taking, including over-the-counter drugs and herbal supplements, to prevent any potential complications.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:** The use of this medication during pregnancy and breastfeeding should be discussed with a healthcare provider to assess the risks and benefits.  Generally, the medication is used cautiously during these periods if benefits clearly outweigh risks.
* **Children and Elderly:**  Use in children (especially under one year) and the elderly should be closely monitored by a physician.  Skin is thinner and more sensitive in these populations.
* **Long-Term Use:** Prolonged use can lead to skin thinning and an increased risk of skin infections.  Always follow the recommended course of treatment provided by your doctor.  Do not use for extended periods without medical supervision.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: Can I use this medication on my face?** A:  Only if prescribed and under the close supervision of a doctor.  Facial skin is more delicate and prone to side effects.

* **Q: How should I store this medication?** A: Store at room temperature, away from direct sunlight and moisture. Keep out of the reach of children.

* **Q: Can I stop using this medication once my symptoms improve?** A:  No.  Complete the prescribed course of treatment, even if symptoms have improved, to ensure the infection is completely cleared and prevent relapse.

* **Q: What if I miss a dose?** A: Apply the medication as soon as you remember, but do not double up on doses.

* **Q: What should I do if I suspect an allergic reaction?** A:  Stop using the medication immediately and contact your doctor or seek emergency medical attention.
