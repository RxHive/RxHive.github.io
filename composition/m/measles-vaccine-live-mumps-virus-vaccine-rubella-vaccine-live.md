# Measles Vaccine Live + Mumps Virus Vaccine + Rubella vaccine Live

**Generic Name:** Measles Vaccine Live + Mumps Virus Vaccine + Rubella Vaccine Live (MMR Vaccine)

**Usage:**

This combination vaccine is primarily used to protect against three highly contagious viral diseases: measles, mumps, and rubella.  Measles can cause a serious rash, fever, cough, and potentially pneumonia or encephalitis (brain inflammation). Mumps is characterized by swollen salivary glands, fever, and headache, and can sometimes lead to more serious complications like meningitis or encephalitis. Rubella, also known as German measles, is usually mild, but infection during pregnancy can cause serious birth defects in the developing baby.  The MMR vaccine is a cornerstone of childhood immunization and is highly effective in preventing these diseases. There are no secondary uses for this vaccine; its sole purpose is preventative.

**Dosage:**

The MMR vaccine is administered as a subcutaneous injection (under the skin).  The standard dosage is typically a single dose for children aged 12 months and older, with a second dose recommended at age 4-6 years or before school entry.  Specific scheduling may vary slightly based on local guidelines and individual circumstances.  Infants younger than 12 months may receive the vaccine under specific circumstances, such as outbreaks, as directed by a healthcare professional.  There are no variations in dosage based on weight or severity of any condition, as this is a preventative vaccine, not a treatment.


**Side Effects:**

Common side effects are usually mild and temporary:

*   Soreness, redness, or swelling at the injection site
*   Mild fever
*   Rash
*   Joint pain (more common in adults)
*   Headache

Less common but serious side effects can include:

*   Severe allergic reactions (anaphylaxis) – characterized by difficulty breathing, swelling of the face, lips, or throat, and a rapid heartbeat. This is a medical emergency requiring immediate attention.
*   Seizures
*   Thrombocytopenic purpura (low platelet count leading to easy bruising or bleeding)
*   Encephalitis (inflammation of the brain)

**It is crucial to seek immediate medical attention if any serious side effects occur.**


**How it Works:**

The MMR vaccine contains live, weakened (attenuated) viruses.  After injection, these viruses trigger an immune response in the body. The body produces antibodies against measles, mumps, and rubella viruses. These antibodies provide long-lasting immunity, protecting against future infection with these viruses.  Essentially, the vaccine allows the body to "practice" fighting the viruses without causing the diseases themselves.

**Precautions:**

*   **Contraindications:**  The MMR vaccine should not be given to individuals with severe allergic reactions to any component of the vaccine, including neomycin.  It should also be avoided in individuals with severely weakened immune systems (e.g., due to HIV/AIDS or certain cancers).  Pregnancy is also a contraindication, as the live attenuated viruses could theoretically harm the fetus.
*   **Interactions:**  Interactions with other medications are rare, but informing your doctor of all medications you are taking is crucial.
*   **Warnings:** Individuals with a history of seizures or thrombocytopenic purpura should be monitored closely after vaccination.
*   **Pregnant women should not receive the MMR vaccine.**  They should get the vaccine *before* becoming pregnant.
*   **Children and Older Adults:**  While generally safe, children and older adults might experience side effects differently.  Consulting a doctor before vaccination is always recommended.


**FAQs:**

*   **Q: How long does the MMR vaccine protect me?** A:  The MMR vaccine provides long-lasting immunity, usually for many years. However, booster doses may be recommended in specific situations, especially for individuals with compromised immune systems.
*   **Q: Can I get measles, mumps, or rubella from the MMR vaccine?** A:  It's highly unlikely. The viruses in the vaccine are weakened, meaning they are unable to cause illness in healthy individuals. However, mild side effects mimicking the disease are possible.
*   **Q: How should I store the MMR vaccine?** A:  The vaccine must be stored according to the manufacturer's instructions, typically refrigerated at a specific temperature range. 
*   **Q: What should I do if I think I've had a reaction to the vaccine?** A:  Contact your healthcare provider immediately.
