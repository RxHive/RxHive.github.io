# Measles Vaccine Live

**Generic Name:** Measles Vaccine Live

**Usage:**

The Measles Vaccine Live is primarily used to prevent measles, a highly contagious viral infection.  Measles is characterized by fever, cough, runny nose, and a distinctive rash.  Left untreated, it can lead to serious complications such as pneumonia, encephalitis (brain inflammation), and even death, particularly in young children and individuals with weakened immune systems.  This vaccine is a crucial part of public health efforts to control and eliminate measles globally.  There are no secondary uses for this vaccine; its sole purpose is measles prevention.

**Dosage:**

The Measles Vaccine Live is administered as a single subcutaneous injection (under the skin).  The standard dosage is typically one dose for children aged six months to five years and one dose for children older than five years up to adulthood.   The specific schedule and number of doses may vary depending on national immunization programs and individual risk factors.  Always follow the recommendations provided by your healthcare provider and relevant health authorities.  There are no tablet or syrup forms of this vaccine.

**Side Effects:**

Common side effects are generally mild and temporary.  These can include:

*   Fever
*   Rash
*   Soreness, redness, or swelling at the injection site
*   Mild cough or runny nose
*   Tiredness
*   Fussiness (in infants and young children)


Less common, but more serious side effects, though rare, might include:

*   Severe allergic reaction (anaphylaxis) – characterized by difficulty breathing, swelling of the face, lips, or tongue, and a rapid heartbeat. This is a medical emergency.
*   Febrile seizures (fevers causing convulsions) –  although rare, particularly in young children.
*   Thrombocytopenia (low platelet count) – leading to easy bruising or bleeding.


Seek immediate medical attention if you experience any serious side effects.

**How it Works:**

The Measles Vaccine Live contains a weakened (attenuated) form of the measles virus.  When administered, this weakened virus triggers an immune response in the body.  The immune system produces antibodies against the virus, providing immunity and protection against future infection by the actual measles virus.  This acquired immunity is long-lasting, offering protection for many years.


**Popular Brand Names (India):**

The specific brand names available in India can vary.  It's best to check with your healthcare provider or local pharmacy for currently available options.  The vaccine is usually available under its generic name or as part of combination vaccines (e.g., MMR – Measles, Mumps, Rubella).


**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:**  The vaccine should not be given to individuals with known severe allergic reactions to any component of the vaccine, including neomycin.  Individuals with severely compromised immune systems may need to consult a healthcare provider before vaccination. Pregnant women should generally avoid this vaccine, although the risk is minimal.  
* **Interactions:**  While there aren't many significant drug interactions, it's crucial to inform your healthcare provider about all medications you are currently taking.
* **Vulnerable Groups:**  Infants younger than six months and individuals with severely weakened immune systems should be approached cautiously. A physician will assess their suitability for the vaccine.

**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long does immunity last after vaccination?**  A:  Measles vaccine-induced immunity is generally long-lasting, but booster doses might be recommended as per national immunization schedules.

* **Q: Can I get measles after receiving the vaccine?**  A:  It's possible to experience a very mild form of measles after receiving the vaccine (a modified or attenuated form of the virus is given), but this is typically much milder than a natural measles infection and doesn't cause severe illness.

* **Q: How should the vaccine be stored?**  A:  Always follow the storage instructions provided with the vaccine. Generally, it needs to be kept refrigerated at a specific temperature range.

* **Q:  What should I do if my child experiences a fever after vaccination?**  A:  A mild fever is a common side effect.  You can manage it with paracetamol (acetaminophen) as directed by your doctor.  Consult your doctor if the fever is high or persists.

* **Q: Is the measles vaccine safe?**  A:  Extensive research has shown the measles vaccine to be very safe and effective.  However, as with any medication, there's a potential for side effects.  Discuss any concerns with your healthcare provider.
