# Measles Vaccine Live + Rubella vaccine Live

**Generic Name:** Measles Vaccine Live + Rubella Vaccine Live (MMR)

**Usage:**

This combined vaccine is primarily used to prevent measles and rubella, two highly contagious viral illnesses.  Measles can cause fever, cough, runny nose, rash, and potentially serious complications like pneumonia and encephalitis (brain swelling). Rubella, also known as German measles, is generally milder but poses a significant risk to pregnant women, potentially causing congenital rubella syndrome in their unborn child, leading to severe birth defects.  The vaccine provides immunity against both viruses, significantly reducing the risk of infection and its associated complications.  There aren't typically secondary uses beyond preventing these two specific viral infections.  The vaccine is part of routine childhood immunization schedules worldwide.

**Dosage:**

This vaccine is administered as a subcutaneous injection (under the skin).  

* **Children:**  Typically, a two-dose schedule is recommended. The first dose is usually given between 12 and 15 months of age, and the second dose is given between 4 and 6 years of age.  Specific scheduling may vary slightly depending on local immunization guidelines.
* **Adults:**  Adults who lack proof of immunity may receive one or two doses, depending on their history and the advice of their healthcare provider. Catch-up vaccination programs exist for adults who missed vaccination in childhood.


**Side Effects:**

* **Common:** Mild fever, rash, soreness or redness at the injection site, mild headache. These are generally temporary and resolve within a few days.
* **Less Common, but Serious:**  Severe allergic reactions (anaphylaxis), thrombocytopenia (low platelet count), encephalitis (rare).  While rare, these serious side effects necessitate immediate medical attention.


**How it Works:**

The MMR vaccine contains weakened (attenuated) live viruses.  When administered, these weakened viruses trigger the body's immune system to produce antibodies against measles and rubella. These antibodies provide long-lasting immunity, protecting against future infection.  The body recognizes the weakened viruses as a threat, mounts an immune response, and "remembers" how to fight these viruses should it encounter them again in the future.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:**  The MMR vaccine should not be given to individuals with known severe allergic reactions to any component of the vaccine, including neomycin or gelatin. Individuals with severely compromised immune systems should consult their doctor before receiving the vaccine.  Pregnant women should generally avoid this vaccine, as live attenuated viruses are used.
* **Interactions:**  While generally safe, interactions with other medications are rare. However, it’s important to inform your doctor about all medications and supplements you are taking before receiving any vaccine.
* **Vulnerable Groups:**  Pregnant women should postpone vaccination until after delivery.  Individuals with compromised immune systems may require altered vaccination schedules or may not be candidates for the vaccine.  Always consult a healthcare provider before administering the vaccine to infants, children, or elderly individuals with underlying health conditions.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long does the vaccine last?**  A: The MMR vaccine provides long-lasting immunity for most individuals, often lasting for many years or even a lifetime.  Booster doses may be recommended in certain situations.
* **Q: Is the MMR vaccine safe?** A: The MMR vaccine is generally considered safe and highly effective.  While side effects can occur, they are usually mild and temporary.  Serious side effects are rare.
* **Q: How should the vaccine be stored?**  A: The MMR vaccine must be stored according to the manufacturer's instructions, typically refrigerated at a specific temperature range to maintain its efficacy.
* **Q: What should I do if I experience a severe reaction?** A: Seek immediate medical attention if you experience any serious allergic reactions, such as difficulty breathing, swelling of the face or throat, or severe rash.
