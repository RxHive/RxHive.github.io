# Mifepristone

**Generic Name: Mifepristone**

**Usage:**

Mifepristone is primarily used to induce medical abortion, in combination with another medication, misoprostol.  It works by blocking the action of progesterone, a hormone crucial for maintaining pregnancy.  By blocking progesterone, the uterine lining is unable to support the pregnancy, leading to its termination.  The effectiveness of mifepristone is highly dependent on gestational age; it is generally most effective during early pregnancy.  Beyond its primary use in medication abortion, research is exploring its potential role in managing certain types of uterine fibroids and Cushing's syndrome, but these are not yet standard treatments.


**Dosage:**

Dosage regimens for mifepristone vary depending on the specific indication and the prescribing physician's judgment. For medical abortion, a single oral dose is typically administered, followed by misoprostol at a specified interval.  The exact dosage of mifepristone and misoprostol, and the timing between administration, are determined by the healthcare provider based on factors like the duration of pregnancy and the patient's overall health.  There is no approved use of mifepristone in children.  The medication is available only in tablet form.  It is crucial to follow the precise instructions provided by your healthcare provider for administration.


**Side Effects:**

* **Common:**  Vaginal bleeding (often heavy), cramping, nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, headache, dizziness, fatigue.
* **Less Common but Serious:** Severe abdominal pain, heavy or prolonged bleeding requiring medical intervention, fever, infection, incomplete abortion requiring surgical intervention, allergic reactions.

**Note:** This is not an exhaustive list.  Any unusual or concerning symptoms should be reported to a healthcare professional immediately.


**How it Works:**

Mifepristone is a progesterone receptor antagonist.  Progesterone is a steroid hormone essential for maintaining a pregnancy.  It prepares the uterine lining for implantation and supports the continuation of the pregnancy.  By blocking the progesterone receptors, mifepristone prevents progesterone from performing its function, thus interfering with the pregnancy. This ultimately leads to the breakdown of the uterine lining and the expulsion of the pregnancy.


**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** Mifepristone should not be used by individuals with certain medical conditions, including ectopic pregnancies (pregnancies outside the uterus), adrenal insufficiency, and porphyria.  Individuals with severe bleeding disorders should also exercise caution.  It is absolutely crucial to inform your physician about all current medications and health conditions before taking mifepristone.
* **Drug Interactions:**  Some medications may interact with mifepristone.  It is essential to disclose all medications and supplements to your physician to avoid potential interactions.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  Mifepristone is intended to terminate a pregnancy.  It is not to be used during breastfeeding unless specifically directed by a healthcare professional.
* **Older Adults & Children:**  There are no established uses of mifepristone in children.  For older adults, careful assessment of their overall health is crucial before prescribing mifepristone.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long does it take to work?** A: The effects of mifepristone, when used for abortion, typically begin within hours to days, though complete expulsion may take longer.
* **Q: Is it safe?** A: Like all medications, mifepristone carries potential risks and side effects.  The risks are generally low when administered correctly by a healthcare professional and under appropriate circumstances.
* **Q: How should it be stored?** A: Store mifepristone as directed by your pharmacist, typically at room temperature and away from moisture and light.
* **Q: What should I do if I experience severe side effects?** A: Seek immediate medical attention if you experience severe abdominal pain, heavy or prolonged bleeding, fever, or any other concerning symptoms.
* **Q: Can I take it if I have other medical conditions?** A:  It's vital to discuss any pre-existing medical conditions with your physician before taking mifepristone to determine its suitability.
