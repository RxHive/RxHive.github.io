# Magnesium Bisglycinate

**Generic Name:** Magnesium Bisglycinate

**Usage:**

Magnesium bisglycinate is a form of magnesium, an essential mineral involved in hundreds of bodily processes.  Its primary use is to treat or prevent magnesium deficiency (hypomagnesemia).  This deficiency can stem from various causes, including poor diet, malabsorption issues (like Crohn's disease or celiac disease), certain medications (like diuretics), and alcohol abuse.  

Magnesium is crucial for numerous bodily functions: muscle and nerve function, blood sugar control, blood pressure regulation, and protein synthesis. Therefore, supplementing with magnesium bisglycinate can indirectly help manage conditions associated with magnesium deficiency, such as muscle cramps, tremors, fatigue, anxiety, and even some cardiovascular problems.  It's important to understand that magnesium bisglycinate doesn't directly *treat* these conditions, but addresses the underlying magnesium deficiency that may be contributing to them.  It’s often used as a supplement to improve overall health and well-being, particularly in individuals with known deficiencies or those at higher risk.


**Dosage:**

Dosage varies considerably depending on individual needs and the reason for supplementation.  There's no single "standard" dose.  A healthcare professional should determine the appropriate dosage based on factors like age, weight, overall health, and the severity of any magnesium deficiency.  

Generally, oral magnesium bisglycinate supplements are available in tablet or capsule form.  Dosages are usually expressed in milligrams (mg) of elemental magnesium.  For example, a typical adult dose might range from 100mg to 400mg of elemental magnesium per day, but this can be significantly higher in some cases, particularly under medical supervision.  Children's dosages are always significantly lower and should only be determined by a pediatrician.  Liquid forms exist but are less common.  It's crucial to follow the instructions on the product label or the advice of a doctor or pharmacist.


**Side Effects:**

* **Common:** Diarrhea, nausea, stomach cramps. These are usually mild and dose-related; reducing the dose often resolves them.
* **Less Common but Serious:**  Severe diarrhea (which can lead to dehydration),  low blood pressure,  irregular heartbeat.  These require immediate medical attention.

*Note:*  While rare, allergic reactions are possible.  Seek immediate medical help if you experience symptoms like hives, swelling, difficulty breathing, or dizziness.


**How it Works:**

Magnesium bisglycinate is a chelated form of magnesium.  "Chelation" means that the magnesium is bound to glycine, an amino acid.  This binding improves magnesium absorption in the intestines compared to other forms of magnesium. Once absorbed, magnesium participates in numerous enzymatic reactions throughout the body.  It plays a vital role in muscle contraction and relaxation, nerve impulse transmission, energy production, and maintaining the balance of electrolytes. By increasing magnesium levels, it helps restore the proper functioning of these processes, alleviating symptoms associated with deficiency.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:**  Individuals with severe kidney disease should exercise extreme caution and consult their doctor before using magnesium bisglycinate, as it can accumulate in the body and cause potentially harmful effects.
* **Drug Interactions:** Magnesium can interact with certain medications, including antibiotics, diuretics, and some heart medications.  Always inform your doctor or pharmacist of all medications you are taking.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  While magnesium is essential during pregnancy and breastfeeding, it’s crucial to consult a healthcare professional to determine a safe and appropriate dosage.
* **Older Adults:**  Older adults may have reduced kidney function and should monitor for side effects closely.
* **Children:**  Dosage in children must be determined by a pediatrician.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: Can I take magnesium bisglycinate without a doctor's recommendation?**  A: While generally considered safe, it’s best to consult a doctor, especially if you have underlying health conditions or are taking other medications.
* **Q: How should I store magnesium bisglycinate?** A: Store it in a cool, dry place, away from direct sunlight and moisture, as per the label instructions.
* **Q: Is it safe to take magnesium bisglycinate long-term?**  A: Long-term use should be discussed with your doctor to monitor magnesium levels and ensure safety.
* **Q: What if I miss a dose?** A:  Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it is close to the next dose. Do not double the dose.
