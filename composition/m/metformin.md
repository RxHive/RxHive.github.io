# Metformin

**Generic Name: Metformin**

**Usage:**

Metformin is primarily used to treat type 2 diabetes.  It helps control blood sugar levels by reducing the amount of glucose your liver produces and improving your body's sensitivity to insulin.  This means your body can use the insulin it already produces more effectively to bring glucose from your blood into your cells for energy.  While its main use is in type 2 diabetes,  it sometimes plays a secondary role in managing polycystic ovary syndrome (PCOS) due to its effects on insulin levels, which can improve ovulation and reduce symptoms like acne and excessive hair growth.  Research is also ongoing to explore its potential use in other conditions, but these are not standard applications yet.


**Dosage:**

Metformin dosage is highly individualized and determined by your doctor based on your specific needs and response to treatment. It's typically started at a low dose and gradually increased.  Dosage forms include tablets (immediate-release and extended-release) and sometimes, less commonly, syrups.  

* **Adults with Type 2 Diabetes:**  The starting dose is usually low, and the maximum daily dose can vary significantly depending on individual response and other factors. Your doctor will adjust your dose accordingly.  Extended-release formulations often allow for once-daily dosing.
* **Children:** Metformin is not typically prescribed for children with type 2 diabetes, unless specifically deemed appropriate by a specialist, due to the limited research in pediatric populations.
* **Dosage Adjustments:**  Kidney function plays a significant role in how your body processes metformin.  Dosage may need to be adjusted, or the medication may need to be discontinued altogether, for people with impaired kidney function.  Age, weight, and the severity of diabetes are other factors that might influence the dosage prescribed.


**Side Effects:**

* **Common:** Nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, abdominal pain, metallic taste in the mouth, loss of appetite. These are often mild and tend to improve as your body adjusts to the medication.
* **Less Common but Serious:**  Lactic acidosis (a rare but serious build-up of lactic acid in the blood), vitamin B12 deficiency, hypoglycemia (low blood sugar – more likely if combined with other diabetes medications).
* **Note:** If you experience any side effects, especially those listed as serious, contact your doctor immediately.


**How it Works:**

Metformin works primarily by decreasing the amount of glucose your liver releases into your bloodstream.  It also enhances your body's sensitivity to insulin, enabling your cells to absorb glucose more effectively.  This dual action helps regulate blood sugar levels and prevent the complications associated with type 2 diabetes.  The exact mechanisms involved are complex and involve several pathways within the body.


**Popular Brand Names (India):**

The availability of specific brands varies by region and pharmacy.  Some commonly available brands in India include (but are not limited to)  Glucophage, Metformin SR, and others.  Always check with your pharmacist or doctor for the most up-to-date information on available brands in your area.


**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** Metformin should not be used by individuals with severe kidney disease, liver disease, or a history of lactic acidosis.
* **Drug Interactions:** Metformin can interact with other medications, such as certain diuretics and medications that affect kidney function.  It's crucial to inform your doctor of all medications you are taking.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:** Metformin should be used with caution during pregnancy and breastfeeding. The risks and benefits need to be carefully weighed by your doctor.
* **Older Adults:**  Older adults may be more susceptible to some side effects of metformin, particularly those related to kidney function.  Careful monitoring is usually recommended.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long does it take to see results?** A: The effects of metformin can vary, but noticeable improvements in blood sugar control may take several weeks.
* **Q: How should I store metformin?** A: Store it as directed on the label, usually in a cool, dry place.
* **Q: Can I stop taking metformin without consulting my doctor?** A: No, abruptly stopping metformin can be harmful. Always consult your doctor before making any changes to your medication regimen.
* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it is almost time for your next dose. Do not double the dose.
* **Q: Is metformin safe for long-term use?** A:  For most individuals with appropriate monitoring, metformin is generally safe for long-term use in managing type 2 diabetes.
