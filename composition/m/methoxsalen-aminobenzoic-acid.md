# Methoxsalen + Aminobenzoic Acid

**Generic Name:** Methoxsalen + Aminobenzoic Acid

**Usage:**

Methoxsalen and aminobenzoic acid are typically combined for the treatment of psoriasis, a chronic autoimmune skin disease characterized by red, itchy, scaly patches.  This combination therapy utilizes the synergistic effects of both drugs to improve treatment outcomes.  Methoxsalen is a photosensitizing agent, meaning it makes the skin more sensitive to ultraviolet (UV) light.  Aminobenzoic acid (PABA) also has some sun-screening properties, though its primary role in this combination is thought to be in enhancing the effectiveness of methoxsalen and potentially reducing some of its side effects.  The treatment involves taking the medication followed by exposure to controlled doses of UVA radiation (PUVA therapy). This combination helps to slow the rapid skin cell growth characteristic of psoriasis, reducing inflammation and improving the appearance of the skin lesions.  While primarily used for psoriasis, this combination may be considered in other skin conditions under the direct supervision of a dermatologist.  The use outside of psoriasis is less common and requires careful evaluation.


**Dosage:**

Dosage is highly individualized and strictly determined by a physician based on the severity of the condition, the patient's age, weight, and overall health.  There is no standard dosage that applies universally.  The medication is usually administered orally in tablet form.  A physician will prescribe a specific dosage regimen, detailing the amount of medication to be taken and the timing of UVA exposure. Children and individuals with certain health conditions may require adjusted dosages or different treatment strategies.  It's crucial to follow the prescribed dosage and treatment plan meticulously, and never alter the dosage without consulting a healthcare professional.  Injectable or syrup forms of this specific combination are not commonly available.


**Side Effects:**

* **Common:** Nausea, vomiting, headache, dizziness, sunburn-like reactions, itching, skin redness.
* **Less Common but Serious:**  Severe sunburn, blistering, increased risk of skin cancer (with long-term use and improper UVA exposure), eye irritation, phototoxicity (increased sensitivity to sunlight), cataracts, and other eye problems. Allergic reactions are also possible.


**How it Works:**

Methoxsalen works by increasing the skin's sensitivity to UVA light.  When the skin is exposed to UVA after methoxsalen intake, it causes a slowing down of the rapid cell growth associated with psoriasis. This process involves the interaction of methoxsalen with DNA, affecting cell division. Aminobenzoic acid's role isn't fully elucidated but it's believed to modulate the effects of methoxsalen, potentially enhancing its therapeutic action and mitigating some side effects.  The combined effect leads to a reduction in skin inflammation and scaling, thus improving psoriasis symptoms.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** This combination therapy should be avoided by individuals with severe liver or kidney disease, individuals with a history of severe sun sensitivity or photosensitivity reactions, pregnant or breastfeeding women, and those with certain types of skin cancer.
* **Drug Interactions:** Interactions with other medications are possible; hence it is critical to inform the physician of all other medications being taken.
* **Specific Warnings:**  Extreme sun exposure should be avoided during and after treatment.  Patients should use protective eyewear during and after UVA exposure to protect their eyes. Regular monitoring of the skin and eyes is essential to detect any potential adverse effects.  Children and the elderly may require more careful monitoring and potentially adjusted dosages.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long does the treatment last?**  A: The duration of treatment varies significantly depending on the severity of psoriasis and individual response. It’s determined by a dermatologist.
* **Q:  Is this treatment safe long-term?** A: Long-term use increases the risk of skin cancer and other serious side effects.  Regular monitoring and careful adherence to treatment guidelines are crucial.
* **Q: How should I store the medication?** A: Store the medication as directed on the label, typically in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight and heat.
* **Q: What should I do if I experience side effects?** A: Report any side effects, particularly serious ones, to your physician immediately.  Do not discontinue treatment without consulting your doctor.
* **Q: Can I use other sunscreens or tanning beds with this treatment?** A: No. Avoid all unnecessary sun exposure and tanning beds during treatment.  Use only sunscreens recommended by your doctor.
