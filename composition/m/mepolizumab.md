# Mepolizumab

**Generic Name: Mepolizumab**

**Usage:**

Mepolizumab is a biologic medication primarily used to treat eosinophilic diseases.  This means it targets conditions where a type of white blood cell called an eosinophil plays a significant role in the disease process.  Its primary use is in managing severe eosinophilic asthma, a form of asthma where eosinophils contribute to significant inflammation and airway narrowing, leading to frequent exacerbations (worsening of symptoms) and limitations in daily life.  

Beyond asthma, mepolizumab has also shown effectiveness in treating other eosinophilic conditions, including eosinophilic granulomatosis with polyangiitis (EGPA), also known as Churg-Strauss syndrome, and hypereosinophilic syndrome (HES). These are rarer conditions characterized by high levels of eosinophils in the blood and damage to various organs.  The specific use of mepolizumab in these conditions will depend on individual patient needs and the severity of their illness, as determined by a physician.


**Dosage:**

Mepolizumab is administered as a subcutaneous injection (under the skin) and is *not* available in tablet or syrup form.  The dosage is typically determined by a physician based on the specific condition being treated and the individual patient's response.  

* **Adults with severe eosinophilic asthma:**  The usual dose is 100mg given subcutaneously every 4 weeks.
* **Children and adolescents with severe eosinophilic asthma:** The dosage varies depending on weight and age; this should be determined by a physician.
* **Other eosinophilic conditions (EGPA, HES):** Dosing varies and is tailored to the individual's needs under close medical supervision.

It's crucial to follow the prescribed dosage and injection schedule precisely, as advised by the prescribing healthcare professional.


**Side Effects:**

* **Common:** Injection site reactions (redness, swelling, itching), headache, nausea, fatigue.
* **Less Common but Serious:**  Infections (including serious ones), allergic reactions (ranging from mild rash to severe anaphylaxis),  worsening of asthma symptoms in some cases.

If you experience any side effects, especially those that are severe or persistent, consult your doctor or healthcare provider immediately.


**How it Works:**

Mepolizumab is a monoclonal antibody.  This means it's a laboratory-made protein designed to target a specific molecule in the body.  In this case, mepolizumab targets interleukin-5 (IL-5), a protein that plays a crucial role in the growth, activation, and survival of eosinophils. By binding to IL-5, mepolizumab effectively blocks its action, reducing the number of eosinophils and thus lessening the inflammation and damage they cause in various conditions like severe eosinophilic asthma.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** Mepolizumab should not be used in individuals with known hypersensitivity (allergy) to mepolizumab or any of its components.
* **Drug Interactions:**  While significant drug interactions are not commonly reported, it is vital to inform your physician about all medications, including over-the-counter drugs, herbal remedies, and supplements you are taking.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:** The use of mepolizumab during pregnancy and breastfeeding requires careful evaluation of the risks and benefits by a physician.
* **Older Adults and Children:**  Dosage adjustments may be necessary for older adults and children, requiring close medical supervision.  

**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long does it take to see results?** A: The time it takes to see improvement varies but generally occurs within several weeks of starting treatment.
* **Q: Can I stop taking mepolizumab if I feel better?** A: No, do not stop taking mepolizumab without consulting your doctor.  Stopping treatment prematurely can lead to a relapse of symptoms.
* **Q: How should I store mepolizumab?** A: Store mepolizumab as directed on the product label, usually refrigerated.
* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Consult your doctor or pharmacist for guidance on missed doses.
