# Mirabegron

**Generic Name: Mirabegron**

**Usage:**

Mirabegron is primarily used to treat overactive bladder (OAB).  OAB is a condition characterized by a sudden, urgent need to urinate, often accompanied by urinary frequency (urinating more often than usual) and urgency urinary incontinence (leaking urine due to urgency).  It works by relaxing the muscles in the bladder, allowing the bladder to hold more urine and reducing the frequency of urination and urgency. While its primary use is for OAB in adults,  it's important to note that its use in children or for other conditions should be determined and overseen by a healthcare professional.  There are currently no approved uses of mirabegron outside of adult overactive bladder.

**Dosage:**

The typical adult dosage of mirabegron is 50 mg once daily.  A doctor may adjust the dosage based on individual response and tolerability.  Higher doses (up to 100 mg daily) may be considered in some cases, but this should always be determined by a healthcare professional.  Mirabegron is available in tablet form.  It should be taken with or without food.  There are no currently approved formulations for children or other routes of administration (e.g., syrup, injection).  **It is crucial to follow the prescribed dosage and instructions provided by your doctor or pharmacist.**


**Side Effects:**

Common side effects of mirabegron can include:

* Increased heart rate
* Headache
* Dry mouth
* Constipation
* Upper respiratory tract infection (common cold symptoms)
* Nausea
* Dizziness

Less common but more serious side effects include:

* High blood pressure
* Allergic reactions (such as rash, hives, itching)
* Difficulty breathing
* Swelling in face, lips, tongue, or throat

**If you experience any serious or concerning side effects, stop taking mirabegron and seek immediate medical attention.**

**How it Works:**

Mirabegron is a beta-3 adrenergic receptor agonist.  In simpler terms, it works by selectively activating beta-3 receptors in the bladder. This activation leads to relaxation of the detrusor muscle, the muscle responsible for bladder contractions.  By relaxing this muscle, mirabegron increases bladder capacity and reduces the frequency and urgency of urination associated with OAB.


**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** Mirabegron should not be used in individuals with severe liver or kidney disease.  Caution should be exercised in individuals with a history of uncontrolled high blood pressure or heart conditions.
* **Drug Interactions:** Mirabegron may interact with certain medications, especially those affecting the heart or liver.  It is vital to inform your doctor of all medications, including over-the-counter drugs, herbal supplements, and vitamins, that you are taking.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  The safety of mirabegron during pregnancy and breastfeeding has not been fully established.  Consult your doctor before taking mirabegron if you are pregnant, breastfeeding, or planning to become pregnant.
* **Older Adults:** Older adults may be more sensitive to the side effects of mirabegron.  Dosage adjustments may be necessary.
* **Children:** Mirabegron is not approved for use in children.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long does it take for mirabegron to work?** A: It may take several weeks to see a noticeable improvement in symptoms.
* **Q: Can I stop taking mirabegron suddenly?** A: No, you should gradually reduce the dose under your doctor’s supervision before stopping treatment. Stopping abruptly may lead to a worsening of symptoms.
* **Q: How should I store mirabegron?** A: Store mirabegron tablets at room temperature, away from moisture and heat.
* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it is almost time for your next dose. Do not take two doses at once.
* **Q: Is mirabegron habit-forming?** A: No, mirabegron is not considered a habit-forming medication.
