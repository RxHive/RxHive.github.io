# Methotrexate

**Generic Name: Methotrexate**

**Usage:**

Methotrexate is a powerful medication primarily used as a disease-modifying antirheumatic drug (DMARD) to treat various autoimmune diseases. Its primary application lies in managing rheumatoid arthritis, a condition marked by chronic inflammation of the joints.  It's highly effective in reducing inflammation, pain, and joint damage associated with this disease.  Beyond rheumatoid arthritis, methotrexate also finds use in treating other autoimmune conditions like psoriatic arthritis, juvenile idiopathic arthritis, and Crohn's disease.  It's also a cornerstone treatment for certain types of cancer, including some leukemias, lymphomas, and breast cancer, usually in combination with other chemotherapy agents.  Additionally, it's used to treat severe forms of psoriasis, a skin condition causing scaly patches.  It's important to remember that methotrexate's use in these varied conditions reflects its ability to suppress the immune system, thereby slowing down the disease processes.


**Dosage:**

Methotrexate dosage is highly individualized and depends on the specific condition being treated, the patient's age, weight, overall health, and response to the medication. It's available in various forms, including tablets, oral solutions, and injections (intramuscular or intravenous).

* **Rheumatoid Arthritis:**  Adults typically start with a low dose, often 7.5 mg once a week, increasing gradually under a doctor's close supervision.
* **Psoriasis:** Doses range from 7.5 mg to 25 mg weekly, adjusted based on individual response and tolerability.
* **Cancer:** Dosage and administration (intravenous or intrathecal) vary significantly based on the specific type of cancer and treatment protocol.  This is strictly determined by an oncologist.
* **Children:** Pediatric doses are significantly lower and calculated based on weight and the specific condition.  Dosage adjustments are frequently necessary.

**It's crucial that the dosage is determined and adjusted solely by a healthcare professional.**  Improper dosage can lead to severe side effects.


**Side Effects:**

Methotrexate can cause a range of side effects, some common and others less frequent but potentially serious.

* **Common Side Effects:** Nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, mouth sores, fatigue, headache, abdominal pain.
* **Less Common but Serious Side Effects:** Liver damage, kidney damage, bone marrow suppression (leading to decreased blood cell counts), lung problems, severe skin reactions, infections.  These are often dose-related and warrant immediate medical attention.

**If you experience any concerning side effects, contact your doctor immediately.**


**How it Works:**

Methotrexate works by interfering with folic acid metabolism, a vital process for cell growth and division.  By inhibiting this process, it slows down the rapid proliferation of cells, which is a key characteristic of both cancerous cells and the overactive immune response seen in autoimmune diseases.  In cancer treatment, this slows the growth of tumor cells. In autoimmune diseases, it dampens the hyperactive immune system, reducing inflammation and associated symptoms.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:**  Methotrexate is contraindicated in individuals with severe liver or kidney disease, pre-existing bone marrow suppression, or known hypersensitivity to the drug.  Pregnancy and breastfeeding are also absolute contraindications.
* **Drug Interactions:** Methotrexate can interact with several other medications, including salicylates, NSAIDs, antibiotics, and other immunosuppressants.  Always inform your doctor about all medications you are taking.
* **Vulnerable Groups:**  Special caution is warranted in older adults, children, and individuals with compromised liver or kidney function.  Regular monitoring is essential.  Pregnancy testing is crucial before starting treatment in women of childbearing age.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long does it take to see results?** A: The time it takes to see effects varies depending on the condition being treated and individual response.  It might take several weeks or even months for noticeable improvement.
* **Q: How should I store Methotrexate?** A: Store methotrexate as directed on the product label, usually in a cool, dry place, away from direct sunlight and moisture.
* **Q: Can I drink alcohol while taking Methotrexate?** A:  Alcohol consumption should be minimized or avoided entirely while on methotrexate due to the potential for increased liver damage.  Discuss alcohol consumption with your doctor.
* **Q: Is Methotrexate addictive?** A: Methotrexate is not considered addictive.
