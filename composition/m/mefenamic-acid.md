# Mefenamic Acid

**Generic Name: Mefenamic Acid**

**Usage:**

Mefenamic acid is a nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drug (NSAID) primarily used to relieve mild to moderate pain. Its main application is managing menstrual cramps (dysmenorrhea), often providing effective relief from the associated pain and discomfort.  It's also sometimes used to treat other types of pain, such as headaches, toothaches, and pain following surgery or injury.  However, it's important to note that mefenamic acid is not typically the first-line treatment for these conditions; other NSAIDs or pain relievers might be preferred.  The use of mefenamic acid for conditions other than dysmenorrhea should always be guided by a healthcare professional.


**Dosage:**

Dosage varies depending on the patient's age, weight, and the specific condition being treated.  A doctor will determine the appropriate dose.  Generally:

* **Adults:**  The usual dose for pain relief is 250-500 mg every six to eight hours, not exceeding 750 mg per day for dysmenorrhea.  Higher doses may be used under strict medical supervision.  Mefenamic acid is typically available in tablet form.
* **Children:** Mefenamic acid is generally not recommended for children under 14 years of age due to safety concerns.  Use in children requires careful evaluation and prescription by a physician.
* **Other Forms:** While tablets are the most common form, other formulations might exist in specific regions, but their availability and usage should be confirmed with a pharmacist or physician.  


**Side Effects:**

* **Common:** Nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, abdominal pain, indigestion, dizziness, and headache.
* **Less Common but Serious:**  Allergic reactions (rash, swelling, difficulty breathing),  stomach ulcers, bleeding in the stomach or intestines, liver problems, kidney problems, high blood pressure.
* **Note:**  If you experience any unusual or concerning side effects, stop taking mefenamic acid and seek immediate medical attention.


**How it Works:**

Mefenamic acid reduces pain and inflammation by inhibiting the production of prostaglandins. Prostaglandins are substances in the body that trigger pain and inflammation in response to injury or other stimuli. By blocking the production of these prostaglandins, mefenamic acid helps to reduce pain and swelling.


**Popular Brand Names (India):**

The specific brand names available in India can vary, and it's best to check with a local pharmacist for current options.  However, many generic versions are widely available.


**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:**  Mefenamic acid should not be used by individuals with known allergies to mefenamic acid or other NSAIDs, severe liver or kidney disease, or active peptic ulcers.
* **Drug Interactions:**  Mefenamic acid can interact with several medications, including anticoagulants (blood thinners), lithium, and certain diuretics.  Inform your doctor about all medications you are currently taking.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  Mefenamic acid is generally not recommended during pregnancy, especially in the later stages, and during breastfeeding due to potential risks to the mother and the baby.
* **Older Adults and Children:**  Use in older adults and children requires careful monitoring and should be guided by a physician due to increased risk of side effects.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long does it take to work?** A:  The onset of pain relief varies but is typically within a few hours.
* **Q: Can I take it with alcohol?** A:  Combining mefenamic acid with alcohol can increase the risk of stomach upset and other side effects. It is best avoided.
* **Q: How should I store it?** A: Store mefenamic acid at room temperature, away from moisture and heat.
* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A:  Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it is almost time for your next dose. Do not take a double dose.
* **Q: Is it habit-forming?** A:  Mefenamic acid is not considered habit-forming.
