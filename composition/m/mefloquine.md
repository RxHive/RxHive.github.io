# Mefloquine

**Generic Name: Mefloquine**

**Usage:**

Mefloquine is an antimalarial medication primarily used to prevent malaria caused by *Plasmodium falciparum*, *Plasmodium vivax*, *Plasmodium ovale*, and *Plasmodium malariae*.  Its prophylactic use is particularly valuable in areas where resistance to other antimalarial drugs is prevalent or where the risk of severe malaria is high.  While primarily a preventative, mefloquine can also be used to treat uncomplicated malaria, though other drugs are often preferred as first-line treatments due to mefloquine's potential side effects.  The decision to use mefloquine for treatment versus prophylaxis should always be made in consultation with a healthcare professional.

**Dosage:**

Dosage varies significantly depending on the use (prophylaxis or treatment), the age and weight of the patient, and the specific malaria parasite involved.  A doctor will determine the correct dosage.  Generally, mefloquine is available as tablets.  It's crucial to follow the prescribed dosage regimen precisely;  adjustments should only be made under a doctor's supervision.  There is no standard pediatric syrup or injection formulation commonly available.

* **Prophylaxis (Prevention):**  Dosage is usually calculated based on weight and administered weekly, beginning one or two weeks before entering a malaria-risk area and continuing for four weeks after leaving.

* **Treatment (of uncomplicated malaria):**  A higher single dose, or a course of doses over a few days, might be prescribed.  This will be decided by the doctor based on the patient's condition and the malaria parasite.

**Never attempt to self-medicate with mefloquine.** Always consult a doctor before using this medication.

**Side Effects:**

* **Common:** Dizziness, nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, headache, insomnia, vivid dreams, anxiety, and fatigue.

* **Less Common but Serious:**  Neuropsychiatric effects (such as depression, confusion, psychosis, seizures, hallucinations), heart rhythm abnormalities, hearing loss, and allergic reactions.

* **Important Note:** If any adverse effects occur, especially those listed as serious, discontinue the medication immediately and consult a doctor.  Early intervention can minimize potential complications.

**How it Works:**

Mefloquine works by interfering with the parasite's ability to reproduce.  Specifically, it inhibits the parasite's DNA replication and protein synthesis, ultimately leading to its death. This process targets the malaria parasite within red blood cells.

**Popular Brand Names (India):**

Brand name variations exist across different manufacturers and regions. It's best to consult a pharmacist or doctor for specific brand availability in your location.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** Mefloquine should be avoided by individuals with a history of seizures, epilepsy, or certain psychiatric conditions.

* **Drug Interactions:** Mefloquine can interact with various medications, including some antidepressants, anti-anxiety medications, and drugs that affect the heart. It's crucial to inform your doctor about all medications you are currently taking.

* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  Mefloquine should be used during pregnancy only if the potential benefits outweigh the risks, and under strict medical supervision.  It's generally recommended to avoid use during breastfeeding due to the risk of the drug passing to the infant through breast milk.

* **Children and Older Adults:** Use in children and older adults requires careful consideration and close monitoring due to potential increased sensitivity to side effects.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: Can I take mefloquine with alcohol?**  A: No, avoid alcohol while taking mefloquine, as it can increase the risk of side effects.

* **Q: How should I store mefloquine?** A: Store in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight and moisture. Keep out of reach of children.

* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Consult your doctor or pharmacist immediately. Do not double up on doses.

* **Q: Is mefloquine safe for long-term use?** A:  Long-term use should only be considered under close medical supervision.  The risks and benefits need to be carefully weighed.

* **Q:  Are there alternative malaria prevention methods?** A: Yes, several other drugs and preventative measures (mosquito nets, insect repellent) are available. Consult your doctor to discuss the best option for your specific situation.
