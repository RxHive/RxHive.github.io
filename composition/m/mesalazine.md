# Mesalazine

**Generic Name: Mesalazine**

**Usage:**

Mesalazine is a medication primarily used to treat inflammatory bowel diseases (IBD).  Its main applications are in managing ulcerative colitis (a type of IBD affecting the large intestine) and Crohn's disease (an IBD that can affect any part of the digestive tract).  Mesalazine works specifically by reducing inflammation in the bowel. While its primary use is in IBD, it may also be used to treat other conditions involving intestinal inflammation in certain circumstances, though this is less common and should be determined by a physician.  The specific use and effectiveness depend significantly on the individual patient and the severity of their condition.

**Dosage:**

Dosage of mesalazine varies greatly depending on the specific condition being treated, the severity of the condition, the patient's age and weight, and the formulation of the medication.  It's crucial to follow the doctor's prescription precisely.  There isn't a universally applicable dosage.  Mesalazine comes in various forms, including tablets, capsules, suppositories, and enemas.  Dosage may be adjusted based on the patient's response to the medication and potential side effects.  For children, dosage is carefully adjusted based on weight and the specific needs dictated by their physician.  A doctor will provide the precise dosage regimen.  This information is for general understanding and should not substitute for a physician's guidance.

**Side Effects:**

Common side effects of mesalazine can include:

* Nausea
* Vomiting
* Diarrhea
* Abdominal pain
* Headache
* Dizziness
* Rash

Less common but more serious side effects can include:

* Severe allergic reactions (anaphylaxis)
* Liver problems
* Kidney problems
* Pancreatitis (inflammation of the pancreas)
* Blood disorders

If you experience any concerning side effects, especially severe or persistent ones, seek immediate medical attention.


**How it Works:**

Mesalazine is an aminosalicylate.  It works by reducing inflammation in the lining of the digestive tract.  The exact mechanism isn't fully understood, but it's believed to act locally within the intestines, decreasing the production of inflammatory substances and thereby lessening the inflammation that causes the symptoms of IBD.  This helps to alleviate pain, reduce bowel movements, and improve overall digestive health.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:**  Mesalazine should not be used by individuals with known hypersensitivity or allergy to mesalazine or its components.
* **Drug Interactions:** Mesalazine can interact with certain medications. It's vital to inform your doctor of all medications, supplements, and herbal remedies you are taking.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  The use of mesalazine during pregnancy and breastfeeding should be carefully considered and discussed with a healthcare professional.  The potential benefits must be weighed against the potential risks to the mother and child.
* **Children and Older Adults:** Dosage adjustments are often necessary for children and older adults based on their individual health conditions and metabolism.
* **Kidney and Liver Function:**  Individuals with impaired kidney or liver function should be closely monitored while using mesalazine, as the drug may be processed differently in these cases.

**FAQs:**


* **Q: How long does it take to work?**  A: The onset of effects varies depending on the individual and the formulation, but it can take several weeks for noticeable improvement in symptoms.
* **Q: Can I stop taking it abruptly?** A: No.  Always follow your doctor's instructions regarding discontinuation.  Stopping abruptly can lead to a flare-up of symptoms.
* **Q: How should I store it?** A: Store mesalazine as directed on the packaging, typically in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight and moisture.
* **Q: What if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it's almost time for the next dose. Don't double the dose.
* **Q: Is mesalazine addictive?** A: No, mesalazine is not considered an addictive medication.
