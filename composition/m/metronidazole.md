# Metronidazole

**Generic Name: Metronidazole**

**Usage:**

Metronidazole is an antibiotic medication primarily used to treat infections caused by certain bacteria and parasites.  Its primary use is tackling infections caused by anaerobic bacteria – bacteria that thrive in oxygen-poor environments. These bacteria are responsible for a range of conditions, including:

* **Bacterial Vaginosis (BV):** A common vaginal infection. Metronidazole is a frequently prescribed treatment.
* **Trichomoniasis:** A sexually transmitted infection (STI) caused by a parasite.
* **Giardiasis and Amebiasis:** Intestinal infections caused by parasites.  Metronidazole is effective against both.
* **Infections of the skin and soft tissues:**  Metronidazole can treat infections that have spread to these areas, especially those caused by anaerobic bacteria.  These might be abscesses, wound infections or cellulitis.
* **Certain types of pelvic inflammatory disease (PID):**  In combination with other antibiotics, it can help manage some forms of this serious infection.
* **Infections following surgery:** Metronidazole may be used preventatively or to treat post-surgical infections.


Secondary uses, often in conjunction with other medications, may include certain types of inflammatory bowel disease and brain abscesses.  However, the precise use in these more complex situations should be determined by a healthcare professional.


**Dosage:**

Dosage varies greatly depending on the specific infection, the patient's age, weight, and overall health.  A doctor will determine the appropriate dose.  The medication comes in various forms, including tablets, capsules, creams, gels, and intravenous solutions.  

* **Adults:**  Typical dosages for infections range from 250mg to 750mg, administered multiple times a day for a period of 5 to 10 days, or even longer depending on the condition.
* **Children:** Dosages for children are calculated based on their weight and the specific infection.  Liquid preparations (syrups) are often used for children.
* **Specific Instructions:**  Always follow the instructions provided by your doctor or pharmacist carefully. Never adjust the dose without consulting them.  Complete the entire course of treatment, even if symptoms improve.


**Side Effects:**

* **Common:** Nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, metallic taste in the mouth, headache, dizziness.
* **Less Common but Serious:** Seizures (rare but serious), peripheral neuropathy (nerve damage in the extremities), blood disorders.  Rare cases of allergic reactions including skin rashes and difficulty breathing can occur.
* **Note:** If you experience any unusual or persistent side effects, consult a doctor immediately.


**How it Works:**

Metronidazole works by entering the bacterial or parasitic cell and damaging its DNA. This prevents the microorganism from reproducing and ultimately kills it. It's particularly effective against anaerobic bacteria and certain parasites because it requires a low-oxygen environment to function effectively.

Many brand names exist.  Checking with a local pharmacist will give you a current list of available brands in your region.  Note that brand availability varies widely.


**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:**  Metronidazole should not be used by individuals with known hypersensitivity (allergy) to it.
* **Drug Interactions:** It can interact with several other medications, including anticoagulants (blood thinners) and alcohol.  Always inform your doctor of all other medications you are taking.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:** Use during pregnancy should be carefully considered and only prescribed if the benefits outweigh the potential risks.  It can pass into breast milk and may affect a nursing infant.  A healthcare professional can help assess the risks.
* **Liver and Kidney Disease:** Individuals with liver or kidney disease may require dose adjustments.
* **Alcohol Consumption:** Avoid alcohol consumption during treatment and for at least 24-48 hours after completing the course, as it can cause a disulfiram-like reaction (severe nausea and vomiting).
* **Elderly:** Older adults may be more susceptible to side effects.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: Can I stop taking Metronidazole if I feel better?**  A: No. Complete the entire prescribed course, even if symptoms improve, to prevent recurrence of the infection.
* **Q: How should I store Metronidazole?** A: Store it in a cool, dry place, away from direct sunlight and moisture. Keep it out of reach of children.
* **Q: Is Metronidazole safe for long-term use?** A: Prolonged use can increase the risk of side effects. It should only be used as directed by a doctor for the prescribed duration.
* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it is almost time for the next dose. Do not double the dose.
* **Q: Can I take Metronidazole with other medications?** A:  Always inform your doctor of all medications, supplements, and herbal remedies you are taking to avoid potential interactions.
