# Mebendazole

**Generic Name: Mebendazole**

**Usage:**

Mebendazole is an antiparasitic medication primarily used to treat intestinal worm infections.  Its main purpose is to eliminate various types of parasitic worms (helminths) that can reside in the intestines, causing a range of symptoms depending on the specific worm and the severity of the infection.  These worms include roundworms (like *Ascaris lumbricoides*), pinworms (*Enterobius vermicularis*), hookworms (*Necator americanus* and *Ancylostoma duodenale*), whipworms (*Trichuris trichiura*), and others.  While its primary use is targeting these intestinal parasites, mebendazole may also be used in certain instances for treating other parasitic infections, though this is usually determined on a case-by-case basis by a healthcare professional.

**Dosage:**

Dosage varies depending on the specific parasite, the patient's age and weight, and the severity of the infection.  It's crucial to follow the prescription instructions precisely.  Generally:

* **Adults and Children over 2 years:** The usual dose is a single dose, or a course of treatment as prescribed by a doctor.  Dosage forms include tablets and suspensions (liquid).
* **Children under 2 years:**  Mebendazole is generally not recommended for children under two years old unless specifically prescribed by a doctor who will determine an appropriate dosage based on weight and the specific infection.

**Dosage forms:**  Mebendazole is available in tablet and suspension (liquid) forms.  Always follow the instructions provided by your doctor or pharmacist on how to administer the medication.

**Side Effects:**

* **Common:**  Nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, abdominal pain, headache. These are usually mild and temporary.
* **Less Common but Serious:** Allergic reactions (rash, itching, swelling, difficulty breathing), liver problems (jaundice, dark urine), and blood disorders.  
* **Important Note:**  If you experience any adverse effects, especially those listed as less common but serious, discontinue use and seek immediate medical attention.

**How it Works:**

Mebendazole works by interfering with the ability of parasitic worms to absorb glucose, a crucial energy source.  By depriving the worms of this energy, it impairs their growth and reproduction, leading to their eventual death and expulsion from the body.  The medication primarily affects the worms' metabolism without significantly impacting the host's cells.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:**  Mebendazole should not be used by individuals with known hypersensitivity (allergy) to the medication.
* **Drug Interactions:**  Inform your doctor about all medications you are currently taking, including over-the-counter drugs and herbal supplements, as interactions are possible.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  The use of mebendazole during pregnancy and breastfeeding should be carefully considered and discussed with a doctor, as the benefits must outweigh potential risks.
* **Children and Older Adults:**  Children under 2 years old generally should not take mebendazole unless directed by a physician. Dosage adjustments may be necessary for older adults depending on their overall health.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long does it take to work?** A: The effects of mebendazole may not be immediately apparent.  It may take several days or even weeks to see a complete resolution of symptoms, as the worms need time to die and be expelled.
* **Q: Can I take mebendazole with other medications?** A:  Always inform your doctor or pharmacist about all medications you are taking to avoid potential interactions.
* **Q: How should I store mebendazole?** A: Store the medication in a cool, dry place, away from direct sunlight and moisture, and out of reach of children.
* **Q: What if I miss a dose?** A:  Consult your doctor or pharmacist for guidance on what to do if you miss a dose.  Do not double the dose without consulting a medical professional.
* **Q: Is mebendazole safe?** A:  Mebendazole is generally safe when used as directed, but it is crucial to follow your doctor's instructions carefully and report any adverse effects immediately.
