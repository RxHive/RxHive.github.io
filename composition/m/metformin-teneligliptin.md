# Metformin + Teneligliptin

**Generic Name: Metformin + Teneligliptin**

**Usage:**

Metformin + Teneligliptin is primarily used to treat type 2 diabetes mellitus in adults.  This combination medication works by addressing two key aspects of the disease. Metformin helps your body use insulin more effectively and reduces the amount of sugar your liver produces. Teneligliptin enhances the action of incretin hormones, which stimulate insulin release and suppress glucagon (a hormone that raises blood sugar).  This dual action leads to better blood sugar control than using either drug alone, making it particularly beneficial for individuals whose blood sugar isn't adequately managed with metformin alone.  There are currently no notable secondary uses approved for this combination therapy.


**Dosage:**

Dosage varies depending on individual response and other health factors. A doctor will determine the appropriate starting dose and adjust it as needed. Typically, treatment begins with a low dose, gradually increasing to reach the target blood sugar control.  This medication is usually available as a fixed-dose combination tablet; there are no syrup or injection formulations.  Children should not typically take this medication. Specific dosage instructions will always be provided by your prescribing physician based on your individual health needs and other factors.


**Side Effects:**

Common side effects often resolve on their own:

* Nausea
* Vomiting
* Diarrhea
* Stomach upset
* Metallic taste in the mouth
* Headache
* Dizziness
* Fatigue

Less common, but serious side effects require immediate medical attention:

* Severe stomach pain
* Lactic acidosis (a build-up of lactic acid in the blood – rare but serious)
* Allergic reactions (rash, itching, swelling)
* Low blood sugar (hypoglycemia) – particularly if combined with other diabetes medications or insufficient food intake

If you experience any concerning side effects, contact your doctor immediately.


**How it Works:**

Metformin and Teneligliptin work synergistically to lower blood sugar levels. Metformin reduces glucose production in the liver and improves insulin sensitivity in muscle and fat tissues. Teneligliptin increases the levels of incretin hormones like GLP-1, which stimulate insulin release from the pancreas when blood sugar is high and reduce glucagon secretion, thus helping to maintain balanced blood sugar levels.  Together, they create a more effective approach to managing type 2 diabetes than either drug alone.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** This medication is contraindicated in individuals with severe kidney disease, liver disease, heart failure, or a history of lactic acidosis.  It should also be avoided during pregnancy and breastfeeding.
* **Drug Interactions:** Many medications can interact with Metformin + Teneligliptin.  Always inform your doctor about all medications, supplements, and herbal remedies you are taking.  Specific interactions may necessitate dosage adjustments or alternative treatment options.
* **Vulnerable Groups:**  Older adults, individuals with impaired kidney or liver function, and those with certain medical conditions require careful monitoring while taking this medication. Pregnancy and breastfeeding are contraindicated.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: Can I stop taking this medication abruptly?**  A: No, always consult your doctor before stopping or changing your dosage.  Sudden cessation can lead to blood sugar fluctuations and other adverse effects.
* **Q: How should I store this medication?** A: Store in a cool, dry place, away from direct sunlight and moisture, as per the instructions on the label.
* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it's almost time for your next dose. Do not double the dose.
* **Q: Is this medication safe for long-term use?** A:  Long-term use is possible under appropriate medical supervision. Regular monitoring is necessary to detect any potential problems.
* **Q:  Can I drink alcohol while taking this medication?** A:  Excessive alcohol consumption can increase the risk of lactic acidosis.  Moderate alcohol consumption may be possible, but always consult your doctor for guidance.
