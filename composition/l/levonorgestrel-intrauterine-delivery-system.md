# Levonorgestrel intrauterine delivery system

**Generic Name:** Levonorgestrel Intrauterine Delivery System (LNG-IUS)

**Usage:**

The primary use of the levonorgestrel intrauterine delivery system (LNG-IUS) is long-term reversible contraception.  It's highly effective at preventing pregnancy for up to five years, sometimes longer depending on the specific device.  Beyond contraception, the LNG-IUS is also sometimes used to treat heavy menstrual bleeding (menorrhagia) associated with fibroids or other uterine conditions.  The reduction in bleeding is a significant benefit for many women. While it can improve some symptoms of endometriosis, it's not typically the first-line treatment for this condition.


**Dosage:**

The LNG-IUS is not administered in doses like a pill or injection.  It's a small, T-shaped device inserted into the uterus by a healthcare professional.  There's no daily or periodic dosage; the levonorgestrel is released continuously and slowly at a rate of approximately 20 micrograms per day.  The device itself remains in place for its approved duration (typically 5 years). There are no dosage adjustments based on age, weight, or severity of condition for its contraceptive use. For menorrhagia, the dosage is consistent across patients.


**Side Effects:**

* **Common:** Irregular bleeding (spotting, heavier bleeding initially, or lighter bleeding/amenorrhea over time), mood changes, acne, weight changes (gain or loss), breast tenderness.
* **Less Common but Serious:**  Pelvic inflammatory disease (PID), ectopic pregnancy (pregnancy outside the uterus – very rare), perforation of the uterus during insertion (rare), device expulsion.  These require immediate medical attention.
* **Important Note:** Any significant changes in bleeding patterns, severe abdominal pain, fever, or signs of infection should be reported to a healthcare provider immediately.


**How it Works:**

The LNG-IUS works primarily by thickening the cervical mucus, making it difficult for sperm to reach the egg.  It also thins the uterine lining (endometrium), making it less hospitable for implantation if fertilization occurs.  The small amount of levonorgestrel released directly into the uterus has an additional local effect on the endometrial tissue, contributing to the reduction in menstrual bleeding in some women.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:**  The LNG-IUS is generally not recommended for women with current pelvic inflammatory disease, unexplained vaginal bleeding, cervical cancer, or known hypersensitivity to levonorgestrel.
* **Interactions:**  There are no significant drug interactions known specifically for the LNG-IUS; however, it’s essential to inform your doctor of all medications you are taking.
* **Vulnerable Groups:**  Pregnant women should not use the LNG-IUS.  Women with a history of ectopic pregnancy, significant clotting disorders, or breast cancer should discuss the risks and benefits with their healthcare provider before use.

**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long does the LNG-IUS last?** A: Typically 5 years, although some devices may remain effective for longer.  Removal is necessary after 5 years or if you wish to become pregnant.
* **Q: Is the LNG-IUS safe?** A: The LNG-IUS is generally considered safe and effective; however, like all medical interventions, there are potential side effects.  Discuss any concerns with your healthcare provider.
* **Q: Can I get pregnant while using the LNG-IUS?** A:  While highly effective, no contraceptive method is 100% effective. Pregnancy is possible, although rare, with the LNG-IUS.
* **Q: What happens if the device is expelled?** A: If you suspect the device has been expelled (it may not always be noticeable), contact your healthcare provider immediately.  You will need a new device inserted and should use a backup method of contraception until confirmation of proper placement.
* **Q: How is the LNG-IUS inserted and removed?** A: The insertion and removal are performed by a healthcare professional in a clinic or doctor’s office.
