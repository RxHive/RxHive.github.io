# Lacidipine

**Generic Name: Lacidipine**

**Usage:**

Lacidipine is a calcium channel blocker primarily used to treat hypertension (high blood pressure).  Its main role is to help lower blood pressure, reducing the strain on the heart and blood vessels. This, in turn, helps decrease the risk of serious complications like heart attack, stroke, and kidney disease. While its primary use is in hypertension management, lacidipine might also be considered, under a doctor's strict supervision,  for the treatment of angina (chest pain due to reduced blood flow to the heart). However, it's crucial to understand that lacidipine is not a first-line treatment for angina and its use in this context is less common.  The decision to prescribe lacidipine for angina will depend heavily on individual patient factors and other health conditions.

**Dosage:**

Dosage of lacidipine varies depending on the individual's condition and response to treatment.  A healthcare professional will determine the appropriate dose. Typically, for adults with hypertension, the starting dose is usually low, often 2mg once daily, and can be gradually increased as needed, under medical guidance, up to a maximum of 8mg per day.  Lacidipine is usually available in tablet form.  It's crucial to take lacidipine exactly as prescribed by a doctor; do not adjust the dosage without consulting them.  There is no established pediatric use for lacidipine; it's not generally recommended for children.


**Side Effects:**

Common side effects of lacidipine can include:

* Headache
* Dizziness
* Flushing (redness of the skin)
* Swelling in ankles or feet
* Fatigue
* Nausea
* Palpitations

Less common but potentially serious side effects can include:

* Irregular heartbeat
* Low blood pressure (hypotension)
* Liver problems (rare)


**If you experience any concerning side effects, particularly those listed as serious or unusual, consult your doctor immediately.**


**How it Works:**

Lacidipine belongs to a class of drugs called dihydropyridine calcium channel blockers.  Calcium plays a crucial role in the contraction of heart muscle and blood vessel walls.  Lacidipine works by blocking the entry of calcium into these cells, causing the blood vessels to relax and widen (vasodilation). This vasodilation reduces resistance to blood flow, thus lowering blood pressure.  By reducing the workload on the heart, lacidipine helps improve blood flow and reduce the risk of cardiovascular events.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** Lacidipine is contraindicated in patients with severe hypotension, cardiogenic shock, or severe aortic stenosis.
* **Drug Interactions:** Lacidipine can interact with other medications, particularly those affecting the liver or affecting blood pressure.  Inform your doctor about all medications, supplements, and herbal remedies you are taking.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:** The use of lacidipine during pregnancy and breastfeeding requires careful evaluation of risks and benefits by a doctor.  It should generally only be used if the potential benefits outweigh the potential risks.
* **Elderly Patients:** Older adults may be more sensitive to the side effects of lacidipine, so careful monitoring is necessary.
* **Liver and Kidney Impairment:**  Patients with impaired liver or kidney function may require dosage adjustments.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: Can I stop taking lacidipine suddenly?** A: No, never stop taking lacidipine suddenly without consulting your doctor.  Sudden cessation can lead to a rebound increase in blood pressure.
* **Q: How should I store lacidipine?** A: Store lacidipine at room temperature, away from moisture and direct sunlight.
* **Q: Can I drink alcohol while taking lacidipine?** A:  Moderate alcohol consumption is generally acceptable, but excessive alcohol use can interact with lacidipine and increase the risk of low blood pressure.  It's best to discuss this with your doctor.
* **Q:  What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it is almost time for your next dose. Do not take a double dose to make up for a missed dose.
* **Q: Is lacidipine addictive?** A: No, lacidipine is not considered an addictive medication.
