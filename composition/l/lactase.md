# Lactase

**Generic Name: Lactase**

**Usage:**

Lactase is an enzyme that helps digest lactose, a sugar found in milk and dairy products.  Its primary medical use is in the treatment of lactose intolerance.  Lactose intolerance occurs when the body doesn't produce enough lactase to break down lactose effectively. This leads to symptoms like bloating, gas, diarrhea, and abdominal cramps after consuming dairy products.  Lactase supplements help alleviate these symptoms by providing the missing enzyme, allowing individuals to better digest lactose and enjoy dairy products without discomfort. While the primary use is managing symptoms of lactose intolerance, lactase can also be used to aid in the digestion of dairy in individuals with temporarily reduced lactase production or those experiencing mild gastrointestinal discomfort after dairy consumption.


**Dosage:**

Lactase dosage varies significantly depending on the individual's degree of lactose intolerance, the amount of dairy consumed, and the formulation of the lactase product.  There isn't a standardized dosage across all products. 

* **Oral formulations (tablets, capsules, liquids):**  Dosage instructions are usually printed on the product label and will specify the amount of lactase (often measured in FCC units or USP units) per dose and how many doses to take with a meal or dairy product.  Generally, adults start with a lower dose and adjust as needed, based on symptom relief. Children's dosages are significantly lower and should always follow the pediatrician's instructions or the product label specifically for children.
* **Liquid forms:** These are often added directly to milk or dairy products. The instructions will indicate how much liquid lactase to add per volume of dairy.

It's crucial to follow the specific dosage instructions provided with the purchased lactase product.  Always consult a healthcare professional for personalized dosage recommendations, particularly for children, pregnant women, and individuals with other health conditions.


**Side Effects:**

Lactase is generally considered safe, but some individuals may experience mild side effects.  These are usually related to the digestive process and include:

* Gas
* Bloating
* Abdominal discomfort
* Nausea

Serious side effects are rare. However, if you experience any severe or persistent side effects, discontinue use and consult a healthcare professional immediately.


**How it Works:**

Lactase works by breaking down lactose into simpler sugars, glucose and galactose. These simpler sugars are easily absorbed in the small intestine, preventing the undigested lactose from reaching the colon, where it ferments and causes the uncomfortable symptoms of lactose intolerance.  Essentially, lactase supplements provide the body with the enzyme it lacks, enabling proper lactose digestion.

**Precautions:**

* **Allergies:** While rare, some individuals may have allergies to lactase supplements. Monitor for any allergic reactions such as hives, rash, or swelling.
* **Interactions:** Lactase generally doesn't interact with other medications. However, it's always wise to inform your doctor or pharmacist about all medications and supplements you're taking.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  Generally considered safe, but consult your doctor before use during pregnancy or breastfeeding.
* **Children:**  Dosage must be adjusted according to age and weight and should always be determined by a pediatrician.
* **Underlying Conditions:** Individuals with any serious health conditions should consult their doctor before starting lactase supplementation.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long does it take to work?** A:  The effects of lactase are usually felt within a short time after consuming dairy along with the supplement, generally within 30 minutes to an hour.
* **Q: Can I take too much lactase?** A:  While unlikely to be harmful, taking significantly more lactase than recommended may lead to mild digestive discomfort.  Stick to the recommended dosage.
* **Q: How should I store lactase?** A:  Storage instructions vary based on the product. Usually, keeping the product in a cool, dry place, away from direct sunlight, is sufficient. Refer to the label for specific instructions.
* **Q: Can lactase cure lactose intolerance?** A:  No, lactase supplements manage the symptoms of lactose intolerance. It doesn't cure the underlying condition.
* **Q: Are there any long-term risks associated with lactase?** A:  No significant long-term risks are associated with lactase supplementation at recommended doses.
