# Levofloxacin + Nitazoxanide

**Generic Name:** Levofloxacin + Nitazoxanide

**Usage:**  This combination medication isn't currently a standard, approved formulation in most major pharmaceutical markets. Levofloxacin, a fluoroquinolone antibiotic, is primarily used to treat bacterial infections affecting the respiratory tract (pneumonia, bronchitis), urinary tract (cystitis, pyelonephritis), skin, and soft tissues.  It's also sometimes used for certain types of eye infections.

Nitazoxanide is an anti-parasitic drug primarily effective against various intestinal parasites, including *Cryptosporidium*, *Giardia*, and certain types of *Entamoeba*.

**Side Effects:**  Because this is not a standard combined drug, side effects would be those of each individual drug.

**Levofloxacin possible side effects:**

* Nausea, vomiting, diarrhea
* Headache
* Dizziness
* Insomnia
* Tendonitis (inflammation of the tendons)
* Tendon rupture (rare but serious)
* Allergic reactions (rash, itching, swelling)
* Photosensitivity (increased sensitivity to sunlight)
* QT interval prolongation (a heart rhythm abnormality)


**Nitazoxanide possible side effects:**

* Nausea, vomiting, diarrhea
* Headache
* Abdominal pain
* Taste disturbances
* Allergic reactions


**Serious, less common side effects:**  Any serious side effects for either drug require immediate medical attention.

**How it Works:** Levofloxacin works by inhibiting bacterial DNA replication and repair, ultimately killing the bacteria. Nitazoxanide's mechanism is more complex and not fully understood, but it's thought to interfere with the energy production of parasites, leading to their death.
