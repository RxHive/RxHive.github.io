# Levocetirizine + Dextromethorphan Hydrobromide

**Generic Name: Levocetirizine + Dextromethorphan Hydrobromide**

**Usage:**

This combination medication is primarily used to relieve symptoms associated with the common cold and other upper respiratory tract infections.  It effectively targets two key symptom groups:  allergic symptoms and cough.  Levocetirizine, an antihistamine, helps alleviate sneezing, runny nose, itchy eyes, and other allergic manifestations. Dextromethorphan hydrobromide, a cough suppressant, reduces the urge to cough.  While its primary use is for symptomatic relief of cold and flu symptoms, it's sometimes prescribed to manage similar symptoms stemming from other upper respiratory illnesses, provided the cough is not productive (i.e., doesn't bring up mucus).  It's crucial to understand that this medication treats symptoms, not the underlying cause of the illness.

**Dosage:**

Dosage varies depending on the specific formulation (tablet, syrup) and the patient's age and weight.  Always follow the instructions provided by your doctor or pharmacist, and refer to the medication packaging.  Generally:

* **Adults:**  A common adult dose might be one tablet or a specified amount of syrup, typically taken once or twice daily, as directed.
* **Children:**  Dosage for children is significantly lower and strictly weight-dependent.  Never administer adult doses to children. Pediatric formulations exist with appropriate concentrations for safe use.  Consult a pediatrician for the correct dosage for your child.


**Side Effects:**

Common side effects can include:

* Drowsiness
* Dry mouth
* Headache
* Dizziness
* Nausea
* Fatigue
* Constipation

Less common but potentially serious side effects can include allergic reactions (rash, itching, swelling, difficulty breathing), fast heartbeat, and seizures.  If you experience any unusual or concerning side effects, seek immediate medical attention.


**How it Works:**

Levocetirizine blocks the action of histamine, a substance released by the body during an allergic reaction.  By inhibiting histamine, it reduces inflammation and alleviates allergic symptoms. Dextromethorphan works in the brain to suppress the cough reflex. It doesn't affect the underlying cause of the cough, but it reduces the frequency and intensity of coughing fits.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:**  This medication is contraindicated for individuals with known hypersensitivity to levocetirizine or dextromethorphan.
* **Drug Interactions:**  Inform your doctor or pharmacist about all other medications you are taking, including over-the-counter drugs and herbal remedies.  Some interactions can occur, potentially leading to adverse effects.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  Use during pregnancy and breastfeeding should only be considered under strict medical supervision. The potential risks and benefits must be carefully weighed.
* **Elderly and Children:**  Dosage adjustments may be necessary for elderly individuals and children. Always follow the prescribed dosage for the specific age group.
* **Driving and Operating Machinery:**  This medication can cause drowsiness; therefore, avoid driving or operating machinery until you know how it affects you.
* **Alcohol Consumption:** Avoid alcohol consumption as it can exacerbate drowsiness.



**FAQs:**

* **Q: Can I take this medication with other cold and flu remedies?** A:  It's crucial to inform your doctor or pharmacist about all medications you are taking to avoid potential interactions.  Do not self-medicate.
* **Q: How long can I take this medication?** A:  Do not exceed the recommended duration of treatment.  Consult your doctor if symptoms persist or worsen.
* **Q: How should I store this medication?** A:  Store as directed on the product label, typically in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight and heat.
* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A:  Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it is nearly time for your next dose.  Do not double the dose.
* **Q: Is this medication addictive?** A:  Dextromethorphan can be abused, and prolonged or excessive use can lead to dependence.  Strictly adhere to the prescribed dosage and duration.
