# Live Attenuated Influenza Vaccine

**Generic Name:** Live Attenuated Influenza Vaccine

**Usage:**

The live attenuated influenza vaccine (LAIV) is primarily used to prevent influenza (flu).  Influenza is a viral respiratory illness that can cause mild to severe illness and, in some cases, lead to hospitalization or even death.  The vaccine works by stimulating the body's immune system to produce antibodies against the influenza viruses included in the vaccine.  This means that if you are exposed to those specific influenza strains later, your body will be better prepared to fight them off, reducing your risk of getting sick or experiencing severe symptoms. LAIV typically targets the strains of influenza A and B viruses that are expected to be most prevalent during a particular flu season. The specific strains included vary year to year.  There are no secondary uses currently approved for LAIV; it is solely for influenza prevention.


**Dosage:**

LAIV is administered as a nasal spray.  Dosage is determined by age:

* **Children:** A single dose is administered to children aged 2-4 years and a separate dose is recommended for children aged 5 years and above. It’s important to follow the physician’s recommendations.
* **Adults:** LAIV is generally not recommended for adults over the age of 49 because its effectiveness is reduced in this age group.

Dosage is usually a single dose per flu season, but consult a doctor for specific guidance.


**Side Effects:**

* **Common:** Runny nose, cough, sore throat, headache, muscle aches. These are usually mild and temporary.
* **Less Common but Serious:** Wheezing, severe allergic reaction (anaphylaxis).  Seek immediate medical attention if you experience these.

Any unusual or concerning side effects should be reported to a healthcare provider.


**How it Works:**

LAIV contains weakened (attenuated) live influenza viruses.  When sprayed into the nose, these viruses replicate in the nasal passages, stimulating an immune response. This response involves the production of antibodies and other immune cells that target and neutralize the influenza viruses. This process creates immunity without causing the flu.  The weakened viruses are not strong enough to cause illness in healthy individuals.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** LAIV should not be given to individuals with weakened immune systems (e.g., due to HIV/AIDS, cancer, or organ transplant), individuals with severe asthma or lung disease, individuals with certain chronic medical conditions or who are taking medication that could suppress immune function,  pregnant women, or anyone who has had Guillain-Barré syndrome. Children should be screened based on health conditions to determine suitability for the vaccination.
* **Drug Interactions:**  Inform your doctor of all medications you are taking, including over-the-counter drugs and herbal remedies, as some medications may interact with the vaccine.
* **Vulnerable Groups:**  Special precautions are necessary for pregnant women, children, and older adults, as mentioned above. Always consult a doctor before administering LAIV to anyone in these groups.  For children under 2 years, there is typically another formulation of the influenza vaccine, such as a shot, that would be preferred.

**FAQs:**

* **Q: Is LAIV effective?** A:  LAIV's effectiveness can vary from year to year depending on how well the vaccine strains match the circulating viruses.  However, it can still offer significant protection against the flu.
* **Q: Can I get the flu from LAIV?** A:  It's highly unlikely to get the flu from LAIV because the viruses are weakened.  Some mild flu-like symptoms might occur, but these are usually mild and short-lived.
* **Q: How is LAIV stored?** A:  LAIV must be stored and handled according to the manufacturer’s instructions, usually refrigerated.
* **Q:  What should I do if I experience a serious side effect?** A: Seek immediate medical attention.
