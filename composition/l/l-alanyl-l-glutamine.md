# L-alanyl-L-glutamine

**Generic Name: L-alanyl-L-glutamine**

**Usage:**

L-alanyl-L-glutamine is a dipeptide, a combination of two amino acids, L-alanine and L-glutamine.  Its primary medical use is in the nutritional support of individuals experiencing glutamine deficiency or those with conditions that impair their ability to synthesize or absorb sufficient glutamine.  This is particularly relevant in situations involving significant stress on the body, such as severe illness, injury (including burns), surgery, or critical illness.  Glutamine plays a vital role in gut health, immune function, and overall protein synthesis.  Therefore, L-alanyl-L-glutamine supplementation helps to improve these functions when they are compromised. While not a primary treatment for any specific disease, it's often used as an adjunctive therapy to support recovery and reduce complications in patients undergoing these stressful events.  Secondary uses might include supporting gut healing after gastrointestinal illnesses or supplementing nutritional intake in individuals with malabsorption syndromes.  It's crucial to understand that L-alanyl-L-glutamine is a supportive therapy, not a cure for underlying diseases.


**Dosage:**

Dosage of L-alanyl-L-glutamine varies significantly depending on the individual's needs, the underlying condition, and the specific formulation.  There isn't a universally accepted standard dosage.  The administration method (oral, intravenous) also affects the dosage.  Medical professionals determine the appropriate dose based on factors such as age, weight, severity of illness, and individual response.  Oral formulations are usually available as powders or capsules, while intravenous administration requires a prescription and specialized medical oversight.  **It is essential to follow the dosage instructions provided by your healthcare provider meticulously; never self-adjust the dosage.**  Information regarding specific dosage ranges for children and adults with particular conditions should be obtained from a physician or other qualified healthcare professional.


**Side Effects:**

* Nausea
* Vomiting
* Diarrhea
* Abdominal discomfort
* Headache
* Allergic reactions (rash, itching, swelling)

Less common, but potentially serious side effects may include significant electrolyte imbalances.  **If you experience any adverse effects, discontinue use and consult your healthcare provider immediately.**


**How it Works:**

L-alanyl-L-glutamine delivers glutamine to the body in a readily absorbable form.  Because free glutamine can be broken down in the gut, this dipeptide protects the glutamine molecule, allowing it to reach the intestines and other tissues intact. Glutamine is a crucial fuel source for cells lining the gut and supports the integrity of the gut barrier, preventing leakage and promoting healing. It's also essential for immune function, supporting the activity and growth of immune cells.  By replenishing glutamine levels, L-alanyl-L-glutamine helps to restore these vital functions, aiding recovery and reducing complications associated with illness or injury.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:**  Individuals with known allergies to L-alanyl-L-glutamine or its components should avoid using it.
* **Drug Interactions:**  While interactions are generally rare, inform your healthcare provider of all medications and supplements you are taking to prevent potential conflicts.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  The safety of L-alanyl-L-glutamine during pregnancy and breastfeeding has not been fully established.  Consult your doctor before using it if you are pregnant or breastfeeding.
* **Elderly and Children:**  Dosage adjustments may be necessary for elderly individuals and children.  A healthcare professional should always supervise the use in these populations.
