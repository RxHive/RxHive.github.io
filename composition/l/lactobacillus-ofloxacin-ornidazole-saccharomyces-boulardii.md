# Lactobacillus + Ofloxacin + Ornidazole + Saccharomyces boulardii

**Generic Name:** Lactobacillus + Ofloxacin + Ornidazole + *Saccharomyces boulardii*

**Usage:** This combination medication is typically used to treat a range of gastrointestinal infections, particularly those involving bacterial and protozoal pathogens alongside gut dysbiosis (imbalance of gut flora).  The primary use is in managing infectious diarrhea caused by bacteria susceptible to ofloxacin and ornidazole, and often complicated by gut flora disruption.  *Saccharomyces boulardii* helps restore the balance of beneficial bacteria in the gut, aiding recovery.  Secondary uses may include treating specific types of gastroenteritis (inflammation of the stomach and intestines) or traveller's diarrhea, though always under a doctor's supervision and after appropriate testing to confirm the causative agents. This combination isn't a first-line treatment for all diarrheal illnesses and its suitability depends on the specific diagnosis.


**Dosage:**  Dosage will be determined by a physician based on the individual's age, weight, severity of the infection, and overall health.  There isn't a standard, universally applicable dosage for this combination as products may vary in their formulation and the ratios of the components.  It's crucial to strictly follow a doctor's prescription.  Typically, the medication might be administered orally (tablets or capsules) or as a suspension for children, though injectable forms of individual components exist (but unlikely in this combination).  Specific instructions on frequency and duration of treatment will be provided by your healthcare provider.


**Side Effects:**

* **Common:** Nausea, vomiting, diarrhea (paradoxically, may initially worsen before improving), abdominal pain, headache, dizziness.  Changes in bowel movements are expected.
* **Less Common but Serious:**  Allergic reactions (rash, itching, swelling, difficulty breathing), *Clostridium difficile* infection (a severe gut infection), changes in liver function tests, prolonged QT interval (heart rhythm abnormality).  These require immediate medical attention.
* ***Saccharomyces boulardii* specific:** Though generally safe, *S. boulardii* can, rarely, cause fungemia (fungal infection in the bloodstream) in immunocompromised individuals.


**How it Works:**

Ofloxacin is a fluoroquinolone antibiotic effective against a broad spectrum of bacteria. Ornidazole is a nitroimidazole antibiotic active against various anaerobic bacteria and protozoa (single-celled organisms).  They work by targeting and destroying these microorganisms causing the infection. *Saccharomyces boulardii*, a probiotic yeast, helps restore the normal gut flora balance.  This is crucial as antibiotic treatment can disrupt this balance, potentially leading to persistent digestive issues. By reintroducing beneficial yeast, it aids in the re-establishment of a healthy gut environment, promoting healing and recovery. Lactobacillus, another probiotic, further contributes to this rebalancing process.

There may not be pre-made fixed-dose combinations readily available; individual components might be prescribed together instead.


**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:**  This combination should not be used by individuals with known hypersensitivity (allergy) to any of its components, or those with a history of QT prolongation or other significant heart conditions.
* **Drug Interactions:** Ofloxacin and ornidazole can interact with various medications, including anticoagulants, theophylline, and certain antacids.  Always inform your doctor about all medications you are taking.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:** Use during pregnancy and breastfeeding should be strictly under medical guidance, carefully weighing the potential benefits against the risks.
* **Children and Elderly:** Dosage adjustments may be necessary for children and elderly individuals based on their overall health and kidney/liver function.
* **Other Precautions:** Avoid alcohol consumption while on ornidazole.  Report any persistent or worsening symptoms to a healthcare provider promptly.

**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long should I take this medication?** A: The duration will be determined by your doctor based on your condition's response.
* **Q: Can I stop taking it once I feel better?** A: No, complete the prescribed course even if symptoms improve to prevent recurrence of the infection.
* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as possible unless it's almost time for the next dose. Don't double the dose.
* **Q: How should I store this medication?** A: Store it as directed on the label, typically in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight and moisture.
* **Q: Is this medication safe for everyone?** A: No, it has potential side effects and contraindications; it's essential to consult a healthcare provider before using it.
