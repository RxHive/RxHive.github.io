# Levetiracetam

**Generic Name: Levetiracetam**

**Usage:**

Levetiracetam is an anti-epileptic drug (AED), primarily used to treat epilepsy in both adults and children.  Its main use is as an add-on therapy for partial-onset seizures (seizures that begin in a specific area of the brain) and myoclonic seizures (brief, shock-like jerks).  It's also approved for use as monotherapy (the only medication used) in some cases of partial-onset seizures in adults.  In addition, levetiracetam may be used to treat certain types of seizures associated with Lennox-Gastaut syndrome, a severe form of childhood epilepsy. While not its primary purpose, it may sometimes be used to treat other neurological conditions under specific circumstances and guidance from a healthcare professional.  The specific application and efficacy vary based on individual needs and response.


**Dosage:**

Dosage of levetiracetam varies greatly depending on the patient's age, weight, condition severity, and the form of medication.  It's crucial to follow the precise instructions provided by a doctor or pharmacist.  Generally:

* **Adults:**  Dosage usually starts low and is gradually increased. Common starting doses are in the range of 500mg to 1000mg twice daily, potentially reaching much higher amounts as determined by the physician.
* **Children:**  Dosage is carefully calculated based on weight and is generally lower than adult doses.  Levetiracetam is available in liquid (syrup) forms for easier administration in children.
* **Forms:** Levetiracetam comes in tablets, dispersible tablets (that dissolve in water), and oral solution (syrup) for convenient administration.  Intravenous (IV) forms exist for use in hospitals when oral administration is impossible.

**It is vital to consult a healthcare professional for accurate dosage information specific to individual needs.**  Never adjust the dosage without explicit guidance from a medical professional.


**Side Effects:**

* **Common:**  Drowsiness, dizziness, fatigue, headache, nausea, and weakness. These are often mild and may subside as the body adjusts to the medication.
* **Less Common but Serious:**  Serious allergic reactions (rash, hives, difficulty breathing), suicidal thoughts or behavior, depression, aggression, and changes in mood or behavior.
* **Note:** This is not an exhaustive list.  If you experience any adverse effects, especially those that are severe or unexpected, immediately contact your doctor or seek medical assistance.


**How it Works:**

Levetiracetam's exact mechanism of action isn't fully understood, but it's believed to involve interacting with synaptic vesicle protein 2A (SV2A), a protein found in nerve cells.  By affecting SV2A, levetiracetam is thought to modulate the release of neurotransmitters, chemicals that transmit signals between nerve cells.  This helps to stabilize the electrical activity in the brain and reduce the likelihood of seizures.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:**  Levetiracetam is generally well-tolerated, but individuals with a known hypersensitivity (allergy) to levetiracetam should avoid it.
* **Drug Interactions:**  Levetiracetam may interact with other medications, including other AEDs. Always inform your doctor or pharmacist about all medications (prescription and over-the-counter), herbal supplements, or vitamins you are taking.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  The use of levetiracetam during pregnancy and breastfeeding should be carefully considered and discussed with a doctor, weighing the benefits against potential risks.
* **Older Adults and Children:**  Dosage adjustments might be needed for older adults and children due to differences in metabolism and body composition.
* **Driving and Operating Machinery:**  Drowsiness is a common side effect. Avoid driving or operating heavy machinery if feeling drowsy or dizzy.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: Can I stop taking levetiracetam suddenly?**  A: No.  Stopping levetiracetam abruptly can lead to seizures. Always follow your doctor's instructions for tapering off the medication gradually.
* **Q: How should I store levetiracetam?** A: Store it at room temperature, away from moisture and direct sunlight.
* **Q: Is levetiracetam habit-forming?** A:  Levetiracetam is not considered a habit-forming drug in the same way as opioids or other substances of abuse. However, it's essential to follow the prescribed dosage and not discontinue it without consulting a doctor.
* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it's almost time for your next dose.  Never double the dose.
