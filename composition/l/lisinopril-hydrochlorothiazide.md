# Lisinopril + Hydrochlorothiazide

**Generic Name:** Lisinopril + Hydrochlorothiazide

**Usage:**

Lisinopril + Hydrochlorothiazide is a combination medication used primarily to treat high blood pressure (hypertension).  It works by combining two different types of blood pressure-lowering drugs:  lisinopril, an ACE inhibitor, and hydrochlorothiazide, a thiazide diuretic.  This combination often provides a more effective blood pressure reduction than either drug alone.  While primarily used for hypertension, it may also be prescribed for some patients with heart failure to reduce fluid retention and improve heart function.  The decision to prescribe this combination is made on a case-by-case basis by a healthcare professional considering individual patient needs and health conditions.

**Dosage:**

Dosage is determined by a doctor based on individual health status and response to treatment.  It's crucial to follow prescribed instructions exactly.  There is no standardized pediatric dosage; this medication is typically not used in children.  Common adult dosages are available in tablet form and range from 10/12.5 mg to 40/25 mg once daily.  The specific dose will be tailored by your doctor based on factors like blood pressure readings and other health conditions.  Never adjust your dosage without consulting your physician.

**Side Effects:**

* **Common:** Dizziness, lightheadedness, fainting, dry cough, fatigue, headache, muscle cramps, nausea, diarrhea, constipation
* **Less Common but Serious:**  Swelling of the face, lips, tongue, or throat (angioedema),  irregular heartbeat, rapid heartbeat,  kidney problems (indicated by changes in urine output or blood tests),  low blood pressure, high potassium levels (hyperkalemia)
* **Important Note:**  If you experience any of these side effects, especially angioedema, stop taking the medication and seek immediate medical attention.

**How it Works:**

Lisinopril works by blocking the action of an enzyme called angiotensin-converting enzyme (ACE).  This enzyme plays a role in narrowing blood vessels. By inhibiting ACE, lisinopril helps relax and widen blood vessels, reducing blood pressure.  Hydrochlorothiazide works as a diuretic, increasing the amount of salt and water excreted in the urine.  This reduction in fluid volume also contributes to lowering blood pressure.  The combination of these two actions provides a synergistic effect, leading to more effective blood pressure control.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** This medication is not suitable for people with certain conditions, including angioedema history, severe kidney disease, or severe liver disease.  Pregnancy and breastfeeding are also contraindications.
* **Drug Interactions:** Many medications can interact with Lisinopril + Hydrochlorothiazide.  Inform your doctor about all medications, supplements, and herbal remedies you are taking.  This is particularly important for potassium-sparing diuretics, potassium supplements, and nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drugs (NSAIDs).
* **Specific Warnings:** Older adults, patients with diabetes, and those with impaired kidney function require careful monitoring while taking this medication.  Regular blood pressure and kidney function checks are important.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: Can I stop taking this medication suddenly?**  A: No, abruptly stopping can lead to a sudden rise in blood pressure.  Always taper off under your doctor's guidance.

* **Q:  Can I drink alcohol while taking this medication?** A: Moderate alcohol consumption may be permissible, but it’s best to discuss this with your doctor.  Excessive alcohol intake can lower blood pressure further, potentially leading to adverse effects.

* **Q: How should I store this medication?** A: Store at room temperature, away from moisture and direct sunlight. Keep out of reach of children.

* **Q: What if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it's almost time for the next dose.  Do not double the dose.

* **Q: Is this medication safe for long-term use?** A:  Long-term use is often necessary for blood pressure control. Your doctor will monitor your progress and adjust treatment as needed.
