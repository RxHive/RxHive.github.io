# Lubiprostone

**Generic Name: Lubiprostone**

**Usage:**

Lubiprostone is primarily used to treat chronic idiopathic constipation (CIC) in adults.  This means it helps relieve constipation that isn't caused by any other known medical condition.  It's also approved for treating irritable bowel syndrome with constipation (IBS-C) in adults.  In both cases, lubiprostone works by increasing the movement of fluid into the intestines, softening the stool and making bowel movements easier. While its primary use is for constipation-related conditions, its mechanism of action might have implications for other gastrointestinal disorders, though further research is needed in those areas.

**Dosage:**

Lubiprostone is typically available as capsules.  The usual adult dose for both CIC and IBS-C is 24 mcg twice daily, taken approximately 12 hours apart.  It's crucial to take lubiprostone exactly as prescribed by your doctor.  There is currently no approved pediatric formulation; hence there's no established dosage for children.  Adjustments to the dosage are not typically made based on weight or severity of the condition, but a healthcare professional should always be consulted if concerns arise.


**Side Effects:**

* **Common:** Nausea, diarrhea, headache, abdominal pain.  These are usually mild and often improve as the body adjusts to the medication.
* **Less Common, but Serious:** Dehydration (due to diarrhea), severe abdominal pain.  These require immediate medical attention.
* **Important Note:** If you experience any concerning side effects, stop taking lubiprostone and contact your doctor immediately.


**How it Works:**

Lubiprostone works by activating chloride channels in the gut.  This action increases the secretion of chloride ions and water into the intestines. The resulting increase in fluid softens the stool and stimulates bowel movements, thus relieving constipation.  It acts locally in the gastrointestinal tract and does not affect other bodily systems to a significant degree.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** Lubiprostone should not be used by individuals with a known hypersensitivity (allergy) to the medication.
* **Drug Interactions:**  While significant drug interactions are not commonly reported, it is advisable to inform your doctor of all medications, including over-the-counter drugs and herbal supplements, you are currently taking.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  The safety of lubiprostone during pregnancy and breastfeeding hasn't been fully established. Consult your doctor before using this medication if you are pregnant, breastfeeding, or planning to become pregnant.
* **Older Adults:**  Older adults might be more susceptible to side effects, particularly dehydration.  Careful monitoring is advisable.
* **Children:** There is no approved use of lubiprostone in children.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long does it take to work?** A:  The effects of lubiprostone can vary, but some improvement in bowel movements might be noticed within a few days to a couple of weeks of starting treatment.
* **Q: Can I take it with food?** A:  Lubiprostone can be taken with or without food.
* **Q: How should I store it?** A: Store lubiprostone capsules at room temperature, away from moisture and direct sunlight.
* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A:  Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it's almost time for the next dose. Do not take two doses at once.
* **Q: Can I stop taking it suddenly?** A:  It's best not to stop taking lubiprostone suddenly.  Consult your doctor before stopping treatment.  A gradual reduction might be recommended.
