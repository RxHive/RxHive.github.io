# Lactobacillus Salivarius + Lactobacillus Brevis + Lactobacillus Plantarum

**Generic Name:** Lactobacillus Salivarius + Lactobacillus Brevis + Lactobacillus Plantarum

**Usage:**

This combination of probiotic strains, *Lactobacillus salivarius*, *Lactobacillus brevis*, and *Lactobacillus plantarum*, is primarily used to support gut health.  Its beneficial effects stem from the bacteria's ability to colonize the intestines, competing with harmful bacteria and potentially restoring a healthy gut microbiome.  This can be helpful in several conditions, though evidence supporting specific uses varies in strength.  

Primary Use:  Improving digestive health. This includes alleviating symptoms associated with mild to moderate diarrhea, bloating, gas, and constipation.  

Secondary Uses:  Research suggests potential benefits in other areas, such as strengthening the immune system, improving lactose tolerance in some individuals, and possibly playing a role in preventing certain infections. However, more robust clinical trials are needed to definitively establish these secondary uses.  It's crucial to remember that probiotics are not a replacement for medical treatment of underlying conditions.


**Dosage:**

Dosage varies greatly depending on the specific product formulation, age, and the condition being treated.  There isn't a universally standardized dosage for this combination.  Always follow the instructions on the product label, or consult a healthcare professional or registered dietitian for personalized guidance.  Product forms can include capsules, tablets, powders for mixing with liquids, or yogurt-based products.


**Side Effects:**

* **Common:**  Mild gastrointestinal discomfort (e.g., gas, bloating) – usually temporary and subsiding as the body adjusts.
* **Less Common:** Allergic reactions (rash, itching, swelling).  In rare cases, individuals with compromised immune systems might experience more serious complications, although this is uncommon with these specific Lactobacillus strains.


**How it Works:**

These Lactobacillus strains are beneficial bacteria that, when ingested in sufficient numbers, can reach the intestines alive.  Once there, they establish themselves, competing with potentially harmful bacteria for resources and space.  This competition helps maintain a balance in the gut microbiome, a crucial factor in digestion and overall health.  Some strains also produce substances that inhibit the growth of harmful bacteria or enhance the gut barrier function. The improvement in gut microbial balance leads to a reduction in symptoms like diarrhea, gas, and bloating.



**Popular Brand Names (India):**

Specific brand names containing this probiotic combination vary widely and are not consistently standardized.  Check the label for the specific strains listed to ensure it contains *Lactobacillus salivarius*, *Lactobacillus brevis*, and *Lactobacillus plantarum*.


**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:**  Generally well-tolerated, but individuals with severe immune deficiencies or those with conditions where bacteria could cause serious problems should consult their doctor before taking probiotics.
* **Drug Interactions:**  Probiotics may interact with some medications, particularly those that suppress the immune system. Consult a physician or pharmacist before using this product if you are taking other medications.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  While generally considered safe, pregnant or breastfeeding women should consult a healthcare professional before using this product.
* **Children:**  Dosage for children should be determined by a healthcare professional based on age and weight.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: Can I take this probiotic with antibiotics?** A:  It's best to space out the use of probiotics and antibiotics by a few hours to avoid interference. Consult your doctor or pharmacist.

* **Q: How should I store this probiotic?** A: Follow the storage instructions on the product label, which typically involves keeping it in a cool, dry place.

* **Q:  What if I experience side effects?** A: If you experience any severe or persistent side effects, discontinue use and consult a healthcare professional.  Mild side effects are often temporary.

* **Q: Are these probiotics effective for everyone?** A: The effectiveness of probiotics varies from person to person. Individual responses to probiotics can differ.
