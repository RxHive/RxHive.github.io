# L-Ornithine L-Aspartate

**Generic Name: L-Ornithine L-Aspartate**

**Usage:**

L-Ornithine L-Aspartate (LOLA) is primarily used to support liver function and reduce the buildup of ammonia in the blood (hyperammonemia).  This is particularly beneficial for individuals with liver diseases like cirrhosis and hepatic encephalopathy (a brain disorder caused by liver failure).  While not a cure for these conditions, LOLA helps manage symptoms and improve overall well-being by assisting the liver in its detoxification processes.  It's also sometimes explored in the context of improving athletic recovery and reducing fatigue, though research in these areas is less conclusive.  The primary use remains focused on liver support.

**Dosage:**

Dosage varies significantly depending on the condition being treated, the patient's age, weight, and overall health.  A healthcare professional will determine the appropriate dose.  Generally, LOLA is available in tablet or oral solution form.  There isn't a standardized pediatric dosage, and its use in children is usually under strict medical supervision.  Injectable forms exist but are typically administered in a hospital setting.  Always follow the instructions provided by your doctor or pharmacist carefully.  Self-adjusting dosages is strongly discouraged.

**Side Effects:**

* **Common:** Nausea, diarrhea, mild stomach upset. These are usually mild and transient.
* **Less Common but Serious:**  Allergic reactions (rash, itching, swelling, difficulty breathing). While rare, these require immediate medical attention.
* **Other Possible Side Effects:** Headache, dizziness.

If you experience any side effects, particularly those listed as less common but serious, consult your doctor or pharmacist immediately.

**How it Works:**

LOLA consists of two amino acids, ornithine and aspartate.  These amino acids play crucial roles in the urea cycle, a vital process in the liver that removes ammonia, a toxic byproduct of protein metabolism.  By supporting the urea cycle, LOLA helps the liver eliminate excess ammonia, reducing its accumulation in the bloodstream. This, in turn, helps alleviate symptoms associated with hyperammonemia, such as confusion, lethargy, and even coma in severe cases.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** LOLA is generally well-tolerated, but individuals with specific kidney or liver conditions might require modified dosages or may not be suitable candidates.  Pre-existing conditions should always be disclosed to your doctor.
* **Drug Interactions:**  While not extensively documented, interactions with other medications are possible.  Inform your healthcare provider about all medications, supplements, and herbal remedies you are taking.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  The safety of LOLA during pregnancy and breastfeeding is not fully established.  Consult your doctor before using this medication if you are pregnant, breastfeeding, or planning to become pregnant.
* **Elderly:** Older adults may require dosage adjustments due to potential changes in liver and kidney function.
