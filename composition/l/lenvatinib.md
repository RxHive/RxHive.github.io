# Lenvatinib

**Generic Name: Lenvatinib**

**Usage:**

Lenvatinib is a targeted therapy primarily used to treat certain types of cancer.  Its main application is in the management of differentiated thyroid cancer (DTC), particularly those that have progressed or spread (metastatic) after other treatments have been tried.  It's also used in treating renal cell carcinoma (RCC), a type of kidney cancer, and hepatocellular carcinoma (HCC), a type of liver cancer, often in combination with other therapies.  In some cases, lenvatinib may be used for other types of cancers, but these uses are often considered off-label or part of ongoing clinical trials.  The specific use and efficacy of lenvatinib depend greatly on the type and stage of the cancer, as well as the patient's overall health.  Always consult with an oncologist to determine the suitability of lenvatinib for a particular case.


**Dosage:**

Lenvatinib is typically administered orally as a capsule.  Dosage varies greatly depending on the specific cancer being treated, the patient's overall health, and other factors.  A physician will determine the appropriate dosage based on these individual factors.  There is no standardized pediatric dosage, as lenvatinib is primarily used in adult populations.  Adjustments to the dosage may be necessary based on side effects or other medical concerns.  It's crucial to follow the prescribed dosage and administration instructions carefully, as provided by your doctor or pharmacist.  Never alter the dosage without consulting your healthcare provider.


**Side Effects:**

Common side effects of lenvatinib can include:

* High blood pressure (hypertension)
* Diarrhea
* Fatigue
* Loss of appetite
* Weight loss
* Nausea
* Vomiting
* Hair thinning
* Hand-foot syndrome (pain, swelling, redness in the palms and soles)
* Decreased white blood cell count (neutropenia)


Less common but serious side effects can include:

* Severe hypertension requiring hospitalization
* Severe diarrhea
* Bleeding
* Heart problems
* Liver problems
* Severe allergic reactions
* Blood clots


If you experience any of these side effects, especially if they are severe or persistent, seek immediate medical attention.


**How it Works:**

Lenvatinib is a tyrosine kinase inhibitor (TKI).  This means it works by blocking the activity of certain enzymes called tyrosine kinases, which are involved in the growth and spread of cancer cells.  By inhibiting these enzymes, lenvatinib can help slow down or stop the growth of cancer cells, thereby controlling the disease.  The specific tyrosine kinases targeted by lenvatinib are those involved in several pathways that contribute to cancer cell proliferation and angiogenesis (the formation of new blood vessels that feed tumors).

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** Lenvatinib should not be taken by individuals with certain pre-existing conditions. These contraindications should be discussed with a physician before starting treatment.
* **Drug Interactions:**  Lenvatinib can interact with other medications, including some common drugs.  It's vital to inform your doctor about all medications you are taking, including over-the-counter drugs, supplements, and herbal remedies.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  Lenvatinib is not recommended for pregnant or breastfeeding women.  Effective contraception should be used during treatment and for a period after treatment completion.
* **Elderly Patients:**  Older adults may be more susceptible to side effects and require dosage adjustments.  Careful monitoring is necessary.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long do I need to take lenvatinib?** A: The duration of treatment depends on your individual response to the medication and the severity of your condition.  Your doctor will determine the appropriate treatment duration.

* **Q: How should I store lenvatinib?** A: Store lenvatinib capsules in a cool, dry place, away from direct sunlight and moisture.

* **Q: Can I drink alcohol while taking lenvatinib?** A:  It's best to avoid or limit alcohol consumption while taking lenvatinib, as it can increase the risk of certain side effects.  Discuss this with your physician.

* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it's almost time for your next dose.  Never take a double dose to make up for a missed one.
