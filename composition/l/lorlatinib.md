# Lorlatinib

**Generic Name: Lorlatinib**

**Usage:**

Lorlatinib is a targeted therapy primarily used to treat certain types of non-small cell lung cancer (NSCLC).  Its primary use is in patients with advanced or metastatic NSCLC that has a specific genetic alteration – a rearrangement or mutation in the ALK gene.  These alterations drive the growth and spread of the cancer. Lorlatinib is typically used after other ALK inhibitors have been tried and haven't worked well enough, or if the cancer has developed resistance to those other medications.  While its primary application is in ALK-positive NSCLC, it's also sometimes investigated or used in clinical trials for other types of cancer involving ALK or related gene mutations.  However, it's crucial to understand that this medication is a prescription drug and its use should be guided solely by a qualified oncologist.


**Dosage:**

The dosage of lorlatinib is determined by a healthcare professional and varies depending on individual factors.  It's typically administered orally as a tablet.  The standard adult dose is usually once daily. Specific dosage adjustments might be needed based on factors like liver or kidney function,  which the doctor will carefully evaluate.  There is no established pediatric dosage;  its use in children is limited to clinical trials and would be under strict medical supervision.  It's essential to follow the prescribed dosage precisely; never adjust the dosage without consulting your doctor.


**Side Effects:**

Lorlatinib, like many cancer medications, can cause side effects.  Some common side effects include:

*   Nausea
*   Vomiting
*   Diarrhea
*   Constipation
*   Fatigue
*   Headache
*   Increased liver enzymes (detected through blood tests)
*   Weight gain
*   Increased cholesterol levels

Less common, but serious side effects can occur and require immediate medical attention. These include:

*   Severe liver damage
*   Lung problems (pneumonitis)
*   Severe allergic reactions
*   Heart problems
*   Neurological issues (such as seizures or vision changes)


If you experience any concerning side effects, contact your healthcare provider immediately.


**How it Works:**

Lorlatinib is a type of tyrosine kinase inhibitor (TKI).  ALK-positive NSCLC cells rely on the ALK protein to grow and divide uncontrollably. Lorlatinib works by specifically blocking the activity of the ALK protein. By inhibiting this protein, the drug interferes with the cancer cells' ability to grow and spread, leading to tumor shrinkage or slowing of disease progression.

**Precautions:**

*   **Contraindications:** Lorlatinib shouldn't be taken by individuals with known hypersensitivity (severe allergic reaction) to lorlatinib or any of its components.
*   **Drug Interactions:**  Lorlatinib can interact with several other medications.  It's crucial to inform your doctor about all medications, supplements, and herbal remedies you are taking.
*   **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  Lorlatinib is not recommended during pregnancy due to potential harm to the developing fetus. Women of childbearing age should use effective contraception while taking this medication. Breastfeeding is generally advised against while using lorlatinib.
*   **Elderly Patients:**  Older adults might be more susceptible to some side effects.  Careful monitoring and dose adjustments may be needed.
*   **Liver and Kidney Function:**  People with impaired liver or kidney function require close monitoring and potentially adjusted dosages to minimize the risk of side effects.
