# Loratadine

**Generic Name: Loratadine**

**Usage:**

Loratadine is an antihistamine primarily used to relieve symptoms associated with allergies.  Its main use is in treating allergic rhinitis (hay fever), which manifests as sneezing, runny nose, itchy eyes, and nasal congestion.  It's also effective in managing urticaria (hives), a skin condition characterized by itchy welts. While not its primary indication, loratadine can sometimes provide relief from itching associated with other skin conditions.  It's important to remember that loratadine treats symptoms, not the underlying cause of the allergy.

**Dosage:**

Dosage varies depending on the patient's age and the specific formulation of loratadine.  Always follow the directions on your prescription label or the product packaging.

* **Adults (12 years and older):**  The typical dose is 10 mg once daily. This is usually administered orally as a tablet or in syrup form.
* **Children (6-11 years):**  The dosage is typically 5-10 mg once daily, depending on the child's weight and the doctor's recommendation.  Syrup formulations are usually preferred for this age group.
* **Children under 6 years:**  Loratadine is generally not recommended for children under 6 years old without explicit medical guidance.  Always consult a pediatrician before administering any medication to young children.

Different formulations (tablets, syrup) may have slightly different absorption rates, but the overall effect remains similar.  It's crucial to take the medication as directed by a healthcare professional.


**Side Effects:**

Loratadine is generally well-tolerated, but some individuals may experience side effects, including:

* Drowsiness (though this is less common than with some other antihistamines)
* Dry mouth
* Headache
* Fatigue
* Nausea
* Stomach upset

Less common but more serious side effects are rare but can include:

* Fast heartbeat
* Seizures
* Allergic reactions (rash, itching, swelling)

If you experience any concerning side effects, stop taking loratadine and contact your doctor or pharmacist immediately.


**How it Works:**

Loratadine is a selective peripheral H1-receptor antagonist.  In simpler terms, it blocks the action of histamine, a chemical released by the body during an allergic reaction. Histamine causes the symptoms of allergies, like itching, sneezing, and runny nose. By blocking histamine's effects, loratadine helps alleviate these symptoms.

**Precautions:**

* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  Consult your doctor before taking loratadine if you are pregnant, breastfeeding, or planning to become pregnant.
* **Liver or Kidney Disease:**  People with liver or kidney problems may need a lower dose or more careful monitoring.
* **Drug Interactions:**  Inform your doctor or pharmacist of all other medications you are taking, including over-the-counter drugs and herbal supplements, as loratadine may interact with some medications.
* **Driving and Operating Machinery:** While drowsiness is less common with loratadine than with some other antihistamines, individuals should be aware of potential drowsiness and avoid activities requiring alertness if affected.
* **Alcohol:** Combining loratadine with alcohol may increase the risk of drowsiness.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: Can I take loratadine with other allergy medications?** A:  Consult your doctor or pharmacist before combining loratadine with other allergy medications to avoid potential interactions.
* **Q: How should I store loratadine?** A: Store loratadine in a cool, dry place, away from direct sunlight and moisture. Keep it out of reach of children.
* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it is almost time for the next dose. Do not double the dose.
* **Q: Is loratadine habit-forming?** A: No, loratadine is not habit-forming.
* **Q:  How long does it take to work?** A: Loratadine typically starts working within 30 minutes to an hour, providing relief for several hours.
