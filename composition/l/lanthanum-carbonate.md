# Lanthanum Carbonate

**Generic Name:** Lanthanum Carbonate

**Usage:**

Lanthanum carbonate's primary medical use is in the management of hyperphosphatemia, a condition characterized by excessively high levels of phosphate in the blood.  This is particularly important in patients with chronic kidney disease (CKD) who have lost the kidney's ability to effectively remove phosphate from the bloodstream.  High phosphate levels in CKD can lead to serious complications such as bone disease, cardiovascular problems, and increased risk of mortality.  Lanthanum carbonate works by binding to phosphate in the digestive tract, preventing its absorption into the bloodstream and thus lowering phosphate levels. While its primary use is in CKD-related hyperphosphatemia, it may also be considered in other conditions where phosphate control is necessary, although this is less common.


**Dosage:**

Dosage of lanthanum carbonate is highly individualized and depends on factors like the patient's serum phosphate levels, overall health, and response to treatment.  It's crucial to follow a doctor's specific instructions.  The medication is typically available as tablets.  Dosage is usually titrated to achieve the target phosphate level.  There are no specific pediatric formulations widely available, making dosage adjustments in children largely based on weight and clinical response under close medical supervision.  Always administer lanthanum carbonate with food to improve absorption and reduce potential gastrointestinal side effects.


**Side Effects:**

* **Common:** Nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, constipation, abdominal pain.
* **Less Common but Serious:**  Intestinal obstruction (though rare), allergic reactions (rash, itching, swelling, difficulty breathing).

**Note:** This is not an exhaustive list.  Any new or worsening symptoms should be reported to a healthcare professional immediately.


**How it Works:**

Lanthanum carbonate is a phosphate binder.  This means it works in the gastrointestinal tract by binding to phosphate ions present in food and preventing their absorption into the bloodstream.  The bound phosphate is then eliminated from the body through the stool.  This mechanism effectively reduces the amount of phosphate circulating in the blood, thereby managing hyperphosphatemia.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** Lanthanum carbonate is generally not recommended for individuals with known hypersensitivity or allergy to lanthanum or any of its components.
* **Drug Interactions:** Lanthanum carbonate can interact with other medications. Consult your doctor or pharmacist about potential interactions with other drugs you are taking, especially those absorbed in the gastrointestinal tract.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  The use of lanthanum carbonate during pregnancy and breastfeeding should be carefully considered and discussed with a healthcare provider to weigh potential risks and benefits.  
* **Older Adults and Children:**  Dosage adjustments may be necessary in older adults and children due to potential variations in renal function and overall health. Close monitoring is required.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long does it take to see results?** A:  The effects of lanthanum carbonate on serum phosphate levels may not be immediately apparent.  It usually takes several weeks for the full therapeutic effect to be observed.  Regular monitoring of phosphate levels is necessary.
* **Q: Can I stop taking lanthanum carbonate suddenly?** A: No.  Stopping abruptly can lead to a rapid increase in serum phosphate levels.  Always follow your doctor's instructions for discontinuation, which usually involves a gradual tapering of the dose.
* **Q: How should I store lanthanum carbonate?** A: Store it as directed on the label, usually in a cool, dry place away from moisture and direct sunlight. Keep it out of reach of children.
* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it is close to the time for your next dose. Do not double the dose to make up for a missed one.  Consult your doctor if you have concerns about missed doses.
