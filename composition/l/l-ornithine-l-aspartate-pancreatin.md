# L-Ornithine L-Aspartate + Pancreatin

**Generic Name:** L-Ornithine L-Aspartate + Pancreatin

**Usage:**

This combination medication is primarily used to support liver function and aid in digestion.  L-ornithine L-aspartate (LOLA) is believed to help the liver process ammonia, a byproduct of protein metabolism that can build up to harmful levels in individuals with liver disease.  Pancreatin is a digestive enzyme supplement that contains amylase, lipase, and protease. These enzymes assist in the breakdown of carbohydrates, fats, and proteins, respectively, improving digestion and nutrient absorption.  

The primary use is in the management of hepatic encephalopathy (a brain disorder due to liver failure), often used in conjunction with other treatments. It may also be used to support liver recovery after acute liver injury or in chronic liver conditions. The addition of pancreatin is beneficial for patients with impaired pancreatic function who also have liver problems, as it helps alleviate digestive issues commonly associated with these conditions.  Secondary uses may include improving nutrient absorption in individuals with malabsorption syndromes or pancreatic insufficiency (though this would usually involve a higher dose of pancreatin than typically found in a combination product).  It's crucial to understand that this combination is supportive therapy and does not cure underlying liver or pancreatic conditions.

**Dosage:**

Dosage varies considerably depending on the specific product, the indication, and the patient's condition.  There's no universally standardized dosage. A healthcare professional will determine the appropriate dose based on individual needs.  The medication may be available in tablet or capsule form.  It's usually taken orally with or after meals.  **Never attempt self-medication or alter dosages without consulting a doctor.**  Information regarding dosages for children is not readily available for this specific combination and should only be determined by a pediatrician.


**Side Effects:**

* **Common:** Nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, abdominal discomfort.
* **Less Common but Serious:** Allergic reactions (rash, itching, swelling, difficulty breathing), pancreatitis (inflammation of the pancreas – requires immediate medical attention).
* **Note:** This is not an exhaustive list.  Any unusual or concerning side effects should be reported to a doctor immediately.


**How it Works:**

L-ornithine L-aspartate facilitates the conversion of ammonia to urea in the liver. Urea is less toxic and is excreted by the kidneys.  By reducing ammonia levels, it helps improve brain function in patients with hepatic encephalopathy. Pancreatin supplements the body's own digestive enzymes, enabling more efficient breakdown and absorption of nutrients from food, easing digestive stress on the already compromised liver and preventing malnutrition.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:**  This combination should be avoided in individuals with known allergies to any of its components.  It should be used with caution in patients with pancreatitis or severe kidney disease.
* **Drug Interactions:**  Potential interactions exist with other medications. Always inform your doctor about all medications, supplements, and herbal remedies you are taking.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  The safety of this combination during pregnancy and breastfeeding is not fully established.  Consult a healthcare professional before use.
* **Elderly and Children:**  Dosage adjustments may be necessary for elderly patients and children. Always follow the advice of a healthcare provider.

* **Q: How should I store this medication?** A: Store as directed on the product label, typically in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight and moisture.
