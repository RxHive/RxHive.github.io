# Leflunomide

**Generic Name: Leflunomide**

**Usage:**

Leflunomide is primarily used to treat **rheumatoid arthritis (RA)**, a chronic autoimmune disease causing joint inflammation and pain.  It's classified as a disease-modifying antirheumatic drug (DMARD) and helps slow the progression of RA, reducing joint damage and improving symptoms.  While its main application is in RA, leflunomide may also be considered for certain cases of **psoriatic arthritis**, another inflammatory arthritis linked to psoriasis.  The decision to use leflunomide for conditions beyond RA is made on a case-by-case basis by a rheumatologist or other qualified specialist.  It's crucial to understand that leflunomide doesn't cure these diseases; it manages symptoms and aims to prevent further damage.


**Dosage:**

Dosage varies depending on the specific condition being treated and the patient's individual needs.  A physician will determine the appropriate dosage.  Typically, treatment begins with a loading dose followed by a lower maintenance dose.  This is usually administered as an oral tablet.  There is no pediatric formulation readily available, and the use of leflunomide in children is generally not recommended.   The drug is not available as a syrup or injection.  Precise dosage instructions will be provided by your doctor or pharmacist, and it's crucial to follow these instructions carefully.  Adjustments may be needed based on how well the patient tolerates the medication and their response to it.

**Side Effects:**

* **Common:** Diarrhea, nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain, headache, dizziness, alopecia (hair loss), elevated liver enzymes.
* **Less Common but Serious:**  Serious liver problems, severe allergic reactions, lung problems,  low blood counts (leukopenia, anemia, thrombocytopenia), peripheral neuropathy (nerve damage).
* **Note:** This is not an exhaustive list.  If you experience any unusual or concerning side effects, contact your healthcare provider immediately.


**How it Works:**

Leflunomide works by inhibiting an enzyme called dihydroorotate dehydrogenase (DHODH).  This enzyme plays a crucial role in pyrimidine synthesis, a process essential for cell growth and proliferation, particularly in rapidly dividing immune cells.  By suppressing DHODH, leflunomide reduces the activity of the immune system, helping to control the inflammation and joint damage characteristic of autoimmune diseases like rheumatoid arthritis.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** Leflunomide should not be used in individuals with severe liver disease, significant bone marrow suppression, or known hypersensitivity to the drug.
* **Drug Interactions:** Leflunomide can interact with several other medications, particularly those affecting the liver or immune system.  It's essential to inform your doctor of all medications, including over-the-counter drugs and herbal supplements, you are taking.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:** Leflunomide is teratogenic (can cause birth defects).  Women of childbearing potential must use effective contraception during treatment and for a considerable period afterward (usually several weeks to months, as determined by a physician using a specific elimination procedure).  It should not be used during pregnancy or breastfeeding.
* **Older Adults and Children:**  Use in older adults requires careful monitoring due to increased risk of side effects.  Leflunomide is generally not recommended for children.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long does it take to see results?** A:  It can take several weeks to months before noticeable improvement in symptoms is seen.
* **Q: Can I stop taking leflunomide suddenly?** A: No, stopping leflunomide abruptly can be risky. Your doctor will guide you on a safe and gradual tapering-off process.
* **Q: How should I store leflunomide?** A: Store at room temperature, away from moisture and direct sunlight.
* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it's close to the time for your next dose. Do not double the dose.
* **Q:  Are there any specific dietary restrictions while taking leflunomide?** A:  While no specific dietary restrictions are mandated, maintaining a healthy diet is always recommended. Discuss any dietary concerns with your doctor.
