# Lisinopril

**Generic Name: Lisinopril**

**Usage:**

Lisinopril is an ACE inhibitor (angiotensin-converting enzyme inhibitor).  Its primary use is in managing high blood pressure (hypertension).  By lowering blood pressure, it helps reduce the risk of serious complications like stroke, heart attack, and kidney disease.  Beyond hypertension, lisinopril also plays a significant role in:

* **Heart failure:**  Lisinopril helps improve the heart's ability to pump blood effectively, easing symptoms and improving the overall prognosis for patients with heart failure.
* **Kidney disease (diabetic nephropathy):** In patients with diabetes, lisinopril can help slow the progression of kidney damage.
* **After a heart attack:**  Lisinopril can be prescribed following a heart attack to reduce the risk of future cardiovascular events.


**Dosage:**

Dosage of lisinopril is highly individualized and depends on the patient's condition, age, and response to treatment.  A healthcare professional will determine the appropriate dose.  Generally, it's available as tablets, and the starting dose for adults is typically low and gradually increased as needed.  Children's dosing is different and requires careful calculation by a physician.  There is no injectable or syrup form of lisinopril commonly available.  The medication should be taken as directed by the doctor; never adjust the dose without consulting them.

**Side Effects:**

Common side effects often mild and may include:

* Dizziness
* Lightheadedness
* Dry cough (a distinctive side effect of ACE inhibitors)
* Headache
* Fatigue
* Nausea
* Muscle cramps


Less common but serious side effects include:

* Angioedema (swelling of the face, lips, tongue, or throat – requires immediate medical attention)
* High potassium levels (hyperkalemia)
* Kidney problems
* Low blood pressure (hypotension)

If you experience any adverse effects, especially those listed as serious, contact your doctor immediately.


**How it Works:**

Lisinopril works by blocking the action of an enzyme called angiotensin-converting enzyme (ACE). ACE plays a role in constricting blood vessels. By inhibiting ACE, lisinopril prevents the constriction of blood vessels, allowing blood to flow more easily, thereby reducing blood pressure.  This mechanism also helps reduce the workload on the heart and improve kidney function.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** Lisinopril should not be used by individuals with a history of angioedema related to ACE inhibitors or those with severe kidney problems.
* **Drug Interactions:**  Lisinopril can interact with several other medications, including potassium supplements, diuretics ("water pills"), and nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drugs (NSAIDs).  Always inform your doctor about all medications you are taking.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  Lisinopril is generally not recommended during pregnancy, especially in the second and third trimesters.  Discuss the risks and benefits with your doctor if you're pregnant or breastfeeding.
* **Older Adults and Children:**  Dosage adjustments are often necessary for older adults and children due to differences in kidney function and body weight.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: Can I stop taking lisinopril suddenly?** A: No, abruptly stopping lisinopril can be harmful.  Always follow your doctor's instructions for tapering off the medication.

* **Q: How should I store lisinopril?** A: Store lisinopril at room temperature, away from moisture and heat.

* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it's almost time for the next dose.  Never double the dose.

* **Q: Can lisinopril cause weight gain?** A:  Weight gain is not a commonly reported side effect, but fluid retention can occur.  It’s essential to monitor your weight and discuss any concerns with your doctor.

* **Q: Is lisinopril safe for long-term use?** A:  Under appropriate medical supervision, lisinopril can be safely used long-term for many individuals.
