# Levocetirizine

**Generic Name: Levocetirizine**

**Usage:** Levocetirizine is primarily used to relieve symptoms associated with allergies.  This includes both seasonal allergies (hay fever) and perennial allergies (allergies that occur year-round).  Its main action is to alleviate symptoms like sneezing, runny nose, itchy eyes, and itchy throat.  While not its primary use, levocetirizine can sometimes be used to treat urticaria (hives), a skin condition characterized by itchy welts.  It's important to remember that levocetirizine treats symptoms, not the underlying cause of the allergy.


**Dosage:**  Dosage varies depending on age and the specific formulation (tablet, syrup).  Always follow your doctor's prescription instructions precisely.

* **Adults (typically 12 years and older):** The usual dose is one 5mg tablet once daily.
* **Children (6-11 years):** The dosage is typically 2.5mg to 5mg once daily, depending on the child's weight and the doctor's recommendation.  Syrup formulations are commonly used for children.
* **Children under 6 years:** Levocetirizine is generally not recommended for children under 6 without specific doctor consultation.
* **Kidney Impairment:**  Dosage adjustments may be necessary for individuals with impaired kidney function. This is because the kidneys play a role in eliminating the drug from the body.  Your doctor will determine the appropriate dosage based on your kidney function.


**Side Effects:**  Like most medications, levocetirizine can cause side effects, though many people experience none or only mild ones.

* **Common Side Effects:**  Drowsiness, dry mouth, headache, fatigue, nausea.
* **Less Common but Serious Side Effects:**  Serious allergic reactions (rare but potentially life-threatening), such as difficulty breathing, swelling of the face, lips, tongue, or throat.  These require immediate medical attention.  Other less common but notable side effects include dizziness, nervousness, and increased appetite.

*If you experience any concerning side effects, contact your healthcare provider immediately.*


**How it Works:** Levocetirizine is an antihistamine.  Histamines are chemicals released by the body in response to allergens. These histamines trigger the allergic reactions we experience, such as sneezing, itching, and a runny nose. Levocetirizine works by selectively blocking the histamine H1 receptors, preventing histamine from binding and thus reducing or preventing the allergic symptoms.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:**  Levocetirizine should be avoided by individuals with a known hypersensitivity (allergy) to levocetirizine or any of its components.
* **Drug Interactions:**  Levocetirizine may interact with certain medications.  Inform your doctor or pharmacist about all medications, including over-the-counter drugs and herbal supplements, that you are currently taking.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  Discuss the use of levocetirizine with your doctor if you are pregnant, breastfeeding, or planning to become pregnant.
* **Elderly:**  Older adults may be more sensitive to the side effects of levocetirizine, particularly drowsiness.  Dosage adjustment may be necessary.
* **Driving and Operating Machinery:**  Levocetirizine can cause drowsiness. Avoid driving or operating machinery until you know how the medication affects you.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: Can I take levocetirizine with alcohol?** A:  Combining levocetirizine with alcohol can increase the risk of drowsiness. It's best to avoid alcohol while taking this medication.
* **Q: How should I store levocetirizine?** A: Store levocetirizine at room temperature, away from moisture and heat, and out of the reach of children.
* **Q: How long does it take to work?** A:  The effects of levocetirizine usually begin within 30 to 60 minutes after taking a dose.
* **Q:  What should I do if I miss a dose?** A:  Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it's almost time for the next dose. Do not take a double dose.
* **Q: Is levocetirizine habit-forming?** A:  No, levocetirizine is not considered habit-forming.
