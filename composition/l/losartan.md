# Losartan

**Generic Name: Losartan**

**Usage:**

Losartan is an angiotensin II receptor blocker (ARB).  Its primary use is in treating high blood pressure (hypertension).  By lowering blood pressure, it reduces the strain on the heart and blood vessels, helping to prevent serious complications like heart attack, stroke, and kidney disease.  

Beyond hypertension, losartan also plays a significant role in managing:

* **Type 2 diabetes with nephropathy (kidney disease):** Losartan can help slow the progression of kidney damage in individuals with type 2 diabetes who already have kidney problems.
* **Heart failure:** In some cases, losartan may be used to treat heart failure, improving symptoms and potentially prolonging life.


**Dosage:**

Dosage varies depending on the condition being treated and the individual's response.  A healthcare professional will determine the appropriate dose.  Generally:

* **Adults (Hypertension):**  The starting dose is typically 50mg once daily, which may be increased to 100mg daily depending on blood pressure response. Higher doses are possible, but always under medical supervision.
* **Adults (Type 2 Diabetes with Nephropathy):** Doses may range from 50mg to 100mg daily, or even higher, depending on the individual's renal function and blood pressure.
* **Children:**  Losartan is available in formulations suitable for children, but the dosage is strictly determined by a pediatrician based on the child's weight and condition.
* **Forms:** Losartan is commonly available as tablets.


**Side Effects:**

Common side effects are generally mild and may include:

* Dizziness
* Lightheadedness
* Headache
* Fatigue
* Nausea
* Diarrhea
* Muscle cramps

Less common, but more serious side effects can occur and require immediate medical attention:

* Swelling of the face, lips, tongue, or throat (angioedema) – This is a serious allergic reaction.
* Severe skin reactions
* Changes in kidney function
* Low blood pressure


**How it Works:**

Losartan works by blocking the action of angiotensin II, a hormone that narrows blood vessels, raising blood pressure. By blocking angiotensin II, losartan allows blood vessels to relax and widen, resulting in lower blood pressure. This effect helps to reduce the strain on the heart and kidneys.

**Precautions:**

* **Pregnancy:** Losartan should not be taken during pregnancy, as it can cause serious harm to the developing fetus.
* **Breastfeeding:** It is advisable to discuss breastfeeding while taking losartan with a healthcare professional.
* **Kidney Disease:**  Individuals with kidney problems may require dose adjustments.
* **Liver Disease:** Losartan should be used cautiously in individuals with liver disease.
* **Interactions:** Losartan can interact with other medications, such as potassium supplements, diuretics, and nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drugs (NSAIDs).  Always inform your doctor of all medications you are taking.
* **Older Adults:** Older adults may be more sensitive to the side effects of losartan and require lower doses.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: Can I stop taking losartan suddenly?** A: No.  Stopping losartan suddenly can lead to a rebound increase in blood pressure.  Always consult your doctor before stopping this medication.
* **Q:  How should I store losartan?** A: Store losartan in a cool, dry place, away from direct sunlight and moisture.
* **Q:  What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it's almost time for your next dose.  Do not double the dose.
* **Q: Is losartan addictive?** A: No, losartan is not addictive.
