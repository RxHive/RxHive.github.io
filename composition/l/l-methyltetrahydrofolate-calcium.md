# L-Methyltetrahydrofolate Calcium

**Generic Name:** L-Methyltetrahydrofolate Calcium

**Usage:**

L-Methyltetrahydrofolate Calcium (L-MTHF Calcium) is a form of folate, a B vitamin crucial for numerous bodily functions, particularly DNA synthesis and cell division.  Its primary medical use is in the management of elevated homocysteine levels, a condition linked to an increased risk of cardiovascular disease.  While not a first-line treatment for conditions directly caused by folate deficiency (like megaloblastic anemia), L-MTHF Calcium is often preferred over folic acid (the synthetic form of folate) because it is directly usable by the body without needing conversion, which some individuals may have difficulty with. This makes it a helpful option for those with genetic variations affecting folate metabolism, such as the MTHFR gene mutation.  It's also sometimes used to support neurological health and reduce symptoms in certain conditions, although more research is needed to solidify these secondary uses.  Always consult a healthcare professional for appropriate usage.

**How it Works:**

L-MTHF Calcium is a naturally occurring form of folate, a coenzyme essential for various metabolic pathways.  Unlike folic acid, it doesn't require enzymatic conversion to be utilized by the body.  It directly participates in the synthesis of DNA and RNA, crucial processes for cell growth and repair.  Furthermore, it plays a vital role in the metabolism of homocysteine, an amino acid.  High levels of homocysteine are associated with an increased risk of heart disease and stroke. By lowering homocysteine levels, L-MTHF Calcium contributes to cardiovascular health.  Its role in neurological function is currently under investigation.
