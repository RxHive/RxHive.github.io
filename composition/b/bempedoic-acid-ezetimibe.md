# Bempedoic acid + Ezetimibe

**Generic Name:** Bempedoic acid + Ezetimibe

**Usage:**

Bempedoic acid and ezetimibe are combined to lower cholesterol levels in adults with high cholesterol (hypercholesterolemia) who haven't reached their cholesterol goals with other treatments.  This combination therapy is particularly useful for people with high LDL cholesterol ("bad" cholesterol) and/or high triglycerides, even if they're already taking statins (another common cholesterol-lowering medication).  The primary use is to reduce the risk of cardiovascular events like heart attacks and strokes in individuals with high cholesterol. While not a primary indication, this combination may also be considered in patients with familial hypercholesterolemia, a genetic condition causing extremely high cholesterol levels.  The decision to use this combination therapy, and which specific dose, should always be made in consultation with a doctor.

**Dosage:**

The typical adult dosage is one tablet of bempedoic acid and ezetimibe combination daily, taken with or without food.  Dosage adjustments based on age, weight, or specific conditions are typically not made with this combination drug; rather the decision to use it at all is made on a case-by-case basis by a medical professional, considering the patient’s overall health.  There is no approved pediatric formulation.  Different strengths of the combination may be available depending on regional availability.  It's crucial to follow the prescription instructions precisely and consult a doctor or pharmacist for any questions regarding dosage or administration.


**Side Effects:**

* **Common:** Muscle aches (myalgia), diarrhea, nausea, vomiting, constipation, upper respiratory tract infections, back pain.
* **Less Common but Serious:**  Severe muscle pain or weakness (rhabdomyolysis – a potentially life-threatening condition requiring immediate medical attention), liver damage (hepatotoxicity), pancreatitis (inflammation of the pancreas).  Allergic reactions, such as rash or itching, are also possible.

**Important Note:** This list is not exhaustive.  Any new or worsening symptoms should be reported to a healthcare provider immediately.

**How it Works:**

Bempedoic acid works by inhibiting an enzyme (ATP-citrate lyase) involved in cholesterol production in the liver.  This reduces the liver's ability to produce cholesterol, ultimately lowering LDL cholesterol levels. Ezetimibe works by preventing the absorption of cholesterol from the intestines, further reducing the amount of cholesterol in the body.  Combined, these two drugs provide a powerful, two-pronged approach to lowering cholesterol.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** This medication is contraindicated in individuals with severe liver disease, hypersensitivity to either bempedoic acid or ezetimibe, or those with a history of rhabdomyolysis.
* **Drug Interactions:** Bempedoic acid and ezetimibe can interact with several other medications, including certain statins, fibrates, and other cholesterol-lowering drugs.  Inform your doctor of all medications, supplements, and herbal remedies you are taking.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:** The safety of bempedoic acid + ezetimibe during pregnancy and breastfeeding is not fully established.  Consult a doctor before using this medication if you are pregnant, breastfeeding, or planning to become pregnant.
* **Elderly Patients:** Older adults may be more susceptible to side effects, particularly muscle problems.  Close monitoring is often recommended.
* **Liver and Kidney Disease:**  Patients with liver or kidney problems should be carefully monitored as the drugs may not be eliminated efficiently.

**FAQs:**

* **Q: Can I stop taking this medication if I feel better?** A: No, discontinue this medication only after consulting with your doctor. Stopping abruptly can be harmful.

* **Q: How should I store this medication?** A: Store at room temperature, away from moisture and direct sunlight.

* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it's almost time for the next dose.  Do not double up on doses.

* **Q: Is this medication safe for long-term use?** A:  Long-term safety needs to be discussed with your doctor, who will monitor you for any adverse effects.

* **Q: Can I drink alcohol while taking this medication?** A:  Excessive alcohol consumption can be detrimental to your liver.  Moderate alcohol use should be discussed with your doctor.
