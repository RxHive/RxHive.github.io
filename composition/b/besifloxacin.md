# Besifloxacin

**Generic Name: Besifloxacin**

**Usage:**

Besifloxacin is an antibiotic used primarily to treat bacterial infections of the eye.  Its main application is in the treatment of bacterial conjunctivitis (pink eye), a common eye infection causing redness, irritation, and discharge.  While primarily used for conjunctivitis, it may also be prescribed for other bacterial infections of the eye's surface, although this is less common and often depends on the specific bacteria involved and the physician's assessment.  It's crucial to understand that besifloxacin only targets bacterial infections; it's ineffective against viral or fungal eye infections.  A proper diagnosis from an eye care professional is essential to ensure the correct treatment.

**Dosage:**

Dosage varies depending on the specific condition being treated and the formulation.  Information below is for general guidance only and should not replace instructions from a healthcare provider.  Always follow your doctor's or ophthalmologist's instructions precisely.

* **Bacterial Conjunctivitis (Adults and Children over 1 year):** Typically, one or two drops of besifloxacin ophthalmic solution are instilled into the affected eye(s) every 12 hours for approximately 7 to 10 days.  The duration of treatment depends on the severity of the infection and the patient's response.  Infants under one year old may have different dosage regimens determined by their physician.

* **Formulations:**  Besifloxacin is primarily available as an ophthalmic solution (eye drops). Other formulations may exist but are less commonly encountered.

* **Administration:**  Proper eye drop administration is crucial for efficacy.  Wash your hands thoroughly before and after application.  Avoid touching the tip of the dropper to the eye or any surface.  Gently pull down the lower eyelid to create a pocket, and instill the drops.  Close your eyes gently for a few minutes after application to allow the medication to spread evenly.

**Side Effects:**

* **Common:**  Burning, stinging, itching, or redness in the eye are relatively common side effects, usually mild and temporary.

* **Less Common but Important:**  Allergic reactions (such as rash, swelling, difficulty breathing), eye pain, blurred vision, changes in vision, increased eye pressure.

* **Serious (Rare):** Although rare, serious side effects can occur.  Seek immediate medical attention if you experience any significant vision changes, severe eye pain, or allergic reactions.

**How it Works:**

Besifloxacin is a fluoroquinolone antibiotic.  It works by interfering with the enzymes essential for bacterial DNA replication and repair. By inhibiting these enzymes, besifloxacin effectively prevents bacteria from multiplying and ultimately helps eliminate the infection. This targeted action makes it effective against a broad range of Gram-positive and Gram-negative bacteria, which are common causes of bacterial conjunctivitis.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:**  Besifloxacin should not be used by individuals with known hypersensitivity (allergy) to besifloxacin or other fluoroquinolone antibiotics.

* **Drug Interactions:**  While interactions are generally uncommon, inform your healthcare provider about all medications you are taking, including prescription and over-the-counter drugs, to avoid potential issues.

* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  The use of besifloxacin during pregnancy or breastfeeding should be carefully considered and only undertaken under the guidance of a healthcare professional.  The potential benefits must outweigh the potential risks.

* **Children:**  Besifloxacin is generally well-tolerated in children over 1 year, but the dosage must be carefully adjusted as per a healthcare professional's prescription.  Infants below the age of 1 year typically require specific dosage considerations.

* **Older Adults:**  Older adults might be more susceptible to certain side effects. Close monitoring is advisable.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long does it take to work?** A: Improvement in symptoms usually begins within a few days, but complete resolution of the infection might take the full course of treatment (7-10 days).

* **Q: Can I use besifloxacin for ear infections?** A: No.  Besifloxacin is specifically formulated for eye infections.  Ear infections require a different type of treatment.

* **Q: How should I store besifloxacin eye drops?** A: Store the drops at room temperature, away from direct sunlight and heat.

* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Apply the missed dose as soon as you remember. If it's almost time for the next dose, skip the missed dose and continue with your regular schedule.  Do not double the dose.

* **Q: Can I wear contact lenses while using besifloxacin?** A: It is generally recommended to avoid wearing contact lenses while using besifloxacin. Check with your ophthalmologist for specific guidance.
