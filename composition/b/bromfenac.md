# Bromfenac

**Generic Name: Bromfenac**

**Usage:**

Bromfenac is primarily used as a nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drug (NSAID) to reduce pain and inflammation in the eye.  Its main application is in the treatment of postoperative inflammation and pain following cataract surgery.  While its primary use is ophthalmic (eye-related), it's crucial to understand that this is *not* a medication for systemic use (meaning it shouldn't be taken orally or injected for other conditions).  Any use outside of its approved ophthalmic applications should be under the strict guidance of a qualified ophthalmologist.  There are no notable secondary uses approved outside of post-cataract surgery pain and inflammation management.

**Dosage:**

Bromfenac is typically administered as an ophthalmic solution (eye drops).  Dosage instructions vary depending on the specific formulation and the physician's prescription, but a common regimen involves one drop in the affected eye(s) several times a day for a prescribed period post-surgery.  Children are typically not prescribed this medication due to the specific application and post-operative setting. The dosage should never be altered without consulting the prescribing ophthalmologist.  There are no oral, injectable, or other forms of bromfenac approved for medical use.

**Side Effects:**

Common side effects of bromfenac ophthalmic solution may include:

* Burning or stinging sensation in the eye
* Temporary blurred vision
* Eye redness or irritation
* Eye dryness

Less common, but potentially serious, side effects require immediate medical attention:

* Severe eye pain
* Vision changes (beyond temporary blurring)
* Allergic reactions (rash, itching, swelling)
* Any unusual changes in eye appearance or function


Always consult a healthcare professional if you experience any adverse effects.


**How it Works:**

Bromfenac, like other NSAIDs, works by inhibiting cyclooxygenase (COX) enzymes.  These enzymes are involved in the production of prostaglandins, which are substances that contribute to inflammation and pain. By reducing prostaglandin production specifically in the eye, bromfenac helps alleviate post-surgical inflammation and pain. Its action is localized to the eye due to the ophthalmic formulation, preventing systemic effects usually seen with oral NSAIDs.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** Bromfenac should not be used by individuals with known hypersensitivity (allergy) to bromfenac or any other NSAIDs.  
* **Drug Interactions:**  While primarily localized to the eye, potential interactions with other medications, especially other NSAIDs, should be discussed with your doctor.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:** The safety of bromfenac during pregnancy and breastfeeding has not been fully established.  Consult your physician before use.
* **Children and Older Adults:**  Use in children is generally not recommended. Older adults may be more susceptible to side effects and require careful monitoring.
* **Contact Lens Use:**  Remove contact lenses before applying bromfenac and wait at least 15 minutes before reinserting them.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long does it take to work?** A: The onset of pain relief and reduced inflammation can vary, but improvement should be noticeable within a few days of starting treatment.

* **Q: Can I use it for other eye conditions?** A: No, bromfenac is specifically for post-cataract surgery inflammation and pain.  Use for other conditions is not recommended.

* **Q: How should I store it?** A: Store the medication at room temperature, away from direct sunlight and moisture.

* **Q: What if I miss a dose?** A: Apply the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it is almost time for the next dose. Don't double the dose.

* **Q: Can I stop using it early if I feel better?** A:  Always follow your ophthalmologist's instructions regarding the duration of treatment.  Stopping early could lead to recurrence of symptoms.
