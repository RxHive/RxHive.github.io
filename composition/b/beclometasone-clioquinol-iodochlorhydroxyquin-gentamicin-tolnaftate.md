# Beclometasone + Clioquinol Iodochlorhydroxyquin + Gentamicin + Tolnaftate

**Usage:**

This combination likely targets infections and inflammation of the skin. Let's look at the individual components:

* **Beclometasone:** A corticosteroid that reduces inflammation and itching.
* **Clioquinol (Iodochlorhydroxyquin):** An antimicrobial agent effective against bacteria and fungi.
* **Gentamicin:** An aminoglycoside antibiotic that fights bacterial infections.
* **Tolnaftate:** An antifungal medication effective against dermatophytes (fungi causing athlete's foot and ringworm).

This combination could be prescribed for skin conditions involving inflammation and bacterial or fungal infection, such as:

* **Infected eczema:**  Inflammation combined with a secondary bacterial or fungal infection.
* **Infected dermatitis:** Similar to eczema, this includes various inflammatory skin conditions with added infection.
* **Certain types of fungal infections complicated by bacterial infection:** The combination of antifungal and antibiotic would combat both.


**Dosage:**

Dosage is highly variable and *must* be determined by a healthcare professional based on the specific condition, the patient's age, weight, and the severity of the infection. There is no standardized dosage for this combination because it is often compounded.  The dosage of each individual component will be determined by the prescribing physician.  The medication is typically applied topically (to the skin).


**Side Effects:**

The side effects listed below are potential side effects of the *individual components*.  A compounded cream may exhibit fewer, more, or different side effects:

* **Common:** Skin burning, stinging, itching, redness, dryness, and irritation at the application site.
* **Less Common but Serious:**  Allergic reactions (rash, swelling, difficulty breathing), skin thinning (with prolonged corticosteroid use),  worsening of existing skin infections, changes in skin pigmentation.
* **Rare but Serious:**  Systemic absorption of corticosteroids (leading to effects on other body systems) particularly with extensive application or use on large skin surfaces.

**Always consult a doctor if any adverse effects occur.**


**How it Works:**

Each component works differently:

* **Beclometasone:** Reduces inflammation by suppressing the immune response in the skin.
* **Clioquinol:** Kills bacteria and fungi by disrupting their cell membranes.
* **Gentamicin:**  Inhibits bacterial protein synthesis, leading to bacterial death.
* **Tolnaftate:** Interferes with fungal cell membrane synthesis, leading to fungal death.

By combining these actions, the medication aims to simultaneously control inflammation and eliminate infection.
