# Beclometasone + Neomycin

**Generic Name: Beclometasone + Neomycin**

**Usage:**

Beclometasone dipropionate and neomycin sulfate are a combination medication typically used to treat inflammatory skin conditions involving bacterial infection.  The primary use is in the treatment of eczema (atopic dermatitis) or other dermatoses that are infected or at risk of secondary bacterial infection. Beclometasone, a corticosteroid, reduces inflammation and itching, while neomycin, an antibiotic, combats bacterial growth. This combination tackles both the inflammatory and the infective components of the skin condition. While primarily used topically (on the skin),  it’s important to note that this combination is not suitable for all skin conditions and should only be used under the direction of a healthcare professional. They will determine if this combination is appropriate for your specific situation.  Other uses may occasionally be considered, but always under strict medical supervision.


**Dosage:**

Dosage instructions vary greatly depending on the specific formulation (cream, ointment, lotion), the affected area, the severity of the condition, and the patient's age.  Therefore, it's crucial to follow the prescribing physician's instructions precisely.  There are no standardized adult or child dosages that can be universally applied. The physician will determine the appropriate amount and frequency of application.  Generally, topical applications are applied thinly to the affected area a few times daily.  **Never exceed the prescribed dosage.**


**Side Effects:**

Common side effects, usually mild and temporary, can include:

* Skin burning or stinging at the application site
* Itching
* Dryness
* Redness
* Thinning of the skin (with prolonged use)
* Folliculitis (inflammation of hair follicles)

Less common, but more serious side effects may include:

* Allergic reactions (rash, hives, swelling)
* Skin infections (fungal or viral)
* Skin discoloration
* Increased susceptibility to infections


If any serious or persistent side effects develop, discontinue use and consult a healthcare professional immediately.


**How it Works:**

Beclometasone dipropionate is a corticosteroid that works by reducing inflammation. It does this by binding to specific receptors within skin cells, decreasing the release of inflammatory substances. This lessens swelling, redness, and itching.  Neomycin sulfate is an aminoglycoside antibiotic that fights against bacteria by interfering with their protein synthesis, ultimately killing the bacteria or inhibiting their growth, preventing further infection.  The combination effectively addresses both the inflammatory and infectious components of many skin conditions.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:**  This medication should not be used on individuals with known allergies to beclometasone, neomycin, or any other components of the formulation.  Individuals with viral or fungal skin infections should also generally avoid this medication.
* **Drug Interactions:**  While interactions are less common with topical applications, inform your physician of all other medications you are currently taking, including over-the-counter drugs and herbal remedies.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  Use during pregnancy or breastfeeding should only occur under strict medical supervision. The risks and benefits must be carefully weighed.
* **Children and Elderly:** Use in children and the elderly requires careful monitoring and dosage adjustment as directed by a healthcare professional. Prolonged use, especially in children, may lead to systemic absorption and potential side effects.
* **Avoid eyes and mucous membranes:** This medication is for external use only. Accidental contact with the eyes or mucous membranes should be immediately rinsed with clean water.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long should I use this medication?** A:  The duration of treatment depends on the severity of the condition and the response to therapy.  Your doctor will determine the appropriate treatment duration. Do not abruptly stop treatment.
* **Q: Can I use this on my face?** A:  Use on the face should only be done as directed by a doctor.  The facial skin is more delicate, and prolonged use may have adverse effects.
* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Apply the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it's almost time for the next dose. Do not double the dose.
* **Q: How should I store this medication?** A: Store according to the label instructions, usually at room temperature, away from direct heat and light. Keep out of reach of children.
