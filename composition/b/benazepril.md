# Benazepril

**Generic Name: Benazepril**

**Usage:** Benazepril is an ACE inhibitor primarily used to treat high blood pressure (hypertension).  By lowering blood pressure, it helps reduce the risk of serious complications like stroke, heart attack, and kidney disease.  It's also prescribed to treat heart failure, protecting the heart and improving its function.  In some cases, it may be used after a heart attack to reduce the risk of further cardiovascular events.  While its primary role is in cardiovascular health, benazepril's blood pressure-lowering effects can also be beneficial for individuals with certain kidney diseases, especially those with diabetes.


**Dosage:**  Dosage of benazepril varies considerably depending on the individual's condition, age, and response to the medication.  A healthcare professional will determine the appropriate dose.  It's typically available as tablets for oral administration.  There is no common syrup or injection form of benazepril.  Adult dosages usually start low and are gradually increased as needed, under careful medical supervision.  Children's dosages are determined based on weight and medical condition;  it's not commonly prescribed for children.  **Never adjust your dosage without consulting your doctor.**


**Side Effects:**  Like all medications, benazepril can cause side effects. Common side effects often resolve on their own, but it's essential to report them to your doctor. These can include:

* Dizziness
* Lightheadedness
* Fatigue
* Dry cough (a relatively common, but sometimes bothersome side effect)
* Nausea
* Headache
* Diarrhea or constipation
* Changes in taste

Less common, but more serious side effects, requiring immediate medical attention, may include:

* Swelling of the face, lips, tongue, or throat (angioedema – a serious allergic reaction)
* Difficulty breathing
* Irregular heartbeat
* Yellowing of the skin or eyes (jaundice)
* Persistent or worsening cough
* Severe skin rash


**How it Works:** Benazepril works by inhibiting the angiotensin-converting enzyme (ACE).  ACE plays a vital role in regulating blood pressure.  By blocking ACE, benazepril prevents the formation of angiotensin II, a hormone that narrows blood vessels, thus increasing blood pressure.  Lowering angiotensin II levels leads to vasodilation (widening of blood vessels), reducing blood pressure and improving heart function.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** Benazepril shouldn't be taken by individuals with a history of angioedema related to ACE inhibitors.  It's also generally avoided in pregnancy (especially the second and third trimesters) and breastfeeding.
* **Drug Interactions:**  Benazepril can interact with other medications, including potassium supplements, diuretics, and certain pain relievers.  Always inform your doctor or pharmacist of all medications you're taking.
* **Specific Populations:**  Older adults and individuals with kidney or liver problems may require adjusted dosages or careful monitoring.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: Can I stop taking benazepril suddenly?** A: No, stopping benazepril abruptly can be harmful. Always taper off the medication under your doctor's guidance.

* **Q: How should I store benazepril?** A: Store benazepril tablets in a cool, dry place, away from direct sunlight and moisture.

* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it's almost time for your next dose.  Don't double the dose to make up for a missed one.

* **Q: Is benazepril addictive?** A: No, benazepril is not addictive.
