# Bempedoic acid + Rosuvastatin

**Generic Name:** Bempedoic acid + Rosuvastatin

**Usage:**

This combination medication is primarily used to lower high cholesterol levels (hyperlipidemia) in adults who haven't achieved their cholesterol goals with statin therapy alone.  Specifically, it's helpful for people with high levels of LDL ("bad") cholesterol and/or triglycerides, and potentially those with high non-HDL cholesterol.  Bempedoic acid and rosuvastatin work through different mechanisms to achieve a greater reduction in cholesterol than either drug alone. While not directly treating a disease, effectively managing cholesterol levels helps reduce the risk of cardiovascular events like heart attack and stroke.  This combination therapy is typically reserved for individuals who cannot tolerate or have not responded sufficiently to higher doses of statins alone.

**Dosage:**

The dosage of Bempedoic acid + Rosuvastatin is determined by a physician and will vary based on individual needs and response to treatment. There is no standard pediatric dosage available at this time, as this combination is primarily approved for adult use.  It's typically administered orally as a single tablet once daily, with the specific strengths of bempedoic acid and rosuvastatin varying depending on the prescription.  The exact dose and whether this combination therapy is appropriate are determined during a consultation with a healthcare provider.  They will take into account factors such as the patient's cholesterol levels, other medical conditions, and other medications being taken.


**Side Effects:**

Common side effects can include:

* Muscle aches (myalgia)
* Headache
* Diarrhea
* Nausea
* Upper respiratory tract infection
* Dizziness

Less common, but more serious side effects can involve:

* Liver damage (hepatotoxicity) – characterized by changes in liver function tests
*  Rhabdomyolysis (muscle breakdown) – a rare but potentially life-threatening condition.


**If you experience any concerning side effects, especially muscle pain or weakness, jaundice (yellowing of the skin or eyes), or dark urine, seek immediate medical attention.**


**How it Works:**

Bempedoic acid and rosuvastatin both lower cholesterol, but through different mechanisms.  Rosuvastatin is a statin, inhibiting an enzyme (HMG-CoA reductase) responsible for cholesterol production in the liver.  This directly reduces the amount of cholesterol produced by the body. Bempedoic acid works by inhibiting ATP citrate lyase (ACL), another enzyme involved in cholesterol synthesis.  By acting on different steps in cholesterol production, this combination achieves a greater cholesterol reduction than either drug alone.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:**  This combination is contraindicated in individuals with pre-existing liver disease, hypersensitivity to either bempedoic acid or rosuvastatin, or those with severe kidney impairment.  Pregnancy and breastfeeding are contraindications.
* **Drug Interactions:** Bempedoic acid and rosuvastatin can interact with several medications, including certain antifungals, antibiotics, and other lipid-lowering drugs.  It's crucial to inform your doctor of all medications, supplements, and herbal remedies you are taking.
* **Specific Warnings:**  Older adults, individuals with pre-existing kidney or liver problems, and those with a history of muscle problems should be closely monitored while taking this medication.  This medication should be avoided during pregnancy and breastfeeding.



**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long does it take to see results?** A:  It may take several weeks to see a significant reduction in cholesterol levels. Regular blood tests will monitor your progress.
* **Q: Can I stop taking this medication if my cholesterol improves?** A:  No, do not stop taking this medication without consulting your doctor.  Stopping abruptly may lead to a rebound increase in cholesterol levels.
* **Q: How should I store this medication?** A: Store the medication at room temperature, away from moisture and light.
* **Q:  What if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it's almost time for your next dose.  Do not double the dose.
* **Q: Is this medication safe for everyone?** A:  No, it's essential to discuss your medical history with your physician to determine if this combination is safe and appropriate for you.
