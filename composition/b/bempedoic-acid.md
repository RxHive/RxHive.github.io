# Bempedoic acid

**Generic Name: Bempedoic acid**

**Usage:**

Bempedoic acid is primarily used to lower high cholesterol (hypercholesterolemia) in adults. Specifically, it targets LDL cholesterol ("bad" cholesterol), a key risk factor for heart disease and stroke.  It's often used in conjunction with other cholesterol-lowering medications like statins, especially when statins alone aren't enough to achieve the desired cholesterol levels or are not tolerated well. While its primary application is in managing high cholesterol, research continues to explore its potential role in other cardiovascular-related conditions.


**Dosage:**

Bempedoic acid is typically administered orally as a tablet.  The standard adult dose is usually 180 mg once daily.  Dosage adjustments based on age, weight, or severity of hypercholesterolemia are generally not required, but individualized treatment plans are determined by a physician based on the patient's overall health and response to the medication.  There is currently no approved dosage for children.  Always follow your doctor's specific instructions.


**Side Effects:**

* **Common:**  Headache, muscle aches, diarrhea, nausea, upper respiratory tract infection, back pain.  These are usually mild and temporary.

* **Less Common but Serious:**  Some patients have reported more serious side effects, such as muscle pain or weakness (myalgia/myopathy), potentially indicating muscle damage.  This is particularly important to monitor, especially in conjunction with statin use.  Allergic reactions (such as rash, itching, swelling) while rare, can also occur.

* **Important Note:**  If you experience any concerning side effects, especially muscle pain, weakness, or allergic symptoms, discontinue use and contact your healthcare provider immediately.


**How it Works:**

Bempedoic acid works by inhibiting an enzyme called ATP-citrate lyase (ACL). ACL plays a crucial role in cholesterol production in the liver. By reducing ACL activity, bempedoic acid lowers the liver's ability to produce cholesterol, thus contributing to lower LDL cholesterol levels in the blood. This mechanism differs from that of statins, offering an alternative option for individuals who cannot tolerate or benefit sufficiently from statins.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** Bempedoic acid should not be used in patients with severe liver disease or hypersensitivity (allergy) to bempedoic acid or any of its components.

* **Drug Interactions:**  It's crucial to inform your doctor about all medications you are taking, including over-the-counter drugs, herbal supplements, and vitamins, as interactions may occur.

* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:** The use of bempedoic acid during pregnancy and breastfeeding is not recommended unless the potential benefits outweigh the potential risks.  Discuss this with your healthcare provider.

* **Older Adults:**  Older adults might be more susceptible to some side effects.  Close monitoring may be necessary.

* **Safety Measures:**  Regular monitoring of cholesterol levels and liver function is typically recommended while taking bempedoic acid.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long does it take to see results?** A:  It may take several weeks to see a significant reduction in cholesterol levels.

* **Q: Can I stop taking bempedoic acid abruptly?** A: No.  Always consult your doctor before stopping the medication, as abrupt cessation may lead to a rebound effect.

* **Q: How should I store bempedoic acid?** A: Store the tablets in a cool, dry place, away from direct sunlight and moisture, as directed on the packaging.

* **Q:  What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it is almost time for the next dose.  Do not double the dose.

* **Q: Is bempedoic acid suitable for everyone with high cholesterol?** A:  No. Your doctor will assess your individual health status and determine if bempedoic acid is an appropriate treatment option for you.
