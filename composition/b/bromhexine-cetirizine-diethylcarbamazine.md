# Bromhexine + Cetirizine + Diethylcarbamazine

**Generic Name:** Bromhexine + Cetirizine + Diethylcarbamazine

**Usage:**  This combination medication isn't a standard, widely-approved formulation.  It's crucial to understand that combining these three drugs in a single preparation requires a physician's prescription and careful consideration.  Each ingredient tackles a distinct medical issue:

* **Bromhexine:**  A mucolytic agent, meaning it thins and loosens mucus in the respiratory tract. It's primarily used to treat coughs associated with respiratory infections like bronchitis and pneumonia, helping to make it easier to expel phlegm.

* **Cetirizine:** An antihistamine that helps relieve allergic symptoms. It blocks the action of histamine, a substance released by the body during an allergic reaction.  It's commonly used to treat symptoms such as sneezing, runny nose, itchy eyes, and hives associated with allergies (hay fever, allergic rhinitis) and urticaria.

* **Diethylcarbamazine:** An antiparasitic drug effective against filarial worms, responsible for diseases like lymphatic filariasis (elephantiasis) and onchocerciasis (river blindness).  It kills or disables these parasitic worms.
