# Bosutinib

**Generic Name: Bosutinib**

**Usage:**

Bosutinib is a targeted therapy primarily used to treat a specific type of chronic myeloid leukemia (CML) called Philadelphia chromosome-positive chronic myeloid leukemia (Ph+ CML).  This means it's effective against CML cases driven by a genetic abnormality involving chromosomes 9 and 22.  It's particularly useful for patients who have not responded well to, or have developed resistance to, other treatments like imatinib.  While its primary use is in CML, it's also being studied for its potential in treating other types of cancer.  However,  its use outside of Ph+ CML is currently limited to research settings.

**Dosage:**

Bosutinib is typically administered orally as a tablet. Dosage varies depending on the patient's individual needs and response to the treatment, and is determined by a healthcare professional.  There is no specific dosage recommendation for children as its use is primarily limited to adults with CML.  The usual starting dose for adults is 400 mg once daily.  The dosage may be adjusted based on factors like side effects, overall health, and the effectiveness of the treatment.  The physician will monitor the patient closely and make necessary adjustments.  Never alter the prescribed dosage without consulting your doctor.


**Side Effects:**

Bosutinib, like many cancer treatments, can cause side effects. Some common side effects include:

* Diarrhea
* Nausea
* Vomiting
* Fatigue
* Headache
* Rash
* Abdominal pain
* Decreased appetite
* Increased liver enzymes (detected through blood tests)

Less common but serious side effects can include:

* Severe liver damage
* Fluid retention (edema)
* Severe allergic reactions
* Heart problems
* Severe skin reactions (Stevens-Johnson syndrome)

**Important Note:** If you experience any of these side effects, especially the serious ones, contact your doctor immediately.  Prompt medical attention is crucial for managing adverse reactions and preventing complications.


**How it Works:**

Bosutinib is a tyrosine kinase inhibitor (TKI).  Simply put, it works by blocking the activity of specific enzymes called tyrosine kinases, particularly the BCR-ABL protein which is abnormally active in Ph+ CML.  This abnormal activity fuels the uncontrolled growth of cancer cells. By inhibiting BCR-ABL, bosutinib helps to slow or stop the growth and spread of leukemia cells.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** Bosutinib should not be taken by individuals with severe liver or kidney problems.
* **Drug Interactions:** Bosutinib can interact with other medications.  It's crucial to inform your doctor about all medications, supplements, and herbal remedies you are taking before starting bosutinib.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:** Bosutinib is not recommended during pregnancy or breastfeeding due to potential harm to the fetus or infant.
* **Older Adults and Children:**  Bosutinib is primarily used in adults; its use in children is limited to clinical trials. Older adults may be more susceptible to certain side effects.  Careful monitoring is needed in these populations.

**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long do I need to take Bosutinib?** A: The duration of treatment depends on individual response and is determined by your healthcare provider. It is often a long-term therapy.

* **Q: How should I store Bosutinib?** A: Store Bosutinib tablets as directed on the prescription label, usually at room temperature, away from moisture and light.

* **Q: Can I stop taking Bosutinib if I feel better?** A: No, you should never stop taking Bosutinib without consulting your doctor.  Stopping prematurely can lead to relapse.

* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it's almost time for the next dose. Never double up on doses.

* **Q: Is Bosutinib addictive?** A: Bosutinib is not considered addictive.
