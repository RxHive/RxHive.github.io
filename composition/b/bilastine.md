# Bilastine

**Generic Name: Bilastine**

**Usage:**

Bilastine is primarily used to relieve the symptoms of allergic rhinitis (hay fever) and urticaria (hives).  Allergic rhinitis involves inflammation of the nasal passages, causing symptoms like sneezing, runny nose, itchy nose and eyes, and congestion. Urticaria presents as itchy, raised welts on the skin. Bilastine works by effectively blocking the action of histamine, a chemical released by the body during allergic reactions that triggers these symptoms.  While its primary use is in these allergic conditions, some studies suggest potential in treating other allergic manifestations, but further research is needed to establish widespread secondary uses.


**Dosage:**

The dosage of bilastine varies depending on the specific formulation and the individual's age and condition.  Typically, for adults suffering from allergic rhinitis or urticaria, the recommended dose is one 20mg tablet once daily.  This can be adjusted by a physician, however, based on individual needs and response to treatment.  There is limited information on children’s dosage of Bilastine, making it crucial to consult a pediatrician before administering it to children. Different formulations (tablets, syrup, etc.) will also have specific dosage instructions which must be strictly followed. Always follow your doctor's prescription and instructions on the medication label.


**Side Effects:**

Common side effects of bilastine are generally mild and may include:

* Headache
* Dizziness
* Fatigue
* Nausea
* Somnolence (sleepiness)

Less common, but more serious side effects are possible.  These warrant immediate medical attention.  If you experience any serious side effects, consult your healthcare provider immediately.

**How it Works:**

Bilastine is a second-generation H1-antihistamine. Histamine is a chemical messenger released during allergic reactions, causing the characteristic symptoms like itching, sneezing, and inflammation. Bilastine works by selectively blocking H1 receptors, preventing histamine from binding and thus reducing the allergic response.  Unlike some older antihistamines, bilastine doesn't readily cross the blood-brain barrier, leading to a lower incidence of drowsiness.

**Precautions:**

* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  The use of bilastine during pregnancy and breastfeeding should only be considered after careful assessment of risks and benefits by a healthcare professional.
* **Liver and Kidney Impairment:**  Individuals with impaired liver or kidney function may require dosage adjustments. Consult your doctor for appropriate dosage.
* **Drug Interactions:**  While generally well-tolerated, potential interactions with other medications are possible.  Always inform your doctor about all medications you are taking, including over-the-counter drugs and herbal remedies.
* **Driving and Operating Machinery:**  Although less sedating than some other antihistamines, bilastine can cause drowsiness in some individuals. Avoid driving or operating machinery if you feel drowsy.
* **Hypersensitivity:**  If you have a known allergy to bilastine or any of its components, you should not use this medication.

**FAQs:**

* **Q: Can I take bilastine with alcohol?** A:  Alcohol can enhance the sedative effects of bilastine. It is best to avoid alcohol consumption while taking this medication.
* **Q: How should I store bilastine?** A: Store bilastine in a cool, dry place, away from direct sunlight and moisture, as directed on the product label.
* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it is nearly time for your next dose. Do not take a double dose to make up for a missed dose.
* **Q: Can I stop taking bilastine suddenly?** A:  Generally, bilastine can be stopped without gradual tapering.  However, it's always best to consult your doctor before stopping any medication.
