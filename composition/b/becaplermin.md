# Becaplermin

**Generic Name: Becaplermin**

**Usage:**

Becaplermin is a medication primarily used to promote the healing of diabetic foot ulcers.  These are sores on the feet that develop due to complications of diabetes, often failing to heal naturally. Becaplermin is a topical treatment, meaning it's applied directly to the affected area.  While its primary use is in treating diabetic foot ulcers, it may occasionally be considered for other types of chronic wounds that are slow to heal, although this is less common and typically under the guidance of a specialist.  It's crucial to remember that becaplermin is not a cure-all for wounds; it assists the healing process but requires proper wound care and management alongside its use.


**Dosage:**

Becaplermin is typically applied topically as a solution or gel. The specific dosage and application frequency depend on the size and severity of the ulcer, as well as the individual patient's response.  A healthcare professional will determine the appropriate dosage and regimen.  Generally, it involves applying a thin layer to the affected area once or twice daily, as directed.  There isn't a standard pediatric dosage as its use in children is generally not established.  Becaplermin is not available in forms like tablets or syrup; it is exclusively used topically.


**Side Effects:**

Common side effects can include:

* Burning or stinging sensation at the application site
* Skin irritation or redness
* Mild pain
* Swelling
* Itching

Less common but more serious side effects may include allergic reactions (rash, hives, difficulty breathing).  

**Important Note:** If you experience any adverse effects, particularly severe or unusual ones, contact your doctor or healthcare provider immediately.


**How it Works:**

Becaplermin is a growth factor.  It works by stimulating the growth and reproduction of cells involved in wound healing, specifically keratinocytes (skin cells) and fibroblasts (cells that produce collagen, a key component of skin tissue). By accelerating cell growth and collagen production, becaplermin helps the wound tissue regenerate more quickly, facilitating the closure of the ulcer.
