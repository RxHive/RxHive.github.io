# Trientine Hydrochloride

**Generic Name:** Trientine Hydrochloride

**Usage:**

Trientine hydrochloride is primarily used to treat Wilson's disease.  This is a rare inherited disorder where the body accumulates too much copper, leading to serious damage to the liver, brain, and other organs.  Trientine acts as a chelating agent, meaning it binds to copper and helps the body eliminate it through urine.  While its primary use is firmly established in Wilson's disease management,  it's not typically used for other copper-related conditions.  The choice between trientine and other chelating agents like penicillamine for Wilson's disease is usually made on a case-by-case basis by a specialist, considering factors like individual patient response and tolerance.

**Dosage:**

Dosage of trientine hydrochloride varies greatly depending on the individual's age, weight, overall health, and the severity of their Wilson's disease.  A physician specializing in liver disorders or metabolic diseases will carefully determine the appropriate dose.  It's usually administered orally, typically as capsules or tablets.  There isn't a standardized pediatric dosage readily available in generalized sources; dosage is highly individualized and determined by a healthcare professional familiar with pediatric Wilson's disease management.  Precise instructions regarding frequency and the total daily amount will always be provided by the prescribing doctor.  Self-adjusting the dosage is extremely dangerous and should never be attempted.


**Side Effects:**

Common side effects can include:

* Nausea
* Vomiting
* Abdominal pain
* Diarrhea
* Headache
* Dizziness
* Metallic taste in the mouth
* Skin rash

Less common, but potentially serious side effects, can include:

* Liver problems (hepatotoxicity) –  requiring immediate medical attention.
* Kidney problems (nephrotoxicity) –  requiring immediate medical attention.
* Blood disorders – requiring immediate medical attention.
* Severe allergic reactions (anaphylaxis) – a medical emergency requiring immediate attention.

Any new or worsening symptoms should be reported to a doctor immediately.


**How it Works:**

Trientine hydrochloride works by binding to copper ions in the body. This copper-trientine complex is then excreted in the urine, effectively removing excess copper from the bloodstream and tissues. By reducing the amount of copper circulating, it helps prevent the damage this excess copper can cause to vital organs in individuals with Wilson's disease.  This chelation process is crucial in mitigating the long-term health consequences associated with this genetic condition.


**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** Trientine hydrochloride should not be used by individuals with known hypersensitivity or allergy to the medication.
* **Drug Interactions:**  Trientine can interact with various medications, particularly those affecting kidney function.  It's crucial to inform your doctor about all other medications, supplements, and herbal remedies you are taking.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:** The safety of trientine during pregnancy and breastfeeding has not been fully established.  Discuss the risks and benefits with your doctor before using it if you are pregnant, breastfeeding, or planning to become pregnant.
* **Elderly and Children:**  Dosage adjustments might be necessary for elderly patients and children due to potential differences in metabolism and kidney function. A physician specializing in the relevant area should always guide treatment.
* **Kidney and Liver Function:**  Trientine is primarily excreted by the kidneys.  Individuals with impaired kidney or liver function require careful monitoring during treatment.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long will I need to take trientine?** A: Treatment with trientine is usually long-term, often lifelong, to manage Wilson's disease effectively.  The duration will be determined by your doctor.

* **Q: Can I stop taking trientine suddenly?** A: No, abruptly stopping trientine can be harmful. Always follow your doctor's instructions regarding dosage and discontinuation.

* **Q: How should I store trientine?** A: Store trientine as directed on the label, typically in a cool, dry place away from moisture and direct sunlight.

* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it's almost time for your next dose. Do not double the dose.

* **Q: Are there any long-term side effects?** A: While long-term use is common, potential long-term side effects like kidney or liver damage are possible, highlighting the importance of regular monitoring by a healthcare professional.
