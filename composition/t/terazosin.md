# Terazosin

**Generic Name:** Terazosin

**Usage:**

Terazosin is primarily used to treat high blood pressure (hypertension).  It's a type of medication called an alpha-blocker. By relaxing the blood vessels, it lowers the resistance to blood flow, thus reducing blood pressure.  This helps prevent serious complications associated with high blood pressure, such as heart attack, stroke, and kidney disease.

Beyond hypertension, terazosin also finds application in treating benign prostatic hyperplasia (BPH), a non-cancerous enlargement of the prostate gland.  In men with BPH, terazosin helps relax the muscles in the prostate and bladder neck, improving urinary flow and reducing symptoms like frequent urination, especially at night, and a weak or interrupted urine stream.  It's important to note that terazosin doesn't cure BPH, but rather manages its symptoms.


**Dosage:**

Dosage of terazosin is determined by a doctor and varies based on individual needs and response to treatment.  It's typically started at a low dose and gradually increased.

* **For Hypertension:**  Adults usually begin with a low dose (1 mg) once daily at bedtime, gradually increasing the dosage as directed by their physician. The maximum recommended daily dose is generally 20 mg.

* **For BPH:**  The starting dose is also typically low (1 mg) once daily at bedtime, with gradual increases as tolerated. The maximum dose is usually the same as for hypertension.

Terazosin is most commonly available as an oral tablet.  There are no syrup or injectable forms widely available. It's crucial to follow the prescribed dosage and schedule precisely; never adjust the dose without consulting a doctor.


**Side Effects:**

Common side effects of terazosin can include:

* Dizziness
* Lightheadedness
* Fatigue
* Headache
* Nausea
* Sleepiness

Less common, but more serious side effects, may include:

* Fainting (syncope)
* Rapid heartbeat (tachycardia)
* Swelling in ankles or feet
* Allergic reactions (rash, itching, difficulty breathing)

If you experience any of these side effects, particularly the more serious ones, contact your doctor immediately.  Stop taking the medication and seek immediate medical attention if you experience severe dizziness or fainting, especially upon standing up.


**How it Works:**

Terazosin works by blocking alpha-1 adrenergic receptors. These receptors are found in the smooth muscles of blood vessels and the prostate. By blocking these receptors, terazosin prevents the activation of these muscles, causing them to relax. This relaxation leads to vasodilation (widening of blood vessels) resulting in lower blood pressure.  In the prostate, this relaxation improves urine flow.


**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** Terazosin should not be used by individuals with a known hypersensitivity or allergy to the medication.

* **Drug Interactions:**  Terazosin can interact with other medications, including certain antidepressants and blood pressure medications.  It's essential to inform your doctor of all medications, including over-the-counter drugs and supplements, you are taking.

* **Vulnerable Groups:**  Special caution is needed for pregnant and breastfeeding women, and older adults.  The risks and benefits should be carefully evaluated by a doctor before prescribing terazosin to these groups.  Children are typically not prescribed this medication unless specifically indicated for a certain condition.

* **Driving and Operating Machinery:**  Because terazosin can cause dizziness and lightheadedness, avoid driving or operating machinery until you know how the medication affects you.

* **Sudden Blood Pressure Drop:** Be aware that you may experience a sudden drop in blood pressure when standing up, particularly during the initial days of treatment.  This is called postural hypotension.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: Can I drink alcohol while taking terazosin?** A:  It's best to limit or avoid alcohol consumption while taking terazosin, as alcohol can exacerbate the dizziness and lightheadedness associated with the medication.

* **Q: How should I store terazosin?** A: Store terazosin at room temperature, away from moisture and direct sunlight.

* **Q: Can I stop taking terazosin suddenly?** A: No, stopping terazosin abruptly can be harmful.  Always follow your doctor's instructions for gradually reducing the dosage.

* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it's almost time for your next dose.  Never double the dose to make up for a missed one.

* **Q: Is terazosin addictive?** A: No, terazosin is not considered an addictive medication.
