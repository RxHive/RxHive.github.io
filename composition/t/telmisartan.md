# Telmisartan

**Generic Name: Telmisartan**

**Usage:**

Telmisartan is primarily used to treat high blood pressure (hypertension).  By lowering blood pressure, it helps reduce the risk of serious complications such as stroke, heart attack, and kidney disease.  It's a type of medication called an angiotensin II receptor blocker (ARB).  While its primary use is in hypertension, telmisartan may also be used, sometimes in combination with other medications, to help reduce the risk of cardiovascular events in people with certain types of heart disease or diabetes, particularly those with evidence of kidney damage or a history of cardiovascular events.  The specific use and dosage will always be determined by a doctor based on an individual's medical history and condition.

**Dosage:**

The dosage of telmisartan is determined by a physician and varies depending on the individual and their condition.  Typically, for adults treating hypertension, a starting dose might be 40mg once daily, which can be increased to 80mg daily as needed, based on blood pressure response and tolerance.  Higher doses are generally not recommended without medical supervision.  Telmisartan is usually available as tablets for oral administration.  There are no pediatric formulations readily available, making it unsuitable for use in children.  The drug should be taken with or without food, as directed by your physician.  Never alter your dosage without consulting your doctor.

**Side Effects:**

* **Common:** Dizziness, lightheadedness, headache, diarrhea, fatigue, upper respiratory infections.
* **Less Common but Serious:**  Low blood pressure (hypotension), kidney problems, hyperkalemia (high potassium levels in the blood), angioedema (swelling of the face, lips, tongue, or throat),  cough.  Allergic reactions, though rare, are possible.

**Important Note:** This is not an exhaustive list.  If you experience any adverse effects, particularly those listed as less common or serious, or any other unusual symptoms, stop taking the medication and contact your doctor immediately.

**How it Works:**

Telmisartan works by blocking the action of angiotensin II, a hormone that narrows blood vessels, causing blood pressure to rise. By blocking angiotensin II, telmisartan allows blood vessels to relax and widen, leading to lower blood pressure. This, in turn, reduces the strain on the heart and blood vessels, lowering the risk of cardiovascular complications.


**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** Telmisartan should not be taken during pregnancy (it can cause serious harm to the developing fetus) or breastfeeding. It's also contraindicated in individuals with known hypersensitivity to telmisartan or any of its components.
* **Drug Interactions:**  Telmisartan can interact with other medications, including diuretics (water pills), potassium supplements, ACE inhibitors, and certain nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drugs (NSAIDs). Always inform your doctor about all medications and supplements you are taking.
* **Specific Warnings:** Older adults, individuals with kidney or liver disease, and those with a history of low blood pressure should be closely monitored while taking telmisartan.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: Can I stop taking telmisartan suddenly?** A: No.  Stopping abruptly can cause a rebound effect, leading to a sudden increase in blood pressure.  Always follow your doctor's instructions regarding tapering off the medication.

* **Q: How should I store telmisartan?** A: Store telmisartan tablets in a cool, dry place, away from direct sunlight and moisture, as directed on the packaging.

* **Q: Can I drink alcohol while taking telmisartan?** A:  Excessive alcohol consumption can lower blood pressure and may increase the risk of side effects.  Moderate alcohol consumption may be acceptable but consult your physician.

* **Q: Is telmisartan safe for long-term use?** A:  For many individuals, telmisartan is safe for long-term use under a doctor's supervision. However, regular monitoring of blood pressure and kidney function is essential.
