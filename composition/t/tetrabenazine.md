# Tetrabenazine

**Generic Name: Tetrabenazine**

**Usage:**

Tetrabenazine is primarily used to treat the motor symptoms of hyperkinetic movement disorders, specifically chorea associated with Huntington's disease. Chorea is characterized by involuntary, jerky movements.  While its primary use focuses on Huntington's disease-related chorea, tetrabenazine is also sometimes used to manage chorea associated with other conditions, such as Sydenham's chorea (a complication of rheumatic fever) and tardive dyskinesia (a movement disorder that can develop as a side effect of certain antipsychotic medications).  It's important to understand that tetrabenazine doesn't cure these conditions; it helps manage the symptoms and improve the patient's quality of life by reducing the severity of the involuntary movements. The decision to use tetrabenazine for conditions other than Huntington's disease-associated chorea is usually made on a case-by-case basis by a specialist.


**Dosage:**

Dosage of tetrabenazine is individualized and carefully determined by a doctor, considering factors like the patient's age, weight, overall health, and the severity of the condition.  It's typically started at a low dose and gradually increased as tolerated to find the most effective dose with minimal side effects.  The medication is usually available as oral capsules or tablets.  There is no standardized pediatric dosage, and its use in children is less common and requires careful monitoring by a specialist. Injection formulations are not typically used.  Precise dosage instructions must always come directly from the prescribing physician.

**Side Effects:**

Common side effects can include:

* **Sedation/Drowsiness:** This is frequently reported and often improves as the body adjusts to the medication.
* **Depression:** A significant side effect that needs close monitoring, especially in individuals with a history of depression.
* **Dizziness:** Can be mild or more severe and may require adjustments to dosage or other interventions.
* **Fatigue:**  Feeling unusually tired or sluggish.
* **Parkinsonism:** Symptoms resembling Parkinson's disease, such as muscle stiffness and rigidity, may occur.
* **Hypotension (low blood pressure):**  Can cause dizziness or fainting.


Less common, but serious side effects include:

* **Neuroleptic Malignant Syndrome (NMS):** A rare but life-threatening condition characterized by muscle rigidity, fever, and altered mental status.  Immediate medical attention is required.
* **Suicidal thoughts or behavior:**  Increased risk, especially in individuals with pre-existing depression or suicidal tendencies.


If any adverse effects occur, it's crucial to contact a healthcare provider immediately.

**How it Works:**

Tetrabenazine works by depleting monoamines, specifically dopamine, in the brain.  In hyperkinetic movement disorders like chorea, there's an imbalance of neurotransmitters, leading to the involuntary movements. By reducing dopamine levels, tetrabenazine helps to lessen the intensity and frequency of these involuntary movements, improving motor control.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** Tetrabenazine should not be used by individuals with known hypersensitivity (allergy) to the medication. Its use should be avoided in patients with severe depression or those with a history of Parkinson's disease.
* **Drug Interactions:**  Tetrabenazine can interact with various medications, including other central nervous system depressants (like sedatives or opioids), antidepressants, and antihypertensives (blood pressure medications).  Always inform your doctor of all medications, supplements, and herbal remedies being taken.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  The use of tetrabenazine during pregnancy and breastfeeding should be carefully considered and only undertaken under the close supervision of a physician, weighing the potential benefits against the potential risks to the mother and child.
* **Older Adults:**  Older adults may be more sensitive to the side effects of tetrabenazine, particularly sedation and hypotension.  Careful dose adjustment is often necessary.
* **Driving and Operating Machinery:**  Because of potential side effects like drowsiness and dizziness, caution is advised when driving or operating machinery.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long does it take for tetrabenazine to work?** A:  The onset of effects can vary, but it may take several weeks to see a significant improvement in symptoms.
* **Q: Can I stop taking tetrabenazine suddenly?** A: No.  Stopping tetrabenazine abruptly can lead to a worsening of symptoms or withdrawal effects.  Always follow your doctor's instructions for tapering off the medication.
* **Q: How should I store tetrabenazine?** A: Store tetrabenazine at room temperature, away from moisture and direct sunlight.
* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it's almost time for the next dose.  Do not double the dose to make up for a missed one.
* **Q: Is tetrabenazine addictive?** A:  Tetrabenazine is not considered an addictive substance in the same way as opioids or other drugs of abuse. However, abrupt cessation should be avoided as it can lead to withdrawal symptoms.
