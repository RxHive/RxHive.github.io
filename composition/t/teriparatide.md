# Teriparatide

**Generic Name: Teriparatide**

**Usage:**

Teriparatide is a medication primarily used to treat osteoporosis, a condition characterized by weak and brittle bones, significantly increasing the risk of fractures.  Its primary use is in individuals with postmenopausal osteoporosis who have a high risk of fracture or those who haven't responded well to other osteoporosis treatments.  It's also sometimes used to treat osteoporosis in men with high fracture risk.  While not its primary indication, teriparatide may be considered in cases of glucocorticoid-induced osteoporosis (osteoporosis caused by long-term use of glucocorticoid medications like prednisone).  It's crucial to understand that teriparatide is not a cure for osteoporosis but rather helps improve bone strength and reduce fracture risk.


**Dosage:**

Teriparatide is administered as a daily injection, usually under the skin (subcutaneous injection).  It is *not* available in tablet or syrup form.  The standard adult dosage is 20 micrograms once daily. This dosage is generally consistent across age groups, but a doctor will determine the appropriate dosage and duration of treatment based on individual factors such as bone density, fracture risk, and overall health.  Children should only receive teriparatide under the strict supervision of a physician, and the dosage will be adjusted according to their specific needs and weight.  The medication should be administered as instructed by the prescribing doctor.


**Side Effects:**

Common side effects can include:

* Nausea
* Dizziness
* Headache
* Leg cramps
* Pain at the injection site

Less common, but more serious side effects, that require immediate medical attention, include:

* Severe leg pain
* Hypercalcemia (high blood calcium levels) – symptoms may include excessive thirst, frequent urination, nausea, vomiting, and constipation.
* Syncope (fainting)
* Signs of kidney stones

If any adverse effects occur, it's vital to contact a healthcare provider immediately.


**How it Works:**

Teriparatide is a parathyroid hormone analog.  Parathyroid hormone (PTH) is a naturally occurring hormone that plays a crucial role in regulating calcium levels in the blood and bone metabolism.  Teriparatide mimics the effects of PTH, specifically stimulating bone formation by encouraging the activity of bone-forming cells (osteoblasts). This leads to an increase in bone mass density, making bones stronger and less prone to fractures.


**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** Teriparatide should not be used by individuals with certain bone conditions like Paget's disease of bone, or a history of bone tumors.  It's also contraindicated in people with high blood calcium levels (hypercalcemia).
* **Drug Interactions:**  Interactions with other medications are possible, so it is crucial to inform your doctor about all medications you are taking, including over-the-counter drugs and supplements.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  The use of teriparatide during pregnancy and breastfeeding should be carefully considered and discussed with a doctor. The potential risks and benefits need to be weighed.
* **Older Adults:**  Older adults may be more sensitive to the side effects of teriparatide, requiring close monitoring.
* **Monitoring:** Regular blood tests to monitor calcium levels are usually required during treatment with teriparatide.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long do I need to take teriparatide?** A: The duration of treatment is determined by your doctor and typically lasts for a specific period, usually not exceeding two years due to potential long-term effects.
* **Q: Can I stop taking teriparatide suddenly?** A: No, you should not stop taking teriparatide suddenly.  Stopping abruptly can potentially affect bone health.  Your doctor should manage the tapering of the medication.
* **Q: How should I store teriparatide?** A: Store the medication as directed on the label, typically refrigerated.  Never freeze.
* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A:  Contact your doctor for guidance on what to do if you miss a dose.  Do not double the dose.
* **Q:  Is teriparatide addictive?** A: No, teriparatide is not addictive.
