# Tacalcitol

**Generic Name: Tacalcitol**

**Usage:**

Tacalcitol is a medication primarily used to treat psoriasis, a chronic skin condition characterized by itchy, red, scaly patches.  It's a topical treatment, meaning it's applied directly to the skin.  Its main role is to slow down the rapid growth of skin cells that's characteristic of psoriasis.  While its primary use is for psoriasis, it might occasionally be considered for other skin conditions involving excessive skin cell turnover, but this would be determined on a case-by-case basis by a dermatologist.  It's important to note that tacalcitol isn't a cure for psoriasis; it manages symptoms and helps improve the appearance and feel of the skin.

**Dosage:**

Dosage varies greatly depending on the specific formulation (cream, ointment, solution), the severity of the psoriasis, the affected area, and the patient's age.  A healthcare professional will determine the appropriate dosage and application frequency.  Generally, a thin film is applied to affected areas once or twice daily.  There are no established pediatric dosages that apply universally.  Always follow the instructions provided by your doctor or pharmacist, and never exceed the recommended dose.  Children should only use tacalcitol under strict medical supervision.  Oral or injectable forms of tacalcitol are not currently available for use.

**Side Effects:**

* **Common:** Skin irritation (burning, stinging, redness), dryness, itching.
* **Less Common but Important:**  Increased sensitivity to sunlight (photosensitivity), rash, skin peeling.  In rare instances, more serious allergic reactions can occur.
* **Serious (Rare):**  While uncommon,  serious reactions requiring immediate medical attention are possible.  These might include severe allergic reactions (anaphylaxis) or widespread skin irritation.

If you experience any unusual or concerning side effects, even seemingly minor ones, consult your doctor or pharmacist immediately.


**How it Works:**

Tacalcitol is a synthetic vitamin D analogue.  It works by binding to vitamin D receptors in skin cells. This binding process helps regulate the growth and differentiation of skin cells, slowing down the abnormally rapid cell turnover that is a hallmark of psoriasis.  By modulating this cellular activity, tacalcitol reduces inflammation and scaling, leading to clearer skin.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:**  Tacalcitol should not be used by individuals with known hypersensitivity (allergy) to tacalcitol or other vitamin D analogues.  
* **Drug Interactions:**  While interactions are relatively rare, inform your doctor about all medications, supplements, and herbal remedies you are taking.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  The use of tacalcitol during pregnancy and breastfeeding requires careful consideration and should be discussed with your doctor to weigh the benefits and risks.
* **Older Adults:**  Older adults might be more susceptible to certain side effects.  Their dosage may need adjustments.
* **Children:** Use in children requires careful medical supervision due to the potential for increased sensitivity.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long does it take to see results?** A:  The time it takes to see improvement varies, but you might notice a difference within a few weeks of consistent use.
* **Q: Can I use tacalcitol on my face?** A:  Use on the face should only be done under strict medical supervision due to the delicate nature of the skin in this area. Your doctor will determine if it’s appropriate.
* **Q:  Can I use sunbeds or sunlamps while using tacalcitol?** A: No. Tacalcitol increases sun sensitivity; avoid excessive sun exposure and the use of tanning beds or sunlamps.
* **Q: How should I store tacalcitol?** A: Store as directed on the product label; typically at room temperature, away from light and moisture.
* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Apply the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it's almost time for the next dose. Don't double up on doses.
