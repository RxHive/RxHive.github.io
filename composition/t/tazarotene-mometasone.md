# Tazarotene + Mometasone

**Generic Name:** Tazarotene + Mometasone

**Usage:**

Tazarotene + Mometasone is a combination medication primarily used to treat moderate to severe plaque psoriasis.  It's a topical treatment, meaning it's applied directly to the skin.  Tazarotene is a retinoid, a type of medication derived from vitamin A, that helps reduce skin inflammation and the rapid turnover of skin cells characteristic of psoriasis. Mometasone is a corticosteroid, which also reduces inflammation and itching. By combining these two active ingredients, this medication offers a synergistic effect, providing more comprehensive relief than either drug alone.  While its primary use is psoriasis, it may sometimes be considered for other inflammatory skin conditions under a doctor's supervision.  However, it's crucial to remember that this medication should only be used as prescribed by a healthcare professional, and suitability for conditions other than psoriasis must be determined on a case-by-case basis.


**Dosage:**

Dosage instructions vary depending on the specific formulation (cream, ointment, etc.) and the severity of the condition. A healthcare professional will provide personalized recommendations.  Generally, a thin film of the cream or ointment is applied to affected areas once daily, usually at bedtime. It's crucial to follow the prescribed application schedule and not exceed the recommended amount.  There are no established dosages for children, and its use in children should be carefully considered and only under the direct guidance of a physician.  Weight-based adjustments are not typically needed.


**Side Effects:**

Common side effects can include:

* Skin irritation (burning, stinging, redness, dryness)
* Itching
* Skin peeling or flaking
* Increased skin sensitivity to sunlight

Less common, but potentially more serious side effects can include:

* Allergic reactions (rash, hives, swelling, difficulty breathing)
* Skin thinning (with prolonged use)
* Stretch marks (with prolonged use)
* Acne-like breakouts


If you experience any side effects, especially those listed as less common or serious, contact your healthcare provider immediately.


**How it Works:**

Tazarotene works by regulating the growth and differentiation of skin cells, reducing the rapid cell turnover that causes plaque psoriasis. It also possesses anti-inflammatory properties. Mometasone, a corticosteroid, directly suppresses the inflammatory response in the skin, reducing redness, swelling, and itching.  Together, these two ingredients work synergistically to address both the rapid cell growth and the inflammation associated with psoriasis.


**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** This medication is contraindicated for people with known hypersensitivity to either tazarotene or mometasone, or any of the inactive ingredients in the formulation.
* **Interactions:**  Interactions with other medications are possible.  Inform your doctor about all medications, including over-the-counter drugs and herbal supplements, you are currently taking.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  The use of this medication during pregnancy or breastfeeding should be carefully considered and only under strict medical supervision. The potential benefits must outweigh the potential risks.
* **Children:** The safety and efficacy in children have not been fully established.  Use in children should only occur under a doctor's close supervision.
* **Sun Sensitivity:**  Increased sun sensitivity is a common side effect. Avoid prolonged sun exposure, and use sunscreen with a high SPF when exposed to sunlight.
* **Wounds:** Avoid applying this medication to open wounds or broken skin.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long does it take to see results?** A: Results vary, but some improvement may be seen within a few weeks of consistent use.  However, it often takes several weeks or months for optimal results.
* **Q: Can I use this medication on my face?** A:  Generally, it's not recommended for the face due to the risk of increased irritation and skin thinning.  Your doctor should determine if it's appropriate for facial use.
* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Apply the medication as soon as you remember, but do not apply double the dose. Continue with your regular schedule.
* **Q: How should I store this medication?** A: Store the medication at room temperature, away from direct heat and light. Keep it out of reach of children.
* **Q: Can I stop using this medication abruptly?** A: It's generally recommended to gradually reduce the frequency of application under your doctor's guidance before stopping completely to minimize potential rebound effects.
