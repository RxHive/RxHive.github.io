# Tadalafil

**Generic Name:** Tadalafil

**Usage:**

Tadalafil is primarily used to treat pulmonary arterial hypertension (PAH) and erectile dysfunction (ED).  In PAH, it works to relax blood vessels in the lungs, improving blood flow and reducing strain on the heart.  For ED, it increases blood flow to the penis, facilitating an erection in response to sexual stimulation.  It's important to remember that tadalafil does not directly cause an erection; sexual stimulation is still required.  While less common, tadalafil may also be used off-label in the treatment of benign prostatic hyperplasia (BPH), a condition causing an enlarged prostate.  Its use in BPH is typically to alleviate urinary symptoms associated with the condition.  The specific use and dosage of tadalafil will always be determined by a healthcare professional based on individual needs and medical history.

**Dosage:**

Dosage varies depending on the condition being treated and the individual patient.  A healthcare professional will determine the appropriate dose.  Generally, for ED, the recommended starting dose is 10 mg taken as needed, at least 30 minutes before sexual activity.  The dose may be increased to 20 mg or decreased to 5 mg based on individual response and tolerance.  For PAH, dosage is typically prescribed at a specific daily amount, and it is crucial to follow the physician's instructions precisely, as this condition requires ongoing management.  Tadalafil is usually available in tablet form.  It should be taken orally with or without food.  There is no pediatric formulation; it's not intended for use in children.

**Side Effects:**

Common side effects can include:

* Headache
* Flushing (redness of the face, neck, and chest)
* Nasal congestion
* Dyspepsia (indigestion)
* Muscle aches
* Back pain
* Dizziness

Less common but serious side effects can include:

* Sudden vision loss
* Sudden hearing loss
* Prolonged erection (priapism)
* Heart attack
* Stroke

If you experience any of these side effects, especially the serious ones, seek immediate medical attention.  Any unusual or concerning side effect should be reported to your doctor.


**How it Works:**

Tadalafil belongs to a class of drugs called phosphodiesterase-5 (PDE5) inhibitors.  It works by increasing levels of cyclic guanosine monophosphate (cGMP), a molecule that relaxes smooth muscles in blood vessels.  This relaxation allows for increased blood flow. In the case of ED, this increased blood flow to the penis facilitates an erection.  In PAH, this improved blood flow to the lungs reduces the pressure in the pulmonary arteries.

**Precautions:**

Tadalafil is contraindicated in individuals with known hypersensitivity to tadalafil or any of its components.  It should be used with caution in individuals with heart conditions, low blood pressure, or a history of stroke.  Concurrent use with nitrates (medications used to treat chest pain) is strictly contraindicated due to the risk of severe hypotension (dangerously low blood pressure).  Tadalafil can interact with other medications, so it's essential to inform your doctor about all medications, supplements, and herbal remedies you are taking.  Pregnant or breastfeeding women should consult their doctor before taking tadalafil.  The safety and efficacy of tadalafil in children have not been established.  Older adults may require dose adjustments based on their overall health and other medications they are taking.

**FAQs:**

* **Q: Can I drink alcohol while taking tadalafil?** A:  While moderate alcohol consumption is usually not a major concern, excessive alcohol intake can increase the risk of side effects.  It's best to discuss alcohol consumption with your doctor.

* **Q: How should I store tadalafil?** A: Store tadalafil at room temperature, away from moisture and direct sunlight.

* **Q: How long does tadalafil last?** A: The duration of action varies depending on the dosage and individual factors, but the effects typically last several hours.

* **Q: Is tadalafil habit-forming?** A: No, tadalafil is not considered habit-forming.

* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: For as-needed use in ED, simply take the dose when needed before sexual activity. For daily use in PAH, take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it is almost time for the next dose. Do not double the dose to make up for a missed one.
