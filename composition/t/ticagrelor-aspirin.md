# Ticagrelor + Aspirin

**Generic Name:** Ticagrelor + Aspirin

**Usage:**

Ticagrelor and aspirin combination therapy is primarily used to prevent blood clots following a heart attack (myocardial infarction) or in patients with unstable angina (chest pain due to reduced blood flow to the heart).  Its main purpose is to reduce the risk of further heart attacks, strokes, and cardiovascular death.  While primarily used in acute coronary syndromes, it may also be considered in other situations where preventing blood clot formation is crucial, always under the direct guidance of a physician. This would only be considered in specific cases based on individual patient needs and risk factors. It's important to remember that this medication is a prescription drug and should only be used under the direction of a healthcare professional.

**Dosage:**

Dosage is determined by a physician based on individual patient needs and should not be altered without consulting them.  Generally, for adults, the standard dosage is one ticagrelor tablet (typically 90mg) twice daily, combined with a daily dose of aspirin (typically 75-100mg). This combination is usually administered orally (by mouth) with or without food.  There is no established dosage for children, as the use of this medication in pediatric patients is not typically recommended.  The exact dosage of both ticagrelor and aspirin, and the duration of treatment, will vary greatly depending on the individual's medical history and current condition.  Never adjust your dosage without seeking advice from your doctor or pharmacist.

**Side Effects:**

* **Common:**  Bleeding (nosebleeds, bruising, gum bleeding), shortness of breath, dizziness, headache, diarrhea, nausea, constipation.
* **Less Common but Serious:**  Severe bleeding (internal bleeding, gastrointestinal bleeding), allergic reactions (rash, hives, swelling), decreased platelet count (thrombocytopenia),  heart problems (new or worsening irregular heartbeat).

**Important Note:** If you experience any unusual bleeding, severe allergic reactions, or chest pain, seek immediate medical attention.  Any concerning side effect should be reported to your doctor or pharmacist.

**How it Works:**

Ticagrelor and aspirin both work to prevent blood clots by inhibiting platelet aggregation. Platelets are blood cells that clump together to form clots. Aspirin inhibits the enzyme cyclooxygenase (COX), which is involved in platelet activation.  Ticagrelor works differently, directly inhibiting the P2Y12 receptor on platelets, further preventing their activation and aggregation. By combining both drugs, a more comprehensive and effective antiplatelet effect is achieved, reducing the risk of blood clots that can lead to heart attacks and strokes.


**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:**  This combination is contraindicated in individuals with known hypersensitivity to ticagrelor, aspirin, or any of their components.  It should be used cautiously in patients with a history of bleeding disorders or active bleeding.
* **Drug Interactions:**  Ticagrelor and aspirin can interact with other medications, including other anticoagulants (blood thinners), nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drugs (NSAIDs), and some herbal supplements.  Always inform your doctor or pharmacist of all medications and supplements you are taking.
* **Vulnerable Groups:**  Pregnant or breastfeeding women should discuss the risks and benefits with their doctor before taking this medication.  The safety and efficacy of this combination in children have not been established.  Older adults may be at increased risk of bleeding.

**FAQs:**

* **Q: Can I stop taking this medication on my own?** A: No. Always follow your doctor's instructions regarding dosage and duration of treatment. Stopping abruptly can increase your risk of cardiovascular events.
* **Q: How should I store this medication?** A: Store at room temperature, away from moisture and light.
* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it is almost time for your next dose.  Never double the dose to make up for a missed one. Consult your doctor or pharmacist if you have concerns.
* **Q: Is it safe to drink alcohol while taking this medication?** A: Excessive alcohol consumption can increase bleeding risk. Moderate alcohol consumption should be discussed with your doctor.
