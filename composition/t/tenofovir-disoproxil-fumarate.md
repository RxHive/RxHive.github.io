# Tenofovir disoproxil fumarate

**Generic Name: Tenofovir disoproxil fumarate**

**Usage:**

Tenofovir disoproxil fumarate is a medication primarily used to treat HIV infection and chronic hepatitis B.  It's an antiviral drug, meaning it works by interfering with the viruses' ability to replicate and spread within the body.

In HIV treatment, tenofovir disoproxil fumarate is almost always used in combination with other antiretroviral medications as part of a highly active antiretroviral therapy (HAART) regimen. This combination approach is crucial for effective HIV management and to prevent the development of drug resistance.  The goal is to suppress the virus to undetectable levels, improving the patient's health and preventing transmission.

For chronic hepatitis B, tenofovir disoproxil fumarate can help reduce the amount of virus in the blood (viral load), improve liver function, and slow the progression of liver damage.  It's often a preferred treatment option due to its effectiveness and relatively tolerable side-effect profile.  Treatment duration varies depending on individual factors and response to therapy.  It's important to note that tenofovir disoproxil fumarate doesn't cure either HIV or Hepatitis B, but it significantly manages these conditions.


**Dosage:**

Dosage varies greatly depending on the condition being treated, the patient's age and health status, and other medications they may be taking. A doctor will determine the appropriate dose.  There is no universally applicable dosage information for children as the prescription is always individualized.  The medication is typically available in tablet form, but other formulations may exist.  It's always crucial to follow the prescribing doctor's instructions precisely.  Never adjust the dose yourself without consulting a medical professional.


**Side Effects:**

Common side effects can include:

* Nausea
* Vomiting
* Diarrhea
* Stomach pain
* Headache
* Fatigue
* Dizziness

Less common, but more serious side effects can include:

* Kidney problems (including acute kidney injury)
* Bone loss (osteoporosis)
* Lactic acidosis (a build-up of lactic acid in the blood)
* Liver problems (rare, especially in patients with pre-existing liver disease)

If you experience any significant or concerning side effects, contact your doctor or healthcare provider immediately.


**How it Works:**

Tenofovir disoproxil fumarate is a nucleotide reverse transcriptase inhibitor (NtRTI).  This means it works by blocking the enzyme reverse transcriptase, which HIV and hepatitis B viruses need to replicate their genetic material. By inhibiting this enzyme, the drug prevents the viruses from making copies of themselves, thus reducing their numbers in the body.


**Precautions:**

* **Kidney disease:**  Tenofovir disoproxil fumarate can affect kidney function, so it's crucial to inform your doctor about any existing kidney problems. Regular monitoring of kidney function may be needed.
* **Bone health:** Tenofovir disoproxil fumarate can sometimes decrease bone mineral density, increasing the risk of fractures.  Your doctor might recommend monitoring bone health through regular tests.
* **Drug interactions:** Tenofovir disoproxil fumarate can interact with other medications.  Always inform your doctor about all other medications, supplements, or herbal remedies you are taking.
* **Pregnancy and breastfeeding:**  The use of tenofovir disoproxil fumarate during pregnancy and breastfeeding should be carefully considered and discussed with a doctor.
* **Older adults:** Older adults may be more susceptible to some side effects.  Careful monitoring is often necessary.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: Can I stop taking tenofovir disoproxil fumarate if I feel better?** A: No.  Stopping treatment prematurely can lead to the virus rebounding and developing resistance to the medication.  Always follow your doctor's instructions regarding treatment duration.

* **Q: How should I store tenofovir disoproxil fumarate?** A: Store the medication as directed on the label, usually at room temperature and away from moisture and heat.

* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it's almost time for your next dose.  Do not double the dose.

* **Q: Is tenofovir disoproxil fumarate addictive?** A: No, tenofovir disoproxil fumarate is not considered an addictive medication.

* **Q:  Are there generic versions of tenofovir disoproxil fumarate available?** A: Yes, generic versions are widely available and generally less expensive than brand-name options.  However, always ensure the medication comes from a reputable source.
