# Ketorolac + Olopatadine

**Generic Name: Ketorolac + Olopatadine**

It's important to note that a fixed-dose combination of Ketorolac and Olopatadine is **not currently available on the market**. Ketorolac and Olopatadine are used separately to treat different conditions.  This information will therefore address each drug individually, as their combined use would require a specific prescription and tailored dosage from a physician.  Combining these drugs without medical supervision is strongly discouraged.


**Ketorolac (NSAID)**

**Usage:** Ketorolac is a nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drug (NSAID) primarily used for short-term management of moderate to severe pain, particularly post-surgical or post-traumatic pain.  It's also sometimes used to reduce inflammation.  It is *not* intended for long-term use due to its potential for side effects.


**Dosage:**  Ketorolac is available in various forms (tablets, injections). Dosage varies significantly depending on the pain severity, patient's weight, and other medical factors. It should *only* be prescribed and administered by a doctor, who will determine the appropriate dose and duration of treatment.  Self-medication is dangerous.


**Side Effects:**

* Common:  Nausea, vomiting, stomach pain, heartburn, dizziness, drowsiness.
* Less Common but Serious:  Kidney problems, stomach ulcers, increased risk of bleeding, allergic reactions (including anaphylaxis),  increased blood pressure.


**How it Works:** Ketorolac works by reducing the production of substances in the body that cause pain and inflammation.


**Olopatadine (Antihistamine)**

**Usage:** Olopatadine is an antihistamine primarily used to relieve the symptoms of allergic conjunctivitis (allergic eye inflammation) and allergic rhinitis (hay fever).  It works by blocking the action of histamine, a chemical released by the body during an allergic reaction.


**Dosage:**  Olopatadine is available as eye drops and nasal sprays. The dosage varies based on the condition and the specific formulation.  Consult the product label or your doctor for the correct dosage.


**Side Effects:**

* Common: Burning or stinging sensation in the eyes (eye drops), mild headache.
* Less Common:  Dry eyes, eye irritation, bitter taste (with nasal spray).


**How it Works:** Olopatadine blocks histamine receptors, preventing histamine from binding and triggering allergic responses.


**Popular Brand Names (India -  Separate for each drug):**  Brand names vary significantly by region and manufacturer. It's best to consult a pharmacist or doctor for locally available brands.


**Precautions (for both Ketorolac and Olopatadine):**


* **Ketorolac:**  Not suitable for long-term use.  Avoid alcohol.  Use with caution in individuals with kidney or liver problems, bleeding disorders, or a history of stomach ulcers.  Not recommended during pregnancy or breastfeeding unless specifically instructed by a doctor.
* **Olopatadine:**  Avoid contact with eyes (nasal spray).  Use with caution if you have glaucoma or other eye conditions.


**Drug Interactions:** Both Ketorolac and Olopatadine can interact with other medications.  It is crucial to inform your doctor about all medications, supplements, and herbal remedies you are taking.
