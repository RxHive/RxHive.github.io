**Usage:**

This hypothetical combination targets a variety of skin infections and inflammatory conditions. Each component works independently:

* **Ketoconazole:**  An antifungal, effective against fungal infections like ringworm, athlete's foot, and yeast infections.
* **Clioquinol (Iodochlorhydroxyquin):**  An antimicrobial agent used against bacteria and fungi.  Historically used for certain skin conditions, but its use is more limited now due to concerns about potential toxicity.
* **Tolnaftate:** Another antifungal primarily used for treating fungal skin infections.
* **Neomycin:** An antibiotic effective against certain bacteria.  Commonly used to prevent secondary bacterial infections in skin conditions.
* **Clobetasol:** A potent corticosteroid that reduces inflammation and itching.  Used for severe inflammatory skin conditions.

A combination of these ingredients might be considered (only by a physician) for severe, complex skin infections with both fungal and bacterial components, along with significant inflammation.  This would likely involve a severe case requiring a powerful combined approach.

**Side Effects:**

The side effects listed below are potential side effects of the *individual components* and not necessarily a comprehensive list for their combination.  The actual side effects would depend on the specific formulation and dosage prescribed.

* **Common:** Itching, burning, skin irritation, redness, dryness at the application site.
* **Less Common but Serious:**  Systemic absorption of corticosteroids (from clobetasol) can lead to Cushing's syndrome-like symptoms (weight gain, high blood pressure, etc.), adrenal suppression, and other serious complications. Allergic reactions (including anaphylaxis), skin thinning, and increased susceptibility to infections are also possible.  Absorption of clioquinol can lead to neurological symptoms in rare instances.  Neomycin can cause allergic contact dermatitis.

**How it Works:**

Each ingredient works through a different mechanism:

* **Ketoconazole & Tolnaftate:** Interfere with the synthesis of ergosterol, a crucial component of fungal cell membranes, leading to fungal cell death.
* **Clioquinol:** Interferes with microbial metabolism and protein synthesis.
* **Neomycin:** Inhibits bacterial protein synthesis, preventing bacterial growth.
* **Clobetasol:** Binds to intracellular glucocorticoid receptors, affecting gene expression and reducing inflammation.
