# Ketorolac + Moxifloxacin

**Generic Name: Ketorolac + Moxifloxacin**

This combination medication is *not* currently available as a commercially produced fixed-dose combination. Ketorolac and moxifloxacin are often prescribed together, but separately, to treat specific infections accompanied by pain and inflammation.  Therefore, the following information describes the individual drugs and their potential combined use, *not* a pre-packaged combination.  Always follow your doctor's prescription instructions.


**Usage:**

Ketorolac and moxifloxacin are used together to manage infections accompanied by significant pain and inflammation.  This is often seen in conditions like:

* **Acute bacterial sinusitis:**  An infection of the sinuses causing pain, pressure, and inflammation.  Moxifloxacin targets the bacteria causing the infection, while ketorolac reduces the pain and swelling.
* **Acute bacterial exacerbations of chronic bronchitis:** Flare-ups of chronic bronchitis involving a bacterial infection, causing pain and inflammation in the airways.
* **Community-acquired pneumonia:**  A lung infection, often bacterial, accompanied by chest pain, coughing, and inflammation.


**Dosage:**

Because Ketorolac and Moxifloxacin are not usually combined in a single pill, dosage instructions would be specific to the individual prescriptions provided by a doctor.  

* **Ketorolac:**  Dosage varies significantly depending on the formulation (tablet, injection), the severity of pain, and the patient's kidney function.  It's crucial to follow a doctor's instructions precisely.  Ketorolac is generally *not* used in children.

* **Moxifloxacin:** Dosage also depends on the infection's severity and the patient's age and kidney/liver function. It is available in tablet and injection forms.  Pediatric use requires careful dosage adjustment based on weight and age guidelines provided by a medical professional.


**Side Effects:**

**Ketorolac (Common):**

* Nausea
* Vomiting
* Stomach upset
* Heartburn
* Dizziness
* Drowsiness


**Ketorolac (Less Common but Serious):**

* Stomach ulcers or bleeding
* Kidney problems
* Allergic reactions (rash, itching, swelling)


**Moxifloxacin (Common):**

* Nausea
* Diarrhea
* Headache
* Dizziness
* Vomiting


**Moxifloxacin (Less Common but Serious):**

* Tendonitis or tendon rupture
* Allergic reactions (rash, itching, swelling, difficulty breathing)
* Liver problems
* QT prolongation (heart rhythm disturbance)


**Note:** This is not an exhaustive list.  Seek immediate medical attention if you experience any severe or unusual side effects.


**How it Works:**

* **Ketorolac:** This is a nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drug (NSAID) that works by reducing the production of substances in the body that cause pain and inflammation.

* **Moxifloxacin:** This is a fluoroquinolone antibiotic that works by interfering with the bacteria's ability to produce essential proteins needed for growth and survival.  This kills the bacteria causing the infection.


**Popular Brand Names (India):**  Due to the lack of a combined formulation, brand names for individual drugs would be provided based on a specific doctor's prescription.  Many brands exist in India for both Ketorolac and Moxifloxacin; asking a pharmacist or doctor for specific brands within your region is best.


**Precautions:**

* **Ketorolac:**  Not for long-term use.  Patients with kidney or liver problems, bleeding disorders, or a history of stomach ulcers should use caution.  It's not suitable for pregnant women, especially in the later stages of pregnancy or during labor.

* **Moxifloxacin:** Not for use in patients allergic to fluoroquinolones.  Use with caution in patients with liver or kidney problems.  Avoid concurrent use with drugs that prolong the QT interval.  It should be used cautiously in pregnant and breastfeeding women.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long will I need to take this medication?** A: The duration of treatment will depend on your specific condition and response to therapy.  Your doctor will determine the appropriate treatment length.

* **Q: Can I drink alcohol while taking this medication?** A:  Alcohol can increase the risk of side effects with both Ketorolac and Moxifloxacin.  Discuss alcohol consumption with your doctor.

* **Q: How should I store this medication?** A: Store both medications as directed on their respective labels, typically in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight.

* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it is almost time for the next dose. Do not double the dose.
