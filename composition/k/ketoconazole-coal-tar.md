# Ketoconazole + Coal Tar

**Generic Name:** Ketoconazole + Coal Tar

**Usage:**

Ketoconazole + Coal Tar is a combination medication primarily used to treat seborrheic dermatitis, a common skin condition characterized by scaly, itchy patches on the scalp (dandruff), face, and other areas of the body.  Ketoconazole, an antifungal agent, targets the yeast *Malassezia globosa*, often implicated in seborrheic dermatitis. Coal tar, a topical anti-inflammatory and anti-proliferative agent, helps reduce inflammation, scaling, and itching associated with the condition.  While its primary use is seborrheic dermatitis, this combination may sometimes be used off-label for other inflammatory skin conditions, but this should always be under the direct supervision of a dermatologist.  It is crucial to remember that this medication is for external use only and should not be ingested.

**Dosage:**

Dosage varies significantly depending on the specific formulation (cream, lotion, shampoo, etc.), the severity of the condition, and the patient's age.  Dosage instructions are always provided on the product label and should be followed carefully. Generally, for adults, application is once or twice daily to the affected area. Children's dosages are usually lower and must be determined by a pediatrician or dermatologist. It's crucial to avoid contact with eyes and mucous membranes.  There are no standard injectable or oral forms of this combination medication.  The medication is applied topically to the skin.

**Side Effects:**

* **Common:**  Itching, burning, redness, dryness at the application site.
* **Less Common but Important:** Allergic reactions (rash, hives, swelling), increased sensitivity to sunlight (photosensitivity).  In rare instances, coal tar can cause discoloration of the skin and nails.
* **Serious (rare):**  Severe allergic reactions (anaphylaxis).  Though uncommon, immediately seek medical attention if you experience symptoms like difficulty breathing, swelling of the face, lips, or tongue, or a rapid heartbeat.

If any side effects occur, it's essential to consult a healthcare professional. They can assess the severity of the side effects and recommend appropriate management or alternative treatment.


**How it Works:**

Ketoconazole works by inhibiting the growth of *Malassezia globosa*, reducing the yeast population that contributes to seborrheic dermatitis. Coal tar has a multi-faceted mechanism. It possesses anti-inflammatory properties, reducing the redness and swelling associated with the condition.  It also slows down the overgrowth of skin cells, minimizing scaling and flaking. By combining these two active ingredients, the medication offers a synergistic effect, addressing both the fungal component and the inflammatory aspects of seborrheic dermatitis.


**Popular Brand Names (India):**

Brand names vary significantly across regions and manufacturers.  It is best to check with a local pharmacy or dermatologist for available brands in your area.  The specific brand name will not change the core components of the medication (ketoconazole and coal tar).


**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:**  The combination should be avoided in individuals with known hypersensitivity to ketoconazole, coal tar, or any other ingredient in the formulation.
* **Drug Interactions:**  While not extensively studied for interactions with other medications, it is prudent to inform your doctor about all medications you are using, both topical and oral.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  Use during pregnancy and breastfeeding should only be considered under strict medical supervision, after carefully weighing the benefits against potential risks.
* **Children and Older Adults:** Children's dosage should be determined by a pediatrician. Older adults may have more sensitive skin and should monitor for any increased skin irritation.
* **Sunlight Exposure:**  Avoid excessive sun exposure while using this medication, due to potential photosensitivity.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long does it take to see results?**  A:  Improvement may be gradual, often taking several weeks of consistent use.
* **Q: Can I use this on my face?** A:  Some formulations are suitable for facial use, but always follow the product label instructions. If unsure, consult a dermatologist.
* **Q: How should I store this medication?** A: Store as directed on the label, usually in a cool, dry place, away from direct sunlight and heat.
* **Q: Can I stop using it once my symptoms improve?** A:  It's crucial to complete the prescribed course of treatment even if symptoms improve to prevent recurrence.  Discuss with your doctor before discontinuing.
* **Q: What should I do if I accidentally get it in my eyes?** A: Rinse thoroughly with plenty of water and seek medical attention if irritation persists.
