# Ketoprofen

**Generic Name: Ketoprofen**

**Usage:** Ketoprofen is a nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drug (NSAID) primarily used to relieve pain and reduce inflammation.  Its primary use is in treating mild to moderate pain associated with various conditions, such as:

* **Musculoskeletal pain:** This includes pain from arthritis (osteoarthritis, rheumatoid arthritis), sprains, strains, back pain, and other musculoskeletal injuries.
* **Menstrual cramps (dysmenorrhea):** Ketoprofen helps alleviate the pain associated with menstruation.
* **Post-surgical pain:** It can be used to manage pain after certain surgical procedures.
* **Dental pain:**  It can offer relief from toothaches and other dental-related pain.
* **Fever:** Although not its primary purpose, ketoprofen can also help reduce fever.


While not its main application, ketoprofen may sometimes be used in other situations at a doctor's discretion.  These "off-label" uses should always be discussed and approved by a healthcare professional.


**Dosage:**  Dosage varies significantly based on the specific condition, the patient's age, weight, and overall health.  Always follow your doctor's prescription carefully.  

* **Adults:**  Typical doses range from 25mg to 150mg, taken several times daily.  Dosage forms include tablets, capsules, and injections.  The injection form is typically administered by a healthcare professional.
* **Children:** Ketoprofen is generally not recommended for children under 16 years old unless specifically directed by a pediatrician.  Appropriate dosage and formulation would be determined based on individual needs and careful consideration of potential risks.


**Never exceed the prescribed dosage.**  Always take ketoprofen with food or milk to minimize potential stomach upset.


**Side Effects:**  Common side effects include:

* Upset stomach
* Nausea
* Vomiting
* Diarrhea
* Heartburn
* Dizziness
* Headache

Less common, but more serious, side effects may include:

* Allergic reactions (rash, itching, swelling, difficulty breathing)
* Stomach ulcers or bleeding
* Kidney problems
* Liver problems
* Increased risk of heart attack or stroke (especially with long-term use or high doses)


**If you experience any serious side effects, stop taking ketoprofen immediately and seek medical attention.**


**How it Works:** Ketoprofen works by inhibiting the production of prostaglandins, chemicals in the body that cause inflammation, pain, and fever. By reducing prostaglandin levels, ketoprofen helps alleviate these symptoms.


**Popular Brand Names (India):**  Specific brand names vary by region and manufacturer.  Consult a pharmacist or your doctor for available brands in your area.  The availability of specific brands can change frequently.


**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:**  Ketoprofen should not be taken by individuals with a known allergy to ketoprofen or other NSAIDs, those with active peptic ulcers, or those with severe liver or kidney disease.
* **Drug Interactions:** Ketoprofen can interact with various medications, including blood thinners (anticoagulants), other NSAIDs, and certain heart medications.  Inform your doctor about all medications you are taking.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  Ketoprofen should be avoided during pregnancy, especially in the later stages, and breastfeeding unless specifically advised by a doctor.
* **Elderly Patients:** Older adults may be more sensitive to ketoprofen's side effects and require lower doses.
* **Children:** Use in children is generally not recommended, except under strict medical supervision.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: Can I take ketoprofen with alcohol?** A: It is generally advised to avoid alcohol while taking ketoprofen, as it can increase the risk of stomach upset and other side effects.
* **Q: How should I store ketoprofen?** A: Store ketoprofen in a cool, dry place, away from direct sunlight and moisture, as directed on the product label.
* **Q: How long does it take to work?** A: The onset of pain relief varies, but often starts within 30-60 minutes of taking the medication.
* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it is almost time for your next dose.  Do not double up on doses.
* **Q: Is ketoprofen addictive?** A: No, ketoprofen is not considered an addictive substance. However, long-term use should only be done under medical supervision.
