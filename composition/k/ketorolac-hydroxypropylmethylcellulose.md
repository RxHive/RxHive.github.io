# Ketorolac + Hydroxypropylmethylcellulose

**Generic Name:** Ketorolac + Hydroxypropylmethylcellulose

**Usage:** This combination medication uses ketorolac, a nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drug (NSAID), to reduce pain and inflammation.  The hydroxypropylmethylcellulose (HPMC) is an inactive ingredient acting as a binder and potentially influencing drug release.  Its primary use is for the short-term management of moderate to severe pain, often post-surgical or post-traumatic pain.  It's important to understand that this combination is not intended for long-term pain management.  While sometimes used for other inflammatory conditions, its primary application remains focused on acute pain relief.  Secondary uses might include managing pain associated with specific medical procedures, depending on physician discretion.

**Dosage:**  Dosage varies significantly based on the specific formulation (tablet, ophthalmic solution etc. – a formulation containing both ketorolac and HPMC is not a standard practice for oral or injectable administration; ketorolac is typically available separately in those forms,  with HPMC commonly found in topical formulations), the patient's age, weight, and the severity of the condition.  *Always follow the prescription instructions provided by your doctor.*  There is no standard pediatric dosage for this specific combination as it's not typically formulated for children.  Adult dosages for ketorolac (when used alone in appropriate formulations) are usually determined by a physician based on individual needs.

**Side Effects:**  Common side effects can include:

*   Gastrointestinal upset (nausea, heartburn, stomach pain)
*   Dizziness
*   Headache
*   Drowsiness
*   Bleeding or bruising (rare, but potentially serious)


Less common but serious side effects may include allergic reactions (rash, swelling, difficulty breathing), kidney problems, liver problems, and increased risk of cardiovascular events.  **If you experience any unusual or concerning side effects, seek immediate medical attention.**

**How it Works:** Ketorolac works by inhibiting the production of prostaglandins, substances in the body that contribute to inflammation and pain.  By reducing prostaglandin production, ketorolac decreases pain signals and reduces swelling.  The HPMC plays no direct role in pain or inflammation reduction but contributes to the formulation's physical properties.

**Popular Brand Names (India):**  It's crucial to understand that a combination of ketorolac and HPMC in a single commercially available oral or injectable dosage form is uncommon.  Ketorolac is available under various brand names in India, but these generally do not include HPMC.  The presence of HPMC is usually found in topical preparations (eye drops, ointments etc.) where ketorolac is sometimes included as an active ingredient.  To determine specific brand names in India, one must consult a pharmacist or physician.


**Precautions:**

*   **Contraindications:** Ketorolac should not be used by individuals with a history of allergic reactions to NSAIDs, active peptic ulcers, severe kidney or liver disease, or those with bleeding disorders.  Pregnancy and breastfeeding require specific consultation with a doctor.
*   **Drug Interactions:** Ketorolac can interact with various medications, including blood thinners, other NSAIDs, and certain diuretics.  It's vital to inform your doctor of all medications you are taking.
*   **Elderly and Children:**  Ketorolac's use in elderly patients and children requires careful monitoring due to potential increased risk of side effects.
*   **Alcohol Consumption:** Combining Ketorolac with alcohol can increase the risk of stomach bleeding and other side effects.


**FAQs:**

*   **Q: How long can I take this medication?** A: Ketorolac is generally for short-term use only.  Your doctor will determine the appropriate duration.
*   **Q: Can I take this medication with other pain relievers?** A: Discuss with your doctor before combining Ketorolac with other pain medications.
*   **Q: How should I store this medication?** A: Store according to the label instructions. This usually involves keeping it in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight.
*   **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Consult your doctor or pharmacist for guidance.  Never double up on doses.
*   **Q: Is this medication safe during pregnancy or breastfeeding?** A:  It's crucial to discuss this with your doctor before taking this medication during pregnancy or while breastfeeding.
