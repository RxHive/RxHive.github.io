# Ketorolac

**Generic Name: Ketorolac**

**Usage:**

Ketorolac is a nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drug (NSAID) primarily used for the short-term management of moderate to severe pain.  Its primary use is for acute pain, often following surgery or trauma.  While effective for pain relief, it's crucial to remember that ketorolac is not intended for long-term use.  It's sometimes used to manage pain related to certain medical procedures,  migraines (though less commonly than other NSAIDs), and musculoskeletal pain like that from sprains or strains. However, its use in these conditions might be secondary to its established role in post-surgical or post-traumatic pain management. The duration of treatment should be kept as short as possible due to the risk of side effects.

**Dosage:**

Dosage varies greatly depending on the route of administration (oral, injection, or topical), the severity of pain, the individual's overall health, and other factors.  A healthcare professional will determine the appropriate dose.  **This information should not be used for self-medication.  Always consult a doctor or pharmacist for accurate dosage instructions.**

* **Adults (Oral):** Typically, the initial dose is 10 mg every 4-6 hours, not to exceed 40 mg per day.  Higher doses are generally not recommended and pose increased risks.
* **Adults (Injection):**  Intramuscular or intravenous routes may be used in hospital settings, with doses usually ranging from 10-30mg.  The specifics are strictly determined by a medical professional.
* **Children:** Ketorolac is generally not recommended for children due to safety concerns.
* **Elderly:**  Older adults may require lower doses due to increased sensitivity to side effects.

**Side Effects:**

* **Common:**  Nausea, vomiting, indigestion, stomach pain, constipation, diarrhea, dizziness, drowsiness.
* **Less Common but Serious:**  Kidney problems (including kidney failure), liver problems, heart problems (including heart attack and stroke – especially with prolonged use), increased risk of bleeding or bruising, allergic reactions (including potentially life-threatening anaphylaxis), ulcers or bleeding in the stomach or intestines.


**If you experience any side effects, especially those listed as serious, contact your doctor or healthcare provider immediately.**


**How it Works:**

Ketorolac works by inhibiting the production of prostaglandins.  Prostaglandins are chemicals in the body that contribute to inflammation, pain, and fever.  By reducing prostaglandin production, ketorolac helps to lessen pain and inflammation at the site of injury or surgery.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** Ketorolac should not be used in individuals with known allergies to ketorolac or other NSAIDs, those with active peptic ulcers, significant bleeding disorders, severe kidney or liver disease, or during the final trimester of pregnancy.
* **Drug Interactions:**  Ketorolac can interact with many other medications, including blood thinners, other NSAIDs, and certain antidepressants.  It's crucial to inform your doctor or pharmacist of all medications you are taking.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  Use during pregnancy, especially in the later stages, and breastfeeding should be avoided unless explicitly advised by a doctor.
* **Elderly and Children:**  Elderly individuals and children are generally discouraged from using ketorolac due to increased risk of adverse effects.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long can I take ketorolac?** A: Ketorolac is for short-term use only.  Your doctor will determine the appropriate duration, typically not exceeding a few days.
* **Q: Can I take ketorolac with alcohol?** A: Combining ketorolac with alcohol can increase the risk of stomach bleeding and other side effects.  It should be avoided.
* **Q: How should I store ketorolac?** A: Store ketorolac as directed on the label, usually at room temperature, away from moisture and direct sunlight.
* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: If you miss a dose, take it as soon as you remember unless it's almost time for your next dose. Do not double the dose.
* **Q: Is ketorolac habit-forming?** A: Ketorolac is not considered habit-forming in the same way as opioids. However, prolonged use should be avoided.
