# Palbociclib

**Generic Name:** Palbociclib

**Usage:**

Palbociclib is a type of medication called a cyclin-dependent kinase 4 and 6 (CDK4/6) inhibitor.  Its primary use is in the treatment of certain types of breast cancer. Specifically, it's used in combination with other medications to treat:

* **Hormone receptor-positive, human epidermal growth factor receptor 2 (HER2)-negative advanced or metastatic breast cancer:** This means it's used for breast cancers that are fueled by hormones (like estrogen) and don't overexpress HER2, a protein that can drive cancer growth.  It's typically used in combination with an aromatase inhibitor (like letrozole or anastrozole) or fulvestrant in postmenopausal women.

* **HR-positive, HER2-negative early-stage breast cancer:**  In this setting, palbociclib is used in combination with endocrine therapy (hormone therapy) after surgery to help prevent the cancer from returning.
