# Polymyxin B + Neomycin

**Generic Name:** Polymyxin B + Neomycin

**Usage:**

Polymyxin B and neomycin are antibiotics commonly combined to treat bacterial infections, primarily affecting the skin and eyes.  This combination therapy leverages the strengths of both antibiotics, providing broader coverage against a range of bacteria.  The primary use is for topical treatment of bacterial skin infections like impetigo, infected wounds, and burns.  It's also sometimes used to treat superficial eye infections (conjunctivitis), though other, potentially less toxic, options are often preferred for ocular use.  This combination is generally *not* suitable for treating systemic infections (those affecting the internal organs), as it's poorly absorbed into the bloodstream when applied topically.


**Dosage:**

Dosage varies greatly depending on the specific formulation (ointment, cream, solution, etc.), the affected area, and the severity of the infection.  There's no single "standard" dosage.  *Always follow the instructions provided by your doctor or pharmacist.*  They will determine the appropriate amount and frequency of application based on your individual needs.  For topical use, the medication is typically applied directly to the affected area several times a day. Pediatric dosage is usually determined by the doctor based on the child's age, weight, and the area to be treated; there aren't specific, pre-defined pediatric dosage guidelines for this combination. Oral or injectable forms of Polymyxin B + Neomycin are rarely used and require strict medical supervision due to the risk of significant side effects.


**Side Effects:**

* **Common:**  Skin irritation (redness, itching, burning), rash.
* **Less Common but Important:** Allergic reactions (ranging from mild hives to severe anaphylaxis – a life-threatening allergic reaction).  Prolonged use can disrupt the normal bacterial flora of the skin, leading to secondary infections (e.g., fungal infections like candidiasis).  Hearing loss (rare but potentially serious, especially with systemic administration, which is uncommon for this combination). Kidney damage (primarily a concern with systemic administration).
* **If you experience any unusual or concerning side effects, discontinue use and contact your healthcare provider immediately.**


**How it Works:**

Polymyxin B and neomycin are both antibiotics that work in different ways to kill bacteria. Polymyxin B disrupts the bacterial cell membrane, causing the bacterial cells to leak their contents and die. Neomycin inhibits bacterial protein synthesis, preventing the bacteria from producing essential proteins needed for survival.  By combining these two antibiotics with different mechanisms of action, the combination is effective against a wider range of bacteria and reduces the likelihood of bacterial resistance developing.


**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:**  This combination should not be used if you have a known allergy to polymyxin B, neomycin, or any component of the formulation.  Individuals with impaired kidney function should exercise extreme caution and should only use this medication under strict medical supervision.
* **Drug Interactions:**  There are no significant drug interactions with topical applications. However, systemic use can interact with other medications.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  The safety of using this combination during pregnancy and breastfeeding hasn't been fully established. Consult your doctor before using it if you are pregnant, breastfeeding, or planning to become pregnant.
* **Children and Elderly:**  Children and the elderly may be more susceptible to side effects.  Careful monitoring is essential.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long should I use this medication?** A:  The duration of treatment depends on the severity of the infection and your doctor's assessment.  Don't stop using it prematurely, even if symptoms improve.
* **Q: Can I use this medication on open wounds?** A:  Generally, yes, but always consult a doctor first, especially for deep or severely infected wounds.
* **Q: How should I store this medication?** A:  Store it at room temperature, away from direct sunlight and moisture, as directed on the packaging.
* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Apply the medication as soon as you remember. If it's almost time for the next dose, skip the missed dose and continue with your regular schedule.  Do not double the dose.
* **Q: Is it okay to use this medication on my face?** A:  Use only as directed by your doctor.  Avoid application to sensitive areas like the eyes unless specifically prescribed for ophthalmic use.
