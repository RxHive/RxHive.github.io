# Pyrimethamine + Sulphadoxine

**Generic Name: Pyrimethamine + Sulphadoxine**

**Usage:**

Pyrimethamine and sulphadoxine are combined in a fixed-dose formulation to treat malaria, specifically *Plasmodium falciparum* malaria.  This combination is particularly effective against chloroquine-resistant strains, making it a crucial tool in areas where resistance to other antimalarials is widespread.  While its primary use is treating malaria, it's sometimes used in the prevention of malaria in specific high-risk situations, under strict medical supervision.  It's important to note that the use of this drug for malaria prevention is becoming less common due to increasing resistance. Always consult a healthcare professional before using this medication.  This medication is not a first-line treatment for malaria in many areas due to rising resistance. Its use is often guided by local malaria treatment guidelines.

**Dosage:**

Dosage varies greatly depending on the patient's age, weight, and the severity of the malaria infection.  A healthcare professional will determine the appropriate dose.  Typically, the medication comes in tablet form.   The dosage is usually a single dose, but in some cases, a second dose may be given after a specified interval, as prescribed by a healthcare provider.  There is no standard pediatric dosage to list here; it is entirely dependent on weight and needs to be carefully calculated and supervised by a physician.  This medication is **not** typically available as a syrup or injection for general use.

**Side Effects:**

* **Common:** Nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, rash, headache.
* **Less Common but Serious:**  Severe allergic reactions (anaphylaxis), bone marrow suppression (leading to reduced blood cell counts), liver damage, Stevens-Johnson syndrome (a severe skin reaction).
* **Important Note:** If any adverse effects occur, especially severe reactions, stop taking the medication and seek immediate medical attention.

**How it Works:**

Pyrimethamine and sulphadoxine work synergistically to kill the malaria parasite.  Sulphadoxine inhibits the parasite's production of folic acid, a vital nutrient. Pyrimethamine further hinders the parasite's ability to utilize folic acid, effectively stopping its growth and reproduction. This combined action leads to the death of the malaria parasite.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** This medication should not be used by individuals with known hypersensitivity (allergy) to either pyrimethamine or sulphadoxine, or to other sulfonamides.  It should also be avoided by those with severe liver or kidney impairment.
* **Drug Interactions:** Many drugs can interact with pyrimethamine/sulphadoxine.  Inform your healthcare provider of all medications, supplements, and herbal remedies you are taking.  This is crucial to avoid potentially dangerous interactions.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  This medication should only be used during pregnancy or breastfeeding under strict medical supervision, as it may carry risks to both the mother and the child. The benefits of treatment must greatly outweigh any potential risks.
* **Older Adults and Children:**  Dosage adjustments are often necessary for both older adults and children, due to potential differences in metabolism and organ function.  Careful monitoring is crucial in these populations.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: Can I take this medication with alcohol?** A: It's best to avoid alcohol while taking this medication, as it can increase the risk of side effects.
* **Q: How should I store this medication?** A: Store in a cool, dry place, away from direct sunlight and moisture. Keep out of reach of children.
* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Contact your healthcare provider for guidance.  Do not take a double dose to make up for a missed one.
* **Q: Is this medication effective against all types of malaria?** A: No, it's primarily effective against *Plasmodium falciparum* malaria, and even then, effectiveness varies due to emerging drug resistance.
* **Q:  How long does it take to work?** A:  Improvement in symptoms is usually seen within a few days, but a complete course of treatment is essential to eliminate the infection.
