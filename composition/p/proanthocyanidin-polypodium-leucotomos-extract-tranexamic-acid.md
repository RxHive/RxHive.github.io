# Proanthocyanidin + Polypodium leucotomos extract + Tranexamic Acid

**Generic Name:** Proanthocyanidin + Polypodium leucotomos extract + Tranexamic Acid

* **Proanthocyanidins:** These plant-derived compounds are often found in supplements marketed for their antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties.  Some research suggests potential benefits in treating urinary tract infections and improving vascular health, but more robust clinical trials are needed to confirm these effects.

* **Polypodium leucotomos extract:** This extract, derived from the fern *Polypodium leucotomos*, is primarily used in dermatology.  Studies indicate it might offer photoprotective effects, potentially reducing sunburn and helping manage certain skin conditions like vitiligo and psoriasis.  However, its precise mechanism and efficacy require further investigation.

* **Tranexamic acid:** This medication is approved for various medical conditions. Its primary use is to control excessive bleeding, such as heavy menstrual bleeding (menorrhagia) or bleeding after surgery or injury. It works by preventing the breakdown of blood clots.  It also shows some promise in reducing hyperpigmentation in certain skin conditions.

**Side Effects:**

* **Proanthocyanidins:**  Generally well-tolerated, but potential gastrointestinal issues like nausea or upset stomach are possible. Allergic reactions are rare but possible.

* **Polypodium leucotomos extract:**  Generally well-tolerated at recommended doses.  Potential side effects are uncommon but may include mild gastrointestinal discomfort.

* **Tranexamic acid:**  Common side effects can include nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, and dizziness.  More serious, though rare, side effects may involve blood clots (thromboembolic events), allergic reactions (rash, itching, swelling), and seizures.


**How it Works:**

Each component has a distinct mechanism of action:

* **Proanthocyanidins:**  Their effects are primarily linked to their antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, protecting cells from damage and potentially reducing inflammation.

* **Polypodium leucotomos extract:** It’s thought to work by protecting skin cells from UV damage, reducing inflammation, and potentially modulating the immune response in the skin.

* **Tranexamic acid:** This inhibits the breakdown of blood clots by interfering with the action of plasminogen, a protein involved in clot dissolution.
