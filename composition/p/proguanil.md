# Proguanil

**Generic Name: Proguanil**

**Usage:**

Proguanil is primarily used to prevent malaria.  It's a medication taken *before*, *during*, and *after* travel to areas where malaria is prevalent.  Its effectiveness varies depending on the specific type of malaria parasite and the geographical region. Proguanil is often used in combination with other antimalarial drugs, particularly atovaquone, to enhance its protective effect and broaden its coverage against different malaria strains.  While not a treatment for *existing* malaria infections, its prophylactic use significantly reduces the risk of contracting this potentially life-threatening disease.  In some regions, proguanil may also be considered as part of a treatment regimen for certain types of toxoplasmosis, a parasitic infection, though this is less common.

**Dosage:**

Dosage varies considerably depending on age, weight, and the specific reason for use (malaria prophylaxis versus other uses).  Always follow a doctor's prescription and instructions carefully.  Generally:

* **Adults (Malaria Prophylaxis):**  The typical dose is 100-200 mg once daily, usually beginning one or two weeks before travel to a malaria-risk area and continuing for four weeks after leaving the area.  
* **Children (Malaria Prophylaxis):**  Dosage for children is calculated based on weight and age; a healthcare professional will determine the appropriate dose.  Liquid formulations are often available for children.
* **Other Uses:**  Dosing for conditions other than malaria prophylaxis will be significantly different and should be determined by a healthcare provider.

Proguanil is usually available as tablets, although liquid formulations may exist for pediatric use.  The medication should be taken with food to minimize gastrointestinal upset.

**Side Effects:**

Common side effects are generally mild and may include:

* Nausea
* Vomiting
* Diarrhea
* Abdominal cramps
* Headache
* Dizziness
* Skin rash

Less common, but potentially more serious, side effects may include:

* Severe allergic reactions (anaphylaxis)
* Blood disorders (rare)
* Liver problems (rare)

If you experience any unusual or concerning side effects, seek immediate medical attention.

**How it Works:**

Proguanil is a type of antimalarial drug that works by interfering with the parasite's ability to reproduce.  Specifically, it inhibits an enzyme crucial for the parasite's growth and development within the human body.  This prevents the parasite from multiplying and causing the disease.  When combined with atovaquone, the two drugs work synergistically, enhancing their overall antimalarial effect.

**Precautions:**

* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:** Proguanil should be used with caution during pregnancy and breastfeeding.  The potential risks and benefits should be carefully weighed by a doctor.
* **Kidney or Liver Disease:** Individuals with impaired kidney or liver function may require adjusted dosages or may not be suitable candidates for proguanil.
* **Drug Interactions:** Proguanil can interact with certain medications, so it's crucial to inform your doctor of all other medications, including over-the-counter drugs and herbal supplements, that you are taking.
* **Allergies:** Individuals with known allergies to proguanil or related medications should avoid its use.

**FAQs:**

* **Q: Can I take proguanil if I have a pre-existing medical condition?** A:  You must inform your doctor of all your medical conditions before starting proguanil. They can assess whether it's safe for you to use.

* **Q: How should I store proguanil?** A: Store proguanil in a cool, dry place, away from direct sunlight and moisture. Keep it out of reach of children.

* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it is almost time for the next dose. Never double the dose.

* **Q: Can proguanil cure malaria?** A: No, proguanil is primarily a preventative medication.  If you develop symptoms of malaria, seek immediate medical attention.  Treatment requires different antimalarial drugs.

* **Q: Is proguanil safe for long-term use?** A:  Long-term use should only be considered under the strict supervision of a healthcare professional. The potential for side effects increases with prolonged use.
