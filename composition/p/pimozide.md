# Pimozide

**Generic Name: Pimozide**

**Usage:**

Pimozide is a potent antipsychotic medication primarily used to treat Tourette's syndrome, a neurological disorder characterized by involuntary movements (tics) and vocalizations.  It's particularly effective in managing severe tics that significantly impair a person's daily life.  While its primary use is for Tourette's, pimozide may also be used off-label in certain cases to treat other conditions like severe schizophrenia or psychotic disorders, though this is less common due to the availability of other, potentially better-tolerated medications for these conditions.  The decision to use pimozide for these off-label purposes should be made in close consultation with a healthcare professional, weighing the potential benefits against the risk of side effects.


**Dosage:**

Dosage of pimozide is highly individualized and carefully determined by a doctor based on the patient's age, weight, overall health, and response to treatment.  It's typically available as oral capsules.  There is no syrup or injectable form.

* **Adults:**  Treatment usually begins with a low dose and is gradually increased as needed under close medical supervision.  The maximum daily dose is generally limited due to the potential for side effects.

* **Children:** Pimozide is sometimes used in children with Tourette's syndrome, but this requires extremely cautious monitoring due to potential side effects.  Dosage is even more carefully adjusted based on age and weight, starting with a very low dose.

**It is crucial to follow the prescribed dosage instructions precisely and not alter the dosage without consulting a doctor.**


**Side Effects:**

Pimozide, like other antipsychotic medications, can cause various side effects.  Some common ones include:

* **Neurological:** Drowsiness, dizziness, restlessness, tremor, muscle stiffness (extrapyramidal symptoms), difficulty with coordination.
* **Cardiovascular:**  Changes in heart rhythm (QT prolongation – a serious potential side effect), low blood pressure.
* **Gastrointestinal:** Nausea, vomiting, constipation.
* **Other:** Weight gain, dry mouth, blurred vision, fatigue.

**Less common, but serious side effects include:**  tardive dyskinesia (a potentially irreversible movement disorder), neuroleptic malignant syndrome (a rare but life-threatening condition characterized by fever, muscle rigidity, altered mental status), and blood disorders.

**Any new or worsening symptoms should be reported to a doctor immediately.**


**How it Works:**

Pimozide works by blocking the activity of dopamine, a neurotransmitter in the brain.  In Tourette's syndrome, an imbalance in dopamine levels is thought to play a role in the development of tics. By reducing dopamine activity, pimozide helps to lessen the severity and frequency of these involuntary movements.  The exact mechanism by which it affects other conditions like schizophrenia is more complex but also relates to its interaction with dopamine and other neurotransmitters.


**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** Pimozide is contraindicated in individuals with known hypersensitivity to the drug or those with certain heart conditions (especially QT prolongation).
* **Drug Interactions:**  Pimozide can interact with many other medications, particularly those affecting the heart or central nervous system.  It's essential to inform your doctor about all medications, supplements, and herbal remedies being taken.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  Pimozide should be used during pregnancy or breastfeeding only if the potential benefits clearly outweigh the risks.  The doctor should carefully assess this balance.
* **Elderly and Children:**  Older adults and children are at increased risk for side effects and require close monitoring.
* **Driving and Operating Machinery:**  Drowsiness and dizziness are possible side effects; caution is advised when driving or operating machinery.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long does it take for pimozide to work?**  A:  The onset of effect can vary, but improvement in symptoms may not be fully noticeable for several weeks.
* **Q: Can I stop taking pimozide suddenly?** A: No, abruptly stopping pimozide can lead to withdrawal symptoms.  Always consult your doctor before making any changes to your medication regimen.
* **Q: How should I store pimozide?** A: Store pimozide at room temperature, away from moisture and heat.
* **Q: Is pimozide addictive?** A: Pimozide is not considered an addictive substance in the same way as opioids, but stopping abruptly can lead to withdrawal symptoms.
* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it is almost time for your next dose. Never double up on doses.
