# Plerixafor

**Generic Name: Plerixafor**

**Usage:**

Plerixafor is a medication primarily used to mobilize hematopoietic stem cells (HSCs) into the bloodstream.  HSCs are the body's building blocks for blood cells.  This mobilization is crucial for patients undergoing a stem cell transplant, a procedure often necessary for treating certain cancers like lymphoma and multiple myeloma, as well as some genetic blood disorders.  By increasing the number of HSCs circulating in the blood, plerixafor makes it easier to collect a sufficient number for transplantation.  While its primary use is in stem cell mobilization before autologous (self-donated) stem cell transplantation, it might also be explored in other areas of research involving stem cell mobilization. However, these uses are not currently standard practice.


**Dosage:**

Plerixafor is administered as a subcutaneous injection (under the skin).  Dosage is determined by a healthcare professional and varies based on factors like the patient's weight, overall health, and the specific transplantation procedure.  There's no established dosage for children. The usual adult dose is a single injection, typically given 24 hours before apheresis (the procedure to collect stem cells).  The exact dose will be calculated by the treating physician to ensure optimal results while minimizing potential side effects.  The medication is not available in tablet or syrup form.


**Side Effects:**

Common side effects of plerixafor can include:

*   Nausea
*   Vomiting
*   Diarrhea
*   Fatigue
*   Headache
*   Muscle aches
*   Rash
*   Low white blood cell count (leukopenia)
*   Decreased platelets (thrombocytopenia)

Less common, but potentially serious side effects can include severe allergic reactions and serious decreases in blood cell counts.  **It's crucial to contact a healthcare provider immediately if any adverse effects occur.**


**How it Works:**

Plerixafor works by blocking the CXCR4 receptor on the surface of HSCs.  This receptor normally keeps HSCs anchored within the bone marrow. By blocking this receptor, plerixafor allows the HSCs to move more freely from the bone marrow into the bloodstream, making them more readily available for collection during apheresis.


**Precautions:**

*   **Contraindications:**  Plerixafor should not be used in patients with known hypersensitivity (allergic reaction) to plerixafor or its components.
*   **Drug Interactions:** Plerixafor may interact with other medications.  It is critical to inform your doctor of all medications, including over-the-counter drugs and herbal supplements, you are currently taking.
*   **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:** The safety of plerixafor during pregnancy and breastfeeding has not been fully established. Pregnant or breastfeeding women should discuss the risks and benefits with their doctor before using this medication.
*   **Elderly Patients:**  Older adults may be more susceptible to side effects.  Careful monitoring is necessary.
*   **Renal and Hepatic Impairment:**  Patients with kidney or liver problems may require dose adjustments or close monitoring.

**FAQs:**

*   **Q: How long does it take for plerixafor to work?**  A: The effects of plerixafor are typically seen within 24 hours of injection, allowing for stem cell collection.
*   **Q: How is plerixafor stored?** A:  Store plerixafor as directed by your pharmacist, usually refrigerated.
*   **Q: Are there any long-term effects of plerixafor?** A:  Long-term effects are generally not a major concern, as it's used for a short duration before a stem cell transplant. However,  post-transplant monitoring for any lingering side effects is standard practice.
*   **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A:  Contact your healthcare provider immediately if you miss a dose, as the timing is crucial for the stem cell collection procedure.
