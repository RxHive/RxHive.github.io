# Phenyl Mercuric Nitrate + Sodium Chloride

**Generic Name:** Phenyl Mercuric Nitrate + Sodium Chloride

**Usage:**

Phenyl Mercuric Nitrate (PMN) combined with Sodium Chloride was historically used as a topical antiseptic and preservative, primarily in ophthalmic solutions and certain wound dressings.  Its primary application was in preventing bacterial and fungal infections in the eye and on minor skin wounds.  However, due to significant toxicity concerns, its use is now extremely limited and largely discontinued in most countries.  The combination was never widely used for systemic treatments (ingestion or injection) due to its inherent dangers. Any remaining usage would be extremely specialized and likely confined to niche applications or historical research contexts.  It is crucial to understand that the information below should be considered historical context and not a recommendation for current use.

**Dosage:**

Precise dosage information for this combination is difficult to provide accurately because its use is essentially obsolete in modern medicine.  Past formulations varied significantly, and safe dosages would be highly dependent on the specific concentration and the application (e.g., eye drops vs. wound dressing).  **Given the toxicity of PMN, attempting to use this combination based on historical information is strongly discouraged.**


**Side Effects:**

Due to the inherent toxicity of phenyl mercuric nitrate, even topical applications carried a risk of side effects.  These could include:

* **Skin irritation:**  Redness, itching, burning, and swelling at the application site.
* **Allergic reactions:**  Rash, hives, itching, swelling of the face, lips, or tongue. Difficulty breathing.
* **Eye irritation:** Burning, stinging, redness, and blurred vision if used in ophthalmic solutions.
* **Systemic toxicity (rare but serious):**  If absorbed through the skin or mucous membranes, PMN can cause kidney damage, neurological problems, and other severe health complications.

**It is important to note that serious side effects, even potentially fatal ones, were a possibility with PMN, further emphasizing its current disuse.** Any adverse reaction necessitates immediate medical attention.


**How it Works:**

Phenyl mercuric nitrate acts as an antiseptic by inhibiting the growth of bacteria and fungi.  The mercury ion disrupts cellular processes in microorganisms, leading to their death or inability to reproduce.  The addition of sodium chloride may have served to enhance the solution's stability or improve its penetration into tissues, although the precise mechanism is not well-documented in modern literature.


**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** This combination is contraindicated in individuals with known hypersensitivity to mercury or other heavy metals.
* **Drug Interactions:** No specific drug interactions were widely studied for this combination, given its limited and outdated use. However, potential interactions with other medications are possible due to mercury's toxicity.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  Use during pregnancy and breastfeeding is absolutely contraindicated due to the significant risk of mercury toxicity to both mother and child.
* **Children and Older Adults:**  Use in children and older adults should be avoided due to increased vulnerability to mercury toxicity.
* **Application:** Care must be taken to avoid contact with eyes and broken skin, except when used as directed for specific ophthalmic purposes (which is now highly unlikely).  Hand washing is critical after use.
