# Permethrin

**Generic Name: Permethrin**

**Usage:**

Permethrin is a synthetic pyrethroid insecticide.  Its primary medical use is in the topical treatment of head lice infestations (pediculosis capitis) and scabies (caused by the *Sarcoptes scabiei* mite).  It's highly effective against these parasites by disrupting their nervous systems, leading to their paralysis and death.  While not a primary medical use, permethrin is also sometimes used as a topical treatment for other insect bites and stings to alleviate itching and inflammation. Note that its use in treating insects is primarily for *external* parasites, not internal infestations or diseases transmitted by insects.  It is crucial to follow a doctor's or pharmacist's instructions precisely, as permethrin's medical applications are limited to specific parasitic infestations.

**Dosage:**

Dosage varies greatly depending on the specific formulation (cream, lotion, shampoo) and the condition being treated.  Always follow the instructions provided by your doctor or on the product label.  There are no standardized dosage recommendations for adults and children applicable to all permethrin products.  For head lice, typically a single application of a permethrin cream rinse or shampoo is needed, with a second application seven to ten days later if necessary. For scabies, a single application of a permethrin cream is usually sufficient, but a second application might be recommended by a healthcare professional after a week.  Children and infants will typically require a different dosage than adults, based on their weight and age.  A doctor or pharmacist should always determine the appropriate dose for children.  Permethrin is not typically administered via injection or tablet; its use is primarily topical.

**Side Effects:**

* **Common:** Burning or stinging sensation at the application site, itching, redness, rash.
* **Less Common but Potentially Serious:**  Allergic reactions (including hives, swelling, difficulty breathing), severe skin irritation, blisters.

**Note:**  If any unusual or severe side effects occur after applying permethrin, discontinue use immediately and consult a healthcare professional.

**How it Works:**

Permethrin works by interfering with the function of sodium channels in the nerve cells of insects and parasites. This disruption causes paralysis and ultimately kills the pests. It's important to note that permethrin is significantly less toxic to mammals (including humans) than to insects due to differences in the structure and function of mammalian nerve cells.  Topical application ensures that the concentration reaching the body's internal systems is minimal.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** Permethrin should not be used by individuals with known hypersensitivity (allergy) to pyrethroids.
* **Drug Interactions:**  While significant drug interactions are rare, informing your doctor or pharmacist of all medications you are currently taking is essential.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  Pregnant and breastfeeding women should consult their healthcare provider before using permethrin.  Use during pregnancy or breastfeeding should only occur under a doctor's supervision.
* **Children and Older Adults:**  Careful monitoring is needed when using permethrin in children and older adults, as they may be more susceptible to side effects. Always follow the specific instructions provided by the healthcare professional.
* **Avoid contact with eyes and mucous membranes.**  If contact occurs, rinse thoroughly with water.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long does permethrin stay in my system?** A: Permethrin is primarily used topically, and significant systemic absorption is generally minimal.  Therefore, it doesn't persist in the body for an extended period.
* **Q: Can I use permethrin more than once?** A:  The number of applications depends on the condition being treated and the specific product instructions. Follow your doctor's or the product label's directions carefully.
* **Q: How should I store permethrin?** A: Store permethrin in a cool, dry place, away from children and pets, in its original container.
* **Q: What should I do if I accidentally ingest permethrin?** A:  Contact a poison control center or your doctor immediately.
* **Q: Is permethrin safe for pets?** A:  Permethrin can be toxic to cats and some other pets.  Keep permethrin products out of reach of your pets. Consult your veterinarian if you have concerns about your pet's exposure.
