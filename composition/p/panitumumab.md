# Panitumumab

**Generic Name: Panitumumab**

**Usage:**

Panitumumab is a monoclonal antibody medication primarily used in the treatment of certain types of colorectal cancer (CRC).  Its main application is for metastatic colorectal cancer (mCRC) that expresses the epidermal growth factor receptor (EGFR).  This means it's effective against cancers where the tumor cells have an excess of EGFR, a protein that promotes cell growth and division.  Panitumumab works by blocking the activity of EGFR, thereby slowing or stopping the growth of cancer cells.  While primarily used for colorectal cancer, it might be considered in other EGFR-expressing cancers, but this is less common and usually in specific clinical trial settings.  The specific use of panitumumab is always determined by a healthcare professional in consultation with the patient's medical history and test results.

**Dosage:**

Panitumumab is administered intravenously (IV) – meaning it's given directly into a vein.  It's not available in tablet or syrup form.  The standard dosage for adults with mCRC is typically 6 mg/kg given as an intravenous infusion every two weeks.  The specific dosage and frequency can be adjusted based on individual factors, such as the patient's response to treatment, overall health, and the severity of the cancer.  There is no established dosage for children as it's not generally used in pediatric populations.  Healthcare professionals carefully monitor patients' response and adjust treatment accordingly.  The infusion process usually takes about an hour.

**Side Effects:**

Common side effects of panitumumab can include:

* Skin rash (including acne-like rash)
* Diarrhea
* Fatigue
* Nausea
* Vomiting
* Headache
* Infusion-related reactions (such as fever, chills, or flushing)

Less common, but potentially serious side effects can include:

* Severe skin reactions (e.g., Stevens-Johnson syndrome)
* Severe diarrhea
* Interstitial lung disease (ILD)
* Heart problems
* Blood clots


**Important Note:** Any new or worsening symptoms should be reported immediately to a healthcare provider.  Prompt attention to side effects can often minimize their impact and prevent serious complications.

**How it Works:**

Panitumumab is a targeted therapy. It functions by specifically binding to the epidermal growth factor receptor (EGFR) on the surface of cancer cells. By binding to EGFR, panitumumab prevents the EGFR from receiving growth signals.  This blockage of the signaling pathway disrupts the cancer cell's ability to grow, divide, and spread.  Essentially, it helps stop the cancer's progression by hindering its key growth mechanisms.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** Panitumumab should not be used in patients with known hypersensitivity (allergic reaction) to panitumumab or any of its components.
* **Drug Interactions:**  There are potential interactions with other medications.  Patients should inform their healthcare provider of all medications, supplements, and herbal remedies they are taking.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:** Panitumumab should be avoided during pregnancy due to potential risks to the fetus.  Women of childbearing age should use effective contraception.  Breastfeeding should be stopped during treatment.
* **Elderly Patients:**  Older adults may be more susceptible to certain side effects, and dosage adjustments might be necessary based on individual health status.
* **Impaired Kidney or Liver Function:**  Dosage adjustments may be required for patients with impaired kidney or liver function.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long will I need to take Panitumumab?** A: The duration of treatment depends on individual response and the progression of the cancer. This is determined by your oncologist.

* **Q: How is Panitumumab stored?** A:  Follow your pharmacist's instructions for storage. Generally, it is stored under refrigeration.

* **Q:  What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Contact your healthcare provider immediately. Do not attempt to adjust your dosage yourself.

* **Q:  Is Panitumumab covered by insurance?** A: Insurance coverage varies. Contact your insurance provider to determine coverage.

* **Q: Are there other treatment options?** A: Yes, numerous other therapies exist for colorectal cancer, and treatment choice depends on various factors, including cancer stage, overall health, and individual responses.  Your oncologist will discuss the best option for you.
