# Pirfenidone

**Generic Name:** Pirfenidone

**Usage:**

Pirfenidone is primarily used to treat idiopathic pulmonary fibrosis (IPF).  IPF is a chronic and progressive lung disease characterized by scarring and stiffening of the lung tissue.  It significantly impacts breathing and overall quality of life. Pirfenidone helps slow the progression of IPF, meaning it can help reduce the rate at which lung damage worsens. While its primary use is in IPF, it's also being investigated for other fibrotic conditions, but these uses aren't yet as widely established or approved.


**Dosage:**

The dosage of pirfenidone is determined by a healthcare professional and is highly individualized.  It typically starts with a low dose and is gradually increased over several weeks to minimize the risk of side effects.  

* **Adults with IPF:**  Treatment generally begins with a low daily dose, gradually increasing to a target daily dose of 801mg to 2403mg, typically divided into three doses taken with food. The exact dosage and rate of increase are determined by the physician based on the individual's tolerance.
* **Children:** Pirfenidone is not currently approved for use in children.
* **Forms:** Pirfenidone is usually available as capsules or tablets for oral administration.

**It's crucial to follow your doctor's prescribed dosage and instructions precisely.**  Do not alter the dose or frequency of administration without consulting your doctor.


**Side Effects:**

Common side effects of pirfenidone can include:

* Nausea
* Diarrhea
* Fatigue
* Headache
* Dizziness
* Skin rash
* Photosensitivity (increased sensitivity to sunlight)
* Loss of appetite
* Abdominal pain
* Weight loss

Less common, but more serious, side effects can include:

* Liver damage (hepatotoxicity) – regular liver function tests are typically monitored during treatment.
* Severe skin reactions
*  Serious allergic reactions.

**If you experience any side effects, particularly serious ones, consult your healthcare provider immediately.**


**How it Works:**

The exact mechanism of how pirfenidone works in IPF isn't fully understood, but it's believed to reduce the production of transforming growth factor-beta (TGF-β), a molecule involved in the scarring process that characterizes IPF.  By inhibiting TGF-β and other inflammatory processes, pirfenidone may help slow down the progression of lung damage.  It also has some antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties.


**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** Pirfenidone should not be used by individuals with severe liver impairment or those with a known hypersensitivity (allergy) to pirfenidone.
* **Drug Interactions:** Pirfenidone may interact with certain medications, including some antidepressants and other drugs metabolized by the liver.  It’s essential to inform your doctor of all medications, including over-the-counter drugs and supplements, you are taking.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:** Pirfenidone should be avoided during pregnancy and breastfeeding unless the potential benefits outweigh the potential risks.  Discuss this with your doctor if you are pregnant, planning to become pregnant, or breastfeeding.
* **Older Adults:** Older adults may be more susceptible to side effects.  Dosage adjustments may be necessary.
* **Photosensitivity:**  Avoid excessive sun exposure and use sunscreen with a high SPF while taking pirfenidone.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long do I need to take pirfenidone?**  A: Pirfenidone is typically a long-term treatment for IPF.  The duration of treatment depends on your individual response and is determined by your doctor.

* **Q: Can I stop taking pirfenidone suddenly?** A: No.  Stopping pirfenidone suddenly can be harmful.  Always follow your doctor's instructions for tapering off the medication.

* **Q: How should I store pirfenidone?** A: Store pirfenidone at room temperature, away from moisture and direct sunlight.

* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it's almost time for your next dose.  Do not take a double dose to make up for a missed dose.

* **Q:  Is pirfenidone addictive?** A: No, pirfenidone is not considered an addictive medication.
