# Perampanel

**Generic Name: Perampanel**

**Usage:**

Perampanel is an anticonvulsant medication primarily used to treat epilepsy.  More specifically, it's an adjunctive therapy, meaning it's used *in addition* to other anti-epileptic drugs (AEDs) to control seizures in adults and children aged 12 years and older with partial-onset seizures, which may or may not spread to the entire brain (secondary generalized seizures).  It’s particularly helpful for managing seizures that are difficult to control with other medications alone. While its primary use is in epilepsy, research is ongoing to explore its potential in other neurological conditions.


**Dosage:**

Perampanel is typically available as tablets.  Dosage is carefully determined by a healthcare professional and is individualized based on the patient's response to treatment and tolerance of the medication.  

* **Adults (18 years and older):** Treatment usually starts with a low dose, gradually increasing over several weeks to reach the optimal effective dose. The maximum daily dose is usually 12mg.

* **Children (12 years and older):**  Dosage is carefully adjusted by a physician based on weight and response to treatment.  It is crucial to follow the specific instructions given by a doctor.

It is extremely important to never adjust the dosage on your own; always consult your healthcare provider for any changes.

**Side Effects:**

Common side effects can include:

* Dizziness
* Drowsiness
* Headache
* Fatigue
* Nausea
* Irritability
* Weight gain

Less common, but serious side effects can include:

* Suicidal thoughts or behavior (seek immediate medical help if these occur)
* Severe skin reactions
* Severe allergic reactions
* Worsening of seizures


If you experience any concerning side effects, stop taking the medication and contact your doctor or other healthcare provider immediately.

**How it Works:**

Perampanel works by selectively blocking AMPA receptors in the brain.  These receptors play a significant role in the transmission of nerve impulses that can trigger seizures. By partially blocking these receptors, perampanel helps to reduce the spread of abnormal electrical activity in the brain, thus helping to prevent or control seizures.


**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** Perampanel is contraindicated (should not be used) in individuals with known hypersensitivity to the medication.

* **Drug Interactions:** Perampanel can interact with other medications, especially those metabolized by the liver. It’s crucial to inform your doctor about all medications, supplements, and herbal remedies you are taking.

* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:** The use of perampanel during pregnancy or breastfeeding requires careful consideration and monitoring by a healthcare professional due to potential risks to the fetus or infant.

* **Older Adults:** Older adults may be more sensitive to the side effects of perampanel, requiring lower doses and careful monitoring.

* **Liver Impairment:**  Patients with liver problems may require dosage adjustments due to potential impact on how the body processes the medication.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: Can I stop taking perampanel suddenly?** A: No, abruptly stopping perampanel can lead to a worsening of seizures.  Always consult your doctor before making any changes to your medication regimen.

* **Q: How should I store perampanel?** A: Store perampanel tablets in a cool, dry place, away from direct sunlight and moisture, as directed on the prescription label.

* **Q: Can I drink alcohol while taking perampanel?** A:  Alcohol can worsen the drowsiness and dizziness associated with perampanel.  It’s best to avoid alcohol while taking this medication.

* **Q:  What should I do if I miss a dose?** A:  Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it is almost time for your next dose. Never take a double dose to make up for a missed one.

* **Q: Will perampanel cure my epilepsy?** A: Perampanel is not a cure for epilepsy but is used to help manage and control seizures.
