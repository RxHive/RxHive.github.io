# Pemetrexed

**Generic Name: Pemetrexed**

**Usage:**

Pemetrexed is a chemotherapy medication primarily used to treat certain types of lung cancer.  Its main application is in the treatment of non-small cell lung cancer (NSCLC), specifically in combination with other chemotherapy drugs like cisplatin or carboplatin.  It's often used for patients with advanced or metastatic disease, meaning the cancer has spread beyond the lungs.  Pemetrexed may also be used in the treatment of malignant pleural mesothelioma, a rare and aggressive cancer affecting the lining of the lungs. While less common, it might be considered for other cancers depending on the specific circumstances and in consultation with an oncologist.  The specific use of pemetrexed and its combination with other treatments will be determined on a case-by-case basis by the patient's healthcare team based on individual factors and the nature of the cancer.

**Dosage:**

Pemetrexed is administered intravenously (IV) – meaning it's injected into a vein.  Dosage is highly individualized and determined by the oncologist based on factors such as the type and stage of cancer, the patient's overall health, and other medications being taken.  There isn't a standard dosage for children as it is primarily used in adult oncology settings.  A typical adult dosage might involve a specific amount of pemetrexed administered over a certain period, often repeated at regular intervals.  The oncologist will carefully monitor the patient's response to treatment and adjust the dosage as needed.  Detailed instructions regarding dosage and administration will always be provided by the healthcare professional managing the treatment.  Pemetrexed is not available in tablet or syrup form.

**Side Effects:**

Pemetrexed, like other chemotherapy drugs, can cause a range of side effects.  These can vary in severity from person to person.  Common side effects may include:

* Fatigue
* Nausea and vomiting
* Decreased appetite
* Diarrhea
* Mouth sores
* Hair thinning or loss
* Anemia (low red blood cell count)
* Low white blood cell count (increasing risk of infection)
* Low platelet count (increasing risk of bleeding)

Less common but serious side effects can include severe allergic reactions, kidney problems, and lung problems.  Any unusual or concerning symptoms should be reported to your doctor or healthcare provider immediately.

**How it Works:**

Pemetrexed works by interfering with the way cancer cells grow and divide.  Specifically, it inhibits enzymes essential for the creation of DNA and RNA, the building blocks of cells.  By disrupting these processes, pemetrexed slows down or stops the growth of cancer cells. This mechanism is particularly effective in certain types of cancer cells that rapidly divide.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** Pemetrexed should not be used in patients with severe kidney or liver impairment.  Pre-existing vitamin B12 deficiency should be treated before starting pemetrexed.
* **Drug Interactions:** Pemetrexed can interact with other medications.  It is crucial to inform your healthcare provider of all medications, supplements, and herbal remedies being taken.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:** Pemetrexed can harm a developing fetus.  Women of childbearing age should use effective contraception during treatment.  Breastfeeding should be avoided during pemetrexed therapy.
* **Older Adults:** Older adults may be more sensitive to the side effects of pemetrexed and require close monitoring.
* **Kidney Function:**  Regular monitoring of kidney function is essential as pemetrexed is primarily eliminated by the kidneys.

**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long will I be on pemetrexed?**  A: The duration of treatment depends on your response to therapy, the type of cancer, and your overall health.  This is determined by your oncologist.
* **Q: How is pemetrexed stored?**  A: Pemetrexed is a medication handled and stored by healthcare professionals.  Specific storage instructions are provided by the pharmacy and healthcare facility where it is prepared and administered.  It is not for home storage.
* **Q:  What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Do not attempt to adjust your dosage or schedule. Contact your oncologist or healthcare provider immediately if you miss a scheduled dose.
* **Q: Are there any long-term effects?** A: Long-term side effects are possible, and they vary among individuals. Your oncologist will discuss and monitor for potential long-term effects.
