# Polymyxin B

**Generic Name: Polymyxin B**

**Usage:**

Polymyxin B is an antibiotic used to treat serious bacterial infections, particularly those caused by Gram-negative bacteria.  Its primary use is in treating infections that are resistant to other antibiotics.  This often makes it a last resort option for infections affecting the skin, eyes, and urinary tract.  While it's sometimes used to treat bloodstream infections (septicemia),  this is generally reserved for cases where other treatments have failed due to the potential for serious side effects.  Polymyxin B is not effective against Gram-positive bacteria or many other types of infections.  It's crucial to remember that using it inappropriately contributes to antibiotic resistance.

**Dosage:**

Dosage of Polymyxin B varies considerably depending on the specific infection, the patient's age and weight, and the route of administration.  There is no standard “one size fits all” dosage.  It’s exclusively prescribed and administered by a doctor or other qualified healthcare professional.  Polymyxin B is available in several forms, including:

* **Topical ointments and drops:** Used for localized skin or eye infections. Dosage will depend on the area of infection and severity.  
* **Intramuscular injections:** Used for more severe systemic infections. Dosage is determined by the physician based on individual factors.
* **Intravenous infusions:**  Administered in hospitals for severe infections requiring high concentrations in the bloodstream. Dosage is meticulously controlled and monitored.


**Side Effects:**

Polymyxin B can have side effects, some common and others less frequent but more serious.  It's crucial to report any adverse effects to your doctor.

* **Common Side Effects:**
    * Pain, redness, or swelling at the injection site (with intramuscular or intravenous administration).
    * Nephrotoxicity (kidney damage) – this is a serious concern, especially with high doses or prolonged use.
    * Neurotoxicity (nerve damage) – symptoms can include numbness, tingling, weakness, or seizures.

* **Less Common but Serious Side Effects:**
    * Allergic reactions (rash, itching, difficulty breathing, swelling).
    * Anaphylaxis (a severe, life-threatening allergic reaction).

**How it Works:**

Polymyxin B is a polypeptide antibiotic that works by disrupting the bacterial cell membrane.  Gram-negative bacteria have an outer membrane that protects them from many antibiotics.  Polymyxin B binds to this outer membrane, increasing its permeability and causing leakage of essential components from the bacterial cell, leading to its death. This targeted action against the bacterial cell membrane makes it effective against many Gram-negative bacteria.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** Polymyxin B should not be used in patients with known hypersensitivity (allergy) to polymyxins.
* **Drug Interactions:**  Certain medications can interact with Polymyxin B, potentially increasing the risk of side effects.  It’s essential to inform your doctor about all medications you're currently taking.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  The use of Polymyxin B during pregnancy or breastfeeding should only be considered if the potential benefits outweigh the risks.  A doctor's guidance is vital.
* **Renal Impairment:**  Patients with kidney problems are at a higher risk of developing nephrotoxicity. Careful monitoring of kidney function is necessary during treatment.
* **Elderly Patients:**  Older adults may be more susceptible to the side effects of Polymyxin B, requiring close monitoring.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long does it take to work?**  A: The time it takes to see an improvement varies depending on the type and severity of the infection.
* **Q: Can I stop taking it when I feel better?** A: No, complete the prescribed course even if you feel better to prevent the infection from recurring and the development of antibiotic resistance.
* **Q: How should I store Polymyxin B?** A: Store it according to the instructions on the label. Usually, this means keeping it at room temperature away from light and moisture.
* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Contact your doctor or pharmacist immediately for guidance.
* **Q: Are there alternatives to Polymyxin B?** A: Yes, other antibiotics may be considered, depending on the type of infection and bacterial resistance.  Always consult with a healthcare professional for treatment options.
