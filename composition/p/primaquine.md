# Primaquine

**Generic Name: Primaquine**

**Usage:**

Primaquine is an antimalarial medication primarily used to prevent and treat malaria caused by *Plasmodium vivax* and *Plasmodium ovale*.  These species of malaria parasites have a dormant liver stage (hypnozoites) that can reactivate months or even years later, causing relapses. Primaquine is unique because it's one of the few drugs effective against these dormant liver stages, thus preventing future relapses.  While it's not effective against the asexual blood stages of malaria on its own, it's crucial in combination with other antimalarials like chloroquine or hydroxychloroquine to completely eradicate the infection and prevent recurrence.  In some cases, it might also be used in combination therapy for other types of malaria, but this is less common.


**Dosage:**

Dosage of primaquine varies significantly depending on the reason for its use (prevention vs. treatment of relapse), the specific malaria parasite involved, the patient's age, weight, and overall health.  It's crucial to follow a doctor's prescription precisely.  There are no standardized pediatric dosages readily available for general consumption as prescription and administration must be overseen by a doctor.  Primaquine is typically available in tablet form, though other formulations may exist.  Incorrect dosage can lead to serious adverse effects.  A healthcare professional will determine the appropriate dose and duration of treatment.


**Side Effects:**

Common side effects can include:

* Nausea
* Vomiting
* Abdominal cramps
* Headache
* Dizziness
* Shortness of breath


Less common but serious side effects include:

*  Hemolytic anemia (destruction of red blood cells), particularly in individuals with glucose-6-phosphate dehydrogenase (G6PD) deficiency.  This is a critical concern and requires thorough testing before primaquine use.
*  Methaemoglobinaemia (a buildup of methemoglobin in the blood).


If you experience any of these side effects, especially those listed as serious, seek immediate medical attention.


**How it Works:**

Primaquine works by targeting the mitochondria of malaria parasites.  Mitochondria are the powerhouses of cells, and primaquine disrupts their function, ultimately killing the parasites.  Its action against hypnozoites (the dormant liver stages) sets it apart from other antimalarials. This unique mechanism is critical for preventing malaria relapses.


**Precautions:**

* **G6PD Deficiency:** Primaquine is absolutely contraindicated in individuals with G6PD deficiency.  This genetic condition can lead to severe hemolytic anemia when primaquine is taken.  Testing for G6PD deficiency is essential before starting primaquine treatment.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  Primaquine should generally be avoided during pregnancy and breastfeeding unless the potential benefits significantly outweigh the risks.  A doctor must assess the situation carefully.
* **Liver and Kidney Disease:**  Patients with liver or kidney disease require careful monitoring when taking primaquine, as it's primarily metabolized by the liver and excreted by the kidneys. Dosage adjustments may be necessary.
* **Drug Interactions:** Primaquine can interact with other medications.  Inform your doctor of all medications, including over-the-counter drugs and herbal supplements, you are taking.
* **Age:**  Dosage adjustments may be needed for older adults and children due to differences in metabolism and potential organ function.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: How should I store primaquine?** A: Store primaquine in a cool, dry place, away from direct sunlight and moisture, as directed on the packaging.
* **Q: Can I take primaquine with alcohol?** A:  Alcohol can exacerbate some side effects.  Consult your doctor before consuming alcohol while on primaquine.
* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A:  Contact your doctor immediately for guidance. Do not double the dose to make up for a missed dose.
* **Q: How long does it take for primaquine to work?** A: The effect varies depending on the use (prophylaxis vs. treatment).  Consult your doctor for specifics regarding your treatment plan.
* **Q: Is primaquine addictive?** A:  Primaquine is not considered an addictive substance.
