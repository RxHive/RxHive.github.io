# Obeticholic acid

**Generic Name: Obeticholic Acid**

**Usage:**

Obeticholic acid is primarily used to treat primary biliary cholangitis (PBC), a chronic liver disease.  It's a bile acid receptor agonist, meaning it works by activating a specific receptor in the body (the farnesoid X receptor or FXR).  In PBC, the liver's bile ducts are damaged, leading to inflammation and scarring. Obeticholic acid helps to reduce this inflammation and slow the progression of the disease.  It's typically used in combination with ursodeoxycholic acid (UDCA) when UDCA alone isn't enough to control the disease.  Research is also exploring its potential use in other liver diseases, such as nonalcoholic steatohepatitis (NASH), but these applications are still under investigation and aren't yet standard treatments.

**Dosage:**

The recommended starting dose for adults with PBC is typically 5mg once daily.  This dose may be increased by a healthcare professional based on individual response and tolerability.  There is no established dosage for children, as obeticholic acid is not currently approved for use in pediatric populations. The medication is available as an oral tablet.  It's important to take the medication as prescribed by your doctor; do not adjust the dose without consulting them.


**Side Effects:**

* **Common:**  Itchiness (pruritus), fatigue, diarrhea, nausea, abdominal pain.
* **Less Common but Serious:**  Increased cholesterol levels, infections, muscle pain.  These warrant immediate medical attention.
* **Rare but Potentially Serious:**  Acute pancreatitis (inflammation of the pancreas), liver injury (hepatotoxicity).

If you experience any adverse effects, especially those listed as less common or serious, consult your healthcare provider immediately.

**How it Works:**

Obeticholic acid works by activating the farnesoid X receptor (FXR), a nuclear receptor found primarily in the liver and intestines.  Activating the FXR reduces the production of bile acids and improves the flow of bile from the liver. In PBC, this reduces inflammation and helps protect the liver from further damage.  The precise mechanisms through which it improves liver health in PBC and other liver conditions are complex and still being actively studied.


**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** Obeticholic acid is contraindicated in patients with severe liver impairment or hypersensitivity to the medication or its components.
* **Drug Interactions:** Obeticholic acid may interact with other medications, particularly those that affect cholesterol or liver function.  It's crucial to inform your doctor about all medications, supplements, and herbal remedies you are taking.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  The safety of obeticholic acid during pregnancy and breastfeeding is not fully established.  Discuss potential risks and benefits with your doctor before using this medication if you are pregnant, breastfeeding, or planning to become pregnant.
* **Older Adults:** Older adults may be more susceptible to side effects.  Careful monitoring is recommended.

**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long does it take to see results?** A:  The effects of obeticholic acid can vary, but improvements in liver function tests are often seen within a few months.
* **Q: Can I stop taking it suddenly?** A: No, you should never stop taking obeticholic acid abruptly.  Always follow your doctor's instructions for tapering off the medication to minimize potential side effects.
* **Q: How should I store it?** A: Store the medication at room temperature, away from moisture and heat.
* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it's almost time for your next dose.  Do not double up on doses.
* **Q: Is it safe to drink alcohol while taking this medication?** A:  It's generally recommended to limit or avoid alcohol consumption while taking obeticholic acid, as it can further stress the liver.  Discuss alcohol consumption with your doctor.
