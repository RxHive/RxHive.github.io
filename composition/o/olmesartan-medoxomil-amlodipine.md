# Olmesartan Medoxomil + Amlodipine

**Generic Name:** Olmesartan Medoxomil + Amlodipine

**Usage:**

Olmesartan medoxomil + amlodipine is a combination medication used primarily to treat high blood pressure (hypertension).  It combines two different types of blood pressure-lowering drugs: an angiotensin II receptor blocker (ARB), olmesartan medoxomil, and a calcium channel blocker, amlodipine.  By working together, these drugs effectively reduce blood pressure.  This combination is often prescribed when a single medication isn't enough to control blood pressure effectively.  While its primary use is hypertension, this combination might be considered in some cases of heart failure to help manage blood pressure and reduce the workload on the heart, but this is a secondary use and requires careful medical assessment.  The use should always be guided by a physician.

**Dosage:**

Dosage is determined by a doctor and varies depending on individual needs and response to treatment. It's not possible to provide specific dosage recommendations here as it’s crucial to follow the prescription exactly.  The medication typically comes in tablet form.  The doctor will consider factors like the severity of your hypertension, your age, kidney function, and other health conditions before determining the appropriate dose.  Adjustments may be made over time based on your blood pressure readings.  Never adjust your dosage without consulting your doctor. There is no approved pediatric dosage for this specific combination.

**Side Effects:**

Common side effects can include:

* Dizziness
* Headache
* Swelling in ankles or feet
* Fatigue
* Nausea
* Cough (less common than with ACE inhibitors)
* Flushing


Less common but serious side effects may include:

* Severe allergic reactions (anaphylaxis) – characterized by difficulty breathing, swelling of the face, lips, tongue, or throat. Seek immediate medical help if this occurs.
* Low blood pressure (hypotension) – leading to dizziness or fainting.
* Kidney problems
* Liver problems


If you experience any concerning side effects, contact your doctor or pharmacist immediately.

**How it Works:**

Olmesartan medoxomil blocks the action of angiotensin II, a hormone that narrows blood vessels, thereby increasing blood pressure.  By blocking angiotensin II, olmesartan helps relax and widen blood vessels, reducing blood pressure. Amlodipine relaxes the muscles in the walls of blood vessels, allowing them to widen and further reducing blood pressure. This combined action of both medications provides a more potent and efficient way to lower blood pressure than either drug alone.


**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** This medication is not suitable for everyone.  Individuals with known allergies to olmesartan, amlodipine, or any of the inactive ingredients should avoid it.  Severe liver disease or problems with the adrenal glands (primary hyperaldosteronism) may also be contraindications.  Consult your doctor to discuss any potential risks.
* **Drug Interactions:**  Olmesartan + amlodipine can interact with other medications, including certain diuretics, potassium supplements, and other blood pressure medications. It's essential to inform your doctor of all other medications, supplements, and herbal remedies you are taking.
* **Pregnancy & Breastfeeding:** This medication is generally not recommended during pregnancy. Consult your doctor immediately if you become pregnant while taking this medication.  It's also recommended to discuss breastfeeding and its implications with your doctor.
* **Older Adults:** Older adults might be more sensitive to the side effects of this medication.  Close monitoring of blood pressure and kidney function is often necessary.
* **Kidney & Liver Disease:**  Dosage adjustments may be necessary for individuals with kidney or liver impairment.

**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long does it take to work?** A: The effect on blood pressure is usually noticeable within a few weeks, but optimal blood pressure control may take longer.

* **Q: Can I stop taking it suddenly?** A: No. Stopping this medication suddenly can lead to a rebound increase in blood pressure.  Always consult your doctor before stopping or changing your dosage.

* **Q: How should I store it?** A: Store it in a cool, dry place, away from direct sunlight and moisture, as instructed on the label.

* **Q: What if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it's almost time for your next dose.  Never double up on doses.

* **Q:  Can I drink alcohol while taking this medication?** A: Excessive alcohol consumption can lower blood pressure and increase the risk of side effects. Moderate alcohol intake should be discussed with your doctor.
