# Oxaliplatin

**Generic Name:** Oxaliplatin

**Usage:**

Oxaliplatin is a chemotherapy drug primarily used in the treatment of colorectal cancer.  Its main application is in advanced colorectal cancer, often in combination with other chemotherapy agents like fluorouracil and leucovorin (known as FOLFOX regimens).  It's also sometimes used in treating other types of cancer, such as stomach cancer and certain types of ovarian cancer, but these are less common primary uses.  The specific treatment regimen, including the combination of drugs and dosage, is determined by a healthcare professional based on the individual patient's condition, other health factors, and the specific type of cancer.

**Dosage:**

Oxaliplatin is administered intravenously (IV) – meaning directly into a vein – and is *never* taken orally. Dosage is highly individualized and determined by a medical oncologist.  It's typically calculated based on body surface area (BSA), which considers both height and weight.  There isn't a standardized dosage for children, as its use in pediatric oncology is less common and highly specialized.  The specific dose, frequency of administration (e.g., every two weeks or three weeks), and duration of treatment will be carefully planned and monitored by the healthcare team. The medication comes as a powder that is mixed with a sterile solution before administration.


**Side Effects:**

Oxaliplatin, like many chemotherapy drugs, can cause a range of side effects.  Common side effects can include:

* Nausea and vomiting
* Diarrhea
* Fatigue
* Peripheral neuropathy (numbness, tingling, or pain in the hands and feet – this can be persistent and even debilitating)
* Mouth sores
* Constipation
* Hearing loss
* Anemia (low red blood cell count)
* Thrombocytopenia (low platelet count)
* Leukopenia (low white blood cell count)
* Allergic reactions (rare but potentially severe)


Less common, but serious side effects include severe allergic reactions, kidney problems, and heart problems.  **Any new or worsening symptoms should be reported immediately to a doctor or other healthcare provider.**


**How it Works:**

Oxaliplatin is a platinum-based chemotherapy drug. It works by damaging the DNA of rapidly dividing cancer cells, interfering with their ability to replicate and grow. This damage ultimately leads to cell death.  While it targets cancer cells preferentially, it can also affect healthy cells, which is why side effects occur.


**Precautions:**

* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:** Oxaliplatin is highly teratogenic (capable of causing birth defects).  Women of childbearing age should use highly effective contraception during treatment and for a period of time after.  Breastfeeding should be stopped during treatment.
* **Kidney Function:**  Oxaliplatin is primarily eliminated through the kidneys.  Patients with impaired kidney function require careful monitoring and potential dosage adjustments.
* **Liver Function:** Liver function should also be monitored, as the liver plays a role in drug metabolism.
* **Drug Interactions:** Oxaliplatin can interact with other medications.  It's crucial to inform your healthcare provider about all medications, including over-the-counter drugs, herbal remedies, and supplements, you are taking.
* **Hypersensitivity:**  Individuals with known hypersensitivity (allergic reaction) to platinum-based compounds should not receive oxaliplatin.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long will I be on oxaliplatin?** A: The duration of treatment varies greatly depending on the individual case and response to therapy. It is determined by your oncologist.

* **Q: How is oxaliplatin stored?** A:  Oxaliplatin is typically stored in a hospital pharmacy under controlled conditions. It is a medication that requires specific handling and storage, and home storage is not appropriate.

* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A:  Never adjust your oxaliplatin dosage or schedule without consulting your healthcare provider.

* **Q: Can I drive while receiving oxaliplatin?** A: Side effects like fatigue and neuropathy may impair driving ability.  Follow your doctor's advice regarding driving while undergoing treatment.

* **Q:  Are there long-term effects?** A:  Long-term side effects, particularly peripheral neuropathy, are possible. Regular monitoring and supportive care are essential.
