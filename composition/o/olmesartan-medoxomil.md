# Olmesartan Medoxomil

**Generic Name: Olmesartan Medoxomil**

**Usage:**

Olmesartan medoxomil is primarily used to treat high blood pressure (hypertension).  Lowering blood pressure helps reduce the risk of serious complications like stroke, heart attack, and kidney disease.  It's an angiotensin II receptor blocker (ARB), a type of medication that works by relaxing blood vessels, making it easier for the heart to pump blood.  While its primary use is hypertension, olmesartan medoxomil may also be used to treat certain types of chronic heart failure, particularly when other treatments aren't fully effective.  This secondary use often involves patients already diagnosed with hypertension and heart failure. It's important to note that the use of olmesartan medoxomil in specific cases should always be guided by a physician's assessment of individual needs and health conditions.

**Dosage:**

Dosage of olmesartan medoxomil varies based on the individual's condition and response to treatment.  It’s usually administered orally as a tablet.  A doctor will determine the appropriate starting dose and may adjust it as needed.

* **Adults (Hypertension):** The typical starting dose is 40 mg once daily.  This can be increased to a maximum of 80 mg daily, depending on blood pressure response.
* **Adults (Heart Failure):** The typical starting dose is lower and may be gradually increased under close medical supervision.
* **Children:**  Olmesartan medoxomil is not generally recommended for children, and its use in this population requires careful evaluation and monitoring by a pediatrician or pediatric cardiologist due to limited data in this age group.  There is no established pediatric dosage.


**Side Effects:**

While generally well-tolerated, olmesartan medoxomil can cause side effects.  The most common are generally mild:

* Dizziness
* Lightheadedness
* Headache
* Fatigue
* Diarrhea
* Upper respiratory infection


Less common, but more serious side effects include:

* Angioedema (swelling of the face, lips, tongue, or throat – this is a medical emergency and requires immediate medical attention)
* Low blood pressure (hypotension)
* Kidney problems
* Changes in blood potassium levels

If you experience any side effects, especially serious ones, contact your doctor or seek immediate medical attention.

**How it Works:**

Olmesartan medoxomil works by blocking the action of angiotensin II, a hormone that narrows blood vessels.  By blocking angiotensin II, olmesartan medoxomil allows blood vessels to relax and widen, reducing blood pressure. This reduced resistance to blood flow makes it easier for the heart to pump blood, improving circulation and lowering the strain on the cardiovascular system. In cases of heart failure, this effect can help improve the heart's ability to pump blood efficiently.

**Precautions:**

* **Pregnancy:**  Olmesartan medoxomil is contraindicated during pregnancy, as it can cause serious harm to the developing fetus.
* **Breastfeeding:**  Consult your doctor before breastfeeding while taking olmesartan medoxomil.
* **Kidney Disease:**  Patients with impaired kidney function may require dose adjustments.
* **Liver Disease:**  Olmesartan medoxomil should be used with caution in patients with liver disease.
* **Drug Interactions:** Olmesartan medoxomil can interact with other medications, including potassium supplements, diuretics, and other blood pressure medications.  Inform your doctor about all medications you are taking.
* **Older Adults:**  Older adults may be more sensitive to the effects of olmesartan medoxomil and require careful monitoring.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: Can I stop taking olmesartan medoxomil suddenly?** A: No, stopping abruptly can lead to a rebound increase in blood pressure.  Always follow your doctor's instructions for tapering off the medication.
* **Q: How should I store olmesartan medoxomil?** A: Store it in a cool, dry place, away from direct sunlight and moisture, as directed on the label.
* **Q:  What if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it's almost time for your next dose.  Don't double up on doses.
* **Q:  Is olmesartan medoxomil addictive?** A: No, olmesartan medoxomil is not considered an addictive medication.
* **Q: Can I drink alcohol while taking olmesartan medoxomil?** A:  Excessive alcohol consumption can lower blood pressure further and increase the risk of side effects. Moderate alcohol intake should be discussed with your doctor.
