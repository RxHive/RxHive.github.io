# Olmesartan Medoxomil + Azelnidipine

**Generic Name:** Olmesartan Medoxomil + Azelnidipine

**Usage:**

Olmesartan medoxomil + azelnidipine is a combination medication primarily used to treat hypertension (high blood pressure).  It combines two active ingredients: olmesartan medoxomil, an angiotensin II receptor blocker (ARB), and azelnidipine, a calcium channel blocker (CCB).  By working together, these ingredients effectively lower blood pressure.  While its primary use is hypertension, it might be considered in managing certain cardiovascular risks associated with high blood pressure, such as reducing the risk of stroke or heart attack in high-risk individuals.  However, the specific use and appropriateness of this combination are always determined by a physician, considering the patient's individual medical history and other health factors. This combination medication is not typically used for children.


**Dosage:**

Dosage is determined individually by a healthcare professional and varies depending on the patient’s blood pressure response and overall health.  This medication typically comes in tablet form.  The standard adult dose is usually one tablet once daily, but this can be adjusted based on individual needs.  There are no established pediatric dosages for this specific combination. It's crucial to follow the prescribed dosage exactly as directed by your doctor.  Never alter the dosage without consulting your physician.


**Side Effects:**

Common side effects can include:

* Dizziness
* Headache
* Fatigue
* Nausea
* Swelling in ankles and feet
* Cough (less common than with ACE inhibitors)

Less common but more serious side effects might include:

* Angioedema (swelling of the face, lips, tongue, or throat) – *Seek immediate medical attention if this occurs.*
* Low blood pressure (hypotension)
* Elevated potassium levels (hyperkalemia)
* Liver problems
* Changes in kidney function


If you experience any adverse effects, even if they seem minor, it is essential to contact your healthcare provider for guidance.


**How it Works:**

Olmesartan medoxomil blocks the action of angiotensin II, a hormone that narrows blood vessels, thereby increasing blood pressure.  Azelnidipine relaxes blood vessels by blocking the entry of calcium into the cells of the blood vessel walls.  This dual action leads to vasodilation (widening of blood vessels), reducing the resistance to blood flow and ultimately lowering blood pressure.


**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:**  This medication is not suitable for individuals with a known hypersensitivity (allergy) to olmesartan medoxomil, azelnidipine, or any other components of the medication.  Pregnant women should not take this medication, as it can harm the developing fetus.

* **Drug Interactions:**  This medication can interact with other drugs, including certain diuretics (water pills), potassium supplements, and other blood pressure medications. Inform your healthcare professional about all medications, supplements, and herbal remedies you are taking.

* **Specific Warnings:** Older adults, individuals with kidney or liver problems, and those with a history of heart failure require careful monitoring while taking this medication.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: Can I stop taking this medication suddenly?** A: No.  Stopping abruptly can cause a sudden spike in blood pressure.  Always consult your doctor before stopping or changing your dosage.

* **Q: How should I store this medication?** A: Store the medication in a cool, dry place, away from direct sunlight and moisture, as directed on the packaging.

* **Q: Can I drink alcohol while taking this medication?** A: Excessive alcohol consumption can lower blood pressure and increase the risk of side effects. Moderate alcohol consumption should be discussed with your doctor.

* **Q: What if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it's almost time for your next dose. Do not take a double dose to make up for a missed one.
