# Ofloxacin + Hydroxypropylmethylcellulose

**Generic Name:** Ofloxacin + Hydroxypropylmethylcellulose

**Usage:**

Ofloxacin + Hydroxypropylmethylcellulose is a combination medication used primarily to treat bacterial infections of the eye.  Ofloxacin is a fluoroquinolone antibiotic effective against a broad range of Gram-positive and Gram-negative bacteria commonly causing ocular infections. The hydroxypropylmethylcellulose (HPMC) acts as a vehicle, a thickening agent that helps the medication stay in contact with the eye for a longer duration, improving its effectiveness. This combination is frequently used for conditions such as bacterial conjunctivitis (pink eye), corneal ulcers, and other bacterial infections of the eye and its surrounding structures.  While primarily used for ophthalmic applications, it's crucial to understand that this specific formulation is *not* intended for systemic (whole-body) infections.  Oral or injectable ofloxacin is used for such purposes, but this combination is strictly for topical ocular use.

**Dosage:**

Dosage instructions will vary depending on the specific formulation (e.g., eye drops, ointment) and the severity of the infection.  A healthcare professional will determine the appropriate dosage. Generally, for adults and children (with appropriate age considerations as determined by the physician),  the medication is applied directly to the affected eye(s) as directed, typically several times a day.  Specific instructions, including the frequency and duration of treatment, are always included on the prescription label and should be followed carefully.  Do not exceed the prescribed dosage.  Children's dosage is always determined by a healthcare professional and will vary based on age and condition.


**Side Effects:**

Common side effects may include:

* Burning or stinging sensation in the eye(s) upon application.
* Temporary blurred vision.
* Mild eye irritation.
* Eye redness.

Less common, but potentially more serious side effects, require immediate medical attention and should prompt a call to your doctor:

* Severe eye pain.
* Vision changes that don't resolve quickly.
* Signs of allergic reaction (rash, swelling, difficulty breathing).
* Changes in eye discharge (color, quantity).


**How it Works:**

Ofloxacin, the antibiotic component, works by interfering with the bacterial DNA replication process. This essentially stops the bacteria from multiplying and allows the body's natural defenses to clear the infection.  The HPMC ensures the ofloxacin remains in contact with the infected area of the eye for an extended period, maximizing its therapeutic effect.  This combined action targets the bacterial infection while providing sustained delivery to the eye.


**Precautions:**

* **Allergy:** Individuals with known allergies to ofloxacin, other fluoroquinolone antibiotics, or HPMC should not use this medication.
* **Contact Lenses:** Remove contact lenses before application and wait at least 15 minutes before reinserting them.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:** Consult your doctor before using this medication if you are pregnant, breastfeeding, or planning to become pregnant. The safety profile in these situations needs individual assessment.
* **Other Medications:** Inform your doctor of all other medications you are currently taking, including over-the-counter drugs and herbal supplements, as interactions are possible.
* **Driving and Operating Machinery:**  Temporary blurred vision may occur, so caution is advised when driving or operating machinery immediately after application.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long does it take to work?** A:  Improvement should be noticeable within a few days, but the full course of treatment should be completed as prescribed, even if symptoms improve sooner.

* **Q: Can I share this medication with others?** A: No, this medication is prescribed for a specific individual and should not be shared.

* **Q: How should I store it?** A: Store at room temperature, away from direct sunlight and moisture.  Keep out of reach of children.

* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Apply the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it's almost time for the next dose.  Do not double the dose.

* **Q:  Is this medication safe for children?** A:  The safety and dosage for children should be determined by a healthcare professional.
