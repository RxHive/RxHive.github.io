# Ornidazole + Diloxanide

**Generic Name: Ornidazole + Diloxanide**

**Usage:**

Ornidazole + Diloxanide is a combination medication primarily used to treat intestinal infections caused by certain parasites.  Its main target is *Entamoeba histolytica*, the parasite responsible for amoebiasis, a disease that can affect the intestines and, in severe cases, the liver.  This combination therapy effectively tackles both the intestinal and extra-intestinal forms of amoebiasis.  Ornidazole, a nitroimidazole, acts directly on the parasite, killing it. Diloxanide furoate, a benzoic acid derivative, helps eliminate the parasite's cysts from the intestines, preventing re-infection.  While not its primary use, this combination might sometimes be considered for other parasitic infections depending on the specific parasite and the physician's assessment.  Always consult a healthcare professional for accurate diagnosis and treatment.


**Dosage:**

Dosage varies depending on the patient's age, weight, and the severity of the infection.  A healthcare professional will determine the appropriate dosage.  Generally, the medication comes in tablet form for adults.  Specific instructions regarding the number of tablets and frequency of administration will be provided by your doctor.  There are usually no formulations specifically designed for children, and pediatric dosage is determined on a case-by-case basis by a physician.  It is crucial to follow the prescribed dosage regimen precisely as instructed.  Never adjust the dosage without consulting a doctor.


**Side Effects:**

Common side effects can include:

* Nausea
* Vomiting
* Diarrhea
* Abdominal pain
* Headache
* Dizziness
* Metallic taste in the mouth

Less common, but potentially serious, side effects may include:

* Allergic reactions (rash, itching, swelling)
* Liver damage (jaundice, dark urine)
* Blood disorders
* Neurological effects (numbness, tingling, seizures)

If you experience any of these side effects, especially the more serious ones, seek immediate medical attention.


**How it Works:**

Ornidazole works by disrupting the DNA of *Entamoeba histolytica*, preventing its replication and ultimately killing the parasite.  Diloxanide furoate, on the other hand, acts by interfering with the parasite's metabolic processes, primarily targeting the cysts in the intestinal tract and preventing their release, thereby reducing the chances of re-infection.  The combination of these two drugs ensures a more comprehensive and effective treatment for amoebiasis.


**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:**  Ornidazole + Diloxanide should not be used by individuals with known hypersensitivity to either Ornidazole or Diloxanide furoate or to other nitroimidazole drugs.
* **Drug Interactions:**  Inform your doctor about all medications you are currently taking, including over-the-counter drugs and herbal supplements, as interactions can occur.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  The use of this medication during pregnancy and breastfeeding should be carefully considered and only undertaken under the strict supervision of a healthcare professional, as the potential risks and benefits need to be weighed.
* **Liver and Kidney Disease:**  Individuals with impaired liver or kidney function may require dose adjustments.
* **Alcohol:**  Avoid alcohol consumption while taking this medication, as it can potentiate side effects.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long does it take to work?** A:  Improvement in symptoms usually starts within a few days, but the full course of treatment must be completed as prescribed.
* **Q: Can I take this medication with other medications?** A:  Always inform your doctor about all other medications you are taking to avoid potential interactions.
* **Q: How should I store this medication?** A: Store in a cool, dry place, away from direct sunlight and moisture. Keep out of reach of children.
* **Q: What if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it's almost time for the next dose.  Do not double the dose.
* **Q: Is this medication safe for everyone?** A:  This medication is generally safe when taken as prescribed, but certain precautions apply to pregnant women, breastfeeding mothers, and individuals with pre-existing medical conditions. Always consult your doctor before starting any new medication.
