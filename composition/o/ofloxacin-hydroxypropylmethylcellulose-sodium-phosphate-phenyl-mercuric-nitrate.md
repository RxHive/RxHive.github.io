# Ofloxacin + Hydroxypropylmethylcellulose + Sodium Phosphate + Phenyl Mercuric Nitrate

**Generic Name:** Ofloxacin + Hydroxypropylmethylcellulose + Sodium Phosphate + Phenyl Mercuric Nitrate

**Usage:** This combination of drugs is primarily used for treating bacterial eye infections.  Ofloxacin is a fluoroquinolone antibiotic that effectively targets a wide range of bacteria commonly causing conjunctivitis (pink eye), keratitis (corneal infection), and other bacterial infections of the eye.  Hydroxypropylmethylcellulose is a viscosity-increasing agent; it helps the medication stay in contact with the eye for a longer time, improving its effectiveness. Sodium phosphate helps adjust the pH to make the solution comfortable for the eye. Phenyl mercuric nitrate acts as a preservative, preventing microbial contamination in the solution.  It's crucial to understand that this combination is specifically for *eye infections* and should not be used for any other condition.


**Dosage:**  Dosage instructions will vary considerably depending on the specific formulation (e.g., eye drops, ointment) and the severity of the infection. A doctor will prescribe the appropriate dosage and frequency.  Generally, for adults and children, it involves instilling one or two drops or a small amount of ointment into the affected eye(s) several times a day, as directed by the physician.  Dosage should never be altered without consulting a healthcare professional.  Children's dosages are typically adjusted based on age and the doctor's assessment.


**Side Effects:**

* **Common:**  Burning, stinging, or itching in the eye; temporary blurred vision.
* **Less Common but Serious:**  Eye pain, excessive tearing, changes in vision (beyond temporary blurring), swelling of the eyelids, allergic reactions (rash, itching, swelling).  In rare instances, more serious systemic side effects associated with Ofloxacin may occur, though this is less likely with topical application.
* **Note:** If any side effects occur, especially those listed as less common but serious, discontinue use and seek medical attention immediately.


**How it Works:** Ofloxacin, the antibiotic component, works by interfering with the bacterial enzyme DNA gyrase, which is essential for bacterial DNA replication and repair. By inhibiting this enzyme, Ofloxacin prevents bacterial growth and ultimately kills the bacteria causing the eye infection. The other ingredients enhance the delivery and preservation of the medication. Hydroxypropylmethylcellulose increases the contact time with the eye, Sodium phosphate ensures the correct pH, and Phenyl mercuric nitrate prevents contamination.


**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:**  This medication should not be used if you have a known allergy to Ofloxacin or any other components of the formulation, including quinolone antibiotics.
* **Drug Interactions:** Although less likely with topical application, potential interactions with other medications should be discussed with a doctor or pharmacist.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  Use during pregnancy or breastfeeding should only occur under the strict supervision of a physician, as the benefits must outweigh the potential risks.
* **Children:** Dosage should always be determined by a pediatrician.
* **Older Adults:** Older adults may be more susceptible to side effects and require careful monitoring.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long should I use this medication?**  A: Continue using the medication as prescribed by your doctor, even if symptoms improve.  Stopping treatment prematurely can lead to recurrence of the infection.
* **Q: Can I use this medication if I wear contact lenses?** A:  Generally, contact lenses should be removed before administering the medication and reinserted after a waiting period (as advised by your doctor or pharmacist).
* **Q: How should I store this medication?** A: Store at room temperature, away from direct sunlight and moisture. Keep out of reach of children.
* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Apply the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it is nearly time for the next dose. Do not double the dose.
* **Q: Is this medication safe for long-term use?** A:  Prolonged use should only be under a doctor's supervision;  long-term use carries a higher risk of side effects.
