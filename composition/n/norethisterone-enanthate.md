# Norethisterone enanthate

**Generic Name:** Norethisterone enanthate

**Usage:**

Norethisterone enanthate is a synthetic progestin, a type of hormone similar to progesterone naturally produced in the body.  Its primary use is in the treatment of **endometriosis**, a condition where the tissue lining the uterus (endometrium) grows outside the uterus, causing pain and potentially infertility.  It works by suppressing the growth of endometrial tissue, reducing symptoms.  

Secondary uses, though less common now with newer treatment options, include:

* **Hormonal contraception:**  While not its primary role currently, it has been used in the past as a long-acting injectable contraceptive.
* **Management of dysfunctional uterine bleeding:**  It can help regulate irregular or heavy menstrual bleeding.
* **Certain types of uterine fibroids:**  In some cases, it may be used to manage symptoms related to uterine fibroids (benign tumors in the uterus).

It's important to note that the use of norethisterone enanthate for these secondary purposes is often considered less frequent now due to the availability of other, potentially better-tolerated treatments.


**Dosage:**

Norethisterone enanthate is typically administered as an **intramuscular injection**.  Dosage and frequency are determined by the healthcare provider based on the specific condition being treated and the individual's response.  

* **Adults (Endometriosis):**  A common regimen involves a single intramuscular injection of 200 mg per month or a dose adjustment based on individual responses.  Treatment cycles may vary from 3 months to a year.
* **Adults (Contraception – Historical Use):**  Specific dosages for contraception varied significantly historically; this is rarely used now.
* **Children:**  Norethisterone enanthate is not typically used in children.

It's crucial to follow the prescribed dosage and schedule precisely as directed by your doctor.  Improper use can impact effectiveness and increase the risk of side effects.


**Side Effects:**

* **Common:**  Weight changes (gain or loss), irregular bleeding, mood changes (including depression), breast tenderness, acne, headaches, and nausea.
* **Less Common, but Serious:**  Blood clots (deep vein thrombosis, pulmonary embolism), liver dysfunction, increased risk of cardiovascular events (heart attack or stroke), and allergic reactions.

Any unexpected or worsening symptoms should be reported to a doctor immediately.


**How it Works:**

Norethisterone enanthate exerts its effects by binding to progesterone receptors in the body.  This interaction inhibits the growth and proliferation of endometrial tissue, thereby reducing the pain and bleeding associated with endometriosis. In terms of contraception, it works by suppressing ovulation and thickening cervical mucus, making it harder for sperm to reach the egg.


**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:**  Norethisterone enanthate should not be used by individuals with certain conditions like blood clots, liver disease, breast cancer, or unexplained vaginal bleeding.  A detailed medical history is vital before initiating treatment.
* **Drug Interactions:**  It can interact with some medications, especially those metabolized by the liver.  Disclose all medications, including over-the-counter drugs and herbal supplements, to your doctor.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  Norethisterone enanthate should not be used during pregnancy or breastfeeding.  It can harm the developing fetus and is not compatible with breastfeeding.
* **Older Adults:**  Older adults may be more susceptible to certain side effects, necessitating careful monitoring.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long does it take to work?** A: The time it takes for norethisterone enanthate to become effective varies, depending on the condition being treated and individual response.  Pain relief from endometriosis might take several weeks.
* **Q: How long should I take it?** A:  The duration of treatment is determined by your healthcare provider based on your condition and response to therapy.
* **Q: How should I store it?** A: Store it as directed on the packaging, typically at room temperature, away from direct sunlight and moisture.
* **Q: Can I stop taking it suddenly?** A: No, never stop taking this medication without consulting your doctor.  Sudden cessation could cause withdrawal symptoms or worsen the underlying condition.
* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A:  Contact your doctor immediately to discuss the best course of action.
