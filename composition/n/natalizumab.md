# Natalizumab

**Generic Name: Natalizumab**

**Usage:**

Natalizumab is a medication primarily used to treat multiple sclerosis (MS) and Crohn's disease.  In MS, it specifically targets relapsing-remitting MS (RRMS) and active secondary progressive MS (SPMS).  Its effectiveness lies in reducing the frequency of relapses and slowing the progression of disability in these conditions.  In Crohn's disease, it's used for those who haven't responded adequately to other treatments, aiming to induce and maintain remission.  It's important to understand that natalizumab is not a cure for either condition; instead, it helps manage symptoms and slow disease progression.  The precise use and effectiveness depend on individual patient response and the severity of the disease.

**Dosage:**

Natalizumab is administered intravenously (IV) by a healthcare professional.  There is no oral or other form of this medication.

* **Multiple Sclerosis:** The standard dose is 300 mg administered as an IV infusion every 4 weeks.  Dosage adjustments based on age or weight are not typically made, but individual response and the severity of the disease will be considered by the prescribing physician.

* **Crohn's Disease:**  The dosage is also typically 300 mg administered IV every 4 weeks.  Again, adjustments aren't usually based on age or weight, but are tailored to the patient's response to treatment and disease severity.

The frequency and duration of treatment are determined by a healthcare professional and are individualized based on the patient's condition and response to the medication.

**Side Effects:**

Common side effects can include:

* Infusion-related reactions (e.g., headache, flushing, nausea, chills)
* Fatigue
* Headache
* Upper respiratory tract infection
* Nausea
* Injection site reactions

Less common, but more serious side effects include:

* **Progressive Multifocal Leukoencephalopathy (PML):** This is a rare but potentially fatal brain infection caused by the JC virus. Regular monitoring for PML is crucial during treatment with natalizumab.
* **Infections:**  Natalizumab can increase the risk of infections, both common and serious.
* **Hypersensitivity reactions:** Allergic reactions can occur, ranging from mild to severe.

**Any new or worsening symptoms should be reported to a doctor immediately.**


**How it Works:**

Natalizumab is a monoclonal antibody that works by blocking the interaction of a specific type of white blood cell (alpha-4 integrin) with adhesion molecules in the blood vessel walls.  This prevents these white blood cells from migrating to the brain and intestines, reducing inflammation and damage in MS and Crohn's disease. By preventing the infiltration of inflammatory cells, it helps slow down the disease process.

**Precautions:**

* **Progressive Multifocal Leukoencephalopathy (PML):** This is a significant risk.  Patients with prior exposure to the JC virus and those on natalizumab for extended periods are at higher risk. Regular monitoring is essential.
* **Infections:** Natalizumab weakens the immune system, increasing the susceptibility to infections.  Patients should be closely monitored for signs of infection.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:** The safety of natalizumab during pregnancy and breastfeeding is not fully established. Consult a healthcare professional to discuss the risks and benefits.
* **Drug Interactions:**  Natalizumab might interact with other medications.  Inform your doctor about all other medications, including over-the-counter drugs and herbal supplements.
* **Other Conditions:**  People with certain other medical conditions might not be suitable candidates for natalizumab.  A thorough medical evaluation is crucial before starting treatment.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long does it take to see results?** A: The time it takes to see results varies depending on the individual and the condition being treated. Improvements may be gradual and not immediately apparent.
* **Q: How is the medication stored?** A:  Natalizumab is administered in a healthcare setting and isn't stored at home.
* **Q: Can I stop taking natalizumab suddenly?** A: No, stopping natalizumab abruptly is not recommended.  Discontinuation should be gradual and supervised by a healthcare professional.
* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Contact your healthcare provider immediately to reschedule your infusion.
* **Q: Are there any long-term effects?** A:  Long-term effects are a possibility, including increased infection risk and the potential for PML.  Regular monitoring is key.  This medication is regularly assessed by health authorities for ongoing safety and efficacy.
