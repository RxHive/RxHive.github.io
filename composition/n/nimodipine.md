# Nimodipine

**Generic Name: Nimodipine**

**Usage:**

Nimodipine is a medication primarily used to prevent cerebral vasospasm after a subarachnoid hemorrhage (SAH), a type of stroke caused by bleeding into the space surrounding the brain.  This vasospasm, a narrowing of blood vessels in the brain, can severely restrict blood flow and lead to further brain damage or death. Nimodipine helps to prevent this dangerous complication by relaxing and widening these blood vessels.  While its primary use is in preventing vasospasm after SAH, research is ongoing to explore its potential in other conditions involving impaired cerebral blood flow, but these are not currently standard uses.  It's crucial to understand that nimodipine does *not* treat the initial bleeding itself; it addresses a potential secondary complication.

**Dosage:**

Dosage is determined by a healthcare professional and varies depending on the individual's condition and response to treatment.  Nimodipine is typically administered orally, most commonly as a capsule or tablet.  There is no pediatric formulation; therefore, dosage for children is not established.  The standard adult dose for preventing cerebral vasospasm after SAH is usually 30 mg every 4 hours, continuously for 21 days, or as directed by a physician.  However, this can be adjusted based on individual factors.  It's vital to follow the prescription instructions precisely.  Intravenous administration may be considered in specific situations, though this is less common due to the availability of oral formulations.

**Side Effects:**

Common side effects can include:

* Headache
* Dizziness
* Flushing
* Nausea
* Low blood pressure (hypotension)
* Swelling in the ankles and feet (edema)
* Fatigue


Less common, but potentially serious, side effects include:

* Severe low blood pressure
* Irregular heartbeat
* Liver problems
* Allergic reactions (rash, itching, swelling)


If you experience any concerning side effects, it's crucial to contact your doctor or seek immediate medical attention.

**How it Works:**

Nimodipine works by selectively blocking calcium channels in the smooth muscle cells of blood vessels in the brain. Calcium plays a critical role in the contraction of these muscles. By inhibiting calcium influx, nimodipine helps to relax the blood vessel walls, preventing or reducing vasospasm and improving blood flow to the brain. This action helps to protect brain tissue from further damage after a subarachnoid hemorrhage.


**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** Nimodipine should not be used in individuals with known hypersensitivity (allergy) to the drug or its components.
* **Drug Interactions:** Nimodipine can interact with other medications, including certain blood pressure medications, affecting their effectiveness or causing adverse effects.  Always inform your doctor of all medications you are taking, including over-the-counter drugs and supplements.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  The use of nimodipine during pregnancy and breastfeeding requires careful consideration of the benefits and risks.  Consult your doctor to assess the potential benefits versus risks.
* **Hepatic Impairment:**  Patients with liver problems may require dosage adjustments due to altered metabolism of the drug.
* **Older Adults:** Older adults may be more susceptible to side effects, particularly hypotension.  Careful monitoring is necessary.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: Can I stop taking nimodipine if I feel better?** A: No,  continue taking the medication as prescribed by your doctor for the full course of treatment, even if you feel better.  Stopping prematurely may increase the risk of complications.

* **Q: How should I store nimodipine?** A: Store nimodipine at room temperature, away from moisture and direct sunlight.

* **Q: Is nimodipine addictive?** A: No, nimodipine is not considered an addictive medication.

* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it is almost time for the next dose. Do not double the dose.

* **Q: Can I drink alcohol while taking nimodipine?** A:  It's best to avoid alcohol while taking nimodipine, as it can interact and worsen some side effects, particularly hypotension.  Consult your physician.
