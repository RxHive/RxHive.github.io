# n-Butyl-2-cyanoacrylate

**Generic Name:** n-Butyl-2-cyanoacrylate

**Usage:**

n-Butyl-2-cyanoacrylate is not a pharmaceutical drug intended for internal consumption or systemic treatment of medical conditions.  Its primary use is as a powerful adhesive, specifically in medical settings for tissue adhesion.  This means it's used to seal wounds and stop bleeding, particularly in situations where sutures might be impractical or undesirable.  This includes situations such as:

* **Closing small wounds:**  It's effective in sealing minor cuts, lacerations, and abrasions, offering a quicker and often less invasive alternative to stitches.
* **Controlling bleeding:**  Its rapid setting time helps control bleeding from small vessels in surgical procedures and in emergency situations.
* **Dental applications:**  It finds use in some dental procedures for bonding materials and sealing minor defects.
* **Surgical tissue approximation:** In some specialized surgeries, it assists in holding tissues together while they heal.


**Dosage:**

There is no "dosage" in the traditional sense for n-butyl-2-cyanoacrylate.  The amount used depends entirely on the size and nature of the wound or area requiring adhesion.  It's applied topically by a healthcare professional in extremely small quantities, precisely where needed.  The application process itself varies depending on the specific medical context.


**Side Effects:**

* **Allergic reactions:** Skin irritation, redness, itching, or rash are possible.  More serious allergic reactions are rare but can occur.
* **Tissue irritation:** Application can cause mild to moderate stinging or burning at the application site.
* **Delayed healing:** In rare cases, the adhesive may interfere with normal wound healing.
* **Excessive hardening:** Incorrect application can lead to areas becoming overly rigid.
* **Infection (rare):**  While rare, there is a risk of infection if proper sterile techniques aren't followed during application.


**Serious (but less common) side effects:**  Anaphylaxis (a severe allergic reaction) is a serious, though rare, potential side effect requiring immediate medical attention.


*Always consult a healthcare provider immediately if you experience any adverse reaction to n-butyl-2-cyanoacrylate.*


**How it Works:**

n-Butyl-2-cyanoacrylate is a type of cyanoacrylate adhesive.  When applied to a wound or tissue, it undergoes a rapid polymerization reaction, meaning its molecules quickly link together to form a strong, flexible bond. This bond effectively seals the wound, stopping bleeding and preventing the entry of bacteria. The process is essentially a highly accelerated form of glueing, tailored for medical use.


**Precautions:**

* **Avoid contact with eyes:**  Accidental contact can lead to serious eye injury.
* **Avoid contact with mucous membranes:**  Similar to the eyes, contact with other mucous membranes can cause significant irritation.
* **Allergy testing:**  Prior allergy testing may be necessary in some cases.
* **Pregnant and breastfeeding women:**  Use should only occur under the direct supervision of a healthcare professional, who will carefully weigh the benefits and risks.
* **Children:**  Use in children should always be under the direct supervision of a healthcare professional.
* **Interaction with other drugs or substances:**  No significant interactions are commonly reported, but it is crucial to inform any healthcare provider of all medications and substances being used.
* **Storage:**  Store in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight and heat.  Keep out of reach of children.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: Is n-butyl-2-cyanoacrylate safe?** A:  When used correctly by a healthcare professional, it's generally safe and effective. However, improper use can lead to complications.
* **Q: How long does it take to set?** A:  The setting time is usually quite rapid, typically within seconds to minutes.
* **Q: How is it removed?** A:  Removal typically involves letting the adhesive naturally degrade over time.  In some cases, a healthcare professional may use specific solvents or techniques to facilitate removal.
* **Q: Can I buy this over the counter?** A: No, it's a medical-grade adhesive and only available for use by healthcare professionals.
* **Q: What happens if it gets in my eye?** A:  Immediate medical attention is crucial.  Seek help immediately from an ophthalmologist or emergency room.
