# Nebivolol + Valsartan

**Generic Name: Nebivolol + Valsartan**

**Usage:**

Nebivolol + Valsartan is a combination medication used primarily to treat hypertension (high blood pressure).  It combines two active ingredients: nebivolol, a beta-blocker, and valsartan, an angiotensin II receptor blocker (ARB).  By working together, these components offer a more comprehensive approach to blood pressure management than either drug alone.  This synergistic effect can be particularly beneficial for patients whose blood pressure isn't adequately controlled with a single medication. While primarily used for hypertension, this combination may sometimes be considered for patients with heart failure, especially those with high blood pressure, although this is often dependent on other health considerations and a doctor's careful assessment.  It is crucial to understand that this medication is prescribed *only* by a doctor and is not a self-treatment option for high blood pressure or any other condition.


**Dosage:**

Dosage of Nebivolol + Valsartan is determined individually by a healthcare professional based on factors like the patient's blood pressure, overall health, and response to the medication.  It typically comes in tablet form.  There's no standard pediatric dosage, as this combination is generally not used in children.  The doctor will carefully adjust the dose to find the optimal balance between blood pressure control and minimizing side effects. Common adult starting dosages involve low-strength tablets, gradually increased until the desired blood pressure is reached.  Always adhere strictly to the doctor's prescription instructions regarding dosage and frequency.


**Side Effects:**

Common side effects can include:

* Dizziness
* Lightheadedness
* Headache
* Fatigue
* Nausea
* Diarrhea
* Constipation
* Swelling in ankles or feet


Less common, but more serious side effects warrant immediate medical attention and include:

*  Sudden shortness of breath or wheezing (possible signs of bronchospasm)
*  Slow heart rate (bradycardia)
*  Severe allergic reactions (rash, itching, swelling of the face, lips, tongue, or throat)
*  Signs of liver problems (jaundice, dark urine, abdominal pain)
*  Persistent cough


If you experience any concerning side effects, contact your doctor or seek immediate medical attention.


**How it Works:**

Nebivolol works by blocking the effects of adrenaline and noradrenaline on the heart, slowing the heart rate and reducing the force of heart contractions. This lowers blood pressure.  Valsartan blocks the action of angiotensin II, a hormone that narrows blood vessels. By blocking angiotensin II, valsartan helps relax and widen blood vessels, further reducing blood pressure.  The combination of these two mechanisms provides a more comprehensive and often more effective reduction in blood pressure than either drug alone.


**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:**  Nebivolol + Valsartan should not be used by individuals with certain conditions, including severe heart failure, severe liver or kidney disease, certain types of heart block, or a history of allergic reaction to either nebivolol or valsartan.
* **Drug Interactions:**  This medication can interact with other drugs, including certain diuretics, other blood pressure medications, and some nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drugs (NSAIDs). Always inform your doctor of all medications, supplements, and herbal remedies you are taking.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  This medication is generally not recommended during pregnancy or breastfeeding. Consult your doctor immediately if you are pregnant, planning to become pregnant, or breastfeeding.
* **Older Adults:** Older adults may be more sensitive to the side effects of this medication, requiring careful monitoring and potential dosage adjustments.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: Can I stop taking this medication suddenly?**  A: No.  Stopping this medication abruptly can lead to a dangerous increase in blood pressure.  Always follow your doctor's instructions regarding discontinuation.

* **Q: How should I store this medication?** A: Store the medication in a cool, dry place, away from direct sunlight and moisture, as directed on the product label.

* **Q: What if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it's almost time for your next dose. Do not double up on doses.

* **Q: Can I drink alcohol while taking this medication?** A:  Moderate alcohol consumption may be acceptable, but excessive alcohol intake can interact negatively with this medication and worsen blood pressure. Consult your doctor.

* **Q:  Will this medication cure my high blood pressure?** A:  This medication helps control high blood pressure, but it's not a cure.  Lifestyle changes, including diet and exercise, are also crucial for long-term blood pressure management.
