# Naphazoline + Chlorpheniramine Maleate + Methylcellulose + Phenyl Mercuric Nitrate

**Generic Name:** Naphazoline + Chlorpheniramine Maleate + Methylcellulose + Phenyl Mercuric Nitrate

**Usage:** This combination medication is primarily used for the symptomatic relief of allergic rhinitis (hay fever) and the common cold.  Its effects target several common symptoms. Naphazoline is a vasoconstrictor, meaning it shrinks swollen blood vessels in the nose, relieving nasal congestion. Chlorpheniramine maleate is an antihistamine that reduces allergy symptoms like sneezing, itching, and runny nose. Methylcellulose acts as a lubricant and viscosity-enhancing agent, helping to soothe irritated nasal passages.  Importantly,  *Phenyl Mercuric Nitrate is a preservative no longer commonly used due to toxicity concerns.*  Products containing this preservative are largely obsolete and should not be used.  **If you encounter a product with this ingredient, do not use it and consult a pharmacist or doctor.**  This information focuses on the active ingredients naphazoline and chlorpheniramine maleate, excluding the now-deprecated phenyl mercuric nitrate.

**Dosage:**  *Because phenyl mercuric nitrate is no longer considered safe, dosage information is not applicable to products containing it.*  Modern formulations without this preservative are rarely found, making specific dosage guidelines difficult to provide.  If you encounter a product with naphazoline and chlorpheniramine, follow the instructions on the label carefully, consulting your pharmacist or doctor if uncertain. Dosage is generally dependent on age and the severity of symptoms,  with specific instructions varying based on the drug formulation (e.g., nasal spray, oral drops).  **Never exceed the recommended dosage.**

**Side Effects:**  The side effects of naphazoline and chlorpheniramine can include:

* **Common:**  Drowsiness, dizziness, dry mouth, headache, insomnia, nausea.
* **Less Common but Serious:**  Rapid heartbeat, high blood pressure, blurred vision, difficulty urinating, allergic reactions (rash, itching, swelling).

If you experience any significant or concerning side effects, stop using the medication and seek immediate medical advice.


**How it Works:** Naphazoline works by constricting blood vessels in the nasal passages, reducing swelling and congestion. Chlorpheniramine blocks the action of histamine, a chemical released by the body during allergic reactions, thereby alleviating symptoms like sneezing, itching, and runny nose. Methylcellulose provides lubrication and helps maintain the medication's consistency in the nasal passages.


**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** This medication should not be used by individuals with known allergies to naphazoline, chlorpheniramine, or other components.  It should also be avoided by those with certain heart conditions, high blood pressure, or glaucoma.
* **Drug Interactions:**  This medication can interact with other drugs, including certain antidepressants and blood pressure medications. Inform your doctor or pharmacist about all medications you are currently taking.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  The use of this medication during pregnancy and breastfeeding should be discussed with a healthcare professional to weigh the benefits and potential risks.
* **Children and Older Adults:** Dosage adjustments may be necessary for children and older adults due to differences in metabolism and potential increased sensitivity to side effects.  Always follow prescribed dosages.
* **Prolonged Use:**  Prolonged use of nasal decongestants like naphazoline can lead to rebound congestion (worsening of symptoms).  Use this medication only for the duration recommended by your healthcare provider.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long can I use this medication?** A: Do not use this medication for extended periods without consulting a healthcare professional.  Prolonged use can lead to rebound congestion.
* **Q: Can I drive after taking this medication?** A:  Drowsiness is a possible side effect. Avoid driving or operating machinery if you feel drowsy.
* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A:  If you miss a dose, take it as soon as you remember unless it is almost time for the next dose. Do not take a double dose.
* **Q: How should I store this medication?** A: Store according to the label instructions, usually at room temperature, away from moisture and direct sunlight.
* **Q:  Are there any alternatives to this medication?** A: Yes, many other allergy and cold medications are available.  Consult your doctor or pharmacist to discuss alternative options.
