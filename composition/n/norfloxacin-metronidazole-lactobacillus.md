# Norfloxacin + Metronidazole + Lactobacillus

**Generic Name:** Norfloxacin + Metronidazole + Lactobacillus

**Usage:**

This combination medication is primarily used to treat bacterial infections of the gastrointestinal tract, particularly those involving both aerobic and anaerobic bacteria.  Norfloxacin is a fluoroquinolone antibiotic effective against a range of aerobic bacteria. Metronidazole targets anaerobic bacteria, those that thrive in oxygen-poor environments.  The addition of *Lactobacillus*, a probiotic, aims to help restore the balance of the gut flora, often disrupted by antibiotic treatment and infection.  This combination is frequently prescribed for conditions such as:

* **Infectious diarrhea:**  Cases caused by a mix of aerobic and anaerobic bacteria.
* **Gastroenteritis:** Inflammation of the stomach and intestines, often bacterial in origin.
* **Bacterial vaginosis (in some formulations):** While not always the primary treatment, it can be considered in certain cases, particularly those with associated gastrointestinal symptoms.  Note that other formulations are typically preferred for BV.

It's crucial to understand that this combination is not suitable for all types of gastrointestinal infections, and a doctor's diagnosis is essential before starting treatment.  Viral infections, for instance, will not respond to this antibiotic combination.

**Dosage:**

Dosage varies greatly depending on the specific formulation (tablet, capsule, suspension), the patient's age, weight, and the severity of the infection.  **This information should not be used as a substitute for a doctor's prescription.**  A healthcare professional will determine the appropriate dose and duration of treatment.  Generally, dosages are individualized and may involve taking the medication several times a day for a specified period (e.g., 7-10 days).  Children's dosages are always significantly lower than adult dosages.

**Side Effects:**

Common side effects can include:

* Nausea
* Vomiting
* Diarrhea
* Abdominal pain
* Headache
* Dizziness
* Rash

Less common, but potentially serious side effects, may include:

* Allergic reactions (e.g., hives, swelling, difficulty breathing)
* Seizures
* Tendonitis or tendon rupture
* Changes in blood cell counts
* *Clostridium difficile* associated diarrhea (CDAD)

**Seek immediate medical attention if you experience any serious side effects.**


**How it Works:**

Norfloxacin and Metronidazole work by targeting bacterial DNA, inhibiting its replication and ultimately leading to bacterial cell death.  Norfloxacin primarily acts against aerobic bacteria, while Metronidazole is effective against anaerobic bacteria.  *Lactobacillus* supplementation aims to replenish beneficial bacteria in the gut that may be depleted by the antibiotics, promoting a healthy gut microbiome and potentially reducing the risk of side effects like diarrhea.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:**  This medication is contraindicated in individuals with known hypersensitivity (allergy) to fluoroquinolones, nitroimidazoles, or any component of the formulation.  Pregnancy and breastfeeding are significant considerations, and this medication should only be used if the benefits clearly outweigh the potential risks.  Always consult a doctor before using this medication during pregnancy or while breastfeeding.
* **Drug Interactions:**  This combination can interact with certain medications, including anticoagulants (blood thinners), antacids, and other antibiotics.  It's crucial to inform your doctor about all medications, supplements, and herbal remedies you are taking.
* **Specific warnings:**  Individuals with a history of seizures, kidney or liver disease, or glucose-6-phosphate dehydrogenase (G6PD) deficiency require careful monitoring while taking this medication. Older adults may be more susceptible to side effects.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: Can I take this medication with alcohol?** A: No.  Metronidazole interacts negatively with alcohol, potentially causing nausea, vomiting, headache, and flushing.  Avoid alcohol consumption during treatment and for at least 24 hours after completing the course.
* **Q: How should I store this medication?** A: Store at room temperature, away from moisture and direct sunlight. Keep out of reach of children.
* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it's almost time for the next dose.  Do not double the dose.
* **Q: Can I stop taking this medication early if I feel better?** A: No.  Complete the entire course of treatment as prescribed by your doctor, even if you feel better, to prevent the infection from recurring and the development of antibiotic resistance.
