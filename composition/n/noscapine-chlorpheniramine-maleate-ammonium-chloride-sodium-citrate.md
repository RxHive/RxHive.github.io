# Noscapine + Chlorpheniramine Maleate + Ammonium Chloride + Sodium Citrate

**Generic Name:** Noscapine + Chlorpheniramine Maleate + Ammonium Chloride + Sodium Citrate

**Usage:** This combination medication is primarily used to relieve the symptoms of the common cold and other upper respiratory tract infections.  It targets several symptoms simultaneously.  Noscapine acts as a cough suppressant (an antitussive), reducing the urge to cough. Chlorpheniramine maleate is an antihistamine, helping to alleviate runny nose, sneezing, and watery eyes associated with allergies and colds. Ammonium chloride and sodium citrate work together as expectorants, thinning the mucus in the airways and making it easier to cough up.  While primarily used for cold symptoms, it might be prescribed off-label for other conditions where these specific symptom reliefs are needed, but this should always be under a doctor's guidance.

**Dosage:** Dosage varies greatly depending on the specific formulation (tablet, syrup, etc.), the age and weight of the patient, and the severity of their symptoms.  Always follow the instructions provided by your doctor or pharmacist.  **Never attempt to self-dose or adjust dosages without professional consultation.**  Formulations for children typically have lower concentrations of the active ingredients, and dosage is usually calculated based on weight.  Adult dosages typically involve taking the medication at regular intervals (e.g., every 4-6 hours), but specific frequency will be prescribed.

**Side Effects:**  Common side effects can include:

* Drowsiness
* Dry mouth
* Dizziness
* Nausea
* Constipation
* Headache
* Stomach upset

Less common but more serious side effects may include:

* Allergic reactions (rash, hives, swelling, difficulty breathing)
* Irregular heartbeat
* Urinary retention
* Confusion (especially in older adults)


If you experience any adverse effects, especially severe or persistent ones, contact your healthcare provider immediately.

**How it Works:** This combination medication works by targeting different aspects of cold and allergy symptoms.  Noscapine acts on the cough center in the brain, reducing the urge to cough. Chlorpheniramine blocks the action of histamine, a substance released during allergic reactions, thereby reducing nasal congestion, sneezing, and watery eyes. Ammonium chloride and sodium citrate increase the fluid content of respiratory secretions, making mucus less viscous and easier to expel through coughing.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** This medication is contraindicated in individuals with certain allergies (to any of the components) or those with specific medical conditions like glaucoma, enlarged prostate, or urinary retention.  Pregnant or breastfeeding women should consult their doctor before using this medication.
* **Drug Interactions:**  This medication can interact with other drugs, including sedatives, antidepressants, and other antihistamines.  Always inform your doctor or pharmacist about all medications you are taking, including over-the-counter drugs and herbal supplements.
* **Specific Warnings:** Older adults and individuals with liver or kidney disease should use this medication with caution under close medical supervision due to the potential for increased risk of side effects.  Children should only use this medication as directed by a physician.  Driving or operating machinery should be avoided if drowsiness occurs.

**FAQs:**

* **Q: Can I take this medication with alcohol?** A: No.  Alcohol can enhance the sedative effects of this medication, increasing the risk of drowsiness and dizziness.
* **Q: How should I store this medication?** A: Store it at room temperature, away from moisture and direct sunlight.  Keep it out of the reach of children.
* **Q: How long can I take this medication?** A:  Do not exceed the prescribed duration.  If symptoms persist beyond a reasonable time (usually 7-10 days for a common cold), consult your physician.
* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it's almost time for the next dose.  Never double the dose.
* **Q: Is this medication habit-forming?** A:  While noscapine has a low potential for abuse, prolonged use should be avoided without physician guidance.
