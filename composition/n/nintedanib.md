# Nintedanib

**Generic Name: Nintedanib**

**Usage:**

Nintedanib is a targeted therapy primarily used to treat two specific lung conditions: idiopathic pulmonary fibrosis (IPF) and systemic sclerosis-associated interstitial lung disease (SSc-ILD).  IPF is a chronic and progressive lung disease characterized by scarring and stiffening of the lung tissue, making it difficult to breathe. SSc-ILD is a similar lung disease occurring in individuals with systemic sclerosis (scleroderma), an autoimmune disorder.  Nintedanib works by slowing the progression of these diseases, helping to prevent further lung damage and improve lung function.  It's important to understand that nintedanib doesn't cure these conditions, but rather aims to manage their symptoms and slow their advancement.  While its primary use is in these pulmonary diseases, research continues to explore its potential in other areas.

**Dosage:**

Nintedanib is typically administered orally as a capsule. Dosage varies depending on the specific condition being treated and the individual's overall health.  A healthcare professional will determine the appropriate dose.  Generally, for adults with IPF or SSc-ILD, the usual starting dose is 150mg twice daily, taken with food.  Dosage adjustments may be necessary based on individual tolerance and response.  There is no established pediatric use of nintedanib.  **It is crucial to strictly follow the prescribed dosage and administration instructions given by your doctor or pharmacist.**  Never alter your dosage without consulting your healthcare provider.

**Side Effects:**

Common side effects of nintedanib often include:

* Diarrhea
* Nausea
* Vomiting
* Decreased appetite
* Fatigue
* Headache
* Liver enzyme elevation (tested through bloodwork)


Less common but serious side effects can include:

* Severe diarrhea
* Severe liver problems
* High blood pressure
* Bleeding
* Perforation of the intestines (rare but potentially life-threatening)


If you experience any severe or concerning side effects, contact your healthcare provider immediately.  They will assess the situation and advise on the appropriate course of action.


**How it Works:**

Nintedanib works by inhibiting specific enzymes called tyrosine kinases.  These enzymes play a role in the processes leading to lung scarring in IPF and SSc-ILD. By blocking these enzymes, nintedanib helps to reduce the production of molecules that contribute to the progression of these diseases. This, in turn, slows down the scarring process and the decline in lung function.


**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** Nintedanib is contraindicated in individuals with known hypersensitivity to nintedanib or any of its components.  Severe liver impairment also represents a contraindication.
* **Drug Interactions:** Nintedanib can interact with other medications, potentially affecting their effectiveness or increasing the risk of side effects.  Inform your doctor about all medications, supplements, and herbal remedies you are taking.  
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:** Nintedanib should not be used during pregnancy unless the potential benefits significantly outweigh the risks.  Women of childbearing potential should use effective contraception during treatment.  Breastfeeding should be discontinued during treatment.
* **Older Adults:** Older adults may be more sensitive to the side effects of nintedanib.  Careful monitoring is necessary.
* **Hepatic Impairment:**  Nintedanib should be used with caution in patients with mild to moderate liver impairment, and is contraindicated in those with severe impairment.  Regular liver function tests are essential.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long will I need to take nintedanib?** A:  The duration of treatment depends on your individual response and your doctor's assessment.  It is usually a long-term treatment for IPF and SSc-ILD.

* **Q: Can I stop taking nintedanib suddenly?** A: No.  Always discuss any changes to your medication regimen with your doctor before stopping treatment. Abrupt cessation could potentially worsen your condition.

* **Q: How should I store nintedanib?** A: Store nintedanib capsules in a cool, dry place, away from direct sunlight and moisture, as directed on the product label.

* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it is almost time for your next dose.  Do not take a double dose to make up for a missed one.  Consult your doctor or pharmacist if you have concerns about missed doses.

* **Q: Is nintedanib safe for everyone?** A:  Nintedanib, like all medications, carries risks and side effects. It's crucial to discuss your individual health status with your doctor to determine if it is appropriate for you.
