* **Thyroxine (T4):** Primarily used to treat hypothyroidism, a condition where the thyroid gland doesn't produce enough thyroid hormone.  This leads to fatigue, weight gain, and other symptoms.

**Side Effects:**

The side effects would depend on the dosage of each individual component and the patient’s overall health.  Potential side effects include:

* **Thyroxine:**  Increased heart rate, anxiety, insomnia, weight loss, tremor, heat intolerance.  In rare cases, more serious cardiac effects.
* **Nicotinamide:** Flushing, nausea, vomiting, liver damage (rare, typically at high doses).
* **Vitamin B Complex:**  Generally well-tolerated, but some individuals may experience gastrointestinal upset (e.g., diarrhea).


**Serious but less common side effects** for any of these components require immediate medical attention.


**How it Works:**

* **Thyroxine:** Replaces the missing thyroid hormone, allowing the body's metabolic processes to function normally.
* **Nicotinamide:**  Plays a vital role in various metabolic pathways, including DNA repair and energy production. It also acts as a precursor to NAD+, a coenzyme essential for cellular function.
* **Vitamin B Complex:** Each B vitamin plays a specific role in metabolism, nerve function, and red blood cell formation.
