# Nilotinib

**Generic Name: Nilotinib**

**Usage:**

Nilotinib is a targeted therapy primarily used to treat certain types of chronic myeloid leukemia (CML).  CML is a cancer of the blood and bone marrow.  Specifically, Nilotinib is effective against CML that has either not responded well to other treatments (like imatinib) or has developed resistance to them.  This means the cancer cells have mutated and are no longer effectively controlled by the initial treatment.  Nilotinib works by targeting a specific protein, BCR-ABL, which is responsible for the uncontrolled growth of leukemia cells.  In some cases, nilotinib may also be used to treat Philadelphia chromosome-positive acute lymphoblastic leukemia (Ph+ ALL), another type of blood cancer.  However, its primary and most widely established use remains in the treatment of CML.


**Dosage:**

Dosage of nilotinib is determined by a physician and varies greatly depending on factors such as the patient's age, weight, overall health, and the specific type and severity of their leukemia.  It's typically administered orally as tablets.  There's no standard pediatric dosage readily available in general guidelines;  dosing for children is carefully individualized based on a comprehensive evaluation by their oncologist.  Adult dosages usually start at a specific milligram amount per day, taken in divided doses.  Adjustments may be necessary based on response to therapy and tolerance of side effects.  Precise dosing instructions are always provided by the prescribing physician and should be followed meticulously.

**Side Effects:**

Common side effects of nilotinib can include:

* Nausea
* Vomiting
* Diarrhea
* Headache
* Fatigue
* Rash
* Muscle aches
* Swelling in the legs and ankles (edema)

Less common, but potentially serious side effects can include:

* Liver problems (hepatotoxicity) – monitor liver function regularly
* Fluid retention (leading to heart failure)
* Serious allergic reactions
* High blood pressure
* Changes in blood counts (neutropenia, thrombocytopenia, anemia)
* Increased risk of bleeding

It is crucial to report any unusual or concerning side effects immediately to a healthcare provider.  Prompt attention to potential side effects can help manage them effectively and prevent serious complications.


**How it Works:**

Nilotinib is a tyrosine kinase inhibitor (TKI).  This means it works by blocking the activity of a specific enzyme, the BCR-ABL tyrosine kinase.  The BCR-ABL protein is a fusion protein formed by a genetic abnormality, the Philadelphia chromosome,  commonly found in CML and Ph+ ALL. This fusion protein signals the cancer cells to grow and divide uncontrollably.  By inhibiting BCR-ABL, nilotinib disrupts this signaling pathway, slowing or stopping the growth of leukemia cells and helping to control the disease.


**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** Nilotinib should not be used in individuals with known hypersensitivity (allergy) to nilotinib or any of its components.
* **Drug Interactions:**  Nilotinib can interact with other medications.  It's crucial to inform your healthcare provider about all medications, supplements, and herbal remedies you are taking before starting nilotinib.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:** Nilotinib is generally not recommended during pregnancy due to potential risks to the developing fetus.  Breastfeeding should be avoided while taking nilotinib.
* **Older Adults and Children:**  Careful monitoring is needed for older adults and children due to potential increased sensitivity to side effects and the need for individualized dosing.
* **Liver and Kidney Function:**  Patients with impaired liver or kidney function may require dosage adjustments.  Regular monitoring of these organ functions is necessary.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long will I need to take nilotinib?** A: The duration of treatment depends on individual response and is determined by your oncologist.  It could range from several months to years.

* **Q: Can I stop taking nilotinib if I feel better?** A:  No, you should never stop taking nilotinib without consulting your physician.  Stopping abruptly can lead to disease progression.

* **Q: How should I store nilotinib?** A: Store nilotinib tablets at room temperature, away from moisture and direct sunlight.

* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it's almost time for the next dose. Do not double the dose.  Consult your doctor or pharmacist if you have any questions.

* **Q:  Are there long-term effects from taking nilotinib?** A: Long-term effects are possible, and regular monitoring by your healthcare provider is crucial to identify and manage them.
