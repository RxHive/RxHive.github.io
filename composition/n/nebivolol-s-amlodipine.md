# Nebivolol + S-Amlodipine

**Generic Name:** Nebivolol + S-Amlodipine

**Usage:**

Nebivolol + S-amlodipine is a combination medication used primarily to treat hypertension (high blood pressure).  It works by targeting two key systems involved in blood pressure regulation.  The nebiivolol component acts as a beta-blocker, slowing down the heart rate and reducing the force of its contractions.  Simultaneously, the S-amlodipine (a calcium channel blocker) relaxes blood vessels, allowing blood to flow more easily. This combined action leads to a more effective reduction in blood pressure compared to using either drug alone.  While its primary use is for hypertension, this combination may sometimes be considered for patients with angina (chest pain) associated with coronary artery disease, though this is generally a secondary application and should be discussed with a physician.


**Dosage:**

Dosage is always determined by a doctor and tailored to individual needs.  There isn't a universally prescribed dose for all patients.  The medication typically comes in tablet form.  Common adult starting doses might involve a single daily tablet containing a specific combination of nebiivolol and S-amlodipine (e.g., 5mg nebivolol/5mg S-amlodipine).  However, the dosage may be adjusted upwards or downwards depending on blood pressure response and individual patient factors like age, kidney or liver function, and other health conditions.  There is no established pediatric dosage for this combination medication.  The medication should be swallowed whole with water, and specific timing instructions (e.g., with or without food) should be followed as directed by the physician.


**Side Effects:**

* **Common Side Effects:** Dizziness, headache, fatigue, nausea, swelling in ankles and feet, slow heart rate (bradycardia).
* **Less Common, but Serious Side Effects:**  Shortness of breath, chest pain, irregular heartbeat, severe allergic reactions (rash, hives, swelling of the face, lips, tongue, or throat), low blood pressure (hypotension).

**Important Note:**  This list is not exhaustive.  If you experience any unusual or concerning side effects, contact your doctor immediately.


**How it Works:**

Nebivolol and S-amlodipine work synergistically to lower blood pressure. Nebivolol, a selective beta-blocker, reduces the heart's workload by decreasing heart rate and the force of contractions.  This reduces the amount of blood pumped out with each beat.  S-amlodipine, a calcium channel blocker, relaxes the blood vessels (vasodilation), thereby decreasing resistance to blood flow.  This combined effect leads to a significant decrease in blood pressure, relieving strain on the cardiovascular system.


**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:**  This medication is contraindicated in patients with certain heart conditions (like severe bradycardia or heart block), severe liver problems, very low blood pressure, or a known allergy to either nebiivolol or amlodipine.
* **Drug Interactions:**  Nebivolol + S-amlodipine can interact with several other drugs, including other blood pressure medications, certain antidepressants, and some heart medications.  It’s crucial to inform your doctor of all medications, supplements, and herbal remedies you are taking.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:** The safety of this combination drug during pregnancy and breastfeeding hasn't been fully established.  Consult your doctor to assess the risks and benefits.
* **Older Adults and Children:**  Older adults may be more susceptible to side effects. Dosage adjustments may be necessary.  This combination is not typically used in children.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: Can I stop taking this medication suddenly?** A: No.  Stopping abruptly can lead to a sudden increase in blood pressure.  Always consult your doctor before making any changes to your medication regimen.
* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it's almost time for your next dose. Do not double the dose.
* **Q: How should I store this medication?** A: Store in a cool, dry place, away from direct sunlight and moisture, as directed on the label.
* **Q: Can I drink alcohol while taking this medication?** A: Excessive alcohol consumption can lower blood pressure further and increase the risk of side effects.  Discuss alcohol consumption with your doctor.
* **Q: Can I drive while taking this medication?** A: Dizziness is a possible side effect.  If you experience dizziness, avoid driving or operating machinery.
