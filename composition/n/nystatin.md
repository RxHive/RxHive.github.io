# Nystatin

**Generic Name: Nystatin**

**Usage:**

Nystatin is an antifungal medication used to treat infections caused by a type of fungus called *Candida*.  These infections commonly manifest as oral thrush (a yeast infection in the mouth), vaginal yeast infections (candidiasis), and diaper rash caused by *Candida*.  While its primary use is treating these superficial fungal infections, nystatin can sometimes be used to treat other *Candida* infections in less common situations, always under the guidance of a healthcare professional.  It's important to understand that nystatin only works against fungi; it's ineffective against bacterial or viral infections.  A proper diagnosis is crucial to ensure effective treatment.

**Dosage:**

Dosage varies significantly depending on the type of infection, the form of nystatin (oral suspension, tablets, cream, ointment, powder), and the patient's age and weight.  Dosage instructions are always provided with the medication and should be followed carefully.  It's crucial to *never* exceed the recommended dosage without consulting a doctor.

* **Oral Thrush:**  The dosage for oral thrush typically involves a specific amount of nystatin oral suspension swished and held in the mouth before swallowing.  Children and adults will receive different dosages based on weight and severity.
* **Vaginal Candidiasis:** Nystatin is often administered as a vaginal tablet or cream, with a specific amount inserted vaginally once or twice daily for a set period.  The exact regimen is prescribed by the doctor.
* **Diaper Rash:**  Nystatin cream or ointment is applied directly to the affected skin area as directed by the healthcare provider.

**Precise dosage information should always come from the prescribing physician or pharmacist, based on individual needs and the specific nystatin formulation.**


**Side Effects:**

Nystatin is generally well-tolerated, but some side effects can occur.  These are usually mild and resolve on their own. Common side effects may include:

* Mild nausea
* Vomiting (rare)
* Diarrhea (rare)
* Mild irritation or burning sensation at the application site (for topical forms)

Serious side effects are rare but require immediate medical attention. These can include:

* Severe allergic reactions (e.g., difficulty breathing, swelling of the face, lips, or tongue)
* Severe stomach pain


**How it Works:**

Nystatin works by binding to sterols in the fungal cell membrane.  This binding disrupts the integrity of the fungal cell membrane, leading to leakage of essential cell components and ultimately causing the fungal cell to die.  Importantly, nystatin does not significantly affect human cells because human cell membranes have different sterol compositions. This selectivity is what makes it relatively safe for use in treating fungal infections.

**Precautions:**

* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  While generally considered safe during pregnancy and breastfeeding when used as directed, it’s always essential to inform your doctor about your pregnancy or breastfeeding status before using any medication.
* **Drug Interactions:**  There are no significant interactions with other medications commonly reported.  However, always inform your doctor or pharmacist of all medications, supplements, or herbal remedies you are taking.
* **Allergic Reactions:**  Individuals with known allergies to nystatin or any of its components should avoid its use.
* **Overdose:**  Accidental overdose is unlikely to cause serious harm, but it’s crucial to contact a poison control center or seek immediate medical attention if suspected.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: Can I stop taking nystatin once my symptoms improve?**  A: No, complete the full course of treatment as prescribed by your doctor, even if you feel better. Stopping early can lead to the infection recurring.

* **Q: How should I store nystatin?** A: Store nystatin as directed on the product label. This usually involves storing it at room temperature, away from moisture and direct sunlight.

* **Q: Is nystatin effective against all types of fungal infections?** A: No. Nystatin is primarily effective against *Candida* species. Other antifungal medications are needed for different types of fungal infections.

* **Q: What should I do if I experience side effects?** A: Mild side effects usually resolve on their own.  However, if you experience any severe side effects or are concerned about any side effects, contact your doctor or pharmacist immediately.
