# Naratriptan

**Generic Name: Naratriptan**

**Usage:**

Naratriptan is a triptan medication primarily used to treat migraine headaches with or without aura.  Its primary purpose is to relieve the pain and associated symptoms of a migraine attack, such as nausea, vomiting, and sensitivity to light and sound.  While not its primary indication,  naratriptan might occasionally be used off-label for cluster headaches in some cases, but this is less common and should always be discussed with a physician.  It's crucial to remember that naratriptan is an *abortive* treatment, meaning it's designed to stop a migraine once it's started, not to prevent migraines from occurring.


**Dosage:**

Naratriptan is typically available as an oral tablet.  The standard adult dosage is 2.5 mg,  taken orally at the onset of a migraine.  A higher dose of 5 mg may be used if needed, but this should only be done under the guidance of a healthcare professional.  There are no established dosages for children, and naratriptan is not generally recommended for pediatric use due to limited data on its safety and effectiveness in younger populations.  The medication should be taken with a glass of water.  Do not exceed the recommended dose.  

**Side Effects:**

Common side effects of naratriptan include:

* Dizziness
* Drowsiness
* Weakness
* Tingling or numbness
* Warmth or flushing sensation
* Nausea
* Dry mouth


Less common, but serious side effects, require immediate medical attention and include:

* Chest pain or tightness
* Severe allergic reaction (e.g., rash, hives, difficulty breathing, swelling)
* Seizures
* Stroke symptoms (e.g., sudden weakness or numbness on one side of the body, difficulty speaking, vision changes)

If you experience any concerning side effects, discontinue use and contact your doctor or other healthcare provider immediately.

**How it Works:**

Naratriptan works by affecting serotonin receptors in the brain. Serotonin is a neurotransmitter believed to play a role in the development of migraine headaches.  By selectively stimulating certain serotonin receptors (5-HT1B/1D), naratriptan constricts blood vessels in the brain and reduces the inflammation associated with migraine pain.  This dual mechanism of action helps to alleviate the symptoms of a migraine attack.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** Naratriptan should not be used by individuals with a history of stroke, heart disease (including angina or uncontrolled hypertension), peripheral vascular disease, or severe liver or kidney impairment.  It's also contraindicated for those with uncontrolled hypertension.
* **Drug Interactions:** Naratriptan may interact with certain medications, including monoamine oxidase inhibitors (MAOIs), ergot derivatives, and some antidepressants.  Always inform your doctor about all medications, supplements, and herbal remedies you are taking.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:** The safety of naratriptan during pregnancy and breastfeeding has not been fully established.  Pregnant women or those who are breastfeeding should discuss the potential risks and benefits with their doctor before using this medication.
* **Older Adults:**  Older adults may be more sensitive to the side effects of naratriptan and may require a lower dose.
* **Caution:**  Do not use naratriptan to treat more than two migraines within a 24 hour period.

**FAQs:**

* **Q: Can I take naratriptan with other migraine medications?** A:  It's crucial to discuss this with your doctor.  Combining naratriptan with other migraine medications could increase the risk of side effects.
* **Q: How should I store naratriptan?** A: Store naratriptan tablets at room temperature, away from moisture and direct sunlight.
* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Naratriptan is used only for acute migraine attacks; there's no need to "make up" a missed dose.  Simply take the medication at the onset of your next migraine.  Do not double the dose.
* **Q:  Can I use naratriptan to prevent migraines?** A: No, naratriptan is an abortive medication designed to stop a migraine in progress, not prevent them.  Other medications are used for migraine prevention.  Discuss preventive options with your doctor.
* **Q:  How long does it take for naratriptan to work?** A:  Relief from migraine pain usually occurs within 2 hours.
