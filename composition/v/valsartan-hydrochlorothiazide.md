# Valsartan + Hydrochlorothiazide

**Generic Name:** Valsartan + Hydrochlorothiazide

**Usage:**

Valsartan + Hydrochlorothiazide is a combination medication used primarily to treat high blood pressure (hypertension).  It's particularly helpful for individuals whose blood pressure isn't adequately controlled with either valsartan or hydrochlorothiazide alone.  Valsartan is an angiotensin II receptor blocker (ARB), working to relax blood vessels and lower blood pressure. Hydrochlorothiazide is a thiazide diuretic, increasing urine production to reduce blood volume and subsequently, blood pressure.  This combination leverages the strengths of both drugs for a more effective blood pressure reduction. In some cases, it may also be used to reduce the risk of cardiovascular events in patients with high blood pressure and evidence of heart or kidney disease.  This is typically a secondary use, prescribed based on individual risk factors and under careful medical supervision.

**Dosage:**

Dosage is determined by a doctor and depends on individual needs and response to treatment. It's not appropriate to self-prescribe or adjust the dosage.  Typically, the medication is administered orally as a tablet, once daily.  Dosage ranges vary, but common adult starting doses include 80mg/12.5mg or 160mg/12.5mg of valsartan/hydrochlorothiazide.  Higher doses may be used, but this should always be under the guidance of a healthcare professional.  The medication is not typically recommended for children, and its use in older adults requires careful monitoring due to potential increased sensitivity to side effects.  Specific dosage adjustments might be necessary for patients with kidney or liver problems.   There are no injectable or syrup formulations of this combination drug.

**Side Effects:**

Common side effects can include:

* Dizziness
* Lightheadedness
* Headache
* Fatigue
* Nausea
* Dry mouth
* Muscle cramps

Less common, but serious side effects can include:

* Swelling in the ankles, feet, or legs
* Persistent cough
* High potassium levels (hyperkalemia)
* Low blood pressure (hypotension)
* Allergic reactions (rash, itching, swelling, difficulty breathing)

If any significant or persistent side effects occur,  it's crucial to seek immediate medical attention.

**How it Works:**

Valsartan blocks the action of angiotensin II, a hormone that narrows blood vessels. By blocking angiotensin II, valsartan allows blood vessels to relax and widen, reducing blood pressure.  Hydrochlorothiazide works by increasing the excretion of sodium and water from the body through the kidneys. This reduces blood volume, further lowering blood pressure. The combination of these two mechanisms provides a more comprehensive approach to blood pressure control than either drug alone.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** Valsartan + Hydrochlorothiazide should not be taken by individuals with known allergy to either valsartan, hydrochlorothiazide, or any of the medication's components.  Severe kidney or liver disease is also a contraindication.
* **Drug Interactions:** Many medications can interact with valsartan + hydrochlorothiazide.  It's crucial to inform your doctor about all medications, supplements, and herbal remedies you are taking.  This is especially important for potassium-sparing diuretics, lithium, and NSAIDs (non-steroidal anti-inflammatory drugs).
* **Pregnancy & Breastfeeding:** This medication is generally not recommended during pregnancy, particularly in the second and third trimesters.  It's also best to avoid breastfeeding while taking this medication.
* **Elderly Patients:** Older adults may be more sensitive to the side effects of this medication, requiring careful dose adjustment and monitoring.

**FAQs:**

* **Q: Can I stop taking this medication suddenly?** A: No.  Sudden discontinuation can lead to a rebound effect, potentially causing a dangerous increase in blood pressure. Always consult your doctor before stopping or changing your dosage.
* **Q: How should I store this medication?** A: Store in a cool, dry place, away from direct sunlight and moisture. Keep it out of reach of children.
* **Q:  What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it's almost time for the next dose. Do not double the dose.
* **Q: Can I drink alcohol while taking this medication?** A: Excessive alcohol consumption can lower blood pressure, potentially leading to an interaction with this medication. Moderate alcohol intake should be discussed with your physician.
* **Q:  Will this medication cause weight gain or loss?** A: The diuretic component might initially cause some fluid loss and weight reduction, but long-term weight changes are less predictable.
