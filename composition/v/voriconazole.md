# Voriconazole

**Generic Name: Voriconazole**

**Usage:**

Voriconazole is an antifungal medication, meaning it fights off fungal infections.  Its primary use is to treat serious fungal infections that are invasive or have spread throughout the body. This includes infections affecting vital organs like the brain (meningitis), lungs (pneumonia), eyes, and heart.  It's particularly effective against a range of fungi including *Aspergillus*, *Candida*, and *Scedosporium*.  While not its primary use, voriconazole may also be used to treat certain fungal infections of the skin, nails, or mucous membranes, particularly in cases where other antifungal treatments haven't worked or are unsuitable.  The decision on whether to use voriconazole for these less severe infections depends on the specific fungus involved, the severity of the infection, and the individual's overall health.  A doctor will carefully weigh these factors.

**Dosage:**

Dosage of voriconazole varies significantly depending on the type and severity of the infection, the patient's age, weight, and kidney or liver function.  It's available in several forms: tablets, oral suspension (liquid), and intravenous (IV) infusion.  A doctor will determine the appropriate dosage and form based on individual needs.  Generally, for adults, the initial dose might be a loading dose followed by a maintenance dose, administered twice daily. Children require a dosage adjusted for their weight and often receive it as an IV infusion or oral suspension.  Precise dosage instructions will always be provided by a healthcare professional.  It's crucial to follow these instructions exactly;  never adjust the dose without consulting a doctor.

**Side Effects:**

Common side effects can include:

* Headache
* Nausea
* Vomiting
* Diarrhea
* Rash
* Dizziness
* Visual disturbances (blurred vision, changes in color perception)


Less common but serious side effects may include:

* Liver problems (hepatotoxicity) – characterized by jaundice, dark urine, abdominal pain.
* Severe skin reactions (Stevens-Johnson syndrome, toxic epidermal necrolysis)
* Heart problems (prolonged QT interval)
* Hallucinations or confusion

If you experience any of these side effects, especially the more serious ones, contact your doctor immediately.

**How it Works:**

Voriconazole works by interfering with the growth and reproduction of fungi. It achieves this by inhibiting the synthesis of ergosterol, a vital component of fungal cell membranes.  Without ergosterol, the fungal cell membranes become unstable and leaky, ultimately leading to the death of the fungal cells.  This mechanism of action makes voriconazole effective against a broad spectrum of fungi.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** Voriconazole should not be used in individuals with known hypersensitivity (allergy) to voriconazole or any of its ingredients.
* **Drug Interactions:** Voriconazole can interact with numerous other medications, affecting their effectiveness or increasing the risk of side effects.  It's crucial to inform your doctor of all other medications, including over-the-counter drugs, herbal supplements, and vitamins, you are taking.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  The use of voriconazole during pregnancy and breastfeeding should be carefully considered, as it may pose risks to the developing fetus or infant.  A doctor will weigh the potential benefits against the potential risks.
* **Liver and Kidney Function:**  Patients with impaired liver or kidney function may require dosage adjustments to avoid toxicity.
* **Visual Disturbances:** The potential for visual disturbances necessitates regular eye exams during treatment.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long do I need to take voriconazole?**  A: The duration of treatment varies depending on the infection’s severity and response to the medication. Your doctor will determine the appropriate treatment length.

* **Q: Can I drink alcohol while taking voriconazole?** A:  It’s generally recommended to avoid alcohol while taking voriconazole, as it can increase the risk of liver damage.

* **Q: How should I store voriconazole?** A: Store voriconazole as directed on the label, typically in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight and moisture.

* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A:  Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it's almost time for your next dose.  Never double the dose to make up for a missed one. Contact your doctor if you have concerns.

* **Q:  Is voriconazole safe for children?** A: Voriconazole can be used in children, but the dosage must be adjusted based on their weight and other factors.  A doctor will determine the appropriate dosage and monitor the child closely for side effects.
