# Varenicline

**Generic Name: Varenicline**

**Usage:**

Varenicline is primarily used to help people quit smoking.  It's a prescription medication that works by reducing withdrawal symptoms and decreasing the rewarding effects of nicotine, making it easier for smokers to abstain.  While its primary use is smoking cessation, it's specifically designed to treat nicotine addiction and isn't generally used for other conditions.  Research into its potential for other applications, such as other forms of addiction, is ongoing, but currently, its approved medical use remains focused on smoking cessation.


**Dosage:**

Varenicline is typically available as a tablet.  The standard adult dosage involves a gradual increase over the first week.  This usually begins with a 0.5 mg dose once daily for three days, followed by increasing to 0.5 mg twice daily for four days.  After this initial phase, the dose is typically increased to 1 mg twice daily for the remainder of the treatment course, which is usually 12 weeks.  However, treatment duration and dosage may be adjusted based on individual needs and response, as determined by a healthcare professional.  

Varenicline is not approved for use in children.  Older adults may require dosage adjustments based on their overall health and other medications they are taking; a doctor should carefully determine the appropriate dose. There are no liquid or injectable forms of varenicline.  Always follow your doctor's instructions precisely regarding dosage and administration.


**Side Effects:**

Common side effects of varenicline can include:

* Nausea
* Vomiting
* Constipation
* Headache
* Sleep disturbances (insomnia, vivid dreams)
* Changes in mood (e.g., irritability, depression)
* Gas
* Dizziness

Less common but more serious side effects can include changes in behavior, such as hostility, aggression, or suicidal thoughts.  Neurological symptoms such as seizures have also been reported, though they are rare.  **If you experience any significant or concerning side effects, contact your doctor immediately.**


**How it Works:**

Varenicline works in the brain by partially activating nicotinic acetylcholine receptors. These receptors are involved in the rewarding effects of nicotine.  By partially activating these receptors, varenicline reduces the cravings and withdrawal symptoms associated with nicotine cessation. It also blocks the rewarding effects of nicotine if a person relapses and smokes, making the experience less satisfying and reducing the likelihood of continued smoking.


**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** Varenicline should not be used by individuals with a history of severe allergic reactions to it.  Its use should be carefully considered in patients with certain cardiovascular conditions, severe kidney or liver impairment, and those with a history of seizures or suicidal thoughts.

* **Drug Interactions:** Varenicline can interact with other medications.  Inform your doctor about all medications, including over-the-counter drugs and herbal supplements, you are currently taking.

* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:** The use of varenicline during pregnancy and breastfeeding should be discussed with a healthcare provider.  The potential risks and benefits need to be carefully weighed.

* **Older Adults and Children:**  Varenicline is not typically recommended for children. Dosage adjustments may be needed for older adults, considering their overall health status and other medications.

* **Driving and Operating Machinery:** Varenicline may cause dizziness or drowsiness. Avoid driving or operating machinery until you know how it affects you.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long does it take to see results?** A:  The effects of varenicline vary from person to person, but many individuals start to notice a reduction in cravings and withdrawal symptoms within a few days to weeks.

* **Q: Can I drink alcohol while taking varenicline?** A:  While there's no absolute contraindication, excessive alcohol consumption is discouraged while using varenicline, as alcohol can worsen some side effects, particularly nausea and dizziness.

* **Q: How should I store varenicline?** A: Store varenicline tablets in a cool, dry place, away from direct sunlight and moisture.  Keep them out of reach of children and pets.

* **Q:  What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: If you miss a dose, take it as soon as you remember, unless it is close to the time for your next dose. Do not double the dose.

* **Q: Is varenicline addictive?** A:  Varenicline itself is not physically addictive in the same way nicotine is, but it's essential to follow your doctor's instructions and complete the prescribed course of treatment.
