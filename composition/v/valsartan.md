# Valsartan

**Generic Name: Valsartan**

**Usage:**

Valsartan is an angiotensin II receptor blocker (ARB).  Its primary use is to treat high blood pressure (hypertension).  By lowering blood pressure, it reduces the strain on the heart and blood vessels, helping to prevent serious complications like heart attack, stroke, and kidney disease.  

Beyond hypertension, valsartan also plays a significant role in treating heart failure. It helps to improve the heart's ability to pump blood effectively and reduces the symptoms associated with this condition.  It may also be used to reduce the risk of cardiovascular events (like heart attack or stroke) in patients who have already had a heart attack or have diabetes with cardiovascular disease.  Finally, some formulations might be used to protect the kidneys in patients with type 2 diabetes and high blood pressure.  The specific use and dosage will always be determined by a physician based on individual health needs and circumstances.


**Dosage:**

Dosage varies widely depending on the condition being treated, the patient's age, weight, and overall health.  It's crucial to follow the prescription exactly as directed by your doctor.  Valsartan is typically available in tablet form.  There are no injectable or syrup forms commonly used.

* **Adults (Hypertension):**  The starting dose is usually 80 mg once daily, which may be increased to a maximum of 320 mg daily, depending on individual response and physician guidance.
* **Adults (Heart Failure):** Dosage starts lower and is gradually increased under close medical supervision.
* **Children:**  Valsartan's use in children is less common and should only be prescribed by a specialist experienced in pediatric cardiology. Dosage is carefully determined based on weight and clinical needs.
* **Older adults:**  Dosage adjustments might be necessary due to potential age-related changes in kidney function.

**Side Effects:**

Common side effects are generally mild and may include:

* Dizziness
* Headache
* Fatigue
* Upper respiratory infection
* Diarrhea
* Nausea

Less common, but potentially serious side effects, include:

* Angioedema (swelling of the face, lips, tongue, or throat) – *Seek immediate medical attention if this occurs.*
* Low blood pressure (hypotension)
* Kidney problems
* Changes in blood potassium levels
* Cough

If you experience any concerning side effects, contact your doctor immediately.


**How it Works:**

Valsartan works by blocking the action of angiotensin II, a hormone that narrows blood vessels. By preventing angiotensin II from binding to its receptors, valsartan causes blood vessels to relax and widen, thus lowering blood pressure. In heart failure, this mechanism helps to reduce the workload on the heart and improve its efficiency.


**Precautions:**

* **Pregnancy:**  Valsartan should not be used during pregnancy, especially in the second and third trimesters, as it can cause serious harm to the developing fetus.
* **Kidney disease:**  Dosage adjustments may be needed for people with kidney problems.
* **Liver disease:**  Valsartan should be used cautiously in individuals with liver disease.
* **Drug interactions:**  Valsartan can interact with certain other medications, including potassium supplements, diuretics, and other blood pressure medications.  Always inform your doctor of all medications, supplements, and herbal remedies you are taking.
* **Older adults:**  Older adults are more susceptible to certain side effects, necessitating careful monitoring and potential dose adjustments.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: Can I stop taking valsartan suddenly?** A: No, abruptly stopping valsartan can lead to a sudden increase in blood pressure. Always follow your doctor's instructions for tapering off the medication.

* **Q: How should I store valsartan?** A: Store valsartan in a cool, dry place, away from direct sunlight and moisture. Keep it out of reach of children.

* **Q: Is valsartan addictive?** A: No, valsartan is not addictive.

* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it's almost time for your next dose.  Never double the dose to make up for a missed one.

* **Q: Can I drink alcohol while taking valsartan?** A: Excessive alcohol consumption can interact with valsartan and lower your blood pressure excessively.  Moderate alcohol intake should be discussed with your physician.
