# Zileuton

**Generic Name: Zileuton**

**Usage:**

Zileuton is a medication primarily used to prevent asthma attacks in adults and children aged 12 years and older.  It's not a rescue medication for immediate relief of asthma symptoms; instead, it works to reduce inflammation in the airways, making it a crucial part of a long-term asthma management plan.  While its primary use is in asthma,  research also explores its potential role in other inflammatory conditions, though this is still largely investigational.  It's important to note that zileuton's effectiveness in treating asthma varies between individuals, and its use should always be guided by a healthcare professional.


**Dosage:**

Zileuton is typically available as tablets.  The dosage varies depending on the patient's age and the severity of their asthma.  A healthcare provider will determine the appropriate dosage individually.  Generally, adults may start with a lower dose and gradually increase it as needed, often taking 600 mg four times daily.  Children aged 12 and older may receive a lower starting dose adjusted based on their weight and response to treatment.  The medication should be taken consistently as prescribed, even when feeling well, to maintain its effectiveness in preventing asthma attacks.  It's crucial to follow a healthcare professional's instructions precisely.  Zileuton is not available as a syrup or injection.


**Side Effects:**

Common side effects can include:

*   Headache
*   Nausea
*   Diarrhea
*   Abdominal pain
*   Elevated liver enzymes (this requires monitoring by blood tests)

Less common, but more serious side effects can include:

*   Jaundice (yellowing of skin and eyes)
*   Liver damage
*   Severe allergic reactions (anaphylaxis)

If you experience any concerning side effects, particularly those listed as more serious, immediately contact your doctor or seek medical attention.


**How it Works:**

Zileuton works by inhibiting the enzyme 5-lipoxygenase (5-LO). This enzyme plays a critical role in the production of leukotrienes, inflammatory substances in the body that contribute significantly to asthma symptoms such as airway narrowing, inflammation, and mucus production. By blocking 5-LO, zileuton reduces the production of leukotrienes, lessening airway inflammation and improving lung function, thereby helping to prevent asthma attacks.


**Precautions:**

*   **Liver Function:** Zileuton can affect liver function; regular liver function tests are essential during treatment.  Individuals with pre-existing liver problems should use this medication with extreme caution, if at all.
*   **Drug Interactions:** Zileuton can interact with other medications.  Always inform your doctor about all medications, supplements, or herbal remedies you are taking.
*   **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  The safety of zileuton during pregnancy and breastfeeding is not fully established.  Pregnant or breastfeeding women should discuss the risks and benefits with their healthcare provider before using this medication.
*   **Children:** Zileuton is approved for children 12 years and older.  Its use in younger children is not recommended.
*   **Older Adults:** Older adults may be more susceptible to side effects, requiring close monitoring.


**FAQs:**

*   **Q: Can I take Zileuton on an as-needed basis?**  A: No, zileuton is a preventative medication, not a rescue medication for immediate asthma relief. It needs to be taken regularly as prescribed.
*   **Q: How should I store Zileuton?**  A: Store Zileuton tablets in a cool, dry place, away from direct sunlight and moisture, as directed on the medication packaging.
*   **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it is almost time for your next dose.  Do not double up on doses.
*   **Q: Can I stop taking Zileuton suddenly?** A:  No, you should not stop taking zileuton suddenly.  Stopping abruptly can lead to a worsening of asthma symptoms.  Talk to your doctor before making any changes to your medication regimen.
*   **Q: Is Zileuton addictive?** A: No, zileuton is not considered an addictive medication.
