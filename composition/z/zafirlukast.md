# Zafirlukast

**Generic Name: Zafirlukast**

**Usage:**

Zafirlukast is a medication primarily used to prevent asthma attacks and manage chronic asthma in adults and children aged 5 years and older.  It belongs to a class of drugs called leukotriene modifiers.  Leukotrienes are substances in the body that trigger inflammation and tightening of the airways, leading to asthma symptoms like wheezing, coughing, and shortness of breath. Zafirlukast works by blocking the action of leukotrienes, thus reducing inflammation and improving lung function.  While primarily used for asthma, it may sometimes be considered for the prevention of exercise-induced bronchoconstriction (EIB), a condition where exercise triggers breathing difficulties.  However, it’s important to note that Zafirlukast is a preventative medication and doesn't provide immediate relief during an asthma attack.  For immediate relief, a rescue inhaler (like albuterol) is necessary.

**Dosage:**

Dosage varies depending on age and the specific formulation.  Typically, Zafirlukast comes as tablets.  

* **Adults and children 12 years and older:**  The usual dose is 20 mg twice daily.
* **Children aged 5 to 11 years:** The usual dose is 10 mg twice daily.

It's crucial to follow the prescribed dosage exactly as directed by your doctor or pharmacist.  Do not adjust the dose on your own. The medication should be taken with or without food.  Zafirlukast is not available in liquid (syrup) or injectable forms.

**Side Effects:**

Common side effects can include:

* Headache
* Nausea
* Diarrhea
* Dizziness
* Stomach pain
* Sleep disturbances

Less common but more serious side effects, which require immediate medical attention, may include:

* Liver problems (jaundice, dark urine, pale stools)
* Allergic reactions (rash, hives, swelling)
* Cholesteatoma (a growth in the middle ear)

If you experience any concerning side effects, contact your doctor or pharmacist immediately.


**How it Works:**

Zafirlukast works by selectively blocking leukotriene receptors in the lungs.  Leukotrienes are inflammatory substances that contribute significantly to asthma symptoms.  By preventing leukotrienes from binding to their receptors, Zafirlukast reduces airway inflammation, relaxes the muscles surrounding the airways, and improves airflow. This results in fewer and less severe asthma attacks.  The effects of Zafirlukast are not immediate; it takes time to build up its preventative effect.


**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** Zafirlukast should not be used by individuals with known hypersensitivity or allergy to the medication.
* **Drug Interactions:**  Consult your doctor or pharmacist about any other medications you are taking, including over-the-counter drugs and herbal supplements, as interactions may occur.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  Discuss the risks and benefits of using Zafirlukast during pregnancy or breastfeeding with your healthcare provider.
* **Liver Disease:**  Zafirlukast should be used with caution in patients with liver disease, as the liver plays a role in its metabolism.
* **Elderly Patients:** Dosage adjustments may be necessary for elderly patients, based on individual needs and overall health.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: Can I take Zafirlukast if I have a sudden asthma attack?** A: No, Zafirlukast is a preventative medication and will not provide immediate relief during an asthma attack. Use a rescue inhaler as directed.
* **Q: How long does it take for Zafirlukast to work?** A: It typically takes several days or weeks to notice the full effects of Zafirlukast.
* **Q: How should I store Zafirlukast?** A: Store Zafirlukast tablets at room temperature, away from moisture and direct sunlight.
* **Q: Can I stop taking Zafirlukast suddenly?** A: No, do not stop taking Zafirlukast abruptly without consulting your doctor.  Stopping the medication suddenly may worsen your asthma.
* **Q: Is Zafirlukast addictive?** A: No, Zafirlukast is not an addictive medication.
