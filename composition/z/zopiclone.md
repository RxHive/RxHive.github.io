# Zopiclone

**Generic Name: Zopiclone**

**Usage:**

Zopiclone is a non-benzodiazepine hypnotic medication primarily used to treat **short-term insomnia**.  This means it's prescribed to help people fall asleep and/or stay asleep when they're experiencing temporary sleep difficulties.  It's specifically for situations where insomnia significantly impacts daily life and isn't a long-term solution for chronic sleep problems.  While not its primary indication, zopiclone may sometimes be used off-label to manage other sleep disorders in specific circumstances under a doctor's careful supervision.  It's crucial to understand that zopiclone is intended for short-term use only, typically no longer than 2-4 weeks.  Prolonged use can lead to dependence and withdrawal symptoms.


**Dosage:**

Dosage is always determined by a doctor and varies based on individual needs and response.  Generally, for adults, the starting dose is 7.5mg taken just before bedtime.  This can be increased to a maximum of 15mg per night if necessary, but only as directed by a physician.  Zopiclone is not typically prescribed for children, and its use in older adults (65 and older) may require lower doses due to potential increased sensitivity.  It's usually available as tablets for oral administration.  Following the prescribed dosage and instructions precisely is crucial.  Crushing or breaking the tablets should only be done if explicitly advised by a pharmacist or doctor.


**Side Effects:**

Common side effects can include:

* Daytime drowsiness
* Dizziness
* Headache
* Nausea
* Dry mouth
* Bitter taste
* Confusion
* Impaired coordination
* Memory problems (anterograde amnesia)

Less common but more serious side effects can involve allergic reactions (rash, itching, swelling), breathing difficulties, and paradoxical reactions (increased anxiety or agitation).  If any adverse effects occur, especially if they are severe or persistent, immediate medical attention should be sought.


**How it Works:**

Zopiclone works by binding to specific receptors in the brain called GABA<sub>A</sub> receptors.  These receptors play a crucial role in regulating sleep and arousal.  By interacting with these receptors, zopiclone enhances the effects of GABA, a neurotransmitter that inhibits neuronal activity. This leads to a calming effect on the brain and promotes sleep onset and maintenance.  Its mechanism differs slightly from benzodiazepines, leading to a potentially reduced risk of certain side effects, but dependence and withdrawal symptoms are still a possibility with prolonged use.


**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** Zopiclone should not be taken if you have a known allergy to zopiclone or similar medications.  It's also contraindicated in individuals with severe respiratory conditions, myasthenia gravis, and sleep apnea.
* **Drug Interactions:** Zopiclone can interact with other medications, including alcohol, opioids, antidepressants, and other central nervous system depressants.  Always inform your doctor about all medications, supplements, and herbal remedies you are taking.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:** Zopiclone should generally be avoided during pregnancy and breastfeeding.  The potential risks to the developing fetus or infant outweigh the benefits.
* **Older Adults:** Older adults may be more sensitive to the effects of zopiclone, necessitating careful dose adjustment and close monitoring.
* **Driving and Operating Machinery:** Zopiclone can significantly impair alertness and coordination. Avoid driving, operating machinery, or engaging in other activities requiring vigilance after taking zopiclone.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long does it take to work?** A: Usually, zopiclone starts working within 30-60 minutes of taking it.
* **Q: Can I take zopiclone with alcohol?** A: No, absolutely not. Combining zopiclone with alcohol can be dangerous and significantly increase the risk of side effects, including respiratory depression.
* **Q: Can I take zopiclone every night?** A: No, it's not intended for long-term use.  It should only be taken for a short period as directed by your doctor.
* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Don't take a double dose to make up for a missed dose.  If you miss a dose, simply skip it and take your next dose at the usual time.  Never adjust your dosage without consulting your doctor.
* **Q: How should I store zopiclone?** A: Store zopiclone in a cool, dry place, away from direct sunlight and heat, and out of the reach of children.
