# Sulphacetamide + Hydrocortisone + Phenyl Mercuric Nitrate

**Generic Name:** Sulphacetamide + Hydrocortisone + Phenyl Mercuric Nitrate

**Usage:** This combination medication is primarily used to treat bacterial conjunctivitis (pink eye) that is accompanied by inflammation and swelling.  Sulphacetamide, an antibiotic, combats the bacterial infection. Hydrocortisone, a corticosteroid, reduces inflammation and swelling. Phenyl mercuric nitrate is an antimicrobial preservative, helping prevent contamination of the eye drops.  While historically used, it's important to note that the use of this combination is decreasing due to concerns surrounding the mercury-containing preservative.  Alternatives with safer preservatives are now often preferred.  This medication is typically applied directly to the affected eye(s).  It's crucial to consult a healthcare professional for diagnosis and treatment as other eye conditions can mimic conjunctivitis.  Self-treating can be harmful and delay appropriate care.


**Dosage:**  Dosage instructions will vary depending on the specific formulation and the physician's prescription.  Typically, ophthalmic (eye) drops are administered one to two drops into the affected eye(s) every two to four hours, as directed.  This combination is not typically available in forms other than eye drops.  **Never adjust the dosage without consulting your doctor.** Children and adults may receive the same dosage, but adjustments may be needed based on the severity of the infection and the doctor's assessment.


**Side Effects:**

* **Common:** Burning or stinging sensation upon application, temporary blurred vision, mild eye irritation.
* **Less Common but Serious:** Allergic reactions (rash, itching, swelling), increased intraocular pressure (eye pressure), secondary infection.  In rare cases, systemic absorption of the medication can lead to more widespread side effects.
* **Mercury-Related Concerns:**  While rare with topical application, mercury exposure can be a significant concern with long-term or excessive use.  
* **Note:** If any side effect is severe or persistent, discontinue use and contact a healthcare professional immediately.


**How it Works:**

Sulphacetamide works by interfering with the ability of bacteria to produce folic acid, a vital nutrient for their growth and survival. This ultimately inhibits bacterial multiplication and helps clear the infection. Hydrocortisone reduces inflammation by suppressing the body's inflammatory response, thus lessening swelling and discomfort associated with conjunctivitis. Phenyl mercuric nitrate acts as a preservative, preventing the growth of microorganisms in the eye drop solution.


**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** This medication should not be used in patients with known allergies to sulphacetamide, hydrocortisone, mercury, or any other component of the formulation.  Patients with viral or fungal eye infections should not use this medication as it is not effective against these types of infections.
* **Drug Interactions:** While rare with topical application, interaction with other medications is possible.  Inform your doctor of all medications, including over-the-counter drugs and herbal supplements, that you are taking.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  Consult your doctor before using this medication during pregnancy or breastfeeding. The safety of this combination during these periods hasn't been extensively studied.
* **Children and Older Adults:**  Use caution when applying this medication to young children or older adults, as they may be more sensitive to its effects.  Care must be taken to avoid accidental ingestion.
* **Contact Lens Use:** Remove contact lenses before applying the eye drops and wait at least 15 minutes before reinserting them.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long should I use this medication?** A:  Continue using the medication as directed by your doctor, even if symptoms improve.  Premature discontinuation can lead to recurrence of the infection.
* **Q: Can I use this medication for other eye conditions?** A: No, this medication is specifically for bacterial conjunctivitis with inflammation. Using it for other conditions could worsen the problem.
* **Q: How should I store this medication?** A: Store the medication at room temperature, away from direct sunlight and moisture. Keep out of reach of children.
* **Q: What should I do if I accidentally ingest the medication?** A:  Contact a poison control center or your doctor immediately if ingestion occurs.
* **Q: Are there safer alternatives?** A: Yes, newer formulations of eye drops for bacterial conjunctivitis exist that avoid the use of mercury-based preservatives.  Discuss these options with your physician.
