# Sitagliptin + Metformin

**Generic Name:** Sitagliptin + Metformin

**Usage:**

Sitagliptin + Metformin is a combination medication used primarily to treat type 2 diabetes mellitus in adults.  This means it's used when your body doesn't produce enough insulin or doesn't use the insulin it produces effectively, leading to high blood sugar levels.  The combination of sitagliptin and metformin is often preferred because it leverages two different mechanisms to manage blood sugar more effectively than either drug alone.  Sitagliptin belongs to a class of drugs called DPP-4 inhibitors, while metformin is a biguanide.  Together, they work synergistically to lower blood glucose levels.  While not a primary use, this combination may be considered in certain cases where diet and exercise alone aren't enough to control blood sugar and where the benefits outweigh the risks for the individual patient.  Always discuss your treatment options with your doctor.


**Dosage:**

Dosage of Sitagliptin + Metformin varies depending on your individual needs and your doctor's assessment.  It's typically started at a lower dose and gradually increased as needed to achieve optimal blood sugar control. There isn't a universally standard dose; the prescription will be tailored to you. This medication comes in tablet form.  The exact dosage and schedule will be determined by your healthcare provider, taking into account your overall health, other medications you are taking, and your response to the treatment.  Children should not typically take this medication.  Elderly patients might require dosage adjustments due to potential kidney function changes.  Never adjust your dosage without consulting your doctor.

**Side Effects:**

Common side effects can include:

* Nausea
* Vomiting
* Diarrhea
* Stomach upset
* Headache
* Dizziness
* Upper respiratory infection

Less common, but more serious side effects might include:

* Severe allergic reactions (rash, hives, swelling, difficulty breathing)
* Lactic acidosis (a rare but serious condition characterized by the buildup of lactic acid in the blood)
* Low blood sugar (hypoglycemia) - particularly if combined with other diabetes medications or if you miss meals.
* Kidney problems

If you experience any concerning side effects, stop taking the medication and seek immediate medical attention.


**How it Works:**

Sitagliptin works by increasing the levels of incretins.  Incretins are hormones that help your body release more insulin after meals and reduce the amount of glucose your liver produces. Metformin works primarily by reducing glucose production in your liver and improving your body's sensitivity to insulin.  By combining these two mechanisms, Sitagliptin + Metformin helps to lower blood sugar levels more effectively than either drug alone.


**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:**  This medication should not be used by people with severe kidney disease, liver disease, or a history of lactic acidosis.  It's also contraindicated in patients with a serious infection, or those who are severely dehydrated.
* **Drug Interactions:** Sitagliptin + Metformin can interact with other medications, including some diuretics and drugs that can affect kidney function.  Inform your doctor of all medications you are taking.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  The use of this medication during pregnancy and breastfeeding should be carefully evaluated with your doctor due to potential risks.
* **Older Adults:** Older adults may require dosage adjustments due to decreased kidney function.
* **Alcohol Consumption:** Excessive alcohol consumption can increase the risk of lactic acidosis.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: Can I take Sitagliptin + Metformin with food?** A: Yes, taking it with food can help reduce stomach upset.
* **Q: How should I store this medication?** A: Store at room temperature, away from moisture and heat.
* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it's almost time for the next dose. Do not double up on doses.
* **Q: Can I stop taking this medication abruptly?** A: No. Always consult your doctor before stopping this medication to avoid potential complications.
* **Q: What are the signs of lactic acidosis?** A: Symptoms include muscle pain or weakness, stomach pain, unusual tiredness, and difficulty breathing.  Seek immediate medical attention if you suspect this.
