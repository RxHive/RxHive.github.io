# S-Amlodipine

**Generic Name:** S-Amlodipine

**Usage:**

S-Amlodipine is a calcium channel blocker primarily used to treat hypertension (high blood pressure).  By relaxing blood vessels, it lowers the resistance to blood flow, thereby reducing the workload on the heart and lowering blood pressure. This is its primary and most common application.

Beyond hypertension, S-Amlodipine also finds use in treating angina pectoris (chest pain caused by reduced blood flow to the heart).  It achieves this by improving blood flow to the heart muscle, reducing the frequency and severity of angina attacks.  In some cases, it may also be used to prevent further cardiovascular events in individuals who have already experienced a heart attack or stroke, although this is typically done in conjunction with other medications.  It's important to note that S-Amlodipine is not a first-line treatment for all cardiovascular conditions, and its use should be determined by a healthcare professional.

**Dosage:**

Dosage of S-Amlodipine varies significantly depending on the individual's condition, age, and response to treatment. It's crucial to follow the prescription exactly as directed by a doctor.  Generally, it is available in tablet form for oral administration.

* **Adults (Hypertension and Angina):**  A common starting dose is 5 mg once daily. This may be increased to 10 mg daily based on individual response and the doctor's assessment.
* **Children:** The use of S-Amlodipine in children is generally limited and requires careful monitoring by a pediatrician due to a lack of extensive studies in pediatric populations.  Dosage is determined by the doctor based on the child's weight and medical condition.
* **Older Adults:**  Older adults may start with a lower dose (2.5 mg) due to increased sensitivity to the medication's effects.  Kidney or liver impairment may also necessitate dose adjustments.

**Side Effects:**

Common side effects are generally mild but can include:

* Headache
* Dizziness
* Swelling in the ankles or feet (peripheral edema)
* Flushing (redness of the face)
* Fatigue
* Nausea

Less common, but more serious side effects require immediate medical attention:

* Severe allergic reactions (anaphylaxis)
* Irregular heartbeat (arrhythmias)
* Chest pain
* Liver damage
* Swelling of the face, lips, tongue, or throat (angioedema)

If any adverse effects occur,  it's essential to contact a healthcare provider immediately.

**How it Works:**

S-Amlodipine belongs to a class of drugs called dihydropyridine calcium channel blockers.  Calcium ions play a vital role in the contraction of heart muscle and smooth muscle in blood vessels.  S-Amlodipine works by blocking the influx of calcium ions into these cells, causing relaxation of the blood vessels. This dilation reduces vascular resistance, leading to lower blood pressure and improved blood flow to the heart.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** S-Amlodipine should not be used by individuals with a known hypersensitivity (allergy) to amlodipine or other dihydropyridine calcium channel blockers.
* **Drug Interactions:**  S-Amlodipine can interact with several other medications, including some grapefruit juices, certain antibiotics, and other cardiovascular drugs.  Always inform your doctor or pharmacist about all medications, supplements, and herbal remedies you are taking.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  The safety of S-Amlodipine during pregnancy and breastfeeding has not been fully established.  Use during these periods should only be considered if the potential benefits outweigh the potential risks. Discuss this with your doctor.
* **Elderly and Individuals with Kidney or Liver Impairment:**  These individuals may require lower doses due to increased sensitivity or impaired metabolism of the drug.
* **Driving and Operating Machinery:**  Dizziness and fatigue are possible side effects. Exercise caution when driving or operating machinery until you know how S-Amlodipine affects you.

**FAQs:**

* **Q: Can I stop taking S-Amlodipine suddenly?** A: No, abruptly stopping S-Amlodipine can lead to a sudden increase in blood pressure and potentially serious health consequences. Always consult your doctor before changing your dosage or stopping treatment.

* **Q: How should I store S-Amlodipine?** A: Store S-Amlodipine at room temperature, away from moisture and direct sunlight. Keep it out of reach of children.

* **Q: Is S-Amlodipine addictive?** A: No, S-Amlodipine is not considered an addictive substance.

* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it's almost time for your next dose.  Never take two doses at once to make up for a missed one.
