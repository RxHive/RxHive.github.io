# Sorbitol Solution

**Generic Name: Sorbitol Solution**

**Usage:**

Sorbitol solution is primarily used as a laxative to relieve constipation.  It works by drawing water into the intestines, softening the stool and making it easier to pass.  This makes it particularly useful for treating occasional constipation or for bowel preparation before medical procedures like colonoscopies.  While not its primary use, sorbitol is also sometimes included in oral medications and other products as a sweetener and filler.  Its role in these cases is not therapeutic, but rather serves as a functional excipient.

**Dosage:**

Dosage of sorbitol solution varies significantly depending on the intended use and the individual's age and health condition.  It's crucial to follow a healthcare professional's instructions precisely.  There is no standardized dosage across all applications.  For constipation relief, an adult might take a specific amount (as prescribed) usually in liquid form, either once or twice daily.  Children's dosages are always lower and strictly determined by a doctor based on factors like weight and age.  Oral forms are most common, but always check the prescribed route and dosage before administering.  Improper dosage can lead to discomfort or even adverse effects.

**Side Effects:**

*   Gas
*   Bloating
*   Abdominal cramping
*   Diarrhea (can be severe in some cases)
*   Nausea
*   Vomiting
*   Dehydration (especially with excessive use or in individuals already dehydrated)

Less common, but potentially serious side effects include:

*   Severe abdominal pain
*   Rectal irritation or bleeding


If you experience any of these side effects, especially the serious ones, contact a healthcare provider immediately.

**How it Works:**

Sorbitol is a sugar alcohol that is poorly absorbed in the small intestine.  This means a significant portion remains in the intestinal tract.  Because of its chemical properties, it draws water into the colon through osmosis. This increase in water content softens the stool and increases its bulk, stimulating bowel movements and relieving constipation.

**Precautions:**

*   **Contraindications:** Sorbitol should not be used by individuals with fructose intolerance or those who have known hypersensitivity (allergy) to sorbitol.  
*   **Drug Interactions:** Sorbitol may interact with certain medications, especially those affecting fluid balance or bowel function.  Always inform your doctor or pharmacist about all medications, supplements, or herbal remedies you are taking.
*   **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:** While generally considered safe in moderate amounts during pregnancy and breastfeeding, it's always best to consult a healthcare provider before use.
*   **Children and Older Adults:**  Children and older adults may be more susceptible to side effects.  Dosage adjustments are often necessary.
*   **Diabetics:** Sorbitol contains calories and can affect blood sugar levels. People with diabetes should monitor their blood glucose carefully if using sorbitol.

**FAQs:**

*   **Q: Can I use sorbitol solution every day?** A: No.  Chronic use of sorbitol as a laxative can lead to dependency and worsen bowel problems. It's intended for occasional relief of constipation.
*   **Q: How should I store sorbitol solution?** A: Store it in a cool, dry place, away from direct sunlight and moisture, as directed on the product label.
*   **Q: Is sorbitol solution safe for long-term use?** A: No, long-term use is not recommended. Consult your doctor if you are experiencing persistent constipation.
*   **Q: What should I do if I overdose on sorbitol solution?** A: Contact your doctor or local poison control center immediately.
*   **Q: Can I mix sorbitol solution with other medications?** A:  Always check with your doctor or pharmacist before mixing it with other medications.  There's a potential for interactions.
