# Secukinumab

**Generic Name: Secukinumab**

**Usage:**

Secukinumab is a biologic medication primarily used to treat moderate to severe plaque psoriasis, a chronic autoimmune skin condition.  It works by targeting a specific protein called interleukin-17A (IL-17A), which plays a significant role in the inflammation and skin cell overproduction characteristic of psoriasis.  Beyond psoriasis, secukinumab also finds application in treating:

* **Psoriatic arthritis:**  This is a type of arthritis associated with psoriasis, causing joint pain, swelling, and stiffness. Secukinumab helps reduce inflammation and improve joint function.
* **Ankylosing spondylitis:** A form of inflammatory arthritis primarily affecting the spine, leading to chronic back pain and stiffness. Secukinumab helps manage the inflammation associated with this condition.
* **Non-radiographic axial spondyloarthritis:** This is a related condition similar to ankylosing spondylitis but without the characteristic x-ray findings.
* **Hidradenitis suppurativa (HS):**  Also known as acne inversa, this chronic inflammatory skin disease causes painful, pus-filled lumps and abscesses in areas like the armpits and groin. Secukinumab can help control the inflammation and reduce the frequency and severity of flares.
* **Ulcerative colitis (UC):** In this inflammatory bowel disease, inflammation affects the colon and rectum, causing symptoms such as diarrhea, abdominal pain, and rectal bleeding. Secukinumab may help to control the inflammation.

The specific use of secukinumab and the most appropriate dosage will be determined by a healthcare professional based on the individual's condition and overall health.


**Dosage:**

Secukinumab is administered as a subcutaneous injection (under the skin) and is *not* available in tablet or syrup form.  Dosage varies depending on the specific condition being treated and the patient's response.  A healthcare professional will determine the correct dosage and schedule. Typical starting doses are often followed by maintenance doses at longer intervals after initial treatment.  Specific dosing instructions will always be provided by the prescribing physician.  Children's dosing is determined on a case-by-case basis and is generally not different than adult dosing.


**Side Effects:**

Common side effects may include:

* Injection site reactions (pain, redness, swelling)
* Headache
* Upper respiratory tract infections (colds)
* Nausea
* Diarrhea
* Fatigue

Less common, but potentially serious, side effects may include:

* Allergic reactions (rash, hives, difficulty breathing, swelling)
* Increased risk of infection (due to immunosuppression)
* Inflammatory bowel disease (IBD) exacerbation
* Candidiasis (yeast infection)
* Depression
* Suicidal thoughts

If any adverse effects occur, it's crucial to contact a healthcare provider immediately.


**How it Works:**

Secukinumab is a monoclonal antibody.  It specifically targets and blocks interleukin-17A (IL-17A), a protein that plays a key role in the inflammatory processes underlying conditions like psoriasis, psoriatic arthritis, and other autoimmune diseases. By neutralizing IL-17A, secukinumab helps reduce inflammation, slowing down the overproduction of skin cells in psoriasis and reducing joint inflammation in arthritis.


**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** Secukinumab is contraindicated in individuals with a known hypersensitivity (allergy) to the medication or its components.
* **Drug Interactions:**  While less common, there can be interactions with other medications. Inform your doctor about all medications you're taking.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  The safety of secukinumab during pregnancy and breastfeeding hasn't been fully established.  Discuss potential risks and benefits with your doctor before use during these periods.
* **Infection Risk:**  Secukinumab can increase susceptibility to infections.  Report any signs of infection to your doctor promptly.
* **Older Adults:** Older adults may be at an increased risk of infection; careful monitoring is necessary.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long does it take to see results?** A: The onset of effects can vary, but improvements are often observed within weeks of starting treatment, though it may take several months to reach optimal response.
* **Q: How is secukinumab stored?** A: Store secukinumab in the refrigerator. Do not freeze.
* **Q: Can I stop taking secukinumab suddenly?** A: No.  Discontinue secukinumab only under the guidance of your healthcare provider to prevent potential relapse or worsening of symptoms.
* **Q: What if I miss a dose?** A: Contact your healthcare provider for instructions on how to proceed.
* **Q:  Is secukinumab safe for long-term use?** A:  Long-term safety is constantly being monitored, but generally, the benefits outweigh the risks for many patients with appropriate monitoring.  Discuss long-term usage with your doctor.
