# Sildenafil

**Generic Name: Sildenafil**

**Usage:**

Sildenafil is primarily used to treat erectile dysfunction (ED), also known as impotence, in adult men.  ED is the inability to achieve or maintain an erection firm enough for satisfactory sexual intercourse.  The drug works by increasing blood flow to the penis, facilitating an erection when sexually stimulated.  

While less common, sildenafil is also approved for the treatment of pulmonary arterial hypertension (PAH). PAH is a type of high blood pressure that affects the arteries in the lungs.  By relaxing the blood vessels in the lungs, sildenafil helps reduce blood pressure in these arteries, improving blood flow and easing symptoms associated with PAH.  It's important to note that the dosage and administration for ED and PAH differ significantly.


**Dosage:**

Dosage varies significantly depending on the condition being treated and the individual's health.  A doctor will determine the appropriate dosage.

* **Erectile Dysfunction:**  The typical starting dose for adults is 50 mg, taken as needed, approximately one hour before sexual activity.  The dosage may be increased to 100 mg or decreased to 25 mg based on individual response and tolerance.  It's generally not recommended to take more than 100 mg in a 24-hour period.

* **Pulmonary Arterial Hypertension:** Dosage for PAH is determined individually by a physician and usually involves a significantly different regimen, often starting with lower doses and gradually increasing as tolerated.  This is a chronic condition requiring ongoing treatment, unlike the "as-needed" use in ED.

Sildenafil is typically available as an oral tablet.  Other forms may exist, but the tablet is the most common.  It's crucial to follow the doctor's instructions precisely regarding dosage and administration.


**Side Effects:**

Common side effects include:

* Headache
* Facial flushing
* Nasal congestion
* Upset stomach
* Dizziness
* Visual disturbances (blurred vision, changes in color perception)

Less common but serious side effects include:

* Prolonged erection (priapism) – requires immediate medical attention.
* Sudden vision loss
* Sudden hearing loss
* Heart attack
* Stroke

If you experience any side effects, especially serious ones, contact your healthcare provider immediately.


**How it Works:**

Sildenafil works by inhibiting a specific enzyme called phosphodiesterase-5 (PDE5).  PDE5 normally breaks down a substance called cyclic guanosine monophosphate (cGMP), which plays a crucial role in blood vessel relaxation.  By inhibiting PDE5, sildenafil allows cGMP levels to increase, leading to relaxation of the smooth muscles in blood vessels.  This increased blood flow is the mechanism behind its effectiveness in both ED and PAH.  In ED, this increased blood flow to the penis facilitates erection.  In PAH, it improves blood flow to the lungs, reducing pressure.


**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** Sildenafil is contraindicated in individuals with known hypersensitivity to the drug, those taking certain nitrates (used to treat angina), and those with severe liver or kidney problems.  It should not be used with other PDE5 inhibitors.

* **Drug Interactions:**  Sildenafil can interact with several other medications, including nitrates, alpha-blockers, and some antifungal medications.  Always inform your doctor of all medications you are taking.

* **Specific Warnings:** Caution is advised in individuals with heart conditions, stroke history, or retinal problems.  Pregnant and breastfeeding women should not use sildenafil.  Its use in children is not typically indicated, except in specific cases of PAH under strict medical supervision.  Older adults may require dosage adjustments due to age-related changes in liver and kidney function.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long does it take to work?** A: For ED, the effects generally begin within 30-60 minutes of taking the medication.

* **Q: How long does the effect last?** A: The effects typically last for 4-5 hours.

* **Q: Can I drink alcohol while taking it?** A: Excessive alcohol consumption can impair the effectiveness of sildenafil and may increase the risk of side effects. Moderate alcohol intake is generally acceptable but consult your doctor.

* **Q: How should I store it?** A: Store sildenafil at room temperature, away from moisture and direct sunlight. Keep it out of reach of children.

* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: For ED, take it as soon as you remember if sexual activity is still planned.  Do not take a double dose. For PAH, follow your doctor's instructions for missed doses.

* **Q: Is it addictive?** A: No, sildenafil is not considered addictive.
