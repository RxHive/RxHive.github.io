# Sodium Cromoglycate

**Generic Name: Sodium Cromoglycate**

**Usage:**

Sodium cromoglycate is a medication primarily used to prevent asthma attacks and allergic reactions.  Its main role is to *prevent* symptoms, not to treat them once they've started.  This means it's most effective when taken regularly, as prescribed, even when you're feeling well.  It works by stabilizing mast cells, preventing the release of histamine and other inflammatory substances that trigger allergic reactions.

Primarily, sodium cromoglycate is used for:

* **Asthma:**  It helps prevent asthma symptoms like wheezing, coughing, and shortness of breath triggered by allergens (like pollen or pet dander) or irritants. It's often part of a broader asthma management plan.
* **Allergic rhinitis (hay fever):**  It can reduce symptoms like sneezing, runny nose, and itchy eyes associated with seasonal allergies or allergies to specific substances.
* **Conjunctivitis (allergic eye inflammation):**  It's available in eye drop form to alleviate symptoms of allergic conjunctivitis, such as itching, redness, and watering eyes.


While less common, it may also be used to treat other allergic conditions based on a doctor's assessment.


**Dosage:**

Dosage varies significantly depending on the specific condition being treated, the formulation of the medication (e.g., inhaler, nasal spray, eye drops, tablets), and the patient's age and weight.  It's crucial to follow the instructions provided by your doctor or pharmacist precisely.  **Never adjust the dosage yourself.**  Examples of dosage ranges are provided below, but these are not exhaustive and should not substitute professional medical advice:

* **Asthma (Inhaler):** Typically, two puffs four times daily, but this can vary greatly depending on individual needs and severity.
* **Allergic Rhinitis (Nasal Spray):**  Usually one or two sprays in each nostril twice daily, again depending on individual needs.
* **Allergic Conjunctivitis (Eye Drops):** One or two drops in the affected eye(s) several times a day.
* **Children:** Dosage for children is adjusted based on their age and weight, and should always be determined by a healthcare professional.


**Side Effects:**

Common side effects are usually mild and may include:

* Mild cough or throat irritation (especially with inhaler formulations)
* Nasal discomfort (with nasal spray)
* A slightly bitter taste (with some formulations)
* Eye irritation (with eye drops)

Less common, but more serious side effects are rare but should be reported to your doctor immediately:

* Severe allergic reactions (anaphylaxis): This is rare but can be life-threatening and requires immediate medical attention. Symptoms may include swelling of the face, tongue, or throat, difficulty breathing, and hives.


**How it Works:**

Sodium cromoglycate works by stabilizing mast cells. Mast cells are immune cells that release histamine and other inflammatory substances when triggered by allergens. By stabilizing these cells, sodium cromoglycate prevents the release of these inflammatory mediators, thus reducing the symptoms of allergic reactions and preventing asthma attacks.  It doesn't treat the underlying cause of the allergy, but it effectively manages the symptoms.


**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** Sodium cromoglycate is generally well-tolerated, but it's essential to inform your doctor of any allergies or pre-existing medical conditions before starting treatment.
* **Drug Interactions:** There are currently no major known significant drug interactions with sodium cromoglycate. However, always inform your doctor or pharmacist of all medications, supplements, or herbal remedies you are taking.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  Use during pregnancy and breastfeeding should only be considered after careful assessment by a doctor.
* **Elderly Patients:**  Dosage adjustments might be necessary for older adults, depending on their overall health.
* **Children:**  Dosage should always be determined by a healthcare professional based on the child's age and weight.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: Can I use sodium cromoglycate to treat an asthma attack that has already started?** A: No, sodium cromoglycate is a preventative medication, not a rescue medication. Use a prescribed reliever inhaler for immediate relief during an asthma attack.
* **Q: How should I store sodium cromoglycate?** A: Store it as directed on the product label.  Generally, this means in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight and heat.
* **Q: Can I stop taking sodium cromoglycate suddenly?** A:  It's best not to stop taking sodium cromoglycate suddenly, especially if you're using it for asthma.  Discuss any changes to your medication regimen with your doctor.
* **Q: What should I do if I experience side effects?** A: Mild side effects often subside.  However, report any concerning or persistent side effects to your doctor or pharmacist immediately.
