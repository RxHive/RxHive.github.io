# Sodium Valproate + Valproic Acid

**Generic Name:** Sodium Valproate + Valproic Acid

**Usage:**

Sodium valproate and valproic acid are anticonvulsant medications, primarily used to treat epilepsy and bipolar disorder.  They work by affecting the balance of certain chemicals in the brain, specifically neurotransmitters, which helps to reduce the frequency and severity of seizures in epilepsy and stabilize mood swings in bipolar disorder.

* **Primary Use:** Epilepsy (various seizure types). This includes both preventing seizures (prophylaxis) and controlling ongoing seizures.

* **Secondary Uses:** Bipolar disorder (manic episodes), migraine prophylaxis (prevention), and sometimes as an adjunctive therapy for other neurological conditions.  It's important to note that the use in migraine and other neurological conditions is less common and often requires careful consideration by a specialist.


**Dosage:**

Dosage is highly individualized and depends on factors like age, weight, specific condition, and response to treatment.  A doctor will carefully determine the appropriate dose.  It's crucial to follow the prescribed dosage regimen precisely and not adjust it without consulting a healthcare professional.  

* **Forms:** Sodium valproate and valproic acid are available in various forms, including tablets, capsules, oral solutions (syrups), and injectable solutions.

* **Adults:**  Starting doses vary, typically ranging from 750mg to 2000mg per day, divided into multiple doses.  The maximum daily dose is usually limited to avoid side effects.

* **Children:** Dosage for children is calculated based on weight and is significantly lower than for adults.  Dosage forms specifically designed for children (e.g., syrups) are available to facilitate accurate administration.

* **Administration:** Oral forms (tablets, capsules, syrups) are typically taken with food to minimize stomach upset. Injectable forms are administered by a healthcare professional in a clinical setting.


**Side Effects:**

* **Common Side Effects:** Nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, stomach pain, weight changes, hair loss, tremor, drowsiness, dizziness.

* **Less Common but Serious Side Effects:** Liver problems (especially in children and young adults), pancreatitis (inflammation of the pancreas), thrombocytopenia (low platelet count),  increased risk of bleeding, suicidal thoughts or behavior.  

**Important:** Any new or worsening symptoms should be reported to a doctor immediately.


**How it Works:**

Sodium valproate and valproic acid primarily work by increasing the levels of the neurotransmitter GABA (gamma-aminobutyric acid) in the brain. GABA is an inhibitory neurotransmitter, meaning it helps to slow down nerve signals. By increasing GABA levels, these medications reduce the excessive electrical activity in the brain that causes seizures and mood instability.  They also affect other neurotransmitter systems, contributing to their therapeutic effects.


**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:**  Sodium valproate and valproic acid are contraindicated in individuals with known hypersensitivity (allergy) to the medication or its components.  They should be used cautiously in individuals with liver disease, kidney disease, or bleeding disorders.

* **Drug Interactions:** These medications can interact with several other drugs, including some antidepressants, anticoagulants (blood thinners), and certain antibiotics.  Always inform your doctor of all medications, supplements, and herbal remedies being taken.

* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  There's a significant risk of birth defects associated with valproate use during pregnancy.  It should only be used in pregnant women when the benefits clearly outweigh the risks and under strict medical supervision.  Valproate can also be passed into breast milk.

* **Children and Older Adults:**  Children and older adults may be more susceptible to side effects. Careful monitoring and dose adjustment are often necessary.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long does it take to work?** A: The time it takes to see effects varies.  For seizure control, it may take several weeks to achieve optimal results. For mood stabilization, it can take even longer.

* **Q: Can I stop taking it suddenly?** A: No.  Sudden cessation can lead to increased seizure frequency or other serious consequences. Always taper off the medication gradually under the guidance of your doctor.

* **Q: How should I store it?** A: Store at room temperature, away from moisture and light, and keep out of the reach of children.

* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it is almost time for your next dose. Do not take a double dose to make up for a missed dose.

* **Q: Is it addictive?** A: Sodium valproate and valproic acid are not considered addictive in the same way as opioids or other substances. However, abruptly stopping treatment can lead to withdrawal symptoms.
