# S-Amlodipine + Hydrochlorothiazide

**Generic Name:** S-Amlodipine + Hydrochlorothiazide

**Usage:**

S-Amlodipine + Hydrochlorothiazide is a combination medication used primarily to treat high blood pressure (hypertension).  It combines two active ingredients: S-amlodipine, a calcium channel blocker, and hydrochlorothiazide, a thiazide diuretic.  By working together, these components effectively lower blood pressure.  The combination therapy is often preferred when a single medication isn't sufficient to manage blood pressure effectively.  While its primary use is hypertension, it might sometimes be used as part of a treatment plan for other conditions related to high blood pressure, such as heart failure (in specific cases and under the guidance of a physician).  It is important to remember that this medication should only be taken under the direct supervision of a healthcare provider.

**Dosage:**

Dosage of S-Amlodipine + Hydrochlorothiazide is determined by a healthcare professional based on individual needs and response. It typically comes in tablet form.  There is no standard pediatric dosage as its use in children isn't common.  Adult dosages commonly range from a low starting dose to higher doses, depending on the individual's blood pressure response and the severity of hypertension. This medication should be taken exactly as prescribed; adjustments should only be made by a physician.

**Side Effects:**

Common side effects can include:

* Headache
* Dizziness
* Swelling in ankles or feet
* Fatigue
* Nausea
* Lightheadedness
* Flushing
* Constipation

Less common, but potentially serious side effects can include:

* Irregular heartbeat
* Chest pain
* Swelling of the face, lips, or tongue (angioedema – seek immediate medical attention)
* Severe allergic reactions
* Liver problems
* Low blood potassium (hypokalemia)

If you experience any side effects, especially those listed as serious, contact your doctor immediately.


**How it Works:**

S-amlodipine relaxes blood vessels, making it easier for the heart to pump blood.  Hydrochlorothiazide works by increasing the amount of salt and water excreted by the kidneys. This reduces the volume of blood circulating, further lowering blood pressure.  The combined action of these two ingredients provides a more effective and often more controlled blood pressure reduction than either drug alone.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** This medication should not be taken by individuals with certain conditions, including severe liver or kidney disease, a history of angioedema, or those allergic to either amlodipine or hydrochlorothiazide.
* **Drug Interactions:**  S-Amlodipine + Hydrochlorothiazide can interact with numerous other medications, including some commonly prescribed drugs.  It is crucial to inform your doctor of all medications, supplements, and herbal remedies you are taking.
* **Pregnant or Breastfeeding Women:**  Use during pregnancy and breastfeeding requires careful consideration by a physician.  The risks and benefits must be carefully weighed.
* **Older Adults:** Older adults may be more sensitive to the side effects of this medication, requiring careful monitoring and potentially lower dosages.
* **Alcohol:** Excessive alcohol consumption can interact negatively with this medication, potentially worsening blood pressure control.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: Can I stop taking this medication suddenly?** A: No.  Stopping abruptly can cause a dangerous spike in blood pressure.  Always follow your doctor's instructions for tapering off the medication.

* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it's almost time for your next dose.  Never double the dose to make up for a missed one.

* **Q: How should I store this medication?** A: Store in a cool, dry place, away from moisture and direct sunlight. Keep out of reach of children.

* **Q: Is it safe to drive while taking this medication?** A: Dizziness and lightheadedness are possible side effects.  If you experience these, avoid driving or operating heavy machinery.
