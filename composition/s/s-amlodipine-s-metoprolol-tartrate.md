# S-Amlodipine + S-Metoprolol Tartrate

**Generic Name:** S-Amlodipine + S-Metoprolol Tartrate

**Usage:**

S-Amlodipine + S-Metoprolol Tartrate is a combination medication used primarily to treat hypertension (high blood pressure).  It combines two active ingredients: S-amlodipine, a calcium channel blocker, and S-metoprolol tartrate, a beta-blocker.  By working together, these components effectively lower blood pressure. This combination therapy is particularly beneficial for patients whose blood pressure isn't adequately controlled with a single medication.  While its primary use is hypertension, it may also be considered in the management of certain types of angina (chest pain) where both blood pressure control and reduction of heart workload are necessary.  It's crucial to understand that this medication is prescribed and its usage determined by a physician based on individual patient needs and health status.  This medication does not address the underlying cause of hypertension or angina; it manages the symptoms.


**Dosage:**

Dosage is always determined and adjusted by a physician based on individual needs and response to treatment.  There's no universally applicable dosage for all patients.  The medication typically comes in tablet form.  A doctor will prescribe the specific strength and frequency of administration.  Children's use of this combination medication is generally not recommended, and the medication is not typically available in forms suitable for children.  Elderly patients may require lower doses due to potential increased sensitivity to the medication's effects.  Adjustments are frequently made based on individual response, monitoring of blood pressure, and presence of other health conditions.  Never alter the prescribed dosage without consulting your doctor.


**Side Effects:**

Common side effects can include:

* Dizziness
* Headache
* Fatigue
* Swelling in ankles or feet
* Nausea
* Slow heart rate (bradycardia)
* Shortness of breath


Less common, but serious side effects requiring immediate medical attention include:

* Chest pain
* Irregular heartbeat (arrhythmia)
* Severe dizziness or fainting
* Swelling of the face, lips, or tongue (angioedema)
* Allergic reactions (rash, itching, hives)
* Severe shortness of breath or wheezing


If you experience any of these side effects, stop taking the medication and seek immediate medical advice.


**How it Works:**

S-amlodipine relaxes blood vessels, allowing blood to flow more easily, thereby lowering blood pressure. S-metoprolol tartrate slows down the heart rate and reduces the force of the heart's contractions, also contributing to lower blood pressure and reduced workload on the heart.  This combined effect helps control hypertension and manage symptoms of angina by improving blood flow and reducing strain on the cardiovascular system.


**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** This medication should not be used by individuals with certain conditions, such as severe heart failure, certain types of heart block, or very slow heart rate.  Individuals with severe liver problems or allergy to either amlodipine or metoprolol should also avoid this medication.
* **Drug Interactions:** This medication can interact with numerous other drugs, including other heart medications, diuretics, and certain antidepressants.  Inform your doctor about all medications, supplements, and herbal remedies you are taking.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  The use of this medication during pregnancy and breastfeeding should be carefully considered and discussed with a doctor.  The benefits must be weighed against potential risks to the mother and child.
* **Older Adults:** Older adults may be more susceptible to side effects, and dosage adjustments are often necessary.
* **Driving and Operating Machinery:** Dizziness and fatigue are potential side effects; caution is advised while driving or operating machinery, especially during the initial stages of treatment.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: Can I stop taking this medication suddenly?**  A: No.  Stopping abruptly can be dangerous and lead to rebound hypertension or other serious complications.  Always follow your doctor's instructions for gradually reducing the dosage.
* **Q:  How should I store this medication?** A: Store at room temperature, away from moisture and direct sunlight.
* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it's almost time for the next dose.  Never double the dose.
* **Q: Can I drink alcohol while taking this medication?** A:  Alcohol may increase the risk of side effects.  Discuss alcohol consumption with your doctor.
* **Q: Will this medication cure my high blood pressure?** A: No.  This medication manages high blood pressure; lifestyle changes such as diet and exercise are also crucial for long-term blood pressure control.
