# Selenium Sulphide

**Generic Name: Selenium Sulphide**

**Usage:**

Selenium sulphide is primarily used topically to treat various fungal skin infections, most notably seborrheic dermatitis (dandruff) and tinea versicolor (a fungal infection causing discolored patches on the skin).  Its effectiveness stems from its ability to disrupt the growth and reproduction of fungi. While its primary application is for dermatological conditions, some formulations might be used to treat other fungal infections under strict medical supervision.  It's crucial to understand that selenium sulphide is not a cure-all for all fungal infections; its efficacy depends on the specific type and severity of the infection.  Always consult a doctor to determine the appropriate treatment.

**Dosage:**

Dosage varies significantly depending on the specific product formulation (shampoos, creams, lotions), the condition being treated, and the patient's age.  There's no universal dosage guideline.  The instructions provided with the specific product should always be followed meticulously.  Generally, for dandruff shampoos containing selenium sulphide, the instructions usually involve applying the shampoo to wet hair, lathering, leaving it on for a few minutes, and then rinsing thoroughly.  The frequency of application varies; some require weekly use, while others might be used less often. Creams and lotions typically require application to the affected area once or twice daily, as directed.  Children's dosage is usually adjusted based on age and the affected area's size; a doctor's guidance is always necessary for children.  It's essential never to exceed the recommended dosage without consulting a healthcare professional.


**Side Effects:**

* **Common:** Mild skin irritation (redness, itching, burning), dryness, hair discoloration (temporary).
* **Less Common but Serious:** Severe skin reactions (allergic contact dermatitis), hair loss (although rare).
* **Note:** If you experience any significant or persistent side effects, discontinue use and seek immediate medical advice.


**How it Works:**

Selenium sulphide's antifungal action is attributed to its ability to interfere with the metabolic processes of fungi.  It disrupts the fungal cell's ability to function properly, ultimately inhibiting its growth and reproduction.  The exact mechanisms are complex, but the end result is a reduction in the fungal population on the skin's surface, alleviating the symptoms of the infection.  Topical application ensures that the medication acts directly on the affected area, minimizing systemic effects.


**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:**  Individuals with known hypersensitivity (allergy) to selenium sulphide should avoid its use.
* **Drug Interactions:**  While interactions with other medications are generally rare, it's advisable to inform your doctor or pharmacist about all medications and supplements you are currently taking.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  The use of selenium sulphide during pregnancy and breastfeeding should only be undertaken under strict medical supervision. The potential risks and benefits should be carefully weighed.
* **Children and Older Adults:**  Use in children and older adults should be under a doctor's careful guidance, with appropriate dosage adjustments based on their individual needs and health status.
* **Avoid Contact with Eyes:**  Accidental eye contact should be immediately flushed with plenty of water.  If irritation persists, seek medical attention.

**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long does it take to see results?** A: The time it takes to see improvement varies depending on the condition and severity.  It may take several weeks of regular use.
* **Q: Can I use selenium sulphide with other skin treatments?** A: Consult your doctor or pharmacist before combining it with other topical medications, as this could potentially lead to adverse reactions.
* **Q: How should I store selenium sulphide?** A: Store as directed on the product label, typically in a cool, dry place, away from direct sunlight and heat. Keep out of reach of children.
* **Q: Is selenium sulphide safe for long-term use?** A: Long-term use should only be undertaken under medical supervision to monitor for potential side effects.
* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Simply apply the medication as soon as you remember, unless it's almost time for the next dose. Do not double the dose.
