# Sunitinib

**Generic Name: Sunitinib**

**Usage:**

Sunitinib is a targeted cancer therapy, primarily used to treat several types of advanced cancers.  Its main use lies in treating certain kidney cancers (renal cell carcinoma, or RCC), specifically those that have spread to other parts of the body (metastatic RCC). It's also approved for treating gastrointestinal stromal tumors (GIST), a rare type of cancer that originates in the digestive tract, particularly those that have progressed after prior treatment.  Sunitinib may also be used to treat other advanced cancers like pancreatic neuroendocrine tumors (pNETs) and certain types of thyroid cancer, though its use in these contexts is often considered more specialized and based on specific patient factors.  The specific use and suitability of sunitinib are always determined in consultation with an oncologist.


**Dosage:**

Sunitinib is typically administered orally as a capsule. Dosage varies depending on the specific cancer being treated and the individual patient's response.  There is no standard pediatric dosage as its use in children is limited and usually only considered in carefully selected clinical trials. The typical adult dosage is usually 50mg once daily for four weeks, followed by a two-week break. This cycle is repeated.  However, a physician may adjust the dosage based on individual tolerance and the severity of side effects.  The medication should always be taken exactly as prescribed by a healthcare professional;  never alter the dosage or frequency without consulting your doctor.  There are no liquid or injectable forms of sunitinib currently available.


**Side Effects:**

Sunitinib, like many cancer therapies, can cause side effects.  These can range in severity from mild to severe.  Some common side effects include:

* Fatigue
* Diarrhea
* Nausea
* Vomiting
* Mouth sores (stomatitis)
* Loss of appetite
* High blood pressure (hypertension)
* Skin changes (rash, redness, dryness)
* Hand-foot syndrome (pain, swelling, redness of the hands and feet)
* Changes in blood counts (low red blood cell count, low white blood cell count, low platelet count)

Less common but serious side effects include heart problems, bleeding, kidney problems, and blood clots.  It's crucial to report any new or worsening symptoms to your doctor immediately.


**How it Works:**

Sunitinib is a tyrosine kinase inhibitor (TKI). Tyrosine kinases are enzymes that play a crucial role in the growth and spread of cancer cells.  Sunitinib works by blocking the activity of specific tyrosine kinases, thus inhibiting the growth and spread of cancer cells.  This targeted approach helps to minimize damage to healthy cells, compared to some more generalized cancer treatments.  The specific tyrosine kinases inhibited by sunitinib are involved in the growth and development of the cancerous cells in the diseases it treats.


**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** Sunitinib is contraindicated (should not be used) in individuals with a known hypersensitivity to the drug or its components.
* **Drug Interactions:**  Sunitinib can interact with other medications, including some herbal supplements. It's vital to inform your doctor of all medications, including over-the-counter drugs and supplements, you are currently taking.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:** Sunitinib is potentially harmful to a developing fetus and should not be used during pregnancy. Women of childbearing age should use effective contraception during treatment and for a certain period after.  It's also not recommended during breastfeeding.
* **Hepatic/Renal Impairment:**  The dosage may need adjustments for people with liver or kidney problems.
* **Older Adults:** Older adults may be more susceptible to side effects and require careful monitoring.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long will I need to take sunitinib?** A: The duration of treatment varies depending on individual response and the type of cancer. Your oncologist will determine the appropriate treatment duration.

* **Q: Can I drive while taking sunitinib?** A:  Fatigue is a common side effect.  If you experience fatigue, avoid driving or operating heavy machinery.

* **Q: How should I store sunitinib?** A: Store sunitinib capsules at room temperature, away from moisture and heat.

* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Contact your doctor for instructions. Do not take a double dose to make up for a missed dose.

* **Q: Is sunitinib addictive?** A: Sunitinib is not an addictive medication.
