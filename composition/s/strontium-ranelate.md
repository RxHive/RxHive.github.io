# Strontium Ranelate

**Generic Name: Strontium Ranelate**

**Usage:**

Strontium ranelate is primarily used to treat osteoporosis in postmenopausal women and men at high risk of fractures.  Osteoporosis is a condition characterized by weakened bones, making them fragile and prone to breaking.  Strontium ranelate works by increasing bone formation and reducing bone resorption (the breakdown of bone tissue). This dual action helps to improve bone mineral density and reduce the risk of fractures, particularly in the spine and hip.  While its primary use is in osteoporosis, research has explored its potential in other bone-related conditions, but these are not generally established clinical uses at this time.

**Dosage:**

Strontium ranelate is typically administered orally as a tablet.  The standard dosage for adults is usually two 2g tablets once daily, taken with a glass of water. Dosage adjustments based on age, weight, or severity of osteoporosis are generally not made, but are determined by a physician based on individual patient factors.  **Crucially, there is no established pediatric dosage or use for strontium ranelate.**  The medication should always be taken as prescribed by a healthcare professional.

**Side Effects:**

* **Common Side Effects:**  Nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, constipation, and flatulence are relatively common.  Headache and dizziness have also been reported.  These side effects are usually mild and temporary.
* **Less Common but Serious Side Effects:** Deep vein thrombosis (DVT), pulmonary embolism (blood clots in the lungs), and allergic reactions (including skin rash, itching, and swelling) are less frequent but require immediate medical attention.  A very small increased risk of cardiovascular events (such as heart attack or stroke) has also been observed.
* **Important Note:**  If you experience any unexpected or concerning side effects, discontinue use and consult your doctor or pharmacist immediately.

**How it Works:**

Strontium ranelate works through a complex mechanism involving both bone formation and resorption.  It's believed to act by stimulating the activity of osteoblasts (cells that build bone) while simultaneously inhibiting the activity of osteoclasts (cells that break down bone). This dual effect leads to a net increase in bone mass and improved bone strength, thereby reducing the risk of fractures.  The exact mechanisms by which it achieves this are still under investigation, but the overall effect is a positive impact on bone health.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** Strontium ranelate should not be used by individuals with a history of significant cardiovascular disease, including recent heart attack or stroke, or those with severe kidney problems.  It is also contraindicated in patients with hypercalcemia (high blood calcium levels) or those who are allergic to any of its components.
* **Drug Interactions:**  Strontium ranelate may interact with certain medications, particularly those affecting calcium metabolism.  It's crucial to inform your doctor of all medications (including over-the-counter drugs and herbal remedies) you are currently taking before starting strontium ranelate.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  The use of strontium ranelate during pregnancy and breastfeeding is generally not recommended due to insufficient safety data.  Women of childbearing age should use effective contraception while taking this medication.
* **Older Adults:**  Older adults may be at increased risk for some of the side effects, particularly cardiovascular events.  Careful monitoring is necessary.

**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long do I need to take strontium ranelate?** A: The duration of treatment is determined by your doctor and depends on your individual needs and response to the medication.  It's typically a long-term treatment for osteoporosis.

* **Q: How should I store strontium ranelate?** A: Store the medication in a cool, dry place, away from direct sunlight and moisture, as instructed on the label.

* **Q: Can I stop taking strontium ranelate suddenly?** A: No.  Always consult your doctor before stopping the medication, as sudden discontinuation may have adverse effects.

* **Q: What if I miss a dose?** A:  Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it's almost time for your next dose. Do not take a double dose to make up for a missed one.

* **Q:  Is strontium ranelate safe for everyone?** A: No, it's crucial to consult your doctor before taking strontium ranelate, especially if you have any pre-existing health conditions.  It is not appropriate for all individuals.
