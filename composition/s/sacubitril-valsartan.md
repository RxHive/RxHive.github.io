# Sacubitril + Valsartan

**Generic Name: Sacubitril/Valsartan**

**Usage:**

Sacubitril/Valsartan is primarily used to treat heart failure in adults.  Specifically, it's prescribed for patients with chronic heart failure (a condition where the heart can't pump enough blood to meet the body's needs) who have reduced ejection fraction (meaning the heart isn't pumping as efficiently as it should).  This medication is particularly beneficial for those who have already experienced worsening heart failure symptoms or have been hospitalized for this reason.  While not its primary use, it may also play a role in reducing the risk of cardiovascular death and hospitalization in this patient population.  It's important to understand that Sacubitril/Valsartan is not a cure for heart failure, but rather a treatment aimed at managing symptoms and improving the patient's overall prognosis.


**Dosage:**

Sacubitril/Valsartan is typically available as a tablet.  The dosage is determined by a doctor based on individual factors and generally starts at a lower dose before being gradually increased.  There is no standard pediatric dose; this medication is not approved for use in children.  Adults usually begin with a lower dose (e.g., 24 mg/26 mg once daily), gradually increasing to a target dose (e.g., 97 mg/103 mg twice daily) over several weeks, as tolerated.  Adjustments might be needed depending on kidney function and other health conditions.  The medication should be taken as directed by a healthcare professional.  Skipping doses or abruptly stopping treatment can be detrimental.


**Side Effects:**

Common side effects can include:

* Low blood pressure (hypotension)
* Dizziness
* Lightheadedness
* Headache
* Fatigue
* Upper respiratory tract infections
* Hyperkalemia (high potassium levels in the blood) –  This is a potentially serious side effect.

Less common, but potentially serious side effects include angioedema (swelling of the face, lips, tongue, or throat), which can be life-threatening.  Any new or worsening symptoms should be reported to a doctor immediately.


**How it Works:**

Sacubitril/Valsartan is a combination drug containing two active ingredients: sacubitril and valsartan. Sacubitril is a neprilysin inhibitor; it works by blocking the breakdown of substances that help widen blood vessels and reduce stress on the heart. Valsartan is an angiotensin receptor blocker (ARB); it blocks the action of a hormone (angiotensin II) that narrows blood vessels and increases blood pressure.  By working together, these two components help to reduce strain on the heart, improve blood flow, and ultimately alleviate symptoms associated with heart failure.


**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** This medication is not suitable for people with a history of angioedema, significant kidney problems, or hypersensitivity to sacubitril or valsartan.
* **Drug Interactions:**  Sacubitril/Valsartan can interact with other medications, including potassium-sparing diuretics, ACE inhibitors (must wait at least 36 hours after stopping an ACE inhibitor before starting Sacubitril/Valsartan), and some nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drugs (NSAIDs).  Always inform your doctor of all medications you are taking.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:** This medication should not be used during pregnancy.  It's important to inform your doctor if you are pregnant, planning to become pregnant, or breastfeeding.
* **Older Adults:**  Older adults may be more sensitive to the side effects of this drug, particularly low blood pressure. Careful monitoring is necessary.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: Can I take Sacubitril/Valsartan with other medications?** A:  It's crucial to inform your doctor about all medications and supplements you are taking, as interactions can occur.
* **Q: How should I store Sacubitril/Valsartan?** A: Store it in a cool, dry place, away from moisture and direct sunlight.
* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A:  Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it's almost time for the next dose.  Do not double the dose.
* **Q:  When should I contact my doctor?** A:  Contact your doctor immediately if you experience any serious side effects, such as angioedema (swelling), severe dizziness, or difficulty breathing.
