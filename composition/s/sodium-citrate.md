# Sodium Citrate

**Generic Name:** Sodium Citrate

**Usage:**

Sodium citrate is a salt used primarily to alkalinize the urine.  This means it helps to make urine less acidic. Its main medical use is in treating and preventing kidney stones, particularly those composed of uric acid or cystine. By making the urine less acidic, sodium citrate helps to dissolve these types of stones and reduces the chance of new ones forming.  

Beyond kidney stones, sodium citrate has several other important applications:

* **Treatment of metabolic acidosis:**  This is a condition where the body has too much acid in its blood. Sodium citrate can help to correct this imbalance.  This is often seen in patients with severe diarrhea or other conditions leading to acid build-up.
* **Blood collection:** Sodium citrate is a crucial anticoagulant added to blood collection tubes to prevent clotting before blood tests are performed. It binds to calcium ions, essential for blood coagulation.
* **Bowel preparation:** Before certain medical procedures, like colonoscopies, sodium citrate solutions are sometimes used as a bowel cleanser to clear the intestines.


**Dosage:**

Dosage of sodium citrate varies significantly depending on the specific condition being treated, the individual's age, weight, and kidney function.  It's crucial to follow the prescription exactly as directed by a healthcare professional.  There isn't a universal "standard" dose.

* **Oral forms (tablets, solutions):**  Doses will be prescribed in milligrams or milliequivalents (mEq) of citrate.  The doctor will determine the appropriate dose and frequency based on the patient's needs.
* **Intravenous (IV) administration:**  IV administration is typically used in the treatment of metabolic acidosis and is strictly controlled by medical professionals in a hospital setting.  Dosage is carefully calculated based on the severity of acidosis and blood tests.

**Side Effects:**

* **Common:** Nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, stomach upset.
* **Less Common but Serious:**  These include metabolic alkalosis (too much base in the blood), electrolyte imbalances (particularly sodium and potassium), and rarely, kidney damage.
* **Allergic reactions:** Although rare, allergic reactions are possible, ranging from mild skin rashes to more severe anaphylaxis.  

**Important Note:** If you experience any side effects, particularly those listed above, contact your doctor immediately.


**How it Works:**

Sodium citrate works by increasing the pH (alkalinity) of the urine.  It does this by releasing citrate ions, which act as a buffer, neutralizing acids in the urine.  This higher pH makes it harder for uric acid and cystine to form crystals and precipitate out, thus preventing stone formation or aiding in their dissolution.  In the case of metabolic acidosis, it helps restore the body's acid-base balance by providing alkali.


**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** Sodium citrate should not be used by individuals with certain kidney conditions, severe heart failure, or those with a history of severe allergic reactions to citrates.
* **Drug Interactions:** Sodium citrate can interact with other medications, particularly those that affect kidney function or electrolyte balance.  Inform your doctor of all medications you are taking.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:** While generally considered safe in moderate doses, pregnant and breastfeeding women should consult their doctor before using sodium citrate.
* **Children and Older Adults:** Dosage adjustments may be needed for children and older adults due to differences in kidney function and metabolism.
* **Caution:**  Excessive intake of sodium citrate can lead to serious side effects. Always follow the prescribed dosage.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: Can I take sodium citrate without a doctor's prescription?** A: No, sodium citrate should only be taken under the guidance of a healthcare professional.  Self-medication can be dangerous.
* **Q: How should I store sodium citrate?** A: Store as directed on the product label, typically in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight.
* **Q: What if I miss a dose?** A: Do not take a double dose to make up for a missed one. Contact your doctor for advice.
* **Q: Is sodium citrate safe for long-term use?** A: Long-term use requires careful monitoring by a doctor to prevent potential side effects.
* **Q: Are there any dietary restrictions while taking sodium citrate?** A: Your doctor may recommend dietary modifications to optimize treatment, such as adjusting dietary sodium intake.
