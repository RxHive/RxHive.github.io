# Sevelamer

**Generic Name: Sevelamer**

**Usage:**

Sevelamer is a phosphate binder primarily used to control high levels of phosphate in the blood (hyperphosphatemia).  This is a common problem for people with chronic kidney disease (CKD), particularly those on dialysis.  Phosphate is naturally present in many foods, and when kidneys don't function properly, they can't remove excess phosphate from the body efficiently.  High phosphate levels can lead to serious health issues such as weakened bones, cardiovascular problems, and even death. Sevelamer works by binding to phosphate in the digestive tract, preventing its absorption into the bloodstream.  While its primary use is in CKD, it may sometimes be used in other conditions where hyperphosphatemia is a concern.

**Dosage:**

Sevelamer is available as a powder for oral suspension and as tablets. Dosage is highly individualized and depends on the patient's phosphate levels, dietary intake, and response to treatment. A doctor will determine the appropriate dose and adjustment frequency based on regular blood tests.  There isn't a standardized pediatric dosage; dosing for children is determined on a case-by-case basis by a nephrologist or other specialist.  Generally, adults begin on a lower dose, which is gradually increased until blood phosphate levels are within the target range. The medication should be taken with meals.  Specific instructions on how to mix the powder or how to take the tablets should always come directly from the prescribing doctor or pharmacist.


**Side Effects:**

* **Common:** Constipation (often the most prevalent side effect), nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, stomach pain, and flatulence.
* **Less Common but Serious:**  Intestinal obstruction (blockage),  worsening of existing constipation, and allergic reactions (rash, itching, swelling).

**Important Note:** If you experience any side effects, especially severe ones, consult your doctor immediately.  Do not stop taking sevelamer without your doctor's advice.


**How it Works:**

Sevelamer is a non-absorbable polymer. This means it passes through the digestive system without being absorbed into the bloodstream.  As it moves through the gut, it binds to phosphate molecules in the food you've eaten. This bound phosphate is then eliminated from the body through the stool, effectively lowering the amount of phosphate absorbed into the bloodstream. This mechanism helps control hyperphosphatemia.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** Sevelamer should not be used in individuals with known hypersensitivity (allergy) to the drug.
* **Drug Interactions:** Sevelamer can interfere with the absorption of certain medications, such as some antibiotics (like tetracycline and ciprofloxacin) and certain thyroid medications (like levothyroxine).  It's crucial to inform your doctor and pharmacist of all medications, supplements, and herbal remedies you are taking.
* **Specific Populations:**  Pregnancy and breastfeeding:  The safety of sevelamer during pregnancy and breastfeeding hasn't been fully established.  Discuss potential risks and benefits with your doctor before using it during these periods. Older adults may be more prone to side effects, particularly constipation. Careful monitoring and potential dosage adjustments may be necessary.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: Can I crush or chew sevelamer tablets?**  A: No, unless specifically instructed by your doctor or pharmacist, do not crush or chew sevelamer tablets.  This can affect how the medication works.
* **Q: How should I store sevelamer?** A: Store sevelamer according to the instructions on the label, usually in a cool, dry place away from moisture and direct sunlight.
* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A:  Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it's almost time for your next dose.  Do not double up on doses.  Consult your doctor if you have questions about missed doses.
* **Q:  Can I eat whatever I want while taking sevelamer?** A:  While sevelamer helps control phosphate, it's still important to follow a diet recommended by your doctor or dietitian, which often includes limiting high-phosphate foods.
* **Q:  How long will I need to take sevelamer?** A: The duration of treatment depends on your individual needs and is determined by your doctor.
