# Sitagliptin

**Generic Name: Sitagliptin**

**Usage:**

Sitagliptin is primarily used to help manage type 2 diabetes.  It's part of a class of drugs called DPP-4 inhibitors.  These medications work by improving the body's natural ability to control blood sugar levels. Specifically, sitagliptin increases the amount of incretin hormones, which stimulate insulin release when blood sugar is high and reduce glucagon secretion when blood sugar is low, helping to regulate glucose more effectively.  While its main use is in type 2 diabetes, it's often used in combination with other diabetes medications like metformin, insulin, or sulfonylureas for better blood sugar control, particularly in cases where lifestyle changes and single-drug therapy are not enough.  It does *not* treat type 1 diabetes.


**Dosage:**

The standard adult dosage is typically 100mg once daily, taken orally with or without food.  Dosage adjustments are not usually necessary based on age or weight, but this should always be determined by a doctor. Children should not typically take this medication.  Sitagliptin is available as an oral tablet.  It's crucial to follow the prescription instructions precisely.  Never change the dosage without consulting your healthcare provider.


**Side Effects:**

* **Common:**  Headache, upper respiratory infections, nausea.
* **Less Common but Important:**  Joint pain, pancreatitis (inflammation of the pancreas – seek immediate medical attention if severe abdominal pain occurs), allergic reactions (rash, itching, swelling).  
* **Serious (rare):**  Severe allergic reactions (anaphylaxis), acute kidney injury.

If you experience any side effects, particularly those listed as less common or serious, contact your doctor or pharmacist immediately.  They can help determine the significance of the side effects and advise on appropriate next steps.


**How it Works:**

Sitagliptin works by inhibiting the activity of an enzyme called dipeptidyl peptidase-4 (DPP-4). DPP-4 breaks down incretin hormones, which play a vital role in regulating blood sugar. By blocking DPP-4, sitagliptin allows these incretin hormones to remain active for longer, leading to increased insulin release when blood sugar is high and decreased glucagon release when blood sugar is low.  This results in improved glucose control in people with type 2 diabetes.


**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** Sitagliptin is generally contraindicated in individuals with a known hypersensitivity to the medication or any of its components.
* **Drug Interactions:**  Sitagliptin can interact with some medications, so it's crucial to inform your doctor or pharmacist of all medications, vitamins, and supplements you are taking.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  The use of sitagliptin during pregnancy and breastfeeding should be discussed with a healthcare provider to carefully weigh the risks and benefits.  
* **Kidney Disease:**  Individuals with severe kidney disease may require dosage adjustments or alternative treatment options.  Kidney function should be monitored regularly while taking sitagliptin.
* **Older Adults:** Older adults may be more susceptible to certain side effects.  Careful monitoring is often recommended.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: Can I take sitagliptin with food?**  A: Yes, you can take sitagliptin with or without food.
* **Q: How should I store sitagliptin?** A: Store sitagliptin tablets in a cool, dry place, away from direct sunlight and moisture.
* **Q: Can I stop taking sitagliptin suddenly?** A: No, you should not stop taking sitagliptin suddenly without consulting your doctor.  Stopping abruptly could lead to uncontrolled blood sugar levels.
* **Q: What if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it's almost time for your next dose.  Do not double the dose.
* **Q:  Is sitagliptin right for me?** A: Only a healthcare professional can determine if sitagliptin is the appropriate medication for your specific situation. They will consider your medical history, current medications, and other relevant factors.
