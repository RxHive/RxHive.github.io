# Selexipag

**Generic Name: Selexipag**

**Usage:**

Selexipag is primarily used to treat pulmonary arterial hypertension (PAH).  PAH is a serious condition where blood pressure in the arteries leading from the heart to the lungs becomes abnormally high. This increased pressure makes it harder for the heart to pump blood, leading to shortness of breath, fatigue, and other symptoms. Selexipag helps to improve exercise capacity and delay disease progression in patients with PAH.  While its primary use is in PAH,  research is ongoing to explore its potential in other conditions involving pulmonary vascular disease, but these are not yet standard uses.


**Dosage:**

Selexipag is typically administered orally as a tablet.  Dosage is usually started low and gradually increased under the close supervision of a physician.  The starting dose for adults is typically low, and it is gradually increased over several weeks to reach a target dose based on individual response and tolerance.  There is no established dosage for children, and it's not approved for pediatric use.  Age, weight, and the severity of PAH significantly influence the appropriate dosage, and a healthcare professional will carefully determine the right regimen for each patient.  The medication should be taken at the same time each day with or without food.  Never adjust the dosage without consulting your doctor.

**Side Effects:**

Common side effects of Selexipag can include:

* Headache
* Diarrhea
* Nausea
* Vomiting
* Dizziness
* Jaw pain
* Flushing (redness of the skin)
* Fatigue

Less common, but more serious side effects, may include:

* Severe allergic reactions (anaphylaxis)
* Worsening of heart failure
* Severe liver problems
* Serious upper respiratory infections

If you experience any adverse effects, especially those listed as serious, contact your healthcare provider immediately.


**How it Works:**

Selexipag works by activating prostacyclin receptors in the lungs and blood vessels. Prostacyclin is a naturally occurring substance that helps relax and widen blood vessels, reducing blood pressure. By mimicking the effects of prostacyclin, Selexipag lowers blood pressure in the pulmonary arteries, improving blood flow to the lungs and reducing the strain on the heart.  This helps alleviate symptoms and slow the progression of PAH.


**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** Selexipag should not be used in individuals with a known hypersensitivity (allergy) to the drug or its components.  It is also contraindicated in patients with severe liver disease.

* **Drug Interactions:** Selexipag can interact with other medications.  It's crucial to inform your doctor about all medications, including over-the-counter drugs, herbal supplements, and vitamins, you are taking.

* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  Selexipag should be avoided during pregnancy and breastfeeding unless the benefits clearly outweigh the potential risks.  Pregnant women and women who are breastfeeding should consult their doctor before taking this medication.

* **Older Adults:**  Older adults may be more sensitive to the side effects of Selexipag.  Dose adjustment may be necessary.

* **Liver and Kidney Function:**  Selexipag is primarily metabolized by the liver and excreted by the kidneys.  Patients with impaired liver or kidney function may require dosage adjustments or close monitoring.



**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long does it take for Selexipag to work?**  A: The effects of Selexipag may not be immediately noticeable. It usually takes several weeks of treatment to see significant improvements in symptoms.

* **Q: Can I stop taking Selexipag suddenly?** A: No.  Stopping Selexipag abruptly can be harmful.  The dosage must be gradually reduced under the guidance of a healthcare professional to minimize the risk of rebound effects.

* **Q: How should I store Selexipag?** A: Store Selexipag tablets in a cool, dry place, away from direct sunlight and moisture.  Keep them out of the reach of children.

* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: If you miss a dose, take it as soon as you remember, unless it's almost time for your next dose. Do not double the dose to make up for a missed dose.
