# Streptomycin

**Generic Name:** Streptomycin

**Usage:**

Streptomycin is an aminoglycoside antibiotic, meaning it's effective against various bacteria.  Its primary use is in the treatment of serious bacterial infections, particularly those caused by susceptible strains of *Mycobacterium tuberculosis*. This makes it a crucial component in tuberculosis (TB) treatment regimens, often used in combination with other antibiotics to prevent the development of drug resistance.  

Beyond TB, streptomycin may be used to treat certain other bacterial infections, though its use in these cases is less common now due to the availability of broader-spectrum and less toxic antibiotics.  These secondary uses might include certain types of pneumonia, endocarditis (infection of the heart valves), and infections in the urinary tract or bloodstream when caused by susceptible bacteria.  However, the selection of streptomycin for these secondary uses is usually made only after careful consideration of alternative treatments, taking into account factors like bacterial susceptibility and potential side effects.


**Dosage:**

Streptomycin dosage is highly individualized and depends on several factors, including the patient's age, weight, the severity of the infection, and the specific bacterium causing the infection. It's always administered under the direct supervision of a healthcare professional.  Dosage is typically determined by body weight and administered intramuscularly (IM) or intravenously (IV) as solutions; oral formulations are not generally used due to poor absorption.   

* **Adults:** Doses typically range from 15 to 45 mg/kg per day, divided into multiple injections over 24 hours.
* **Children:** Dosage for children is also based on body weight and needs careful adjustment by a pediatrician or other specialist.
* **Specific Conditions:**  Dosage regimens vary significantly depending on whether it's used to treat TB (requiring prolonged courses) or other acute infections.


**Side Effects:**

Streptomycin, like other aminoglycosides, can cause several side effects.  These range from mild to serious, and prompt medical attention is needed if any concerning symptoms arise.

* **Common Side Effects:**
    * Nausea and vomiting
    * Dizziness
    * Loss of hearing (vertigo)
    * Headache
    * Skin rash
    * Kidney problems (nephrotoxicity)

* **Serious but Less Common Side Effects:**
    * Hearing loss (ototoxicity) - this can be permanent.
    * Kidney damage (nephrotoxicity) - potentially leading to kidney failure.
    * Nerve damage (neuropathy) - affecting balance and coordination.
    * Allergic reactions – including anaphylaxis (a life-threatening reaction).


**How it Works:**

Streptomycin works by interfering with bacterial protein synthesis.  Specifically, it binds to bacterial ribosomes, the cellular machinery responsible for creating proteins. By binding to the ribosomes, streptomycin prevents the bacteria from producing the proteins they need to survive and multiply, ultimately leading to bacterial death. This mechanism selectively targets bacteria, while generally sparing human cells, although the potential for toxicity to human cells exists, as evidenced by the side effects.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** Streptomycin is contraindicated in individuals with known hypersensitivity (allergy) to aminoglycosides.
* **Drug Interactions:**  Streptomycin may interact with certain other medications, including diuretics (water pills) and other nephrotoxic drugs (drugs that can harm the kidneys).  Always inform your doctor of all medications, supplements, and herbal remedies you are taking.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:** Use during pregnancy should be carefully weighed against the potential risks to the fetus.  Its use is generally avoided during pregnancy unless absolutely necessary.  It's also important to discuss breastfeeding with your doctor, as streptomycin can be excreted in breast milk.
* **Elderly and Children:**  Older adults and children are at increased risk of experiencing adverse effects from streptomycin, requiring careful monitoring of kidney and hearing function.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long does it take to see results from streptomycin treatment?** A: This depends on the infection. For acute infections, improvement might be seen within a few days, but TB treatment requires much longer courses, often spanning many months.
* **Q: How is streptomycin stored?** A: Streptomycin should be stored according to the instructions on the label, typically at a cool and dry place, away from direct sunlight.
* **Q:  Are there any long-term effects of streptomycin?** A:  Long-term use carries a risk of permanent hearing loss and kidney damage. Regular monitoring is crucial.
* **Q: What should I do if I experience side effects?** A: Report any side effects, especially severe ones, to your doctor immediately.
