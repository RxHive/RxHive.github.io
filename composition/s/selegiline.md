# Selegiline

**Generic Name: Selegiline**

**Usage:**

Selegiline is primarily used in the treatment of Parkinson's disease.  It acts as a monoamine oxidase type B (MAO-B) inhibitor.  This means it blocks the action of an enzyme that breaks down dopamine, a neurotransmitter crucial for movement and coordination. By preventing the breakdown of dopamine, selegiline helps to increase dopamine levels in the brain, alleviating some of the motor symptoms associated with Parkinson's disease, such as tremors, rigidity, and slow movement.  

In the early stages of Parkinson's, selegiline may be used as monotherapy (sole treatment), or it can be used in combination with other Parkinson's medications to enhance their effectiveness.  Additionally, there's some limited research exploring its potential role in treating other conditions like depression and Alzheimer's disease, but these uses are not as widely established or accepted as its application in Parkinson's.


**Dosage:**

Dosage of selegiline varies significantly based on the individual's condition, age, and response to treatment. A healthcare professional will always determine the appropriate dosage.  It's typically administered orally as a tablet.

* **Adults (Parkinson's Disease):** Treatment usually begins with a low dose, often 5 mg once daily, gradually increasing under the supervision of a doctor based on the patient's tolerance and response.  Higher dosages may be prescribed, but this is determined on a case-by-case basis.

* **Children:**  Selegiline is not generally recommended for use in children.  Its safety and efficacy in pediatric populations haven't been adequately established.

**Important Note:**  Never adjust the dosage of selegiline without consulting your doctor.


**Side Effects:**

Common side effects of selegiline can include:

* Nausea
* Insomnia
* Dizziness
* Headache
* Constipation
* Dry mouth
* Orthostatic hypotension (a sudden drop in blood pressure upon standing)

Less common but more serious side effects may include:

* Hallucinations
* Confusion
* Liver problems (rare but potentially severe)
* Serotonin syndrome (a rare but potentially life-threatening condition, especially when combined with certain other medications)

If you experience any adverse effects, particularly those listed as serious, you should consult your healthcare provider immediately.


**How it Works:**

Selegiline's primary mechanism of action is its selective inhibition of monoamine oxidase type B (MAO-B). MAO-B is an enzyme that metabolizes dopamine, a neurotransmitter critical for motor control. By inhibiting MAO-B, selegiline prevents the breakdown of dopamine, leading to increased dopamine levels in the brain.  This increased dopamine availability helps alleviate the motor symptoms associated with Parkinson's disease by improving nerve signal transmission.


**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** Selegiline is contraindicated in individuals with a history of hypersensitivity (allergy) to the drug. It should be used with extreme caution (or avoided entirely) in patients with a history of certain heart conditions, liver disease, or a predisposition to seizures.

* **Drug Interactions:** Selegiline can interact with various other medications, particularly those affecting the central nervous system, antidepressants (especially MAOIs), and certain pain relievers.  It's vital to inform your doctor about all medications, supplements, and herbal remedies you are taking before starting selegiline.

* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:** The safety of selegiline during pregnancy and breastfeeding hasn't been fully established. Consult your doctor before using selegiline if you are pregnant, breastfeeding, or planning to become pregnant.

* **Older Adults:** Older adults may be more susceptible to side effects, particularly orthostatic hypotension.  Careful monitoring and potentially lower doses are often necessary.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long does it take for selegiline to work?** A:  The onset of effects varies, but improvement in Parkinson's symptoms might be observed within several weeks of consistent treatment.

* **Q: Can I stop taking selegiline suddenly?** A: No, abruptly stopping selegiline can lead to withdrawal symptoms.  Always consult your doctor before making any changes to your medication regimen.

* **Q: How should I store selegiline?** A: Store selegiline in a cool, dry place, away from direct sunlight and moisture, as directed on the prescription label.

* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: If you miss a dose, take it as soon as you remember, unless it's nearly time for your next dose. Never double the dose to make up for a missed one.

* **Q: Is selegiline habit-forming?** A: Selegiline is not considered habit-forming in the same way as some other medications. However, it's crucial to follow your doctor's prescribed dosage and not increase the dose without their guidance.
