# Safinamide

**Generic Name: Safinamide**

**Usage:**

Safinamide is primarily used to treat Parkinson's disease.  Specifically, it's used as an add-on therapy to levodopa in patients with Parkinson's disease experiencing "off" periods – fluctuations in motor control where medication effectiveness wears off between doses.  These "off" periods can cause significant disability and reduced quality of life. Safinamide helps to reduce the duration and severity of these "off" periods, improving motor control and overall function. While not a first-line treatment for Parkinson's, its role in managing motor fluctuations is significant.  Research is also exploring its potential in other neurological conditions, but these uses remain investigational at this time.

**Dosage:**

Safinamide is typically available as an oral tablet.  The standard adult starting dose is usually 50mg once daily, taken in the morning.  A physician might increase this dose to 100mg once daily after a suitable period of evaluation, based on individual response and tolerability.  Dosage adjustments are crucial and should always be guided by a healthcare professional.  There is no established dosage for children, as safinamide is not currently approved for pediatric use.  Dosage may need to be carefully considered in patients with kidney or liver impairment.  Specific adjustments based on individual circumstances should be determined in consultation with a doctor.


**Side Effects:**

Common side effects of safinamide may include:

* Dizziness
* Nausea
* Insomnia
* Headache
* Fatigue
* Constipation
* Upper respiratory tract infections
* Fall

Less common, but potentially serious side effects, include:

*  Serious allergic reactions (anaphylaxis)
*  Suicidal thoughts or behaviour (though a causal link is not definitively established)
*  Liver problems (hepatotoxicity)


If you experience any adverse effects, particularly those listed above as potentially serious, contact your healthcare provider immediately.

**How it Works:**

Safinamide works through a dual mechanism.  Firstly, it inhibits an enzyme called MAO-B (monoamine oxidase B). MAO-B breaks down dopamine in the brain.  By inhibiting MAO-B, safinamide increases the levels of dopamine, which is a neurotransmitter crucial for motor control and is deficient in Parkinson's disease. Secondly, safinamide also acts as a sodium channel blocker, which likely contributes to its overall effectiveness in managing motor fluctuations.  This dual mechanism of action offers a unique approach to improving motor control in Parkinson's disease.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** Safinamide is contraindicated in individuals with a history of hypersensitivity to safinamide or any of its components.  It should also be avoided in patients taking certain medications (see drug interactions below).

* **Drug Interactions:** Safinamide interacts with various drugs, especially those metabolized by the same liver enzymes or those affecting the central nervous system.  It's crucial to inform your doctor of all medications you are taking, including over-the-counter drugs, herbal supplements, and vitamins.

* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:** The safety of safinamide during pregnancy and breastfeeding is not fully established.  Women of childbearing age should use effective contraception while taking safinamide.  Discussion with a doctor is essential before using safinamide during pregnancy or while breastfeeding.

* **Older Adults:** Older adults may be more susceptible to the side effects of safinamide, requiring careful dose adjustment and monitoring.

* **Liver and Kidney Impairment:** Safinamide is mainly metabolized by the liver and excreted by the kidneys. Patients with impaired liver or kidney function require careful monitoring and potential dose adjustments.

**FAQs:**

* **Q: Can I stop taking safinamide suddenly?** A: No, it's important to follow your doctor's instructions regarding discontinuation.  Sudden cessation can lead to withdrawal symptoms.

* **Q: How should I store safinamide?** A: Store safinamide at room temperature, away from moisture and direct sunlight.

* **Q: Is safinamide addictive?** A: Safinamide is not considered an addictive substance.

* **Q: Can safinamide cure Parkinson's disease?** A: No, safinamide does not cure Parkinson's disease; it's used to manage specific symptoms.

* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: If you miss a dose, take it as soon as you remember, unless it's almost time for your next dose.  Do not take a double dose.
