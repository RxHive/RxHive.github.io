# Sucralfate + Mupirocin

**Generic Name: Sucralfate + Mupirocin**

This combination medication, Sucralfate + Mupirocin, is not currently available as a commercially approved, fixed-dose combination product.  Sucralfate and mupirocin are used separately to treat distinct conditions.  The information below describes each drug individually, as they are not typically prescribed together.  If you have a prescription that appears to contain this combination, please consult your doctor or pharmacist to clarify the instructions and verify the medication’s composition.

**Sucralfate (alone):**

* **Usage:** Sucralfate is used to treat and prevent ulcers in the stomach and duodenum (a part of the small intestine).  Its primary use is in treating active duodenal ulcers and gastric ulcers.  It can also be used to prevent recurrence of ulcers in certain cases, and may be used for treating ulcers associated with nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drug (NSAID) use.

* **Dosage:** Dosage varies depending on the individual and the specific condition.  It is usually administered orally, as a tablet or suspension.  The typical adult dose ranges from 1 gram to 2 grams four times daily, on an empty stomach, one hour before meals and at bedtime.  Children's dosing will vary significantly based on age and weight and should be determined by a physician.


* **Side Effects:**
    * Constipation (common)
    * Nausea (less common)
    * Dizziness (rare)
    * Darkening of stool (due to the drug itself, not a cause for alarm)


* **How it Works:** Sucralfate works by forming a protective barrier over ulcers in the stomach and duodenum.  This barrier protects the ulcer from further damage by stomach acid and helps it heal.  It doesn't directly reduce stomach acid production, but instead acts as a physical shield.


* **Precautions:** Sucralfate should not be taken with antacids.  It should be taken one hour before or two hours after any other medications. Its use in pregnant women and nursing mothers should be discussed with a doctor.



**Mupirocin (alone):**

* **Usage:** Mupirocin is an antibiotic ointment used to treat skin infections caused by bacteria, specifically *Staphylococcus aureus* and *Streptococcus pyogenes*. It's primarily used for superficial skin infections like impetigo, and sometimes for minor burns or wounds with bacterial infection.

* **Dosage:** Mupirocin is applied topically to the affected area multiple times per day (typically two to three times) as directed by a doctor or the product label. The duration of treatment varies according to the severity of the infection and typically lasts for 7-10 days.



* **Side Effects:**
    * Burning or stinging sensation at the application site (common)
    * Redness or itching (less common)
    * Allergic reactions (rare but possible, including serious allergic reactions)



* **How it Works:** Mupirocin is a topical antibiotic that inhibits bacterial protein synthesis, effectively killing bacteria and stopping the infection from spreading.

* **Precautions:** Mupirocin is for external use only. Avoid contact with eyes.  Do not use for deep wounds or severe infections.  If the infection worsens or does not improve after a few days, seek medical attention. Long-term or excessive use can lead to resistance.




**FAQs (for Sucralfate and Mupirocin separately):**


**Sucralfate:**

* **Q: How long do I need to take Sucralfate?** A: The duration of treatment varies depending on the condition and the individual's response, typically several weeks.  Always follow your doctor's instructions.
* **Q: Can I take Sucralfate with other medications?** A:  No, take Sucralfate at least one hour before or two hours after other medications to maximize its effectiveness and minimize interactions. 
* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it is close to the time of your next dose. Do not double the dose.


**Mupirocin:**

* **Q: Can I use Mupirocin on any skin infection?** A: No, Mupirocin is specifically for bacterial skin infections. Consult a doctor for diagnosis and appropriate treatment before using.
* **Q: Can I use Mupirocin on open wounds?** A: Only minor open wounds. For deeper wounds, consult a doctor immediately.
* **Q:  How should I store Mupirocin?** A: Store at room temperature, away from direct sunlight and moisture.
