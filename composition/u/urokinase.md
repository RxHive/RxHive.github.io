# Urokinase

**Generic Name: Urokinase**

**Usage:**

Urokinase is a thrombolytic agent, meaning it dissolves blood clots.  Its primary use is in treating acute massive pulmonary embolism (PE), a serious condition where a blood clot blocks a major artery in the lung.  This is often a life-threatening situation requiring immediate intervention.  Urokinase can also be used in other situations involving dangerous blood clots, such as deep vein thrombosis (DVT) and peripheral arterial occlusion, though its use in these areas is less common now due to the availability of other, often preferred, thrombolytic drugs.  Historically, it's also been employed in certain urological procedures to break down clots obstructing urine flow, though this application is becoming less prevalent with the advent of minimally invasive techniques.  The specific application and suitability of urokinase are determined by a physician considering the individual patient's condition and risk factors.

**Dosage:**

Urokinase is administered intravenously (IV) and dosage is highly individualized and determined by a physician based on factors including the severity of the clot, patient weight, and overall health.  There isn't a standard "adult" or "child" dosage.  Treatment typically involves a loading dose followed by a continuous infusion.  Specific dosage regimens are complex and are meticulously calculated by healthcare professionals experienced in managing these conditions.  The medication does not come in tablet or syrup form; it is exclusively given intravenously.  Precise administration must be performed by trained medical personnel due to the serious potential consequences of incorrect dosage or administration.


**Side Effects:**

* Bleeding (most common and potentially serious side effect, ranging from minor bruising to life-threatening internal bleeding)
* Fever
* Allergic reactions (rash, itching, swelling)
* Hypotension (low blood pressure)
* Nausea
* Vomiting
* Headache


**Less Common, but Serious Side Effects:**

* Stroke
* Cardiac arrhythmias
* Anaphylaxis (severe allergic reaction)

**Note:**  If any adverse effects occur, especially significant bleeding, seek immediate medical attention.

**How it Works:**

Urokinase is a naturally occurring enzyme that breaks down fibrin, a protein that forms the mesh-like structure of blood clots. By targeting fibrin, urokinase helps dissolve the clot, restoring normal blood flow.  This action is crucial in conditions like pulmonary embolism where a blocked artery can lead to severe respiratory compromise and death.  The enzyme's specificity for fibrin minimizes the risk of damaging the body's natural clotting mechanisms, although bleeding remains a significant risk.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** Urokinase is contraindicated in patients with active internal bleeding, recent major surgery, uncontrolled hypertension, severe hepatic or renal impairment, and a history of hemorrhagic stroke.
* **Drug Interactions:**  Urokinase can interact with anticoagulants (blood thinners) and other medications affecting clotting.  It's essential to inform your doctor about all medications you are taking.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  The use of urokinase during pregnancy and breastfeeding requires careful consideration of the risks and benefits by the attending physician.  It should only be used if the potential benefits outweigh the potential risks to the mother and fetus.
* **Older Adults and Children:**  Urokinase use in these populations requires cautious monitoring due to potential increased risk of bleeding and other adverse effects.  Dosage adjustments may be necessary.
