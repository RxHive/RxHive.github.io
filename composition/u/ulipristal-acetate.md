# Ulipristal acetate

**Generic Name:** Ulipristal acetate

**Usage:**

Ulipristal acetate is primarily used as an emergency contraceptive, also known as the "morning-after pill."  It works by delaying or preventing ovulation, thus hindering fertilization.  This makes it effective in preventing pregnancy after unprotected sex or contraceptive failure, such as a broken condom or missed birth control pill.  It is most effective when taken within 120 hours (5 days) of unprotected sex, but its effectiveness decreases with time.

While its primary use is emergency contraception, ulipristal acetate also holds a secondary role in treating uterine fibroids, specifically in managing heavy menstrual bleeding (menorrhagia) associated with these benign tumors.  In this context, it's often considered when other treatment options haven't worked or are not suitable.  Research is ongoing to explore its potential in other areas, but these are not currently approved clinical applications.


**Dosage:**

Dosage for ulipristal acetate varies significantly depending on the intended use.

* **Emergency Contraception:**  The typical dose is a single 30mg tablet taken orally as soon as possible, ideally within 120 hours (5 days) of unprotected sex.  There is no specific dosage adjustment for age or weight in this context.


* **Uterine Fibroids (menorrhagia):**  The dosage regimen for uterine fibroids is more complex and involves a daily oral dose determined by the healthcare provider based on individual factors. It's crucial to follow the prescribed schedule meticulously.  This isn't a single dose and requires a doctor’s prescription and ongoing monitoring.


Ulipristal acetate is available only as an oral tablet.  There are no syrup or injectable forms.


**Side Effects:**

Common side effects can include:

* Headache
* Nausea
* Abdominal pain
* Fatigue
* Irregular menstrual bleeding (changes in the timing, amount, or duration of bleeding)
* Breast tenderness
* Dizziness

Less common but more serious side effects may include:

* Severe abdominal pain
* Severe allergic reactions (such as difficulty breathing, swelling of the face, lips, tongue, or throat)
* Severe nausea and vomiting


**If you experience any concerning side effects, particularly those listed as serious, contact your healthcare provider immediately.**


**How it Works:**

In emergency contraception, ulipristal acetate primarily works by delaying or inhibiting ovulation.  It also appears to affect other aspects of the reproductive process, making it more difficult for sperm to fertilize an egg or for a fertilized egg to implant in the uterus.

For uterine fibroids, ulipristal acetate reduces the size of fibroids, thus decreasing the associated heavy menstrual bleeding. The exact mechanism in this context isn't fully understood, but it involves impacting hormone production and fibroid growth.



**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** Ulipristal acetate should not be used by individuals with certain liver conditions or those currently taking specific medications, such as certain enzyme-inducing drugs.  Your healthcare provider will assess for potential contraindications.

* **Drug Interactions:**  Ulipristal acetate can interact with other drugs.  It's crucial to inform your doctor about all medications, supplements, and herbal remedies you're currently taking.

* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  Ulipristal acetate is not recommended during pregnancy. Its use during breastfeeding is not well established.

* **Children and Older Adults:**  Ulipristal acetate is not typically used in children.  Dosage adjustments for older adults may be necessary depending on individual health conditions.  Always consult a doctor.

* **Allergic Reactions:**  Individuals with known allergies to ulipristal acetate should avoid using it.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: Is ulipristal acetate effective if I take it more than 5 days after unprotected sex?**  A: While it's most effective within 120 hours, some degree of protection may still be provided, but its effectiveness diminishes significantly as time passes.

* **Q: Does ulipristal acetate protect against sexually transmitted infections (STIs)?**  A: No, ulipristal acetate only prevents pregnancy; it does not offer protection against STIs.  Always use appropriate barrier methods, such as condoms, to prevent STIs.

* **Q: How should I store ulipristal acetate?** A: Store it in a cool, dry place, away from direct sunlight and moisture, as directed on the packaging.

* **Q: Can I use ulipristal acetate as a regular birth control method?** A: No, ulipristal acetate is an emergency contraceptive, not a regular birth control method.  It's intended for occasional use only.

* **Q:  What should I do if I miss a dose of ulipristal acetate (for fibroid treatment)?** A: Contact your doctor immediately; do not attempt to self-adjust the dosage or schedule.
