# Gefitinib

**Generic Name:** Gefitinib

**Usage:**

Gefitinib is a targeted therapy primarily used in the treatment of non-small cell lung cancer (NSCLC).  Its primary use is for patients with locally advanced or metastatic NSCLC whose tumors have a specific genetic mutation called an epidermal growth factor receptor (EGFR) mutation.  This mutation makes the cancer cells particularly sensitive to gefitinib's effects.  While primarily used for lung cancer, research is ongoing to explore its potential in other cancers with EGFR mutations.  It's crucial to understand that gefitinib's effectiveness depends on the presence of this specific EGFR mutation; testing for this mutation is essential before starting treatment.

**Dosage:**

Dosage of gefitinib is determined by a healthcare professional and varies depending on individual factors and the specific condition being treated.  The standard adult dose is typically 250mg once daily, taken orally as a tablet.  There are no established pediatric dosing guidelines as gefitinib is primarily used in adult populations.  Adjustments might be necessary based on factors like liver or kidney function, but this should always be done under the supervision of a doctor.  Gefitinib is not available in injectable or syrup form; it's administered orally as a tablet.


**Side Effects:**

Common side effects of gefitinib can include:

* Diarrhea
* Rash
* Acne-like skin eruptions
* Nausea
* Vomiting
* Fatigue
* Loss of appetite
* Dry skin

Less common but serious side effects that require immediate medical attention include:

* Severe skin reactions (Stevens-Johnson syndrome, toxic epidermal necrolysis)
* Interstitial lung disease (inflammation of the lungs)
* Liver problems (hepatotoxicity)
* Severe diarrhea
* Blood clots

If you experience any concerning side effects, contact your doctor immediately.


**How it Works:**

Gefitinib is a tyrosine kinase inhibitor (TKI).  It works by selectively blocking the activity of the EGFR protein, which plays a crucial role in the growth and spread of certain cancer cells, especially those with EGFR mutations. By inhibiting EGFR, gefitinib slows down or stops the uncontrolled growth of these cancer cells, helping to control the disease.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** Gefitinib should not be used in individuals with known hypersensitivity to the drug or its components.
* **Drug Interactions:** Gefitinib can interact with other medications.  Inform your doctor about all medications, including over-the-counter drugs, supplements, and herbal remedies, you are taking.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:** Gefitinib is generally not recommended during pregnancy or breastfeeding due to the potential risk to the developing fetus or infant.
* **Older Adults and Children:**  While primarily used in adults, careful monitoring is necessary for older adults due to potential increased sensitivity to side effects.  Gefitinib is not typically used in children.
* **Liver and Kidney Function:**  Patients with impaired liver or kidney function may require dosage adjustments.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long does it take to see results from gefitinib?** A: The response to gefitinib varies from person to person.  Some patients may see improvements within a few weeks, while others may take longer.  Regular monitoring is crucial.
* **Q: Can I stop taking gefitinib if I feel better?** A: No.  Do not stop taking gefitinib without consulting your doctor.  Stopping treatment prematurely could lead to disease progression.
* **Q: How should I store gefitinib?** A: Store gefitinib tablets at room temperature, away from moisture and direct sunlight.
* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it's almost time for your next dose.  Do not double up on doses.
* **Q: Are there any long-term effects of gefitinib?** A: Long-term use of gefitinib can have potential side effects.  Regular monitoring and follow-up with your healthcare provider are necessary to manage any complications and ensure long-term safety.
