# Ginkgo biloba extract EGb

**Generic Name:** Ginkgo biloba extract EGb

**Usage:**

Ginkgo biloba extract EGb is primarily used to improve cognitive function and blood circulation.  Its most common application is in managing symptoms associated with mild cognitive impairment, such as memory loss, difficulty concentrating, and confusion.  It's also sometimes used to treat symptoms of vertigo (dizziness) and tinnitus (ringing in the ears), although the evidence supporting these uses is less robust than for cognitive function.  While some individuals use it to support overall mental well-being or enhance memory, more research is needed to confirm these benefits definitively. It's crucial to understand that EGb is not a cure for dementia or Alzheimer's disease, but it may help manage some associated symptoms.

**Dosage:**

Dosage recommendations for Ginkgo biloba extract EGb vary depending on the specific product and the condition being treated.  Generally, adults may take 40-240 mg of standardized extract daily, divided into multiple doses.  However, this is not a universally agreed-upon standard, and some preparations use different standardization methods.  Always follow the dosage instructions provided on the specific product packaging. There's limited data on optimal dosages for children, so its use in children is generally not recommended without specific medical guidance.  Ginkgo biloba extract EGb is typically available in tablet or capsule form; other forms are less common.  Always consult a healthcare professional to determine the appropriate dosage and form for your individual needs.

**Side Effects:**

* Headache
* Upset stomach
* Dizziness
* Diarrhea
* Allergic reactions (rash, itching, swelling)

Less common, but more serious side effects, include bleeding (increased risk with anticoagulant use), and interactions with other medications.  If you experience any adverse effects, discontinue use and consult a doctor immediately.

**How it Works:**

Ginkgo biloba extract EGb is believed to work through multiple mechanisms.  It is thought to improve blood flow by inhibiting platelet aggregation (reducing blood clotting) and by potentially dilating blood vessels.  Improved blood flow to the brain is believed to contribute to its cognitive benefits.  It may also act as an antioxidant, protecting brain cells from damage caused by free radicals. The exact mechanisms and the extent to which these mechanisms contribute to the observed effects are still under investigation.

**Precautions:**

* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  Use during pregnancy and breastfeeding is generally discouraged due to insufficient safety data.
* **Bleeding Disorders:** Ginkgo biloba extract EGb can increase the risk of bleeding, especially when combined with anticoagulants (blood thinners) or antiplatelet medications (like aspirin). Consult your doctor before use if you have a bleeding disorder or are taking these medications.
* **Surgery:**  Stop taking Ginkgo biloba extract EGb at least two weeks before any scheduled surgery to reduce the risk of bleeding.
* **Drug Interactions:**  Ginkgo biloba extract EGb can interact with various medications, including anticoagulants, antiplatelets, antidepressants, and certain seizure medications. Consult your doctor or pharmacist about potential interactions before combining it with other drugs.
* **Allergic Reactions:** Some individuals may experience allergic reactions. If you have a history of allergies, particularly to plants in the *Ginkgo* family, exercise caution.
