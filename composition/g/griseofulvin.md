# Griseofulvin

**Generic Name: Griseofulvin**

**Usage:**

Griseofulvin is an antifungal medication primarily used to treat fungal infections of the skin, hair, and nails.  These infections, often called dermatophytoses, are caused by fungi like *Trichophyton*, *Epidermophyton*, and *Microsporum*.  It's particularly effective against infections like ringworm (tinea corporis), athlete's foot (tinea pedis), jock itch (tinea cruris), and fungal infections of the scalp (tinea capitis) and nails (onychomycosis). While its primary use is in dermatophytosis,  it may sometimes be considered for other superficial fungal infections, but this is less common and usually only after consultation with a specialist. The effectiveness of griseofulvin varies depending on the type and severity of the infection and the individual's response to the medication.


**Dosage:**

Dosage varies greatly depending on the type and severity of the infection, the patient's age and weight, and the specific formulation of griseofulvin.  It's crucial to follow a doctor's prescription precisely.  

* **Adults:**  Typical doses range from 500mg to 1000mg daily, usually given in a single dose or divided into two doses.  Higher doses might be prescribed for severe infections or infections of the nails.
* **Children:** Dosing for children is weight-based and should be determined by a pediatrician.  
* **Forms:** Griseofulvin is available in tablet and suspension (liquid) forms. The absorption rate differs; the microsize formulation is generally absorbed better than the ultramicrosize formulation.

**It is vital to take griseofulvin with a fatty meal to improve absorption.**


**Side Effects:**

* **Common:** Headache, nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, abdominal discomfort, dizziness, fatigue, and allergic skin reactions (rash, itching).
* **Less Common but Serious:** Liver damage (hepatotoxicity), blood disorders (leukopenia, neutropenia), and neurological effects (headaches, vertigo, confusion).  
* **Note:**  If you experience any unusual symptoms, such as persistent nausea, jaundice (yellowing of the skin or eyes), unusual bruising or bleeding, or changes in mental state, contact your doctor immediately.

**How it Works:**

Griseofulvin works by interfering with the growth of fungal cells. It is selectively taken up by actively growing fungal cells. Once inside, it disrupts the formation of the fungal cell wall, preventing the fungus from growing and replicating, ultimately leading to its death. Unlike many other antifungal drugs, griseofulvin doesn't directly kill the fungus; it inhibits its growth and reproduction.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** Griseofulvin should not be used in individuals with known hypersensitivity (allergy) to the medication or porphyria.
* **Drug Interactions:** Griseofulvin can interact with several other medications, including anticoagulants (blood thinners), oral contraceptives, and some drugs used to treat epilepsy.  Inform your doctor about all medications you are taking.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:** Griseofulvin should only be used during pregnancy or breastfeeding if the benefits outweigh the potential risks.  A doctor should carefully assess the situation before prescribing it.
* **Children:**  Use in children should be closely monitored by a pediatrician.
* **Older Adults:** Older adults may be more susceptible to side effects and require careful monitoring.
* **Alcohol:**  Excessive alcohol consumption should be avoided while on griseofulvin, as it can exacerbate potential liver effects.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long does it take to see results?** A:  The time it takes to see improvement varies depending on the infection's location and severity.  It can take several weeks or even months, especially for nail infections. Consistent treatment is crucial.
* **Q: Can I stop taking it early if I feel better?** A: No, completing the full course of treatment is essential even if symptoms improve. Stopping early could lead to the infection recurring or becoming resistant to the medication.
* **Q: How should I store griseofulvin?** A: Store it as directed on the label, usually in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight and moisture.
* **Q:  Is griseofulvin safe for long-term use?** A:  Long-term use should only be considered under a doctor's close supervision due to the potential for side effects. 
* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember unless it is almost time for the next dose.  Never double up on doses.
