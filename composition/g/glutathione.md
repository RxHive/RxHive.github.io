# Glutathione

**Generic Name: Glutathione**

**Usage:**

Glutathione is a naturally occurring antioxidant found in most cells of the body.  It plays a crucial role in protecting cells from damage caused by free radicals, unstable molecules that can harm cells and contribute to aging and disease. While not a drug in the traditional sense (it's often considered a supplement), glutathione is used therapeutically or as a supportive therapy for a variety of conditions, though evidence supporting its effectiveness varies across different applications.

Its *primary* purported use is in supporting liver health.  It’s believed to aid in detoxification processes and protect liver cells from damage.  

*Secondary* uses investigated include:

* **Neurological conditions:** Some studies explore its potential role in managing symptoms of neurological diseases, although more research is needed to solidify these findings.
* **Skin conditions:**  Topical application is sometimes explored for its potential antioxidant and brightening effects on the skin.
* **Immune support:**  Its role in immune function is being investigated, but conclusive evidence regarding its direct impact on immune response is still lacking.


It's crucial to understand that the evidence supporting many of these secondary uses is not yet conclusive, and more robust clinical trials are needed.  Glutathione should not be considered a cure or primary treatment for any disease.

**Dosage:**

There is no standard dosage for glutathione as a medication since it's not regulated as a conventional drug in many regions.  Dosage recommendations, when provided, vary significantly depending on the intended use (e.g., intravenous infusion, oral supplement), the form of glutathione (e.g., reduced glutathione, S-acetyl glutathione), and the individual's condition and response.  Dosage is usually determined by a healthcare professional.  Intravenous administration, if used, is always carried out under strict medical supervision due to potential complications.  Oral supplements come in varying strengths and forms (capsules, tablets, powders).

**Specific dosage information should be determined solely by a healthcare provider.**  Self-treating with glutathione without professional guidance is strongly discouraged.  Dosage for children is not established and is not recommended without specific guidance from a pediatrician.

**Side Effects:**

* **Common:** Nausea, vomiting, diarrhea.
* **Less Common but Serious:**  Allergic reactions (rash, itching, swelling), kidney problems (rare but requires immediate medical attention).

**Note:**  Even seemingly mild side effects warrant a call to a doctor.  Immediate medical help is required for severe reactions.


**How it Works:**

Glutathione works primarily as an antioxidant.  It neutralizes free radicals, preventing them from damaging cells.  This action is vital for protecting cells from oxidative stress, a process implicated in many diseases.  Its role in liver detoxification involves assisting in the metabolism and excretion of harmful substances. Its mechanisms of action in other proposed applications are still under investigation.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** Individuals with certain allergies or pre-existing medical conditions might need to avoid glutathione.  Consult a doctor before use.
* **Drug Interactions:** Glutathione might interact with certain medications.  It's essential to inform your doctor about all medications and supplements you are taking.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:** The safety of glutathione during pregnancy and breastfeeding isn't fully established. Consult a doctor before using it if you are pregnant, breastfeeding, or planning to become pregnant.
* **Elderly and Children:**  Use in children and the elderly requires extra caution and professional supervision due to potential variations in metabolism and response.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: Is glutathione safe?** A:  Generally considered safe when used as directed under medical supervision. However, individual reactions vary, and potential side effects exist.
* **Q:  Where can I buy glutathione?** A: It’s available as a dietary supplement in many pharmacies and health food stores, both offline and online.
* **Q: How should I store glutathione?** A: Store according to the manufacturer's instructions. Usually, this involves keeping it in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight.
* **Q:  Can glutathione cure diseases?** A: No.  It's not a cure for any disease, but it’s being explored for its potential supportive role in certain health conditions.  Always consult a healthcare provider for diagnosis and treatment.
* **Q: What are the risks associated with intravenous glutathione?** A: Intravenous administration carries risks of allergic reactions and other complications.  It must only be administered under strict medical supervision.
