# Clarithromycin

**Generic Name: Clarithromycin**

**Usage:**

Clarithromycin is a macrolide antibiotic, primarily used to treat bacterial infections. Its effectiveness lies in its ability to inhibit bacterial protein synthesis, effectively stopping the growth and reproduction of susceptible bacteria.  This makes it a valuable tool against a range of infections.  Its primary use is in treating respiratory tract infections such as bronchitis, pneumonia, and sinusitis caused by susceptible bacteria. It's also frequently prescribed for skin and soft tissue infections, and certain sexually transmitted infections.  Secondary uses include treating infections of the stomach and intestines (like *Helicobacter pylori* infections, often in combination with other medications), and certain types of ear infections.  It's important to remember that clarithromycin only works against bacteria; it’s ineffective against viral infections like the common cold or influenza.  A doctor will determine the appropriate use based on the specific infection and its cause.

**Dosage:**

Dosage varies significantly depending on the infection being treated, the patient's age, weight, and kidney/liver function.  A doctor will tailor the prescription accordingly.  Generally:

* **Adults:**  The typical dose is 250mg or 500mg twice daily.  Higher doses may be prescribed for severe infections.
* **Children:** Dosage is determined by weight and the specific infection.  Liquid formulations (syrups) are commonly used for children.  A doctor will calculate the correct dosage.

Clarithromycin is available in several forms including tablets, capsules, extended-release tablets, and oral suspensions (syrups).  Always follow the doctor's instructions precisely regarding dosage and frequency.

**Side Effects:**

Common side effects are generally mild and may include:

* Nausea
* Vomiting
* Diarrhea
* Abdominal pain
* Headache
* Dizziness
* Taste disturbances (metallic taste)

Less common but more serious side effects can include:

* Severe allergic reactions (anaphylaxis) – characterized by difficulty breathing, swelling of the face, lips, tongue, or throat
* Liver damage (hepatotoxicity) – indicated by jaundice (yellowing of skin and eyes), dark urine, and abdominal pain
* Clostridium difficile-associated diarrhea (CDAD) – a severe form of diarrhea caused by an overgrowth of bacteria in the gut
* Changes in heart rhythm (QT prolongation) – particularly in individuals with pre-existing heart conditions

If you experience any concerning side effects, discontinue use and seek immediate medical attention.


**How it Works:**

Clarithromycin works by binding to the 50S ribosomal subunit of bacteria.  This prevents the bacteria from synthesizing proteins, which are essential for their growth and survival.  By inhibiting protein synthesis, clarithromycin effectively stops the bacteria from reproducing and allows the body's immune system to clear the infection.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** Clarithromycin should not be used by individuals with known allergies to macrolide antibiotics.
* **Drug Interactions:**  Clarithromycin can interact with many other medications, including statins, anticoagulants (blood thinners), certain antifungal medications, and some heart medications. Inform your doctor of all medications, supplements, and herbal remedies you are taking.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  Use during pregnancy and breastfeeding should only be considered if the benefits clearly outweigh the potential risks.  A doctor should make this assessment.
* **Children:**  Use in children should be guided by a pediatrician who will assess the risk-benefit profile based on the child's age and health condition.
* **Older Adults:** Older adults may be more susceptible to side effects and require careful monitoring.
* **Liver and Kidney Disease:** Dose adjustments may be necessary for individuals with impaired liver or kidney function.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: Can I stop taking clarithromycin once I feel better?**  A: No, complete the prescribed course even if symptoms improve to prevent the infection from recurring and the development of antibiotic resistance.

* **Q: How should I store clarithromycin?** A: Store at room temperature, away from moisture and direct sunlight. Keep it out of reach of children.

* **Q: Is clarithromycin safe for everyone?** A:  Clarithromycin is generally safe when used as directed, but it can cause side effects.  Discuss its suitability with your doctor, especially if you have pre-existing health conditions.

* **Q:  What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it is almost time for the next dose. Do not take a double dose to make up for a missed one.

* **Q:  Can I drink alcohol while taking clarithromycin?** A:  While there isn't a strict prohibition, alcohol can worsen some of the side effects such as nausea and stomach upset. It's best to avoid alcohol or limit consumption while on the medication.
