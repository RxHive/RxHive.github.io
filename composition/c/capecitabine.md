# Capecitabine

**Generic Name: Capecitabine**

**Usage:**

Capecitabine is an oral chemotherapy medication primarily used to treat various types of cancer.  Its main application is in the treatment of colorectal cancer, often in combination with other chemotherapy drugs or as a single agent.  It's also used to treat breast cancer, stomach cancer, and other solid tumors, again usually as part of a broader treatment plan determined by an oncologist.  The specific use and combination of drugs will depend entirely on the individual's cancer type, stage, and overall health.  It's crucial to remember that capecitabine is a powerful medication, and its use is always under the strict supervision of a qualified healthcare professional.  It's never self-prescribed.


**Dosage:**

Dosage of capecitabine is highly individualized and determined by the oncologist based on factors like the patient's weight, overall health, type of cancer, and response to treatment. It's typically administered orally in tablet form.  There's no standard dosage applicable to all patients.  Children generally do not receive capecitabine due to its potent nature and the need for precise dosing based on body surface area, which is difficult to accurately determine and monitor in children undergoing chemotherapy.  The physician will carefully calculate the appropriate dose and schedule, usually involving multiple cycles of treatment spread over a period of time. Detailed instructions on the correct way to take the medication (e.g., with or without food) will be provided by the prescribing physician or pharmacist.


**Side Effects:**

Capecitabine, like other chemotherapy drugs, can cause various side effects.  These can range from mild to severe.  Common side effects include:

* Diarrhea
* Hand-foot syndrome (pain, redness, swelling, and blistering of the palms and soles)
* Nausea and vomiting
* Mouth sores (stomatitis)
* Fatigue
* Loss of appetite
* Rash
* Abdominal pain


Less common but serious side effects can include heart problems, nerve damage, and severe infections.  Any new or worsening symptoms should be reported to a healthcare provider immediately.


**How it Works:**

Capecitabine is a prodrug, meaning it's converted into an active form (5-fluorouracil or 5-FU) within the body.  5-FU interferes with the creation of DNA and RNA, essential components of rapidly dividing cells, such as cancer cells.  By disrupting this process, capecitabine slows or stops the growth of cancer cells.  However, it's important to note that this effect can also affect healthy cells, leading to some of the side effects mentioned above.

**Precautions:**

* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:** Capecitabine is contraindicated in pregnant women and should be avoided during breastfeeding due to potential harm to the fetus or infant.  Effective contraception is essential during treatment.
* **Liver and Kidney Disease:**  Patients with impaired liver or kidney function may require dosage adjustments.
* **Drug Interactions:** Capecitabine can interact with other medications.  It's crucial to inform your healthcare provider of all medications, supplements, and herbal remedies you are taking.
* **Older Adults:** Older adults may be more susceptible to side effects and may require dose adjustments.
* **Allergies:**  Those with known allergies to capecitabine or its components should not use this medication.

**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long do I need to take capecitabine?** A: The duration of treatment varies depending on the individual's condition and response to therapy. Your oncologist will determine the treatment schedule.
* **Q: Can I drink alcohol while taking capecitabine?** A: It's best to avoid alcohol while taking capecitabine, as it can worsen some side effects, particularly nausea.
* **Q: How should I store capecitabine?** A: Store capecitabine tablets at room temperature in a dry place, away from moisture and direct sunlight.  Always follow the storage instructions provided with the medication.
* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A:  Contact your doctor or pharmacist for guidance on missed doses.  Never double up on doses without professional advice.
