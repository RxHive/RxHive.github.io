# Cefuroxime axetil

**Generic Name:** Cefuroxime axetil

**Usage:**

Cefuroxime axetil is an antibiotic belonging to the cephalosporin family.  Its primary use is to treat bacterial infections in various parts of the body.  This includes respiratory tract infections like bronchitis and pneumonia; ear infections (otitis media); skin and soft tissue infections such as cellulitis and abscesses; urinary tract infections; and certain sexually transmitted infections.  While its primary role tackles bacterial infections, it's important to remember that it's effective only against bacteria susceptible to cefuroxime.  The doctor will determine the appropriate use based on the specific infection and its causative bacteria.  In some cases, it might be used as a preventative measure before certain surgical procedures to reduce the risk of infection.

**Dosage:**

Dosage varies significantly depending on the patient's age, weight, the severity of the infection, and the specific form of the medication.  A doctor will always prescribe the appropriate dose.  Generally:

* **Adults:**  Dosages range from 250mg to 500mg twice daily, usually taken with food.  Higher doses may be prescribed for severe infections.  The medication is commonly available as tablets or capsules.
* **Children:** The dosage is calculated based on weight and the specific infection.  Liquid suspensions (syrups) are usually prescribed for children.  The prescribing physician will provide precise instructions.
* **Intravenous (IV) Administration:** In severe cases, cefuroxime (the active component – axetil is the form suitable for oral intake) may be administered intravenously, requiring hospitalization.  Dosage and administration are strictly managed by medical professionals.

**Side Effects:**

* **Common:** Diarrhea, nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain, headache, rash. These are usually mild and temporary.
* **Less Common but Serious:**  Severe allergic reactions (anaphylaxis – characterized by difficulty breathing, swelling of the face, lips, or tongue), Clostridium difficile-associated diarrhea (a serious intestinal infection), seizures (especially in patients with pre-existing conditions), liver problems.
* **Important Note:**  If you experience any significant or concerning side effect, seek immediate medical attention.


**How it Works:**

Cefuroxime axetil is a beta-lactam antibiotic.  This means it works by interfering with the production of peptidoglycan, a crucial component of bacterial cell walls.  By disrupting this process, the antibiotic weakens the bacterial cell structure, leading to bacterial cell death and effectively combating the infection.  Once ingested, axetil is converted to cefuroxime in the body, which then performs its antibacterial function.

**Precautions:**

* **Allergies:**  Individuals with known allergies to penicillin or other cephalosporin antibiotics should not take cefuroxime axetil.  Severe allergic reactions can occur.
* **Drug Interactions:**  Cefuroxime axetil may interact with certain medications, including anticoagulants (blood thinners) and some diuretics.  Inform your doctor about all medications you are taking.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:** Use during pregnancy or breastfeeding should only be considered after careful evaluation of the benefits and risks by a healthcare provider.
* **Kidney Disease:**  Dosage adjustments may be necessary for individuals with kidney impairment.
* **Alcohol Consumption:** While not a strict contraindication, excessive alcohol consumption may interact with the medication and should be discussed with your physician.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: Can I stop taking cefuroxime axetil when I feel better?**  A: No, complete the prescribed course of treatment, even if you feel better, to prevent the infection from recurring and the development of antibiotic-resistant bacteria.

* **Q: How should I store cefuroxime axetil?** A: Store at room temperature, away from moisture and direct sunlight. Follow the storage instructions on the product label.

* **Q: Is cefuroxime axetil safe for children?** A:  Yes, but only under the strict supervision and prescription of a pediatrician.  The dosage is carefully calculated based on the child's weight and the infection's severity.

* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it's almost time for your next dose. Do not double the dose.

* **Q: Can I take cefuroxime axetil with other medications?** A: Consult your doctor or pharmacist before combining cefuroxime axetil with any other medication to avoid potential drug interactions.
