* **Glycerin (Glycerol):** A humectant (attracts and retains moisture), glycerin is widely used in many products, including topical creams and ointments to moisturize the skin, laxatives (osmotic laxative), and even some pharmaceutical formulations as a solvent or excipient (inactive ingredient).  Its medical uses are not as a primary treatment but as a supporting agent.

Research suggests potential benefits in eye health, specifically related to cataract formation and age-related macular degeneration.
