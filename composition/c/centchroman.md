# Centchroman

**Generic Name: Centchroman**

**Usage:**

Centchroman is primarily used as a long-acting oral contraceptive.  Its mechanism allows for extended periods of contraception, offering a convenient option for women seeking birth control.  While primarily known for its contraceptive effects, research also explores its potential role in managing uterine fibroids, a common condition causing heavy menstrual bleeding and pelvic pain.  However, its use in managing fibroids is not yet as widely established or accepted as its contraceptive use.  It's crucial to understand that Centchroman's use in treating fibroids remains an area of ongoing research and should only be considered under the close supervision of a healthcare professional.


**Dosage:**

Dosage varies depending on the intended use and the individual's condition.  For contraception, the typical adult dosage is a single 30mg tablet taken daily, consistently at the same time each day.  There are no standard pediatric doses established for Centchroman.  The medication is usually available in tablet form for oral administration.  Always follow the instructions provided by your prescribing physician.  Do not adjust the dose or discontinue treatment without consulting your doctor.


**Side Effects:**

Common side effects of Centchroman can include:

* Irregular menstrual bleeding (e.g., spotting, amenorrhea)
* Weight changes (gain or loss)
* Headaches
* Nausea
* Breast tenderness
* Mood changes

Less common, but potentially serious side effects may occur and require immediate medical attention.  These can include:

* Severe abdominal pain
* Signs of blood clots (e.g., chest pain, shortness of breath, leg pain)
* Severe allergic reactions (e.g., rash, hives, difficulty breathing)


If you experience any concerning side effects, seek immediate medical advice.


**How it Works:**

Centchroman's contraceptive action is primarily achieved by altering the hormonal environment in the body, specifically by inhibiting ovulation (the release of an egg).  Its mechanism involves impacting the hypothalamic-pituitary-ovarian axis, interfering with the normal hormonal signaling that governs the menstrual cycle.  Its potential effect on uterine fibroids is thought to involve reducing the production of hormones that stimulate fibroid growth.  The exact mechanisms behind this latter effect are still being investigated.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** Centchroman is contraindicated in individuals with known hypersensitivity to the drug, liver disease, breast cancer, undiagnosed vaginal bleeding, and pregnancy.  It should also be avoided in individuals with a history of blood clots.
* **Drug Interactions:** Centchroman can interact with other medications.  Always inform your doctor about all medications, supplements, and herbal remedies you are taking.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:** Centchroman is not intended for use during pregnancy.  Its use while breastfeeding should be discussed with a doctor.
* **Older Adults and Children:**  Centchroman is not typically recommended for use in children or older adults outside the specific clinical trials and under close medical supervision.
