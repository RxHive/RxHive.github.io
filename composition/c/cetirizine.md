# Cetirizine

**Generic Name: Cetirizine**

**Usage:**

Cetirizine is an antihistamine primarily used to relieve symptoms associated with allergies.  Its main use is in treating allergic rhinitis (hay fever), which causes symptoms like sneezing, runny nose, itchy eyes and nose, and congestion.  It's also effective in treating urticaria (hives), a skin condition characterized by itchy welts.  While not its primary indication, cetirizine may sometimes be used to help manage the symptoms of chronic idiopathic urticaria (chronic hives that don't have a known cause).  The effectiveness of cetirizine in treating other conditions related to allergies is still being studied.

**Dosage:**

Dosage varies depending on age, weight, and the specific condition being treated. Always follow your doctor's prescription.  Generally:

* **Adults:** The usual dose is 10mg once daily.
* **Children (6-12 years):** The usual dose is 5-10mg once daily, often dependent on weight.  A doctor will determine the appropriate dosage.
* **Children under 6 years:**  Cetirizine is generally not recommended for children under 6 years unless specifically prescribed by a pediatrician.

Cetirizine is available in various forms, including tablets, syrup (liquid), and sometimes as a solution for injection (though this is less common for everyday allergy relief).  Follow the instructions provided by your doctor or pharmacist on how to administer the medication correctly.


**Side Effects:**

Common side effects are generally mild and may include:

* Drowsiness
* Headache
* Dry mouth
* Fatigue
* Dizziness
* Nausea
* Stomach upset

Less common but more serious side effects are possible and require immediate medical attention.  These include:

* Allergic reactions (such as rash, swelling, difficulty breathing)
* Fast heartbeat
* Seizures
* Liver problems

If you experience any concerning side effects, consult your doctor or pharmacist immediately.

**How it Works:**

Cetirizine is a selective antagonist of peripheral H1-receptors.  In simpler terms, it blocks the action of histamine, a chemical released by the body during an allergic reaction.  Histamine is responsible for many of the symptoms of allergies, such as itching, sneezing, and swelling. By blocking histamine, cetirizine helps reduce these symptoms and provides relief.

**Precautions:**

* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:** Consult your doctor before taking cetirizine if you are pregnant, breastfeeding, or planning to become pregnant.
* **Kidney Problems:**  Individuals with kidney problems may require a dosage adjustment.
* **Interactions with Other Drugs:** Cetirizine may interact with certain medications. Inform your doctor about all the medications, supplements, and herbal remedies you are taking.
* **Alcohol:** Avoid alcohol consumption while taking cetirizine, as it can increase drowsiness.
* **Driving and Operating Machinery:**  Cetirizine can cause drowsiness; avoid activities requiring alertness until you know how it affects you.
* **Older Adults:** Older adults may be more sensitive to the effects of cetirizine.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: Can I take cetirizine with other medications?** A:  Always inform your doctor or pharmacist about all medications you are taking to avoid potential interactions.
* **Q: How should I store cetirizine?** A: Store cetirizine as directed on the label, typically at room temperature away from moisture and heat.
* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A:  Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it's almost time for your next dose. Do not double the dose.
* **Q: Is cetirizine habit-forming?** A: No, cetirizine is not considered habit-forming.
* **Q: How long does it take for cetirizine to work?** A:  It usually takes 30-60 minutes for cetirizine to start working, but the full effect may take a few hours.
