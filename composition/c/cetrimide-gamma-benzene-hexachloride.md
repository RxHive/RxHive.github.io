# Cetrimide + Gamma Benzene Hexachloride

**Generic Name:** Cetrimide + Gamma Benzene Hexachloride (This combination is less common now and may not be widely available.  Information provided is based on the individual components' properties and historical uses.)

**Usage:**

This combination was historically used primarily for treating skin infections, particularly those caused by a range of bacteria and lice. Cetrimide, a quaternary ammonium compound, acts as a disinfectant and antiseptic, effectively killing bacteria. Gamma Benzene Hexachloride (lindane), an organochlorine insecticide, is effective against lice and other parasitic insects.  The combination aimed to provide both antiseptic and parasiticidal action.  It's crucial to note that due to concerns about the toxicity of lindane, this combination is less frequently prescribed now. Modern treatments generally favor safer alternatives.

**Side Effects:**

* **Common:** Skin irritation (redness, itching, rash), dryness, burning sensation at the application site.
* **Less Common but Serious:**  Allergic reactions (including anaphylaxis – a life-threatening allergic reaction),  neurotoxicity (with lindane – particularly in children and with prolonged use or high doses), seizures. Lindane absorption through the skin can cause central nervous system effects.


**How it Works:**

Cetrimide disrupts the bacterial cell membrane, leading to cell death and preventing bacterial growth.  Lindane interferes with the nervous system of lice and other insects, leading to their paralysis and death.
