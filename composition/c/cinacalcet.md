# Cinacalcet

**Generic Name: Cinacalcet**

**Usage:**

Cinacalcet is primarily used to control high levels of calcium in the blood (hypercalcemia) in people with secondary hyperparathyroidism.  This condition often occurs in individuals with chronic kidney disease who are undergoing dialysis.  The parathyroid glands become overactive, producing too much parathyroid hormone (PTH), which leads to elevated calcium levels. Cinacalcet helps to regulate PTH production, thereby lowering calcium levels.  It's also used to treat hypercalcemia in patients with parathyroid carcinoma (cancer of the parathyroid glands). While less common, it may be considered in other cases of hyperparathyroidism where other treatments haven't been effective.

**Dosage:**

Dosage is individualized and determined by a doctor based on the patient's condition and response to treatment. It typically starts at a low dose and is gradually increased until the desired blood calcium levels are achieved.  Dosage forms generally include tablets for oral administration.

* **Adults with secondary hyperparathyroidism:** The initial dose is usually low, and adjustments are made based on blood calcium and PTH levels.  Specific dosing instructions will be provided by a doctor.
* **Adults with parathyroid carcinoma:**  Dosing is similarly individualized and monitored closely.
* **Children:** Cinacalcet is not typically used in children, and its use in pediatric populations is not well established.

**Important Note:**  This is not an exhaustive list of dosage variations, and it's crucial to follow a doctor's specific instructions.

**Side Effects:**

* **Common:** Nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, constipation, muscle cramps, headache, fatigue.
* **Less Common but Serious:**  Hypotension (low blood pressure), bradycardia (slow heart rate), seizures, severe allergic reactions.
* **Other Possible Side Effects:**  These can vary significantly from person to person and require immediate medical attention if experienced.


It's vital to report any unusual symptoms or side effects to a healthcare provider immediately.


**How it Works:**

Cinacalcet is a calcimimetic agent.  This means it mimics the action of calcium on the calcium-sensing receptors in the parathyroid glands. By activating these receptors, cinacalcet reduces the production and secretion of parathyroid hormone (PTH). Lower PTH levels then lead to a decrease in the amount of calcium released from the bones into the bloodstream, thus helping to regulate calcium levels back to a normal range.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:**  Cinacalcet should not be used in patients with known hypersensitivity (allergy) to the drug.
* **Drug Interactions:** Cinacalcet may interact with other medications, particularly those affecting calcium levels or kidney function.  It's essential to inform your doctor about all medications, supplements, and herbal remedies you are taking.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:** The safety of cinacalcet during pregnancy and breastfeeding has not been fully established.  It should only be used during pregnancy or breastfeeding if the potential benefits outweigh the potential risks.  Consult your doctor.
* **Older Adults and Patients with Renal Impairment:**  Dosage adjustments may be necessary for older adults and patients with kidney problems. Close monitoring is crucial in these populations.

* **Q: How should I store cinacalcet?** A: Store it as directed on the label, usually at room temperature away from moisture and light.
