# Calcium Citrate Malate + Calcitriol

**Generic Name:** Calcium Citrate Malate + Calcitriol

**Usage:**

Calcium Citrate Malate + Calcitriol is primarily used to treat hypoparathyroidism, a condition where the parathyroid glands don't produce enough parathyroid hormone (PTH).  PTH is crucial for regulating calcium levels in the blood.  Insufficient PTH leads to low blood calcium (hypocalcemia), which can cause a range of symptoms including muscle cramps, tingling sensations, and even seizures in severe cases. This combination medication supplies both calcium (in the form of calcium citrate malate, offering better absorption than some other calcium salts) and calcitriol (the active form of vitamin D), which works synergistically with PTH to maintain healthy calcium levels.  While its primary use is in hypoparathyroidism, it may also be used in other conditions characterized by low blood calcium levels, though this would typically be determined on a case-by-case basis by a physician.


**Dosage:**

Dosage is highly individualized and determined by a doctor based on factors like the patient's age, weight, overall health, and the severity of hypocalcemia.  There's no standardized dosage applicable to all.  The medication may be available in tablet or capsule form.  A doctor will prescribe the appropriate strength and frequency, typically adjusting the dose based on regular blood calcium level monitoring.  Children's dosages will be significantly different from adult dosages, always following the prescription precisely.  There is no injection form of this combination medication currently widely available.

**Side Effects:**

* **Common:** Nausea, vomiting, constipation, headache, dizziness.
* **Less Common but Serious:** Hypercalcemia (high blood calcium levels – symptoms include excessive thirst, frequent urination, constipation, nausea, vomiting, weakness, and confusion), kidney stones, arrhythmias.

*Note:*  This is not an exhaustive list. If you experience any unexpected or concerning side effects, immediately consult your doctor.


**How it Works:**

Calcium citrate malate provides a readily absorbable source of calcium, directly increasing blood calcium levels.  Calcitriol, the active form of vitamin D, enhances calcium absorption from the intestines and promotes calcium retention by the kidneys.  Together, they compensate for the deficiency of PTH, helping to restore and maintain healthy blood calcium levels.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** This medication is not suitable for individuals with hypercalcemia (high blood calcium levels) or those with severe kidney disease.
* **Drug Interactions:**  This medication can interact with other drugs, including some heart medications, diuretics, and certain antibiotics.  Inform your doctor of all medications and supplements you are taking.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:** Use during pregnancy and breastfeeding should only be considered under strict medical supervision due to the potential effects on the developing fetus or infant.
* **Older Adults and Children:**  Dosage adjustments are crucial for both these groups due to varying physiological needs and potential sensitivities.
* **Kidney Function:**  Regular monitoring of kidney function is essential, especially in patients with pre-existing kidney conditions.

* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it's almost time for your next dose. Do not double the dose.
* **Q: How should I store this medication?** A: Store as directed on the label, typically in a cool, dry place, away from direct sunlight and moisture.
* **Q: Can I take this medication with food?** A:  It's generally recommended to take it with food to minimize gastrointestinal side effects. Follow your doctor's instructions.
