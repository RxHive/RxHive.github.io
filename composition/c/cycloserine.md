# Cycloserine

**Generic Name: Cycloserine**

**Usage:**

Cycloserine is an antibiotic primarily used to treat tuberculosis (TB), specifically in cases resistant to other TB medications.  It's often employed as a second-line drug when first-line treatments prove ineffective.  This means it's used when the TB bacteria have developed resistance to more commonly used antibiotics.  While its primary role is in treating tuberculosis, it has also shown some limited effectiveness in treating other bacterial infections, but this is not its standard or recommended use.  The decision to use cycloserine should always be made in consultation with a physician due to its potential side effects.

**Dosage:**

Dosage of cycloserine varies considerably depending on the patient's age, weight, the severity of the infection, and the overall health of the individual.  A doctor will determine the appropriate dosage based on these factors.  Generally, it's administered orally in tablet form.  There is no standard pediatric dosage readily available; pediatric use is very specific and necessitates careful monitoring and dosing adjustments by a qualified physician. Dosage often begins low and is gradually increased as tolerated to achieve therapeutic blood levels, minimizing side effects. Specific instructions on dosage and frequency must come directly from a prescribing physician.  Intramuscular injection is rarely used and is usually only considered under specific circumstances dictated by a physician.

**Side Effects:**

Cycloserine can cause a range of side effects, some common and others less frequent but more serious.  It's crucial to report any adverse effects to your doctor immediately.

* **Common Side Effects:** Nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, dizziness, drowsiness, headache, nervousness, and confusion.
* **Less Common but Serious Side Effects:**  Severe allergic reactions (including anaphylaxis), seizures, peripheral neuropathy (nerve damage in the extremities), psychosis (loss of contact with reality), and depression.  These require immediate medical attention.

**How it Works:**

Cycloserine works by interfering with the growth and reproduction of bacteria. It inhibits bacterial cell wall synthesis, impacting the ability of bacteria, including *Mycobacterium tuberculosis*, to build and maintain their protective outer layers.  This ultimately weakens the bacteria and makes them more susceptible to the body's immune response and other treatments.  Its specific mechanism involves interfering with bacterial enzymes involved in cell wall formation.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** Cycloserine is contraindicated in individuals with a known hypersensitivity or allergy to the medication.
* **Drug Interactions:** Cycloserine can interact with other medications, including some antidepressants and anticonvulsants. It's crucial to inform your physician about all medications you are currently taking.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:** Cycloserine should be used during pregnancy or breastfeeding only if the potential benefits clearly outweigh the potential risks to the mother and child.  A physician must carefully assess the risks and benefits.
* **Elderly and Children:**  Both elderly and pediatric patients require careful monitoring due to increased susceptibility to side effects.  Dosage adjustments are often necessary.
* **Renal or Hepatic Impairment:** Patients with impaired kidney or liver function may require dosage adjustments or more frequent monitoring.

**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long does it take to see results?** A:  The time to see improvement varies depending on the condition being treated and the individual's response.  TB treatment, for example, is a long-term process.
* **Q: How should I store cycloserine?** A: Store as directed on the medication label, usually in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight and moisture.
* **Q: Can I stop taking cycloserine if I feel better?** A: No, do not stop taking cycloserine without consulting your doctor.  Stopping treatment prematurely can lead to antibiotic resistance and relapse of the infection.
* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it's almost time for your next dose. Do not double the dose.
* **Q:  Is cycloserine addictive?** A: Cycloserine is not considered addictive in the same way as opioids or other drugs of abuse. However, stopping abruptly can lead to withdrawal-like symptoms, necessitating gradual tapering under medical supervision.
