# Caffeine

**Generic Name: Caffeine**

**Usage:**

Caffeine's primary medical use is as a central nervous system stimulant.  It's primarily used to treat drowsiness and fatigue, often associated with conditions like narcolepsy or shift work sleep disorder.  While not a first-line treatment for these, it can provide temporary relief.  Secondary uses include its inclusion in some headache medications, where its vasoconstrictive properties may help alleviate pain.  It's also found in some over-the-counter (OTC) cold and flu medications, primarily to counteract the drowsiness induced by other ingredients.  Importantly, caffeine's medical uses are generally limited to short-term symptom relief and are not intended as a long-term solution for any underlying medical condition.


**Dosage:**

Dosage varies greatly depending on the formulation (tablet, capsule, liquid), the individual's sensitivity, and the intended effect. There isn't a universally standardized medical dose for treating conditions like fatigue.  For over-the-counter medications containing caffeine, the recommended dosage is usually stated on the product label.  It is crucial to adhere to these instructions.  Exceeding recommended doses can lead to significant side effects.   Children, pregnant women, and individuals with pre-existing health conditions should always consult a healthcare professional before using any product containing caffeine.  Medical professionals will often adjust dosage based on individual factors.


**Side Effects:**

* **Common:**  Headache, nervousness, restlessness, insomnia, irritability, stomach upset, increased heart rate, and increased urination.
* **Less Common but Serious:**  Anxiety, panic attacks, rapid or irregular heartbeat (palpitations), tremors, seizures, and high blood pressure.  Allergic reactions, though rare, are possible.

**Note:**  If you experience any adverse effects, discontinue use and consult a healthcare provider immediately.
