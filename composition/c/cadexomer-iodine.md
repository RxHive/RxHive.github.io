# Cadexomer Iodine

**Generic Name: Cadexomer Iodine**

**Usage:**

Cadexomer iodine is a topical antiseptic and wound dressing used primarily to treat infected wounds and burns.  Its primary use is in managing chronic wounds like leg ulcers, pressure sores (pressure ulcers), and diabetic foot ulcers. These wounds often have a significant bacterial burden, and cadexomer iodine helps to reduce this infection while promoting a healthy healing environment.  Secondary uses may include treating superficial wounds like cuts and abrasions, though other, less complex antiseptic solutions are often preferred for these minor injuries.  It’s important to remember that cadexomer iodine is for external use only and should never be ingested.


**Dosage:**

Cadexomer iodine is not available in tablet, syrup, or injectable forms.  It comes as a paste or powder, usually applied directly to the affected area.  Dosage varies considerably depending on the size and nature of the wound.  A healthcare professional should determine the appropriate amount and frequency of application. There are no specific dosage recommendations for children based on age or weight, as the amount used is directly related to the wound size, not overall patient size.


**Side Effects:**

* **Common:** Mild skin irritation (redness, itching), burning sensation at the application site.  These are usually temporary and subside once the medication is removed.
* **Less Common but Serious:** Allergic reactions (rash, hives, swelling, difficulty breathing).  These are rare but require immediate medical attention.  Any signs of a severe allergic reaction necessitate immediate contact with emergency services.
* **Note:**  If you experience any unusual or persistent side effects, discontinue use and consult a healthcare provider immediately.


**How it Works:**

Cadexomer iodine is a complex of iodine and a dextran polymer.  The dextran acts as a carrier, delivering iodine slowly to the wound bed.  Iodine, a well-known antiseptic, kills bacteria and other microorganisms, thus reducing the infection level in the wound.  At the same time, the dextran component absorbs wound exudate (fluid), helping to keep the wound clean and moist, promoting healing. This dual action of killing bacteria and managing wound moisture contributes to a favorable healing environment.
