# Cefixime + Lactobacillus

**Generic Name:** Cefixime + Lactobacillus

**Usage:**

Cefixime + Lactobacillus is a combination medication used primarily to treat bacterial infections, particularly those of the respiratory tract (like bronchitis and pneumonia) and urinary tract (like cystitis and pyelonephritis).  Cefixime, a third-generation cephalosporin antibiotic, directly targets and kills harmful bacteria.  Lactobacillus, a beneficial bacterium, helps restore the balance of the gut flora often disrupted by antibiotics. This combination aims to effectively treat the infection while minimizing the risk of antibiotic-associated diarrhea and other gastrointestinal side effects common with antibiotics alone. While primarily used for bacterial infections, the specific application should always be determined by a healthcare professional based on individual symptoms and diagnosis. Secondary uses might occasionally include treating certain types of ear infections or skin infections, but this is not always standard practice.

**Dosage:**

Dosage of Cefixime + Lactobacillus varies significantly depending on factors such as the patient's age, weight, the specific infection being treated, and the severity of the illness.  A doctor will prescribe the appropriate dosage.  The medication may be available in tablet or syrup form.  It’s crucial to follow the prescribed dosage and duration of treatment precisely as directed by a healthcare professional.  Self-adjusting the dosage or prematurely stopping the medication can lead to incomplete treatment and the development of antibiotic-resistant bacteria.  Information on specific dosages for adults and children will be provided by the prescribing physician, as this varies based on formulation and indication.

**Side Effects:**

* **Common:** Diarrhea, nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain, bloating, headache, dizziness.
* **Less Common but Serious:**  Allergic reactions (rash, itching, swelling, difficulty breathing), pseudomembranous colitis (severe inflammation of the colon), changes in blood counts.
* **Note:**  This is not an exhaustive list.  Any new or worsening symptoms should be reported to a doctor immediately.

**How it Works:**

Cefixime works by interfering with the ability of bacteria to build their cell walls, ultimately leading to their death.  Lactobacillus, on the other hand, acts as a probiotic, replenishing the beneficial bacteria in the gut.  This helps prevent the disruption of the normal gut flora which can often lead to side effects like diarrhea, a common consequence of antibiotic use.  By working together, cefixime effectively combats the infection while lactobacillus helps maintain a healthy gut microbiome, minimizing the likelihood of gastrointestinal issues.

**Popular Brand Names (India):**

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:**  This medication should not be used by individuals with known allergies to cephalosporin antibiotics or lactobacillus.
* **Drug Interactions:**  Cefixime can interact with certain medications, including anticoagulants (blood thinners) and some types of diuretics.  Always inform your doctor of all medications, supplements, and herbal remedies you are taking.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:** Use during pregnancy and breastfeeding should only be considered under strict medical supervision, weighing the potential benefits against the risks.
* **Children and Older Adults:**  Dosage adjustments may be necessary for children and older adults due to differences in metabolism and potential organ function.
* **Alcohol:** Consumption of alcohol should be avoided while taking this medication as it can increase the risk of side effects.

**FAQs:**

* **Q: Can I stop taking the medication once I feel better?**  A: No, it's crucial to complete the full course of antibiotics as prescribed by your doctor, even if symptoms improve.  Stopping early can lead to treatment failure and antibiotic resistance.
* **Q: How should I store the medication?** A: Store as directed on the product label, typically in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight and moisture.
* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it's almost time for the next dose.  Never double up on doses.
* **Q: Is this medication safe for long-term use?** A: Prolonged use of antibiotics can lead to the development of antibiotic-resistant bacteria and other potential complications.  It should only be used for the duration prescribed by your doctor.
