# Candesartan

**Generic Name: Candesartan**

**Usage:**

Candesartan is an angiotensin II receptor blocker (ARB).  Its primary use is to treat high blood pressure (hypertension), helping to lower blood pressure and reduce the risk of serious cardiovascular events like stroke and heart attack.  This is achieved by blocking the action of angiotensin II, a hormone that narrows blood vessels.  

Beyond hypertension, candesartan also finds use in treating heart failure, specifically to improve symptoms and reduce hospitalizations in patients with reduced ejection fraction (a measure of the heart's pumping efficiency).  It can also be prescribed to slow the progression of chronic kidney disease in patients with type 2 diabetes and high blood pressure.  Importantly, the specific use and dosage will always be determined by a doctor based on individual needs and health status.


**Dosage:**

Dosage varies significantly depending on the condition being treated, the patient's age, weight, and kidney function.  A doctor will determine the appropriate dose.  Generally, candesartan is available as an oral tablet. There are no injectable or syrup forms widely available.  

* **Hypertension:**  Dosage typically ranges from 4mg to 32mg once daily.
* **Heart failure:**  Dosage is usually started at a lower dose and gradually increased as tolerated under close medical supervision.
* **Diabetic nephropathy:** Dosage is tailored to individual needs and monitored closely.

**It is crucial to follow a doctor's instructions precisely regarding dosage and frequency.**  Never adjust the dose on your own.

**Side Effects:**

* **Common:** Dizziness, headache, fatigue, diarrhea, nausea, upper respiratory infection.  These are usually mild and transient.
* **Less Common but Serious:**  Low blood pressure (hypotension), kidney problems, hyperkalemia (high potassium levels in the blood), angioedema (swelling of the face, lips, tongue, or throat – seek immediate medical attention if this occurs), cough.
* **If you experience any adverse effects, especially those listed as serious, contact your doctor immediately.**


**How it Works:**

Candesartan works by blocking the effects of angiotensin II, a hormone that causes blood vessels to constrict. By blocking angiotensin II's action, candesartan allows blood vessels to relax and widen, reducing blood pressure.  This reduced pressure eases the workload on the heart and kidneys, improving their function in conditions like hypertension and heart failure.  In diabetic patients with kidney disease, it helps protect the kidneys from further damage.

**Precautions:**

* **Pregnancy:**  Candesartan should not be used during pregnancy as it can cause serious harm to the developing fetus.
* **Breastfeeding:**  Consult a doctor before using candesartan if breastfeeding.
* **Kidney Disease:**  Dosage adjustments are usually necessary for individuals with kidney problems.
* **Liver Disease:**  Caution is advised in patients with liver disease.
* **Drug Interactions:**  Candesartan can interact with certain other medications, including potassium-sparing diuretics, potassium supplements, and some NSAIDs.  Always inform your doctor of all medications you are taking.
* **Older Adults:**  Older adults may be more sensitive to the side effects of candesartan.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: Can I stop taking candesartan suddenly?**  A: No.  Stopping candesartan abruptly can lead to a rebound effect, causing a sudden increase in blood pressure.  Always follow your doctor's instructions for tapering off the medication.
* **Q:  How should I store candesartan?** A: Store candesartan tablets in a cool, dry place, away from direct sunlight and moisture, as per the instructions on the packaging.
* **Q:  Is candesartan addictive?** A: No, candesartan is not an addictive medication.
* **Q:  What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it is almost time for your next dose. Do not double the dose.
