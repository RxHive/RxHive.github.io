# Cyclosporine topical

**Generic Name: Cyclosporine topical**

**Usage:**

Cyclosporine topical is primarily used to treat severe forms of keratitis (inflammation of the cornea), specifically those caused by immune system overactivity. This includes conditions like severe dry eye disease, allergic keratoconjunctivitis, and certain types of corneal graft rejection.  Its effectiveness lies in its ability to suppress the immune response that contributes to the inflammation and damage in the cornea.  While primarily used for the eye, some formulations might be used off-label for other skin conditions characterized by inflammation, though this should always be under the direct supervision of a doctor.  It's crucial to understand that cyclosporine topical is not a first-line treatment and is typically reserved for cases unresponsive to other therapies.

**Dosage:**

Dosage of cyclosporine topical varies significantly depending on the specific formulation (e.g., eye drops, ointment), the condition being treated, and the physician's judgment.  There isn't a standardized dosage across all conditions or age groups.  A doctor will determine the appropriate frequency and amount of application based on individual needs. For instance, eye drops might be prescribed one to four times daily, while an ointment might be used once or twice daily. Pediatric dosages will always be determined by a pediatrician or ophthalmologist.  Never attempt to adjust the dosage without consulting your healthcare professional.  Instructions will be provided with the prescription.

**Side Effects:**

Common side effects of cyclosporine topical are generally mild and may include:

* Burning or stinging sensation in the eye(s) upon application.
* Eye irritation or redness.
* Blurred vision (temporary).
* Headaches.

Less common, but more serious side effects can include:

* Increased risk of infection.
* Eye pressure changes (potentially leading to glaucoma).
* Allergic reactions (rash, itching, swelling).

If you experience any side effects, particularly those listed as more serious, contact your doctor immediately.

**How it Works:**

Cyclosporine topical works by selectively inhibiting calcineurin, an enzyme involved in the activation of T-cells.  T-cells are a crucial part of the immune system; their overactivity contributes to the inflammatory process in conditions like severe keratitis.  By suppressing the activity of T-cells in the affected area, cyclosporine topical reduces inflammation and helps the cornea heal.  The effect is localized to the application site, minimizing systemic side effects compared to oral cyclosporine.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** Cyclosporine topical should generally be avoided in individuals with known hypersensitivity to cyclosporine.
* **Drug Interactions:**  Inform your doctor about all medications you are taking, including over-the-counter drugs and herbal supplements, as interactions are possible.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  The use of cyclosporine topical during pregnancy and breastfeeding should only be considered if the potential benefits outweigh the risks.  Your doctor should be consulted.
* **Elderly and Children:**  Dosage adjustments may be needed for elderly patients or children; careful monitoring by a healthcare professional is essential.
* **Infection:**  Because cyclosporine topical suppresses the immune system locally, it is crucial to practice good hygiene to prevent infection.

* **Q: How long does it take to see results?** A: The time to see improvement varies depending on the condition and individual response, but it can take several weeks.
* **Q: Can I use contact lenses while using cyclosporine topical?** A:  Generally, it's advised to avoid contact lenses during treatment. Check with your doctor for specific guidance.
* **Q: How should I store cyclosporine topical?** A: Follow the storage instructions provided with the medication; typically, it involves storing it at room temperature, away from direct sunlight and moisture.
* **Q: Can I stop taking it abruptly?** A: No, always follow your doctor's instructions regarding the duration of treatment.  Stopping abruptly may worsen the condition.
