# Cefalexin

**Generic Name:** Cefalexin

**Usage:**

Cefalexin is a first-generation cephalosporin antibiotic.  Its primary use is to treat bacterial infections affecting various parts of the body.  This includes infections of the skin and soft tissues (like boils, cellulitis, and impetigo), respiratory tract infections (such as pneumonia and bronchitis – though it's less effective against some pneumonia types), urinary tract infections (UTIs), and ear infections (otitis media).  It's also sometimes used to prevent infection after certain surgical procedures.  It's important to note that cefalexin is effective only against bacterial infections; it won't work against viral infections like the common cold or influenza.  The specific bacterial types susceptible to cefalexin vary, and a doctor will determine if it's the right antibiotic for a particular infection.

**Dosage:**

Dosage varies significantly depending on the type of infection, the patient's age and weight, and the form of the medication (tablets, capsules, oral suspension/syrup, or intravenous injection).  A doctor will always prescribe the appropriate dose.  Generally, for adults, dosages range from 250mg to 1000mg every 6-8 hours.  Children's dosages are calculated based on weight and are significantly lower.  The oral forms (tablets, capsules, syrup) are usually taken with food to reduce stomach upset.  Intravenous administration is done in a clinical setting by healthcare professionals.  Never adjust your dosage without consulting your physician.

**Side Effects:**

* **Common:** Diarrhea, nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain, indigestion.
* **Less Common but Potentially Serious:**  Allergic reactions (rash, itching, hives, swelling, difficulty breathing – seek immediate medical attention if these occur),  pseudomembranous colitis (a severe bowel inflammation), changes in blood counts.
* **Rare but Serious:** Seizures (especially in patients with pre-existing kidney problems or those receiving high doses), liver damage.

Any persistent or worsening side effects require immediate medical attention.  Don't hesitate to contact your healthcare provider if you experience any concerns.


**How it Works:**

Cefalexin works by interfering with the bacteria's ability to build their cell walls.  Bacterial cell walls are crucial for their survival and structure.  By disrupting this process, cefalexin weakens the bacterial cells, leading to their death and ultimately resolving the infection.  It's a bactericidal antibiotic, meaning it kills bacteria directly.


**Popular Brand Names (India):**

**Precautions:**

* **Allergy:**  Individuals with a known allergy to penicillin or other cephalosporin antibiotics should not take cefalexin.
* **Kidney Disease:** Patients with kidney problems may require dosage adjustments to prevent drug accumulation and potential side effects.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:** Cefalexin should only be used during pregnancy or breastfeeding under strict medical supervision.
* **Interactions:** Cefalexin may interact with certain medications, including anticoagulants (blood thinners) and some types of oral contraceptives.  Always inform your doctor of all medications you are taking.
* **Alcohol:** While not strictly contraindicated, excessive alcohol consumption should be avoided while taking cefalexin, as it can increase the risk of some side effects.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: Can I stop taking cefalexin once I feel better?** A: No, it's crucial to complete the entire course of antibiotics as prescribed by your doctor, even if you feel better.  Stopping early can lead to the recurrence of the infection and the development of antibiotic-resistant bacteria.

* **Q: How should I store cefalexin?** A: Store cefalexin at room temperature, away from moisture and direct sunlight.  Keep it out of reach of children.

* **Q: Is cefalexin safe for children?** A: Yes, but only under a doctor's supervision and at a dosage appropriate for their age and weight.

* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it's almost time for your next dose.  Never double up on doses.
