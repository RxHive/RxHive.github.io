# Cefadroxil + Lactobacillus Acidophilus

**Generic Name:** Cefadroxil + *Lactobacillus acidophilus*

**Usage:** This combination medication is primarily used to treat bacterial infections, particularly those affecting the skin, ears, throat, and urinary tract, while simultaneously replenishing beneficial gut bacteria.  Cefadroxil, a cephalosporin antibiotic, tackles the infection by killing bacteria.  *Lactobacillus acidophilus*, a probiotic, helps restore the balance of gut flora often disrupted by antibiotic treatment. This dual action aims to effectively treat the infection while minimizing the risk of antibiotic-associated diarrhea (AAD) – a common side effect of antibiotics.  Secondary uses might include treating certain types of pneumonia or other bacterial infections, but this should always be determined by a physician.

**Dosage:**  Dosage varies significantly depending on the individual's age, weight, the severity of the infection, and the specific formulation (tablet, capsule, or syrup).  A doctor will determine the appropriate dosage.  **Never attempt self-medication.**  It is crucial to follow the prescribed dosage and duration of treatment precisely, as stopping the medication prematurely can lead to treatment failure and the development of antibiotic-resistant bacteria.  Information on specific dosages for adults and children must be obtained from a medical professional or pharmacist based on the specific brand and formulation.

**Side Effects:**

* **Common:** Diarrhea, nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain, rash, itching.
* **Less Common but Serious:** Allergic reactions (ranging from mild hives to severe anaphylaxis), Clostridium difficile-associated diarrhea (CDAD), changes in blood counts.
* **Rare:**  Serious liver or kidney problems.

**Note:** If you experience any of these side effects, especially severe reactions or persistent diarrhea, contact your doctor immediately.

**How it Works:** Cefadroxil works by interfering with the bacteria's ability to build their cell walls, ultimately leading to their death.  This action effectively combats the bacterial infection.  Simultaneously, *Lactobacillus acidophilus*, a probiotic strain, helps restore the natural balance of beneficial bacteria in the gut.  Antibiotics can disrupt this balance, making the gut more vulnerable to harmful bacteria and leading to diarrhea.  The probiotic helps prevent this by competing with harmful bacteria for resources and potentially producing substances that inhibit their growth.

**Popular Brand Names (India):**  Brand names vary by region and manufacturer. A pharmacist or doctor in India can provide a current list of available brands containing Cefadroxil and *Lactobacillus acidophilus*.

**Precautions:**

* **Allergies:** This medication should not be used by individuals with known allergies to cephalosporin antibiotics or *Lactobacillus acidophilus*.
* **Drug Interactions:**  Inform your doctor about all other medications you are taking, including over-the-counter drugs, supplements, and herbal remedies, as interactions are possible.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  Use during pregnancy and breastfeeding should only be considered if the potential benefits outweigh the potential risks. Consult your doctor before use.
* **Kidney/Liver Impairment:**  Dosage adjustments may be necessary for individuals with kidney or liver problems.
* **Children:** Dosage should be carefully determined by a physician based on the child's weight and age.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: Can I stop taking this medication if I feel better?** A: No.  Complete the prescribed course of treatment even if you feel better to ensure the infection is completely eradicated and prevent the development of antibiotic resistance.
* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it is almost time for the next dose. Do not double the dose.
* **Q: How should I store this medication?** A: Store it as directed on the label, typically in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight and moisture. Keep it out of reach of children.
* **Q: Is it safe to consume alcohol while taking this medication?** A: It's best to avoid alcohol while taking this medication, as it can potentially interact with the antibiotic and lead to increased side effects. Consult your doctor.
* **Q:  Can I take this medication with dairy products?** A:  It's generally fine, but check your specific product's instructions as some formulations might advise against taking it with certain foods or drinks.
