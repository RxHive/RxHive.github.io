# Chloroquine + Primaquine

**Generic Name:** Chloroquine + Primaquine

**Usage:**

Chloroquine and primaquine, when combined, are primarily used to treat malaria caused by the *Plasmodium vivax* and *Plasmodium ovale* parasites.  These parasites cause a type of malaria that can relapse even after initial treatment with chloroquine alone, because a dormant stage of the parasite (hypnozoites) remains in the liver. Primaquine targets and eliminates these hypnozoites, preventing future relapses.  Chloroquine, on the other hand, works to kill the active parasites circulating in the bloodstream. This combination is crucial for a complete cure in these specific types of malaria.  While not a primary use, this combination may also be considered in some cases of severe malaria where chloroquine is already being administered to manage the immediate symptoms, and the addition of primaquine helps prevent relapse, though this approach would be determined by a physician.


**Dosage:**

Dosage is strictly determined by a healthcare professional and will vary considerably depending on the patient's age, weight, the severity of the malaria, and other health factors.  There is no standardized "over-the-counter" dosage for this combination.  The medication is typically available as tablets, and the dosages of both chloroquine and primaquine within the combination tablet will be prescribed by the doctor.  Children's dosages are significantly lower than adult dosages and must be carefully calculated based on weight and age.  It's crucial to follow the physician's prescription exactly.  Self-medicating with this drug is extremely dangerous.


**Side Effects:**

* **Common:** Nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, headache, dizziness, abdominal cramps, itching.
* **Less Common but Serious:**  Blood abnormalities (including hemolytic anemia, especially in individuals with G6PD deficiency), visual disturbances, heart rhythm problems, severe allergic reactions.
* **Note:** Any unexpected or worsening symptoms should be reported immediately to a doctor.


**How it Works:**

Chloroquine works by accumulating within the parasite, interfering with its ability to digest hemoglobin (a protein in red blood cells) and hindering its growth and replication. Primaquine, on the other hand, is a radical-generating drug that targets the hypnozoites in the liver, ultimately destroying them and preventing future malaria attacks.  Together, they offer a comprehensive approach to treating *P. vivax* and *P. ovale* malaria.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:**  This medication should not be used by individuals with known hypersensitivity to either chloroquine or primaquine.  It's particularly crucial to check for Glucose-6-Phosphate Dehydrogenase (G6PD) deficiency, a genetic condition that can lead to severe blood complications when primaquine is used.
* **Drug Interactions:**  Chloroquine and primaquine can interact with various medications, including certain heart drugs, blood thinners, and other antimalarials.  Full disclosure of all medications is vital when taking this combination.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  Use during pregnancy and breastfeeding should only be considered under strict medical supervision, weighing the potential risks against the benefits.
* **Elderly and Children:**  Dosage adjustments are crucial for these populations due to potential increased sensitivity to side effects.
* **Alcohol:** Avoid alcohol consumption while taking this medication.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: Can I stop taking this medication once I feel better?** A: No, complete the prescribed course even if symptoms improve to ensure a complete cure and prevent relapse.
* **Q: How should I store this medication?** A: Store in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight and moisture, as directed on the packaging.
* **Q: Is this medication safe for long-term use?** A:  No, it's not intended for long-term use.  The course of treatment is usually short-term.
* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Consult your doctor immediately; do not double up on the missed dose.
* **Q:  Is it okay to use this drug to prevent malaria?** A: No,  this specific combination is not generally recommended for malaria prophylaxis (prevention). Other medications are better suited for this purpose.
