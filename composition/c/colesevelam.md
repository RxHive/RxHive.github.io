# Colesevelam

**Generic Name: Colesevelam**

**Usage:**

Colesevelam is a medication primarily used to lower high cholesterol levels, specifically low-density lipoprotein (LDL) cholesterol, often referred to as "bad" cholesterol.  It's primarily prescribed as an adjunct to diet and exercise in individuals with hyperlipidemia (high cholesterol) who haven't achieved their cholesterol goals through lifestyle changes alone.  It's particularly helpful for people with type 2 diabetes who need to manage both their blood sugar and cholesterol.  While not its primary use, colesevelam may also play a secondary role in reducing triglyceride levels.  The effectiveness of colesevelam depends on consistent adherence to the prescribed dosage and a healthy lifestyle.

**Dosage:**

The dosage of colesevelam is determined by a healthcare professional based on individual needs and response to treatment.  Typically, it's taken orally with meals.  There's no standard pediatric dosage as its use is mainly focused on adults. The usual adult starting dose is often in the range of 3.75 grams per day, taken with meals. The dose can be adjusted upward (generally not exceeding 6.25 grams per day) based on cholesterol levels. It's crucial to follow your physician's instructions exactly, as exceeding the recommended dose can increase the risk of side effects.  Colesevelam is typically available as granules or tablets for oral administration; liquid formulations are less common.  

**Side Effects:**

Common side effects can include:

* Constipation
* Abdominal bloating or discomfort
* Gas
* Diarrhea
* Nausea

Less common, but more serious side effects might include allergic reactions (rash, itching, swelling, difficulty breathing).  These require immediate medical attention.   It's important to report any side effect, particularly persistent or worsening symptoms, to your doctor.

**How it Works:**

Colesevelam works by binding to bile acids in the digestive tract. Bile acids are essential for the absorption of fats and cholesterol. By binding to these bile acids, colesevelam prevents their reabsorption into the bloodstream. The liver then uses cholesterol to produce more bile acids, effectively lowering cholesterol levels in the body. This mechanism of action makes it a useful tool in managing hyperlipidemia, particularly when combined with dietary modifications and exercise.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** Colesevelam is not suitable for everyone. Individuals with known allergies to colesevelam or its components should avoid it.  Its use should be carefully considered in people with certain bowel conditions.
* **Drug Interactions:** Colesevelam can interact with some medications, affecting their absorption.  It's crucial to inform your healthcare provider about all other medications, supplements, and herbal remedies you are taking.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:** The safety of colesevelam during pregnancy and breastfeeding isn't fully established.  Consult your doctor before using it if you're pregnant, breastfeeding, or planning to become pregnant.
* **Older Adults and Children:**  The use of colesevelam is primarily focused on adults.  Careful consideration is needed for older adults and children, and dosage adjustments might be necessary based on individual health conditions.

**FAQs:**

* **Q: Can I stop taking colesevelam if my cholesterol levels improve?** A: No, don't stop taking colesevelam without consulting your doctor.  They will monitor your progress and decide when and if it's safe to adjust or discontinue the medication.
* **Q: What if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember unless it's close to the time for your next dose.  Don't double the dose to make up for a missed one.
* **Q: How should I store colesevelam?** A: Store it as directed on the label, typically in a cool, dry place away from moisture and direct sunlight.
* **Q:  Are there any dietary restrictions while taking colesevelam?** A:  Maintaining a healthy diet low in saturated and trans fats is crucial for optimal effectiveness.  Your doctor or a registered dietitian can provide guidance on a suitable diet plan.
