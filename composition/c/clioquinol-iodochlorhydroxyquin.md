**Usage:**

Clioquinol iodochlorhydroxyquin is an antimicrobial agent primarily used to treat various skin and intestinal infections caused by bacteria, fungi, and protozoa. It's also been used historically in treating amoebiasis (a parasitic infection of the intestines) and some bacterial skin infections, although newer, more targeted medications are often preferred for these conditions now. The use of clioquinol has diminished significantly in recent years due to concerns about potential neurological side effects.  It's crucial to consult a doctor before using this medication.

These serious side effects are rare but necessitate immediate medical attention.


**How it Works:**

Clioquinol iodochlorhydroxyquin works by interfering with the growth and reproduction of microorganisms.  It achieves this through several mechanisms, including disruption of microbial cell membranes and interference with essential enzyme functions. Its actions against bacteria, fungi, and some protozoa make it effective against a range of infections.
