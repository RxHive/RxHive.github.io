# Capmatinib

**Generic Name: Capmatinib**

**Usage:**

Capmatinib is a targeted therapy primarily used to treat certain types of non-small cell lung cancer (NSCLC).  Specifically, it's approved for patients with metastatic NSCLC harboring a specific type of genetic alteration called exon 20 insertion mutations in the *MET* gene.  These mutations drive the growth of cancer cells.  While its primary use is in metastatic NSCLC, research continues to explore its potential in other *MET* exon 14 skipping mutations-positive cancers.  It's important to note that capmatinib is not a treatment for all types of lung cancer or other cancers; its effectiveness is highly dependent on the presence of these specific genetic mutations.  A doctor will determine if capmatinib is appropriate based on a patient's individual cancer profile and other medical factors.


**Dosage:**

Capmatinib is typically administered orally as tablets.  Dosage is determined by a healthcare professional and is usually individualized based on factors such as the patient's overall health, the severity of their cancer, and potential interactions with other medications.  There isn't a universally standardized dosage across all patients.  A common starting dose for adults might be 400mg once daily, but this can be adjusted depending on the individual's response and tolerance to the medication.  There is no approved pediatric dosage at this time.  It's crucial to follow a healthcare provider's instructions precisely regarding dosage and administration.  Never adjust the dosage without consulting with your doctor.


**Side Effects:**

Common side effects of capmatinib can include:

*   Diarrhea
*   Nausea
*   Vomiting
*   Fatigue
*   Constipation
*   Decreased appetite
*   Rash
*   Increased liver enzymes (detected through blood tests)

Less common but serious side effects may include:

*   Interstitial lung disease (ILD) – inflammation and scarring of the lungs. This is a serious side effect and requires immediate medical attention.
*   Liver damage (hepatotoxicity)
*   Severe allergic reactions


If you experience any side effects, especially those listed above, contact your healthcare provider immediately.


**How it Works:**

Capmatinib is a tyrosine kinase inhibitor (TKI).  Tyrosine kinases are enzymes that play a critical role in cell growth and division.  In cancers with *MET* exon 20 insertions, these enzymes are abnormally active, promoting uncontrolled cancer cell growth.  Capmatinib works by selectively targeting and blocking the activity of these mutated *MET* enzymes. By inhibiting the *MET* kinase, capmatinib helps slow or stop the growth of cancer cells.

**Precautions:**

*   **Contraindications:** Capmatinib should not be used in patients with known hypersensitivity (allergy) to capmatinib or any of its components.
*   **Drug Interactions:**  Capmatinib may interact with other medications.  It's vital to inform your doctor about all medications, including over-the-counter drugs, herbal supplements, and vitamins, you are taking.
*   **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  Capmatinib is not recommended for use during pregnancy or breastfeeding due to potential harm to the fetus or infant.  Discuss this with your doctor before and during pregnancy.
*   **Older Adults and Children:** While dosage adjustments may be necessary in older adults based on other health conditions, there is no approved pediatric dosage.
