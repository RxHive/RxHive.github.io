# Clotrimazole + Selenium

**Generic Name: Clotrimazole + Selenium**

**Usage:**

Clotrimazole and selenium sulfide are antifungal and anti-dandruff agents, respectively.  The combination of these two medications is primarily used to treat seborrheic dermatitis, a common skin condition characterized by red, itchy, and scaly patches, often on the scalp (dandruff), face, and other areas rich in oil glands.  Clotrimazole tackles the fungal component often associated with seborrheic dermatitis, while selenium sulfide combats the excessive scaling and inflammation. While primarily used for seborrheic dermatitis, some formulations might be used to treat other fungal skin infections, though this is less common for this specific combination.  Always consult a doctor to determine if this combination is appropriate for your specific skin condition.

**Dosage:**

Dosage varies greatly depending on the specific formulation (cream, lotion, shampoo) and the condition being treated.  There are no universally standardized dosages for this combination.  Instructions for use will be clearly printed on the product packaging and should always be followed.  A healthcare professional will determine the appropriate application frequency and duration based on individual needs.  Children's dosage is usually adjusted based on age and body surface area – always follow the advice of a pediatrician or dermatologist.  **This information should not be taken as a prescription. Never self-medicate; seek professional medical advice.**

**Side Effects:**

* **Common:** Mild skin irritation (redness, burning, itching), dryness.
* **Less Common but Serious:** Allergic reactions (rash, hives, swelling, difficulty breathing).  While rare, severe allergic reactions necessitate immediate medical attention.


**How it Works:**

Clotrimazole is an antifungal agent that works by interfering with the fungal cell membrane's structure, leading to the death of the fungal cells. Selenium sulfide is an antifungal and anti-inflammatory agent.  Its exact mechanism is not completely understood, but it is thought to inhibit fungal growth and reduce inflammation, thus lessening the symptoms of seborrheic dermatitis. The combination provides a dual approach to managing the condition.

**Precautions:**

* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:** Consult a doctor before use during pregnancy or breastfeeding.
* **Children:** Use as directed by a pediatrician.
* **Allergic Reactions:** Discontinue use and seek medical advice immediately if an allergic reaction occurs.
* **Other Medications:** Inform your doctor about all other medications you are taking to avoid potential interactions.
* **Open Wounds/Broken Skin:** Avoid applying to open wounds or severely broken skin.
* **Eye Contact:** Avoid contact with eyes. Rinse thoroughly with water if contact occurs.
