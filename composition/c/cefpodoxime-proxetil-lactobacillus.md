# Cefpodoxime Proxetil + Lactobacillus

**Generic Name: Cefpodoxime Proxetil + Lactobacillus**

**Usage:**

Cefpodoxime proxetil + Lactobacillus is a combination medication used to treat bacterial infections, particularly those affecting the respiratory tract (like bronchitis, pneumonia, sinusitis), ears (otitis media), skin, and urinary tract.  Cefpodoxime proxetil is a cephalosporin antibiotic effective against a broad range of bacteria.  The addition of Lactobacillus, a beneficial bacterium, aims to mitigate the disruption of the gut's natural flora often caused by antibiotics. This helps prevent antibiotic-associated diarrhea, a common side effect of antibiotic treatment.  While the primary use is treating bacterial infections, the combination's benefit lies in reducing the risk of gastrointestinal disturbances associated with antibiotic use.


**Dosage:**

Dosage varies significantly depending on the patient's age, weight, the specific infection being treated, and the severity of the illness.  A physician will determine the appropriate dose.  The medication is typically available in tablet and syrup forms.  

* **Adults:**  Dosage ranges can vary widely, often starting with a specific amount once or twice a day, adjusted as per the doctor's assessment.
* **Children:**  Dosage is calculated based on weight and age and is usually lower than adult doses. Syrup formulations are common for children.

**Crucially:**  Never adjust the dosage or discontinue treatment without consulting your doctor.  Follow the prescribed instructions meticulously.


**Side Effects:**

* **Common Side Effects:** Diarrhea (though often reduced by the Lactobacillus), nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain, headache, dizziness.
* **Less Common but Serious Side Effects:** Allergic reactions (rash, itching, swelling, difficulty breathing), severe diarrhea (pseudomembranous colitis), changes in liver function tests, seizures (rare).

**Note:** If you experience any of these side effects, especially serious ones, contact a healthcare professional immediately.


**How it Works:**

Cefpodoxime proxetil interferes with the bacterial cell wall's synthesis, ultimately leading to bacterial cell death.  This kills the harmful bacteria causing the infection. Lactobacillus, a probiotic, works by replenishing the beneficial bacteria in the gut. This helps restore the balance of gut flora, potentially preventing or reducing the severity of diarrhea, a frequent side effect of antibiotics.

**Precautions:**

* **Allergies:**  Do not use this medication if you have a known allergy to cephalosporin antibiotics or any of its components.  Inform your doctor of any past allergic reactions to antibiotics.
* **Interactions:**  This medication may interact with other drugs.  It's crucial to inform your doctor of all other medications, supplements, or herbal remedies you are taking.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  Use during pregnancy and breastfeeding should be discussed with a doctor to weigh the risks and benefits.
* **Kidney and Liver Disease:**  Individuals with impaired kidney or liver function may require dosage adjustments.
* **Children and Older Adults:** Dosage should be carefully determined and monitored by a physician.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: Can I stop taking this medication when I feel better?**  A: No, complete the entire course of treatment prescribed by your doctor, even if symptoms improve.  Stopping early can lead to recurring infections and antibiotic resistance.
* **Q: How should I store this medication?**  A: Store the medication as directed on the label, usually in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight and moisture.
* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?**  A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it's almost time for your next dose.  Do not double the dose.
* **Q: Is this medication safe for children?**  A:  Yes, but only under the strict supervision and dosage recommendations of a pediatrician.
