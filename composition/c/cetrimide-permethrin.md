# Cetrimide + Permethrin

**Generic Name:** Cetrimide + Permethrin

**Usage:**

Cetrimide + Permethrin is primarily used topically to treat head lice infestations (pediculosis capitis).  Cetrimide is a quaternary ammonium compound with antiseptic properties, helping to kill lice and their eggs (nits). Permethrin, a synthetic pyrethroid, is an insecticide that also effectively paralyzes and kills lice.  The combination offers a broader spectrum of action against lice and often improves efficacy compared to using either ingredient alone. While its primary use is for head lice, this combination might sometimes be considered (under strict medical supervision) for other ectoparasite infestations, though this is less common and requires careful evaluation by a healthcare professional.


**Dosage:**

Dosage instructions vary greatly depending on the specific formulation (cream, lotion, shampoo) and the manufacturer.  **It's crucial to strictly follow the instructions provided on the product label or by your healthcare provider.**  Generally, for head lice treatment, the medication is applied to dry hair, left on for a specified duration (usually indicated on the packaging), and then thoroughly rinsed out.  Repeat applications might be necessary after a certain interval, again as per the product instructions.  There are usually separate formulations for adults and children, and different dosages for differing hair lengths.  **Never attempt to adjust dosages without consulting a doctor or pharmacist.**


**Side Effects:**

Common side effects can include:

* Mild skin irritation (itching, redness) at the application site.
* Burning sensation on application.
* Allergic reactions (rash, hives).


Less common, but potentially serious side effects:

* Severe allergic reactions (anaphylaxis):  Seek immediate medical attention if you experience symptoms like difficulty breathing, swelling of the face or throat, or severe rash.
* Neurotoxicity (rare, primarily with higher doses or prolonged exposure):  Symptoms might include dizziness, tremors, or seizures.


If any adverse effects occur, consult a healthcare provider immediately.


**How it Works:**

Cetrimide disrupts the cell membranes of lice, leading to their death. Permethrin interferes with the nerve function of lice, causing paralysis and subsequent death.  By combining these two active ingredients, the medication offers a dual mechanism of action, enhancing its effectiveness against lice infestation.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:**  Avoid use if you have known allergies to either cetrimide, permethrin, or any other ingredient in the formulation.
* **Drug Interactions:**  Although rare with topical applications, inform your doctor or pharmacist about all other medications you are taking.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:** Consult your doctor before using this medication during pregnancy or breastfeeding.  The topical application generally poses less risk than systemic medication, but it's essential to seek professional advice.
* **Children:** Use only formulations specifically designed for children and follow the recommended dosage strictly.
* **Elderly:**  Monitor elderly patients for any adverse reactions more closely.
* **Eye Contact:** Avoid contact with eyes; rinse thoroughly with water if accidental contact occurs.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long does it take to work?** A:  The effect varies, but lice are usually killed within a few hours of application.  However, repeat application as directed is crucial to kill newly hatched lice.

* **Q:  Can I reuse the leftover medication?** A: No, discard any leftover medication after use.

* **Q: How should I store it?** A: Store at room temperature, away from direct sunlight and heat. Keep out of reach of children.

* **Q: What if I miss a dose?** A:  Follow the prescribed treatment schedule. If in doubt, consult your doctor or pharmacist.

* **Q: Is it safe for pets?** A: Keep this medication away from pets. It is not intended for use on animals.
