# Cefadroxil

**Generic Name: Cefadroxil**

**Usage:**

Cefadroxil is an antibiotic belonging to the cephalosporin family.  Its primary use is to treat bacterial infections.  This includes infections of the middle ear (otitis media), throat (pharyngitis), tonsils (tonsillitis), skin, and urinary tract.  It's effective against a range of gram-positive and some gram-negative bacteria.  While primarily used for these common infections,  it may sometimes be prescribed for other bacterial infections based on a doctor's assessment of the specific bacteria causing the illness and its sensitivity to cefadroxil.  The choice of antibiotic always depends on the individual's specific situation and test results.


**Dosage:**

Dosage varies depending on the type of infection, the patient's age and weight, and the specific formulation of cefadroxil. A doctor will determine the appropriate dosage.  Generally:

* **Adults:**  The typical dose ranges from 500mg to 1 gram, taken once or twice daily.
* **Children:** Dosage is calculated based on weight and is usually lower than the adult dose.  Cefadroxil is often available as a suspension (liquid) for children, making it easier to administer.
* **Different Forms:** Cefadroxil is available in oral forms like tablets and capsules, as well as a suspension (liquid). It is *not* typically given intravenously.

**Always follow your doctor's instructions precisely regarding dosage and frequency.**


**Side Effects:**

Common side effects can include:

* Diarrhea
* Nausea
* Vomiting
* Stomach cramps
* Rash
* Vaginal yeast infection (in women)
* Headache


Less common but serious side effects can include:

* Severe allergic reactions (anaphylaxis), characterized by difficulty breathing, swelling of the face, lips, or tongue.
* Clostridium difficile-associated diarrhea (a severe type of diarrhea caused by bacteria)
* Seizures (in patients with pre-existing kidney problems or those receiving high doses)


**If you experience any unusual or concerning side effects, seek immediate medical attention.**


**How it Works:**

Cefadroxil works by interfering with the ability of bacteria to build their cell walls.  This weakens the bacterial structure, eventually leading to their death.  By targeting the bacterial cell wall, cefadroxil is effective in stopping the bacteria's growth and proliferation, allowing the body's immune system to clear the infection.

**Precautions:**

* **Allergy:**  Do not take cefadroxil if you are allergic to penicillin or other cephalosporin antibiotics.
* **Kidney Disease:**  Cefadroxil is primarily excreted through the kidneys.  Individuals with kidney problems may require a dosage adjustment or alternative treatment.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  Consult your doctor before taking cefadroxil if you are pregnant, breastfeeding, or planning to become pregnant.
* **Drug Interactions:**  Cefadroxil can interact with certain medications, including some anticoagulants (blood thinners). Inform your doctor of all medications you are currently taking.
* **Alcohol:** While there isn't a direct, serious interaction, excessive alcohol consumption is generally not advisable while taking antibiotics.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: Can I stop taking cefadroxil once I feel better?** A: No, it's crucial to complete the entire course of antibiotics prescribed by your doctor, even if you feel better.  Stopping early can lead to incomplete treatment and potential recurrence of the infection.

* **Q: How should I store cefadroxil?** A: Store cefadroxil as directed on the label, usually at room temperature and away from moisture and direct sunlight.

* **Q: Is cefadroxil safe for children?** A: Yes, but only under the supervision and guidance of a pediatrician. The dosage will be carefully determined based on the child's weight and the severity of the infection.

* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it's almost time for the next dose.  Do not double the dose.

* **Q: Can cefadroxil treat viral infections?** A: No, cefadroxil is an antibiotic effective only against bacterial infections.  It does not treat viral infections like the common cold or influenza.
