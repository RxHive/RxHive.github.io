# Cefixime + Ornidazole + Lactobacillus

**Generic Name:** Cefixime + Ornidazole + Lactobacillus

**Usage:** This combination medication is primarily used to treat infections of the gastrointestinal tract that involve both bacterial and parasitic components.  It's particularly effective against infections caused by bacteria susceptible to cefixime (a cephalosporin antibiotic) and parasites susceptible to ornidazole (an antiprotozoal and antibacterial agent). The lactobacillus component helps restore the balance of beneficial bacteria in the gut, which can be disrupted by the antibiotics.  This is crucial because antibiotic treatment can sometimes lead to diarrhea and other digestive problems.  While the primary use is for gastrointestinal infections, the specific infections treated might vary depending on the prescribing physician's assessment.  This combination might be considered for conditions like bacterial dysentery, certain types of diarrhea, and other infections where both bacterial and parasitic organisms are suspected.


**Dosage:** Dosage will be determined by a healthcare professional and varies based on age, weight, and the severity of the infection.  There's no single universally applicable dosage.  It's crucial to follow the prescribed dosage exactly.  The medication is typically available in tablet and syrup forms.  Syrup is often preferred for children.  Specific dosage instructions will be provided with the prescription.  **Never self-medicate or alter the prescribed dosage.**


**Side Effects:**

* **Common:** Diarrhea, nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain, headache, dizziness.
* **Less Common but Serious:** Allergic reactions (rash, itching, swelling, difficulty breathing),  severe diarrhea (possibly indicative of *Clostridium difficile* infection), changes in blood counts, liver function abnormalities.
* **Note:**  This is not an exhaustive list.  If you experience any unusual or concerning side effects, seek immediate medical attention.


**How it Works:**

Cefixime works by inhibiting the synthesis of bacterial cell walls, ultimately leading to bacterial cell death.  Ornidazole disrupts the DNA of certain parasites and bacteria, preventing their replication and growth.  The lactobacillus component acts as a probiotic, helping to replenish the beneficial bacteria in the gut, which are often affected by the antibiotics.  By combining these three components, the medication addresses both the bacterial and parasitic aspects of certain infections while mitigating the potential disruption of the gut flora.


**Popular Brand Names (India):**  Brand names vary widely across regions and manufacturers.  It's best to consult a pharmacist or physician for specific brand availability in your area.  Generic formulations are also readily available.


**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** This medication should not be used by individuals with known allergies to cephalosporin antibiotics, nitroimidazoles (like ornidazole), or any of the inactive ingredients.
* **Drug Interactions:**  Inform your doctor about all medications, supplements, and herbal remedies you are currently taking, as interactions are possible.  This is especially important with anticoagulants (blood thinners).
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  The use of this medication during pregnancy or breastfeeding should be carefully considered and only prescribed by a doctor if the benefits outweigh the potential risks.
* **Children and Older Adults:** Dosage adjustments may be necessary for children and older adults based on individual needs and health status.
* **Liver and Kidney Disease:**  Patients with liver or kidney disease may require dosage adjustments or close monitoring.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long should I take this medication?** A:  The duration of treatment depends on the severity of the infection and will be determined by your doctor.  Never stop taking the medication prematurely even if you feel better.
* **Q: Can I drink alcohol while taking this medication?** A:  No, alcohol consumption should be avoided while taking this medication, especially ornidazole, as it can cause harmful interactions.
* **Q: How should I store this medication?** A: Store in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight and out of reach of children.
* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it's almost time for the next dose.  Do not double the dose.
* **Q: Is this medication safe for everyone?** A:  Not necessarily.  Certain pre-existing conditions and allergies may make it unsuitable. Consult with a healthcare professional to assess suitability.
