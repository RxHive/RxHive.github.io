# Cefalexin + Probenecid

**Generic Name:** Cefalexin + Probenecid

**Usage:**

Cefalexin and probenecid are combined to treat bacterial infections, specifically those caused by susceptible bacteria.  The combination leverages the strengths of both drugs. Cefalexin, a cephalosporin antibiotic, directly fights the bacteria. Probenecid works by blocking the kidneys' reabsorption of cefalexin, thereby increasing the amount of cefalexin available to combat the infection. This is particularly helpful when a higher concentration of the antibiotic is needed to overcome the infection effectively.  The primary use is for treating bacterial infections, especially urinary tract infections (UTIs) where high cefalexin levels are beneficial. While this combination isn't routinely used for all bacterial infections, its primary strength lies in achieving higher and sustained cefalexin blood levels for specific infections.


**Dosage:**

Dosage is determined by a healthcare professional based on the individual's age, weight, the severity of the infection, and overall health. There isn't a standard universally recommended dose.  The medication typically comes in tablet form for oral administration.  Specific instructions will be provided by the prescribing doctor or pharmacist.  **It is crucial to never self-prescribe or adjust the dosage without consulting a healthcare professional.**  This combination isn't typically used in children without explicit physician guidance.


**Side Effects:**

* **Common:** Nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, stomach upset, abdominal pain.  These are usually mild and temporary.
* **Less Common but Serious:** Allergic reactions (rash, itching, swelling, difficulty breathing), changes in urine color, seizures (rare),  severe diarrhea (potentially indicating *Clostridium difficile* infection).  
* **Other Possible Side Effects:**  Headache, dizziness, fatigue.

**Important Note:**  If you experience any side effects, especially serious ones, contact your healthcare provider immediately.


**How it Works:**

Cefalexin interferes with bacterial cell wall synthesis, ultimately leading to bacterial death.  Probenecid works by inhibiting the tubular secretion of cefalexin in the kidneys. This means that less cefalexin is eliminated from the body, leading to higher and more prolonged levels of the antibiotic in the bloodstream. The sustained higher concentration of cefalexin makes it more effective against the bacteria causing the infection.

Brand names vary widely by region and manufacturer.  A healthcare professional or pharmacist can provide information on locally available brands containing this combination.  It's important to note that not all brands may contain the exact same dosage of both drugs.


**Precautions:**

* **Allergy:** Individuals allergic to penicillin or other cephalosporin antibiotics should avoid this medication.
* **Kidney Disease:** Patients with kidney problems may require dose adjustments.  This combination should be used with caution in those with impaired renal function.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:** Use during pregnancy and breastfeeding should only occur under the strict supervision of a doctor.
* **Drug Interactions:**  This combination can interact with other medications, including anticoagulants (blood thinners) and certain diuretics.  Inform your doctor about all medications you are taking.
* **Alcohol:**  Combining this medication with alcohol is not recommended.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: Can I take this medication with other medicines?** A:  Always inform your doctor or pharmacist about all medications, vitamins, and supplements you are taking to avoid potential interactions.
* **Q: How should I store this medication?** A: Store it as directed on the label, typically in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight and moisture.  Keep out of reach of children.
* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A:  Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it's almost time for the next dose.  Do not double the dose.
* **Q: Is this medication safe for everyone?** A:  No, this medication is not suitable for everyone.  Consult your doctor to determine if it's appropriate for your specific health condition.
* **Q:  What if I experience an allergic reaction?** A: Seek immediate medical attention if you experience any signs of an allergic reaction such as rash, itching, swelling, or difficulty breathing.
