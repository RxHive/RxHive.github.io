# Clemastine

**Generic Name: Clemastine**

**Usage:** Clemastine is an antihistamine primarily used to relieve the symptoms of allergies.  Its main use is in treating allergic rhinitis (hay fever), which manifests as sneezing, runny nose, itchy eyes, and nasal congestion. It's also effective against urticaria (hives), characterized by itchy welts on the skin.  While not its primary application, clemastine can sometimes be used to help control the itching associated with other skin conditions.  It's important to note that clemastine only treats the *symptoms* of allergies and doesn't address the underlying cause.


**Dosage:**  Dosage varies depending on the formulation (tablet, syrup), the patient's age and weight, and the severity of symptoms.  A healthcare professional will determine the appropriate dose.  Generally, for adults, a common dose is 1-2mg twice daily.  Children's dosages are significantly lower and must be carefully calculated based on weight and age, always following the instructions provided by a doctor or pharmacist.  Never exceed the recommended dosage.  Different preparations (tablets, syrups) have varying strengths, so always refer to the product labeling.

**Side Effects:**

* **Common:** Drowsiness, dry mouth, blurred vision, constipation.
* **Less Common but Serious:**  Fast heartbeat (tachycardia), difficulty urinating, seizures (rare but potentially serious).
* **Allergic Reactions:** Although rare, allergic reactions such as rash, hives, swelling of the face, lips, or tongue (angioedema), difficulty breathing can occur.  **Seek immediate medical attention if any allergic reaction is suspected.**

**How it Works:** Clemastine is a first-generation antihistamine. It works by blocking the action of histamine, a substance released by the body during an allergic reaction. Histamine causes the symptoms of allergies like sneezing, itching, and inflammation. By blocking histamine's effects, clemastine helps alleviate these symptoms.


**Popular Brand Names (India):**  The availability of specific brand names can vary by region and time. It's best to check with a local pharmacist for currently available brands containing clemastine.  Note that brand names are not standardized across all countries.


**Precautions:**

* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  Consult a doctor before using clemastine during pregnancy or breastfeeding.
* **Children:**  Children should only use clemastine under strict medical supervision due to potential side effects.  Dosage must be carefully calculated based on weight and age.
* **Elderly:**  Older adults are more susceptible to side effects, particularly drowsiness and confusion.  Lower doses may be necessary.
* **Alcohol and Other Medications:**  Avoid alcohol and other central nervous system depressants (such as sedatives) while taking clemastine, as the combination can increase drowsiness. Inform your doctor or pharmacist about all other medications you are taking, including over-the-counter drugs, to avoid potential interactions.
* **Glaucoma and Urinary Problems:**  Clemastine should be used with caution in individuals with glaucoma or difficulty urinating, as it can worsen these conditions.

**FAQs:**

* **Q: Can I drive after taking clemastine?**  A: Clemastine can cause drowsiness. Avoid driving or operating machinery until you know how it affects you.
* **Q: How should I store clemastine?** A: Store clemastine as directed on the product label, typically in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight and heat.  Keep out of reach of children.
* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it's almost time for the next dose. Do not take a double dose to make up for a missed one.
* **Q: Can I take clemastine long-term?** A:  Long-term use should only be undertaken under a doctor's supervision.
