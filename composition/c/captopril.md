# Captopril

**Generic Name: Captopril**

**Usage:**

Captopril is an ACE inhibitor (angiotensin-converting enzyme inhibitor).  Its primary use is in managing hypertension (high blood pressure). By inhibiting the ACE enzyme, it prevents the formation of angiotensin II, a powerful vasoconstrictor (a substance that narrows blood vessels).  Lowering angiotensin II levels leads to vasodilation (widening of blood vessels), thus reducing blood pressure.

Beyond hypertension, captopril is also used to treat:

* **Congestive heart failure (CHF):**  It helps reduce the workload on the heart by lowering blood pressure and improving blood flow.
* **Diabetic nephropathy (kidney disease):** In patients with diabetes, captopril can slow the progression of kidney damage.
* **Post-myocardial infarction (heart attack):** It can help reduce the risk of death and heart failure after a heart attack.


**Dosage:**

Dosage of captopril varies greatly depending on the individual's condition, age, and response to treatment.  A doctor will determine the appropriate dosage. It's typically available as oral tablets.  There is no injectable or syrup form widely available.  A typical starting dose for adults with hypertension might be 25 mg twice daily, but this can be adjusted up to 150 mg daily, or even higher, divided into doses. Children’s dosages are determined strictly by a physician based on weight and medical condition.  **It is crucial to follow the prescribed dosage regimen exactly as directed by a healthcare professional.**  Never adjust the dosage without consulting your doctor.


**Side Effects:**

* **Common:** Dizziness, lightheadedness, headache, fatigue, dry cough, nausea, diarrhea, rash.
* **Less Common but Serious:** Angioedema (swelling of the face, lips, tongue, or throat – seek immediate medical attention if this occurs), changes in kidney function, low blood pressure (hypotension), hyperkalemia (high potassium levels in the blood).
* **Note:**  This is not an exhaustive list.  Any new or worsening symptoms should be reported to a doctor immediately.

**How it Works:**

Captopril works by inhibiting the angiotensin-converting enzyme (ACE).  ACE plays a crucial role in the renin-angiotensin-aldosterone system (RAAS), a hormonal system that regulates blood pressure and fluid balance.  By blocking ACE, captopril prevents the conversion of angiotensin I to angiotensin II.  This reduction in angiotensin II leads to vasodilation (widening of blood vessels), decreased blood volume, and ultimately lower blood pressure.  It also reduces the release of aldosterone, a hormone that causes the body to retain sodium and water.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** Captopril should not be used by individuals with a history of angioedema related to ACE inhibitors, or those with severe renal artery stenosis (narrowing of the renal artery).
* **Drug Interactions:** Captopril can interact with other medications, including potassium-sparing diuretics, potassium supplements, and NSAIDs (nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drugs).  Always inform your doctor about all medications you are taking.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:** Captopril is generally avoided during pregnancy and breastfeeding due to potential harm to the fetus or infant.
* **Elderly Patients:** Older adults may be more sensitive to the side effects of captopril, requiring careful monitoring and dosage adjustments.
* **Renal Impairment:**  Dosage adjustments are often necessary for individuals with kidney problems.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: Can I stop taking captopril suddenly?** A: No.  Stopping captopril abruptly can lead to a rebound effect, causing a sudden increase in blood pressure.  Always consult your doctor before stopping the medication.
* **Q: How should I store captopril?** A: Store captopril tablets in a cool, dry place, away from direct sunlight and moisture.
* **Q: Is captopril safe for long-term use?** A: For many, captopril is safe for long-term use under medical supervision.  Regular checkups and monitoring of kidney function are essential.
* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it is almost time for your next dose. Do not take a double dose.
* **Q: Can I drink alcohol while taking captopril?** A: Moderate alcohol consumption may be permissible, but it's best to discuss this with your doctor. Excessive alcohol intake can interact negatively with captopril.
