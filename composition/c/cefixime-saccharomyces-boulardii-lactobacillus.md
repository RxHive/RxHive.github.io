# Cefixime + Saccharomyces boulardii + Lactobacillus

**Generic Name:** Cefixime + *Saccharomyces boulardii* + *Lactobacillus*

**Usage:** This combination medication is typically used to treat diarrhea, particularly infectious diarrhea, often accompanied by bacterial infections.  Cefixime, a third-generation cephalosporin antibiotic, targets the bacterial infection.  *Saccharomyces boulardii* and *Lactobacillus*, both probiotics, help restore the balance of gut flora disrupted by the infection and diarrhea.  This three-pronged approach aims to combat the infection directly while simultaneously supporting the gut's natural healing process. While the primary use is for diarrheal illnesses, this combination might be considered in situations where a bacterial infection is suspected and the physician wants to simultaneously support gut health.  It's crucial to understand that this is not a first-line treatment for all types of diarrhea. A doctor should always determine the appropriate course of action based on the specific diagnosis.

Always follow a doctor's or pharmacist's instructions regarding dosage and administration.


**Side Effects:**

* **Common:** Nausea, vomiting, diarrhea (paradoxically, though usually milder than the original infection), abdominal pain, bloating, gas, headache, rash.
* **Less Common but Serious:** Allergic reactions (ranging from mild skin rashes to severe anaphylaxis),  *Clostridium difficile* infection (a serious bowel infection), changes in bowel habits (constipation or persistent diarrhea).  Cefixime can also rarely cause liver problems.
* **Note:** This is not an exhaustive list. If you experience any adverse effects, contact your healthcare provider immediately.


**How it Works:** Cefixime works by inhibiting the synthesis of bacterial cell walls, effectively killing bacteria that cause infection. *Saccharomyces boulardii* and *Lactobacillus* are probiotic yeasts and bacteria, respectively.  They help restore the beneficial bacteria in the gut that are often depleted during diarrheal illness.  By repopulating the gut with healthy microorganisms, these probiotics aid in digestion, nutrient absorption, and compete with harmful bacteria, helping to shorten the duration and severity of diarrhea.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** Cefixime should be avoided in individuals with known allergies to cephalosporin antibiotics.  Probiotics are generally safe, but individuals with compromised immune systems or those at risk of serious infections should exercise caution and consult with a doctor.
* **Drug Interactions:** Cefixime may interact with certain medications, particularly anticoagulants (blood thinners).  Always inform your doctor of all medications, supplements, and herbal remedies you are taking.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:** The safety of this combination during pregnancy and breastfeeding hasn't been fully established.  A doctor should carefully assess the risks and benefits before prescribing it to pregnant or breastfeeding women.
* **Children and Elderly:**  Dosage adjustments may be necessary for children and elderly individuals based on their age, weight, and overall health.
* **Alcohol:** Alcohol consumption might interact with Cefixime and should be discussed with your doctor.
