# Calcitriol + Calcium citrate + Vitamin K + Methylcobalamin + Zinc Oxide + Magnesium Oxide + L-Methyl Folate

**Generic Name:** Calcitriol + Calcium citrate + Vitamin K + Methylcobalamin + Zinc Oxide + Magnesium Oxide + L-Methyl Folate

**Usage:** This combination of nutrients is not a single drug with FDA or equivalent international approval for a specific condition.  Instead, it represents a multi-nutrient formula targeting several aspects of bone health, nervous system function, and overall well-being.  Each component plays a distinct role:

* **Calcitriol (Vitamin D3 active form):** Essential for calcium absorption, bone health, and immune function.  It's often used to treat hypocalcemia (low blood calcium) and conditions affecting bone mineral density, like osteoporosis in certain cases (though typically in conjunction with other therapies).

* **Calcium citrate:** Provides calcium, crucial for building and maintaining strong bones and teeth. Calcium citrate is better absorbed than some other calcium forms.

* **Vitamin K:** Plays a vital role in blood clotting and bone health.  Specific forms like Vitamin K2 (menaquinone) are particularly important for bone metabolism.  This combination likely contains Vitamin K1 (phylloquinone) or a blend.

* **Methylcobalamin (Vitamin B12):** Essential for nerve function, red blood cell formation, and DNA synthesis. Deficiencies can lead to neurological problems and anemia.

* **Zinc Oxide:**  An essential mineral with numerous functions, including immune support, wound healing, and protein synthesis.

* **Magnesium Oxide:** An essential mineral involved in hundreds of bodily processes, including muscle and nerve function, blood sugar control, and blood pressure regulation.

* **L-Methylfolate (Vitamin B9):** An active form of folic acid, crucial for cell growth and development, particularly important during pregnancy and for preventing neural tube defects.


The primary use of this combination is often as a nutritional supplement to address potential deficiencies in these nutrients. It might be prescribed or recommended by healthcare professionals for individuals with diagnosed deficiencies or those at increased risk (e.g., older adults, pregnant women, individuals with malabsorption syndromes).  It is *not* a treatment for specific diseases in itself, but rather a supportive measure to address nutritional shortfalls that *may* contribute to certain health problems.

**Dosage:** There's no standard dosage for this specific combination as formulations vary greatly. Dosages for individual components vary widely based on age, health status, and the specific product.  It's crucial to follow the instructions provided on the product label or as directed by a healthcare professional.  Self-medication is not recommended.  Adult and child dosages will be specified on the product packaging and should be followed strictly. The different components are usually combined in a tablet or capsule form.

**Side Effects:**

* **Common:**  Gastrointestinal upset (nausea, constipation, diarrhea), headache.
* **Less Common but Serious:** Hypercalcemia (high blood calcium levels - potentially leading to kidney stones or other issues), allergic reactions (rash, itching, swelling), neurological symptoms (if high doses of B12 are used).

*Note:* This is not an exhaustive list.  Seek immediate medical attention if you experience any severe or concerning side effects.

**How it Works:** Each nutrient works independently to fulfill its specific biological roles.  Calcitriol improves calcium absorption; calcium strengthens bones; Vitamin K aids in bone health and coagulation; Methylcobalamin supports nerve function; Zinc and Magnesium participate in various enzymatic processes; and L-Methylfolate is essential for cell growth and development. The combination aims to provide synergistic support for overall health and well-being.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:**  Individuals with hypercalcemia, kidney stones, or certain medical conditions should not use this supplement without consulting a doctor.
* **Drug Interactions:**  This combination can interact with some medications, such as certain heart medications, diuretics, and antibiotics. Always inform your doctor about all medications and supplements you are taking.
* **Pregnancy/Breastfeeding:**  Pregnant and breastfeeding women should consult their doctor before taking this or any supplement.
* **Children:**  Dosage for children should be determined by a pediatrician.
* **Elderly:**  Older adults may have altered absorption or increased sensitivity to some components.  Consult a doctor before use.
