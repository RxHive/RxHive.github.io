# Crizotinib

**Generic Name:** Crizotinib

**Usage:**

Crizotinib is a targeted cancer therapy primarily used to treat certain types of non-small cell lung cancer (NSCLC).  Specifically, it's effective against NSCLCs that have a specific genetic abnormality: a rearrangement or mutation in the ALK (anaplastic lymphoma kinase) gene, or a ROS1 (ROS proto-oncogene 1) gene alteration.  These genetic changes drive the uncontrolled growth of cancer cells. Crizotinib works by blocking the activity of the abnormal ALK or ROS1 proteins, thus hindering cancer cell proliferation.  While primarily used for lung cancer, it may also be considered in some cases of other cancers harboring ALK or ROS1 rearrangements, though this is less common.  The exact application and suitability are determined on a case-by-case basis by an oncologist based on individual patient factors and tumor characteristics.


**Dosage:**

Dosage of crizotinib is determined by a physician and varies depending on individual factors like the patient's overall health, the specific type and stage of cancer, and response to treatment.  There isn't a standard dosage applicable to all patients. Typically, crizotinib is administered orally as a tablet. The standard adult starting dose is usually in the range of 250mg twice daily, but this can be adjusted based on the patient's tolerance and efficacy.  Children's dosing requires careful consideration and adjustment based on weight and other factors; this is always determined by a specialist pediatric oncologist.  There are no injectable or syrup formulations of crizotinib.  It's crucial to follow the prescribed dosage and schedule precisely as instructed by the healthcare professional.


**Side Effects:**

Crizotinib, like other cancer therapies, can cause side effects.  Common side effects include:

* Nausea and vomiting
* Diarrhea
* Constipation
* Fatigue
* Loss of appetite
* Vision changes (blurred vision, visual disturbances)
* Dizziness
* Headache
* Cough
* Elevated liver enzymes (detected through blood tests)

Less common but serious side effects include:

* Severe liver damage
* Severe lung problems (interstitial lung disease)
* Severe heart rhythm problems
* Serious allergic reactions

It is imperative to report any unusual or concerning side effects to your doctor immediately.  Early detection and management of side effects are vital to ensure patient safety and optimal treatment outcomes.


**How it Works:**

Crizotinib is a tyrosine kinase inhibitor (TKI).  Tyrosine kinases are enzymes that play a critical role in cell signaling and growth.  In certain cancers like ALK-positive NSCLC, these enzymes are abnormally activated due to genetic alterations. Crizotinib specifically targets and blocks the activity of the abnormal ALK and ROS1 tyrosine kinases.  By inhibiting these enzymes, crizotinib interferes with the cancer cells' ability to grow, divide, and spread. This targeted approach aims to minimize harm to healthy cells while effectively suppressing cancer growth.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** Crizotinib should not be used by individuals with severe hypersensitivity to the drug or its components.
* **Drug Interactions:** Crizotinib can interact with other medications. It's vital to inform your doctor about all medications (prescription, over-the-counter, and herbal supplements) you are taking.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:** Crizotinib is not recommended during pregnancy unless the potential benefits outweigh the risks.  Breastfeeding should be avoided during crizotinib therapy.
* **Children and Older Adults:**  Dosage adjustments may be necessary for children and older adults due to differences in metabolism and potential increased susceptibility to side effects.  Careful monitoring is crucial in these groups.
* **Liver and Lung Function:**  Regular monitoring of liver and lung function is recommended during treatment due to the potential for liver and lung toxicity.

**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long do I need to take crizotinib?**  A: The duration of treatment depends on several factors, including your response to therapy, the type and stage of your cancer, and your overall health.  Your oncologist will determine the optimal duration.

* **Q: How should I store crizotinib?** A: Store crizotinib tablets in their original container at room temperature, away from moisture and direct sunlight.

* **Q: Can I stop taking crizotinib without consulting my doctor?** A: No, abruptly stopping crizotinib can have negative consequences.  Always consult your oncologist before making any changes to your treatment plan.

* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: If you miss a dose, take it as soon as you remember unless it's nearly time for the next dose. Don't double up on doses.

* **Q: Are there any specific dietary recommendations while taking crizotinib?** A: It's generally recommended to maintain a balanced diet and stay well-hydrated while on crizotinib.  Your healthcare provider can provide more specific dietary advice based on your individual needs and any side effects experienced.
