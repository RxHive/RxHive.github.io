# Chlorpromazine

**Generic Name: Chlorpromazine**

**Usage:**

Chlorpromazine is a medication primarily used as an antipsychotic.  Its main application lies in managing the symptoms of schizophrenia and other psychotic disorders. This includes helping to control hallucinations, delusions, and disorganized thinking.  Beyond its primary use in psychosis, chlorpromazine also finds secondary applications in managing severe anxiety, nausea and vomiting (particularly in cases of post-operative nausea), and acute agitation.  It's important to note that its use in these secondary conditions is often less prevalent than its use in treating psychosis and is generally reserved for specific situations where other treatments are ineffective or unsuitable.  The specific use and appropriateness of chlorpromazine will always be determined by a healthcare professional.


**Dosage:**

Dosage of chlorpromazine is highly individualized and depends on the patient's age, weight, medical condition, and response to treatment.  A doctor will carefully determine the appropriate dosage.  It's available in various forms including tablets, syrup (for children or those who have difficulty swallowing pills), and injectable solutions for immediate effect.  

* **Adults:**  Starting doses are typically low and gradually increased as needed, often ranging from 25mg to 100mg daily, divided into several doses. Higher doses may be necessary in some cases, but this is determined solely by a prescribing physician.
* **Children:** Dosage for children is significantly lower and strictly determined by a pediatrician based on weight and age.  Syrup formulations are often used for children. 
* **Injection:** Injectable chlorpromazine is typically used for short-term management of acute agitation or severe psychosis, often in a hospital setting, under direct medical supervision.

**Never adjust your dosage without consulting your doctor.**


**Side Effects:**

Chlorpromazine, like many medications, can cause side effects.  These vary in severity and frequency.

* **Common Side Effects:**  Drowsiness, dizziness, dry mouth, constipation, blurred vision, weight gain, low blood pressure (hypotension), photosensitivity (increased sensitivity to sunlight).
* **Less Common but Serious Side Effects:**  Neuroleptic malignant syndrome (NMS – a rare but potentially life-threatening condition characterized by high fever, muscle rigidity, altered mental status), extrapyramidal symptoms (EPS – movement disorders such as tremors, muscle stiffness, restlessness), tardive dyskinesia (TD – involuntary movements, particularly of the face and mouth, that can be irreversible), jaundice (yellowing of the skin and eyes), blood disorders.

**If you experience any adverse effects, especially serious ones, seek immediate medical attention.**


**How it Works:**

Chlorpromazine works primarily by blocking dopamine receptors in the brain. Dopamine is a neurotransmitter involved in regulating mood, movement, and thought processes. By blocking dopamine receptors, chlorpromazine reduces the overactivity of dopamine that's believed to contribute to the symptoms of psychosis, such as hallucinations and delusions.  The drug also affects other neurotransmitters, which contributes to its effects on anxiety and nausea.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** Chlorpromazine is contraindicated in individuals with known hypersensitivity to the drug or related phenothiazines.
* **Drug Interactions:** Chlorpromazine can interact with many other medications, including antidepressants, antihistamines, and certain pain relievers. It's crucial to inform your doctor about all medications, supplements, and herbal remedies you're taking.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  The use of chlorpromazine during pregnancy and breastfeeding should be carefully considered, as potential risks to the fetus or infant may outweigh the benefits.  A healthcare professional should be consulted.
* **Elderly and Children:**  Older adults and children may be more susceptible to side effects. Dosage adjustments are typically needed.
* **Driving and Operating Machinery:** Chlorpromazine can cause drowsiness and dizziness, affecting the ability to drive or operate machinery.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long does it take to work?** A:  The onset of effects varies, but improvements in symptoms are often seen within days or weeks of starting treatment.
* **Q: Can I stop taking it suddenly?** A: No, abruptly stopping chlorpromazine can lead to withdrawal symptoms.  Always taper off the medication under the guidance of your doctor.
* **Q: How should I store it?** A: Store chlorpromazine at room temperature, away from moisture and direct sunlight.
* **Q: Is it addictive?** A: Chlorpromazine is not considered physically addictive in the same way as opioids, but tolerance can develop, requiring dosage adjustments under medical supervision.
* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it's almost time for the next dose. Don't double up on doses.
