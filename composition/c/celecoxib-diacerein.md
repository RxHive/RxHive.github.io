# Celecoxib + Diacerein

**Generic Name: Celecoxib + Diacerein**

This combination medication is not currently available as a fixed-dose combination approved by major regulatory bodies like the FDA or equivalent international agencies.  While celecoxib and diacerein are used individually to treat certain conditions, their combined use is not standard practice due to potential overlapping side effects and lack of sufficient clinical evidence supporting their combined efficacy. **Usage:**

* **Celecoxib:** Primarily used to relieve pain and reduce inflammation associated with osteoarthritis and rheumatoid arthritis. It may also be used to treat acute pain, such as after dental surgery.  It's a COX-2 inhibitor, meaning it selectively targets a specific enzyme involved in inflammation.

* **Diacerein:** Primarily used to treat osteoarthritis. It is believed to work by slowing the breakdown of cartilage and potentially stimulating its repair. It's often used in cases where other treatments have not been fully effective.


**Dosage:**  Because Celecoxib + Diacerein is not an approved combination, dosage information below is for each drug *individually*.  **A doctor must determine the appropriate dosage and combination (if any) for an individual patient.**

* **Celecoxib:** Dosage varies based on condition and individual response. A doctor will prescribe the appropriate amount.  It's typically available as tablets.

* **Diacerein:** Dosage also varies, and is usually started at a lower dose and gradually increased.  It is typically available as tablets or capsules.  Dosage should always be determined by a physician.


**Side Effects:**

**Celecoxib:**

* Common:  Headache, nausea, dizziness, diarrhea, stomach pain, swelling in the ankles or feet.
* Less Common but Serious: Increased risk of heart attack, stroke, blood clots, heart failure, kidney problems, severe allergic reactions.

**Diacerein:**

* Common: Diarrhea, nausea, abdominal pain.
* Less Common but Serious: Liver problems (jaundice, dark urine), kidney problems, allergic reactions.


**How it Works:**

* **Celecoxib:**  Reduces pain and inflammation by selectively inhibiting COX-2, an enzyme that plays a major role in producing prostaglandins – chemicals that cause pain, swelling, and inflammation.

* **Diacerein:**  Its exact mechanism is not completely understood, but it's thought to influence the production and breakdown of cartilage within joints.  It may also have some anti-inflammatory effects.
