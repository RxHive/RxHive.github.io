# Cefixime

**Generic Name: Cefixime**

**Usage:**

Cefixime is an antibiotic belonging to the cephalosporin family.  Its primary use is to treat bacterial infections, particularly those affecting the respiratory tract (like bronchitis and pneumonia), ears (otitis media), urinary tract (cystitis and pyelonephritis), and skin.  It's also sometimes used to treat gonorrhea, a sexually transmitted infection, although newer guidelines often recommend other antibiotics as first-line treatment for this condition.  The specific bacterial infection being treated will determine the appropriateness of cefixime.  It's crucial to understand that cefixime is effective only against certain bacteria; it won't work against viral infections like the common cold or flu.  A doctor's diagnosis is essential to ensure cefixime is the right choice of treatment.

**Dosage:**

Dosage varies significantly depending on the infection being treated, the patient's age, weight, and kidney function.  A healthcare professional will determine the appropriate dose.  Generally, for adults, oral cefixime is typically prescribed in doses ranging from 200mg to 400mg once or twice daily.  Children's dosages are much lower and are always calculated based on weight and the specific infection. Cefixime is available in various forms including tablets, capsules, and oral suspensions (syrups).  Injectable forms exist but are generally reserved for severe infections or when oral administration isn't feasible.  Always follow the prescribed dosage and duration of treatment precisely as directed by a doctor or pharmacist.  Never stop taking cefixime prematurely, even if you feel better, as this can lead to treatment failure and the development of antibiotic-resistant bacteria.

**Side Effects:**

* **Common:** Diarrhea, nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain, headache, dizziness.
* **Less Common but Serious:**  Severe allergic reactions (anaphylaxis – characterized by difficulty breathing, swelling of the face, lips, or tongue),  pseudomembranous colitis (a severe intestinal infection), seizures (especially in individuals with pre-existing seizure disorders or kidney impairment), changes in liver function tests.
* **Note:** This is not an exhaustive list.  If you experience any unusual or concerning side effects, contact your doctor or other healthcare provider immediately.

**How it Works:**

Cefixime works by interfering with the ability of bacteria to build their cell walls.  Bacteria need intact cell walls to survive.  By inhibiting cell wall synthesis, cefixime weakens the bacteria, leading to their death and ultimately resolving the infection.  This mechanism is known as bactericidal action.

**Popular Brand Names (India):**

The specific brand names available will vary by region and pharmacy.  However, some commonly available brands in India include, but are not limited to, Cefix, Cefspan, and others.  Always check with your local pharmacist for availability.

**Precautions:**

* **Allergies:**  Cefixime should not be used by individuals with known allergies to cephalosporin antibiotics or penicillin (cross-reactivity can occur).
* **Kidney Disease:**  Dosage adjustments are usually necessary for individuals with impaired kidney function.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:** Cefixime should be used during pregnancy and breastfeeding only if the benefits clearly outweigh the potential risks.  A doctor should be consulted.
* **Interactions:** Cefixime may interact with certain medications, including anticoagulants (blood thinners) and certain antacids.  Inform your doctor or pharmacist of all medications you are currently taking.
* **Alcohol:**  Avoid alcohol consumption while taking cefixime, as it may increase the risk of side effects.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: Can I take cefixime with other medications?** A:  Always inform your doctor or pharmacist of all other medications you are taking, including over-the-counter drugs and supplements, to avoid potential interactions.
* **Q: How should I store cefixime?** A: Store cefixime as directed on the label, usually at room temperature, away from moisture and direct sunlight.
* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it's almost time for the next dose.  Do not double the dose.
* **Q: Is cefixime safe for children?** A:  Cefixime can be used in children, but the dosage must be carefully determined by a doctor based on the child's weight and the infection being treated.
* **Q: Can cefixime cause antibiotic-resistant bacteria?** A:  Improper or incomplete use of antibiotics can contribute to the development of antibiotic-resistant bacteria.  Always follow your doctor's instructions carefully and complete the full course of treatment.
