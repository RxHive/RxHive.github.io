# Cefadroxil + Probenecid

**Generic Name:** Cefadroxil + Probenecid

**Usage:**

This combination medication is primarily used to treat certain bacterial infections, particularly those affecting the urinary tract (urinary tract infections or UTIs).  Cefadroxil, a cephalosporin antibiotic, directly targets and kills bacteria causing the infection. Probenecid works by preventing the kidneys from eliminating cefadroxil too quickly. This allows for a higher and more sustained level of cefadroxil in the bloodstream, improving the effectiveness of the antibiotic treatment, especially against stubborn infections. While its primary use focuses on UTIs,  it might be considered in other bacterial infections under specific circumstances and with a physician's prescription.  This should never be used without explicit guidance from a healthcare provider.


**Dosage:**

Dosage of Cefadroxil + Probenecid is determined by a doctor based on the individual's age, weight, and the severity of the infection.  There's no standard "over-the-counter" dosage available as it requires a prescription.  The medication is typically available in tablet form.  Information regarding specific dosages for adults and children should be obtained directly from a doctor or pharmacist and strictly followed.  Never adjust the dosage without consulting a healthcare professional.


**Side Effects:**

* **Common:** Nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, stomach upset, headache, dizziness.
* **Less Common but Serious:** Allergic reactions (rash, itching, hives, swelling, difficulty breathing),  severe diarrhea (possibly indicating *Clostridium difficile* infection), changes in blood counts, seizures (especially in individuals with pre-existing conditions).

**Important Note:**  If you experience any of these side effects, particularly serious ones, seek immediate medical attention.


**How it Works:**

Cefadroxil, the antibiotic component, works by interfering with the bacterial cell wall synthesis, ultimately leading to bacterial cell death.  Probenecid, a uricosuric agent, inhibits the renal tubular secretion of cefadroxil.  This means it slows down the rate at which the kidneys remove cefadroxil from the bloodstream. By prolonging the presence of cefadroxil, its effectiveness against the infection is increased. This combination ensures a higher concentration of the antibiotic reaches the site of infection for a longer duration, thereby improving treatment outcomes.

**Precautions:**

* **Allergies:**  This medication should not be used by individuals with known allergies to cephalosporin antibiotics or probenecid.
* **Kidney Problems:**  Patients with kidney disease may require dosage adjustments; careful monitoring is necessary.
* **Liver Problems:**  Individuals with liver disease should use this medication cautiously under close medical supervision.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  Use during pregnancy and breastfeeding should only occur under strict medical guidance, carefully weighing the benefits against potential risks.
* **Drug Interactions:** This medication can interact with other drugs; always inform your doctor about all medications, supplements, and herbal remedies you are taking.
* **Alcohol:**  Alcohol consumption should be avoided while on this medication as it can potentially increase side effects.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long should I take this medication?**  A: The duration of treatment is determined by your doctor based on the type and severity of your infection and your response to therapy.  Never stop taking the medication early, even if you feel better.
* **Q: Can I take this medication with food?** A: Follow your doctor's or pharmacist's instructions regarding taking this medication with or without food.
* **Q: How should I store this medication?** A: Store at room temperature, away from moisture and direct sunlight. Keep out of reach of children.
* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it's almost time for your next dose. Do not double the dose.
* **Q: Can I take this medication if I have other health problems?** A: Always inform your doctor of all your health conditions before starting this medication.
