**Usage:**

* **Chlorpromazine:** Primarily used to treat schizophrenia and other psychotic disorders. It can also be used to manage nausea and vomiting, especially in cases of severe postoperative nausea. It has sedative properties and may be used for anxiety and agitation in some situations.

* **Trihexyphenidyl:** Primarily used to treat Parkinson's disease and other extrapyramidal symptoms (EPS) caused by antipsychotic medications like chlorpromazine and trifluoperazine. EPS are movement disorders that can include tremors, rigidity, and dystonia.

* **Trifluoperazine:** Primarily used to treat schizophrenia and other psychotic disorders.  It's a potent antipsychotic and is often reserved for cases that haven't responded well to other treatments.

**Side Effects:**

The side effects can vary depending on the drug and the dosage.  Common side effects *could* include (but are not limited to, as this is a hypothetical combination):


* **Chlorpromazine:** Drowsiness, dizziness, dry mouth, constipation, weight gain, low blood pressure, photosensitivity.  Serious, but less common side effects include neuroleptic malignant syndrome (NMS) and tardive dyskinesia (TD).


* **Trihexyphenidyl:** Dry mouth, blurred vision, constipation, urinary retention, drowsiness, dizziness, nervousness.


* **Trifluoperazine:** Similar side effects to chlorpromazine, including EPS, drowsiness, dizziness, dry mouth, and weight gain. NMS and TD are also potential serious side effects.



**How it Works:**

* **Chlorpromazine and Trifluoperazine:** These are antipsychotics that primarily work by blocking dopamine receptors in the brain. Dopamine is a neurotransmitter involved in regulating mood and movement.  By blocking dopamine, they help reduce psychotic symptoms.


* **Trihexyphenidyl:** This is an anticholinergic drug. It works by blocking the action of acetylcholine, another neurotransmitter.  This helps counteract the extrapyramidal side effects that can be caused by dopamine blockers.
