# Cyclandelate

**Generic Name: Cyclandelate**

**Usage:**

Cyclandelate is a medication primarily used to improve blood circulation in the extremities (arms and legs).  Its main application is in the treatment of intermittent claudication, a condition characterized by pain in the legs and buttocks caused by reduced blood flow during physical activity.  This pain typically subsides with rest.  While primarily used for peripheral vascular disease (PVD), it may sometimes be used off-label to address other circulatory issues, though its efficacy in these cases isn't as definitively established.  Always consult a physician to determine if cyclandelate is the appropriate treatment for your specific condition.

**Dosage:**

Dosage for cyclandelate varies depending on the individual's condition, age, and response to the medication.  It's crucial to strictly adhere to the dosage prescribed by a doctor.  Typical adult doses may range from 1200-1600 mg per day, usually divided into several doses throughout the day.  Children's dosage is determined by a physician based on weight and condition.  Cyclandelate is typically available in tablet form.  There are no injectable or syrup formulations widely available.  Medication should always be taken as directed.

**Side Effects:**

* **Common:**  Headache, flushing, heartburn, nausea, dizziness, weakness, and drowsiness. These are usually mild and may lessen over time.
* **Less Common but Serious:** Rapid heartbeat (tachycardia),  hypotension (low blood pressure), and allergic reactions (rash, itching, swelling).

**Important Note:** If you experience any adverse effects, particularly serious ones, discontinue use and contact your healthcare provider immediately.

**How it Works:**

Cyclandelate is a peripheral vasodilator. This means it works by relaxing the muscles in the walls of blood vessels, particularly those in the extremities. This widening of blood vessels improves blood flow, easing the pain and discomfort associated with reduced circulation.  The mechanism involves influencing calcium channels within the smooth muscle cells of the blood vessel walls.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** Cyclandelate is not suitable for individuals with hypersensitivity to the drug or those with certain severe heart conditions.  Consult your physician about pre-existing conditions.
* **Drug Interactions:** Cyclandelate can interact with other medications.  It’s essential to inform your doctor of all medications, including over-the-counter drugs and supplements, you are taking.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:** The safety of cyclandelate during pregnancy and breastfeeding has not been fully established.  Consult your doctor before using this medication if you are pregnant, breastfeeding, or planning to become pregnant.
* **Elderly Patients:** Older adults may be more sensitive to the side effects of cyclandelate. Careful monitoring and adjusted dosages are often necessary.
