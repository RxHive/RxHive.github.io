# Cabozantinib

**Generic Name: Cabozantinib**

**Usage:**

Cabozantinib is a targeted therapy primarily used to treat certain types of cancer.  Its main applications focus on advanced cancers that have spread or are difficult to remove surgically.  These include:

* **Advanced Renal Cell Carcinoma (RCC):** This is a type of kidney cancer. Cabozantinib is often used after other treatments have failed.
* **Advanced Hepatocellular Carcinoma (HCC):**  This is the most common type of liver cancer. Cabozantinib is used in specific situations, often after other therapies haven't been successful.
* **Medullary Thyroid Cancer (MTC):**  This is a rare type of thyroid cancer. Cabozantinib is an option for patients with advanced or metastatic MTC that has progressed after prior therapies.
* **Other Cancers:** Research is ongoing to explore its potential use in other types of cancer.


The specific use of cabozantinib will be determined by a physician based on the type and stage of cancer, the patient's overall health, and their response to previous treatments.


**Dosage:**

Dosage of cabozantinib is determined by a doctor and varies greatly depending on the specific cancer being treated, the patient's overall health, and other medications they might be taking.  It’s typically administered orally as a tablet.  There isn't a universally standardized dosage across all indications.  **Dosage instructions must always be followed precisely as prescribed.**  There is no typical dosage for children, as it's generally not used in pediatric populations.  Adjustments might be made based on factors like kidney or liver function.


**Side Effects:**

Common side effects can include:

* Diarrhea
* Fatigue
* Nausea
* Vomiting
* Hand-foot syndrome (pain, swelling, redness of the palms and soles)
* High blood pressure
* Loss of appetite
* Mouth sores


Less common but serious side effects may include:

* Bleeding
* Blood clots
* Heart problems
* Liver problems
* Severe infections


If you experience any side effects, especially those that are severe or persistent, it's crucial to contact your doctor immediately.


**How it Works:**

Cabozantinib is a tyrosine kinase inhibitor (TKI).  In simple terms, it works by blocking the activity of certain proteins, specifically receptor tyrosine kinases (RTKs), that play a significant role in the growth and spread of cancer cells. By inhibiting these proteins, cabozantinib interferes with the cancer cells' ability to grow, divide, and spread. This targeted approach helps to minimize damage to healthy cells, though side effects are still possible.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** Cabozantinib is not suitable for everyone.  People with certain medical conditions, such as severe liver or kidney problems, may not be able to take it.
* **Drug Interactions:** Cabozantinib can interact with other medications.  It is essential to inform your doctor about all medications, supplements, and herbal remedies you are taking.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:** Cabozantinib should not be used during pregnancy or breastfeeding unless the benefits significantly outweigh the potential risks.
* **Older Adults:** Older adults may be more sensitive to the side effects of cabozantinib, requiring careful monitoring and potential dosage adjustments.
