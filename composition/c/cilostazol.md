# Cilostazol

**Generic Name: Cilostazol**

**Usage:**

Cilostazol is primarily used to improve symptoms of intermittent claudication (IC), a condition characterized by leg pain and cramping during exercise due to reduced blood flow in the arteries of the legs.  This improvement manifests as an increased walking distance before pain begins.  While its primary use is in managing IC,  it sometimes plays a secondary role in improving overall blood flow in patients with peripheral artery disease (PAD), a broader term encompassing conditions affecting blood circulation in the limbs.  It's important to note that cilostazol does *not* cure PAD or IC; it manages symptoms and may improve quality of life for those affected.  The use of cilostazol should always be under the guidance of a physician, who will assess the individual's condition and suitability for treatment.

**Dosage:**

The typical adult dosage is one 100mg tablet taken twice daily, usually with meals.  Dosage adjustments based on age, weight, or severity of the condition are generally not recommended without direct consultation with a healthcare professional.  There are no established pediatric dosages as the drug is primarily prescribed for adults.  Cilostazol is only available in oral tablet form; there are no syrups or injections.  It's crucial to swallow the tablets whole with water, and never to crush, chew, or break them.

**Side Effects:**

* **Common:** Headache, diarrhea, nausea, dizziness, flushing, palpitations.
* **Less Common but Serious:**  Chest pain, shortness of breath, irregular heartbeat (arrhythmia), severe allergic reactions (rash, hives, swelling of the face, lips, tongue, or throat).  
* **Note:** This list isn't exhaustive.  If you experience any concerning side effects, especially those listed as less common but serious, discontinue use immediately and contact your doctor or other healthcare provider.

**How it Works:**

Cilostazol works by inhibiting an enzyme called phosphodiesterase III (PDE III). This inhibition leads to an increase in cyclic adenosine monophosphate (cAMP) levels within the body.  Increased cAMP levels relax blood vessel walls (vasodilation), improving blood flow, particularly in the legs.  Additionally, cilostazol inhibits platelet aggregation (clumping together of blood platelets), reducing the risk of blood clot formation.  This dual mechanism of action – vasodilation and antiplatelet effects – contributes to the relief of intermittent claudication symptoms.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** Cilostazol is contraindicated in patients with severe heart failure, uncontrolled hypertension, recent history of stroke or transient ischemic attack (TIA), and those with a known hypersensitivity (allergy) to the medication.
* **Drug Interactions:** Cilostazol can interact with other medications, particularly those affecting bleeding or blood clotting.  It's vital to inform your doctor about all medications (including over-the-counter drugs and supplements) you are currently taking.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:** The safety of cilostazol during pregnancy and breastfeeding hasn't been fully established.  Consult your doctor before using this medication if you are pregnant, planning to become pregnant, or breastfeeding.
* **Older Adults and Children:** The use of cilostazol in older adults requires careful monitoring due to a higher risk of side effects.  It is not recommended for use in children.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long does it take for cilostazol to work?**  A: The onset of symptom relief varies, but noticeable improvements in walking distance may occur within a few weeks of starting treatment.
* **Q: Can I stop taking cilostazol suddenly?** A: No, always consult your doctor before stopping cilostazol.  Sudden cessation may lead to a worsening of symptoms.
* **Q: How should I store cilostazol?** A: Store cilostazol tablets in a cool, dry place, away from direct sunlight and moisture.  Keep them out of reach of children.
* **Q:  What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it's almost time for the next dose.  Never double up on doses.
* **Q: Is cilostazol addictive?** A: No, cilostazol is not considered an addictive medication.
