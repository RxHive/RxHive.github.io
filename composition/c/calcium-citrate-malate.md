# Calcium Citrate Malate

**Generic Name:** Calcium Citrate Malate

**Usage:** Calcium citrate malate is primarily used as a dietary supplement to increase calcium intake.  Calcium is a crucial mineral for numerous bodily functions, including strong bones and teeth, muscle function, nerve transmission, and blood clotting.  This specific form of calcium is often preferred because it's more easily absorbed by the body, particularly those with low stomach acid, compared to other calcium salts like calcium carbonate.  While not a treatment for specific diseases in itself, it's frequently recommended to help prevent or manage conditions associated with calcium deficiency, such as:

* **Osteoporosis and Osteopenia:** These conditions involve decreased bone density, increasing the risk of fractures. Calcium supplementation plays a key role in maintaining bone health and slowing bone loss.
* **Hypocalcemia:** This is a condition where blood calcium levels are abnormally low.  Calcium citrate malate can help raise these levels, although it should be used under the guidance of a healthcare professional.
* **Pre-eclampsia (in some cases):**  In certain cases, pregnant women with low calcium may be advised to take calcium citrate malate to help manage symptoms of pre-eclampsia, but this is only used in conjunction with a doctor's direct supervision.


**Dosage:** Dosage varies significantly depending on individual needs and age. A healthcare professional should always determine the appropriate dose.  General guidelines are usually presented in milligrams (mg) of elemental calcium.  This means that the dosage is often related to the calcium content rather than the total weight of the tablet or capsule.

* **Adults:**  The typical daily intake of elemental calcium often ranges from 500mg to 1500mg, spread out across the day, but this is highly variable based on individual circumstances.
* **Children:**  Dosage for children depends entirely on age, weight, and dietary calcium intake; pediatric guidance is essential.

The medication is typically available in tablet or capsule form.  Liquid forms are less common.  Always follow the instructions on the packaging or as directed by a healthcare provider.


**Side Effects:**  Generally, calcium citrate malate is well-tolerated. However, some people may experience:

* **Constipation:** This is a relatively common side effect.
* **Nausea:**  Less frequent but can occur.
* **Gas:** Another relatively common side effect.
* **Abdominal discomfort:** Can range from mild to moderate.

**Serious but less common side effects:**  Hypercalcemia (high blood calcium levels) is possible, particularly with excessive intake. Symptoms may include nausea, vomiting, constipation, fatigue, and confusion.  Seek immediate medical attention if you experience these symptoms.


**How it Works:** Calcium citrate malate is a readily absorbed form of calcium.  The citrate and malate components help to improve absorption, especially in individuals with reduced stomach acid production (such as older adults). Once absorbed, calcium plays its vital role in various bodily processes, contributing to bone mineralization, muscle contraction, nerve function, and blood clotting.


**Popular Brand Names (India):**  Specific brand names vary widely across regions and may change over time.  It's crucial to check with local pharmacies for currently available options.  The generic name "Calcium Citrate Malate" is sufficient for most pharmacists.


**Precautions:**

* **Kidney stones:** Individuals with a history of kidney stones should exercise caution and discuss supplementation with their doctor.
* **Hypercalcemia:**  Those already experiencing high blood calcium levels should avoid calcium citrate malate supplementation.
* **Medication interactions:** Some medications can interact with calcium, including certain antibiotics and diuretics. Consult your doctor or pharmacist about potential interactions.
* **Pregnancy and breastfeeding:** While generally considered safe, pregnant and breastfeeding women should consult their healthcare provider before starting calcium citrate malate supplementation.
* **Older adults:**  Older adults may require a lower dose due to potential kidney function changes.



**FAQs:**

* **Q: Can I take calcium citrate malate without a doctor's prescription?** A: While generally available over-the-counter, it's advisable to consult a doctor, especially if you have pre-existing conditions or are taking other medications.
* **Q: How should I store calcium citrate malate?** A: Store in a cool, dry place, away from moisture and direct sunlight.
* **Q: What if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it's almost time for your next dose.  Don't double the dose.
* **Q: Is calcium citrate malate safe for long-term use?** A: Long-term use is generally safe when taken as directed by a healthcare professional. Regular monitoring of blood calcium levels may be recommended.
