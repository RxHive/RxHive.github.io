# Calcium Gluconate + Calcium Lactobionate

**Generic Name:** Calcium Gluconate + Calcium Lactobionate

**Usage:**

Calcium gluconate and calcium lactobionate are both salts of calcium, an essential mineral vital for numerous bodily functions.  This combination is primarily used to treat hypocalcemia, a condition characterized by low levels of calcium in the blood.  Hypocalcemia can stem from various causes, including vitamin D deficiency, parathyroid gland dysfunction, kidney disease, and certain medications.  The combination offers a readily absorbable form of calcium, helping to quickly restore normal calcium levels.  While its primary use is in treating hypocalcemia, it may also be used in certain emergency situations like treating calcium channel blocker overdose, where a rapid increase in blood calcium is necessary to counteract the effects of the overdose.  It's crucial to understand that this combination is used under strict medical supervision and is not a general calcium supplement.


**Dosage:**

Dosage varies significantly depending on the individual's age, weight, severity of hypocalcemia, and the specific form of administration.  A healthcare professional will determine the appropriate dose.  There is no standard dosage information that can be provided here as dosages are highly individualized and prescribed based on blood calcium levels and clinical judgment. The medication can be administered intravenously (IV), intramuscularly (IM), or orally (though oral forms are less common for rapid correction of hypocalcemia).  Intravenous administration is the usual method for severe hypocalcemia requiring rapid correction.  Oral forms, if available, are usually tablets or capsules.  Children's dosages are significantly different from adult dosages and require careful calculation based on body weight and clinical need.


**Side Effects:**

* **Common:** Nausea, vomiting, constipation, headache, flushing, and a feeling of warmth.  These side effects are usually mild and transient.
* **Less Common but Serious:**  Rapid intravenous administration can lead to cardiac arrhythmias (irregular heartbeat), hypotension (low blood pressure), and tissue necrosis (tissue death) at the injection site if the injection is not properly administered.  Allergic reactions, though rare, are also possible.

**Important:** Any adverse effect, even seemingly minor, should be reported to a healthcare professional immediately.


**How it Works:**

Calcium gluconate and calcium lactobionate are readily absorbed in the body.  They provide supplemental calcium, helping to restore normal blood calcium levels. Calcium is essential for a multitude of physiological processes, including muscle contraction, nerve transmission, blood clotting, and bone health.  By increasing the calcium levels in the blood, this combination alleviates symptoms of hypocalcemia and helps to restore the normal function of the systems dependent on calcium.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:**  This combination is contraindicated in patients with hypercalcemia (high blood calcium levels), digitalis toxicity, and severe renal impairment.
* **Drug Interactions:**  Calcium can interact with various medications, including tetracyclines, some diuretics, and certain cardiac glycosides.  Always inform your doctor about all medications, supplements, and herbal remedies you are taking.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  Use during pregnancy and breastfeeding should be under strict medical supervision due to the potential impact on the mother and infant.
* **Elderly Patients:**  Elderly patients may be more susceptible to side effects and require careful monitoring.
* **Children:**  Dosage in children must be carefully calculated by a healthcare professional.



**FAQs:**

* **Q: Can I take this medication without a doctor's prescription?**  A: No. This medication should only be taken under strict medical supervision and is prescribed based on individual needs and blood tests.
* **Q: How should I store this medication?** A: Store as directed on the label.  Generally, it should be stored in a cool, dry place, away from direct sunlight and moisture.
* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Consult your doctor or pharmacist regarding missed doses. Do not double the dose to compensate for a missed dose.
* **Q:  Is this medication safe for long-term use?** A: Long-term use should only be considered under close medical supervision and with regular monitoring of blood calcium levels.
