# Carbazochrome

**Generic Name: Carbazochrome**

**Usage:**

Carbazochrome is primarily used to reduce capillary bleeding and enhance capillary resistance.  This means it helps strengthen blood vessels, making them less prone to leakage and reducing the risk of bleeding, particularly from small blood vessels.  Its main application is in treating conditions where easy bruising or bleeding is a prominent symptom. This includes conditions like purpura (a condition involving small red or purple spots on the skin), epistaxis (nosebleeds), and other minor bleeding occurrences.  While it has been historically used in a wider range of conditions, current medical practice tends to limit its application to those mentioned above, primarily due to the availability of more effective treatments for many previously considered indications.

**Dosage:**

Dosage of carbazochrome varies depending on the specific condition being treated, the patient's age and overall health, and the form of the medication.  There's no universally standardized dosage across all formulations and geographical locations.  Precise dosage instructions should always come directly from a prescribing physician.  Generally, the medication is available in tablet and injection forms.  It's crucial to follow the physician's prescription exactly and to not exceed the recommended dosage.  The physician will consider factors such as the severity of the bleeding and the patient's response to the treatment when determining the appropriate dose.  Pediatric dosages are specifically determined by the treating physician based on the child's weight and condition.

Serious, though less common, side effects are possible.  If you experience any unusual or concerning symptoms after taking carbazochrome, seek immediate medical attention.


**How it Works:**

Carbazochrome is a synthetic derivative of adrenaline (epinephrine). It works by stabilizing capillary walls, making them less permeable and reducing their fragility. This improved integrity of the blood vessels minimizes leakage and helps to control bleeding, particularly from small capillaries. While the precise mechanism isn't fully elucidated, its effect on capillary resistance is well-documented.
