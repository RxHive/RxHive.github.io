# Cisapride

**Generic Name: Cisapride**

**Usage:**

Cisapride is a prokinetic agent, primarily used to improve the movement of food through the digestive tract.  Its main application is in treating gastroesophageal reflux disease (GERD), a condition where stomach acid flows back into the esophagus, causing heartburn and other symptoms.  It can also be used to help relieve symptoms associated with gastroparesis, a condition where the stomach empties too slowly.  While it was once used more broadly, its use has significantly decreased due to potential cardiovascular side effects.  Note that its use is now largely restricted and may not be readily available in many regions.  Always consult a physician before using cisapride.

**Dosage:**

Dosage varies considerably depending on the individual, the specific condition being treated, and the physician's judgment.  There is no standard pediatric dosage as its use in children is limited due to safety concerns.  Adult dosages generally start lower and are adjusted based on response and tolerance.  Cisapride was typically available in tablet form.  Precise dosage instructions must always come directly from a prescribing physician, as self-medication is dangerous.

**Side Effects:**

* **Common:** Diarrhea, nausea, headache, abdominal cramping
* **Less Common but Serious:**  Prolonged QT interval (can lead to serious heart rhythm disturbances), palpitations, dizziness.

**Important Note:**  Any new or worsening symptoms, especially those related to the heart (irregular heartbeat, palpitations, fainting), should be reported immediately to a healthcare provider.  Cisapride carries significant risks and should only be used under strict medical supervision.


**How it Works:**

Cisapride works by affecting the motility of the gastrointestinal tract. It increases the contractions of the muscles in the esophagus and stomach, helping food move more efficiently through the digestive system.  This action reduces the likelihood of stomach acid refluxing back into the esophagus and improves the emptying of the stomach in gastroparesis.  The drug achieves this by influencing specific receptors in the gut.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** Cisapride is contraindicated in patients with certain heart conditions, including prolonged QT interval, congenital long QT syndrome, and a history of torsades de pointes (a type of irregular heartbeat).  It should also be avoided by individuals with liver or kidney disease.
* **Drug Interactions:** Cisapride interacts with many medications, especially those that can prolong the QT interval.  It is critical to inform your doctor about all medications, supplements, and herbal remedies you are taking.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  The safety of cisapride during pregnancy and breastfeeding is not established.  Avoid use unless specifically directed by a doctor who has carefully weighed the risks and benefits.
* **Elderly Patients:** Older adults are generally more sensitive to the side effects of cisapride and require careful monitoring.
