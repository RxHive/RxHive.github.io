# Crisaborole

**Generic Name: Crisaborole**

**Usage:**

Crisaborole is a topical medication primarily used to treat mild to moderate atopic dermatitis (eczema) in adults and children aged two years and older.  Atopic dermatitis is a chronic inflammatory skin condition characterized by itchy, inflamed skin. Crisaborole works directly on the skin to reduce inflammation and itching associated with this condition. While its primary use is in atopic dermatitis, research is ongoing to explore its potential in other skin conditions.


**Dosage:**

Crisaborole is available as a topical ointment.  Dosage varies depending on the age and affected area of the patient.  Always follow the directions provided by your healthcare professional or the instructions included with your prescription. Generally:

* **Adults and Children (2 years and older):**  A thin layer of crisaborole ointment is applied twice daily to the affected areas of the skin.  The amount used should be enough to cover the affected area but avoid excessive application.  

The dosage is not typically adjusted based on weight or the severity of the condition, but the healthcare provider will determine the appropriate application based on the individual needs.  

**Side Effects:**

* **Common:**  Burning sensation at the application site, skin irritation, application site pain, itching. These are usually mild and temporary.

* **Less Common but Serious:**  Serious allergic reactions (rare), such as swelling of the face, lips, tongue, or throat; difficulty breathing; hives.  Contact your healthcare provider immediately if you experience these symptoms.

**How it Works:**

Crisaborole works by inhibiting phosphodiesterase 4 (PDE4), an enzyme involved in the inflammatory process.  By blocking PDE4, crisaborole reduces the release of inflammatory substances in the skin, leading to a decrease in inflammation and itching.  It acts directly on the skin, rather than affecting the whole body. This localized action minimizes potential systemic side effects.

**Precautions:**

* **Avoid contact with eyes:** If accidental contact occurs, rinse thoroughly with water.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  Consult your doctor before using crisaborole if you are pregnant, breastfeeding, or planning to become pregnant.  
* **Children:** Use only as directed by a physician for children aged two years and older.
* **Drug Interactions:** While generally well-tolerated, inform your healthcare provider of all medications you are currently taking, including over-the-counter drugs and herbal supplements.
* **Allergic Reactions:** Stop use and seek immediate medical attention if you experience a severe allergic reaction.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long does it take to see results?** A: You may begin to see improvements in your skin within a few days, but the full therapeutic effect may take several weeks.
* **Q: Can I use crisaborole with other topical medications?** A:  Discuss with your healthcare provider before using crisaborole concurrently with other topical medications, especially corticosteroids.
* **Q: How should I store crisaborole ointment?** A: Store at room temperature, away from direct sunlight and moisture.
* **Q: Is crisaborole addictive?** A: No, crisaborole is not considered an addictive medication.
* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Apply the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it is almost time for the next dose. Do not apply a double dose.
