**How it Works:**

Each component works differently:

* **Cinnarizine:** Blocks calcium channels in the inner ear, reducing the transmission of nerve impulses that cause vertigo.

* **Dimenhydrinate:** Blocks histamine receptors in the brain, reducing nausea and vomiting. It may also have some direct effects on the vestibular system (inner ear).

**Precautions:**

* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  Use should be carefully considered and only under strict medical supervision during pregnancy and breastfeeding.
* **Interactions with Other Drugs:** This combination might interact with other medications, especially other central nervous system depressants (e.g., sedatives, opioids).  Inform your doctor about all medications you are taking.
* **Driving and Operating Machinery:** The drowsiness caused by this combination may impair judgment and coordination.  Avoid driving or operating heavy machinery until the effects are known.
* **Alcohol:** Avoid alcohol consumption as it may worsen drowsiness and other side effects.
* **Elderly:**  Elderly individuals are more susceptible to side effects, especially drowsiness and confusion.
* **Liver and Kidney Disease:**  Dosage adjustments may be necessary in patients with liver or kidney impairment.
