# Cabergoline

**Generic Name: Cabergoline**

**Usage:**

Cabergoline is a dopamine agonist, primarily used to treat hyperprolactinemia, a condition characterized by excessively high levels of prolactin in the blood.  High prolactin levels can lead to various symptoms in both men and women, including:

* **In women:** Irregular or absent menstrual periods (amenorrhea), infertility, galactorrhea (spontaneous milk production), and decreased libido.  It's often used to restore fertility in women with hyperprolactinemia-related infertility.

* **In men:** Decreased libido, erectile dysfunction, and infertility.

Beyond hyperprolactinemia, cabergoline has secondary uses, though these are less common and often require specialized medical consultation.  These include:

* **Parkinson's disease:**  Cabergoline can be used as an adjunct therapy in some cases, helping to manage motor symptoms. However, its use in Parkinson's is less frequent than other medications due to potential side effects.
* **Acromegaly:** While less common, it may be used in certain situations to treat acromegaly, a condition characterized by excessive growth hormone production.


**Dosage:**

Dosage varies greatly depending on the condition being treated, the individual's response, and the physician's judgment.  It's crucial to follow a doctor's prescription exactly.  Generally, treatment starts with a low dose and is gradually increased.  Cabergoline is typically administered orally as a tablet.  There is no syrup or injectable form commonly available.  Dosage information provided here is for general understanding only and should not be interpreted as a prescription.   **Always consult a physician for proper dosage.**

* **Adults (Hyperprolactinemia):**  Treatment usually begins with a low dose, often 0.25mg to 0.5mg once or twice a week.  The dose can be adjusted based on the response and the presence of side effects.
* **Children:** Cabergoline is generally not recommended for use in children due to limited data on safety and efficacy.

**Side Effects:**

* **Common:** Nausea, vomiting, dizziness, headache, fatigue, constipation, and orthostatic hypotension (sudden drop in blood pressure upon standing).
* **Less Common but Serious:**  Fibrosis (scarring of tissues) particularly affecting the heart valves, pulmonary hypertension (high blood pressure in the lungs), and sudden cardiac death. These serious side effects are rare but warrant immediate medical attention.

* **Other possible side effects:**  Changes in skin pigmentation, shortness of breath, chest pain, and abnormal dreams.


**It is imperative to contact a healthcare provider immediately if you experience any concerning side effects.**


**How it Works:**

Cabergoline works by mimicking the effects of dopamine, a neurotransmitter in the brain.  By stimulating dopamine receptors, it inhibits the release of prolactin from the pituitary gland. This reduces high prolactin levels, alleviating the symptoms associated with hyperprolactinemia. In Parkinson's disease, its dopamine agonist effect helps improve motor control.

**Precautions:**

* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  Cabergoline is contraindicated during pregnancy and breastfeeding due to potential harm to the fetus or infant.
* **Heart Conditions:**  Individuals with a history of heart valve problems or pulmonary hypertension should exercise extreme caution and may not be suitable candidates for this medication.
* **Liver and Kidney Disease:** Dosage adjustments may be necessary for those with impaired liver or kidney function.
* **Drug Interactions:** Cabergoline can interact with several other medications. Inform your doctor about all medications, supplements, and herbal remedies you are taking.
* **Elderly:**  Older adults may be more susceptible to side effects and require careful monitoring.

**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long does it take to see results?** A:  The time it takes to see improvements varies depending on the individual and the condition being treated.  It can take several weeks or months.
* **Q: How should I store cabergoline?** A: Store the medication in a cool, dry place, away from direct sunlight and moisture.  Keep it out of reach of children.
* **Q: Can I stop taking cabergoline suddenly?** A: No.  Always consult your doctor before stopping the medication, as sudden discontinuation may lead to rebound effects.
* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A:  If you miss a dose, take it as soon as you remember, unless it is almost time for your next dose. Do not double the dose.
