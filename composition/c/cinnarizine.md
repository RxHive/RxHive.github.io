# Cinnarizine

**Generic Name: Cinnarizine**

**Usage:**

Cinnarizine is primarily used to treat vertigo (dizziness) and motion sickness.  Its effects stem from its ability to influence blood flow and reduce the effects of histamine in the body.  Therefore, it's sometimes also prescribed to help manage conditions where poor blood flow to the inner ear or brain is a contributing factor, such as Ménière's disease (although this is not a primary or solely effective treatment).  It's important to note that cinnarizine isn't a cure for these conditions; it helps manage symptoms.  The specific use and efficacy will depend on the individual and the underlying condition.  Always consult a doctor to determine if cinnarizine is the appropriate treatment for your specific situation.

**Dosage:**

Dosage varies significantly depending on the condition being treated, the individual's age, weight, and overall health.  A doctor will determine the appropriate dosage.  Typically, for adults, it might range from 15mg to 75mg per day, taken in divided doses.  Children's dosages are significantly lower and strictly determined by a pediatrician based on weight and age.  The medication comes in various forms, including tablets and potentially syrups (although availability of specific formulations may vary by region). The tablets should be swallowed whole with water.  Injection is not a standard route of administration for cinnarizine.  It is crucial to follow the prescribed dosage and frequency precisely as directed by your doctor.

**Side Effects:**

Common side effects can include:

* Drowsiness
* Fatigue
* Dry mouth
* Weight gain
* Dizziness
* Headache
* Gastrointestinal disturbances (e.g., nausea, constipation)

Less common, but more serious side effects, requiring immediate medical attention, include:

* Severe allergic reactions (rash, swelling, difficulty breathing)
* Jaundice (yellowing of the skin or eyes)
* Changes in blood cell counts

If you experience any unexpected or concerning side effects, discontinue use and contact your healthcare provider immediately.


**How it Works:**

Cinnarizine is a calcium channel blocker with antihistamine properties.  It works by affecting the movement of calcium ions into certain cells, specifically those in blood vessels. This helps to relax and widen blood vessels, improving blood flow, particularly to the inner ear and brain. Its antihistamine properties help reduce the effects of histamine, a substance that can contribute to the symptoms of vertigo and motion sickness.  By improving blood flow and reducing the effects of histamine, cinnarizine helps alleviate the symptoms associated with vertigo and motion sickness.

**Precautions:**

* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  Cinnarizine should be used with caution during pregnancy and breastfeeding.  A doctor should carefully assess the risks and benefits.
* **Elderly Patients:**  Older adults may be more susceptible to side effects, particularly drowsiness.  Careful monitoring is necessary.
* **Liver and Kidney Disease:**  Patients with liver or kidney disease require careful dosage adjustment due to the potential for altered drug metabolism and excretion.
* **Driving and Operating Machinery:**  Due to the potential for drowsiness, avoid driving or operating machinery until you know how cinnarizine affects you.
* **Drug Interactions:**  Cinnarizine can interact with other medications, including certain antidepressants and sedatives.  Inform your doctor of all medications you are currently taking.  Alcohol should be avoided or consumed in moderation while using this medication.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long does it take for cinnarizine to work?** A: The onset of effect varies, but some relief from vertigo symptoms may be noticed within a few days, while others might take longer.

* **Q: Can I stop taking cinnarizine suddenly?** A:  No, always follow your doctor's instructions on stopping the medication.  Sudden discontinuation might lead to withdrawal symptoms.

* **Q: How should I store cinnarizine?** A: Store it in a cool, dry place, away from direct sunlight and moisture, as instructed on the packaging.

* **Q: Is cinnarizine addictive?** A:  Cinnarizine is not considered an addictive substance.

* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it is almost time for the next dose. Do not double the dose to make up for a missed one.
