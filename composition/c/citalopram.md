# Citalopram

**Generic Name: Citalopram**

**Usage:**

Citalopram is primarily used to treat major depressive disorder (MDD), a common mood disorder characterized by persistent sadness, loss of interest, and other symptoms.  It's also effective in managing other conditions, including:

* **Generalized Anxiety Disorder (GAD):**  Citalopram helps reduce excessive worry and anxiety that is difficult to control.
* **Panic Disorder:** It can lessen the frequency and severity of panic attacks.
* **Obsessive-Compulsive Disorder (OCD):** While not a first-line treatment, it can help manage obsessive thoughts and compulsive behaviors.
* **Premenstrual Dysphoric Disorder (PMDD):**  This is a severe form of premenstrual syndrome (PMS) with significant mood disturbances; citalopram can help alleviate symptoms.

The specific use and effectiveness of citalopram will depend on individual factors and should always be determined by a healthcare professional.


**Dosage:**

Dosage is highly individualized and determined by a doctor based on the patient's condition, response to treatment, and other factors.  Generally:

* **Adults (MDD, GAD, Panic Disorder, OCD, PMDD):** The usual starting dose is 20mg once daily.  This may be increased gradually by the doctor to a maximum of 40mg daily, depending on individual needs and tolerability.
* **Children:** Citalopram is generally *not* recommended for children and adolescents.  Its use in this population requires careful evaluation and monitoring by a specialist.
* **Elderly:**  Lower starting doses may be recommended due to increased sensitivity to medication.
* **Forms:** Citalopram is usually available as oral tablets.  Liquid formulations may exist, but are less common.

**It is crucial to strictly follow the prescription instructions provided by your doctor. Never adjust the dosage on your own.**


**Side Effects:**

Common side effects (occurring in more than 10% of patients) may include:

* Nausea
* Headache
* Dry mouth
* Insomnia or drowsiness
* Sweating
* Dizziness
* Fatigue
* Constipation
* Decreased libido (reduced sex drive)
* Weight changes (gain or loss)


Less common but serious side effects that require immediate medical attention include:

* Serotonin syndrome (a rare but potentially life-threatening condition characterized by high fever, muscle rigidity, confusion, and rapid heart rate)
* Mania or hypomania (abnormally elevated mood and energy)
* Seizures
* Allergic reactions (rash, itching, swelling)
* Bleeding problems


**If you experience any concerning side effects, contact your doctor or pharmacist immediately.**


**How it Works:**

Citalopram is a selective serotonin reuptake inhibitor (SSRI).  Serotonin is a neurotransmitter (chemical messenger) in the brain that plays a significant role in mood regulation.  SSRIs work by blocking the reuptake of serotonin, increasing its availability in the brain's synapses. This increased serotonin activity helps alleviate symptoms of depression and other mood disorders. The effect is gradual, with noticeable improvements typically taking several weeks.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** Citalopram should not be used in individuals with a known hypersensitivity to the drug or those taking MAO inhibitors (another class of antidepressants).
* **Drug Interactions:**  Citalopram can interact with several other medications, including other antidepressants, certain pain relievers, and some herbal supplements. It is essential to inform your doctor about all medications and supplements you are taking.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  Use during pregnancy and breastfeeding requires careful consideration and consultation with a healthcare professional.  The potential risks and benefits should be weighed carefully.
* **Older Adults:**  Older adults may be more susceptible to side effects.  Dosage adjustments may be necessary.
* **Alcohol:**  Avoid excessive alcohol consumption while taking citalopram.
* **Driving and Operating Machinery:**  Citalopram may cause drowsiness or dizziness.  Avoid activities requiring alertness until you know how the medication affects you.



**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long does it take to work?** A:  It usually takes several weeks for citalopram to become fully effective.  Improvements may be gradual.
* **Q: Can I stop taking it suddenly?** A: No, abruptly stopping citalopram can lead to withdrawal symptoms.  Always consult your doctor before discontinuing treatment.  They will help you gradually reduce your dose.
* **Q: How should I store it?** A: Store citalopram in a cool, dry place, away from direct sunlight and moisture. Keep it out of reach of children.
* **Q: Is it addictive?** A: Citalopram is not considered physically addictive, but it is important to follow your doctor's prescribed dosage and not stop taking it without their guidance.  Psychological dependence can occur but it's less likely with proper medical management.
* **Q: What if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember unless it is close to the time for your next dose.  Never take a double dose to make up for a missed one.
