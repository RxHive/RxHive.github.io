# Calcium Polystyrene Sulphonate

**Generic Name:** Calcium Polystyrene Sulfonate

**Usage:**

Calcium polystyrene sulfonate is a medication primarily used to treat **hyperkalemia**, a condition characterized by dangerously high levels of potassium in the blood.  High potassium can disrupt the heart's rhythm and function, potentially leading to life-threatening arrhythmias.  This medication is specifically designed to lower potassium levels by removing excess potassium from the body through the digestive system. While its primary use is in hyperkalemia, it may sometimes be used in other situations where potassium needs to be carefully managed, but this is usually determined on a case-by-case basis by a medical professional.

**Dosage:**

Dosage of calcium polystyrene sulfonate is highly individualized and depends on the severity of hyperkalemia, the patient's age, weight, and overall health.  It's crucial to follow your doctor's prescription precisely.  This medication typically comes as an oral powder or suspension.  There isn't a standardized dosage for children; pediatric use requires careful monitoring and dose adjustment by a physician. The medication is generally *not* administered via injection.  Instructions for mixing and taking the medication will be provided by your pharmacist and physician.


**Side Effects:**

* **Common:** Constipation is a frequent side effect.  Nausea, vomiting, and abdominal discomfort may also occur.
* **Less Common, but Serious:**  Hypokalemia (low potassium levels – a paradoxical effect if the medication is overused),  alkalosis (elevated blood pH), dehydration, and bowel obstruction (especially in patients already prone to constipation).  Severe allergic reactions, though rare, are also possible.

* **Important Note:** Any new or worsening symptoms, especially gastrointestinal issues or changes in heart rhythm, should be reported to a doctor immediately.


**How it Works:**

Calcium polystyrene sulfonate is a cation-exchange resin.  This means it works in the intestines by exchanging sodium ions for potassium ions.  The potassium ions bind to the resin, preventing their absorption into the bloodstream.  The bound potassium is then eliminated from the body through the stool. This process effectively lowers the potassium level in the blood.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:**  This medication should not be used in patients with known allergies to its components, those with bowel obstructions, or those experiencing severe constipation.
* **Drug Interactions:** Calcium polystyrene sulfonate can interact with other medications, such as digoxin and some antibiotics.  Inform your doctor of all medications you are taking, including over-the-counter drugs and herbal supplements.
* **Specific Populations:**  Pregnant and breastfeeding women, as well as children and older adults, should be under close medical supervision while taking this medication.  Dosage adjustments are often necessary.
* **Safety Measures:**  Adequate hydration is crucial while taking this medication to prevent constipation and dehydration. Regular monitoring of potassium levels is necessary to ensure the treatment is effective and safe.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long does it take to work?** A: The effect on potassium levels can vary, but typically it takes several hours to see a noticeable change.
* **Q: Can I take this medication with food?** A:  Follow your doctor's instructions regarding food intake.  It's often recommended to take this medication with plenty of water.
* **Q: How should I store it?** A: Store as directed on the label, typically in a cool, dry place away from moisture and direct sunlight.
* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Contact your doctor or pharmacist for guidance.  Do not double the dose to compensate for a missed one.
* **Q: Is this medication safe for long-term use?** A:  Long-term use is generally not recommended;  it is typically used for short-term management of hyperkalemia.  Your doctor will determine the appropriate duration of treatment.
