# Cinnarizine + Dimenhydrinate

**Generic Name:** Cinnarizine + Dimenhydrinate

**Usage:**

Cinnarizine + Dimenhydrinate is a combination medication primarily used to treat vertigo (a sensation of spinning or dizziness) and nausea associated with various conditions affecting the inner ear and balance system.  Its primary use is in managing symptoms of Meniere's disease, labyrinthitis (inner ear inflammation), and vestibular neuronitis (inflammation of the vestibular nerve). It can also be used to alleviate motion sickness symptoms, including nausea, vomiting, and dizziness experienced during travel by car, sea, or air.  While not a first-line treatment for these conditions, it offers symptomatic relief, making it easier for patients to manage their daily lives.  The combination leverages the distinct mechanisms of its components: cinnarizine acts on the inner ear and brain to reduce dizziness, while dimenhydrinate combats nausea and vomiting.


**Dosage:**

Dosage varies significantly depending on the specific formulation (tablet, syrup), the patient's age, weight, and the severity of their symptoms.  It's crucial to follow the prescription provided by a healthcare professional.  Generally, adults may receive 1-2 tablets or the equivalent in syrup three times daily.  Dosage for children is significantly lower and must be determined by a doctor based on the child's age and weight.  There are typically no injectable formulations of this combination widely available.  Always adhere to the prescribed dosage and frequency; do not exceed the recommended amount without consulting a doctor.


**Side Effects:**

* **Common:** Drowsiness, dry mouth, blurred vision, headache, fatigue, constipation.
* **Less Common but Serious:**  Allergic reactions (rash, itching, swelling), heart palpitations, jaundice (yellowing of the skin and eyes), changes in blood pressure.

If you experience any adverse effects, especially those listed as less common but serious, discontinue use and seek immediate medical attention.


**How it Works:**

Cinnarizine is a calcium channel blocker that primarily affects blood vessels in the inner ear and brain, reducing dizziness and improving blood flow. Dimenhydrinate is an antihistamine with anticholinergic and antiemetic properties.  It works by blocking histamine receptors in the brain, thereby reducing nausea and vomiting. The combination provides a synergistic effect, addressing both the dizziness and nausea associated with the target conditions.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** This medication should be avoided by individuals with known allergies to cinnarizine or dimenhydrinate. It's also generally not recommended for those with glaucoma, prostate enlargement, or urinary retention.
* **Drug Interactions:**  Cinnarizine + Dimenhydrinate can interact with other medications, including sedatives, alcohol, and certain antidepressants.  Always inform your doctor of all medications you are currently taking.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:** Use during pregnancy and breastfeeding should only be considered under strict medical supervision, as the potential risks to the fetus or infant need careful assessment.
* **Elderly:** Older adults may be more susceptible to side effects, especially drowsiness, and require careful monitoring.
* **Children:**  Dosage in children must be determined by a physician.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: Can I drive after taking this medication?** A: Drowsiness is a common side effect. Avoid driving or operating machinery until you know how the medication affects you.
* **Q: How should I store this medication?** A: Store in a cool, dry place, away from direct sunlight and out of reach of children.
* **Q: Can I take this medication with alcohol?** A: Avoid alcohol consumption while taking this medication, as it can worsen side effects like drowsiness.
* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it is almost time for your next dose.  Do not double the dose.
* **Q: How long will it take to feel better?** A: The time it takes to experience symptom relief varies. Consult your doctor if you don't notice improvement after a reasonable period.
