# Candesartan + Hydrochlorothiazide

**Generic Name:** Candesartan + Hydrochlorothiazide

**Usage:**

Candesartan + Hydrochlorothiazide is a combination medication used primarily to treat high blood pressure (hypertension).  It works by tackling the problem from two angles.  Candesartan is an angiotensin II receptor blocker (ARB), which helps relax blood vessels, making it easier for the heart to pump blood. Hydrochlorothiazide is a diuretic (water pill), which helps the body get rid of excess salt and water, thus lowering blood volume and blood pressure. This combination often proves more effective than either drug alone.  While its primary use is hypertension, it might sometimes be considered for treating certain types of heart failure where blood pressure management is a key component, although this would be under strict medical supervision.  It's crucial to understand this medication is only effective as prescribed by a doctor and shouldn't be self-prescribed or used for conditions it isn't specifically indicated for.

**Dosage:**

Dosage is determined individually by a healthcare professional based on several factors, including the patient's age, weight, overall health, and the severity of their hypertension.  It's typically administered as an oral tablet.  There is no standard dosage for children as this combination is generally not recommended for pediatric use. Adult dosages usually start low and are adjusted gradually as needed, typically ranging from a low dose to higher, carefully monitored doses, depending on the patient's response. The specific combination of Candesartan and Hydrochlorothiazide will also vary in strength depending on the prescription.  It’s vital to follow the doctor's instructions precisely regarding dosage and frequency of taking the medication.

**Side Effects:**

Common side effects can include:

* Dizziness
* Lightheadedness
* Headache
* Fatigue
* Nausea
* Diarrhea
* Muscle cramps
* Dry cough (less common than with ACE inhibitors)

Less common, but potentially serious, side effects include:

*  High potassium levels (hyperkalemia)
*  Low blood pressure (hypotension)
*  Kidney problems
* Allergic reactions (rash, itching, swelling)

If you experience any side effects, particularly those listed as serious, contact your doctor or healthcare provider immediately.


**How it Works:**

Candesartan blocks the action of angiotensin II, a hormone that narrows blood vessels, increasing blood pressure. By blocking angiotensin II, candesartan helps blood vessels relax and widen, leading to decreased blood pressure. Hydrochlorothiazide increases the excretion of sodium and water from the body through urine. This reduces blood volume, further contributing to lower blood pressure.  Together, these actions provide a synergistic effect, more effectively controlling hypertension than either drug alone.

**Precautions:**

* **Pregnancy:** This medication is generally not recommended during pregnancy, particularly in the second and third trimesters, as it can harm the developing fetus.
* **Breastfeeding:** Consult your doctor before using this medication while breastfeeding.
* **Kidney Disease:**  Careful monitoring of kidney function is necessary, particularly in patients with pre-existing kidney disease.
* **Liver Disease:**  Use should be cautious in those with liver problems.
* **Diabetes:** This medication can affect blood sugar levels, requiring careful monitoring in diabetic patients.
* **Drug Interactions:** This medication can interact with several other drugs. Always inform your doctor of all medications, supplements, and herbal remedies you are taking.
* **Older Adults:** Older adults may be more susceptible to side effects, and dosage adjustments may be necessary.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: Can I stop taking this medication abruptly?** A: No.  Always consult your doctor before stopping this medication.  Sudden cessation can lead to a rebound effect, potentially causing a dangerous spike in blood pressure.
* **Q: How should I store this medication?** A: Store it as directed on the label, typically in a cool, dry place away from moisture and direct sunlight.
* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it's almost time for your next dose.  Do not double the dose.
* **Q: Is this medication habit-forming?** A: No, it is not habit-forming.
* **Q: Can I drink alcohol while taking this medication?** A:  Excessive alcohol consumption can lower blood pressure and interact with this medication. Discuss alcohol consumption with your doctor.
