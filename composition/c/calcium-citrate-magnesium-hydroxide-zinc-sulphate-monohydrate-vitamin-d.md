# Calcium citrate + Magnesium Hydroxide + Zinc Sulphate Monohydrate + Vitamin D

**Generic Name:** Calcium citrate + Magnesium Hydroxide + Zinc Sulphate Monohydrate + Vitamin D

**Usage:** This combination of minerals and vitamins is primarily used to address nutritional deficiencies.  It's often prescribed to correct or prevent deficiencies in calcium, magnesium, zinc, and vitamin D. These deficiencies can contribute to a range of health problems.  

Calcium is crucial for strong bones and teeth, muscle function, and nerve transmission. Magnesium supports muscle and nerve function, blood sugar control, and blood pressure regulation. Zinc plays a vital role in immune function, wound healing, and cell growth. Vitamin D is essential for calcium absorption, bone health, and immune function.  Therefore, this combination is frequently used in individuals at risk of or already experiencing deficiencies in these nutrients, such as pregnant or breastfeeding women, older adults, individuals with malabsorption disorders, or those following restrictive diets. Secondary uses might include supporting bone health in individuals with osteoporosis risk factors or aiding in wound healing.  The exact indication will always be determined by a healthcare professional based on individual needs.

**Dosage:** Dosage varies significantly depending on the individual's age, weight, health status, and specific deficiency levels. A doctor will determine the appropriate dose.  There's no universally applicable dosage.  This medication is typically available in tablet form. It's crucial to follow your doctor's instructions precisely and not exceed the recommended dosage.  Do not begin or alter this medication independently.

**Side Effects:**

* **Common:** Constipation, nausea, diarrhea, stomach upset.
* **Less Common but Serious:**  Kidney stones (especially with pre-existing kidney conditions), hypercalcemia (high blood calcium levels),  hypermagnesemia (high blood magnesium levels).  These require immediate medical attention.

*Note:*  This is not an exhaustive list.  Any new or worsening symptoms should be reported to a healthcare provider immediately.

**How it Works:**  Each component of this combination works independently to address its respective nutrient deficiency. Calcium citrate provides readily absorbable calcium. Magnesium hydroxide is a readily absorbable form of magnesium that also acts as an antacid (reducing stomach acid). Zinc sulphate monohydrate supplies zinc, and vitamin D facilitates calcium absorption from the gut and regulates calcium and phosphorus levels in the blood.  By supplying these essential nutrients, the body can better perform its various functions.


**Popular Brand Names (India):**  Specific brand names vary widely based on the manufacturer and formulation. It's best to consult a pharmacist or doctor for locally available options.  Brand names are not typically standardized across different manufacturers or regions.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:**  This combination may not be suitable for individuals with certain kidney conditions, hypercalcemia, hypermagnesemia, or those with a history of kidney stones.  It is crucial to disclose all existing medical conditions to the doctor before starting this medication.
* **Drug Interactions:**  This medication can interact with other medications, such as diuretics, tetracyclines, and some heart medications.  Always inform your doctor about all medications, supplements, and herbal remedies you are taking.
* **Specific Warnings:** Pregnant and breastfeeding women, children, and older adults should only take this medication under the guidance of a healthcare professional, as the appropriate dosage and potential risks might differ.

* **Q:  What if I experience side effects?** A:  Report all side effects to your doctor immediately.  Stop taking the medication only if directed by your doctor.
