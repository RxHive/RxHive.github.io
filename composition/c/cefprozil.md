# Cefprozil

**Generic Name: Cefprozil**

**Usage:**

Cefprozil is an antibiotic belonging to the cephalosporin class.  Its primary use is in treating bacterial infections of the ears, nose, throat, and respiratory tract. This includes conditions like acute otitis media (middle ear infection), pharyngitis (sore throat), tonsillitis, and sinusitis.  It's also sometimes used to treat skin infections and pneumonia, though other antibiotics might be preferred for these conditions depending on the specific bacteria involved.  It's crucial to remember that cefprozil only works against bacterial infections; it's ineffective against viral infections like the common cold or influenza.  The doctor will determine the most appropriate use based on the individual's specific infection and its severity.

**Dosage:**

Dosage varies significantly depending on the patient's age, weight, and the severity of the infection.  A doctor will always prescribe the correct dose.  Generally, for adults, the standard dose is 250-500 mg every 12 hours. For children, the dosage is typically calculated based on weight, often ranging from 15-30mg/kg/day divided into two doses.  Cefprozil is available in different forms, including oral suspension (liquid) and tablets.  The liquid formulation is often preferred for children or individuals who have difficulty swallowing pills.  Always follow the prescribed dosage and administration instructions carefully.  Never exceed the recommended dosage.

**Side Effects:**

Common side effects of cefprozil are generally mild and may include:

* Diarrhea
* Nausea
* Vomiting
* Stomach upset
* Dizziness
* Headache
* Rash

Less common, but more serious side effects can include:

* Severe allergic reactions (anaphylaxis), characterized by difficulty breathing, swelling of the face, lips, or tongue, and hives.  Seek immediate medical attention if this occurs.
* Clostridium difficile-associated diarrhea (CDAD), a serious intestinal infection.
* Seizures (in individuals with pre-existing conditions or high doses)


If any concerning side effects develop, consult a healthcare professional immediately.

**How it Works:**

Cefprozil works by interfering with the ability of bacteria to build their cell walls.  Bacteria require strong cell walls to survive.  By inhibiting the production of these walls, cefprozil weakens and ultimately kills the bacteria, allowing the body's immune system to effectively clear the infection.

**Precautions:**

* **Allergies:**  Cefprozil is a cephalosporin antibiotic.  Individuals with a known allergy to penicillin or other cephalosporins should not take cefprozil.
* **Drug Interactions:**  Cefprozil may interact with certain medications. Inform your doctor about all medications, including over-the-counter drugs, herbal supplements, and vitamins, you are currently taking.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  Consult your doctor before taking cefprozil if you are pregnant, breastfeeding, or planning to become pregnant.  The risks and benefits need to be carefully evaluated.
* **Kidney and Liver Disease:**  Individuals with impaired kidney or liver function may require dosage adjustments.
* **Children:**  Cefprozil should only be used in children under the guidance and prescription of a pediatrician.
* **Elderly:**  Older adults may be more susceptible to side effects.

**FAQs:**

* **Q: Can I stop taking cefprozil when I feel better?**  A: No.  Complete the entire course of medication as prescribed, even if you feel better, to prevent the infection from returning and the development of antibiotic resistance.
* **Q: Can I take cefprozil with alcohol?** A:  It's best to avoid alcohol while taking cefprozil, as it may increase the risk of side effects.
* **Q: How should I store cefprozil?** A: Store cefprozil at room temperature, away from moisture and direct sunlight. Keep it out of the reach of children.
* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it is almost time for the next dose.  Do not double the dose.
