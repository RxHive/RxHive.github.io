# Cidofovir

**Generic Name: Cidofovir**

**Usage:**

Cidofovir is an antiviral medication primarily used to treat serious cytomegalovirus (CMV) infections in individuals with compromised immune systems.  This includes people with HIV/AIDS, organ transplant recipients, and those undergoing bone marrow transplants.  CMV is a common virus that usually causes no symptoms in healthy individuals, but can lead to life-threatening complications in people with weakened immunity.  Cidofovir's primary action targets CMV, preventing the virus from replicating and spreading.  While its main use is in treating CMV retinitis (infection of the retina), it can also be used for other serious CMV infections, depending on the severity and the patient's health status. The decision to use cidofovir and its specific application are always made in consultation with a physician.


**Dosage:**

Cidofovir is typically administered intravenously (IV) by a healthcare professional.  There is no oral form. Dosage is highly individualized and determined by factors including the patient's weight, kidney function, the type and severity of the infection, and their overall health.  Generally, adults receive a dose based on their weight, usually ranging from 5 mg/kg to 7.5 mg/kg. Treatment is often given weekly. Pediatric dosing is also weight-based and requires careful monitoring by a physician.  Because cidofovir can be hard on the kidneys,  probenecid is often given concurrently to reduce the amount of cidofovir excreted by the kidneys and thereby minimize kidney damage.  Specific dosage instructions are crucial and must be followed exactly as prescribed by a doctor.


**Side Effects:**

* **Common:** Nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, abdominal pain, headache, fatigue.
* **Less Common but Serious:**  Kidney problems (nephrotoxicity) – this is a significant risk and requires careful monitoring of kidney function; low blood cell counts (leukopenia, neutropenia, thrombocytopenia); increased liver enzyme levels; electrolyte imbalances.
* **Rare but Severe:**  Severe kidney failure requiring dialysis; allergic reactions.

**Note:** Any new or worsening side effects should be reported immediately to a healthcare provider.


**How it Works:**

Cidofovir is a nucleotide analog. This means it mimics a building block of viral DNA. When CMV tries to replicate its DNA, it incorporates cidofovir into the new viral DNA. This prevents further viral replication and stops the virus from spreading.  It effectively inhibits CMV replication, allowing the body's immune system to control the infection.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:**  Cidofovir is contraindicated in individuals with severe kidney impairment.
* **Drug Interactions:**  Cidofovir interacts with several medications, including aminoglycosides (antibiotics), and other nephrotoxic drugs (drugs harmful to the kidneys). Always inform your doctor of all medications you are taking.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  Cidofovir should be used during pregnancy only if the potential benefits clearly outweigh the risks to the fetus.  It is generally not recommended during breastfeeding due to the potential for transfer to the infant.
* **Elderly:**  Elderly patients are more susceptible to kidney damage, so careful monitoring is essential.
* **Children:** Pediatric use requires careful dosage adjustment and close monitoring.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long will I need to take cidofovir?** A: The duration of treatment depends on the severity of the infection and your response to the medication. Your doctor will determine the appropriate treatment length.
* **Q: Can I take other medications while on cidofovir?** A: Always inform your doctor or pharmacist about all medications, supplements, and herbal remedies you are taking.  Some medications can interact with cidofovir.
* **Q: How is cidofovir stored?** A:  Follow the storage instructions provided by your pharmacist.  Typically, it requires refrigeration.
* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Contact your doctor immediately.  Do not double the dose.
* **Q: Is cidofovir safe?** A: Like all medications, cidofovir has potential side effects.  Careful monitoring by your healthcare provider is crucial to minimize risks.
