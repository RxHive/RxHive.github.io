# Dalfampridine

**Generic Name: Dalfampridine**

**Usage:**

Dalfampridine is primarily used to improve walking in adults with multiple sclerosis (MS) who have experienced walking difficulties due to the disease.  Specifically, it targets those experiencing MS-related gait impairment. While its primary use is focused on MS, research is ongoing to explore its potential in other neurological conditions affecting mobility, but these are not currently approved clinical applications.  It's crucial to understand that dalfampridine doesn't cure MS or reverse its progression; it aims to improve the symptoms of impaired walking.

**Dosage:**

Dalfampridine is typically available as an extended-release tablet.  The standard dosage for adults is 10mg taken twice daily, with a 12-hour interval between doses.  Dosage adjustments based on age, weight, or severity are generally not made, but individual responses vary, and a healthcare professional should always guide dosing decisions.  Dalfampridine is not currently formulated for children or available in forms like syrup or injection.  Strict adherence to the prescribed dosage and timing is vital for efficacy and safety.


**Side Effects:**

* **Common Side Effects:** These include dizziness, headache, nausea, and vomiting.  These side effects are usually mild and may resolve over time as the body adjusts to the medication.
* **Less Common but Serious Side Effects:** Seizures and changes in heart rhythm are possible, though rare.  These require immediate medical attention.  Allergic reactions, while uncommon, can also occur.
* **Important Note:**  If you experience any unexpected or concerning side effects, promptly contact your doctor.  Early intervention can help manage or mitigate potential issues.


**How it Works:**

Dalfampridine works by blocking potassium channels in the nerve cells.  In individuals with MS, damage to the myelin sheath (the protective covering around nerve fibers) can disrupt the transmission of nerve impulses.  By blocking these potassium channels, dalfampridine helps to improve the conduction of electrical signals along the nerves, leading to enhanced nerve function and, consequently, improved walking ability.  The exact mechanisms are complex and still being researched.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** Dalfampridine should not be used by individuals with severe kidney problems, a history of seizures, or those taking certain medications that interact with it (see below).
* **Drug Interactions:**  Dalfampridine interacts with several medications, particularly those affecting the kidneys or nervous system.  Always inform your doctor about all medications, supplements, and herbal remedies you are taking.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:** The safety of dalfampridine during pregnancy and breastfeeding is not fully established.  Consult your doctor before taking this medication if you are pregnant, breastfeeding, or planning to become pregnant.
* **Older Adults and Children:** Dalfampridine's use is generally not recommended for children and requires careful consideration for older adults due to increased risk of side effects.  Monitoring is particularly important in these populations.
* **Kidney Function:**  Regular monitoring of kidney function is essential, especially given the risk of kidney-related adverse events.

* **Q: Can I stop taking dalfampridine suddenly?** A: No.  Always follow your doctor's instructions for stopping the medication.  Sudden cessation could potentially lead to withdrawal-like effects.
* **Q: How should I store dalfampridine?** A: Store the medication as directed on the label, typically in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight and moisture.
* **Q:  Is dalfampridine addictive?** A: No, dalfampridine is not considered an addictive substance.
* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A:  Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it's almost time for the next dose. Do not double the dose.
