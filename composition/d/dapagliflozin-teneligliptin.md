# Dapagliflozin + Teneligliptin

**Generic Name: Dapagliflozin + Teneligliptin**

**Usage:**

Dapagliflozin + Teneligliptin is primarily used to treat type 2 diabetes mellitus in adults.  This combination medication works by addressing two different aspects of the disease.  It's particularly helpful for individuals whose blood sugar levels aren't adequately controlled with diet, exercise, and metformin alone, or those who are already taking metformin but require additional help.  While not its primary use, this combination may sometimes be considered as a part of a broader treatment strategy for patients with chronic kidney disease linked to type 2 diabetes.  The specific appropriateness of this combination therapy is always determined by a doctor considering the individual patient's overall health and medical history.

**Dosage:**

The typical adult dose is one tablet once daily, usually taken with or without food. The specific dosage will be determined by your doctor based on your individual needs and response to treatment. There are no established dosage recommendations for children, as this medication is not generally prescribed for pediatric use.  This medication is usually available as a tablet; other forms are not commonly available.  Always follow your doctor's instructions precisely regarding dosage and administration.  Never adjust the dosage yourself.

**Side Effects:**

Common side effects can include:

* Urinary tract infections
* Yeast infections (candidiasis)
* Increased urination
* Dehydration
* Dizziness
* Headache
* Nausea
* Constipation


Less common but serious side effects can include:

* Severe allergic reactions (anaphylaxis)
* Ketoacidosis (a dangerous buildup of ketones in the blood)
*  Leg or foot amputations (increased risk in certain individuals)


If you experience any concerning side effects, contact your doctor immediately.

**How it Works:**

Dapagliflozin is a sodium-glucose cotransporter-2 (SGLT2) inhibitor.  This means it works in the kidneys to prevent the reabsorption of glucose (sugar) into the bloodstream. Instead, excess glucose is excreted in the urine, lowering blood sugar levels. Teneligliptin is a dipeptidyl peptidase-4 (DPP-4) inhibitor.  It enhances the action of incretins, hormones that stimulate the release of insulin and suppress glucagon, another hormone that raises blood sugar.  By working through these two different mechanisms, the combination helps to improve blood sugar control more effectively than either drug alone in many patients.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:**  This medication should not be used by individuals with a known allergy to dapagliflozin, teneligliptin, or any of its components.  It is also generally not recommended for people with severe kidney problems or those with type 1 diabetes.
* **Drug Interactions:**  This medication can interact with other drugs.  It's crucial to inform your doctor about all medications, including over-the-counter drugs, supplements, and herbal remedies, you are currently taking.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  The safety of this medication during pregnancy and breastfeeding hasn't been fully established.  Consult your doctor before using this medication if you are pregnant, breastfeeding, or planning to become pregnant.
* **Older Adults:** Older adults may be more susceptible to certain side effects, particularly dehydration and low blood pressure. Close monitoring is often recommended.
* **Driving and Operating Machinery:** Dizziness is a possible side effect; caution is advised if operating heavy machinery or driving.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: Can I stop taking this medication suddenly?**  A: No, it's essential to talk to your doctor before stopping this medication to avoid potential complications.  They will help you gradually discontinue the medication if necessary.
* **Q: How should I store this medication?**  A: Store it at room temperature, away from moisture and direct sunlight, as instructed on the packaging.
* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it's almost time for your next dose. Don't take a double dose to make up for a missed one.
* **Q: Is this medication suitable for everyone with type 2 diabetes?** A: No. This is a prescription medication, and its suitability depends on individual health conditions and the severity of diabetes. A doctor will determine if this combination is appropriate for you.
