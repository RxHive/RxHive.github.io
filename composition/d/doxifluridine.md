# Doxifluridine

**Generic Name: Doxifluridine**

**Usage:**

Doxifluridine is an anti-cancer medication, specifically an antimetabolite.  Its primary use is in the treatment of certain types of cancer, most notably, cancers of the eye, particularly those affecting the cornea and conjunctiva.  It's often used topically (applied directly to the eye) for these conditions. While it has shown some activity against other cancers in research settings, its use outside of ocular cancers is limited due to its potential for significant side effects when administered systemically (throughout the body).

**Dosage:**

Dosage of doxifluridine varies significantly depending on the specific condition being treated and the method of administration.  There are no standard pediatric or adult dosages applicable across all conditions, as usage is highly specialized to ophthalmic applications. It is crucial to follow the healthcare provider's prescription explicitly.  The medication is typically administered as an ophthalmic solution (eye drops). The exact dosage, frequency, and duration of treatment will be determined by the ophthalmologist based on the patient's individual needs and response to the medication.

**Side Effects:**

Common side effects of topical doxifluridine can include:

* Eye irritation (burning, stinging, redness)
* Temporary blurry vision
* Increased sensitivity to light (photophobia)
* Eye discharge

Less common, but more serious, side effects may include:

* Corneal ulceration
* Eye pain
* Severe allergic reactions (including swelling of the face, lips, or throat)


**Always consult a healthcare professional immediately if you experience any adverse effects.**

**How it Works:**

Doxifluridine is a pyrimidine nucleoside analog. This means it's a synthetic molecule similar to natural building blocks of DNA and RNA. Once applied to the eye, it enters cancer cells and interferes with the synthesis of DNA and RNA, ultimately preventing the cancer cells from replicating and growing.  This targeted disruption of cellular processes leads to the destruction of cancerous tissue.

**Precautions:**

* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  The use of doxifluridine during pregnancy and breastfeeding should be carefully considered and discussed with a healthcare provider.  The potential risks to the fetus or infant need to be weighed against the benefits of treatment.
* **Interactions:** Doxifluridine may interact with other medications.  It's crucial to inform your doctor or pharmacist of all medications you are currently taking, including over-the-counter drugs and herbal supplements.
* **Allergic Reactions:** Individuals with a history of allergies to doxifluridine or similar medications should exercise caution.
* **Liver or Kidney Disease:**  Patients with impaired liver or kidney function may require dosage adjustments or close monitoring.
* **Contact Lenses:**  Contact lenses should be removed before applying doxifluridine eye drops and not reinserted until at least 15 minutes afterward.
