**Usage:**

* **Diltiazem:** This calcium channel blocker primarily treats hypertension (high blood pressure) and angina (chest pain due to reduced blood flow to the heart).  It's also sometimes used to control certain types of abnormal heart rhythms (arrhythmias) and prevent migraines.

* **Lidocaine:** This is a local anesthetic primarily used to numb a specific area of the body, often before minor surgical procedures or other painful medical interventions. It can also be administered intravenously to treat certain life-threatening heart rhythm abnormalities (ventricular arrhythmias).

* **Lidocaine:** Available in topical creams, gels, injections, and patches. Dosage is highly variable depending on the route of administration and intended use.

**Side Effects:**

**Diltiazem:**

* Common: Headache, dizziness, nausea, edema (swelling), fatigue.
* Less Common but Serious:  Slow heart rate (bradycardia), low blood pressure (hypotension), heart block, liver problems.

**Lidocaine:**

* Common:  Drowsiness, dizziness, nausea, vomiting.
* Less Common but Serious: Allergic reactions (rare but potentially life-threatening), changes in heart rhythm, neurological effects (e.g., confusion, seizures).


**How it Works:**

* **Diltiazem:** Relaxes blood vessels by blocking calcium channels in the heart and blood vessel walls, leading to reduced blood pressure and improved blood flow.

* **Lidocaine:** Blocks nerve impulses, preventing the transmission of pain signals and temporarily numbing the affected area.  In its intravenous form, it stabilizes the heart's electrical activity.

**Precautions:**

* **Diltiazem:** Should be used cautiously in patients with heart block, low blood pressure, or liver disease.  It can interact with many other drugs.  Caution is advised during pregnancy and breastfeeding.

* **Lidocaine:**  Allergic reactions are possible.  Patients with heart conditions or liver/kidney disease require special monitoring.  Caution is advised during pregnancy and breastfeeding.
