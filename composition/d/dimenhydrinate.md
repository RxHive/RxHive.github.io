# Dimenhydrinate

**Generic Name: Dimenhydrinate**

**Usage:**

Dimenhydrinate is primarily used to treat and prevent nausea and vomiting.  This makes it helpful in various situations, including motion sickness (whether from cars, boats, airplanes, or other forms of travel),  post-operative nausea and vomiting, and nausea associated with labyrinthitis (inner ear inflammation). It's also sometimes used to relieve the dizziness and vertigo associated with inner ear problems.  While not its primary use, dimenhydrinate possesses mild antihistamine properties and can provide relief from some allergy symptoms, though other antihistamines are generally preferred for this purpose.  The use of dimenhydrinate for allergy symptoms is less common than its use for nausea and vomiting.


**Dosage:**

Dosage varies significantly based on age, weight, and the specific condition being treated.  Always follow your doctor's instructions carefully.  A physician will determine the appropriate dose for your needs.  Generally, though:

* **Adults:**  For nausea and vomiting, the typical adult dose is 50-100 mg every 4-6 hours as needed, not to exceed 400 mg in 24 hours.
* **Children:** Dosage for children is strictly determined by their weight and age, usually calculated by a physician based on milligrams per kilogram of body weight.  It's crucial to never administer medication to a child without consulting a doctor or pharmacist first.

Dimenhydrinate is available in various forms, including tablets, capsules, syrups (especially for children), and injectable solutions (primarily for hospital use).  Follow the administration instructions printed on the medication packaging or as directed by your healthcare provider.


**Side Effects:**

* **Common:** Drowsiness, dry mouth, blurred vision, constipation, dizziness.
* **Less Common but Serious:**  Fast or irregular heartbeat, difficulty urinating, seizures (rare but possible, particularly at high doses).  Allergic reactions, such as skin rash, itching, or swelling, are also possible.

If you experience any side effects, particularly those that are severe or persistent, consult your doctor or pharmacist immediately.


**How it Works:**

Dimenhydrinate is an antihistamine and anticholinergic. It works by blocking the action of acetylcholine and histamine in the brain, specifically in areas that control nausea and vomiting.  Acetylcholine and histamine play roles in the central nervous system's response to stimuli that trigger nausea. By blocking their action, dimenhydrinate helps reduce the signals that cause nausea and vomiting.  Its anticholinergic effect also helps to reduce the symptoms of vertigo and dizziness.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** Dimenhydrinate should not be used by individuals with certain conditions, such as narrow-angle glaucoma, severe lung disease, or a history of allergic reactions to dimenhydrinate or similar medications.  Pregnant and breastfeeding women should only use dimenhydrinate under strict medical supervision due to potential risks to the fetus or infant.
* **Drug Interactions:** Dimenhydrinate can interact with various medications, including alcohol, sedatives, and other central nervous system depressants.  It's essential to inform your doctor of all medications (including over-the-counter drugs and herbal supplements) you are taking before starting dimenhydrinate.
* **Elderly and Children:**  Elderly individuals and young children are more susceptible to the side effects of dimenhydrinate, especially drowsiness and dizziness. Careful monitoring and dose adjustments are often necessary.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: Can I take dimenhydrinate with alcohol?** A: No. Combining dimenhydrinate with alcohol can significantly increase drowsiness and other side effects.
* **Q: How should I store dimenhydrinate?** A: Store it at room temperature, away from moisture and direct sunlight. Keep it out of reach of children.
* **Q: How long does it take to work?** A:  The onset of effect varies, but relief from nausea and vomiting usually occurs within 30-60 minutes.
* **Q:  Is dimenhydrinate habit-forming?** A: No, dimenhydrinate is not considered habit-forming.
* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it is close to the time for your next dose.  Do not double up on doses.
