# Dimethyl fumarate

**Generic Name:** Dimethyl fumarate

**Usage:**

Dimethyl fumarate is primarily used to treat relapsing-remitting multiple sclerosis (RRMS).  This is a form of MS where periods of disease activity (relapses) are followed by periods of remission, where symptoms improve or disappear.  It's approved to reduce the frequency of relapses and slow the progression of disability in adults with RRMS.  While its primary use is in MS, research is ongoing to explore its potential in other autoimmune diseases, though these are not currently standard uses.

**Dosage:**

Dosage and administration of dimethyl fumarate varies depending on the specific brand and formulation.  Generally, it's available as oral capsules.  A healthcare professional will determine the appropriate starting dose and adjust it as needed based on individual response and tolerability.  It’s crucial to follow the prescribed dosage and schedule meticulously.  There are no established pediatric dosage guidelines, and use in children is not currently approved.  Detailed instructions will be provided by the prescribing physician, and the medication packaging will also contain critical information.

**Side Effects:**

* **Common Side Effects:** Flushing (redness of the face, neck, and chest), nausea, diarrhea, stomach upset.
* **Less Common, but Serious Side Effects:**  Lymphopenia (low white blood cell count),  hepatotoxicity (liver damage),  progressive multifocal leukoencephalopathy (PML - a rare and serious brain infection), allergic reactions.
* **Important Note:** If you experience any unusual or concerning side effects, contact your healthcare provider immediately.  This is not an exhaustive list, and individual reactions can vary.

**How it Works:**

Dimethyl fumarate is thought to work by modulating the immune system.  It reduces the activity of immune cells that contribute to the inflammation and damage characteristic of MS and possibly other autoimmune conditions.  The exact mechanisms are still being fully elucidated, but it's believed to influence several pathways involved in immune cell activation and migration. This reduction in immune activity helps to lessen the frequency of relapses and slow the progression of the disease.

**Popular Brand Names (India):**

Specific brand names vary by region and manufacturer. It's essential to consult with your physician or pharmacist for accurate local availability.  The generic form is also widely available and may be prescribed.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** Dimethyl fumarate should not be used in individuals with known hypersensitivity (allergic reaction) to the drug or any of its components.
* **Drug Interactions:**  Dimethyl fumarate may interact with other medications.  It is crucial to inform your doctor about all medications, including over-the-counter drugs and supplements, you are taking.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:** Dimethyl fumarate is not recommended during pregnancy or breastfeeding unless the potential benefits significantly outweigh the risks.
* **Older Adults and Children:**  The use in children is not currently approved, and caution is advised in older adults due to potential increased sensitivity to side effects.  Dosage adjustments may be necessary based on individual health status.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long does it take to see results?** A: The time it takes to see benefits varies between individuals, but improvements in MS symptoms may not be immediately noticeable.  Consistent use as directed is crucial.
* **Q: Can I stop taking it abruptly?** A: No, always follow your physician's instructions for tapering off the medication to avoid potential complications. Stopping abruptly is not recommended.
* **Q: How should I store it?** A: Store as directed on the packaging label, typically in a cool, dry place, away from direct sunlight and moisture.
* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A:  Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it's almost time for your next dose.  Never double up on doses.
* **Q:  Is dimethyl fumarate addictive?** A: No, dimethyl fumarate is not considered an addictive substance.
