**Side Effects:**

The combination of dicyclomine and dextropropoxyphene carried a significant risk of side effects, including:

* **Common:**  Drowsiness, dizziness, constipation, dry mouth, blurred vision, nausea.
* **Less Common but Serious:**  Rapid heart rate (tachycardia), respiratory depression, allergic reactions (rash, itching, swelling), liver damage, seizures.
* **Dextropropoxyphene-Specific Risks:**  Dextropropoxyphene carries a significant risk of overdose and serious heart problems, including potentially fatal cardiac arrhythmias.

**If you experience any adverse effects, stop taking the medication and consult a healthcare provider immediately.**


**How it Works:**

Dicyclomine is an anticholinergic agent. It works by blocking the action of acetylcholine, a neurotransmitter that causes muscle contractions in the gastrointestinal tract.  This reduces spasms and cramping.  Dextropropoxyphene is an opioid analgesic; it works by binding to opioid receptors in the brain and spinal cord, reducing the perception of pain.  This combination attempted to address both the pain and the muscle spasms associated with certain gastrointestinal conditions.
