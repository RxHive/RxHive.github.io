# Diloxanide + Tinidazole

**Generic Name:** Diloxanide + Tinidazole

**Usage:**

Diloxanide furoate and tinidazole are a combination medication primarily used to treat intestinal infections caused by amoebae, specifically *Entamoeba histolytica*.  This combination tackles both the intestinal and extra-intestinal stages of amoebiasis. Diloxanide furoate primarily acts on the intestinal phase, while tinidazole targets both intestinal and extra-intestinal (liver) forms of the infection.  While the primary use is amoebiasis, this combination may sometimes be considered for other parasitic infections depending on the specific guidelines and expert opinion of a healthcare professional.  It's crucial to remember this medication should only be used under the direct supervision of a doctor who can diagnose the infection accurately.

**Dosage:**

Dosage varies depending on age and weight, and always needs to be prescribed by a physician.  There isn't a universally standardized pediatric formulation. Adult dosages typically involve taking one or two tablets, typically twice a day, for a specific duration (usually 10 days),  but this entirely depends on the doctor's assessment.  The medication is usually available in tablet form.  It's essential to follow the prescribed dosage and duration meticulously; do not adjust the dosage without consulting a doctor.  Never self-medicate.  Liquid formulations for children might be available in certain markets, again, only as prescribed by a medical professional.

**Side Effects:**

Common side effects can include:

* Nausea
* Vomiting
* Diarrhea
* Abdominal pain
* Metallic taste in the mouth
* Headache
* Dizziness
* Mild allergic reactions (rash, itching)

Less common but serious side effects may include:

* Severe allergic reactions (anaphylaxis)
* Blood disorders
* Liver damage
* Peripheral neuropathy (nerve damage)

If any adverse effects occur, immediately seek medical advice.


**How it Works:**

Diloxanide furoate is an amoebicide that works by interfering with the metabolism of *Entamoeba histolytica* within the intestines, leading to its death and elimination from the body. Tinidazole is a nitroimidazole antibiotic with a broader spectrum of activity. It enters the amoeba cells and disrupts their DNA, ultimately leading to their destruction. This combined action ensures both the intestinal and potentially the extra-intestinal stages of amoebiasis are addressed.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** This medication is contraindicated in individuals with known hypersensitivity to diloxanide furoate, tinidazole, or other nitroimidazoles.
* **Drug Interactions:**  Consult your doctor about any other medications you are currently taking, including over-the-counter drugs and herbal supplements, as interactions are possible.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:** The use during pregnancy and breastfeeding requires careful consideration and should only be undertaken under strict medical supervision, due to potential risks to the fetus or infant.
* **Liver and Kidney Impairment:** Patients with impaired liver or kidney function may require dosage adjustments or close monitoring.
* **Alcohol Consumption:**  Avoid alcohol consumption during treatment and for several days after, as it can exacerbate side effects.
* **Elderly Patients:**  Older adults may be more susceptible to side effects and require careful monitoring.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: Can I take this medication if I have other health conditions?**  A:  Always inform your doctor about all your health conditions before starting this medication.
* **Q: How should I store this medication?** A: Store at room temperature, away from moisture and direct sunlight. Keep out of reach of children.
* **Q: How long does it take to work?** A: The effects may not be immediately noticeable, and full resolution of the infection may take some time, as directed by your doctor.
* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it's almost time for the next dose. Don't double the dose.
* **Q: Is this medication safe for children?** A:  Pediatric use requires strict medical supervision and appropriate dosage adjustments, and is not always recommended.
