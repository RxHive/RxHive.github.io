# Dimercaprol

**Generic Name: Dimercaprol**

**Usage:**

Dimercaprol is an antidote, meaning it's used to counteract the effects of certain poisons.  Its primary use is in treating severe poisoning from arsenic, mercury, and gold.  These heavy metals can disrupt many bodily functions if they accumulate to toxic levels. Dimercaprol works by binding to these metals, forming complexes that are then excreted from the body, effectively removing the toxins.  While its primary application is in acute heavy metal poisoning, it may also be considered in cases of less severe exposure, depending on the specifics of the situation and medical advice. The decision to use dimercaprol, and the dosage, is always made on a case-by-case basis by a healthcare professional.


**Dosage:**

Dimercaprol is administered intramuscularly (IM) – injected into a muscle – because oral administration is ineffective. Dosage is highly individualized based on the severity of poisoning, the patient's weight, and age.  A healthcare professional carefully calculates the dose.  There is no standardized dosage for children or adults that can be presented here.  The exact regimen, including frequency and total duration of treatment, is determined by the treating physician, who monitors the patient's response closely.


**Side Effects:**

* **Common:**  Nausea, vomiting, increased blood pressure, elevated heart rate, headache, burning sensation at the injection site, fever, and pain.
* **Less Common but Serious:**  Increased liver enzyme levels (indicating potential liver damage), kidney problems, decreased blood cell counts, and allergic reactions.

**Important Note:**  If you experience any adverse effects, especially those listed as serious, contact your doctor immediately.


**How it Works:**

Dimercaprol contains sulfhydryl (–SH) groups which have a strong affinity for certain heavy metals like arsenic, mercury, and gold.  These metals normally bind to proteins and other molecules in the body, disrupting their function.  Dimercaprol effectively competes with these cellular components, binding to the heavy metals and forming less toxic complexes. These metal-dimercaprol complexes are then more readily excreted from the body via the urine and feces, reducing the overall toxicity.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** Dimercaprol is contraindicated in patients with known hypersensitivity to the drug.
* **Drug Interactions:**  Interactions with other medications are possible; a physician should be aware of all medications a patient is taking.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  Use during pregnancy and breastfeeding should be carefully weighed against the risks and benefits by a healthcare professional. The potential harm to the fetus or infant must be considered.
* **Elderly and Children:**  Dose adjustments may be necessary in older adults and children due to variations in metabolism and organ function. Careful monitoring is crucial in these populations.
* **Liver and Kidney Function:** Pre-existing liver or kidney disease may necessitate cautious use and close monitoring as these organs are involved in the elimination of dimercaprol and the metal-dimercaprol complexes.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: How is dimercaprol stored?** A:  Store as directed by the physician or pharmacist, usually in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight and moisture.  Specific instructions are usually provided with the medication.
* **Q: Is dimercaprol a cure for heavy metal poisoning?** A: It's an antidote that helps remove heavy metals, but it doesn't reverse all the damage that may have already occurred.  Treatment depends on the severity of the poisoning.
* **Q:  Can I take dimercaprol without a doctor's prescription?** A:  Absolutely not. Dimercaprol is a potent medication used only under strict medical supervision.  Self-medication can be extremely dangerous.
* **Q: What if I miss a dose?** A:  Never attempt to adjust the dosage yourself; contact your doctor or the prescribing healthcare provider immediately to get guidance.
