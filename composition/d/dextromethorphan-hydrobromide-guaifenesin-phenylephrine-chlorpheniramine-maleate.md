# Dextromethorphan Hydrobromide + Guaifenesin + Phenylephrine + Chlorpheniramine Maleate

**Generic Name:** Dextromethorphan Hydrobromide + Guaifenesin + Phenylephrine + Chlorpheniramine Maleate

**Usage:** This combination medication is typically used to relieve symptoms associated with the common cold or flu, particularly those involving a cough, congestion, and runny nose.  The four active ingredients work together to target different aspects of these symptoms:

* **Dextromethorphan Hydrobromide:** This is a cough suppressant, meaning it helps to reduce the urge to cough. It's effective against both dry and wet coughs.
* **Guaifenesin:** This is an expectorant, assisting the body in thinning and clearing mucus from the airways. This helps relieve chest congestion.
* **Phenylephrine:** This is a decongestant that helps shrink swollen blood vessels in the nasal passages, relieving nasal congestion and sinus pressure.
* **Chlorpheniramine Maleate:** This is an antihistamine that helps reduce runny nose and sneezing associated with allergies or the common cold.


This combination is primarily used for symptomatic relief of these upper respiratory symptoms, not to cure the underlying illness itself.  It's important to understand that this medication treats the *symptoms*, not the *cause* of the cold or flu.


**Dosage:**  Dosage varies significantly depending on the specific formulation (tablet, syrup, etc.), the manufacturer, and the age and weight of the patient.  **Always follow the instructions on the product label or as directed by a doctor or pharmacist.**  Never exceed the recommended dose.  Self-medicating children should be done only under the supervision of a medical professional.  Specific dosage instructions will be provided on the product packaging.  Adult and pediatric dosages are not standardized and vary across products.


**Side Effects:**  Common side effects may include:

* Drowsiness
* Dizziness
* Dry mouth
* Headache
* Nausea
* Constipation
* Upset stomach


Less common, but potentially serious side effects may include:

* Allergic reactions (rash, itching, swelling, difficulty breathing)
* Rapid heartbeat
* High blood pressure
* Seizures (rare, but more likely with higher doses or pre-existing conditions)


If you experience any unusual or concerning side effects, stop taking the medication and consult a healthcare professional immediately.


**How it Works:**  As mentioned above, each component of this combination medication works differently:

* **Dextromethorphan:** Affects the cough center in the brain, reducing the cough reflex.
* **Guaifenesin:** Increases the fluid content of respiratory tract secretions, making them easier to expel.
* **Phenylephrine:** Acts on alpha-adrenergic receptors in blood vessels, causing vasoconstriction and reducing nasal swelling.
* **Chlorpheniramine:** Blocks histamine receptors, reducing the effects of histamine on the body, thereby relieving allergy symptoms and reducing mucus production.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** This medication should not be taken by individuals with certain pre-existing conditions, such as glaucoma, heart disease, high blood pressure, thyroid problems, or prostate enlargement.  Pregnant or breastfeeding women should consult a doctor before use.
* **Drug Interactions:** This combination can interact with other medications, including MAO inhibitors, certain antidepressants, and other cold or allergy medications.  Inform your doctor or pharmacist about all medications you are taking.
* **Alcohol:** Avoid alcohol consumption while using this medication, as it can enhance drowsiness and other side effects.
* **Driving/Operating Machinery:** Drowsiness is a common side effect; avoid driving or operating machinery until you know how this medication affects you.
* **Elderly and Children:**  Use caution with elderly patients and young children, as they may be more susceptible to side effects.  Dosage adjustments might be necessary.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long can I take this medication?**  A:  Generally, this medication should only be used for a short period (e.g., 7-10 days).  If symptoms persist or worsen, consult a doctor.
* **Q: Can I take this with other medications?** A:  Always inform your doctor or pharmacist about all medications, supplements, or herbal remedies you are taking to avoid potential interactions.
* **Q: How should I store this medication?** A: Store as directed on the product label, typically at room temperature, away from moisture and heat.
* **Q: Is this medication safe for pregnant or breastfeeding women?** A:  Consult your doctor before taking this medication if you are pregnant or breastfeeding.
* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A:  Do not take a double dose to make up for a missed one.  Take the next dose at the regular scheduled time.
