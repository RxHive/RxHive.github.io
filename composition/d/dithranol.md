# Dithranol

**Generic Name: Dithranol**

**Usage:**

Dithranol is a topical medication primarily used to treat psoriasis, a chronic skin condition characterized by red, scaly patches.  It's particularly effective for plaque psoriasis, the most common type.  While its primary use is in psoriasis management, it may also be considered for other inflammatory skin conditions, although this is less common and usually only under the direct supervision of a dermatologist.  Its effectiveness varies depending on the severity and location of the affected skin.  It's important to note that dithranol is a potent medication, and its use should always be guided by a healthcare professional.

**Dosage:**

Dithranol is available in various forms including ointments, creams, and pastes, with concentrations ranging from 0.1% to 1%.  Dosage and application frequency are highly individualized and determined by a doctor based on the severity of the psoriasis, the patient's skin sensitivity, and their response to treatment.  Generally, treatment begins with low concentrations and short contact times (e.g., a few minutes initially), gradually increasing both as tolerated.  There are no established pediatric dosages; use in children is generally discouraged due to the risk of irritation.  Specific application instructions—such as whether to apply a thin layer to affected areas, or to use an occlusive dressing—will be provided by a physician.

**Side Effects:**

* **Common:** Skin irritation (redness, burning, itching), staining of skin and clothing (a characteristic reddish-brown discoloration), dryness, peeling.
* **Less Common but Serious:** Severe skin irritation, blistering, inflammation.  Allergic reactions are possible, although rare.

**Note:**  Any significant skin irritation or allergic reactions require immediate medical attention.  Do not continue using dithranol if you experience severe side effects.

**How it Works:**

Dithranol's exact mechanism of action isn't fully understood, but it's believed to work by slowing down the rapid growth of skin cells characteristic of psoriasis.  It also has anti-inflammatory properties, helping to reduce inflammation and redness associated with the condition.  It achieves this by influencing the activity of specific enzymes and cells within the skin.  The medication's effectiveness is largely attributed to its ability to modulate cell turnover and inflammation in psoriatic lesions.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** Dithranol should not be used on broken or infected skin.  Individuals with severe kidney or liver disease should exercise caution.
* **Drug Interactions:**  While major drug interactions are uncommon, inform your doctor about all medications you are taking, including over-the-counter drugs and herbal remedies.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  Use during pregnancy and breastfeeding should be avoided unless the benefits clearly outweigh the potential risks, and only under the strict supervision of a physician.
* **Children and Older Adults:** Use in children is generally discouraged due to increased risk of skin irritation. Older adults may have more sensitive skin and require close monitoring.

**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long does it take to see results?** A:  The time to see improvement varies, but some noticeable effects might be seen within a few weeks with consistent use.
* **Q: Can I use dithranol on my face?** A:  Generally, it's not recommended for the face due to its potential for strong irritation on more sensitive skin.
* **Q: How should I store dithranol?** A: Store at room temperature, away from direct sunlight and moisture.  Keep out of reach of children.
* **Q: What should I do if I stain my clothes?** A:  Staining is a common side effect; it is very difficult to remove stains from fabrics, so protect clothing while applying the medication.
