# Dasatinib

**Generic Name: Dasatinib**

**Usage:**

Dasatinib is a targeted therapy primarily used to treat certain types of chronic myeloid leukemia (CML) and Philadelphia chromosome-positive acute lymphoblastic leukemia (Ph+ ALL).  CML is a cancer of the blood-forming cells in the bone marrow.  Ph+ ALL is a similar type of leukemia characterized by a specific genetic abnormality. Dasatinib works by targeting a specific protein, BCR-ABL tyrosine kinase, which is abnormally active in these cancers and drives their growth.  While its primary use is in these leukemias, it may also be used in some cases of myelofibrosis, a disorder affecting bone marrow and blood cell production.  The specific use and appropriateness of dasatinib are always determined by a doctor based on individual patient factors and disease characteristics.


**Dosage:**

Dasatinib dosage is highly individualized and determined by a healthcare professional.  It's typically administered orally as a tablet.  There's no standard pediatric dosage readily available in generalized literature; it's entirely dependent on the child's specific condition, weight, and response to treatment.  The adult dosage also varies considerably based on the specific condition being treated and the patient's response.   It’s crucial to strictly follow the prescribed dosage and schedule provided by your physician.  Changes in dosage should only be made under medical supervision.  Self-adjustment is dangerous and ineffective.


**Side Effects:**

Common side effects can include:

* Fluid retention (edema)
* Nausea
* Diarrhea
* Fatigue
* Headaches
* Muscle or joint pain (myalgia and arthralgia)
* Rash

Less common but serious side effects may include:

* Liver problems (hepatotoxicity)
* Heart problems (cardiomyopathy)
* Pleural effusion (fluid buildup around the lungs)
* Severe allergic reactions

If you experience any adverse effects, particularly if they are severe or persistent,  seek immediate medical attention.


**How it Works:**

Dasatinib works by inhibiting the activity of the BCR-ABL tyrosine kinase enzyme.  This enzyme is abnormally active in CML and Ph+ ALL, leading to uncontrolled cell growth. By blocking this enzyme's activity, dasatinib prevents the cancer cells from proliferating and helps to control the disease.  This is a targeted approach, meaning it primarily affects cancer cells while minimizing impact on healthy cells, though some side effects can occur as a consequence of the drug's mechanism of action.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** Dasatinib is contraindicated in patients with known hypersensitivity to the drug.
* **Drug Interactions:** Dasatinib can interact with other medications, including certain antifungals, and some medications that impact the liver's function.  Always inform your doctor about all medications, supplements, and herbal remedies you are taking.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:** Dasatinib should be avoided during pregnancy and breastfeeding, as it may harm the developing fetus or infant. Effective contraception is essential.
* **Older Adults and Children:**  Special caution is required when administering dasatinib to older adults and children due to potential increased sensitivity to side effects.  Dosage adjustments may be necessary.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long will I need to take dasatinib?**  A: The duration of dasatinib treatment depends on your specific condition, response to therapy, and your doctor's assessment. It can range from months to years.

* **Q: How should I store dasatinib?** A: Store dasatinib tablets at room temperature, away from moisture and heat, as directed on the label.

* **Q: Can I stop taking dasatinib if I feel better?** A: No.  Stopping treatment prematurely can lead to disease progression.  Always follow your doctor's instructions regarding medication.

* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: If you miss a dose, take it as soon as you remember unless it is almost time for the next dose. Don't take a double dose to make up for a missed dose.
