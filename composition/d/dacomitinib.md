# Dacomitinib

**Generic Name: Dacomitinib**

**Usage:**

Dacomitinib is a targeted therapy primarily used in the treatment of non-small cell lung cancer (NSCLC).  Specifically, it's indicated for patients with metastatic NSCLC whose tumors have a specific mutation in the epidermal growth factor receptor (EGFR), known as exon 19 deletions or exon 21 (L858R) mutations.  These mutations drive the growth of the cancer cells.  Dacomitinib's primary role is to block the action of EGFR, thereby slowing or stopping the cancer's progression. While its primary use is in metastatic NSCLC, research continues to explore its potential in other types of cancer and disease contexts.


**Dosage:**

The dosage of dacomitinib is determined by a physician based on individual patient factors.  It's typically administered orally as a tablet.  For adults with NSCLC, a common starting dose is 45 mg once daily.  Dosage adjustments may be necessary based on individual tolerance and response to treatment, as well as other health considerations.  There is no established pediatric dosage as the medication is primarily used to treat adult cancers.  The medication should only be taken as prescribed by a healthcare professional;  self-adjustment of dosage is strongly discouraged.


**Side Effects:**

Dacomitinib, like many cancer treatments, can cause side effects.  Common side effects include:

* Diarrhea
* Nausea
* Vomiting
* Fatigue
* Rash
* Dry skin
* Nail changes (e.g., discoloration, thickening)
* Mouth sores (stomatitis)

Less common, but serious side effects can also occur, including:

* Interstitial lung disease (ILD) – a serious lung condition requiring immediate medical attention.
* Severe diarrhea leading to dehydration.
* Heart problems (arrhythmias).
* Liver damage.

It's crucial to report any new or worsening symptoms to a doctor immediately.  Prompt medical attention can help manage or mitigate side effects and prevent serious complications.


**How it Works:**

Dacomitinib works by selectively inhibiting the epidermal growth factor receptor (EGFR) tyrosine kinase.  EGFR is a protein on the surface of certain cells, including cancer cells, that plays a key role in cell growth and division.  By blocking EGFR, dacomitinib prevents the signals that tell cancer cells to grow and multiply.  This targeted approach helps limit damage to healthy cells compared to chemotherapy which affects a broader range of cells.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** Dacomitinib is contraindicated in patients with a known hypersensitivity to the drug or any of its components.
* **Drug Interactions:**  Dacomitinib can interact with other medications.  It's vital to inform your doctor about all medications, supplements, and herbal remedies you are taking.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:** Dacomitinib is not recommended during pregnancy or breastfeeding.  Women of childbearing potential should use effective contraception during treatment.
* **Elderly Patients:** Older adults may be more susceptible to certain side effects.  Careful monitoring is necessary.
* **Liver and Kidney Impairment:** Dosage adjustments may be required in patients with liver or kidney impairment.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long do I need to take dacomitinib?** A: The duration of treatment depends on individual response and is determined by your oncologist.
* **Q: Can I drink alcohol while taking dacomitinib?** A: It's best to avoid alcohol or limit alcohol consumption while taking this medication, as it can worsen some side effects, especially nausea and vomiting. Discuss with your physician.
* **Q: How should I store dacomitinib?** A: Store tablets in a cool, dry place, away from moisture and direct sunlight, as directed on the medication label.
* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: If you miss a dose, take it as soon as you remember unless it's almost time for the next dose.  Never double the dose.
* **Q: Are there any long-term effects?** A:  Long-term effects are possible with any cancer treatment and should be discussed with your oncologist.  Regular monitoring is vital.
