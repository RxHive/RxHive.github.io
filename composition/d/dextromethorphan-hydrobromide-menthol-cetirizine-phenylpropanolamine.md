# Dextromethorphan Hydrobromide + Menthol + Cetirizine + Phenylpropanolamine

**Generic Name:** Dextromethorphan Hydrobromide + Menthol + Cetirizine + Phenylpropanolamine

**Usage:** This combination of medications is not typically found as a single, fixed-dose formulation.  Each ingredient addresses different symptoms, often associated with the common cold or flu.  Let's break down the individual components and their roles:

* **Dextromethorphan Hydrobromide:** A cough suppressant (antitussive). It works on the cough center in the brain to reduce the urge to cough.  Its primary use is for the relief of non-productive coughs (coughs not producing mucus).

* **Menthol:** A topical analgesic and counterirritant. It provides a cooling sensation, which can temporarily relieve nasal congestion and minor aches and pains associated with colds and the flu.  It does not directly treat the underlying illness.

* **Cetirizine:** An antihistamine. It blocks the action of histamine, a substance released by the body during allergic reactions. In this combination, it's likely included to relieve symptoms like runny nose, sneezing, and itchy eyes associated with allergies or cold symptoms.

* **Phenylpropanolamine:** A decongestant (sympathomimetic amine). It works by narrowing blood vessels in the nasal passages, reducing swelling and congestion.  **Importantly, phenylpropanolamine has been largely removed from over-the-counter medications in many countries due to concerns about its cardiovascular effects.**  Its presence in this combination suggests an older or less commonly available formulation.
