# Dalteparin

**Generic Name: Dalteparin**

**Usage:**

Dalteparin is a low-molecular-weight heparin (LMWH).  Its primary use is in the prevention and treatment of blood clots (thrombosis).  This includes:

* **Deep Vein Thrombosis (DVT):**  This is a blood clot that forms in a deep vein, usually in the leg. Dalteparin helps prevent these clots from forming and growing, reducing the risk of serious complications like pulmonary embolism (PE).
* **Pulmonary Embolism (PE):**  This is a life-threatening condition where a blood clot travels to the lungs. Dalteparin is used to treat PE and prevent further clots.
* **Prevention of Blood Clots after Surgery:**  Patients undergoing major surgery, particularly orthopedic procedures like hip or knee replacement, are at higher risk of developing DVT. Dalteparin is frequently prescribed to prevent this.
* **Treatment of Unstable Angina and Non-ST-Segment Elevation Myocardial Infarction (NSTEMI):** In certain situations, dalteparin may be used in conjunction with other treatments to manage these heart conditions. This is a secondary use and the specifics would be determined by a cardiologist.


**Dosage:**

Dalteparin is administered via injection, typically subcutaneous (under the skin).  Dosage is determined by a healthcare professional and varies significantly depending on the patient's weight, specific condition, and response to treatment.  There is no standard pediatric dosage that can be provided here;  dosing for children requires careful calculation and monitoring by a physician.  Adult dosages usually range from a few thousand units to tens of thousands of units once or twice daily, as directed by a doctor.  The specific dosage, frequency, and duration of treatment are crucial and should always be determined and overseen by a medical professional.


**Side Effects:**

* **Common:** Bruising at the injection site, bleeding (nosebleeds, gum bleeding), mild pain at the injection site.
* **Less Common but Serious:**  Severe bleeding (internal bleeding, gastrointestinal bleeding), thrombocytopenia (low platelet count – a condition that increases bleeding risk), allergic reactions (rash, itching, swelling, difficulty breathing).
* **Important Note:** Any unusual bleeding, bruising, or allergic reaction should be reported to a doctor immediately.


**How it Works:**

Dalteparin works by inhibiting the activity of certain clotting factors in the blood.  This prevents the formation and growth of blood clots.  It's a type of anticoagulant, meaning it thins the blood, reducing its tendency to clot.  The specific mechanisms involve its interaction with antithrombin III, a natural blood protein that inhibits clotting.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** Dalteparin should not be used in individuals with a history of serious allergic reactions to heparin or other similar medications, uncontrolled bleeding, or certain types of bleeding disorders.
* **Drug Interactions:**  Many medications can interact with dalteparin, including some pain relievers, aspirin, and other anticoagulants.  It's essential to inform your doctor about all medications, supplements, and herbal remedies you are taking.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  The use of dalteparin during pregnancy and breastfeeding should be carefully considered and monitored by a doctor, as it may carry risks.
* **Older Adults and Children:**  Older adults and children may require adjusted dosages and close monitoring due to increased sensitivity or different metabolic rates.
