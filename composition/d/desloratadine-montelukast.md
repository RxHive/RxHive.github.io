# Desloratadine + Montelukast

**Generic Name:** Desloratadine + Montelukast

**Usage:**

Desloratadine + Montelukast is a combination medication used primarily to relieve symptoms of allergic rhinitis (hay fever) and asthma.  Allergic rhinitis causes symptoms like sneezing, runny nose, itchy eyes and nose, and congestion.  Asthma involves inflammation and narrowing of the airways in the lungs, leading to wheezing, coughing, shortness of breath, and chest tightness. This combination medication tackles both the allergic inflammation (desloratadine) and the airway constriction (montelukast) that contribute to these conditions. While its primary use is for symptom relief, it's crucial to understand that it doesn't cure the underlying conditions.  In some cases, it may be prescribed for other allergic conditions as determined by a doctor.

**Dosage:**

Dosage varies significantly depending on age and the specific formulation (tablet, syrup).  A doctor will determine the appropriate dosage based on individual needs and should always be consulted before starting or altering the medication.  

* **Adults:**  Dosage information will be provided on the prescription label and usually involves taking one tablet daily.
* **Children:** Dosage for children is highly individualized based on weight and age and will be prescribed by a pediatrician.  Syrup forms are often used for children.
* **Administration:**  Tablets are typically swallowed whole with water, and syrup should be administered as directed on the label, often with a measuring device to ensure accurate dosing.

**Side Effects:**

Common side effects are generally mild and may include:

* Headache
* Dizziness
* Drowsiness
* Nausea
* Upset stomach
* Diarrhea
* Fatigue

Less common but more serious side effects may include:

* Allergic reactions (rash, hives, itching, difficulty breathing, swelling of the face, lips, or tongue)
* Fast heartbeat
* Changes in mood or behavior
* Sleep disturbances
* Liver problems


If you experience any concerning side effects, discontinue use and consult a healthcare professional immediately.

**How it Works:**

Desloratadine is an antihistamine that blocks the action of histamine, a chemical released by the body during an allergic reaction. By blocking histamine, desloratadine reduces the symptoms associated with allergic inflammation like sneezing, itching, and runny nose.  Montelukast is a leukotriene receptor antagonist.  Leukotrienes are substances that cause inflammation and airway constriction in the lungs and contribute to asthma and allergic rhinitis symptoms. By blocking leukotrienes, montelukast helps relax the airways and reduce inflammation. This combined action effectively manages both the allergic inflammation and airway constriction responsible for the symptoms.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** This medication is not suitable for everyone.  Individuals with known allergies to desloratadine, montelukast, or any of the inactive ingredients should avoid it.  Consult a healthcare professional about any pre-existing conditions, especially liver or kidney disease.
* **Drug Interactions:** This medication can interact with other drugs. It is crucial to inform your doctor of all medications, supplements, and herbal remedies you are taking.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  Consult your doctor before using this medication if you are pregnant, breastfeeding, or planning to become pregnant.
* **Children and Older Adults:**  Dosage adjustments might be necessary for children and older adults.
* **Driving and Operating Machinery:**  Drowsiness is a potential side effect; therefore, caution should be exercised when driving or operating machinery.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long does it take to work?** A:  The onset of effects can vary, but many experience relief within a few hours.  However, full therapeutic benefit may take several days.
* **Q: Can I stop taking it suddenly?** A: It's generally advised to follow your doctor's instructions regarding stopping the medication.  Sudden cessation may lead to a return of symptoms.
* **Q: How should I store it?** A:  Store it as directed on the packaging, usually in a cool, dry place, away from direct sunlight and moisture.
* **Q: What if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it's almost time for the next dose.  Don't double up on doses.
* **Q: Is it habit-forming?** A:  No, this medication is not considered habit-forming.
