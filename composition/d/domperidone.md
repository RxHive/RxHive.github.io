# Domperidone

**Generic Name: Domperidone**

**Usage:**

Domperidone is primarily used to relieve symptoms associated with nausea and vomiting.  Its main application is in managing gastrointestinal motility disorders, meaning problems with the movement of food through the digestive system.  This often translates to treating symptoms like feeling sick to the stomach, vomiting, and the feeling of fullness or bloating after meals.  While not a cure for underlying conditions, domperidone helps manage these distressing symptoms.  It can be particularly helpful for people experiencing these symptoms due to conditions such as gastroparesis (delayed stomach emptying), functional dyspepsia (indigestion), or post-operative nausea and vomiting.  In some cases, it may also be used to promote milk production in breastfeeding mothers experiencing difficulty. However, this use requires careful consideration and consultation with a healthcare professional.

**Dosage:**

Dosage varies depending on the individual's age, weight, and the specific condition being treated.  A doctor will determine the appropriate dosage.  It's crucial to follow their instructions precisely.  Domperidone is available in various forms including tablets, oral suspensions (syrups), and in some instances, injections (though injections are less common for general use).  Standard adult dosages typically range from 10mg to 40mg, taken three or four times daily,  but this can vary greatly.  Children's dosages are significantly lower and strictly determined by a pediatrician based on weight and age.  Never administer domperidone to a child without explicit instructions from a doctor.  The medication should generally be taken before meals to optimize its effect on gastric motility.

**Side Effects:**

* **Common:**  Headache, dizziness, drowsiness, dry mouth, diarrhea, constipation. These are usually mild and temporary.
* **Less Common but Serious:**  Prolonged QT interval (a heart rhythm abnormality),  extrapyramidal symptoms (involuntary muscle movements, particularly in children),  allergic reactions (rash, itching, swelling).  
* **Important Note:**  Any unexpected or concerning side effect warrants immediate consultation with a doctor.

**How it Works:**

Domperidone works by blocking the action of dopamine in the brain and the gastrointestinal tract. Dopamine, a neurotransmitter, can influence movement and nausea. By blocking its action in the brain, domperidone helps alleviate nausea and vomiting.  In the gastrointestinal tract, it helps increase the contractions of the stomach and upper intestines, promoting the efficient movement of food. This helps relieve symptoms like fullness and bloating.  Unlike some other antiemetic drugs, domperidone does not readily cross the blood-brain barrier, thus minimizing its potential for neurological side effects.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** Domperidone should not be used by individuals with known hypersensitivity (allergy) to the drug,  or those with certain heart conditions (especially prolonged QT interval).
* **Drug Interactions:** Domperidone can interact with various medications, including some antifungals, antibiotics, and other drugs affecting heart rhythm.  It's crucial to inform your doctor about all medications, including over-the-counter drugs and herbal supplements, you are taking.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  Use during pregnancy and breastfeeding should only be considered under strict medical supervision due to potential risks.
* **Children and Older Adults:**  Children and older adults require careful dose adjustments and monitoring due to increased risk of side effects.
* **Liver and Kidney Impairment:**  Individuals with liver or kidney disease may require dose adjustments or close monitoring.

**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long does it take to work?** A: The onset of effect can vary, but symptom relief is usually noticeable within a short time.
* **Q: Can I take it with food?** A: It's generally recommended to take it *before* meals.
* **Q: How should I store it?** A: Store it in a cool, dry place, away from direct sunlight and moisture, as directed on the packaging.
* **Q: Is it addictive?** A: No, domperidone is not considered an addictive medication.
* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it's almost time for the next dose. Do not double the dose.
