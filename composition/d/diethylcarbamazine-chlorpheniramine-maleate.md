# Diethylcarbamazine + Chlorpheniramine Maleate

**Generic Name:** Diethylcarbamazine + Chlorpheniramine Maleate

**Usage:** This combination medication is primarily used to treat filariasis, a parasitic infection spread by mosquitoes.  Diethylcarbamazine is the active ingredient targeting the parasitic worms causing filariasis (like *Wuchereria bancrofti*, *Brugia malayi*, and *Brugia timori*), while chlorpheniramine maleate is an antihistamine that helps alleviate allergic reactions often associated with the infection, such as itching, swelling, and rash.  While its primary use is in treating filariasis, this combination might sometimes be used to address other parasitic infections, though this is less common and requires careful medical supervision.  Always consult a doctor before using this medication for any condition.


**Dosage:** Dosage varies significantly based on age, weight, and the severity of the infection. A doctor will determine the appropriate dose.  Generally, it's available in tablet and syrup forms.  Children's dosages are significantly lower than adult dosages and should always be prescribed and administered by a medical professional.  Precise instructions for administration, frequency, and duration of treatment will be provided with the prescription.  This medication should never be self-administered.


**Side Effects:**

* **Common:** Nausea, vomiting, headache, dizziness, drowsiness, stomach upset.
* **Less Common but Serious:**  Fever, allergic reactions (rash, itching, swelling, difficulty breathing),  liver problems (jaundice, dark urine), changes in blood counts.
* **Note:** This list isn't exhaustive.  If you experience any unexpected or concerning side effects, stop taking the medication and consult your doctor immediately.


**How it Works:** Diethylcarbamazine works by paralyzing and killing the filarial worms in the body.  This helps reduce the parasitic load and alleviate the symptoms of filariasis. Chlorpheniramine maleate is an antihistamine that blocks the action of histamine, a chemical released by the body during allergic reactions. By blocking histamine, it reduces the symptoms of itching, swelling, and rash associated with filariasis and other allergic responses.


**Popular Brand Names (India):**  Specific brand names vary across regions and manufacturers.  Your doctor or pharmacist can provide information on currently available brands in your area.


**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** This medication is contraindicated in individuals with known hypersensitivity to either diethylcarbamazine or chlorpheniramine maleate.
* **Drug Interactions:** This medication can interact with several other drugs. Inform your doctor of all medications, supplements, and herbal remedies you are taking.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:** Use during pregnancy and breastfeeding should only be considered under strict medical supervision due to potential risks to the developing fetus or nursing infant.
* **Children and Older Adults:**  Dosage adjustments are necessary for children and older adults. Careful monitoring is essential in these groups.
* **Liver and Kidney Disease:**  Caution is advised in individuals with liver or kidney impairment, as drug metabolism and excretion might be affected.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: Can I stop taking this medication once my symptoms improve?**  A: No.  Complete the prescribed course of treatment, even if you feel better.  Stopping early can lead to recurrence of the infection.
* **Q: How should I store this medication?** A: Store in a cool, dry place, away from direct sunlight and moisture, as directed on the packaging.
* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it's almost time for the next dose.  Do not double the dose.
* **Q: Is this medication habit-forming?** A: No, this medication is not considered habit-forming.
* **Q: Can I drink alcohol while taking this medication?** A:  Avoid alcohol consumption while taking this medication as it can worsen drowsiness.  Consult your doctor for specific advice.
