# Dicloxacillin

**Generic Name: Dicloxacillin**

**Usage:**

Dicloxacillin is a penicillinase-resistant penicillin antibiotic.  This means it's effective against certain bacteria that can break down (inactivate) regular penicillin. Its primary use is in treating infections caused by susceptible strains of Gram-positive bacteria.  These infections commonly manifest as skin and soft tissue infections (like boils, abscesses, cellulitis, and impetigo), and pneumonia (lung infection).  It's sometimes also used to treat certain types of bone and joint infections, and endocarditis (infection of the heart lining). However, the increasing resistance of bacteria to antibiotics necessitates careful consideration of its use, and a doctor should always confirm susceptibility testing before prescribing. Dicloxacillin is not effective against many common infections and should not be used for viral infections (like colds or the flu).

**Dosage:**

Dosage varies greatly depending on the individual's age, weight, the severity of the infection, and the specific form of the medication.  A doctor will determine the appropriate dose.  Generally, adult dosages range from 250mg to 500mg every 6 hours, while children's dosages are carefully calculated based on weight and are usually significantly lower.  Dicloxacillin is available in capsules, tablets, and oral suspensions (liquids). It's crucial to follow the prescribed dosage and frequency exactly as directed by your physician.  This medicine should be taken with a full glass of water and preferably on an empty stomach (at least one hour before or two hours after meals) to enhance absorption.  Intravenous (injection) forms of dicloxacillin are rarely used, and are typically administered in a hospital setting.


**Side Effects:**

* **Common:** Nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, abdominal cramps, indigestion.
* **Less Common but Important:**  Rash (including severe allergic reactions), itching,  dizziness, headache.
* **Serious (Less Common but Require Immediate Medical Attention):** Severe allergic reactions (anaphylaxis – characterized by difficulty breathing, swelling of the face, lips, or tongue, and hives), seizures, pseudomembranous colitis (severe inflammation of the colon), liver damage (jaundice).

If you experience any of these side effects, especially the serious ones, seek immediate medical attention.


**How it Works:**

Dicloxacillin works by interfering with the formation of bacterial cell walls.  Bacteria need strong cell walls to survive. Dicloxacillin inhibits the enzymes that bacteria use to build these walls, leading to cell death and stopping the infection from spreading.  Its penicillinase-resistant nature means it can overcome a bacterial defense mechanism that renders many other penicillins ineffective.

**Popular Brand Names (India):**

Specific brand names vary widely depending on the manufacturer and availability within different regions of India.  It's best to consult a pharmacist or physician for locally available options.

**Precautions:**

* **Allergies:** Dicloxacillin should not be used by people with known allergies to penicillin or other beta-lactam antibiotics (cephalosporins, carbapenems, monobactams).
* **Kidney/Liver problems:** Individuals with impaired kidney or liver function may require dose adjustments.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:** Use during pregnancy and breastfeeding should only occur under strict medical supervision, weighing the benefits against potential risks.
* **Interactions:**  Dicloxacillin can interact with certain medications, including anticoagulants (blood thinners) and oral contraceptives. Inform your doctor of all medications you are currently taking.
* **Alcohol:** Avoid alcohol consumption while taking dicloxacillin, as it can increase the risk of side effects.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: Can I stop taking dicloxacillin once I feel better?** A: No, continue taking the medication for the full prescribed course, even if you feel better, to prevent the infection from returning and to avoid antibiotic resistance.
* **Q: How should I store dicloxacillin?** A: Store dicloxacillin at room temperature, away from moisture and direct sunlight. Keep it out of reach of children.
* **Q: What if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it is almost time for the next dose. Do not take a double dose.
* **Q: Is dicloxacillin safe for children?** A: Yes, but only under strict medical supervision, with the dosage carefully adjusted based on the child's weight and the severity of the infection.
* **Q: Can dicloxacillin cause yeast infections?** A:  Yes, like many antibiotics, dicloxacillin can disrupt the balance of good bacteria in the body, potentially leading to yeast infections.
