# Diclofenac + Codeine

**Generic Name: Diclofenac + Codeine**

**Usage:** Diclofenac + Codeine is a combination medication used to relieve moderate to severe pain.  It's primarily prescribed for conditions causing acute pain, where both inflammation and pain relief are needed.  This often includes:

* **Post-surgical pain:** Pain following surgery, helping manage both the inflammatory response and the pain sensation.
* **Severe musculoskeletal pain:** Conditions like acute back pain, severe arthritis flares, or other painful musculoskeletal injuries where strong pain relief is required.
* **Dental pain:**  Relief from significant dental pain, especially after procedures.
* **Other sources of moderate to severe pain:**  While not a first-line treatment for all pain, it may be used in cases where other treatments haven't been effective or are unsuitable.


It's crucial to understand that this combination is for short-term pain relief, and long-term use should be avoided due to the risk of side effects.  It's not appropriate for mild pain or conditions where only inflammation needs to be treated.


**Dosage:**  Dosage varies significantly depending on the specific formulation (tablet, capsule, etc.), the severity of the pain, and the individual patient's response.  *This information should not be used in place of a doctor's prescription.*  A healthcare professional will determine the appropriate dosage and duration of treatment.  Generally, adults will receive a specific number of tablets or capsules at regular intervals, as prescribed.  Children's dosage is strictly determined by weight and age and is almost always significantly lower than adult dosages.   Codeine is particularly concerning in children, and use is often restricted.  **Never administer this medication to a child without specific instructions from a pediatrician.**


**Side Effects:**  Common side effects can include:

* Nausea and vomiting
* Constipation
* Dizziness
* Drowsiness
* Headache
* Stomach upset
* Indigestion
* Allergic reactions (rash, itching, swelling)

* Liver problems (jaundice, dark urine)
* Kidney problems
* Breathing difficulties
