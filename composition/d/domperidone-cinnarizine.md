# Domperidone + Cinnarizine

**Generic Name:** Domperidone + Cinnarizine

**Usage:**

Domperidone + Cinnarizine is a combination medication primarily used to treat symptoms related to gastrointestinal disorders and vertigo (dizziness).  The combination leverages the properties of both its constituent drugs. Domperidone acts as a prokinetic agent, stimulating the movement of food through the digestive tract. This helps to relieve symptoms like nausea, vomiting, and bloating. Cinnarizine, on the other hand, is an antihistamine with antiemetic (anti-vomiting) and antivertigo properties. It helps to reduce dizziness, nausea, and vomiting associated with inner ear problems or motion sickness.  Therefore, this combination is frequently prescribed for conditions where both gastrointestinal upset and dizziness are present.  While its primary use is for these symptoms, some doctors might prescribe it off-label for other related conditions, always under professional guidance.

**Dosage:**

Dosage will vary depending on the patient's age, weight, and specific condition, as determined by a physician.  There is no universally standard dosage.  It's crucial to follow the doctor's prescription precisely.  The medication is commonly available in tablet form.  Liquid formulations (syrups) may exist but are less common.  The medication should be taken as directed, typically with or without food, as advised by the prescribing doctor.  Never exceed the prescribed dose. Pediatric use requires careful consideration and should only be determined by a healthcare professional who can adjust the dosage according to the child’s weight and age.  There are no standard injectable formulations of this combination readily available.

**Side Effects:**

* **Common:**  Drowsiness, headache, dry mouth, constipation, and diarrhea. These are generally mild and may subside as the body adjusts to the medication.
* **Less Common but Serious:**  Extrapyramidal symptoms (involuntary muscle movements), galactorrhea (spontaneous milk production), amenorrhea (absence of menstruation), and prolonged QT interval (a heart rhythm abnormality).  These require immediate medical attention.
* **Note:** This is not an exhaustive list.  If you experience any unusual or concerning side effects, consult your doctor or pharmacist immediately.

**How it Works:**

Domperidone works by blocking dopamine receptors in the chemoreceptor trigger zone (CTZ) in the brain and the gastrointestinal tract.  Blocking these receptors helps reduce nausea and vomiting and stimulates gastric motility (the movement of food through the stomach and intestines).  Cinnarizine acts as a calcium channel blocker and also blocks histamine receptors. This reduces the effects of histamine, a neurotransmitter involved in the sensation of dizziness and nausea.  The combined action of both drugs addresses both gastrointestinal symptoms and vertigo.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:**  This medication should not be used by individuals with known hypersensitivity to either domperidone or cinnarizine.  It's also contraindicated in patients with certain types of heart conditions, especially those with prolonged QT intervals.
* **Drug Interactions:**  Domperidone + Cinnarizine can interact with various medications, including some antidepressants, antibiotics, and antifungal agents.  It's crucial to inform your doctor about all medications you are currently taking, including over-the-counter drugs and herbal supplements.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:** The safety of this combination during pregnancy and breastfeeding hasn't been definitively established.  Consult a doctor before using this medication if you are pregnant, breastfeeding, or planning to become pregnant.
* **Elderly and Children:**  Special caution is required when prescribing this medication to the elderly and children, as they may be more susceptible to side effects. Dosage adjustment may be necessary.

**FAQs:**

* **Q: Can I take this medication with alcohol?**  A:  It's best to avoid alcohol while taking this medication as it can enhance the sedative effects of cinnarizine.
* **Q: How should I store this medication?** A: Store in a cool, dry place, away from direct sunlight and moisture. Keep out of reach of children.
* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it's nearly time for the next dose.  Don't double the dose.
* **Q: Can I stop taking this medication suddenly?** A:  It's generally not advisable to stop taking this medication suddenly. Consult your doctor before discontinuing treatment to avoid potential withdrawal symptoms or a recurrence of your symptoms.
