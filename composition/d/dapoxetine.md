# Dapoxetine

**Generic Name: Dapoxetine**

**Usage:**

Dapoxetine is primarily used to treat premature ejaculation (PE) in adult men.  PE is characterized by the consistent or recurrent ejaculation with minimal sexual stimulation before the individual wishes. Dapoxetine helps to increase the time it takes to ejaculate, allowing for more prolonged sexual intercourse and potentially improving sexual satisfaction for both partners.  It's crucial to understand that Dapoxetine is not a cure for PE, but rather a treatment aimed at managing its symptoms.  It is important to note that Dapoxetine is specifically indicated for this condition and is not used to treat other sexual dysfunction issues, such as erectile dysfunction.  There are no notable secondary uses approved for this medication.


**Dosage:**

Dapoxetine is typically available as tablets.  The standard dosage for adults is 30mg or 60mg taken orally, as directed by a physician, one to three hours before anticipated sexual activity. Dosage should not exceed 60mg per dose and should be carefully determined by a healthcare professional based on individual needs and response.  Dapoxetine is not recommended for use in children or women, and there are no established pediatric dosage guidelines.  Taking more than one dose within 24 hours is strongly discouraged.


**Side Effects:**

Common side effects of Dapoxetine may include:

* Nausea
* Dizziness
* Headache
* Diarrhea
* Fatigue
* Insomnia
* Somnolence (sleepiness)


Less common, but more serious side effects, although rare, might include:

* Syncope (fainting)
* Seizures
* Severe allergic reactions
* Suicidal thoughts or behavior (this warrants immediate medical attention)

If any side effects occur, particularly those listed as serious, it's vital to immediately contact a healthcare professional for guidance.  The severity and frequency of side effects can vary between individuals.


**How it Works:**

Dapoxetine is a selective serotonin reuptake inhibitor (SSRI). SSRIs work by affecting the levels of serotonin, a neurotransmitter in the brain that plays a role in regulating mood and sexual function. By increasing serotonin levels in specific areas of the brain, Dapoxetine helps to delay ejaculation.  The exact mechanisms through which it achieves this are complex and still under investigation, but the impact on serotonin neurotransmission is the primary pathway.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** Dapoxetine is contraindicated in individuals with known hypersensitivity to the drug, severe liver or kidney impairment, and those currently using monoamine oxidase inhibitors (MAOIs) or have used them within the last 14 days.
* **Drug Interactions:** Dapoxetine can interact with several medications, including other SSRIs, MAOIs, and certain antidepressants.  Always inform your doctor about all medications, supplements, or herbal remedies you are taking.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:** Dapoxetine is not recommended for use during pregnancy or breastfeeding.
* **Elderly:** Dosage adjustments might be needed for older adults due to potential age-related changes in drug metabolism.
* **Alcohol:** Combining Dapoxetine with alcohol can increase the risk of side effects.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long does Dapoxetine take to work?** A:  The effects generally start within one to three hours after taking the medication.
* **Q: Can I take Dapoxetine every day?** A: No. Dapoxetine is taken on an as-needed basis, typically before anticipated sexual activity. Daily use is not recommended and may increase the risk of side effects.
* **Q: How should I store Dapoxetine?** A: Store Dapoxetine in a cool, dry place, away from direct sunlight and moisture, as per the instructions on the label.
* **Q:  What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Do not take a double dose. Simply take the next dose as scheduled. If you have concerns, contact your doctor.
* **Q: Is Dapoxetine habit-forming?** A: While not physically addictive in the same way as some substances, prolonged use without professional supervision could lead to dependence or reliance on the medication. This underscores the importance of responsible use and consultation with a healthcare professional.
