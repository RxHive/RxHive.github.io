# Dextromethorphan Hydrobromide + Diphenhydramine

**Generic Name:** Dextromethorphan Hydrobromide + Diphenhydramine

**Usage:** This combination medication is primarily used to relieve the symptoms of the common cold and flu, particularly coughs and the accompanying symptoms of runny nose and sneezing.  Dextromethorphan hydrobromide is a cough suppressant (an antitussive) that works by affecting the cough center in the brain. Diphenhydramine is an antihistamine that helps to dry up runny noses and reduce sneezing by blocking the action of histamine in the body.  While primarily used for symptomatic relief of cold and flu, it's crucial to understand this combination doesn't treat the underlying viral infection itself.  The medication only addresses the bothersome symptoms.


**Dosage:** Dosage varies greatly depending on the specific product, the age and weight of the patient, and the severity of symptoms.  Always follow the instructions on the product label or as directed by a doctor or pharmacist.  Generally, liquid formulations are more common for children, with dosage carefully measured using a marked measuring device. Tablets are usually prescribed for adults.  Never exceed the recommended dosage.  Consult a healthcare professional for appropriate dosage if you have any questions or concerns, especially for children, older adults, or those with underlying health conditions.  Self-medication should be avoided, especially for prolonged use or if symptoms worsen.

**Side Effects:**

* **Common:** Drowsiness, dizziness, dry mouth, constipation, nausea.
* **Less Common but Serious:**  Allergic reactions (rash, itching, swelling, difficulty breathing), rapid heartbeat, confusion, hallucinations (especially in higher doses or in sensitive individuals).  
* **Important Note:**  If you experience any of these side effects, particularly the serious ones, discontinue use and seek immediate medical attention.


**How it Works:** Dextromethorphan works centrally in the brain, reducing the urge to cough. Diphenhydramine blocks the effects of histamine, a substance released by the body during an allergic reaction or infection. This blockage helps to reduce nasal congestion, sneezing, and watery eyes.  The combination offers a dual action against cold and flu symptoms.


**Popular Brand Names (India):**  Brand names vary considerably across regions and may not all be available in India.  It's best to consult a pharmacist or check the local pharmaceutical listings for available brands containing this combination.


**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** This medication should not be used by individuals with certain medical conditions, including glaucoma, asthma, or enlarged prostate. It's also contraindicated in individuals with known allergies to either dextromethorphan or diphenhydramine.  Always inform your doctor or pharmacist about any existing health conditions or allergies.
* **Drug Interactions:**  This medication can interact with other drugs, including certain sedatives, antidepressants, and other medications that affect the central nervous system.  It is crucial to inform your doctor or pharmacist of all medications, including over-the-counter drugs and supplements, you are currently taking.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  Use during pregnancy or breastfeeding should only be considered after careful evaluation by a healthcare professional.  The risks and benefits need to be weighed carefully.
* **Children and Older Adults:**  Children and older adults may be more sensitive to the side effects of this medication.  Careful dosage adjustments and close monitoring are essential.
* **Driving and Operating Machinery:**  Drowsiness is a common side effect. Avoid driving or operating machinery until you know how this medication affects you.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long can I take this medication?** A:  Generally, this medication is for short-term use only (a few days).  If symptoms persist or worsen after a few days, consult a doctor.
* **Q: Can I take this with alcohol?** A:  No. Alcohol can worsen the drowsiness and other side effects. Avoid alcohol consumption while using this medication.
* **Q: How should I store this medication?** A: Store in a cool, dry place, away from direct sunlight and out of reach of children.
* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: If you miss a dose, take it as soon as you remember, unless it's close to the time for your next dose.  Do not double the dose.
* **Q:  Is it safe to take this medication every day?** A: No, this medication is intended for symptomatic relief of cold and flu and should not be used for prolonged periods. Consult your doctor if symptoms persist.
