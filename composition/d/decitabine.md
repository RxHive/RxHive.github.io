# Decitabine

**Generic Name: Decitabine**

**Usage:**

Decitabine is a medication primarily used in the treatment of myelodysplastic syndromes (MDS) and acute myeloid leukemia (AML).  MDS is a group of cancers affecting the bone marrow, leading to insufficient production of healthy blood cells. AML is a rapid-growing cancer of the blood and bone marrow, characterized by an overproduction of abnormal myeloblasts. Decitabine works by targeting and modifying the DNA within cancerous cells, ultimately leading to their death.  While its primary use is in these blood cancers, research continues to explore its potential applications in other types of cancer.

**Dosage:**

Decitabine is typically administered intravenously (IV) by a healthcare professional. Dosage varies significantly depending on the specific condition being treated, the patient's overall health, and their response to the medication.  There's no standardized pediatric dosage as its use in children is less common and usually within clinical trial settings.  Adult dosages are determined individually by a physician and are often adjusted based on factors like kidney and liver function. The medication comes in a vial that needs to be diluted before administration. Detailed instructions for preparation and administration are provided by the manufacturer and are crucial for safe usage.  There are no oral or syrup formulations of decitabine currently available.

**Side Effects:**

Common side effects include:

* Nausea and vomiting
* Diarrhea
* Fatigue
* Low blood counts (neutropenia, thrombocytopenia, anemia) leading to increased risk of infection and bleeding
* Fever
* Headache
* Mouth sores


Less common but serious side effects can include:

* Severe allergic reactions
* Liver damage
* Kidney problems
* Bleeding disorders


**It is crucial to report any side effects, especially those that are severe or persistent, to your doctor immediately.**

**How it Works:**

Decitabine is a nucleoside analog, meaning it's structurally similar to the building blocks of DNA.  Once inside cancer cells, decitabine gets incorporated into the DNA, interfering with the cells' ability to replicate and function properly. This process inhibits DNA methylation, a chemical modification that can silence tumor suppressor genes, effectively reactivating these genes and triggering programmed cell death (apoptosis) in the cancerous cells.  Importantly, it targets rapidly dividing cells more effectively, thus exhibiting a degree of selectivity for cancer cells compared to normal cells.

**Precautions:**

* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:** Decitabine can harm a developing fetus.  Women should avoid pregnancy during and after treatment.  Breastfeeding is typically discouraged while on this medication.
* **Kidney and Liver Impairment:** Dosage adjustments may be necessary for individuals with reduced kidney or liver function.
* **Drug Interactions:** Decitabine can interact with certain medications.  Inform your doctor of all medications, including over-the-counter drugs and supplements, you are taking.
* **Infections:** Decitabine can decrease the body's ability to fight infections; therefore, prompt medical attention for any signs of infection is vital.
* **Bleeding:** Because decitabine can reduce blood platelet count, there is an increased risk of bleeding.  Care should be taken to avoid injury.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long does Decitabine treatment last?** A: The duration of treatment varies depending on the patient's response and the specific medical condition. Your oncologist will determine the appropriate treatment schedule.
* **Q: Can I store Decitabine at home?** A: No. Decitabine requires special storage conditions.  It's always administered in a clinical setting and not stored at home.
* **Q: Is Decitabine safe for everyone?** A:  Decitabine carries potential side effects and is not suitable for all individuals.  Your doctor will assess your health status to determine if it's appropriate for you.
* **Q:  What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Contact your doctor immediately to discuss the missed dose and determine the best course of action.  Do not attempt to adjust the dosage yourself.
