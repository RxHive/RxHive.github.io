# Desloratadine

**Generic Name: Desloratadine**

**Usage:**

Desloratadine is an antihistamine primarily used to relieve symptoms associated with allergies.  Its main use is in treating allergic rhinitis (hay fever), which causes sneezing, runny nose, itchy eyes, and nasal congestion.  It's also effective in managing urticaria (hives), a skin condition characterized by itchy welts. While not its primary indication, desloratadine can sometimes provide relief from other allergy-related symptoms like itching and skin rashes.  It works by blocking the effects of histamine, a substance released by the body during an allergic reaction.  Importantly, desloratadine is a non-drowsy antihistamine, meaning it's less likely to cause sleepiness compared to older generations of antihistamines.

**Dosage:**

Dosage varies depending on age and the specific formulation.  Always follow your doctor's prescription and instructions carefully.

* **Adults and adolescents (12 years and older):** The usual dose is one 5 mg tablet once daily.
* **Children (6-11 years):** The dosage is usually one 2.5 mg tablet or appropriate syrup dosage once daily.  Consult a physician for precise instructions, as syrup concentrations can vary.
* **Children under 6 years:** Desloratadine is generally not recommended for children under 6 years of age unless specifically prescribed by a doctor.

Desloratadine is available in tablet and syrup forms.  The tablets should be swallowed whole with water, and the syrup should be measured accurately using the provided measuring device.  Do not exceed the recommended dosage.


**Side Effects:**

Common side effects are generally mild and may include:

* Headache
* Fatigue (tiredness)
* Dry mouth
* Dizziness
* Nausea
* Stomach upset

Less common, but more serious side effects are possible.  Seek immediate medical attention if you experience:

* Severe allergic reactions (e.g., difficulty breathing, swelling of the face, lips, tongue, or throat)
* Fast heartbeat
* Seizures
* Hallucinations

If you experience any unusual or concerning side effects, contact your healthcare provider immediately.


**How it Works:**

Desloratadine is a selective peripheral H1-receptor antagonist. This means it specifically blocks histamine from binding to H1 receptors found on cells in various tissues, including those in the nose, eyes, and skin. Histamine's binding to these receptors triggers the allergic response, causing symptoms like itching, sneezing, and swelling. By preventing histamine from binding, desloratadine effectively reduces or prevents these symptoms.

**Precautions:**

* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:** Consult your doctor before using desloratadine if you are pregnant, breastfeeding, or planning to become pregnant.
* **Kidney or Liver Problems:**  People with impaired kidney or liver function may need a dosage adjustment.  Discuss this with your doctor.
* **Other Medications:** Inform your doctor about all medications you are taking, including over-the-counter drugs and herbal remedies, as interactions may occur.
* **Alcohol:** Avoid alcohol consumption while taking desloratadine, as it can increase the risk of drowsiness (although desloratadine itself is less likely to cause drowsiness than some other antihistamines).
* **Driving and Operating Machinery:** While generally non-drowsy, some individuals may experience slight dizziness.  Exercise caution when driving or operating machinery.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: Can I take desloratadine with other medications?** A: Always inform your doctor or pharmacist about all medications you are taking to avoid potential interactions.
* **Q: How should I store desloratadine?** A: Store desloratadine at room temperature, away from moisture and direct sunlight.  Keep it out of reach of children.
* **Q:  What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it is almost time for your next dose.  Do not double the dose.
* **Q: Can I stop taking desloratadine suddenly?** A:  Generally, desloratadine can be stopped without tapering, but always follow your doctor’s advice.
* **Q: Is desloratadine habit-forming?** A: No, desloratadine is not considered habit-forming.
