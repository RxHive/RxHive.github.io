# Disulfiram

**Generic Name: Disulfiram**

**Usage:**

Disulfiram's primary use is in the treatment of alcohol dependence. It's not a cure for alcoholism, but it acts as a deterrent.  By interfering with the body's metabolism of alcohol, it causes unpleasant side effects if alcohol is consumed, making continued drinking significantly less appealing. This aversive therapy helps individuals maintain abstinence.  While its primary use is focused on alcohol dependence, there have been some limited explorations of its potential in other areas, but these are not standard practice.  


**Dosage:**

Dosage varies depending on the individual and their physician's guidance.  Generally, for adults, the initial dose is typically 250mg daily for one to two weeks, followed by a maintenance dose of 125-500mg daily.  Children should not receive this medication. Disulfiram is typically administered orally as a tablet.  The medication should always be started under strict medical supervision. Adjustments to dosage are made based on the patient's response and tolerance, and are determined solely by a physician. It’s crucial to never alter the dosage without explicit instruction from a doctor.


**Side Effects:**

* **Common:**  Metallic taste in the mouth, drowsiness, headache, nausea, vomiting.
* **Less Common but Serious:**  Hepatitis (inflammation of the liver), optic neuritis (inflammation of the optic nerve), peripheral neuropathy (nerve damage in the arms and legs), psychosis, severe allergic reactions.
* **Note:** This is not an exhaustive list.  Any new or worsening symptoms should be reported to a healthcare provider immediately.


**How it Works:**

Disulfiram inhibits the enzyme aldehyde dehydrogenase (ALDH), which plays a crucial role in the breakdown of acetaldehyde, a toxic byproduct of alcohol metabolism.  When a person taking disulfiram consumes alcohol, acetaldehyde accumulates in the body, leading to unpleasant symptoms like flushing, nausea, vomiting, headache, and palpitations.  This unpleasant reaction acts as a powerful disincentive for alcohol consumption.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** Disulfiram is contraindicated in individuals with severe liver or kidney disease, heart disease, brain damage, or a history of psychosis.  It should not be used by pregnant or breastfeeding women.
* **Drug Interactions:** Disulfiram interacts with many medications, including metronidazole, warfarin, and isoniazid.  It is crucial to inform your doctor of all medications, supplements, and herbal remedies you are taking.
* **Alcohol Consumption:**  Complete abstinence from alcohol is absolutely necessary while taking disulfiram.  Even small amounts of alcohol can trigger severe adverse reactions.
* **Vulnerable Groups:**  Special caution is required for elderly individuals and those with pre-existing medical conditions. Close monitoring is essential.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long does it take to work?** A: The effect of disulfiram is immediate upon alcohol consumption; it doesn't prevent alcohol cravings, but rather creates an unpleasant physical response to drinking.
* **Q: Can I stop taking it suddenly?** A: No. Disulfiram should be discontinued gradually under the guidance of a physician.
* **Q: How should I store it?** A: Store in a cool, dry place, away from children and direct sunlight.
* **Q: What if I accidentally consume alcohol while on disulfiram?** A: Seek immediate medical attention. Symptoms can be severe.
* **Q: Is disulfiram addictive?** A: Disulfiram itself is not addictive, but its use requires strict adherence to medical advice and monitoring.
