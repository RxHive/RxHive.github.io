# Durvalumab

**Generic Name: Durvalumab**

**Usage:**

Durvalumab is a type of immunotherapy medication classified as a programmed death-ligand 1 (PD-L1) inhibitor.  Its primary use is in treating certain types of cancer that have progressed or haven't responded to other treatments.  Specifically, it's approved for use in:

* **Lung Cancer:**  Durvalumab is commonly used in non-small cell lung cancer (NSCLC) patients, often after chemotherapy.  This includes patients whose cancer has progressed after prior platinum-based chemotherapy and those with a high PD-L1 expression.  It's also used in combination with other treatments for certain NSCLC patients.

* **Bladder Cancer:** Durvalumab is approved for use in patients with locally advanced or metastatic urothelial carcinoma (a type of bladder cancer) who have not progressed after platinum-based chemotherapy.

* **Other Cancers:**  Ongoing research is exploring its potential in other cancers, but these uses are not yet standard practice.  Always consult with your oncologist about the most current treatment options.

The use of Durvalumab is highly dependent on the individual's specific cancer type, its stage, and previous treatment responses.  It's a targeted therapy, meaning it works specifically on cancer cells and often in combination with other treatments, rather than affecting the whole body like chemotherapy.


**Dosage:**

Durvalumab is administered intravenously (IV) by a healthcare professional.  The specific dosage and schedule vary significantly depending on the type of cancer being treated and the patient's overall health. It is crucial to follow your oncologist's prescribed dosage regimen precisely.  There is no standard dosage for children, as Durvalumab is primarily used in adult oncology.  The dosage is usually determined based on body surface area or weight, depending on the treatment protocol.  There is no oral or tablet form of this medication.


**Side Effects:**

Common side effects can include:

* Fatigue
* Cough
* Diarrhea
* Nausea
* Rash
* Constipation
* Decreased appetite
* Shortness of breath


Less common but potentially serious side effects include:

* Infusion-related reactions (allergic reactions during IV administration)
* Pneumonitis (inflammation of the lungs)
* Hepatitis (inflammation of the liver)
* Colitis (inflammation of the colon)
* Endocrine disorders (affecting hormone production)
* Kidney problems


**Important:**  If you experience any concerning side effects, including those listed above, contact your healthcare provider immediately.


**How it Works:**

Durvalumab works by blocking the PD-L1 protein, found on some cancer cells.  PD-L1 helps cancer cells evade detection and destruction by the body's immune system. By blocking PD-L1, Durvalumab allows the immune system to recognize and attack the cancer cells more effectively. This is a form of immunotherapy, leveraging the body's natural defenses to fight the disease.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** Durvalumab is contraindicated in individuals with known hypersensitivity to the drug or its components.

* **Drug Interactions:**  Inform your doctor about all medications you are currently taking, including over-the-counter drugs, herbal supplements, and vitamins, as interactions can occur.

* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  Durvalumab should be avoided during pregnancy and breastfeeding unless the benefits clearly outweigh the potential risks.  Discuss this with your doctor if you are pregnant, planning to become pregnant, or breastfeeding.

* **Older Adults and Children:**  While the primary usage is in adults, special considerations might be necessary for older adults or children if Durvalumab is ever used in these populations. Close monitoring is crucial.
