# Dolutegravir + Lamivudine + Tenofovir disoproxil fumarate

**Generic Name:** Dolutegravir + Lamivudine + Tenofovir disoproxil fumarate

**Usage:**

This combination medication is primarily used to treat Human Immunodeficiency Virus (HIV) infection in adults and children weighing at least 40 kg (approximately 88 lbs).  It's a crucial part of antiretroviral therapy (ART), a treatment strategy that combines several drugs to suppress the HIV virus and prevent it from replicating. By controlling the virus, ART significantly improves the quality of life for people living with HIV and reduces their risk of transmitting the virus to others.  While its primary use is in HIV treatment, there are currently no established secondary uses for this specific combination.


**Dosage:**

The typical adult dosage is one tablet taken once daily with or without food.  Dosage for children weighing at least 40 kg is also typically one tablet once daily, although precise dosing adjustments might be necessary based on individual factors and should be determined by a healthcare professional. This medication is generally available as a single tablet formulation containing all three active components.  There are no syrup or injectable forms of this specific triple-combination drug.  It's crucial to follow the prescribed dosage and regimen strictly as directed by a doctor.  Never adjust the dosage or frequency on your own.


**Side Effects:**

Common side effects can include:

* Nausea
* Diarrhea
* Headache
* Dizziness
* Fatigue
* Sleep disturbances
* Rash

Less common but serious side effects can include:

* Liver problems (hepatitis)
* Kidney problems
* Pancreatitis (inflammation of the pancreas)
* Allergic reactions (including serious ones)
* Changes in bone mineral density
*  Neurological effects

If you experience any side effects, particularly those listed as serious, seek immediate medical attention.  This list is not exhaustive, and other side effects may occur.


**How it Works:**

This combination medication contains three different antiretroviral drugs, each targeting a specific step in the HIV life cycle:

* **Dolutegravir:** This is an integrase strand transfer inhibitor (INSTI). It works by preventing the HIV virus from integrating its genetic material into the DNA of human cells.  Without this integration, the virus cannot replicate.

* **Lamivudine:** This is a nucleoside reverse transcriptase inhibitor (NRTI). It interferes with the enzyme reverse transcriptase, which HIV needs to convert its RNA into DNA, a necessary step for viral replication.

* **Tenofovir disoproxil fumarate:**  Also an NRTI, tenofovir works similarly to lamivudine, inhibiting reverse transcriptase and blocking viral replication.


By combining these three drugs with different mechanisms of action, the medication achieves greater effectiveness in suppressing HIV replication than any single drug could on its own. This synergistic effect is critical in managing HIV infection and preventing disease progression.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:**  This medication is contraindicated for individuals with known hypersensitivity to any of its components.
* **Drug Interactions:**  There are potential interactions with other medications; a healthcare professional should be consulted about any other medications being taken.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  This medication should only be used during pregnancy and breastfeeding under the close supervision of a healthcare professional. The benefits must be carefully weighed against any potential risks to the mother and child.
* **Older Adults and Children:**  While this medication is used in children weighing at least 40kg, close monitoring is often recommended for both children and older adults due to potential variations in how they metabolize the drugs.
* **Kidney and Liver Function:**  Individuals with impaired kidney or liver function may require dosage adjustments.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: Can I stop taking this medication once I feel better?** A: No, strictly adhere to the prescribed dosage and duration of treatment. Stopping prematurely can lead to drug resistance and disease progression.

* **Q: How should I store this medication?** A: Store it as directed on the label, typically in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight and moisture.

* **Q: Is this medication safe for long-term use?** A:  Yes, for most individuals, but ongoing monitoring of kidney function and other potential side effects is essential.

* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it's nearly time for your next dose.  Never double up on doses.
