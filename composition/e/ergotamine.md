# Ergotamine

**Generic Name: Ergotamine**

**Usage:**

Ergotamine is primarily used to treat migraine headaches.  It's particularly effective in aborting a migraine attack that's already begun, meaning it helps stop the headache once it starts. While it doesn't prevent migraines from occurring, it can significantly reduce their severity and duration.  Less commonly, ergotamine may be used in conjunction with other medications for the treatment of cluster headaches, though this is less frequent and typically only considered when other treatments haven't been successful.  It's crucial to understand that ergotamine is a treatment for *acute* migraine attacks, not a preventative medication.

**Dosage:**

Dosage for ergotamine varies greatly depending on the specific formulation (tablets, suppositories, or injections), the severity of the headache, and the individual's response to the medication.  *It's essential to follow a doctor's prescription precisely.*  Self-adjusting the dosage can be dangerous.

There is no standard pediatric dosage for ergotamine as its use in children is generally discouraged due to potential side effects.  Adult dosages are usually determined by a healthcare professional based on individual needs and can range from a single dose to several doses over a short period (typically not exceeding 10mg in 24 hours).  The medication is usually taken at the first sign of a migraine or as soon as the headache begins.  Exceeding recommended dosages can lead to serious complications.

**Side Effects:**

* **Common:** Nausea, vomiting, dizziness, tingling in the extremities, weakness, cold hands and feet.
* **Less Common but Serious:**  High blood pressure, chest pain, muscle pain or cramps in the legs and arms, slow or irregular heartbeat, visual disturbances, numbness, and coldness in the extremities.  In rare cases, ergotamine can cause serious vascular complications such as stroke or heart attack.  Prolonged or excessive use can lead to ergotamine toxicity, a serious condition requiring immediate medical attention.

**If you experience any unusual or concerning side effects, seek immediate medical attention.**

**How it Works:**

Ergotamine works by constricting blood vessels in the brain.  Migraines are believed to be associated with the dilation (widening) of blood vessels in the brain.  By narrowing these vessels, ergotamine helps reduce the throbbing pain characteristic of migraines. The exact mechanism of action is still not completely understood, and it may also interact with other neurotransmitters in the brain to provide pain relief.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** Ergotamine should not be used by individuals with certain cardiovascular conditions (e.g., peripheral vascular disease, coronary artery disease, hypertension), severe liver or kidney disease, pregnancy, breastfeeding, or those with a history of ergotamine toxicity.
* **Drug Interactions:**  Ergotamine can interact with several other medications, including some antidepressants, antibiotics, and certain cardiovascular drugs. It's crucial to inform your doctor about all medications, supplements, or herbal remedies you are taking.
* **Specific Warnings:** Older adults and individuals with pre-existing health conditions should exercise extra caution when using ergotamine.  It's especially critical to avoid overuse as this greatly increases the risk of serious complications.

**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long does it take to work?** A:  The onset of action varies depending on the formulation and individual response, but effects can be seen within 30 minutes to a few hours.
* **Q: Can I take it daily?** A: No.  Ergotamine should only be taken as needed for acute migraine attacks and not regularly.  Daily or frequent use can lead to serious complications.
* **Q: How should I store it?** A: Store ergotamine as directed on the label, typically in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight and moisture.
* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A:  Don't take a double dose.  If you miss a dose, wait until your next scheduled dose.  If your headache persists, consult your doctor.
* **Q: Is it safe for pregnant or breastfeeding women?** A: No, ergotamine is contraindicated during pregnancy and breastfeeding due to potential harm to the developing fetus or infant.
