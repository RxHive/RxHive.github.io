# Eletriptan

**Generic Name: Eletriptan**

**Usage:**

Eletriptan is a triptan medication primarily used to treat **migraine headaches with or without aura**.  Its primary function is to relieve the pain, nausea, and other symptoms associated with a migraine attack.  While not specifically indicated for other conditions, it may sometimes provide relief from cluster headaches in some individuals, though this is not a standard or approved use.  It's important to note that eletriptan is an *abortive* medication, meaning it's designed to stop a migraine attack that's already started, not to prevent future attacks.  Prevention requires different medications.


**Dosage:**

Eletriptan is typically available as an oral tablet.  The standard adult dose is 40 mg taken at the very first sign of a migraine.  This dose should not be repeated within 24 hours.  It's crucial to take eletriptan as soon as possible after the onset of migraine symptoms for optimal effectiveness.  **Eletriptan is not recommended for children or adolescents,** and there is no established pediatric dosage.  There are no dosage adjustments based on weight or liver/kidney function routinely made,  but individuals with severe liver or kidney impairment should consult their doctor before taking it.


**Side Effects:**

* **Common Side Effects:**  Dizziness, drowsiness, tingling or numbness, feeling warm, fatigue, nausea. These are usually mild and temporary.
* **Less Common but Serious Side Effects:** Chest tightness or pain (report immediately), heart palpitations, high blood pressure, allergic reactions (rash, itching, swelling, difficulty breathing).

**If you experience any adverse effects, especially those listed as serious, consult your healthcare provider immediately.**


**How it Works:**

Eletriptan works by narrowing blood vessels in the brain and blocking the release of certain chemicals that trigger migraine pain.  These chemicals, including serotonin, are involved in the inflammation and pain pathways associated with migraines. By constricting blood vessels and reducing the release of these pain-inducing substances, eletriptan helps alleviate the symptoms of a migraine.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** Eletriptan should not be used by individuals with severe heart disease, uncontrolled high blood pressure, or a history of stroke or transient ischemic attack (TIA).  People with severe liver or kidney problems should also exercise caution and consult their doctor.
* **Drug Interactions:** Eletriptan can interact with certain medications, including other triptans, MAO inhibitors, and ergotamine-containing drugs.  Always inform your doctor about all medications, supplements, and herbal remedies you are taking.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:** The use of eletriptan during pregnancy and breastfeeding should be discussed with a doctor to weigh the benefits against potential risks.
* **Elderly Patients:**  Elderly individuals may be more susceptible to side effects.  Careful monitoring is advised.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: Can I take eletriptan with other pain relievers?** A:  Consult your doctor.  Combining it with other medications, especially other migraine treatments, may increase the risk of side effects.
* **Q: How should I store eletriptan?** A: Store eletriptan tablets in a cool, dry place, away from direct sunlight and moisture.  Keep it out of reach of children.
* **Q: How long does it take to work?** A: Eletriptan typically provides relief within 30 minutes to 2 hours.
* **Q: What should I do if a dose is missed?** A: Do not take a double dose. Take the next dose only when you have a migraine.
* **Q: Is eletriptan addictive?** A:  Eletriptan is not considered an addictive substance, but overuse can lead to medication overuse headache.  Follow your doctor's instructions carefully.
