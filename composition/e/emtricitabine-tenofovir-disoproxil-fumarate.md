# Emtricitabine + Tenofovir disoproxil fumarate

**Generic Name:** Emtricitabine + Tenofovir disoproxil fumarate

**Usage:**

Emtricitabine + Tenofovir disoproxil fumarate is a combination antiretroviral medication primarily used to treat Human Immunodeficiency Virus (HIV) infection in adults and children.  It's a crucial part of antiretroviral therapy (ART), a regimen designed to suppress HIV replication, slowing the progression of the disease and improving the quality of life for individuals living with HIV.  While its primary use is HIV treatment, it's also sometimes considered for pre-exposure prophylaxis (PrEP) – a preventative measure to reduce the risk of HIV acquisition in individuals at high risk of infection.  This preventative use requires careful medical supervision and should only be undertaken under the guidance of a healthcare professional.

**Dosage:**

Dosage varies significantly based on age, weight, and overall health.  This information should *never* substitute the advice of a prescribing physician.  Generally, for adults, a common dosage is one tablet taken orally once daily.  Pediatric dosages are carefully calculated based on weight and age and are always determined by a doctor.  The medication is typically available in tablet form.  Liquid formulations may exist but aren't as common.  It's crucial to follow prescribed instructions precisely, including when and how to take the medication. Skipping doses or altering the dosage without medical consultation can compromise treatment effectiveness and potentially lead to drug resistance.

**Side Effects:**

Common side effects can include:

* Nausea
* Vomiting
* Diarrhea
* Headache
* Fatigue
* Dizziness
* Stomach pain
* Rash

Less common, but serious side effects may include:

* Kidney problems (including acute kidney injury)
* Bone loss (osteoporosis)
* Lactic acidosis (a buildup of lactic acid in the blood)
* Liver problems
* Changes in fat distribution in the body (lipodystrophy)
* Pancreatitis


**If you experience any concerning side effects, contact your doctor immediately.**

**How it Works:**

Emtricitabine and tenofovir disoproxil fumarate work by inhibiting HIV's replication. Emtricitabine is a nucleoside reverse transcriptase inhibitor (NRTI) that blocks the enzyme reverse transcriptase, preventing HIV from converting its RNA into DNA.  Tenofovir disoproxil fumarate, also an NRTI, works similarly, preventing the incorporation of viral DNA into the host cell's genome. By stopping this crucial step, the medication significantly reduces the amount of HIV in the body, allowing the immune system to recover and reducing the risk of opportunistic infections and disease progression.

**Precautions:**

* **Kidney function:** This medication can affect kidney function; regular monitoring is necessary, especially for individuals with pre-existing kidney disease or risk factors.
* **Bone health:**  Bone loss is a potential side effect; regular monitoring and lifestyle adjustments may be recommended.
* **Liver function:** Liver problems can occur; regular monitoring might be necessary, especially for individuals with liver disease.
* **Drug interactions:** This medication can interact with other drugs.  It's crucial to inform your doctor of all medications, supplements, and herbal remedies you are taking.
* **Pregnancy and breastfeeding:**  Use during pregnancy or breastfeeding requires careful consideration and monitoring by a healthcare professional.
* **Older adults:**  Older adults may be more susceptible to side effects and require careful dose adjustments.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: Can I stop taking this medication if I feel better?** A: No, it is crucial to continue taking this medication as prescribed by your doctor, even if you feel better. Stopping prematurely can lead to drug resistance and HIV rebound.
* **Q: How should I store this medication?** A: Store the medication in a cool, dry place, away from direct sunlight and moisture.  Follow the instructions provided on the packaging.
* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember unless it is almost time for your next dose.  Never double up on doses; contact your doctor or pharmacist for guidance.
* **Q: Is this medication safe for long-term use?** A: With regular monitoring, this medication is considered safe for long-term use in managing HIV.  However, ongoing monitoring is essential to manage potential long-term side effects.
