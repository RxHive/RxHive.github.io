# Escitalopram Oxalate

**Generic Name: Escitalopram Oxalate**

**Usage:**

Escitalopram oxalate is primarily used to treat major depressive disorder (MDD), also known as clinical depression.  It's a selective serotonin reuptake inhibitor (SSRI), meaning it works by increasing the levels of serotonin, a neurotransmitter in the brain that plays a crucial role in mood regulation.  By boosting serotonin, escitalopram helps alleviate symptoms of depression such as sadness, loss of interest in activities, sleep disturbances, fatigue, and feelings of hopelessness.

Beyond MDD, escitalopram is also sometimes prescribed for:

* **Generalized Anxiety Disorder (GAD):**  It can help manage excessive worry and anxiety that persists for a prolonged period.
* **Obsessive-Compulsive Disorder (OCD):** It's used to reduce the frequency and severity of obsessive thoughts and compulsive behaviors.
* **Panic Disorder:** It can help control panic attacks and the associated anxiety.
* **Post-traumatic Stress Disorder (PTSD):**  While not a first-line treatment, it may be used in some cases to help manage symptoms.
