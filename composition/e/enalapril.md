# Enalapril

**Generic Name: Enalapril**

**Usage:**

Enalapril is an ACE inhibitor (angiotensin-converting enzyme inhibitor), primarily used to treat high blood pressure (hypertension).  Lowering blood pressure reduces the risk of serious complications like stroke, heart attack, and kidney disease.  It's highly effective in managing hypertension, both on its own and in combination with other medications.

Beyond hypertension, enalapril also plays a crucial role in:

* **Heart failure:** Enalapril helps improve the heart's ability to pump blood effectively, easing symptoms and improving quality of life for people with heart failure.  It's often a cornerstone of heart failure treatment.
* **Diabetic nephropathy:**  This is kidney damage caused by diabetes. Enalapril can slow the progression of this damage and help protect kidney function.
* **Myocardial infarction (heart attack):**  After a heart attack, enalapril can help reduce the risk of future cardiovascular events.

The specific use of enalapril will depend on the individual's condition and the doctor's assessment.


**Dosage:**

Dosage is highly individualized and determined by a healthcare professional based on factors like age, weight, kidney function, and the severity of the condition.  

* **Adults (Hypertension):**  Typically starts at a low dose and is gradually increased as needed.  Common starting doses range from 2.5mg to 10mg once or twice daily.
* **Adults (Heart Failure):**  Usually starts at a lower dose and is increased slowly under careful medical supervision.
* **Children:**  Use in children is less common and requires careful dosage adjustment under strict medical guidance.  It's not typically used in infants or very young children.

Enalapril is available in tablet form.  There are no syrup or injectable formulations commonly used.  Always follow your doctor's prescribed dosage and instructions precisely.


**Side Effects:**

* **Common:** Dizziness, lightheadedness, fatigue, headache, dry cough, nausea, diarrhea.  These are usually mild and often subside as the body adjusts to the medication.
* **Less Common but Serious:**  Angioedema (swelling of the face, lips, tongue, or throat – seek immediate medical attention), decreased kidney function, low blood pressure (hypotension), changes in blood potassium levels.


**How it Works:**

Enalapril works by blocking the action of an enzyme called angiotensin-converting enzyme (ACE).  ACE plays a role in raising blood pressure by narrowing blood vessels. By inhibiting ACE, enalapril prevents the formation of angiotensin II, a powerful vasoconstrictor (a substance that constricts blood vessels). This leads to relaxation and widening of blood vessels, lowering blood pressure and reducing strain on the heart.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** Enalapril should not be used by individuals with a history of angioedema related to ACE inhibitors.  Pregnancy (especially during the second and third trimesters) and breastfeeding are also contraindications.
* **Interactions:** Enalapril can interact with several other medications, including potassium-sparing diuretics, potassium supplements, and some NSAIDs (nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drugs).  Inform your doctor about all medications you are taking.
* **Vulnerable Groups:** Older adults and those with kidney problems require careful dose adjustment and monitoring.  Pregnant women should avoid this medication.
* **Safety Measures:**  Regular monitoring of blood pressure and kidney function is often recommended while on enalapril.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: Can I stop taking enalapril suddenly?**  A: No, you should never stop taking enalapril abruptly.  Sudden discontinuation can lead to a rebound increase in blood pressure.  Always consult your doctor before stopping or changing your medication.
* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it is almost time for your next dose.  Do not double the dose to make up for a missed one.
* **Q: How should I store enalapril?** A: Store enalapril in a cool, dry place, away from direct sunlight and moisture.
* **Q: Is enalapril addictive?** A: No, enalapril is not addictive.
* **Q: What should I do if I experience side effects?** A: If you experience any concerning side effects, particularly angioedema, contact your doctor immediately.
