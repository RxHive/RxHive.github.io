# Enzalutamide

**Generic Name:** Enzalutamide

**Usage:**

Enzalutamide is primarily used to treat metastatic castration-resistant prostate cancer (mCRPC).  This means it's used in men with prostate cancer that has spread to other parts of the body and continues to grow even after hormone therapy (castration) has been employed.  It works by blocking the effects of androgens, male hormones that fuel the growth of some prostate cancer cells.  While its primary use is in mCRPC,  it may sometimes be considered in other stages of advanced prostate cancer under specific circumstances, always guided by a physician's assessment.  It's important to understand that enzalutamide is not a cure for prostate cancer, but rather a treatment aimed at slowing its progression and improving quality of life.

**Dosage:**

The standard adult dosage of enzalutamide is typically 160 mg once daily, taken orally with food.  There isn't a specific pediatric formulation or dosage as this medication is primarily used in adult men with prostate cancer. Dosage adjustments based on age, weight, or severity are generally not made unless specifically advised by a healthcare professional due to potential interactions and effects of other medication being used concurrently.  Enzalutamide is available as a capsule.  It's crucial to follow the prescription instructions precisely as prescribed by your doctor.

**Side Effects:**

Common side effects can include:

* Fatigue
* Hot flashes
* Nausea
* Diarrhea
* Decreased appetite
* Constipation
* Dizziness
* Swelling of hands, feet or ankles
* Increased risk of falls

Less common, but serious side effects, warrant immediate medical attention and include:

* Seizures
* Liver problems (jaundice, elevated liver enzymes)
* Serious allergic reactions (rash, hives, difficulty breathing, swelling of face, lips, or tongue)

Always consult your doctor if you experience any concerning side effects.  This list is not exhaustive, and individual experiences can vary.

**How it Works:**

Enzalutamide is an androgen receptor inhibitor.  Androgen receptors are proteins inside prostate cancer cells that bind to male hormones (androgens) like testosterone.  This binding stimulates the growth and spread of the cancer. Enzalutamide blocks these receptors, preventing androgens from binding and thus hindering the cancer's ability to grow and proliferate.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:**  Enzalutamide is contraindicated in patients with known hypersensitivity to the drug.
* **Drug Interactions:** Enzalutamide can interact with several other medications.  Always inform your doctor about all medications, supplements, and herbal remedies you are taking.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:** Enzalutamide is not intended for use in women and may harm an unborn baby. Women of childbearing potential should use effective contraception during treatment and for several weeks after stopping treatment.
* **Elderly Patients:**  Older adults may be more sensitive to side effects.  Careful monitoring is often necessary.
* **Liver Function:** Enzalutamide is primarily metabolized by the liver.  Patients with pre-existing liver disease may require close monitoring of liver function.

**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long do I need to take enzalutamide?** A: The duration of treatment depends on your individual response to the medication and your overall health condition. Your doctor will determine the appropriate length of therapy.
* **Q: Can I stop taking enzalutamide if I feel better?** A: No, do not stop taking enzalutamide without consulting your doctor first. Stopping treatment prematurely could allow the cancer to progress.
* **Q: How should I store enzalutamide?** A: Store enzalutamide at room temperature, away from moisture and direct sunlight. Keep it out of the reach of children.
* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it's almost time for your next dose. Do not take two doses at once to make up for a missed dose.  Consult your doctor or pharmacist for specific advice if you are unsure what to do.
