# Eltrombopag

**Generic Name: Eltrombopag**

**Usage:**

Eltrombopag is a medication primarily used to treat thrombocytopenia, a condition characterized by a low platelet count in the blood.  Platelets are essential for blood clotting, so low levels increase the risk of bleeding.  Its main application is in adults with chronic immune thrombocytopenic purpura (ITP), a condition where the body's immune system mistakenly attacks and destroys its own platelets.  It's also used in adults with hepatitis C who have low platelet counts that prevent them from receiving treatment with interferon-based therapies.  While not a primary use, Eltrombopag may be considered in certain situations for patients with aplastic anemia (a condition where the bone marrow doesn't produce enough blood cells) or those undergoing stem cell transplantation who require platelet support.  The precise use and appropriateness of Eltrombopag always depend on individual patient factors and a physician's assessment.


**Dosage:**

Dosage varies significantly based on the specific condition being treated and the patient's individual response. A healthcare professional will determine the appropriate dose.  Generally, Eltrombopag is available as oral tablets.   Typical adult starting doses for ITP range from 50mg once daily, potentially adjusted upwards based on response and blood platelet levels.  Children's dosing is determined based on their weight and overall health, always under the strict guidance of a pediatric hematologist.  There is no standard syrup or injectable formulation widely available. It's crucial to follow the prescribed dosage and schedule precisely as directed by your doctor or pharmacist.


**Side Effects:**

* **Common:** Nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, fatigue, headache, dizziness, abdominal pain.
* **Less Common but Serious:**  Serious allergic reactions (anaphylaxis), liver problems (hepatotoxicity),  thrombosis (blood clots), vision changes.
* **Note:** This is not an exhaustive list.  Any new or worsening symptoms should be reported to a healthcare provider immediately.

**How it Works:**

Eltrombopag works by mimicking the action of thrombopoietin (TPO), a natural hormone that stimulates the production of platelets in the bone marrow.  By binding to TPO receptors on platelet-producing cells, Eltrombopag triggers increased platelet production, thereby raising the platelet count in the blood to safer levels and reducing the risk of bleeding.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** Eltrombopag is contraindicated in individuals with known hypersensitivity (allergy) to the drug.
* **Drug Interactions:**  It's crucial to inform your doctor about all other medications you are taking, including over-the-counter drugs and herbal supplements, as interactions are possible.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:** Eltrombopag should be used with extreme caution during pregnancy and breastfeeding, only if the potential benefits clearly outweigh the risks.  A physician should assess the potential risks to both mother and child.
* **Older Adults:**  Older adults may be more susceptible to certain side effects and may require dose adjustments.
* **Liver Impairment:**  Eltrombopag should be used with caution in patients with liver problems, as it's primarily metabolized by the liver.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long does it take to work?** A: The time it takes to see an effect varies but improvements in platelet counts are usually observed within several weeks.
* **Q: Can I stop taking it suddenly?** A: No, stopping abruptly can lead to a rapid decrease in platelet count.  Always follow your doctor's instructions for tapering off the medication.
* **Q: How should I store it?** A: Store it as directed on the product label, usually at room temperature away from moisture and direct sunlight.
* **Q:  What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it is almost time for the next dose. Do not double the dose.
* **Q: Is it safe to drink alcohol while taking Eltrombopag?** A:  It's best to discuss alcohol consumption with your doctor.  Alcohol may interact with the medication or exacerbate some side effects.
