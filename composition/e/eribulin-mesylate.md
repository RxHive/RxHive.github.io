# Eribulin Mesylate

**Generic Name:** Eribulin Mesylate

**Usage:**

Eribulin mesylate is a chemotherapy drug primarily used to treat certain types of advanced cancer.  Its main application is in the treatment of metastatic breast cancer that has progressed after treatment with other chemotherapies, including taxanes and anthracyclines.  It's also approved for the treatment of liposarcoma (a type of soft tissue sarcoma) and pleuropulmonary blastoma (a rare cancer affecting the lungs and pleura) in adults who have received prior chemotherapy.  While primarily used for these specific cancers, ongoing research explores its potential in other cancers as well.  It's crucial to understand that eribulin mesylate is not a "cure" for cancer, but rather a treatment aimed at slowing its progression and potentially extending life.

**Dosage:**

Eribulin mesylate is administered intravenously (IV) by a healthcare professional.  There's no oral or syrup form available.  The dosage is determined by the healthcare provider based on factors like the patient's overall health, body weight, and the specific type and stage of cancer.  Generally, the standard dose is 1.4 mg/m² administered over 2-5 minutes on days 1 and 8 of a 21-day cycle.  This dose may be adjusted or the treatment schedule altered depending on the patient's response to the medication and any side effects experienced.  Dosage information specific to children is not readily available as the drug is primarily used in adult populations with the indicated cancers.

**Side Effects:**

Common side effects can include:

* Neutropenia (low white blood cell count)
* Fatigue
* Alopecia (hair loss)
* Nausea and vomiting
* Constipation
* Peripheral neuropathy (numbness and tingling in the extremities)
* Anemia (low red blood cell count)
* Thrombocytopenia (low platelet count)

Less common but serious side effects can include:

* Severe allergic reactions
* Serious infections
* Cardiac problems
* Liver toxicity


It is crucial to report any side effects, both common and serious, to your doctor immediately.  They can adjust the dosage or suggest supportive care to manage these side effects.


**How it Works:**

Eribulin mesylate works by interfering with the microtubules within cancer cells. Microtubules are essential components of the cell's structure and play a key role in cell division. Eribulin mesylate binds to microtubules, preventing their normal function. This disruption halts cell division and ultimately leads to the death of cancer cells.  This mechanism differs from that of many other chemotherapies, offering a potential benefit in situations where other treatments have failed.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** Eribulin mesylate should not be used in patients with hypersensitivity to the drug or its components.
* **Drug Interactions:**  Eribulin mesylate may interact with other medications. It's essential to inform your doctor about all medications, including over-the-counter drugs, herbal supplements, and vitamins, you are taking.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:** Eribulin mesylate is known to be harmful to a developing fetus. Women of childbearing age should use effective contraception during treatment and for a specified period after treatment.  Breastfeeding should be discontinued during treatment.
* **Elderly Patients:**  Elderly patients may be more susceptible to side effects. Careful monitoring is required.
* **Hepatic Impairment:**  Patients with liver problems may need dosage adjustments.
* **Cardiac Function:**  Eribulin mesylate can affect cardiac function; careful monitoring is essential.
