# Evolocumab

**Generic Name: Evolocumab**

**Usage:**

Evolocumab is a medication primarily used to lower low-density lipoprotein cholesterol (LDL-C), often referred to as "bad" cholesterol.  Its main application is in adults with high cholesterol, particularly those with a high risk of cardiovascular disease (CVD). This risk can stem from various factors including a family history of heart disease, existing heart conditions, diabetes, or persistently high cholesterol levels despite lifestyle changes and other cholesterol-lowering medications (statins).  Evolocumab is typically used *in addition to* statins, not as a replacement.  In some cases, it might be used alone if statins aren't tolerated or are ineffective.  While its primary use focuses on reducing LDL-C, lowering cholesterol levels ultimately aims to decrease the risk of heart attack, stroke, and other cardiovascular events.


**Dosage:**

Evolocumab is administered as a subcutaneous injection (under the skin).  Dosage is typically determined by a healthcare professional based on individual needs and response to treatment.  There isn't a standard dose applicable to everyone.  A common starting dose for adults might be 140 mg every two weeks, but this can be adjusted upward or downward depending on cholesterol levels and individual response.  Evolocumab is not approved for use in children, and its use in pregnant or breastfeeding women requires careful consideration and discussion with a doctor due to a lack of sufficient data on safety during these periods.


**Side Effects:**

Common side effects can include:

* Injection site reactions (pain, redness, swelling, bruising)
* Upper respiratory tract infections
* Back pain
* Muscle aches
* Nausea


Less common, but serious side effects, requiring immediate medical attention, can include:

* Allergic reactions (rash, hives, difficulty breathing, swelling of the face, lips, or tongue)
* Liver damage


It's crucial to report any unusual or concerning side effects to a doctor immediately.


**How it Works:**

Evolocumab is a monoclonal antibody.  It works by targeting and binding to a protein called PCSK9 in the blood.  PCSK9 normally interferes with the liver's ability to remove LDL-C from the bloodstream. By blocking PCSK9, evolocumab helps the liver more effectively clear LDL-C, thus reducing cholesterol levels.

**Precautions:**

* **Allergies:** Individuals with known allergies to evolocumab or any of its components should not use this medication.
* **Liver problems:**  Evolocumab should be used cautiously in patients with liver disease. Liver function tests are often monitored during treatment.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:** The safety of evolocumab during pregnancy and breastfeeding hasn't been fully established.  Its use during these periods should be discussed extensively with a doctor.
* **Other medications:** Evolocumab may interact with other medications. It's essential to inform your doctor about all medications, supplements, and herbal remedies you are taking.
* **Injection site reactions:**  Proper injection technique is important to minimize injection site reactions.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long does it take to see results?** A:  Reductions in LDL-C are usually seen within a few weeks of starting treatment.
* **Q: Can I stop taking evolocumab if my cholesterol is under control?** A:  Dosage adjustments and the continuation of treatment should be determined by your doctor based on your ongoing cholesterol levels and overall cardiovascular risk.  Do not discontinue treatment without consulting your doctor.
* **Q: How should I store evolocumab?** A:  Storage instructions will be provided with the medication; generally, it should be refrigerated.
* **Q: Is evolocumab a cure for high cholesterol?** A: No, it's a medication to manage high cholesterol and reduce its associated risks. Lifestyle modifications like diet and exercise remain important.
* **Q:  Is evolocumab suitable for everyone with high cholesterol?** A:  No.  Your doctor will assess your individual needs and risk factors to determine if evolocumab is appropriate for you.
