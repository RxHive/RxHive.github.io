# Everolimus

**Generic Name: Everolimus**

**Usage:** Everolimus is a powerful medication primarily used to target and inhibit the growth of cancer cells.  Its main applications lie in treating certain types of kidney cancer (renal cell carcinoma),  neuroendocrine tumors (NETs),  breast cancer (specifically hormone receptor-positive, HER2-negative advanced breast cancer), and  lymphangioleiomyomatosis (LAM), a rare lung disease.  It's also sometimes used in combination with other therapies for these conditions.  While its primary use is in oncology,  research is ongoing to explore its potential in other areas.  The specific use of everolimus will always be determined by a medical professional considering the patient's individual circumstances and diagnosis.


**Dosage:**  Everolimus dosage is highly individualized and depends on many factors, including the specific condition being treated, the patient's age, weight, overall health, and response to the medication.  It's usually administered orally as tablets.  A doctor will determine the correct starting dose and may adjust it based on the patient's response and any side effects experienced.  There's no standardized pediatric dosage as the use in children is limited to specific cancer indications under strict medical supervision.  Precise dosage information must come directly from a prescribing physician, who will carefully tailor it to the individual's needs.  The medication should always be taken exactly as prescribed.


**Side Effects:** Everolimus, like many cancer treatments, can cause side effects.  Some common side effects include:

*   Mouth sores (stomatitis)
*   Fatigue
*   Diarrhea
*   Nausea
*   Rash
*   Loss of appetite
*   Pneumonitis (inflammation of the lungs - a serious side effect)
*   Increased cholesterol levels
*   Increased risk of infection


Less common but serious side effects can also occur.  Any new or worsening symptoms should be reported to a doctor immediately.  This list is not exhaustive, and other side effects are possible.  Your doctor will be able to provide more detailed information specific to your situation.


**How it Works:** Everolimus is a type of drug called an mTOR inhibitor.  mTOR (mammalian target of rapamycin) is a protein that plays a crucial role in cell growth, division, and metabolism.  In many cancers, mTOR is overactive, leading to uncontrolled cell growth. Everolimus works by binding to and inhibiting mTOR, thereby slowing down or stopping the growth of cancer cells.  This targeted approach minimizes damage to healthy cells, although some side effects are unfortunately unavoidable.

**Precautions:**

*   **Contraindications:** Everolimus is contraindicated in patients with severe hypersensitivity to everolimus or any of its components.
*   **Drug Interactions:** Everolimus can interact with several other medications.  It's crucial to inform your doctor about all medications, supplements, and herbal remedies you are taking.
*   **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:** Everolimus is known to harm a developing fetus and is contraindicated during pregnancy.  Women of childbearing potential should use effective contraception during treatment.  It's also not recommended during breastfeeding.
*   **Older Adults and Children:**  Older adults may be more susceptible to side effects.  Use in children is limited and only in specific cancer indications under close medical supervision.  Careful monitoring is essential for both age groups.
*   **Infections:**  Everolimus can increase the risk of infections.  Any signs of infection should be reported immediately.

*   **Q: Can I stop taking everolimus abruptly?** A: No.  Always follow your doctor's instructions for stopping the medication.  Sudden cessation may be harmful.

*   **Q: Are there any long-term effects of everolimus?** A:  Long-term effects can vary.  Regular monitoring by your doctor is important to manage potential risks and side effects.
