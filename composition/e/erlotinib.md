# Erlotinib

**Generic Name: Erlotinib**

**Usage:**

Erlotinib is a targeted cancer therapy primarily used to treat non-small cell lung cancer (NSCLC).  Its main application is in patients whose cancer has progressed after treatment with certain chemotherapy regimens, or in those with specific genetic mutations (like EGFR mutations) making their cancer particularly sensitive to this medication.  While primarily used for lung cancer, erlotinib may also be used in other advanced cancers showing sensitivity to EGFR inhibition, though these are less common and would be determined by a physician on a case-by-case basis.  It’s crucial to understand that erlotinib is not a cure for cancer but rather a treatment aimed at slowing disease progression and improving quality of life.

**Dosage:**

Erlotinib is typically administered orally as a tablet.  The standard adult dose is usually 150 mg once daily. Dosage adjustments are sometimes necessary based on individual patient factors such as kidney or liver function, and are made under the close supervision of an oncologist. There is no established pediatric dosage as its use in children is limited.  The medication should be taken at roughly the same time each day, usually with or without food, according to physician instructions.  Skipping doses should be avoided, and any changes to the prescribed dosage must be made in consultation with the prescribing physician.

**Side Effects:**

Common side effects of erlotinib can include:

*   Diarrhea
*   Rash
*   Nausea
*   Fatigue
*   Loss of appetite
*   Cough
*   Shortness of breath

Less common but more serious side effects can involve:

*   Severe skin reactions (such as Stevens-Johnson syndrome)
*   Interstitial lung disease (inflammation of the lung tissue)
*   Liver problems
*   Severe diarrhea
*   Severe dehydration


If any of these side effects occur, especially if they are severe or persistent, it is crucial to contact a doctor immediately. Early intervention can often help manage or mitigate these side effects.


**How it Works:**

Erlotinib is a tyrosine kinase inhibitor (TKI).  Tyrosine kinases are proteins involved in cell growth and division.  In many cancers, particularly NSCLC with EGFR mutations, these kinases are overactive, leading to uncontrolled cell growth. Erlotinib works by selectively blocking the activity of these specific tyrosine kinases, thereby inhibiting the growth and spread of cancer cells.  It’s important to note this drug targets specific types of cancer cells and may not be effective against all types of cancer.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** Erlotinib should not be used in individuals with known hypersensitivity (allergy) to the drug or any of its components.
* **Drug Interactions:** Erlotinib can interact with other medications.  It’s vital to inform your doctor about all medications, supplements, and herbal remedies you are taking.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:** Erlotinib is generally not recommended during pregnancy or breastfeeding due to potential harm to the developing fetus or infant.  Discuss the risks and benefits with your doctor if pregnancy is a possibility.
* **Older Adults and Children:**  While dosage adjustments may be needed for older adults based on kidney or liver function, erlotinib is primarily used in adults. Use in children is limited due to lack of adequate data.
* **Liver and Kidney Function:** Individuals with impaired liver or kidney function may require dosage adjustments or closer monitoring.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long will I need to take erlotinib?** A: The duration of treatment with erlotinib depends on your individual response to the medication and the progression of your cancer. Your doctor will determine the appropriate treatment duration.

* **Q: Can I stop taking erlotinib if I feel better?** A: No, stopping treatment without consulting your doctor can be harmful.  Even if you feel better, continued treatment is crucial for managing the cancer.

* **Q: How should I store erlotinib?** A: Store erlotinib at room temperature away from moisture and heat.  Follow the specific storage instructions provided on the medication label.

* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it is almost time for the next dose.  Do not double the dose to make up for a missed dose.

* **Q: Are there any long-term effects of erlotinib?** A: Like all medications, erlotinib can have potential long-term side effects. Regular monitoring and check-ups with your oncologist are essential to identify and manage any potential long-term issues.
