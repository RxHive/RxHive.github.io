# Ebastine + Montelukast

**Generic Name: Ebastine + Montelukast**

**Usage:**

Ebastine + Montelukast is a combination medication used primarily to treat allergic rhinitis (hay fever) and asthma in both adults and children.  Allergic rhinitis is characterized by symptoms like sneezing, runny nose, itchy eyes and nose, and nasal congestion. Asthma involves inflammation and narrowing of the airways in the lungs, leading to wheezing, coughing, shortness of breath, and chest tightness.  This combination drug tackles these conditions by addressing different aspects of the allergic response and airway inflammation.  While primarily used for these conditions, it may sometimes be considered for other allergic respiratory issues, but this should always be under the guidance of a healthcare professional.

**Dosage:**

Dosage varies significantly based on age, weight, and the specific condition being treated.  A doctor will prescribe the appropriate dose.  The medication typically comes in tablet form, though syrup formulations might exist for children.  Precise instructions will accompany the prescription.  It is crucial to follow the prescribed dosage regimen precisely; do not adjust the dosage without consulting your doctor.

**Side Effects:**

* **Common Side Effects:** Headache, drowsiness, nausea, diarrhea, dizziness, fatigue.  These are generally mild and temporary.
* **Less Common but Serious Side Effects:**  Rarely, serious side effects can occur, including allergic reactions (rash, itching, swelling, difficulty breathing), liver problems, and changes in mood or behavior.  
* **Important Note:**  If you experience any concerning side effects, stop taking the medication and seek immediate medical attention.

**How it Works:**

This combination medication works through a dual mechanism.  Ebastine is an antihistamine, which blocks the action of histamine, a chemical released by the body during an allergic reaction.  This helps reduce the symptoms of allergic rhinitis. Montelukast is a leukotriene receptor antagonist; it blocks leukotrienes, inflammatory substances that contribute to both allergic rhinitis and asthma.  By inhibiting both histamine and leukotrienes, the medication effectively reduces inflammation and alleviates symptoms.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:**  Individuals with known hypersensitivity to either ebastine or montelukast should not use this medication.
* **Drug Interactions:**  Inform your doctor about all medications you are currently taking, including over-the-counter drugs and herbal supplements, as interactions can occur.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  The use of ebastine + montelukast during pregnancy and breastfeeding should be carefully considered and discussed with a healthcare professional.
* **Children and Older Adults:**  Dosage adjustments may be necessary for children and older adults based on their age and overall health.
* **Liver and Kidney Disease:** Individuals with impaired liver or kidney function may require dosage adjustments or careful monitoring.

**FAQs:**

* **Q: Can I take this medication with alcohol?**  A:  Alcohol may increase drowsiness. Avoid alcohol consumption while taking this medication.
* **Q: How should I store this medication?**  A: Store at room temperature, away from moisture and direct sunlight.
* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?**  A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it is almost time for the next dose. Do not take a double dose.
* **Q: Can this medication cause weight gain?**  A: Weight changes are not commonly reported as a side effect. However, any significant weight changes should be discussed with a doctor.
* **Q:  Can I stop taking this medication suddenly?**  A: No, abruptly stopping this medication might worsen your symptoms. Consult your doctor before stopping treatment.
