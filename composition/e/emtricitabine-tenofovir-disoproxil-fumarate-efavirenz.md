# Emtricitabine + Tenofovir disoproxil fumarate + Efavirenz

**Generic Name:** Emtricitabine + Tenofovir disoproxil fumarate + Efavirenz

**Usage:** This combination medication is a potent antiretroviral therapy (ART) primarily used to treat Human Immunodeficiency Virus (HIV) infection in adults and children.  Its primary use is to suppress the HIV virus, reducing the viral load in the blood and improving the CD4 cell count (a type of immune cell depleted by HIV).  This helps to improve the immune system's strength and slow the progression of HIV to Acquired Immunodeficiency Syndrome (AIDS).  While not a cure, this combination therapy is crucial in managing HIV and improving the quality of life for those infected.  This specific combination is particularly useful for its effectiveness and once-daily dosing convenience.

**Dosage:**  Dosage is determined by a healthcare professional and varies based on factors such as age, weight, and overall health.  The typical adult dosage is one tablet taken orally once daily.  Pediatric dosing is determined based on weight and age by the prescribing physician.  This medication is available only as a fixed-dose combination tablet; there are no syrup or injection forms.  It's crucial to follow the prescribed dosage and schedule precisely.  Never adjust the dose without consulting a doctor.

**Side Effects:**

* **Common Side Effects:**  Nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, headache, dizziness, fatigue, rash, insomnia, and abnormal dreams.
* **Less Common but Serious Side Effects:**  Severe skin reactions (Stevens-Johnson syndrome), liver problems (hepatitis), pancreatitis, depression, suicidal thoughts, and peripheral neuropathy (nerve damage in the hands and feet).
* **Note:** This list is not exhaustive.  If you experience any unexpected or concerning side effects, seek immediate medical attention.

**How it Works:** This medication is a combination of three different antiretroviral drugs:

* **Emtricitabine:**  A nucleoside reverse transcriptase inhibitor (NRTI). It works by interfering with the HIV virus's ability to copy its genetic material, thus hindering its replication.
* **Tenofovir disoproxil fumarate:** Another NRTI, similar in mechanism to emtricitabine.  It also blocks the virus's replication process.
* **Efavirenz:** A non-nucleoside reverse transcriptase inhibitor (NNRTI). It works by a different mechanism than the NRTIs, preventing the virus from producing new copies of itself.  The combination of these three drugs, with different mechanisms, makes it harder for the virus to develop resistance.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:**  Efavirenz is contraindicated in individuals with significant liver disease.  This medication should not be taken with certain other drugs due to potential interactions.
* **Drug Interactions:**  Many drugs can interact with this combination, including some herbal supplements. It's essential to inform your doctor about all medications, supplements, and herbal remedies you are taking.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:** Use during pregnancy should be discussed with a healthcare professional to weigh risks and benefits.  This medication can pass into breast milk, so breastfeeding may not be recommended while taking it.
* **Elderly and Children:**  Dosage adjustments may be necessary for older adults or children. Close monitoring is essential.

**FAQs:**

* **Q: Can I stop taking this medication if I feel better?** A: No. Stopping treatment prematurely can lead to the development of drug resistance and a faster progression of HIV.  Always follow your doctor's instructions.
* **Q: How should I store this medication?** A: Store at room temperature, away from moisture and direct sunlight. Keep out of reach of children.
* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it is almost time for your next dose. Do not double the dose to make up for a missed dose.
* **Q: Can this medication cure HIV?** A: No, this medication does not cure HIV, but it helps to manage the infection and prevent progression to AIDS.  It's vital to adhere to treatment as prescribed.
