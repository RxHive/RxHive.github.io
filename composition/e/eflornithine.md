# Eflornithine

**Generic Name: Eflornithine**

**Usage:**

Eflornithine is primarily used to treat African trypanosomiasis, also known as sleeping sickness. This is a parasitic disease spread by the tsetse fly, causing severe neurological and systemic problems if left untreated.  It's a life-threatening illness, and eflornithine is a crucial part of treatment regimens, often in combination with other medications.  While its primary use is for sleeping sickness, it also has a secondary, albeit much less common, use in treating some types of facial hair growth (hirsutism) in women.  This application is often considered off-label and should only be undertaken under strict medical supervision due to the drug's potential side effects and the availability of other, possibly better-suited treatments.

**Dosage:**

Dosage for eflornithine varies greatly depending on the condition being treated and the patient's individual characteristics.  It's crucial to follow a doctor's specific instructions meticulously.  The medication is typically administered intravenously (IV) for sleeping sickness treatment.  Precise dosage and duration are determined by a healthcare professional based on factors like the patient's weight, the stage of the infection, and their overall health.  For hirsutism, topical creams are sometimes used, but this is an off-label application, and dosages would be guided entirely by the prescribing physician.  There is no standard pediatric dosage for sleeping sickness as treatment protocols are specific to the individual's condition.


**Side Effects:**

Common side effects can include:

* Nausea
* Vomiting
* Diarrhea
* Headaches
* Dizziness
* Seizures (rare but serious)
* Changes in blood counts
* Allergic reactions (rash, itching, swelling)

Less common, but potentially serious side effects, necessitate immediate medical attention. These may include severe allergic reactions, significant changes in blood pressure, or neurological symptoms.  Any unusual or concerning symptoms should be reported to a healthcare provider immediately.

**How it Works:**

Eflornithine works by inhibiting an enzyme called ornithine decarboxylase, which is essential for the growth and survival of the *Trypanosoma brucei* parasite that causes sleeping sickness. By blocking this enzyme, the drug interferes with the parasite's ability to replicate and ultimately helps to eliminate the infection. In the case of its use for hirsutism, the mechanism is less well understood but is believed to relate to its effect on cell growth and possibly hair follicle function.

* **Children and Older Adults:**  Dosage adjustments may be necessary for children and older adults due to potential differences in metabolism and sensitivity to the drug.  Close monitoring is required.
* **Kidney and Liver Function:** Individuals with impaired kidney or liver function may require dose adjustments or close monitoring to minimize the risk of adverse effects.
