# Exemestane

**Generic Name: Exemestane**

**Usage:**

Exemestane is an aromatase inhibitor.  Its primary use is in the treatment of hormone-receptor-positive breast cancer in postmenopausal women.  This means it's effective against breast cancers that depend on estrogen for growth.  Exemestane works by blocking the enzyme aromatase, which is responsible for converting androgens (male hormones) into estrogens (female hormones). By lowering estrogen levels, it helps to slow or stop the growth of these types of breast cancers.  While its primary use is in postmenopausal women with advanced breast cancer, it may also be used in adjuvant therapy (treatment after surgery to reduce the risk of cancer recurrence) in these same patients.  It's important to note that exemestane is not suitable for premenopausal women or men due to its mechanism of action.

**Dosage:**

The standard dosage for adults (postmenopausal women) is typically one 25 mg tablet taken orally once daily.  Dosage adjustments are not usually based on age or weight, but the physician will determine the appropriate course of treatment based on the individual patient's health and response to the medication.  Exemestane is available only as an oral tablet; there are no syrup or injectable forms.  It should be taken at the same time each day with or without food, as directed by the prescribing physician.

**Side Effects:**

* **Common Side Effects:** Hot flashes, joint pain (arthralgia), muscle pain (myalgia), fatigue, nausea, vomiting, rash, hair thinning.
* **Less Common, but Serious Side Effects:**  Increased risk of osteoporosis (weakening of bones), cardiovascular events (heart attack, stroke),  liver problems (hepatotoxicity - rare).  Allergic reactions, though rare, can be serious.

**Note:**  This list is not exhaustive.  If you experience any adverse effects, particularly those that are persistent, worsening, or concerning, you should immediately contact your healthcare provider.

**How it Works:**

Exemestane works by irreversibly inhibiting the aromatase enzyme.  Aromatase is crucial in the conversion of androgens to estrogens.  By blocking this enzyme, exemestane significantly reduces estrogen levels in the body.  This reduction in estrogen deprives hormone-receptor-positive breast cancer cells of the fuel they need to grow and proliferate, thus slowing or halting tumor progression.  It’s important to understand that it targets estrogen production, not the estrogen already present in the body.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** Exemestane is contraindicated in premenopausal women and men.  It should not be used during pregnancy or breastfeeding.
* **Drug Interactions:** Exemestane can interact with other medications.  Always inform your doctor and pharmacist of all medications, supplements, and herbal remedies you are taking.
* **Vulnerable Groups:**  Older adults may be at increased risk of osteoporosis and cardiovascular events.  Careful monitoring is necessary.  The medication is contraindicated in pregnant and breastfeeding women.  Children should not take this medication.
* **Safety Measures:**  Regular monitoring of bone mineral density (due to osteoporosis risk) and liver function is recommended during treatment.

* **Q: How should I store Exemestane?** A: Store it at room temperature, away from moisture and light, as directed on the label.
* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it is almost time for the next dose.  Do not double the dose.
* **Q: Is Exemestane addictive?** A: No, Exemestane is not considered an addictive medication.
* **Q: How long will I need to take Exemestane?** A: The duration of treatment is determined by your doctor based on your individual needs and response to the medication.
