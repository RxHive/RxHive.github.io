# Enalapril + Hydrochlorothiazide

**Generic Name: Enalapril + Hydrochlorothiazide**

**Usage:**

Enalapril + Hydrochlorothiazide is a combination medication used primarily to treat hypertension (high blood pressure).  It's particularly effective for people whose blood pressure isn't adequately controlled with either enalapril or hydrochlorothiazide alone.  Enalapril is an ACE inhibitor, which works by relaxing blood vessels, and hydrochlorothiazide is a thiazide diuretic, which helps the body get rid of excess salt and water. This combined action leads to a more significant reduction in blood pressure than either drug could achieve individually.

While primarily used for hypertension, this combination may also be prescribed for heart failure, specifically to reduce the symptoms and improve the overall prognosis for patients with mild to moderate heart failure.  It's crucial to understand that this medication is prescribed by a doctor and the specific use will depend on individual needs and medical history.


**Dosage:**

Dosage is determined individually by a healthcare professional and depends on the patient's condition and response to treatment.  There's no standard dosage for children.  This medication is typically available as an oral tablet.  Common adult starting doses might range from a low dose (e.g., 5mg/12.5mg) to higher doses (e.g., 20mg/25mg), with adjustments based on blood pressure readings and individual tolerance.  The medication is usually taken once daily.  Always follow your doctor’s instructions precisely.  Never alter your dosage without consulting your physician.


**Side Effects:**

* **Common Side Effects:** Dizziness, lightheadedness, headache, fatigue, dry cough, nausea, diarrhea, muscle cramps.
* **Less Common, but Serious Side Effects:**  Angioedema (swelling of the face, lips, tongue, or throat – seek immediate medical attention), kidney problems, low blood pressure, hyperkalemia (high potassium levels in the blood), dehydration, changes in blood sugar levels.  This list isn't exhaustive, and other side effects are possible.

**Important Note:** If you experience any concerning side effects, contact your doctor immediately.


**How it Works:**

Enalapril works by blocking the action of an enzyme called angiotensin-converting enzyme (ACE).  ACE is involved in the body's system that regulates blood pressure. By blocking ACE, enalapril prevents the constriction of blood vessels, leading to lower blood pressure. Hydrochlorothiazide works by increasing the excretion of sodium and water from the body through the urine. This reduces blood volume, which further contributes to lower blood pressure.  The combination of these two mechanisms provides a synergistic effect, leading to a more substantial and sustained reduction in blood pressure.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** This medication shouldn't be taken by people with certain kidney problems, liver disease, or those who are allergic to ACE inhibitors or sulfa drugs (hydrochlorothiazide is a sulfa drug).
* **Drug Interactions:** Many medications can interact with enalapril + hydrochlorothiazide.  Always inform your doctor or pharmacist about all medications, supplements, and herbal remedies you're taking.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  This medication is generally not recommended during pregnancy or breastfeeding. Consult your doctor immediately if you are pregnant, plan to become pregnant, or are breastfeeding.
* **Older Adults and Children:** Dosage adjustments might be necessary for older adults and children due to potential variations in kidney and liver function.
* **Driving and Operating Machinery:** Dizziness and lightheadedness are potential side effects.  Avoid driving or operating machinery until you know how this medication affects you.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long does it take to work?** A: The full effect of the medication may take several weeks to achieve.
* **Q: Can I stop taking it suddenly?** A: No, abruptly stopping can be harmful.  Always follow your doctor's instructions for tapering off the medication.
* **Q: How should I store it?** A: Store the medication in a cool, dry place, away from direct sunlight and moisture. Keep it out of reach of children.
* **Q: What if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it's almost time for the next dose.  Never double the dose.
* **Q: Is it safe to drink alcohol while taking this medication?** A: Alcohol can worsen the side effects of this medication, particularly low blood pressure. Limit or avoid alcohol consumption while taking this medication.
