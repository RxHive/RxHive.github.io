# Enclomiphene + Acetylcysteine

**Generic Name:** Enclomiphene + Acetylcysteine

**Usage:**  Currently, there is no established medical use for a fixed-dose combination of enclomiphene and acetylcysteine.  Enclomiphene and acetylcysteine are used separately for distinct medical purposes.  Enclomiphene is a selective estrogen receptor modulator (SERM) primarily used off-label in some regions to treat male infertility caused by low sperm count or poor sperm motility. Acetylcysteine is a mucolytic agent used to thin and loosen mucus in the lungs, primarily to treat conditions like chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD) and cystic fibrosis.  The combination of these two drugs is not a standard therapeutic approach.  Any purported use of this combination should be considered unproven and potentially unsafe without rigorous clinical trial support.

*   **Acetylcysteine:** Breaks down disulfide bonds in mucus, reducing its viscosity. This makes it easier to cough up and clear mucus from the airways.
