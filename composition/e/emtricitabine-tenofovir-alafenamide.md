# Emtricitabine + Tenofovir Alafenamide

**Generic Name:** Emtricitabine + Tenofovir Alafenamide

**Usage:**

Emtricitabine + Tenofovir Alafenamide is a combination antiviral medication primarily used to treat HIV-1 infection in adults and children weighing at least 25 kg (approximately 55 lbs).  It's a crucial part of antiretroviral therapy (ART), a combination of drugs that suppress the HIV virus, preventing it from replicating and damaging the immune system.  While its primary use is in treating HIV, it's also sometimes used pre-exposure prophylaxis (PrEP), meaning it can be taken by HIV-negative individuals at high risk of infection to help prevent them from contracting the virus.  However, the use of this combination specifically for PrEP is less common than other approved PrEP regimens.  The effectiveness of this combination therapy hinges on consistent adherence to the prescribed dosage.


**Dosage:**

The typical adult dosage is one tablet taken orally once daily with food.  Dosage for children weighing at least 25 kg should be determined by a healthcare professional based on weight and individual needs.  This medication is usually available as a single-tablet formulation, simplifying adherence. There are no syrup or injection forms currently available for this specific combination.  It's crucial to follow the prescribed dosage and regimen exactly as directed by a doctor.  Adjustments may be necessary based on factors such as kidney function and other health considerations.


**Side Effects:**

Common side effects can include:

* Nausea
* Diarrhea
* Headache
* Dizziness
* Fatigue
* Stomach pain
* Vomiting


Less common, but more serious side effects may include:

* Severe allergic reactions (rash, difficulty breathing, swelling of the face, lips, tongue, or throat)
* Kidney problems (reduced kidney function, kidney failure)
* Changes in bone mineral density
* Lactic acidosis (a buildup of lactic acid in the blood)
* Liver problems


If you experience any of these side effects, particularly the serious ones, seek immediate medical attention.


**How it Works:**

Emtricitabine and tenofovir alafenamide are both nucleoside/nucleotide reverse transcriptase inhibitors (NRTIs).  They work by interfering with the HIV virus's ability to replicate itself.  Specifically, they block the enzyme reverse transcriptase, which is vital for the virus to convert its RNA into DNA, a step necessary for it to integrate into the host's cells.  By inhibiting this enzyme, the drugs reduce the viral load, improving the immune system's ability to fight the infection.  Tenofovir alafenamide is a newer version of tenofovir, offering improved absorption and reduced kidney side effects compared to its predecessor, tenofovir disoproxil fumarate.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** This medication should not be taken by individuals with known hypersensitivity (allergy) to emtricitabine, tenofovir alafenamide, or any components of the formulation.
* **Drug Interactions:**  This medication can interact with several other drugs.  It is crucial to inform your doctor about all medications, supplements, and herbal remedies you are taking.
* **Kidney Function:**  Individuals with pre-existing kidney problems should be closely monitored while taking this medication, as it can affect kidney function.  Kidney function tests are usually recommended.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:** This medication should only be used during pregnancy or breastfeeding under strict medical supervision.  The benefits must outweigh the potential risks to the mother and child.
* **Older Adults:**  Older adults may require dosage adjustments due to potential age-related changes in kidney function.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: Can I stop taking this medication if I feel better?** A: No.  Stopping the medication prematurely can lead to drug resistance and a worsening of the infection.  It's essential to continue treatment as prescribed by your doctor.
* **Q: How should I store this medication?** A: Store the medication at room temperature, away from moisture and direct sunlight.
* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it is almost time for the next dose.  Do not take a double dose to make up for a missed one.
* **Q: Can I drink alcohol while taking this medication?** A: Moderate alcohol consumption is generally acceptable, but excessive alcohol use may exacerbate certain side effects.  It's best to discuss alcohol consumption with your doctor.
* **Q: Are there long-term effects associated with this medication?** A: Long-term use may be associated with bone density changes and kidney issues. Regular monitoring by a healthcare professional is crucial.
