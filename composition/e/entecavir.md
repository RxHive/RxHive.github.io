# Entecavir

**Generic Name: Entecavir**

**Usage:**

Entecavir is an antiviral medication primarily used to treat chronic hepatitis B (HBV) infection in adults and children.  It's a highly effective drug that works by reducing the amount of HBV in the body, thus slowing the progression of liver disease.  This, in turn, can help prevent serious complications like cirrhosis (scarring of the liver) and liver cancer. While its primary use is in chronic HBV, it's sometimes considered in cases of acute HBV infection under specific circumstances and guided by a physician's expertise.  The decision to use entecavir in acute HBV is based on factors such as the severity of the infection and the patient's overall health.


**Dosage:**

Dosage of entecavir varies depending on the patient's age, weight, and the severity of their HBV infection.  It's crucial to follow a doctor's prescribed dosage exactly.  

* **Adults with chronic HBV:**  The typical dose is one 0.5 mg tablet once daily.  In some cases, a doctor may adjust the dosage to 1 mg daily.
* **Children with chronic HBV:**  Dosage for children is determined by weight and age and should be strictly as prescribed by a healthcare professional.  Liquid formulations may be used for children who cannot swallow tablets.

Entecavir is available as an oral tablet.  It's usually taken once daily, with or without food.  It’s essential to take the medication at the same time each day to maintain consistent levels in the bloodstream.  Never alter the dosage or stop taking the medication without consulting a doctor.


**Side Effects:**

Common side effects are usually mild and may include:

* Headache
* Fatigue
* Nausea
* Diarrhea
* Abdominal pain

Less common, but more serious, side effects require immediate medical attention and include:

* Severe allergic reactions (rash, swelling, difficulty breathing)
* Liver problems (jaundice, dark urine, pale stools)
* Muscle pain
* Lactic acidosis (a build-up of lactic acid in the blood)

If you experience any of these side effects, contact your doctor immediately.


**How it Works:**

Entecavir works by interfering with the HBV virus's ability to replicate.  Specifically, it inhibits the action of reverse transcriptase, an enzyme crucial for the HBV virus to multiply. By blocking this enzyme, entecavir significantly reduces the viral load in the bloodstream, leading to improved liver function and a slower progression of the disease.

**Precautions:**

* **Pregnancy and breastfeeding:**  Entecavir should be used cautiously during pregnancy and breastfeeding.  The benefits must outweigh the potential risks to the mother and child.  Consult a doctor immediately if you are pregnant, planning to become pregnant, or breastfeeding.
* **Liver disease:**  Entecavir should be used with caution in patients with severe liver disease.
* **Kidney disease:**  Dosage adjustments may be necessary for individuals with kidney problems.
* **Drug interactions:**  Entecavir can interact with other medications.  Inform your doctor about all medications, supplements, and herbal remedies you are taking.
* **Alcohol consumption:**  Excessive alcohol consumption can worsen liver damage.  Limit or avoid alcohol while taking entecavir.

**FAQs:**

* **Q: Can I stop taking entecavir if I feel better?** A: No.  Stopping entecavir prematurely can lead to viral rebound and worsening of the disease.  Continue taking it as prescribed, even if you feel better, unless your doctor instructs otherwise.
* **Q: How should I store entecavir?** A: Store entecavir tablets in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight and moisture. Keep them out of reach of children.
* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it's almost time for your next dose.  Do not take a double dose to make up for a missed one.
* **Q: Is entecavir a cure for hepatitis B?** A: No, entecavir is not a cure for hepatitis B. It manages the infection and helps prevent complications.  Long-term treatment is usually necessary.
