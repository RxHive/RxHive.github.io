# Ethinyl Estradiol + Desogestrel

**Generic Name:** Ethinyl Estradiol + Desogestrel

**Usage:** Ethinyl estradiol and desogestrel is a combination hormonal contraceptive pill primarily used for **contraception**.  This means it prevents pregnancy.  It works by preventing ovulation (the release of an egg from the ovary), thickening the cervical mucus (making it harder for sperm to reach the egg), and altering the uterine lining (making it less suitable for implantation of a fertilized egg). While its primary use is contraception, it may also be prescribed off-label to help manage certain conditions associated with hormonal imbalances, though this is less common and should only be done under the guidance of a healthcare professional.  It's crucial to understand that this medication is not a treatment for these conditions, but may help manage some symptoms.

**Dosage:**  The dosage is typically one tablet taken orally once daily, at approximately the same time each day, for 21 days, followed by a 7-day break during which a withdrawal bleed usually occurs.  Then, a new pack is started.  There are variations in the formulation (e.g., 21-day packs, 28-day packs with placebo pills for the last 7 days).  The specific dose of ethinyl estradiol and desogestrel (e.g., 30 mcg/150 mcg, 20 mcg/150 mcg, etc.) will be prescribed by a doctor based on individual factors and needs. The medication comes in tablet form.  It's crucial to follow the prescribed dosage and schedule exactly as directed by your doctor or pharmacist.  This medication is not suitable for children.

**Side Effects:** Common side effects can include:

*   Breast tenderness
*   Headache
*   Nausea
*   Weight changes
*   Mood changes
*   Breakthrough bleeding (bleeding between periods)
*   Spotting
*   Acne

Less common but serious side effects can include:

*   Blood clots (deep vein thrombosis, pulmonary embolism)
*   High blood pressure
*   Liver problems
*   Gallbladder disease
*   Stroke
*   Heart attack

If you experience any serious side effects, seek immediate medical attention. Any unusual or concerning side effects should be reported to your doctor.


**How it Works:** This combined oral contraceptive pill contains two hormones: ethinyl estradiol (an estrogen) and desogestrel (a progestin).  Ethinyl estradiol suppresses the release of follicle-stimulating hormone (FSH) and luteinizing hormone (LH) from the pituitary gland. These hormones are essential for ovulation.  Desogestrel further thickens cervical mucus and alters the uterine lining, creating additional barriers to pregnancy.  The combined action of these hormones effectively prevents pregnancy.

**Precautions:**

*   **Contraindications:** This medication should not be used by individuals with certain conditions, including blood clots, stroke, heart attack, liver disease, breast cancer, or undiagnosed vaginal bleeding.  Women who smoke, especially those over 35, should discuss the risks with their doctor before using this medication.
*   **Drug Interactions:** Some medications can interact with ethinyl estradiol and desogestrel, potentially reducing the effectiveness of the pill or increasing the risk of side effects.  Always inform your doctor or pharmacist of all medications, supplements, and herbal remedies you are taking.
*   **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:** This medication is not recommended during pregnancy. It is generally not recommended during breastfeeding, as hormones can pass into breast milk, although your doctor may consider the benefits and risks in individual cases.
*   **Older Adults:** This medication is not typically used in older adults outside the context of managing menopausal symptoms (and even then would be a highly individual decision made with a doctor).


**FAQs:**

*   **Q: Can I miss a pill?** A:  Missing pills can reduce the effectiveness of the pill. Follow the instructions provided with your medication carefully. If you miss a pill, consult the package insert or your doctor.

*   **Q: What should I do if I experience side effects?** A:  Report any unusual or concerning side effects to your doctor immediately.

*   **Q: How should I store this medication?** A: Store at room temperature away from moisture and direct sunlight.

*   **Q: Is this medication effective against sexually transmitted infections (STIs)?** A: No.  This medication only prevents pregnancy.  It does not protect against STIs.  Use barrier methods (condoms) for STI protection.

*   **Q: How long does it take to become effective?** A:  Effectiveness begins once the medication is taken daily as prescribed, but it's typically advised to use a backup method of contraception for the first seven days of the first pack.
