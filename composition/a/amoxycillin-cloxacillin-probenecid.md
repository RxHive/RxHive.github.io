# Amoxycillin + Cloxacillin + Probenecid

**Generic Name: Amoxycillin + Cloxacillin + Probenecid**

**Usage:**

This combination medication is primarily used to treat severe bacterial infections, particularly those resistant to single antibiotic therapies.  Amoxycillin and cloxacillin are both antibiotics, but they target bacteria in slightly different ways. Amoxycillin is an aminopenicillin, effective against a broad range of bacteria, while cloxacillin is a penicillinase-resistant penicillin, meaning it's effective against bacteria that can produce enzymes to break down standard penicillins.  Probenecid is added to this combination to enhance the effects of amoxycillin and cloxacillin. It does this by blocking the kidneys' reabsorption of these antibiotics, leading to higher and longer-lasting blood levels. This makes the treatment more effective against serious infections.  The specific infections this combination might treat are usually determined by a doctor based on lab results identifying the bacteria and its susceptibility to the antibiotics.  It's crucial to understand that this combination is *not* a first-line treatment for most bacterial infections; its use is generally reserved for more serious, resistant strains.

**Dosage:**

Dosage is strictly determined by a healthcare professional and will vary significantly depending on the individual's age, weight, the severity of the infection, and the specific bacterial strain involved. There isn't a standardized dosage that can be safely provided here.  This medication is typically not available in syrup form for children due to the complexity of combining these drugs, and it is usually administered orally as tablets or capsules. Injection may be considered in severe cases under direct medical supervision.  It is crucial to follow the prescribed dosage and administration schedule precisely.

**Side Effects:**

* **Common Side Effects:** Nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, stomach cramps, indigestion, rash.
* **Less Common but Serious Side Effects:**  Allergic reactions (ranging from mild skin reactions to life-threatening anaphylaxis), jaundice (yellowing of the skin and eyes),  severe abdominal pain, changes in urine color, signs of infection not improving or worsening.
* **Note:** This list is not exhaustive. If any adverse effects occur, it is crucial to seek immediate medical attention.

**How it Works:**

Amoxycillin and cloxacillin both work by inhibiting the synthesis of bacterial cell walls. This weakens the bacteria's structure, leading to their death.  They work on different types of bacteria, providing broader coverage. Probenecid works by preventing the kidneys from reabsorbing amoxycillin and cloxacillin, thus increasing their concentration in the bloodstream and prolonging their action. This allows the antibiotics to better combat the infection.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** This medication is contraindicated in individuals with a known allergy to penicillin or any of its components.
* **Drug Interactions:**  This combination can interact with many other medications, including anticoagulants (blood thinners), methotrexate, and some oral contraceptives. It's essential to inform your doctor of all medications and supplements you are taking.
* **Specific Warnings:**  Pregnant or breastfeeding women should carefully discuss the risks and benefits with their doctor before using this medication.  Caution is also advised for individuals with kidney or liver impairment, and the elderly.

**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long should I take this medication?** A: The duration of treatment is determined by your doctor based on the severity of your infection and your response to the therapy. Never stop taking the medication prematurely, even if you feel better.
* **Q: Can I drink alcohol while taking this medication?** A:  It’s best to avoid alcohol while on this medication, as it can increase the risk of side effects, especially gastrointestinal upset.
* **Q: How should I store this medication?** A: Store the medication at room temperature, away from moisture and direct sunlight, as directed on the label.
* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A:  Contact your doctor or pharmacist for guidance.  Do not double the dose to make up for a missed one.
* **Q: Is this medication safe for children?** A: This combination's use in children is determined by a doctor, considering factors like age and infection severity. It isn't generally prescribed in children in a readily available, easily administered format.
