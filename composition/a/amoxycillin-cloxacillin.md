# Amoxycillin + Cloxacillin

**Generic Name:** Amoxycillin + Cloxacillin

**Usage:**

Amoxycillin and cloxacillin is a combination antibiotic used to treat a range of bacterial infections.  Its primary use is targeting infections caused by bacteria susceptible to both amoxicillin (a penicillin-type antibiotic) and cloxacillin (a penicillinase-resistant penicillin).  This makes it effective against a broader spectrum of bacteria than either drug alone.  Commonly treated infections include:

* **Skin and soft tissue infections:**  Boils, abscesses, cellulitis, and wound infections.  The combination's effectiveness against Staphylococcus aureus, a common cause of skin infections, is particularly noteworthy.
* **Respiratory tract infections:**  Some pneumonia types, and potentially severe bronchitis, though other antibiotics are often preferred for these conditions.  This combination's use in respiratory infections is less common than its use for skin infections.
* **Bone and joint infections (Osteomyelitis):** In some cases, particularly when a mixed infection is suspected.


While this combination can treat various infections, it’s crucial to remember it is not effective against all types of bacteria or viral infections.  A doctor’s diagnosis is essential to ensure appropriate treatment.  The specific bacteria causing the infection must be identified or strongly suspected to justify using this combination.


**Dosage:**

Dosage varies significantly depending on the patient's age, weight, the severity of the infection, and the specific formulation (tablets, capsules, or liquid suspension for children).  It's absolutely crucial to follow a doctor's precise instructions.  **Never adjust the dosage without consulting a healthcare professional.**  General guidelines are provided below but should not replace personalized medical advice.


* **Adults:**  Typical dosages may range from 500mg to 1000mg of amoxicillin and a similar or slightly lower dose of cloxacillin administered every 8 hours.  
* **Children:**  Dosage is determined by weight and age and is usually administered as a liquid suspension.  A doctor will calculate the appropriate dosage.


**Side Effects:**

Common side effects, often mild, can include:

* Nausea
* Vomiting
* Diarrhea
* Stomach upset
* Skin rash
* Headache
* Dizziness

Less common but more serious side effects that require immediate medical attention include:

* Allergic reactions (including severe anaphylaxis – difficulty breathing, swelling of the face, lips, tongue, or throat)
* Severe diarrhea (potentially indicating *Clostridium difficile* infection)
* Jaundice (yellowing of the skin or eyes)
* Seizures
* Liver damage

If you experience any concerning side effects, stop taking the medication and seek immediate medical advice.


**How it Works:**

Amoxicillin and cloxacillin are both beta-lactam antibiotics. They work by interfering with the synthesis of peptidoglycan, a crucial component of bacterial cell walls.  This disruption prevents the bacteria from building and maintaining their cell walls, leading to their death.  Cloxacillin, specifically, is resistant to the action of beta-lactamases – enzymes produced by some bacteria to break down penicillin-type antibiotics.  This resistance explains why the combination is effective against a wider range of bacteria compared to amoxicillin alone.


**Precautions:**

* **Allergies:**  This combination should be avoided if you have a known allergy to penicillin or cephalosporin antibiotics.
* **Liver or Kidney problems:**  Dosage adjustments may be necessary for individuals with impaired liver or kidney function.
* **Pregnancy and breastfeeding:**  Use during pregnancy and breastfeeding should only occur under strict medical supervision.
* **Interactions:**  This medication may interact with certain other drugs, including anticoagulants (blood thinners). Inform your doctor about all medications you are taking.
* **Inflammatory bowel disease:**  Use should be approached cautiously in patients with pre-existing conditions like colitis.
* **Children:**  Use in young children requires careful monitoring for side effects.


**FAQs:**


* **Q: Can I stop taking the medication early if I feel better?**  A: No.  Continue taking the medication as prescribed even if you feel better to ensure the infection is fully cleared.  Stopping early can lead to recurrence or antibiotic resistance.
* **Q: How should I store Amoxycillin + Cloxacillin?** A: Store it as directed on the label, usually at room temperature, away from moisture and heat.
* **Q: Is this medication safe for everyone?** A:  No.  It's not suitable for people allergic to penicillin or those with certain pre-existing conditions. Consult a doctor before using it.
* **Q: What if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it is almost time for the next dose. Do not double the dose.
* **Q: Can I drink alcohol while taking this medication?** A:  It's best to avoid alcohol while taking antibiotics.  Check with your doctor or pharmacist if you have specific concerns.
