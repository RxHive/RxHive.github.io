# Apremilast

**Generic Name: Apremilast**

**Usage:**

Apremilast is primarily used to treat adults with moderate to severe plaque psoriasis who haven't responded well enough to other treatments, like topical therapies or phototherapy.  It's also approved for treating active psoriatic arthritis in adults, again when other treatments haven't provided sufficient relief.  While its primary use focuses on these two conditions, research is ongoing to explore its potential in other inflammatory diseases.  It's important to understand that apremilast is not a cure for these conditions, but rather a treatment aimed at managing symptoms and improving quality of life.

**Dosage:**

Apremilast comes as a tablet taken orally.  Dosage is typically determined by a doctor based on individual needs and response to treatment.  

* **Adults (Psoriasis and Psoriatic Arthritis):**  A common starting dose is 30mg twice daily.  This dose may be adjusted by the physician based on individual response and tolerability.  There's no specific dosage adjustment recommended based on weight in adults.

* **Children:** Apremilast is not currently approved for use in children.


**Important Administration Guidelines:**  Apremilast tablets should be swallowed whole with water.  They can be taken with or without food.  Consistency in taking the medication as prescribed is crucial for effectiveness.  Never alter the dosage without consulting a doctor.

**Side Effects:**

Common side effects can include:

* Nausea
* Diarrhea
* Headache
* Upper respiratory tract infection
* Weight loss

Less common but serious side effects may include:

* Depression
* Anxiety
* Increased risk of infections
* Allergic reactions (rash, itching, swelling)


**If you experience any adverse effects, particularly those listed as serious, consult your healthcare provider immediately.**

**How it Works:**

Apremilast works by inhibiting phosphodiesterase 4 (PDE4).  PDE4 is an enzyme that plays a role in inflammation. By inhibiting PDE4, apremilast helps to reduce the body's inflammatory response, lessening the symptoms of psoriasis and psoriatic arthritis.  Essentially, it helps to "turn down" the inflammatory processes contributing to these conditions.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** Apremilast should not be used in individuals with known hypersensitivity to the drug.

* **Drug Interactions:**  Apremilast may interact with other medications.  It's crucial to inform your doctor of all medications, supplements, and herbal remedies you are taking before starting apremilast.

* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  The use of apremilast during pregnancy and breastfeeding should be carefully considered and discussed with a healthcare professional due to potential risks.

* **Older Adults and Individuals with Liver or Kidney Impairment:**  Dosage adjustments may be necessary for these populations.  Close monitoring is essential.

* **Suicide Risk:**  Reports of suicidal thoughts and behavior have been associated with apremilast use.  Close monitoring for changes in mood and behavior is necessary, particularly during initial treatment.  Seek immediate help if you experience such symptoms.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: Can I stop taking apremilast suddenly?** A: No, abruptly stopping apremilast can lead to symptom flare-ups.  Always follow your doctor's instructions for tapering off the medication.

* **Q: How should I store apremilast?** A: Store apremilast tablets at room temperature, away from moisture and direct sunlight.

* **Q: Will apremilast cure my psoriasis/psoriatic arthritis?** A: No, apremilast manages symptoms and improves quality of life but doesn't cure these conditions.

* **Q: What if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it's almost time for the next dose.  Do not double the dose.
