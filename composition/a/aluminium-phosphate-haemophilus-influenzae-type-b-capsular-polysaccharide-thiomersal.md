# Aluminium Phosphate + Haemophilus Influenzae Type B Capsular Polysaccharide + Thiomersal

**Generic Name:** Aluminium Phosphate + *Haemophilus influenzae* type b (Hib) Capsular Polysaccharide + Thiomersal

**Usage:** This combination is primarily used as a vaccine to prevent infection by *Haemophilus influenzae* type b (Hib) bacteria.  Hib bacteria can cause serious illnesses, particularly in young children, such as bacterial meningitis (infection of the membranes surrounding the brain and spinal cord), epiglottitis (a life-threatening infection of the airway), pneumonia (lung infection), and sepsis (a life-threatening bloodstream infection).  This vaccine is highly effective in preventing these severe infections.  There are no notable secondary uses for this specific combination.  It's crucial to understand that this vaccine does not treat existing Hib infections; it prevents them.

**Dosage:** This vaccine is administered as an injection. The specific dosage and schedule vary depending on the manufacturer's formulation and the age of the recipient.  Generally, infants and young children receive a series of injections according to a schedule recommended by their healthcare provider.  Precise dosing information should always be obtained from the product packaging and a healthcare professional.  There are no oral or tablet forms of this vaccine.


**Side Effects:**

* **Common:** Pain, redness, or swelling at the injection site.  Low-grade fever.  Fussiness or irritability.
* **Less Common but Serious:** Allergic reactions (ranging from mild rash to severe anaphylaxis requiring immediate medical attention).  High fever.  Seizures (rare).

**Note:** If any of these side effects occur, or if you have any concerns, contact a healthcare provider immediately.


**How it Works:**  The Hib capsular polysaccharide is a component of the Hib bacteria's outer surface.  When injected, it stimulates the body's immune system to produce antibodies against this polysaccharide.  These antibodies then provide protection against future Hib infections. Aluminium phosphate acts as an adjuvant, meaning it enhances the immune response to the polysaccharide, making the vaccine more effective. Thiomersal, a mercury-based preservative, was historically included in some formulations to prevent bacterial contamination. However, many modern formulations no longer contain thiomersal due to concerns about mercury exposure.  Always check the specific product labeling to confirm the presence or absence of thiomersal.


**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:**  This vaccine should not be given to individuals with a known severe allergic reaction to any component of the vaccine (including aluminium phosphate, Hib polysaccharide, or thiomersal, if present).
* **Interactions:**  While interactions with other medications are generally rare, it's important to inform your healthcare provider of all medications, including over-the-counter drugs, supplements, and herbal remedies, that you are taking.
* **Pregnant and Breastfeeding Women:**  The use of this vaccine during pregnancy or breastfeeding should be discussed with a healthcare provider to weigh the benefits against potential risks.
* **Children and Older Adults:**  The dosage and scheduling recommendations for children and older adults differ, and healthcare providers will determine the appropriate approach.
* **Safety Measures:**  The vaccine should be administered by a healthcare professional in a setting where emergency care is readily available in case of an allergic reaction.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: Is this vaccine safe?** A:  This vaccine has a strong safety profile and is generally well-tolerated. However, as with any vaccine, there is a small risk of side effects.
* **Q: How is the vaccine stored?** A:  The vaccine must be stored according to the manufacturer's instructions, usually under refrigeration.
* **Q: How long does the protection last?** A:  The duration of protection varies depending on the age of the recipient and the specific vaccine formulation.  A healthcare provider can advise on booster shots if needed.
* **Q:  Does this vaccine contain thiomersal?** A:  Many modern formulations of this vaccine do not contain thiomersal. Check the product packaging for specific information.
* **Q:  Can I get this vaccine if I have a mild cold?** A:  A mild illness is usually not a reason to postpone the vaccine. However, consult with your healthcare provider to ensure it's the right time for you.
