# Astemizole

**Generic Name: Astemizole**

**Usage:**

Astemizole is an antihistamine, primarily used to relieve symptoms of allergies.  Its main application is in treating allergic rhinitis (hay fever), which manifests as sneezing, runny nose, itchy eyes, and nasal congestion.  While once used more broadly, its application has significantly decreased due to serious cardiac side effects (discussed below).  It's important to note that astemizole is no longer widely available in many countries, including the United States, due to these safety concerns. Its use is largely restricted to specific situations where other, safer antihistamines are ineffective or unsuitable.  A healthcare professional should always be consulted before using astemizole.

**Side Effects:**

* **Common:** Drowsiness, dry mouth, constipation, blurred vision, dizziness.
* **Less Common but Serious:**  Prolonged QT interval (a serious heart rhythm abnormality that can lead to potentially fatal heart problems), torsades de pointes (a type of irregular heartbeat),  arrhythmias.

*It's vital to report any unusual symptoms, especially those affecting the heart, to a doctor immediately.*  Even seemingly minor side effects could indicate a serious problem.

**How it Works:**

Astemizole works by blocking histamine receptors in the body. Histamine is a chemical released by the body during an allergic reaction, causing the symptoms of allergy.  By blocking these receptors, astemizole prevents histamine from binding and triggering these symptoms, providing relief from allergy symptoms.  However, this action also affects other histamine receptors in the body, which can contribute to some of the side effects.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** Astemizole should not be used by individuals with known heart rhythm problems, a history of prolonged QT interval, or those taking certain medications that can also prolong the QT interval (see drug interactions).
* **Drug Interactions:** Astemizole interacts with many medications, including some antifungal agents, macrolide antibiotics, and antidepressants.  Taking astemizole alongside these medications significantly increases the risk of dangerous heart rhythm disturbances.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:** Astemizole should generally be avoided during pregnancy and breastfeeding due to the potential risk to the fetus or infant.
* **Children and Older Adults:** The use of astemizole in children and older adults requires extra caution due to potential increased sensitivity to its side effects, particularly cardiac side effects.
* **Liver and Kidney Disease:**  Patients with liver or kidney impairment may require a reduced dose or may not be suitable candidates for astemizole.
