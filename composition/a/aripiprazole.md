# Aripiprazole

**Generic Name:** Aripiprazole

**Usage:** Aripiprazole is primarily used to treat schizophrenia and bipolar disorder.  In schizophrenia, it helps manage positive symptoms like hallucinations and delusions, as well as negative symptoms like social withdrawal and lack of motivation.  For bipolar disorder, it's used to treat both manic and depressive episodes, helping stabilize mood swings and prevent future episodes.  It's also sometimes prescribed for major depressive disorder, particularly in cases that haven't responded well to other treatments, and for irritability associated with autistic spectrum disorder in children and adolescents.  It's important to remember that aripiprazole is a prescription medication and should only be used under the guidance of a healthcare professional.


**Dosage:**  Dosage varies significantly depending on the individual, their condition, age, and response to treatment. A doctor will determine the appropriate dosage.  For adults with schizophrenia or bipolar disorder, it typically starts low and gradually increases.  It's available in various forms including tablets, oral solution (liquid), and injectable formulations (for both short-term and long-acting administration).  Children's dosages are carefully calculated based on weight and age, and a physician will determine the suitable form and dosage.  Never alter your prescribed dosage without consulting your doctor.


**Side Effects:**  Like all medications, aripiprazole can cause side effects.  Common side effects include:

*   Weight gain
*   Sleepiness or insomnia
*   Restlessness or anxiety
*   Dizziness
*   Nausea
*   Constipation
*   Dry mouth
*   Tremors
*   Increased prolactin levels (can lead to menstrual irregularities in women and breast enlargement in men)

Less common but serious side effects include:

*   Neuroleptic malignant syndrome (NMS) – a rare but potentially life-threatening condition characterized by high fever, muscle rigidity, altered mental state, and unstable blood pressure.
*   Tardive dyskinesia (TD) – a movement disorder that can be irreversible.
*   Suicidal thoughts or actions (particularly in younger adults).

If you experience any concerning side effects, contact your healthcare provider immediately.


**How it Works:** Aripiprazole is classified as an atypical antipsychotic.  It works primarily by affecting the balance of dopamine and serotonin in the brain. These are neurotransmitters, chemicals that transmit signals between nerve cells.  Aripiprazole acts as a partial agonist at dopamine D2 and serotonin 5-HT1A receptors. This means it partially stimulates these receptors, helping to regulate their activity.  This complex interaction helps to stabilize neurotransmission, reducing the symptoms of schizophrenia and bipolar disorder.

**Precautions:**

*   **Contraindications:** Aripiprazole should not be used in individuals with known hypersensitivity to the medication.
*   **Drug Interactions:**  Aripiprazole can interact with other medications, so it’s crucial to inform your doctor about all medications, supplements, and herbal remedies you are taking.
*   **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  The use of aripiprazole during pregnancy or breastfeeding requires careful consideration by a healthcare professional, weighing the benefits against potential risks.
*   **Elderly Patients:**  Elderly patients may be more sensitive to the side effects, especially those related to movement disorders.
*   **Children:**  Aripiprazole is used in children and adolescents, but only under strict medical supervision due to potential side effects and developmental considerations.


**FAQs:**

*   **Q: Can I stop taking aripiprazole suddenly?**  A: No.  Stopping aripiprazole abruptly can lead to withdrawal symptoms.  Always follow your doctor's instructions for tapering off the medication.
*   **Q: How should I store aripiprazole?** A: Store aripiprazole as directed on the label, usually at room temperature away from moisture and light.
*   **Q: Can I drink alcohol while taking aripiprazole?** A:  Alcohol can increase the risk of drowsiness and other side effects. Consult your doctor about alcohol consumption while on this medication.
*   **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it's almost time for your next dose. Don't double the dose.
*   **Q:  Will aripiprazole cause addiction?** A: Aripiprazole is not considered an addictive substance, but it should only be used as prescribed.
