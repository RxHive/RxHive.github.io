# Amoxycillin + Bromhexine

**Generic Name: Amoxycillin + Bromhexine**

**Usage:**

Amoxycillin + Bromhexine is a combination medication used primarily to treat respiratory tract infections that involve both bacterial and mucus-related symptoms.  The amoxycillin component is a penicillin-based antibiotic that fights bacterial infections, while bromhexine is an expectorant that helps thin and loosen mucus, making it easier to cough up. This combination is frequently prescribed for conditions like:

* **Acute bronchitis:**  An inflammation of the bronchial tubes usually caused by a viral or bacterial infection.  The combination tackles both the infection and the accompanying mucus buildup.
* **Pneumonia (certain types):**  While pneumonia often requires broader antibiotic treatment, in cases where a bacterial infection is confirmed and excessive mucus is present, this combination may be part of a treatment plan.
* **Sinusitis (bacterial):**  This combination might be used for bacterial sinusitis where mucus buildup is a significant symptom.  However, it's crucial to note that the underlying cause needs proper diagnosis.
* **Other respiratory infections:**  The combination may be considered for other similar infections, always under the guidance of a physician.


It's important to understand that this medication is not suitable for all respiratory infections, especially those caused solely by viruses.  A healthcare professional must diagnose the condition and determine the appropriateness of this treatment.


**Dosage:**

Dosage varies considerably depending on the patient's age, weight, and the severity of the infection.  A doctor will prescribe the appropriate dose.  The medication is typically available in tablet and syrup forms.

* **Adults:** Dosage varies widely; typically, a doctor might prescribe one or two tablets or a specific amount of syrup several times daily.
* **Children:**  Dosage is strictly determined by weight and age and is always prescribed by a pediatrician. Syrup formulations are common for children.

It's crucial to follow the prescribed dosage and duration of treatment precisely as directed by the healthcare provider.  Never adjust the dosage or discontinue the medication prematurely without consulting a doctor.


**Side Effects:**

* **Common:** Nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, stomach upset, headache, dizziness.
* **Less Common but Serious:** Allergic reactions (rash, itching, swelling, difficulty breathing), changes in liver function,  blood disorders.

If you experience any of these side effects, particularly serious ones, seek immediate medical attention.


**How it Works:**

Amoxycillin works by interfering with the synthesis of bacterial cell walls, leading to bacterial death and thus treating the infection.  Bromhexine acts as an expectorant by reducing the viscosity (thickness) of mucus in the respiratory tract. This allows for easier removal of mucus through coughing, thus relieving congestion and improving breathing.


**Precautions:**

* **Allergic Reactions:** This medication should not be used by individuals with known allergies to penicillin or bromhexine.  Severe allergic reactions can be life-threatening.
* **Liver Issues:** Individuals with pre-existing liver problems should exercise caution and inform their doctor before taking this medication.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  The use of this medication during pregnancy or breastfeeding requires careful consideration and consultation with a healthcare provider.
* **Interactions with Other Drugs:**  Inform your doctor about all other medications, including over-the-counter drugs and herbal remedies, you are taking to avoid potential interactions.
* **Elderly and Children:** Dosage adjustment is necessary for elderly patients and children, as determined by a physician.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: Can I take this medication with alcohol?** A:  It's generally recommended to avoid alcohol while taking this medication, as it can increase the risk of side effects.
* **Q: How should I store this medication?** A: Store it as directed on the label, usually at room temperature and away from moisture and direct sunlight.
* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it's nearly time for the next dose.  Do not double the dose.
* **Q:  Is this medication suitable for children?** A:  It may be suitable for children under a doctor’s strict supervision, with the dosage adjusted based on the child's weight and age.  A pediatrician should determine appropriateness and dosage.
* **Q: Can I stop taking the medication when I feel better?** A:  No, complete the full course of treatment as prescribed by your doctor, even if you feel better before finishing the medication.  Stopping early can lead to treatment failure and the development of antibiotic resistance.
