# Amoxycillin + Probenecid

**Generic Name: Amoxycillin + Probenecid**

**Usage:**

Amoxycillin and probenecid are combined to enhance the effectiveness of amoxycillin, an antibiotic.  This combination is primarily used to treat infections caused by bacteria susceptible to amoxycillin, particularly those that are difficult to eradicate due to their resistance to excretion from the body.  The primary use is in treating gonococcal infections (a sexually transmitted infection caused by *Neisseria gonorrhoeae*).  While amoxycillin alone can treat some forms of gonorrhea, adding probenecid helps maintain higher levels of amoxycillin in the blood for a longer duration, improving the chance of successful treatment.  In some cases, this combination may be considered for other bacterial infections where maximizing amoxycillin's concentration is beneficial, but this is less common and should only be done under the strict guidance of a doctor.

**Dosage:**

Dosage of amoxycillin and probenecid varies depending on the specific infection being treated, the patient's age, weight, and overall health.  A doctor will determine the appropriate dose.  There is no standard pediatric dosage for this combination as it's primarily used for adult gonorrhea treatment.  Typically, the combination involves a single dose of amoxycillin (e.g., 3.5g-5g) administered with 1g of probenecid.  This is usually given orally as tablets. It's crucial to follow your doctor's instructions precisely regarding the dosage and administration method.  Injectable forms of amoxycillin may be used in certain situations, but not always in conjunction with probenecid in this form.  **It's vital to never adjust dosage on your own; always consult a healthcare professional.**


**Side Effects:**

Common side effects of Amoxycillin + Probenecid can include:

* Nausea
* Vomiting
* Diarrhea
* Stomach upset
* Rash
* Headache
* Dizziness

Less common but more serious side effects may include:

* Allergic reactions (anaphylaxis, a severe, life-threatening allergic reaction)
* Liver inflammation (hepatitis)
* Kidney problems
* Seizures (particularly in individuals with pre-existing conditions)


If you experience any side effects, especially serious ones, contact your doctor immediately.


**How it Works:**

Amoxycillin is a beta-lactam antibiotic that works by interfering with the synthesis of bacterial cell walls, ultimately leading to bacterial cell death.  Probenecid inhibits the reabsorption of amoxycillin in the kidneys. This means more amoxycillin stays in the bloodstream for a longer time, allowing it to continue fighting the infection more effectively.  Essentially, probenecid helps to boost the antibiotic's effectiveness and duration of action.


**Precautions:**

* **Allergies:**  Individuals with known allergies to penicillin or cephalosporin antibiotics should not take amoxycillin.  Allergic reactions can range from mild rashes to severe life-threatening anaphylaxis.
* **Kidney Problems:** Caution is advised in patients with kidney disease as probenecid can affect kidney function.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  The use of amoxycillin + probenecid during pregnancy and breastfeeding should only be considered under strict medical supervision.  The potential benefits must outweigh the potential risks to the mother and child.
* **Drug Interactions:** Amoxycillin + probenecid can interact with other medications.  Inform your doctor about all medications, supplements, and herbal remedies you are taking.  Specific examples include methotrexate, anticoagulants (blood thinners), and other nephrotoxic drugs (drugs harmful to the kidneys).
* **Alcohol:** Excessive alcohol consumption should be avoided while taking this medication.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: Can I take this medication with food?** A: It's best to take amoxycillin with food to minimize stomach upset.  Your doctor will advise on the best time to take the medication.
* **Q: How should I store this medication?** A: Store the medication in a cool, dry place, away from direct sunlight and moisture, as directed on the label.
* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Contact your doctor immediately for guidance.  Do not double the dose to make up for a missed dose.
* **Q: Is this medication safe for children?** A: The combination is not typically used in children.  The specific treatment for bacterial infection in children will be determined by a pediatrician.
* **Q: How long will I need to take this medication?** A: The duration of treatment depends on the severity of the infection and your response to therapy.  Your doctor will determine the appropriate length of treatment.  It's crucial to complete the entire course of antibiotics even if symptoms improve.
