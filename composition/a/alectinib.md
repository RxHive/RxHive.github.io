# Alectinib

**Generic Name: Alectinib**

**Usage:**

Alectinib is a targeted cancer therapy primarily used to treat a specific type of non-small cell lung cancer (NSCLC).  This is NSCLC that has a particular genetic mutation called an *ALK* (anaplastic lymphoma kinase) gene rearrangement.  These rearrangements lead to uncontrolled cell growth. Alectinib works by specifically targeting and blocking the activity of the abnormal ALK protein, thereby inhibiting tumor growth.  While its primary use is in ALK-positive NSCLC, it may also be used in some cases of other cancers with ALK mutations, but this is less common and typically determined on a case-by-case basis by an oncologist.  The specific application of alectinib is always determined in consultation with an oncologist who can assess individual patient needs and tumor characteristics.


**Dosage:**

The dosage of alectinib is determined by a physician based on individual factors. It’s typically administered orally as capsules.  The standard adult starting dose is usually 600 mg twice daily (1200mg total daily dose).  However, this can be adjusted based on factors like the patient's response to treatment, potential side effects, and overall health.  There is no established pediatric dosage for alectinib as its use is primarily in adult populations with advanced cancers.  The medication should always be taken exactly as prescribed and never adjusted independently.


**Side Effects:**

Common side effects of alectinib can include:

*   Fatigue
*   Nausea
*   Constipation
*   Diarrhea
*   Increased Liver Enzymes (indicated by blood tests)
*   Cough
*   Headache
*   Vision changes (blurred vision)
*   Increased weight


Less common but serious side effects can include:

*   Pneumonitis (inflammation of the lungs) – requires immediate medical attention.
*   Severe liver problems.
*   Serious allergic reactions.


It is crucial to report any new or worsening symptoms to your doctor immediately.


**How it Works:**

Alectinib is a type of tyrosine kinase inhibitor (TKI).  ALK is a protein kinase that plays a role in cell growth and division.  In ALK-positive NSCLC, a genetic alteration leads to excessive activity of this protein, driving uncontrolled cancer cell growth. Alectinib binds to and inhibits the activity of the abnormal ALK protein, effectively slowing or stopping the growth of cancer cells. This targeted approach minimizes damage to healthy cells compared to traditional chemotherapy, which often affects healthy cells as well.


**Precautions:**

*   **Contraindications:** Alectinib should not be taken by individuals with known hypersensitivity to alectinib or any of its components.
*   **Drug Interactions:**  Alectinib can interact with other medications.  It's vital to inform your doctor of all medications, supplements, and herbal remedies you are taking before starting alectinib. Certain medications may need to be adjusted or avoided.
*   **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:** Alectinib should not be used during pregnancy unless the potential benefits clearly outweigh the risks.  Women should use effective contraception while taking alectinib and for a period after treatment cessation.  Breastfeeding should be avoided while taking this medication.
*   **Older Adults:** Older adults may be more susceptible to certain side effects.  Careful monitoring is usually recommended.
*   **Liver and Kidney Function:**  Patients with impaired liver or kidney function may require dosage adjustments.



**FAQs:**

*   **Q: How long do I need to take alectinib?** A: The duration of treatment is determined by your oncologist based on your individual response and the progression of your cancer.
*   **Q: Can I stop taking alectinib if I feel better?** A: No, do not stop taking alectinib without consulting your doctor.  Stopping prematurely could allow the cancer to return or progress.
*   **Q: How should I store alectinib?** A: Store alectinib capsules at room temperature, away from moisture and direct sunlight.
*   **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it is almost time for your next dose.  Never double up on doses.
*   **Q:  Are there any long-term effects of alectinib?** A:  Long-term effects are possible, and ongoing monitoring by your physician is essential to detect and manage any potential issues.  The specific long-term effects will vary from patient to patient.
