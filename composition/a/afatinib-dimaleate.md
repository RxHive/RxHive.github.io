# Afatinib dimaleate

**Generic Name:** Afatinib dimaleate

**Usage:**

Afatinib dimaleate is a targeted therapy primarily used in the treatment of certain types of non-small cell lung cancer (NSCLC).  Specifically, it's effective against NSCLC tumors that have a specific genetic mutation: the epidermal growth factor receptor (EGFR) mutation.  These mutations drive the growth and spread of cancer cells. Afatinib dimaleate works by blocking the EGFR, effectively slowing or stopping the cancer's progression.  While its primary use is in NSCLC, research is ongoing to explore its potential in other cancer types with EGFR mutations.  It's crucial to remember that afatinib dimaleate is a prescription medication and should only be used under the guidance of an oncologist or qualified healthcare professional. The specific use and suitability will depend on individual patient factors and tumor characteristics.

**Dosage:**

Dosage of afatinib dimaleate is determined by a healthcare professional and varies depending on the individual's condition and response to treatment. It's typically administered orally as a tablet.  There is no standard pediatric dosage established, as it is primarily used in adults with NSCLC.  A common starting dose for adults is 40mg once daily.  This may be adjusted based on factors like side effects and the patient's overall health.  Always follow the prescribed dosage and instructions provided by your doctor.  Never alter the dose without consulting your physician.

**Side Effects:**

Afatinib dimaleate, like many cancer therapies, can cause side effects.  Common side effects include:

* Diarrhea
* Rash
* Dry skin
* Nausea
* Vomiting
* Fatigue
* Loss of appetite
* Mouth sores (stomatitis)
* Nail changes (paronychia)

Less common but serious side effects can include:

* Severe diarrhea
* Severe skin reactions (including Stevens-Johnson syndrome)
* Interstitial lung disease (inflammation of the lungs)
* Liver problems
* Heart problems


If you experience any significant or concerning side effects, immediately contact your doctor or healthcare provider.  Early intervention can often mitigate the severity of adverse effects.

**How it Works:**

Afatinib dimaleate is a tyrosine kinase inhibitor (TKI).  Tyrosine kinases are enzymes that play a crucial role in cell growth and division.  In EGFR-mutated NSCLC, these enzymes are overactive, fueling cancer growth. Afatinib dimaleate works by binding to and blocking the EGFR tyrosine kinase, preventing the signaling pathways that promote cancer cell proliferation and survival.  By inhibiting this crucial process, the drug helps slow or stop the growth of cancer cells.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** Afatinib dimaleate should not be used in patients with severe liver problems or hypersensitivity to the drug.
* **Drug Interactions:**  Afatinib dimaleate can interact with other medications.  Always inform your doctor about all medications, supplements, and herbal remedies you are taking.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:** Afatinib dimaleate should not be used during pregnancy due to potential harm to the fetus.  Women of childbearing age should use effective contraception during treatment.  It's also advised to avoid breastfeeding while taking this medication.
* **Older Adults:** Older adults may be more susceptible to side effects, and dosage adjustments may be necessary.
* **Liver and Kidney Function:**  Patients with impaired liver or kidney function may require dosage adjustments or close monitoring.

**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long will I need to take afatinib dimaleate?** A: The duration of treatment depends on your individual response to the medication and the progression of your cancer. This will be determined by your oncologist.
* **Q: How should I store afatinib dimaleate?** A: Store the medication at room temperature, away from moisture and direct sunlight. Keep it out of reach of children.
* **Q: Can I stop taking afatinib dimaleate suddenly?** A: No.  Always follow your doctor's instructions regarding discontinuation of the medication. Sudden stopping can potentially lead to adverse effects.
* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it is almost time for your next dose. Do not double the dose.
* **Q: Are there any long-term effects of afatinib dimaleate?** A: Long-term effects can vary, and regular monitoring by your healthcare provider is essential to detect and manage any potential complications.
