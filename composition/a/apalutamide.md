# Apalutamide

**Generic Name: Apalutamide**

**Usage:**

Apalutamide is a type of medication called a nonsteroidal antiandrogen.  Its primary use is in the treatment of non-metastatic castration-resistant prostate cancer (nmCRPC).  This means it's used for prostate cancer that hasn't spread to other parts of the body but is still growing even after hormone therapy (castration) has been implemented.  It works by blocking the effects of androgens, hormones that fuel the growth of some prostate cancer cells.  Currently, there are no approved secondary uses for apalutamide beyond nmCRPC.


**Dosage:**

Apalutamide is typically administered orally as a tablet.  The standard dosage for adults with nmCRPC is 240 mg once daily.  There are no established dosages for children, as apalutamide is not indicated for use in pediatric populations.  Dosage adjustments based on age, weight, or severity of the condition are not typically made unless specifically directed by a physician, taking into account individual patient factors and response to treatment.  The medication should be taken with food to minimize potential gastrointestinal upset.


**Side Effects:**

* **Common:** Fatigue, diarrhea, nausea, rash, hot flashes, decreased appetite, constipation, and increased liver enzyme levels.
* **Less Common but Serious:**  Serious allergic reactions (anaphylaxis),  liver injury (hepatotoxicity), seizures, and  cardiovascular events.

**It is crucial to contact a healthcare professional immediately if any concerning side effects occur.**


**How it Works:**

Apalutamide works by blocking the action of androgens, specifically testosterone and dihydrotestosterone (DHT), in prostate cancer cells.  These hormones bind to androgen receptors within the cancer cells, stimulating their growth. Apalutamide competitively inhibits this binding, effectively preventing the androgen receptors from receiving signals that promote cancer cell proliferation. This slows or stops the growth of the cancer.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** Apalutamide should not be used in patients with known hypersensitivity to the drug.
* **Drug Interactions:** Apalutamide can interact with other medications, particularly those metabolized by the same liver enzymes. This necessitates careful monitoring and potential dosage adjustments under a physician's guidance.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:** Apalutamide is contraindicated in women who are pregnant or breastfeeding.  Women of childbearing age should use effective contraception during treatment.
* **Elderly Patients:**  Elderly patients may be more susceptible to some side effects, requiring close monitoring.
* **Liver Function:** Apalutamide is primarily metabolized by the liver. Patients with impaired liver function may require dosage adjustments or careful monitoring of liver enzymes.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long will I need to take Apalutamide?** A: The duration of treatment is determined by your doctor based on your individual response to therapy and disease progression.
* **Q: Can I drink alcohol while taking Apalutamide?** A: Moderate alcohol consumption is generally permissible, but excessive alcohol intake should be avoided as it may exacerbate some side effects.  Always consult your doctor for guidance.
* **Q: How should I store Apalutamide?** A: Store the tablets at room temperature, away from moisture and direct sunlight.
* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it's almost time for your next dose. Do not double the dose.
* **Q: Can I stop taking Apalutamide suddenly?** A: No, abruptly stopping treatment should be avoided.  Always consult your doctor before making any changes to your medication regimen.
