# Amlodipine + Benazepril

**Generic Name: Amlodipine + Benazepril**

**Usage:**

Amlodipine + Benazepril is a combination medication used primarily to treat high blood pressure (hypertension).  It's particularly helpful for people whose blood pressure isn't adequately controlled with a single medication.  This combination therapy leverages the synergistic effects of two active ingredients: amlodipine, a calcium channel blocker, and benazepril, an ACE inhibitor.  By working together, they effectively lower blood pressure, reducing the risk of heart attack, stroke, and kidney disease.  While primarily used for hypertension, it might also be considered in certain cases of heart failure, although this is usually a secondary use and determined by a physician based on individual patient factors.  It is crucial to understand that this combination medication is prescribed only by a doctor and should not be self-administered.


**Dosage:**

Dosage is always determined and adjusted by a healthcare professional based on individual needs and response to treatment. There is no universally applicable dosage for children, and this combination drug is generally not prescribed for children.  Typical adult starting dosages for Amlodipine + Benazepril tablets range from 5mg/10mg to 10mg/20mg once daily.  The dosage might be increased gradually, again under the close supervision of a doctor, if blood pressure isn't controlled effectively.  The medication is usually taken orally with or without food.  This combination is available as tablets only; there are no syrups or injectables.  It is crucial to follow your doctor's instructions precisely regarding dosage and administration.  Never alter the dosage without consulting your doctor.


**Side Effects:**

Common side effects can include:

* Dizziness
* Headache
* Fatigue
* Swelling in ankles, feet, or legs
* Dry cough
* Nausea
* Changes in bowel habits (constipation or diarrhea)

Less common, but more serious side effects require immediate medical attention and include:

* Swelling of the face, lips, tongue, or throat (angioedema – a serious allergic reaction)
* Irregular heartbeat
* Chest pain
* Severe dizziness or fainting
* Yellowing of the skin or eyes (jaundice)
* Significant changes in kidney function


Always consult your healthcare provider if you experience any concerning side effects.


**How it Works:**

Amlodipine relaxes blood vessels, making it easier for blood to flow. This lowers blood pressure. Benazepril works by blocking the production of a hormone called angiotensin II, which narrows blood vessels. By reducing angiotensin II, benazepril allows blood vessels to relax and widen, further reducing blood pressure.  The combined effect of these two mechanisms results in a more significant and sustained reduction in blood pressure than either drug alone.


**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:**  Amlodipine + Benazepril should not be taken by individuals with a history of angioedema (swelling of the face, lips, tongue, or throat) related to ACE inhibitors, or those with severe liver disease.
* **Drug Interactions:**  This medication can interact with several other drugs, including potassium supplements, diuretics, and some nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drugs (NSAIDs).  Always inform your doctor of all medications, supplements, and herbal remedies you are taking.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:** This medication is generally not recommended during pregnancy, especially during the second and third trimesters.  Discuss risks and benefits with your doctor if you are pregnant or planning a pregnancy.  The use during breastfeeding also requires careful consideration and discussion with a healthcare professional.
* **Older Adults:** Older adults may be more sensitive to the side effects of this medication.  Dosage adjustments might be necessary.
* **Kidney and Liver Disease:** Individuals with kidney or liver problems require careful monitoring while taking this medication, as dosage adjustments might be needed to prevent adverse events.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: Can I stop taking this medication suddenly?** A: No, stopping abruptly can be dangerous.  Always follow your doctor's instructions for tapering off the medication.
* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Take it as soon as you remember, unless it's almost time for the next dose.  Do not double the dose.
* **Q: How should I store this medication?** A: Store it at room temperature, away from moisture and direct sunlight.
* **Q: Can I drink alcohol while taking this medication?** A: Excessive alcohol consumption can interact with this medication and should be avoided or minimized.  Discuss alcohol consumption with your healthcare provider.
* **Q:  What if I experience dizziness?** A: Dizziness is a common side effect.  Avoid driving or operating machinery if you feel dizzy.  This should improve over time but if it persists or worsens, speak to your doctor immediately.
