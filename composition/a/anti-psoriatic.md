# ANTI-PSORIATIC

**Generic Name:** Anti-psoriatic (Note:  "Anti-psoriatic" is a class of drugs, not a single medication.  This information applies generally to this drug class, but specific details will vary greatly depending on the individual drug within the class. Always consult a doctor or pharmacist for information specific to your prescription.)

**Usage:**

Anti-psoriatic medications are used to treat psoriasis, a chronic autoimmune disease causing skin inflammation, redness, scaling, and itching.  The primary use is to manage the symptoms of plaque psoriasis, the most common type.  Some anti-psoriatic drugs may also be used to treat psoriatic arthritis, a form of arthritis that often co-occurs with psoriasis, and may help manage related conditions like nail psoriasis and psoriatic spondylitis.  The specific condition treated will depend on the individual medication prescribed.  Treatment goals vary from managing mild symptoms to suppressing severe disease activity.


**Dosage:**

Dosage varies dramatically depending on the specific anti-psoriatic medication (e.g., methotrexate, biologics like adalimumab, topical corticosteroids, etc.), the patient's age, weight, overall health, and the severity of the psoriasis.  There is no single "standard" dosage.  Dosage is always determined and adjusted by a doctor based on individual needs and response to treatment.  Medications come in various forms including tablets, capsules, injections, creams, ointments, and solutions.  Administration methods will differ significantly depending on the drug formulation.


**Side Effects:**

Common side effects vary greatly depending on the specific medication but may include:

* Nausea
* Vomiting
* Diarrhea
* Headache
* Fatigue
* Skin rash
* Injection site reactions (for injectable medications)
* Increased risk of infection


Less common but serious side effects may include:

* Liver damage
* Kidney damage
* Bone marrow suppression
* Increased risk of certain cancers
* Allergic reactions
* Cardiovascular problems (in some cases)


If you experience any side effects, particularly those that are severe or persistent, contact your healthcare provider immediately.


**How it Works:**

Anti-psoriatic medications work through different mechanisms, depending on the specific drug.  Some, such as topical corticosteroids, reduce inflammation directly at the site of the psoriasis. Others, like biologics, target specific parts of the immune system involved in the development of psoriasis, reducing the inflammatory response and slowing the disease progression.  Still others, like methotrexate, interfere with cell division, helping to control the rapid skin cell growth characteristic of psoriasis.


**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** Certain anti-psoriatic medications are contraindicated in individuals with specific health conditions such as severe liver or kidney disease, certain infections, or allergies to specific ingredients.
* **Drug Interactions:**  Many anti-psoriatic medications can interact with other drugs.  It's crucial to inform your doctor about all medications, supplements, and herbal remedies you are taking.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  Many anti-psoriatic drugs carry risks during pregnancy and breastfeeding. Discuss these risks with your doctor before using these medications if you are pregnant, planning to become pregnant, or breastfeeding.
* **Children and Older Adults:**  Dosage adjustments and careful monitoring may be necessary for children and older adults.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long does it take to see results?** A: The time it takes to see improvement varies significantly depending on the specific medication and the severity of the psoriasis. Some treatments provide quicker relief than others.
* **Q: Can I stop taking the medication once my symptoms improve?** A:  Generally, no.  Psoriasis is a chronic condition, and stopping treatment prematurely can lead to a relapse.  Your doctor will determine when and how to adjust or discontinue medication.
* **Q: How should I store my medication?** A: Follow the storage instructions on the medication label carefully.
* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Refer to your medication's instructions or contact your doctor or pharmacist for guidance.  Do not double the dose to compensate for a missed one.
* **Q: Are there any lifestyle changes that can help manage my psoriasis?** A:  Yes, lifestyle modifications like stress management, maintaining a healthy diet, and avoiding triggers (like certain soaps or stress) can be beneficial.  Your doctor can advise you further.
