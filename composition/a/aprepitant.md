# Aprepitant

**Generic Name: Aprepitant**

**Usage:**

Aprepitant is primarily used to prevent nausea and vomiting caused by chemotherapy (CINV – Chemotherapy-Induced Nausea and Vomiting).  It's highly effective in preventing both acute (occurring soon after chemotherapy) and delayed (occurring days after chemotherapy) CINV.  This makes it a crucial part of managing the side effects of cancer treatment.  It's also sometimes used to prevent nausea and vomiting caused by highly emetogenic (vomiting-inducing) surgery. While less common, it may be considered for other forms of severe nausea and vomiting when other treatments haven't been effective, though this would be under strict medical supervision.


**Dosage:**

Dosage varies depending on the reason for prescribing aprepitant and the patient's individual circumstances.  A doctor will determine the appropriate dosage.  It's typically administered orally as a capsule or tablet.  There is no standard pediatric formulation, and usage in children is not common except in the context of carefully managed chemotherapy regimens.  Adults may receive a single daily dose or a course of medication over several days, depending on the treatment protocol.  Always follow your doctor's instructions precisely regarding dosage and frequency.


**Side Effects:**

* **Common:**  Fatigue, hiccups, constipation, diarrhea, indigestion,  abdominal pain, headache, and dizziness.
* **Less Common but Serious:**  Severe allergic reactions (anaphylaxis),  irregular heartbeat, and liver problems.  These are rare but require immediate medical attention.

**Note:** This is not an exhaustive list. If you experience any concerning side effects, contact your doctor or pharmacist immediately.


**How it Works:**

Aprepitant belongs to a class of drugs called neurokinin-1 (NK1) receptor antagonists.  It works by blocking the action of substance P, a neurotransmitter in the brain that plays a significant role in triggering nausea and vomiting.  By blocking the NK1 receptors, aprepitant prevents substance P from sending signals that initiate the vomiting reflex, thus effectively preventing nausea and vomiting.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** Aprepitant should not be taken by individuals with known hypersensitivity (allergy) to aprepitant or any of its components.
* **Drug Interactions:** Aprepitant can interact with certain medications, particularly those metabolized by the same liver enzymes. It is crucial to inform your doctor about all medications, supplements, and herbal remedies you are taking.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:** The safety of aprepitant during pregnancy and breastfeeding is not fully established. Discuss the risks and benefits with your doctor before using this medication if you are pregnant, breastfeeding, or planning to become pregnant.
* **Elderly Patients:** Older adults may require dose adjustments or close monitoring due to potential changes in liver and kidney function.
* **Hepatic Impairment:** Individuals with liver problems may need a reduced dose or more careful monitoring.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long does it take for aprepitant to work?** A: The onset of action varies, but it usually starts working within a few hours after taking the medication.
* **Q: Can I drink alcohol while taking aprepitant?** A: It's best to avoid alcohol while taking aprepitant as it may increase the risk of side effects.  Always check with your doctor or pharmacist.
* **Q: How should I store aprepitant?** A: Store at room temperature, away from moisture and direct sunlight, and out of reach of children.
* **Q: Can I stop taking aprepitant if I feel better?** A: No,  do not stop taking aprepitant without consulting your doctor, even if you feel better. Complete the prescribed course.
* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it's almost time for your next dose.  Do not double the dose.
