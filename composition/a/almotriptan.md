# Almotriptan

**Generic Name: Almotriptan**

**Usage:**

Almotriptan is a triptan medication primarily used to treat acute migraine attacks in adults.  It's specifically designed to relieve the throbbing headache pain, as well as associated symptoms like nausea and sensitivity to light and sound (photophobia and phonophobia).  It's important to note that almotriptan is not a preventative medication; it's intended to treat a migraine *attack* as it's happening, not to stop migraines from occurring in the first place.  While not its primary use,  almotriptan may also be considered for the treatment of cluster headaches in some cases, but this should always be discussed with a healthcare professional.


**Dosage:**

Almotriptan is typically available as an oral tablet.  The standard adult dose is 12.5 mg.  This should be taken at the first sign of a migraine attack.  A higher dose of 25 mg may be used if the initial dose is ineffective, but this should only be done under the guidance of a doctor.  Almotriptan is *not* recommended for use in children or adolescents.  There are no syrup or injection formulations of almotriptan currently available.  The tablet should be swallowed whole with water and should not be crushed or chewed.


**Side Effects:**

Common side effects, which usually are mild and temporary, can include:

* Dizziness
* Drowsiness
* Tingling or numbness
* Warmth or flushing
* Dry mouth
* Fatigue
* Nausea
* Weakness

Less common, but more serious side effects, require immediate medical attention and include:

* Chest pain or tightness
* Severe allergic reactions (e.g., hives, swelling of the face, lips, tongue, or throat)
* Seizures
* Stroke symptoms (e.g., sudden numbness or weakness, especially on one side of the body; confusion; trouble speaking; vision problems)

If you experience any concerning side effects, stop taking almotriptan and contact your doctor or seek immediate medical attention.


**How it Works:**

Almotriptan works by narrowing blood vessels in the brain.  During a migraine attack, blood vessels in the brain widen, contributing to the pain. Almotriptan constricts these vessels, helping to reduce the headache pain. It also affects the release of certain substances in the brain that trigger migraines.  This dual mechanism of action helps alleviate the symptoms of a migraine.


**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** Almotriptan should not be used by individuals with certain cardiovascular conditions, such as uncontrolled high blood pressure, coronary artery disease, or peripheral vascular disease.  It is also contraindicated in individuals with a history of stroke or transient ischemic attack (TIA).  It should be avoided within 24 hours of using another triptan or ergot medication.
* **Drug Interactions:** Almotriptan can interact with certain medications, particularly MAO inhibitors, so it's crucial to inform your doctor about all medications you are taking, including over-the-counter drugs and herbal supplements.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:** The safety of almotriptan during pregnancy and breastfeeding is not fully established.  Consult your doctor before taking almotriptan if you are pregnant, breastfeeding, or planning to become pregnant.
* **Older Adults:**  Older adults may be more susceptible to side effects.  Dosage adjustments might be necessary.
* **Liver and Kidney Disease:**  Individuals with liver or kidney disease may require dosage adjustments or may not be suitable candidates for almotriptan.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long does it take almotriptan to work?**  A:  Almotriptan typically starts working within 30 minutes to an hour, with peak effectiveness often seen within 2 hours.

* **Q: Can I take almotriptan with other pain relievers?** A:  It's best to consult your doctor before combining almotriptan with other pain medications, especially other migraine treatments.

* **Q: How should I store almotriptan?** A: Store almotriptan tablets at room temperature, away from moisture and direct sunlight.

* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Almotriptan is used on an "as-needed" basis for acute migraine attacks.  Do not take a double dose to make up for a missed dose.

* **Q: Is almotriptan addictive?** A: No, almotriptan is not considered an addictive medication.
