# Azilsartan medoxomil + Cilnidipine

**Generic Name:** Azilsartan medoxomil + Cilnidipine

**Usage:**

Azilsartan medoxomil + Cilnidipine is a combination medication primarily used to treat hypertension (high blood pressure).  It works by targeting two different aspects of blood pressure regulation.  Azilsartan medoxomil is an angiotensin II receptor blocker (ARB), preventing the constriction of blood vessels. Cilnidipine is a calcium channel blocker (CCB), relaxing blood vessels and slowing the heart rate. This combined action leads to a more effective lowering of blood pressure than either drug alone.  While its primary use is hypertension, this combination may be considered for patients who haven't achieved adequate blood pressure control with a single medication.

**Dosage:**

Dosage varies based on individual patient needs and response to treatment.  A healthcare professional will determine the appropriate dosage. Typically, it's available as a tablet, and dosage is adjusted according to blood pressure readings and individual tolerability.  There is no established dosage for children, as this medication is typically only prescribed for adults.  Never adjust the dosage yourself; always follow your doctor's instructions.

**Side Effects:**

Common side effects can include:

* Dizziness
* Headache
* Swelling in ankles or feet
* Fatigue
* Nausea
* Flushing

Less common, but potentially serious side effects include:

* Angioedema (swelling of the face, lips, tongue, or throat) – Seek immediate medical attention if this occurs.
* Liver problems (jaundice, dark urine, light-colored stools) – Contact your doctor immediately.
* Low blood pressure (hypotension) – Symptoms may include dizziness or fainting.
* Cough
* Irregular heartbeat

If you experience any side effects, especially those listed as serious, consult your doctor immediately.

**How it Works:**

Azilsartan medoxomil blocks the action of angiotensin II, a hormone that narrows blood vessels, thereby increasing blood pressure. By blocking this hormone, azilsartan medoxomil helps to relax and widen blood vessels. Cilnidipine works by blocking the entry of calcium into the heart and blood vessel muscle cells. This reduces the force and frequency of the heart's contractions and relaxes blood vessels, further lowering blood pressure.  The combination of these two actions provides a synergistic effect, more effectively managing hypertension.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:**  This medication should not be used by people with a history of angioedema related to ACE inhibitors or ARBs, severe liver problems, or hypersensitivity to azilsartan medoxomil or cilnidipine.
* **Drug Interactions:**  Numerous medications can interact with this combination. It is vital to inform your doctor of all other medications, including over-the-counter drugs, herbal remedies, and supplements you are taking.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  This medication is generally not recommended during pregnancy. Consult your doctor immediately if you become pregnant while taking this medication. The use during breastfeeding requires careful consideration and medical advice.
* **Elderly Patients:**  Older adults may be more susceptible to side effects, requiring careful monitoring and dose adjustment as needed.
* **Kidney and Liver Impairment:**  Dose adjustments are usually necessary for those with kidney or liver problems.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: Can I stop taking this medication suddenly?** A: No, never stop taking this medication suddenly without consulting your doctor. Abrupt discontinuation can lead to a rebound effect, increasing your blood pressure.
* **Q: How should I store this medication?** A: Store at room temperature, away from moisture and direct sunlight. Keep out of reach of children.
* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it's almost time for your next dose.  Do not double the dose.
* **Q: Is this medication safe for long-term use?** A:  Long-term use is common for managing hypertension, however regular monitoring is necessary. Your doctor will assess your continued need for the medication.
* **Q: Can I drink alcohol while taking this medication?** A:  Moderate alcohol consumption is generally acceptable, but excessive alcohol can lower blood pressure further and increase the risk of side effects. Consult your doctor about alcohol consumption.
