# Ampicillin + Dicloxacillin

**Generic Name: Ampicillin + Dicloxacillin**

**Usage:**

Ampicillin + Dicloxacillin is a combination antibiotic used to treat a range of bacterial infections.  Its primary use is in tackling infections caused by bacteria susceptible to both ampicillin (a penicillin-type antibiotic) and dicloxacillin (a penicillinase-resistant penicillin).  This combination is often effective against a broader spectrum of bacteria than either drug alone.  Commonly treated infections include skin and soft tissue infections (like cellulitis, abscesses, impetigo), and certain types of pneumonia.  While not a first-line treatment for most conditions, it may be chosen when a wider antibacterial effect is needed or when the specific bacteria causing the infection are known to be sensitive to both components.  The precise indication will always be determined by a doctor based on the individual patient and their infection.


**Dosage:**

Dosage varies greatly depending on the individual's age, weight, the severity of the infection, and the specific formulation (tablets, capsules, or liquid suspension for oral administration).  A doctor will determine the appropriate dosage and duration of treatment.  There is no universally applicable dosage and self-medication is strongly discouraged.  Always follow a doctor's instructions precisely.  The information below is for illustrative purposes only and should not be interpreted as a prescription:

* **Adults:** Dosage typically ranges from 250mg to 500mg of each component multiple times a day (e.g., every 6 or 8 hours), depending on the doctor's assessment.
* **Children:** Dosage is calculated based on weight and is significantly lower than adult doses. A pediatrician will determine the correct dosage for children.
* **Forms:**  The combination is usually available as oral capsules or tablets. Liquid suspensions might be formulated for children. Injectable forms are less common for this specific combination.


**Side Effects:**

* **Common:** Nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, stomach upset, abdominal pain, rash.
* **Less Common but Serious:**  Allergic reactions (ranging from mild hives to severe anaphylaxis), pseudomembranous colitis (a serious bowel infection), jaundice (yellowing of skin and eyes), changes in blood counts.
* **Note:**  This is not an exhaustive list.  If you experience any unusual or concerning side effects, contact your healthcare provider immediately.


**How it Works:**

Ampicillin and dicloxacillin are both beta-lactam antibiotics. They work by interfering with the synthesis of peptidoglycan, a crucial component of bacterial cell walls.  This disruption weakens the bacterial cell wall, leading to cell death and preventing the bacteria from multiplying.  Ampicillin is susceptible to breakdown by certain bacterial enzymes (beta-lactamases), while dicloxacillin is resistant to many of these enzymes.  The combination thus provides broader coverage against a wider array of bacteria.

**Precautions:**

* **Allergies:**  This combination should not be used by individuals with a known allergy to penicillin antibiotics.  A history of allergic reactions to other beta-lactam antibiotics (like cephalosporins) increases the risk of an allergic reaction to this combination.
* **Interactions:**  Ampicillin and dicloxacillin can interact with other medications, potentially altering their effectiveness or increasing the risk of side effects. Inform your doctor about all medications (prescription, over-the-counter, herbal) you are taking.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  Use during pregnancy and breastfeeding should only occur under strict medical supervision.
* **Kidney and Liver Disease:**  Dosage adjustments may be necessary for individuals with impaired kidney or liver function.
* **Children and Older Adults:**  Specific caution and dosage adjustments are often required for these age groups.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: Can I take this antibiotic if I'm breastfeeding?** A:  Consult your doctor; it may be possible but requires careful consideration of the potential risks and benefits.
* **Q:  How long should I take this medication?** A:  The duration of treatment is determined by your doctor and depends on the severity of the infection. Do not stop treatment prematurely.
* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A:  Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it's almost time for the next dose.  Do not double the dose.
* **Q: How should I store this medication?** A: Store as directed on the label, typically in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight and moisture.
* **Q:  Can I drink alcohol while taking this medication?** A:  It's best to avoid alcohol while taking antibiotics as it can sometimes affect the gut and interact with medication. Consult your doctor.
