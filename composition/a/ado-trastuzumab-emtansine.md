# Ado-trastuzumab emtansine

**Generic Name:** Ado-trastuzumab emtansine

**Usage:**

Ado-trastuzumab emtansine is an antibody-drug conjugate (ADC) primarily used to treat HER2-positive breast cancer.  This means it's effective against breast cancers whose cells have an overabundance of the HER2 protein, a protein that helps cancer cells grow and divide.  Specifically, it's often used in patients whose cancer has progressed after treatment with other HER2-targeted therapies like trastuzumab and lapatinib, or in those with metastatic HER2-positive breast cancer (cancer that has spread to other parts of the body).  While its primary use is in breast cancer, research is ongoing to explore its potential in other HER2-positive cancers.  It's important to understand that this medication is a targeted therapy, meaning it focuses on cancer cells expressing the HER2 protein, minimizing harm to healthy cells.


**Dosage:**

Ado-trastuzumab emtansine is administered intravenously (IV) by a healthcare professional.  The standard dosage for adults is typically 3.6 mg/kg given every three weeks.  Dosage adjustments may be necessary based on individual factors like the patient's overall health, kidney function, and response to treatment.  There is no established dosage for children, as the drug's use in pediatric populations isn't yet fully defined.  The medication comes as a concentrate that is diluted before intravenous administration.


**Side Effects:**

Common side effects include:

* Fatigue
* Nausea
* Vomiting
* Diarrhea
* Low blood cell counts (neutropenia, thrombocytopenia, anemia)
* Hair loss (alopecia)
* Peripheral neuropathy (numbness or tingling in the extremities)
* Liver function abnormalities


Less common but serious side effects can occur and require immediate medical attention.  These include:

* Severe allergic reactions
* Severe lung problems
* Heart problems
* Severe infections


It is crucial to report any side effects to your doctor or healthcare provider immediately.  They can assess the severity and determine the appropriate course of action.


**How it Works:**

Ado-trastuzumab emtansine works by combining two components: a monoclonal antibody (trastuzumab) that targets the HER2 protein and a potent chemotherapy drug (emtansine). The trastuzumab portion specifically binds to HER2-positive cancer cells, essentially acting like a guided missile. Once attached, the emtansine portion is released inside the cancer cell, where it interferes with the cell's ability to grow and divide, ultimately leading to its death. This targeted approach minimizes damage to healthy cells while focusing on eliminating cancer cells.


**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** Ado-trastuzumab emtansine is contraindicated in patients with severe hypersensitivity to the drug or its components.
* **Drug Interactions:**  Interactions with other medications are possible.  It’s critical to inform your doctor about all medications, supplements, and herbal remedies you are taking.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  Ado-trastuzumab emtansine should not be used during pregnancy due to potential harm to the developing fetus.  Women of childbearing potential should use effective contraception during treatment.  It's also not recommended during breastfeeding.
* **Older Adults and Children:**  Dosage adjustments may be necessary for older adults with impaired kidney or liver function.  As mentioned earlier, the use in children is not established.
* **Cardiac Function:**  Close monitoring of heart function is necessary, as the drug can affect the heart.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long will I be on Ado-trastuzumab emtansine?** A: The duration of treatment varies depending on individual response and disease progression.  Your healthcare provider will determine the appropriate treatment duration.

* **Q: How is the medication stored?** A:  Specific storage instructions will be provided by your pharmacy. Generally, it requires refrigeration.

* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Contact your healthcare provider immediately to discuss rescheduling your dose.  Do not attempt to adjust your dosage independently.

* **Q: Are there long-term effects?** A:  Long-term effects are possible.  Regular monitoring by your doctor is crucial to assess and manage any potential complications.

* **Q:  What if I experience a severe side effect?** A: Seek immediate medical attention. Contact your doctor, go to the nearest emergency room, or call emergency services.
