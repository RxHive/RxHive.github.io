# Amlodipine + Hydrochlorothiazide

**Generic Name:** Amlodipine + Hydrochlorothiazide

**Usage:**

Amlodipine + Hydrochlorothiazide is a combination medication used primarily to treat high blood pressure (hypertension).  It combines two active ingredients: amlodipine, a calcium channel blocker, and hydrochlorothiazide, a diuretic (water pill).  By working together, these ingredients effectively lower blood pressure.  The primary goal is to reduce the risk of serious cardiovascular events like stroke, heart attack, and kidney disease associated with uncontrolled hypertension.  While less common, this combination may also be used to treat fluid retention (edema) in patients with heart failure, although other medications might be preferred in this setting.  The use of this combination is always determined by a physician based on individual patient needs and risk factors.


**Dosage:**

Dosage is determined by a doctor and varies greatly depending on the individual's blood pressure, overall health, and response to the medication.  It's typically prescribed as an oral tablet.  There isn't a standard dosage applicable to everyone.  Common adult starting doses might range from 5mg of amlodipine and 12.5mg of hydrochlorothiazide per day, but adjustments are frequently made based on effectiveness and side effects.  This medication is **not typically prescribed for children**.  The doctor will closely monitor blood pressure and adjust the dosage accordingly. It is crucial to follow your physician's instructions precisely and not alter the dosage without their guidance.


**Side Effects:**

Common side effects can include:

* Headache
* Dizziness
* Swelling in ankles and feet
* Fatigue
* Lightheadedness
* Nausea
* Flushing
* Muscle cramps


Less common, but potentially serious side effects include:

* Irregular heartbeat
* Swelling of the face, lips, tongue, or throat (angioedema – seek immediate medical attention)
* Severe allergic reactions
* Liver problems
* Low blood pressure
* Kidney problems
* High blood sugar


If you experience any concerning side effects, especially those listed above as potentially serious, contact your doctor immediately.


**How it Works:**

Amlodipine relaxes blood vessels, making it easier for blood to flow.  Hydrochlorothiazide helps the body get rid of excess salt and water through urine, which reduces blood volume and subsequently lowers blood pressure.  The combined effect of these two actions results in a more significant and sustained decrease in blood pressure compared to using either medication alone.


**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:**  This medication should not be used by individuals with certain conditions such as severe liver or kidney disease, an allergy to sulfa drugs (hydrochlorothiazide is a sulfa drug), or severe narrowing of the aortic valve.
* **Drug Interactions:**  Many medications can interact with amlodipine and hydrochlorothiazide.  Inform your doctor about all medications, supplements, and herbal remedies you are taking.  Certain antibiotics, antifungals, and other blood pressure medications can impact its effectiveness or increase the risk of side effects.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  The use of this medication during pregnancy and breastfeeding should be carefully considered by your doctor.  The risks and benefits need to be weighed, and alternative treatments may be preferred.
* **Elderly Patients:**  Older adults are more susceptible to some side effects, such as dizziness and low blood pressure.  Dosage adjustments may be necessary.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: Can I stop taking this medication suddenly?**  A: No, abruptly stopping this medication can lead to a dangerous rebound increase in blood pressure.  Always consult your doctor before stopping or changing your dosage.

* **Q: How should I store this medication?** A: Store the tablets at room temperature, away from moisture and heat.

* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it is almost time for your next dose. Do not double the dose.

* **Q: Can I drink alcohol while taking this medication?** A:  Excessive alcohol consumption can lower blood pressure further and increase the risk of side effects.  Moderate alcohol intake should be discussed with your doctor.

* **Q: Will this medication cure my high blood pressure?** A: No, this medication manages high blood pressure; it doesn't cure it.  It’s vital to maintain a healthy lifestyle including diet, exercise, and stress management, in addition to taking your medication as prescribed.
