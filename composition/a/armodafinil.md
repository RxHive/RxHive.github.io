# Armodafinil

**Generic Name: Armodafinil**

**Usage:**

Armodafinil is primarily used to treat excessive daytime sleepiness (EDS) associated with narcolepsy and obstructive sleep apnea hypopnea syndrome (OSAHS).  It's a wakefulness-promoting agent, meaning it helps individuals stay awake and alert during the day.  While its primary use focuses on these sleep disorders, it may also be prescribed off-label (meaning not officially approved for this use by regulatory bodies) for other conditions characterized by excessive daytime sleepiness, such as shift work sleep disorder.  However, off-label use should always be discussed with and approved by a healthcare professional.  The decision to prescribe armodafinil for any condition depends entirely on individual circumstances and should be made in consultation with a doctor.


**Dosage:**

The typical adult dosage of armodafinil is usually 150 mg taken once daily in the morning.  However, your doctor may adjust this based on your response to the medication and your individual needs.  The dosage may be increased to a maximum of 250 mg daily in some cases, again determined by your doctor.  Armodafinil is generally available in tablet form. There are no approved syrup or injection formulations.  It's crucial to follow your doctor's prescribed dosage and not alter it without their consultation.  Armodafinil is not typically used in children, and its use in older adults may require careful monitoring and dosage adjustments due to potential age-related factors affecting metabolism and kidney function.

**Side Effects:**

* Headache
* Nausea
* Dizziness
* Anxiety
* Difficulty sleeping (insomnia)
* Dry mouth
* Upset stomach
* Nervousness
* Diarrhea
* Rash


**Serious but less common side effects:**  Serious side effects are rare but can include allergic reactions (such as rash, swelling, difficulty breathing), serious heart problems, and severe psychiatric reactions (such as mania or psychosis).  If you experience any serious or concerning side effects, contact your doctor immediately.

**How it Works:**

Armodafinil is a eugeroic, meaning it promotes wakefulness.  Its exact mechanism of action isn't fully understood, but it's believed to increase the levels of certain neurotransmitters in the brain, including dopamine and orexin (hypocretin). These neurotransmitters play a crucial role in regulating wakefulness and sleep-wake cycles. By increasing these levels, armodafinil helps to promote wakefulness and reduce excessive daytime sleepiness.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** Armodafinil should not be taken by individuals with known hypersensitivity or allergy to armodafinil or modafinil.  It's also contraindicated in individuals with uncontrolled hypertension or severe cardiovascular disease.
* **Drug Interactions:** Armodafinil can interact with several other medications, including certain antidepressants, anticoagulants, and hormonal contraceptives. It's vital to inform your doctor about all other medications, supplements, or herbal remedies you are taking.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  The use of armodafinil during pregnancy and breastfeeding should be carefully considered and only undertaken under the strict guidance of a healthcare professional, weighing the potential benefits against the risks to the mother and child.
* **Older Adults and Children:**  As mentioned previously, dosage adjustments may be needed for older adults due to potential age-related factors.  Armodafinil is generally not used in children.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: Can I stop taking Armodafinil suddenly?** A: No, it's generally recommended to gradually reduce the dosage under your doctor's supervision to avoid potential withdrawal symptoms.

* **Q: How should I store Armodafinil?** A: Store it at room temperature, away from moisture and direct sunlight, in its original container.

* **Q: Is Armodafinil addictive?** A:  While not as addictive as some other stimulants, it can lead to dependence with prolonged use.  It's crucial to follow your doctor's instructions carefully and not exceed the prescribed dose.

* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: If you miss a dose, take it as soon as you remember, unless it's almost time for your next dose. Do not double the dose.
