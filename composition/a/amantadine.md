# Amantadine

**Generic Name: Amantadine**

**Usage:**

Amantadine is primarily used to treat influenza A virus infections.  However, its use for influenza has significantly decreased due to widespread resistance of the virus to the drug.  Its more common current use is in managing the symptoms of Parkinson's disease.  In Parkinson's, amantadine helps reduce rigidity, tremor, and bradykinesia (slowness of movement). It's also sometimes used to treat certain types of dyskinesia (involuntary movements) that can occur as a side effect of levodopa, a common Parkinson's medication. Finally, it may be used off-label for certain neurological conditions or in the management of some types of drug-induced extrapyramidal symptoms.  It's crucial to understand that amantadine is not a cure for any of these conditions, but rather a treatment aimed at alleviating symptoms.


**Dosage:**

Dosage varies significantly depending on the condition being treated and the individual patient.  A healthcare professional will determine the appropriate dose.

* **Parkinson's Disease (Adults):** Typically starts at a low dose and is gradually increased as needed.  The usual range is 100-300 mg per day, often divided into two or three doses.

* **Influenza A (Adults):**  Historically, dosages ranged from 200-400 mg/day but its effectiveness against influenza is significantly limited due to viral resistance.

* **Children:** Amantadine is generally not recommended for children, except in very specific circumstances and under strict medical supervision due to potential risks and lack of sufficient clinical data.

* **Forms:** Amantadine is available in oral tablet and syrup forms. Injection is not a standard route of administration.

* **Administration:**  Amantadine tablets and syrup should be taken as directed by a doctor.  It's usually taken with or without food.


**Side Effects:**

Common side effects can include:

* Dizziness
* Lightheadedness
* Nausea
* Vomiting
* Insomnia
* Anxiety
* Swelling in the ankles or legs
* Dry mouth
* Constipation
* Skin rash

Less common, but more serious side effects include:

* Hallucinations
* Confusion
* Severe depression
* Seizures
* Liver problems

If you experience any adverse effects, particularly those listed as serious, contact your doctor immediately.


**How it Works:**

In Parkinson's disease, amantadine's precise mechanism isn't fully understood, but it's thought to increase the release of dopamine, a neurotransmitter that plays a vital role in movement control.  It may also influence other neurotransmitters and block certain receptors in the brain, contributing to its effects on symptoms.  In the case of influenza A, amantadine prevents the virus from entering and infecting cells by blocking the M2 ion channel protein in the viral membrane.


**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:**  Amantadine should not be used in individuals with known hypersensitivity to the drug.  It's also generally avoided in patients with severe liver or kidney disease.

* **Drug Interactions:**  Amantadine can interact with several other medications, including some anticholinergics, antihistamines, and central nervous system depressants.  Always inform your doctor about all medications, supplements, and herbal remedies you are taking.

* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:** The safety of amantadine during pregnancy and breastfeeding is not fully established.  Consult your doctor before using it if you are pregnant, breastfeeding, or planning to become pregnant.

* **Older Adults:**  Older adults are more susceptible to side effects, particularly those affecting the central nervous system.  Close monitoring is necessary.

* **Driving and Operating Machinery:** Amantadine can cause dizziness and drowsiness.  Avoid driving or operating machinery until you know how it affects you.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long does it take for amantadine to work?** A:  The time it takes to see effects varies depending on the condition and individual. For Parkinson's, it may take several days or weeks to experience improvement.  For influenza, effects are usually seen more quickly.

* **Q: Can I stop taking amantadine suddenly?** A: No, you should never stop taking amantadine suddenly without consulting your doctor.  Sudden cessation can lead to withdrawal symptoms or worsening of the underlying condition.

* **Q: How should I store amantadine?** A: Store amantadine at room temperature, away from moisture and direct sunlight.  Keep it out of reach of children.

* **Q: Is amantadine addictive?** A: Amantadine is not considered to be addictive in the same way as opioids or other substances. However, abrupt discontinuation can lead to withdrawal symptoms, so gradual tapering is essential if stopping treatment.  Always follow your doctor's instructions.

* **Q: Can I take amantadine with alcohol?** A:  Combining amantadine with alcohol can increase the risk of side effects, especially drowsiness and dizziness. It's best to avoid alcohol while taking this medication.
