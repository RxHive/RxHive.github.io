# Avanafil

**Generic Name: Avanafil**

**Usage:**

Avanafil is primarily used to treat erectile dysfunction (ED), also known as impotence, in adult men.  ED is the inability to achieve or maintain an erection firm enough for satisfactory sexual intercourse.  Avanafil works by increasing blood flow to the penis, which is necessary for achieving an erection.  It's important to note that avanafil does not increase libido (sexual desire); it only assists in achieving and maintaining an erection when sexual stimulation is present.  There are no notable secondary uses for avanafil outside of treating ED in adult males.

**Dosage:**

Avanafil is typically available as tablets for oral administration.  The dosage is determined by a healthcare provider and usually starts at 100 mg taken as needed, approximately 30 minutes before sexual activity.  The dose may be increased to 200 mg or decreased to 50 mg based on individual response and tolerability.  However, exceeding 200 mg in a 24-hour period is not recommended.  Avanafil is not recommended for use in children or women.  Dosage adjustments based on age or weight are typically not necessary, but a doctor should always assess individual needs.

**Side Effects:**

Common side effects of avanafil can include:

* Headache
* Flushing (redness of the face, neck, and chest)
* Nasal congestion
* Back pain
* Muscle aches
* Dizziness
* Nausea


Less common, but serious side effects, can include:

* Sudden loss of vision or hearing
* Prolonged erection (priapism)
* Low blood pressure
* Heart attack or stroke

If you experience any of these side effects, seek immediate medical attention.  Always consult your doctor if you experience any adverse effects.

**How it Works:**

Avanafil belongs to a class of medications called phosphodiesterase-5 (PDE5) inhibitors.  These medications work by increasing the levels of cyclic guanosine monophosphate (cGMP) in the penis.  cGMP is a chemical messenger that helps to relax the muscles in the blood vessels of the penis, allowing increased blood flow. This increased blood flow is crucial for achieving and maintaining an erection in response to sexual stimulation.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** Avanafil should not be used by individuals with a history of heart problems, low blood pressure, uncontrolled high blood pressure, stroke, or certain eye conditions.  It is also contraindicated in people taking certain medications, particularly nitrates used to treat angina (chest pain).
* **Drug Interactions:** Avanafil can interact with other medications, including nitrates, some antifungal medications, and some HIV medications.  Inform your doctor about all medications you are taking, including over-the-counter drugs and herbal supplements.
* **Specific Warnings:**  Avanafil is not recommended for use in pregnant women, children, or individuals with severe liver or kidney disease.  Older adults should use avanafil with caution and under close medical supervision due to an increased risk of side effects.

**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long does avanafil last?**  A: The effects of avanafil typically last for several hours, but the duration can vary depending on the individual and the dosage.
* **Q: Can I drink alcohol while taking avanafil?** A:  While moderate alcohol consumption is generally not prohibited, excessive alcohol use can worsen side effects and reduce the effectiveness of avanafil.  It's best to discuss alcohol consumption with your doctor.
* **Q: How should I store avanafil?** A: Store avanafil tablets in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight and moisture, ideally at room temperature.
* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Avanafil is taken as needed, so missing a dose is not usually a cause for concern. Take it when you are sexually active and follow the prescribed dosage.
