# Astaxanthin + Coenzyme Q + Levo-carnitine + Lycopene + Zinc Sulphate Monohydrate + Selenium + Piperine

**Generic Name:** Astaxanthin + Coenzyme Q10 + Levo-carnitine + Lycopene + Zinc Sulphate Monohydrate + Selenium + Piperine

**Usage:** This combination of ingredients is often marketed as a nutritional supplement aimed at supporting overall health and well-being, rather than treating specific medical conditions or diseases.  It's not a drug approved for the treatment of any particular illness.  The individual components have various purported benefits:

* **Astaxanthin:** A powerful antioxidant associated with potential benefits for eye health, skin health, and reducing inflammation.
* **Coenzyme Q10 (CoQ10):** An antioxidant that plays a role in energy production within cells. It's sometimes used to support heart health.
* **Levo-carnitine:**  Assists in transporting fatty acids into cells for energy production. It's sometimes used to improve energy levels and athletic performance.
* **Lycopene:** A carotenoid antioxidant found in tomatoes and other red fruits. It's associated with potential benefits for cardiovascular health and may play a role in protecting against certain cancers.
* **Zinc Sulphate Monohydrate:**  Provides zinc, an essential mineral involved in numerous bodily functions, including immune function and wound healing.
* **Selenium:** An essential trace mineral with antioxidant properties, supporting thyroid function and immune health.
* **Piperine:**  An alkaloid found in black pepper, believed to enhance the bioavailability (absorption) of other nutrients.

This combination aims to synergistically enhance the effects of each individual component.  However,  clinically proven benefits for specific diseases from this *combination* are lacking.  More research is needed to establish definitive efficacy for treating any particular medical condition.
