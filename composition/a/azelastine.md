# Azelastine

**Generic Name: Azelastine**

**Usage:** Azelastine is primarily used to relieve the symptoms of allergic rhinitis (hay fever) and conjunctivitis (allergic eye inflammation).  This means it helps manage symptoms like sneezing, runny nose, itchy eyes and nose, and watery eyes.  While its primary use is for allergic conditions, it may sometimes be used off-label to treat other conditions, but this should always be under the direct guidance of a doctor.


**Dosage:**  Azelastine comes in various forms including nasal spray and ophthalmic solution (eye drops). Dosage varies considerably based on the form and the specific condition being treated.  A doctor will determine the appropriate dosage and form based on the individual's age, weight, and the severity of their symptoms.  Always follow the prescription instructions carefully.  It's crucial to note that self-adjusting the dosage can be dangerous and should be avoided.  There is no standardized single dosage for all individuals.


**Side Effects:**  Like all medications, azelastine can cause side effects. Common side effects usually mild and temporary, can include:

*   Burning or stinging sensation in the nose or eyes (for nasal spray and eye drops respectively)
*   Headache
*   Bitter taste
*   Drowsiness
*   Dry mouth/nose/throat
*   Nosebleeds (less common with nasal spray)


Less common but more serious side effects are possible and require immediate medical attention. These can include:

*   Severe allergic reactions (e.g., swelling of the face, lips, or tongue; difficulty breathing)
*   Fast or irregular heartbeat
*   Seizures


If you experience any concerning side effects, stop using the medication and contact your healthcare provider immediately.


**How it Works:** Azelastine is an antihistamine.  Histamine is a chemical released by the body during an allergic reaction, causing inflammation and the typical allergy symptoms. Azelastine works by blocking the action of histamine at its receptor sites, thus reducing inflammation and alleviating the associated symptoms.  It specifically targets the H1 receptor.

**Precautions:**

* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  Consult your doctor before using azelastine if you are pregnant, breastfeeding, or planning to become pregnant.
* **Children:** Use in children should be as per the doctor's prescription, adhering to the correct age-appropriate dosage.
* **Older Adults:**  Older adults may be more sensitive to the side effects of azelastine. A doctor should carefully assess the risks and benefits before prescribing it.
* **Drug Interactions:**  Inform your doctor or pharmacist about all other medications, vitamins, or supplements you are taking, as azelastine may interact with other drugs.
* **Alcohol:** Avoid alcohol consumption while taking azelastine as it can increase drowsiness.
* **Driving and Operating Machinery:**  Azelastine can cause drowsiness. Avoid driving or operating machinery if you experience this side effect.
