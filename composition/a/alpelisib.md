# Alpelisib

**Generic Name: Alpelisib**

**Usage:**

Alpelisib is a targeted therapy primarily used to treat certain types of advanced breast cancer.  Its main application is in adults with hormone receptor-positive, human epidermal growth factor receptor 2 (HER2)-negative advanced or metastatic breast cancer that has progressed after endocrine therapy.  This means it's used in patients whose cancer has spread and no longer responds to hormone treatments.  While its primary use is in breast cancer, research is ongoing to explore its potential in other cancers involving similar molecular pathways.  It's crucial to understand that alpelisib is not a treatment for all breast cancers; its use is specific to certain types with particular characteristics.

**Dosage:**

Dosage and administration of alpelisib are determined by a healthcare professional and are tailored to the individual patient's condition and response to treatment. Typically, alpelisib is administered orally as a tablet.  The standard adult dose is usually given once daily.  The specific dose may vary, and adjustments might be necessary based on factors such as the patient's overall health, liver function, and the severity of side effects.  There is currently no approved pediatric dosage for alpelisib.  It is essential to follow the prescribing healthcare professional's instructions meticulously.


**Side Effects:**

Alpelisib, like other cancer therapies, can cause side effects. Some common side effects include:

* Diarrhea
* Nausea
* Vomiting
* Fatigue
* Skin rash
* Increased blood sugar (hyperglycemia)
* Decreased blood sugar (hypoglycemia)
* Elevated cholesterol or triglycerides


Less common but potentially serious side effects include:

* Severe diarrhea
* Severe skin reactions
* Liver problems (hepatotoxicity)
* Lung problems (pneumonitis)
* Heart problems (QT prolongation)


It's crucial to report any concerning side effects to your doctor immediately.  They can provide guidance on managing side effects and determine if dosage adjustments or alternative treatments are necessary.


**How it Works:**

Alpelisib is a kinase inhibitor.  Kinases are enzymes that play a vital role in cell growth and division.  In some cancers, including certain breast cancers, the PI3K/AKT/mTOR pathway is overactive, promoting uncontrolled cell growth. Alpelisib works by selectively blocking the PI3Kα enzyme, a key component of this pathway. By inhibiting PI3Kα, alpelisib helps to slow or stop the growth of cancer cells.


**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** Alpelisib is contraindicated in patients with severe liver impairment.
* **Drug Interactions:** Alpelisib can interact with other medications. It's essential to inform your healthcare provider of all medications, including over-the-counter drugs, herbal remedies, and supplements, you are taking.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:** Alpelisib is not recommended during pregnancy or breastfeeding due to the potential risk to the fetus or infant.  Effective contraception should be used.
* **Older Adults and Children:**  The safety and effectiveness of alpelisib in older adults and children have not been fully established.  Careful monitoring is essential for these patient populations.
* **Liver Function:** Regular monitoring of liver function is crucial during alpelisib treatment.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long do I need to take alpelisib?** A: The duration of alpelisib treatment depends on your individual response to the therapy and is determined by your healthcare provider.
* **Q: How should I store alpelisib?** A: Store alpelisib tablets at room temperature, away from moisture and heat.
* **Q: Can I drink alcohol while taking alpelisib?** A: It's best to discuss alcohol consumption with your doctor while on alpelisib, as it can potentially interact with the medication or worsen side effects.
* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: If you miss a dose, take it as soon as you remember, unless it's almost time for the next dose. Don't double the dose.
* **Q: Are there any long-term effects of alpelisib?** A:  Long-term effects are still being studied.  Regular monitoring by your healthcare provider is important.
