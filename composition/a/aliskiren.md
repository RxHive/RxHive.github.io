# Aliskiren

**Generic Name: Aliskiren**

**Usage:**

Aliskiren is a medication primarily used to treat high blood pressure (hypertension).  It's a direct renin inhibitor, meaning it works by blocking the renin-angiotensin-aldosterone system (RAAS).  This system plays a crucial role in regulating blood pressure. By inhibiting renin, aliskiren reduces the production of angiotensin II, a powerful vasoconstrictor (a substance that narrows blood vessels), thus lowering blood pressure.

While its primary use is in managing hypertension,  it's sometimes considered in combination with other blood pressure medications in patients whose blood pressure isn't adequately controlled by other treatments.  It's important to note that aliskiren is not typically a first-line treatment for hypertension and its use is often considered in specific clinical situations based on individual patient needs and a doctor's assessment.


**Dosage:**

Dosage of aliskiren varies based on individual patient factors and the physician's clinical judgment.  It's usually available as an oral tablet.  There is no pediatric formulation readily available, and its use in children is not generally established.  

Typical adult starting doses range from 150 mg to 300 mg once daily.  The dose may be adjusted by the healthcare provider based on blood pressure response.  However, exceeding 300 mg daily is generally not recommended due to the potential for increased side effects without a proportional increase in blood pressure lowering. The medication should be taken with or without food.  Precise dosage instructions should always be followed as provided by the prescribing physician.


**Side Effects:**

Common side effects of aliskiren may include:

* Dizziness
* Diarrhea
* Upper respiratory tract infection
* Cough
* Hyperkalemia (high potassium levels in the blood)
* Fatigue


Less common, but potentially more serious side effects include:

* Angioedema (swelling of the face, lips, tongue, or throat) – **Seek immediate medical attention if this occurs.**
* Hypotension (low blood pressure)
* Renal impairment (kidney problems)

If you experience any adverse effects, particularly those listed above, consult your doctor immediately.


**How it Works:**

Aliskiren works by directly inhibiting renin, an enzyme crucial to the RAAS.  Renin is the first step in a cascade of events leading to the production of angiotensin II, a potent vasoconstrictor that elevates blood pressure.  By blocking renin, aliskiren prevents the formation of angiotensin II, leading to vasodilation (widening of blood vessels) and reduced blood pressure.  This effect helps to regulate blood pressure and brings it back to a healthier range.


**Precautions:**

* **Pregnancy:** Aliskiren is contraindicated (should not be used) during pregnancy as it can cause serious harm to the developing fetus.
* **Breastfeeding:** It's unknown whether aliskiren passes into breast milk.  Consult your doctor before breastfeeding while taking this medication.
* **Kidney disease:** Aliskiren should be used with caution in patients with impaired kidney function.  Dose adjustments may be necessary.
* **Diabetes:** Patients with diabetes should be monitored closely for hyperkalemia (high potassium levels).
* **Other medications:** Aliskiren can interact with other medications, especially ACE inhibitors, ARBs, and diuretics.  Inform your doctor of all medications you are taking, including over-the-counter drugs and herbal supplements.
* **Older adults:** Older adults may be more sensitive to the side effects of aliskiren.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: Can I stop taking aliskiren suddenly?**  A: No, you should never stop taking aliskiren abruptly.  Gradual dose reduction under medical supervision is essential to avoid potential rebound hypertension.

* **Q: How should I store aliskiren?** A: Store aliskiren tablets in a cool, dry place, away from direct sunlight and moisture.  Keep out of reach of children.

* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it's almost time for the next dose.  Do not double the dose.

* **Q: Is aliskiren addictive?** A:  No, aliskiren is not considered an addictive medication.

* **Q:  Can I drink alcohol while taking aliskiren?** A:  Excessive alcohol consumption can interfere with blood pressure control. Moderate alcohol intake should be discussed with your physician.
