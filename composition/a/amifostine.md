# Amifostine

**Generic Name: Amifostine**

**Usage:**

Amifostine is a cytoprotective agent, meaning it helps protect healthy cells from damage caused by certain types of cancer treatments.  Its primary use is to reduce the severe side effects, specifically kidney damage and mouth sores (mucositis), associated with high-dose cisplatin-based chemotherapy. This is particularly relevant for patients undergoing treatment for cancers like ovarian, head and neck, and bladder cancer.  While not a first-line treatment for these cancers themselves, amifostine plays a crucial supportive role in mitigating the harsh side effects of chemotherapy, allowing patients to tolerate higher doses of the cancer-fighting drugs and potentially improving treatment outcomes.  Amifostine is currently *not* approved for use outside the context of reducing cisplatin-induced nephrotoxicity (kidney damage) and mucositis.  Research into its potential use in other areas, such as radiation therapy protection, is ongoing but hasn't led to widespread clinical application.


**Dosage:**

Amifostine is administered intravenously (IV) – it's not available in tablet or syrup form.  Dosage is determined by a healthcare professional and is carefully calculated based on the patient's body surface area (BSA) and the specific chemotherapy regimen. There's no standard dosage applicable to all patients.  Pediatric dosing is similarly individualized and requires careful consideration of the child's age, weight, and overall health.  Generally, amifostine is given as a single IV infusion 30 minutes prior to cisplatin administration.  Precise instructions on infusion rate and total dose will be provided by the prescribing oncologist or other qualified healthcare professional.


**Side Effects:**

Common side effects of amifostine can include:

*   Low blood pressure (hypotension)
*   Nausea and vomiting
*   Fatigue
*   Fever
*   Facial flushing
*   Injection site reactions (pain, swelling, redness)

Less common, but potentially serious side effects, may include allergic reactions (anaphylaxis) and severe changes in blood counts.  Any unusual symptoms or worsening of existing conditions should be reported immediately to a doctor or other healthcare provider.


**How it Works:**

Amifostine works by acting as a "selective radioprotector."  It's converted in the body to a more active form that protects healthy cells, particularly in the kidneys and gastrointestinal tract, from the damaging effects of cisplatin. This protection is achieved through several mechanisms, including scavenging free radicals (highly reactive molecules that cause cellular damage) and interfering with the processes by which cisplatin causes cell death. Importantly, this protective effect is relatively more pronounced in normal cells than in cancer cells, thus minimizing negative impacts on the effectiveness of chemotherapy itself.


**Precautions:**

*   **Contraindications:** Amifostine should not be used in individuals with a known hypersensitivity (allergy) to the drug.
*   **Drug Interactions:** Amifostine can interact with other medications, potentially affecting their effectiveness or increasing the risk of side effects.  It's crucial to inform your doctor of all medications, including over-the-counter drugs and supplements, you are taking.
*   **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:** Amifostine should only be used during pregnancy if the potential benefits outweigh the risks.  Breastfeeding should be avoided during and for a period after amifostine treatment.
*   **Older Adults and Children:** Dosage adjustments may be necessary for older adults and children due to differences in metabolism and potential organ function changes.
*   **Kidney and Liver Function:** Impaired kidney or liver function may necessitate dose adjustments or close monitoring.

**FAQs:**

*   **Q: How long will I be on amifostine?**  A: Amifostine is generally only given during a course of cisplatin-based chemotherapy and is not a long-term medication.
*   **Q: Can I drive after receiving amifostine?** A: Low blood pressure is a possible side effect; consult your healthcare provider about driving safety.
*   **Q: How is amifostine stored?** A:  Storage guidelines will be provided by your pharmacist, usually involving refrigeration.
*   **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A:  Amifostine is administered by healthcare professionals; missed doses must be discussed with your oncologist or nurse.
*   **Q: Are there any long-term effects of amifostine?** A:  Long-term effects are generally uncommon, but any concerns should be discussed with your healthcare provider.
