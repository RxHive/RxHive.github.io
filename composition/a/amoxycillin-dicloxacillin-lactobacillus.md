# Amoxycillin + Dicloxacillin + Lactobacillus

**Generic Name:** Amoxycillin + Dicloxacillin + Lactobacillus

**Usage:** This combination medication is typically used to treat bacterial infections, leveraging the synergistic effects of its components.  Amoxycillin and dicloxacillin are both antibiotics belonging to the penicillin family, but with slightly different spectrums of activity. Amoxycillin is a broad-spectrum antibiotic effective against a wide range of bacteria, while dicloxacillin is more focused on penicillinase-resistant staphylococci (bacteria that can inactivate amoxicillin).  The addition of *Lactobacillus*, a probiotic, aims to mitigate the disruption of gut flora that can occur with antibiotic use, potentially reducing the risk of antibiotic-associated diarrhea.  

The primary use is for bacterial infections where both amoxycillin and dicloxacillin activity is desired, often skin and soft tissue infections. Secondary uses might include certain types of pneumonia or other infections based on the prescribing physician's judgment and sensitivity testing of the infecting bacteria.  It's crucial to understand that this medication is only effective against bacterial infections; it will not treat viral infections like the common cold or influenza.

**Dosage:**  Dosage is strictly determined by a healthcare professional based on individual factors such as age, weight, the severity of the infection, and the specific strain of bacteria involved.  There is no standardized, universally applicable dosage for this combination.  This medication is typically not available as a pre-mixed combination; rather, amoxicillin and dicloxacillin might be prescribed alongside a probiotic supplement containing Lactobacillus.   The physician will prescribe appropriate dosages of each component separately.  Dosage forms will vary; amoxicillin and dicloxacillin can be found as capsules, tablets, or suspensions (syrups). Lactobacillus is usually available in capsule or powder form. Always follow the healthcare provider's instructions carefully.

**Side Effects:**

* **Common:** Diarrhea, nausea, vomiting, abdominal cramps, indigestion, rash, itching.
* **Less Common but Serious:**  Severe allergic reactions (anaphylaxis – characterized by difficulty breathing, swelling of the face, lips, or tongue), Clostridium difficile-associated diarrhea (CDAD – a severe intestinal infection), liver damage, jaundice, blood disorders.

If you experience any adverse effects, especially severe ones, seek immediate medical attention.

**How it Works:** Amoxycillin and dicloxacillin are beta-lactam antibiotics. They work by interfering with the synthesis of bacterial cell walls, leading to bacterial cell death.  Amoxycillin acts on a wider range of bacteria, while dicloxacillin is particularly effective against penicillinase-producing bacteria.  *Lactobacillus* is a probiotic bacterium that helps maintain a healthy balance of microorganisms in the gut. By repopulating the gut with beneficial bacteria, it helps reduce the likelihood of digestive disturbances often associated with antibiotic use.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:**  This medication should not be used by individuals with a known allergy to penicillin or cephalosporin antibiotics.
* **Drug Interactions:** This combination may interact with other medications, including anticoagulants (blood thinners) and oral contraceptives.  Always inform your doctor of all medications, supplements, or herbal remedies you are taking.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  The use of this combination during pregnancy and breastfeeding should be carefully evaluated by a physician.  The benefits must be weighed against potential risks to the mother and child.
* **Children and Older Adults:**  Dosage adjustments might be necessary for children and older adults based on factors like renal function and overall health.
* **Allergic Reactions:**  Be vigilant for signs of allergic reactions, such as hives, swelling, or difficulty breathing.  Seek immediate medical help if these symptoms occur.

**FAQs:**

* **Q: Can I stop taking this medication early if I feel better?**  A: No. Complete the prescribed course of antibiotics, even if you feel better, to prevent the recurrence of infection and the development of antibiotic resistance.
* **Q: How should I store this medication?** A: Store as directed by the pharmacist or physician, usually in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight and heat.
* **Q: Is this medication safe for everyone?** A:  No. Individuals with allergies to penicillin, kidney disease, or certain other conditions may not be suitable candidates. Consult your doctor.
* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it is almost time for the next dose.  Never double the dose.
* **Q:  Can I take this medication with alcohol?** A:  Consult your doctor or pharmacist. Alcohol may interact with certain antibiotics.
