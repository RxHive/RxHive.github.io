# Amoxycillin + Lactobacillus

**Generic Name:** Amoxycillin + Lactobacillus

**Usage:**

Amoxycillin + Lactobacillus is a combination medication used primarily to treat bacterial infections while simultaneously supporting the gut's healthy bacteria population.  Amoxycillin, a penicillin-type antibiotic, effectively targets a wide range of bacteria responsible for various infections. These infections may include respiratory tract infections (like bronchitis and pneumonia), ear infections (otitis media), skin infections, urinary tract infections, and certain sexually transmitted infections.  The addition of Lactobacillus, a probiotic, aims to mitigate the disruption to the gut flora that can be a side effect of antibiotic treatment.  By replenishing beneficial bacteria, it helps to prevent or lessen common issues such as diarrhea, often associated with antibiotic use. While the primary use is treating bacterial infections, this combination may sometimes be used as a preventative measure alongside antibiotics in situations where gut flora disruption is a significant concern. The exact indications will always depend on a healthcare professional's assessment.


**Dosage:**

Dosage of Amoxycillin + Lactobacillus will vary significantly depending on factors like the patient's age, weight, the severity of the infection, and the specific formulation of the medication.  It is crucial to follow a doctor's prescription precisely.  

There is no single "standard" dosage.  The amoxycillin component's dosage is typically determined by body weight and the type of infection being treated, ranging from a few hundred milligrams to over a gram per dose. The Lactobacillus component's dosage is usually standardized per capsule or tablet.  

The medication might be available in different forms: tablets, capsules, or liquid suspensions (syrups) for oral administration. In some cases, amoxycillin may also be administered intravenously, although this usually happens in hospitals and is not typical for the combination product. Always carefully follow the instructions provided by your doctor or pharmacist regarding how and when to take the medication.


**Side Effects:**

* **Common Side Effects:** Diarrhea (often lessened by the Lactobacillus), nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain, bloating, gas, rash, headache, and yeast infections (thrush).
* **Less Common but Serious Side Effects:** Allergic reactions (ranging from mild skin rashes to severe anaphylaxis – requiring immediate medical attention), Clostridium difficile-associated diarrhea (a severe intestinal infection), and liver problems.  
* **Note:** This is not an exhaustive list. If you experience any unusual or concerning side effects, consult your doctor or pharmacist immediately.


**How it Works:**

Amoxycillin works by interfering with the ability of bacteria to build their cell walls. This weakens and eventually kills the bacteria, thus clearing the infection. Lactobacillus, on the other hand, works by colonizing the gut and competing with harmful bacteria for nutrients and space.  This helps to restore the balance of the gut flora, minimizing the potential for antibiotic-associated diarrhea and other digestive disturbances.  The combination aims for effective infection control coupled with gut health maintenance.


**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:**  Amoxycillin should not be used by individuals with a known allergy to penicillin or cephalosporin antibiotics.  Always inform your healthcare provider about your medical history, especially any allergies.
* **Drug Interactions:**  Amoxycillin can interact with certain medications, including anticoagulants (blood thinners) and some types of birth control pills.  Consult your doctor or pharmacist about any other medications you are taking.
* **Vulnerable Groups:**  Pregnant women, breastfeeding mothers, and children should use this medication only under strict medical supervision.  Dosage adjustments and monitoring are often necessary for these groups. Older adults may also be at increased risk for side effects.
* **Other Precautions:** Avoid alcohol consumption while taking amoxycillin.  Complete the entire course of prescribed medication, even if symptoms improve, to prevent recurrence of the infection.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: Can I take Amoxycillin + Lactobacillus without a prescription?**  A: No.  This medication should only be used under the guidance of a healthcare professional.
* **Q: How should I store Amoxycillin + Lactobacillus?** A: Store as directed on the packaging, typically in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight and moisture.
* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it's almost time for your next dose. Do not double the dose.
* **Q: Is it safe to take this medication with other probiotics?** A:  Discuss with your doctor or pharmacist.  While often safe, certain combinations may affect the effectiveness of either.
* **Q: Can this medication cure all bacterial infections?**  A: No.  Its effectiveness depends on the type of bacteria causing the infection.  A healthcare provider will diagnose the infection and determine the appropriate treatment.
