# Amoxycillin + Dicloxacillin

**Generic Name:** Amoxycillin + Dicloxacillin

**Usage:**

Amoxycillin and dicloxacillin are both antibiotics, specifically penicillin-type antibiotics.  This combination medication is primarily used to treat a range of bacterial infections, particularly those involving skin and skin structure infections (like impetigo, cellulitis, and abscesses).  It's effective against a broad spectrum of bacteria, but its strength lies in tackling infections caused by both gram-positive and some gram-negative bacteria that are susceptible to penicillin.  While not its primary use, this combination may sometimes be considered for certain types of respiratory tract infections when a broad-spectrum approach is deemed necessary.  However, the selection of antibiotics should always be guided by a healthcare professional based on the specific infection and bacterial sensitivity testing.  This combination is not a "first-line" treatment for many conditions and is generally reserved for situations where a healthcare provider judges it to be the most appropriate choice.

**Dosage:**

Dosage for amoxycillin + dicloxacillin varies considerably based on factors like the patient's age, weight, the severity of the infection, and the specific formulation (capsules, tablets, or liquid suspension).  *It is crucial to follow the prescription instructions provided by your doctor precisely.*  There is no standard dosage that can be safely provided here.  A physician will determine the appropriate dosage and duration of treatment.  Incorrect dosage can lead to treatment failure or increase the risk of side effects.  Children's dosages are significantly lower than adult dosages and are carefully calculated based on weight.  Liquid suspensions may be easier to administer to children.


**Side Effects:**

Common side effects can include:

* Nausea
* Vomiting
* Diarrhea
* Stomach upset
* Rash
* Itching

Less common but more serious side effects may include:

* Allergic reactions (including severe, life-threatening anaphylaxis – characterized by difficulty breathing, swelling of the face, lips, or tongue)
* Clostridium difficile-associated diarrhea (a severe form of diarrhea)
* Liver damage
* Seizures (especially in patients with pre-existing kidney or liver issues)

If you experience any concerning side effects, especially those listed as more serious, contact your healthcare provider immediately.

**How it Works:**

Amoxycillin and dicloxacillin both interfere with the ability of bacteria to build their cell walls.  Bacteria rely on these cell walls for structural integrity and survival.  By disrupting cell wall synthesis, both antibiotics weaken the bacteria, leading to their death and preventing further infection spread. Amoxycillin is a broader-spectrum antibiotic than dicloxacillin, effectively targeting a wider range of bacteria. Combining them provides coverage against a wider array of organisms, increasing the chances of successful treatment.

**Precautions:**

* **Allergic Reactions:**  This combination should be avoided by individuals with known allergies to penicillin or cephalosporin antibiotics.  A history of allergic reactions to penicillin is a serious contraindication.
* **Kidney or Liver Disease:** Dosage adjustments are often necessary for patients with impaired kidney or liver function.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:** The use of this medication during pregnancy and breastfeeding should be carefully considered and discussed with a healthcare provider.  The benefits must outweigh potential risks to the mother and child.
* **Drug Interactions:** This medication may interact with certain other drugs, including anticoagulants (blood thinners) and oral contraceptives. Always inform your doctor of all medications, supplements, and herbal remedies you are taking.
* **Children:** The appropriate dosage for children must be determined by a physician based on weight and the specific infection.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: Can I take this medication with alcohol?**  A: It's best to avoid alcohol while taking this medication, as it can potentially increase the risk of stomach upset and other side effects. Always consult your doctor or pharmacist.
* **Q: How should I store this medication?** A: Store at room temperature, away from moisture and heat.  Keep it out of reach of children.
* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it's almost time for your next dose.  Do not double the dose.
* **Q: Can I stop taking the medication early if I feel better?** A: No, it's crucial to complete the entire course of antibiotics prescribed by your doctor, even if you feel better.  Stopping early can lead to treatment failure and the development of antibiotic resistance.
* **Q: Is this medication safe for everyone?** A: This medication is not safe for everyone.  Pre-existing conditions, allergies, and interactions with other medications can significantly affect its safety profile. Always consult a doctor before starting this or any medication.
