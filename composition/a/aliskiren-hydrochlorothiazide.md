# Aliskiren + Hydrochlorothiazide

**Generic Name:** Aliskiren + Hydrochlorothiazide

**Usage:**

Aliskiren + Hydrochlorothiazide is a combination medication used primarily to treat high blood pressure (hypertension) in adults.  It's particularly useful for individuals whose blood pressure isn't adequately controlled by either aliskiren or hydrochlorothiazide alone.  Aliskiren is a direct renin inhibitor, meaning it works by blocking the renin-angiotensin-aldosterone system (RAAS), a key regulator of blood pressure. Hydrochlorothiazide is a thiazide diuretic; it helps the body get rid of excess salt and water, thus lowering blood pressure. Combining these two medications often results in a more effective blood pressure reduction than either drug used individually.  While its primary use is in hypertension,  it's not typically used for other conditions.

**Dosage:**

Dosage is determined by a healthcare professional based on individual needs and response.  It's crucial to follow prescribed instructions precisely.  Generally, the combination is available as a tablet.  There are various strengths available, and the doctor will prescribe the appropriate dose.  This medication is not generally recommended for use in children, and there's limited information regarding its use in the elderly beyond standard hypertension management.  Dosage adjustments may be needed for those with kidney or liver impairment.  *Never adjust your dosage without consulting your doctor.*

**Side Effects:**

Common side effects can include:

* Dizziness
* Lightheadedness
* Fatigue
* Diarrhea
* Nausea
* Muscle cramps
* Dry cough (less common than with ACE inhibitors)
* Increased urination

Less common but serious side effects can include:

* Angioedema (swelling of the face, lips, tongue, or throat) – seek immediate medical attention if this occurs.
* Allergic reactions
* High potassium levels (hyperkalemia)
* Low blood pressure (hypotension)
* Kidney problems


If you experience any concerning side effects, contact your doctor or seek immediate medical attention as needed.

**How it Works:**

Aliskiren works by inhibiting renin, an enzyme crucial in the production of angiotensin II. Angiotensin II is a powerful vasoconstrictor (narrows blood vessels), thus increasing blood pressure. By blocking renin, aliskiren reduces angiotensin II levels, leading to vasodilation (widening of blood vessels) and lower blood pressure. Hydrochlorothiazide works by increasing the excretion of sodium and water from the body through the kidneys. This reduces blood volume, consequently lowering blood pressure.  The combined effect of these two mechanisms results in a more significant and sustained reduction in blood pressure.


**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:**  This medication is contraindicated in individuals with known allergy to aliskiren, hydrochlorothiazide, or any of its ingredients.  It should be avoided in individuals with severe kidney impairment, severe liver impairment, or those with a history of angioedema associated with ACE inhibitors or ARBs.
* **Drug Interactions:**  Aliskiren + Hydrochlorothiazide can interact with several other medications, including other blood pressure medications, potassium supplements, and certain pain relievers.  Inform your doctor about all medications, supplements, and herbal remedies you are taking.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  This medication is generally not recommended during pregnancy or breastfeeding.  Discuss risks and benefits with your healthcare provider.
* **Elderly Patients:**  Older adults may be more susceptible to side effects, requiring careful monitoring and potential dose adjustments.
* **Kidney and Liver Impairment:**  Dosage adjustments are necessary for individuals with kidney or liver problems.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long does it take to work?** A: Blood pressure lowering effects usually become noticeable within a few weeks of starting the medication.
* **Q: Can I stop taking it suddenly?** A: No, abruptly stopping this medication can lead to a sudden rise in blood pressure.  Always follow your doctor's instructions for discontinuation.
* **Q: What if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it's almost time for your next dose.  Never double up on doses.
* **Q: How should I store it?** A: Store at room temperature, away from moisture and heat.
* **Q: Is this medication addictive?** A: No, this medication is not addictive.
