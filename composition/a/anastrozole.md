# Anastrozole

**Generic Name: Anastrozole**

**Usage:**

Anastrozole is primarily used to treat hormone-receptor-positive breast cancer in postmenopausal women.  It works by lowering the amount of estrogen in the body.  Estrogen can fuel the growth of certain types of breast cancer cells, so by reducing estrogen levels, anastrozole helps slow or stop the cancer's growth.  This is its primary and most common use.  While not as common, anastrozole may also be used in certain cases of advanced breast cancer in premenopausal women, often in conjunction with other treatments.  It's crucial to understand that anastrozole is not a cure for breast cancer but a treatment option to help manage and control the disease.  The specific use and effectiveness will depend on the individual patient and their cancer type.

**Dosage:**

The standard adult dosage is typically one 1 mg tablet taken orally once daily.  Dosage is generally not adjusted based on weight or age in postmenopausal women. Children should not take anastrozole.  Anastrozole is only available in tablet form; there are no syrup or injectable formulations.  The tablet should be swallowed whole with water.  It's essential to follow the prescribed dosage and schedule precisely as directed by the healthcare provider.  Any changes to the dosage should only be made under the guidance of a physician.

**Side Effects:**

Common side effects can include:

* Hot flashes
* Joint pain (arthralgia)
* Muscle pain (myalgia)
* Headaches
* Nausea
* Vaginal dryness
* Fatigue
* Weight changes


Less common but more serious side effects can include:

*  Severe allergic reactions (rash, swelling, difficulty breathing)
*  Bone thinning (osteoporosis) – increased risk of fractures
*  Heart problems
*  Changes in liver function


If you experience any adverse effects, especially those listed as serious, contact your healthcare provider immediately.

**How it Works:**

Anastrozole is an aromatase inhibitor.  Aromatase is an enzyme in the body that converts androgens (male hormones) into estrogens (female hormones). By blocking the action of aromatase, anastrozole significantly reduces the production of estrogen, particularly in postmenopausal women who primarily produce estrogen through this pathway. This reduction in estrogen levels helps to starve estrogen-dependent breast cancer cells, hindering their growth and potentially leading to tumor shrinkage or slowed progression.

**Precautions:**

* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:** Anastrozole is contraindicated during pregnancy and breastfeeding.  It can harm the developing fetus.  Reliable contraception is essential for women of childbearing age.
* **Liver and Kidney Impairment:**  Anastrozole should be used cautiously in individuals with pre-existing liver or kidney disease.  Dosage adjustments may be necessary.
* **Osteoporosis:** Anastrozole can increase the risk of osteoporosis. Bone density monitoring may be advisable for long-term users.
* **Drug Interactions:** Anastrozole may interact with other medications.  Always inform your doctor or pharmacist of all medications, supplements, or herbal remedies you are taking.
* **Older Adults:**  Older adults may be more susceptible to side effects.  Close monitoring is often recommended.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: Can I stop taking anastrozole if I feel better?**  A: No, never stop taking anastrozole without first consulting your doctor.  Stopping prematurely can lead to disease progression.
* **Q: How should I store anastrozole?** A: Store anastrozole tablets in a cool, dry place, away from direct sunlight and moisture. Keep them out of reach of children.
* **Q: Is anastrozole addictive?** A: No, anastrozole is not considered an addictive medication.
* **Q: What if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it's almost time for your next dose.  Do not take a double dose to make up for a missed one.
