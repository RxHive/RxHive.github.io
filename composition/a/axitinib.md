# Axitinib

**Generic Name: Axitinib**

**Usage:**

Axitinib is a targeted cancer therapy primarily used to treat advanced renal cell carcinoma (RCC), a type of kidney cancer.  It's often used after other treatments haven't been successful.  Specifically, it's approved for patients with RCC that has spread to other parts of the body (metastatic RCC) or that cannot be surgically removed.  While its primary use is in RCC, research is ongoing to explore its potential in other cancer types, although it's not currently widely approved for these alternative applications.  Understanding the specific indication for axitinib is crucial, and a healthcare professional should always guide treatment decisions.

**Dosage:**

Axitinib is typically administered orally as a tablet. Dosage is determined by a doctor based on individual factors and is not a one-size-fits-all approach.  The usual adult starting dose is 5 mg taken twice daily.  This dosage may be adjusted based on tolerance and response to treatment.  There is no established dosage for children, as axitinib is generally not used in pediatric populations due to a lack of sufficient data on safety and efficacy in this age group.  The medication should be taken with food to minimize gastrointestinal upset.  Never alter the prescribed dosage without consulting a doctor.

**Side Effects:**

Common side effects can include:

*   High blood pressure (hypertension)
*   Diarrhea
*   Nausea
*   Fatigue
*   Hand-foot syndrome (pain, swelling, redness, and blistering of the hands and feet)
*   Decreased appetite
*   Weight loss
*   Headache


Less common but serious side effects may occur, including bleeding, heart problems, and liver problems.  Any significant or persistent side effects should be immediately reported to a healthcare provider.

**How it Works:**

Axitinib is a tyrosine kinase inhibitor (TKI).  Simply put, it works by blocking specific enzymes (tyrosine kinases) that are crucial for the growth and spread of cancer cells.  By inhibiting these enzymes, axitinib interferes with the signals that tell cancer cells to multiply, ultimately slowing or stopping tumor growth.  It is important to note that axitinib targets specific types of tyrosine kinases that are often overactive in cancer cells, aiming to minimize effects on healthy cells, though some side effects inevitably occur.

**Precautions:**

*   **Contraindications:** Axitinib should not be used in individuals with a known hypersensitivity (allergy) to axitinib or any of its components.
*   **Drug Interactions:** Axitinib can interact with various other medications, including some commonly prescribed drugs.  It is essential to inform your doctor about all medications, supplements, and herbal remedies you are taking.
*   **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:** Axitinib is categorized as pregnancy category D, meaning it poses a risk to a developing fetus.  It's contraindicated in pregnant women and should not be used during breastfeeding.  Effective contraception is essential during treatment.
*   **Elderly:**  Older adults may be more susceptible to certain side effects, necessitating close monitoring.
*   **Liver and Kidney Impairment:** Dosage adjustments may be necessary for individuals with impaired liver or kidney function.


**FAQs:**

*   **Q: How long do I need to take axitinib?** A: The duration of treatment depends on individual response and is determined by a healthcare professional.
*   **Q: Can I drink alcohol while taking axitinib?** A: It is best to avoid alcohol while on axitinib, as it can worsen some side effects, especially those related to the liver and gastrointestinal system.  Consult your doctor for guidance.
*   **Q: How should I store axitinib?** A: Store axitinib tablets at room temperature, away from moisture and heat.  Keep the medication out of reach of children.
*   **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it is nearly time for the next dose.  Do not double the dose.  Consult your doctor or pharmacist if you have questions.
