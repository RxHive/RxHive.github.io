# Acalabrutinib

**Generic Name:** Acalabrutinib

**Usage:**

Acalabrutinib is a targeted therapy primarily used to treat certain types of blood cancers. Its main application is in managing chronic lymphocytic leukemia (CLL) and mantle cell lymphoma (MCL).  These are cancers affecting specific types of white blood cells.  Acalabrutinib works by selectively inhibiting a specific enzyme called Bruton's tyrosine kinase (BTK), which plays a crucial role in the growth and survival of these cancerous cells.  While primarily used for CLL and MCL, it’s also been investigated and sometimes used in other B-cell lymphomas, though these uses are often subject to ongoing clinical trials or are considered off-label (meaning outside the officially approved uses).  The specific application depends on the individual patient's diagnosis and overall health.


**Dosage:**

Dosage of acalabrutinib is determined by a healthcare professional and varies based on the specific condition being treated and the patient's individual characteristics.  It’s typically administered orally as a tablet.  There is no pediatric formulation; therefore, the dosage in children is not established.  The standard adult dosage frequently involves taking a specific number of tablets once or twice daily.  However, adjustments might be necessary based on factors like kidney or liver function, and any adverse reactions experienced.  It's crucial to strictly follow the prescription instructions provided by your doctor or pharmacist.


**Side Effects:**

*   Fatigue
*   Diarrhea
*   Nausea
*   Headache
*   Bruising or bleeding (easily)
*   Upper respiratory tract infection
*   Rash

Less common, but serious side effects, requiring immediate medical attention, can include:

*   Atrial fibrillation (irregular heartbeat)
*   Severe bleeding
*   Severe infections
*   Serious allergic reactions


If you experience any adverse effects, even those not listed here, it's vital to contact your healthcare provider promptly.


**How it Works:**

Acalabrutinib targets and blocks Bruton's tyrosine kinase (BTK), a crucial enzyme involved in the signaling pathways of B-cells.  In CLL and MCL, these B-cells become cancerous and uncontrolled. By inhibiting BTK, acalabrutinib interferes with the signals that promote the growth, survival, and spread of these malignant cells.  This ultimately leads to a reduction in the cancer cell population and can improve patient outcomes.

*   **Older Adults:** Older adults may be more susceptible to certain side effects, requiring careful monitoring and potential dosage adjustments.
*   **Hepatic or Renal Impairment:**  Individuals with liver or kidney problems may require dosage adjustments to prevent accumulation of the drug in the body.
