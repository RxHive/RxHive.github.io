# Acitretin

**Generic Name: Acitretin**

**Usage:**

Acitretin is a retinoid, a type of medication derived from vitamin A. Its primary use is in the treatment of severe, chronic forms of psoriasis and other skin conditions that haven't responded to other treatments.  Specifically, it's effective against pustular psoriasis (characterized by pus-filled blisters), erythrodermic psoriasis (affecting a large area of the body), and palmoplantar pustulosis (affecting the palms of the hands and soles of the feet).  While not a primary use, acitretin may also be considered for treating severe acne that hasn't improved with other therapies.  It's crucial to understand that acitretin is a powerful medication reserved for cases where other, less potent treatments have failed.  The decision to use acitretin is made in consultation with a dermatologist or other qualified healthcare professional.

**Dosage:**

Dosage of acitretin is highly individualized and determined by a doctor based on the severity of the condition and the patient's response. It's typically available as a capsule.  There is no standardized pediatric dosage; acitretin use in children is rare and requires careful monitoring by a specialist.  For adults, the starting dose might be relatively low and gradually increased under medical supervision.  The duration of treatment varies, often extending for several months or even longer, depending on the individual's response and the nature of the condition.  A doctor will carefully monitor the patient’s progress and adjust the dosage accordingly.  Incorrect dosages can lead to serious complications.  It is absolutely vital to follow the doctor’s prescribed dosage instructions precisely.


**Side Effects:**

Common side effects can include:

* Dry skin, lips, and mucous membranes
* Itching
* Nosebleeds
* Hair thinning
* Headaches
* Nausea
* Diarrhea
* Muscle and joint pain
* Increased sensitivity to sunlight

Less common, but potentially serious side effects include:

* Liver abnormalities (requiring monitoring of liver function)
* Increased cholesterol or triglycerides (requiring regular blood tests)
* Inflammation of the pancreas (pancreatitis)
* Bone problems (especially with long-term use)
* Depression


If you experience any concerning side effects, especially those listed above, contact your doctor immediately.

**How it Works:**

Acitretin works by regulating cell growth and differentiation, primarily in the skin.  Retinoids like acitretin bind to receptors within skin cells, affecting the production and turnover of skin cells. This helps to reduce inflammation and the rapid, abnormal cell growth characteristic of conditions like psoriasis and severe acne.  By slowing down this overactive cell growth, acitretin helps to restore a more normal skin cell cycle, leading to clearer skin and a reduction in the symptoms of the targeted condition.

**Precautions:**

* **Pregnancy:** Acitretin is extremely teratogenic, meaning it can cause severe birth defects.  Women of childbearing age must use highly effective contraception for at least three years before, during, and after treatment. Pregnancy tests are usually mandatory before starting treatment.
* **Alcohol:**  Alcohol consumption should be minimized or avoided altogether while taking acitretin.
* **Vitamin A:**  Simultaneous use of other vitamin A derivatives (including retinoids like isotretinoin) is contraindicated.
* **Liver function:**  Liver function tests should be performed regularly to monitor potential liver damage.
* **Interactions:** Acitretin can interact with several medications; inform your doctor of all medications, supplements, and herbal remedies you are taking.
* **Children:** Use in children is generally avoided due to the potential for serious side effects.
* **Elderly:**  Older adults may be more susceptible to side effects, requiring careful monitoring.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long does it take to see results?** A: The time to see improvement varies greatly depending on the individual and the condition being treated.  Improvement may not be noticeable for several weeks or even months.

* **Q: Can I drink alcohol while taking acitretin?** A:  Alcohol consumption should be limited or avoided entirely.

* **Q:  What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it's almost time for the next dose.  Do not double the dose.

* **Q: How should I store acitretin?** A: Store at room temperature, away from moisture and direct sunlight.

* **Q: Can I stop taking acitretin abruptly?** A: No, always consult your doctor before stopping treatment.  Stopping abruptly can sometimes lead to a flare-up of symptoms.
