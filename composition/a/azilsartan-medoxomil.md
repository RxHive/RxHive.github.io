# Azilsartan medoxomil

**Generic Name:** Azilsartan medoxomil

**Usage:** Azilsartan medoxomil is primarily used to treat high blood pressure (hypertension).  It's an angiotensin II receptor blocker (ARB), meaning it works by blocking the effects of angiotensin II, a hormone that narrows blood vessels. By blocking this hormone, azilsartan medoxomil helps relax and widen blood vessels, allowing blood to flow more easily, thus lowering blood pressure.  While its primary use is hypertension, it may also be considered in combination with other medications for the treatment of chronic kidney disease in patients with type 2 diabetes and hypertension.  The use in chronic kidney disease is typically reserved for specific patient populations and requires careful assessment by a physician.


**Dosage:**  The dosage of azilsartan medoxomil is determined by a doctor based on individual needs and health status.  Generally, for adults with hypertension, a starting dose of 40 mg once daily is common.  This may be increased to 80 mg daily if needed, but this adjustment should only be made under the guidance of a healthcare professional.  There is no established pediatric dosage, and use in children is not typically recommended. Azilsartan medoxomil is typically available as an oral tablet. It should be taken with or without food.  Never alter the prescribed dosage without consulting your doctor.


**Side Effects:**  Like most medications, azilsartan medoxomil can cause side effects, although many people experience none or only mild ones. Common side effects include:

* Dizziness
* Headache
* Upper respiratory infection
* Diarrhea
* Nausea
* Fatigue

Less common, but potentially serious side effects include:

* Angioedema (swelling of the face, lips, tongue, or throat) – seek immediate medical attention if this occurs.
*  Hypotension (low blood pressure) – may cause dizziness or fainting.
* Hyperkalemia (high potassium levels in the blood) – particularly in patients with kidney problems.
* Renal impairment (worsening kidney function).


If you experience any concerning side effects, contact your doctor or other healthcare provider immediately.


**How it Works:** Azilsartan medoxomil works by blocking the action of angiotensin II, a hormone that causes blood vessels to constrict. By blocking angiotensin II's effects, azilsartan medoxomil allows blood vessels to relax and widen, reducing blood pressure.  This lowered blood pressure helps reduce the strain on the heart and kidneys, which is beneficial in both hypertension and in slowing the progression of chronic kidney disease in some patients with type 2 diabetes.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** Azilsartan medoxomil should not be used by people who are allergic to it or other ARBs.
* **Drug Interactions:**  Azilsartan medoxomil can interact with other medications, including potassium supplements, potassium-sparing diuretics, and some nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drugs (NSAIDs). Always inform your doctor of all medications you are taking.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:** Azilsartan medoxomil should generally be avoided during pregnancy and breastfeeding.  Consult your doctor immediately if you become pregnant while taking this medication.
* **Older Adults and Individuals with Kidney or Liver Problems:**  Dosage adjustments may be necessary for older adults and individuals with impaired kidney or liver function.  Close monitoring by a healthcare provider is essential in these populations.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: Can I stop taking azilsartan medoxomil suddenly?** A: No, stopping abruptly can be harmful. Always follow your doctor's instructions for tapering off the medication.
* **Q: How should I store azilsartan medoxomil?** A: Store it in a cool, dry place, away from direct sunlight and moisture. Keep it out of reach of children.
* **Q:  What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it is almost time for your next dose.  Do not take a double dose to make up for a missed one.
* **Q: Is azilsartan medoxomil addictive?** A: No, azilsartan medoxomil is not considered an addictive medication.
* **Q: Can I drink alcohol while taking azilsartan medoxomil?** A:  Excessive alcohol consumption can lower blood pressure further and may increase the risk of side effects. Moderation is advised.
