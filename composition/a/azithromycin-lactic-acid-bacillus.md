# Azithromycin + Lactic acid bacillus

**Generic Name: Azithromycin + Lactic acid bacillus**

**Usage:** This combination medication is primarily used to treat bacterial infections of the gastrointestinal tract, particularly those that cause diarrhea.  The azithromycin component targets harmful bacteria, while the lactic acid bacillus (a type of probiotic) helps restore the healthy bacterial balance in the gut, which can be disrupted by the infection or by the antibiotic itself.  This combination aims to treat the infection effectively while minimizing the disruption to the gut microbiome. While its primary use is in gastrointestinal infections, specific indications may vary depending on the formulation and physician's assessment.  Always consult a healthcare professional for diagnosis and treatment.

**Dosage:**  Dosage varies significantly based on age, weight, specific infection, and the formulation (e.g., tablets, capsules, suspensions).  There is no single universally applicable dosage for "Azithromycin + Lactic acid bacillus" as the precise ratio and formulation differ between manufacturers.  **It is crucial to follow the prescribing physician's instructions precisely.**  A doctor will determine the appropriate dose of azithromycin based on the severity of the infection and the patient's characteristics.  The lactic acid bacillus component's dosage will also be determined by the formulation and the physician. Never adjust the dosage without consulting a doctor.

**Side Effects:**

* **Common:** Diarrhea (possibly paradoxical in the context of this combination therapy, due to the azithromycin initially disrupting the gut flora), nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain.
* **Less Common but Serious:**  Severe allergic reactions (anaphylaxis),  liver problems (hepatitis),  irregular heartbeat (QT prolongation),  pseudomembranous colitis (severe inflammation of the colon).
* **Note:** This list is not exhaustive.  Any new or worsening symptoms should be reported immediately to a healthcare provider.

**How it Works:** Azithromycin is a macrolide antibiotic that works by binding to bacterial ribosomes, preventing protein synthesis and ultimately killing the bacteria. Lactic acid bacillus is a probiotic bacterium that helps restore the normal gut flora.  By replenishing beneficial bacteria, it aids in the recovery process and helps prevent or reduce the risk of side effects associated with antibiotic use, such as diarrhea.

It's important to check with a pharmacist or doctor for locally available options.  This combination is not a standardized formulation and may exist under numerous brand names.

**Precautions:**

* **Allergies:** This medication should not be used by individuals with known allergies to azithromycin or any component of the formulation.
* **Drug Interactions:** Azithromycin can interact with several other drugs, including certain heart medications, anticoagulants, and others.  Inform your doctor about all medications, supplements, and herbal remedies you are taking.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:** The safety of this combination during pregnancy and breastfeeding is not fully established.  Consult your doctor before using this medication if you are pregnant, breastfeeding, or planning to become pregnant.
* **Liver and Kidney Disease:** Individuals with liver or kidney problems may require dosage adjustments or may not be suitable candidates for this medication.
* **Children:**  Dosage for children is determined by weight and the specific formulation. Always follow your doctor's instructions.
* **Older Adults:**  Older adults are more prone to adverse effects and may require close monitoring.

* **Q: How long should I take this medication?** A:  The duration of treatment depends on the infection and the physician's prescription.  Do not stop taking the medication prematurely, even if symptoms improve.
* **Q: How should I store this medication?** A:  Store according to the instructions on the packaging, usually in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight and heat.
* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A:  Consult the product information or your doctor.  Generally, take the missed dose as soon as you remember unless it is almost time for the next dose. Do not double the dose.
