# Amlodipine

**Generic Name: Amlodipine**

**Usage:**

Amlodipine is a calcium channel blocker primarily used to treat high blood pressure (hypertension).  By relaxing blood vessels, it lowers the resistance to blood flow, reducing the workload on the heart and thus lowering blood pressure.  This primary use makes it a cornerstone medication in preventing strokes, heart attacks, and other cardiovascular complications.

Beyond hypertension, amlodipine also finds significant use in treating angina (chest pain caused by reduced blood flow to the heart).  It helps to improve blood supply to the heart muscle, relieving chest pain and improving overall heart function.  In some cases, it may also be prescribed for certain types of heart failure, though usually in combination with other medications. It's important to remember that amlodipine is prescribed by a doctor based on individual needs and should not be self-prescribed.


**Dosage:**

Dosage varies significantly depending on the individual's condition, age, and other health factors. A doctor will determine the appropriate dose.  Typically, for adults, amlodipine is available as tablets in strengths ranging from 2.5mg to 10mg, usually taken once daily.  Children's dosage is determined on a case-by-case basis by a pediatrician, considering their weight and specific condition.  There are no injectable or syrup formulations of amlodipine widely available. The medication should be taken as prescribed and not adjusted without consulting a physician.


**Side Effects:**

* **Common:** Headache, dizziness, swelling in the ankles or feet (peripheral edema), fatigue, and flushing.  These are usually mild and tend to subside as the body adjusts to the medication.

* **Less Common but Serious:**  Rapid or irregular heartbeat (palpitations), chest pain, shortness of breath, swelling of the face, lips, tongue, or throat (angioedema - a medical emergency), and severe allergic reactions.

**If you experience any of these side effects, particularly the less common serious ones, seek immediate medical attention.**


**How it Works:**

Amlodipine works by blocking the entry of calcium ions into the muscle cells of the heart and blood vessels. Calcium ions play a crucial role in the contraction of these muscles. By reducing calcium influx, amlodipine causes the heart muscle to relax and contract less forcefully, and the blood vessels to widen. This combined effect reduces blood pressure and improves blood flow to the heart.


**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** Amlodipine should not be used by individuals with known severe hypersensitivity (allergic reaction) to amlodipine or other dihydropyridine calcium channel blockers.

* **Drug Interactions:** Amlodipine can interact with several other medications, including grapefruit juice (which can increase blood levels), certain antibiotics, and some antifungals.  Always inform your doctor about all medications, supplements, and herbal remedies you are taking.

* **Specific Warnings:**  Pregnant or breastfeeding women should discuss the use of amlodipine with their doctor.  Older adults may be more susceptible to certain side effects.  Individuals with liver or kidney impairment require careful monitoring while taking amlodipine.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: Can I stop taking amlodipine suddenly?**  A: No.  Stopping amlodipine abruptly can lead to a rebound increase in blood pressure.  Always consult your doctor before stopping or changing your dosage.

* **Q: How should I store amlodipine?** A: Store amlodipine at room temperature, away from moisture and direct sunlight.

* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it's almost time for your next dose. Do not double the dose.

* **Q: Is amlodipine addictive?** A: No, amlodipine is not addictive.
