# Human chorionic gonadotropin hCG

**Generic Name:** Human chorionic gonadotropin (hCG)

**Usage:**

Human chorionic gonadotropin (hCG) is a hormone naturally produced during pregnancy by the developing placenta.  Medically, it's primarily used to treat infertility in both men and women.  In women, it triggers ovulation, making it crucial in assisted reproductive technologies like in-vitro fertilization (IVF).  It can also be used to support the early stages of pregnancy in cases of threatened miscarriage, although this use is becoming less common.

In men, hCG stimulates the testes to produce testosterone, making it helpful in treating hypogonadism (low testosterone levels) due to certain conditions. This can improve symptoms like decreased libido, erectile dysfunction, and fatigue associated with low testosterone.  Other less common uses include treating delayed puberty and certain types of uterine bleeding.  It's important to note that hCG is not a fertility treatment in and of itself; it plays a specific role in stimulating specific processes.

**Dosage:**

hCG dosage varies significantly depending on the specific condition being treated and the patient's individual characteristics.  Dosage is typically determined and adjusted by a physician.  It's almost exclusively administered via injection (intramuscular or subcutaneous) and rarely comes in other forms like tablets or syrups.  There are no standardized pediatric dosages as usage in children is rare and highly specific to particular conditions managed under a physician's close care.  Adult dosages range considerably, from a few hundred International Units (IU) to several thousand IU, depending on the treatment goal.

**Side Effects:**

* **Common:** Headaches, fatigue, mood swings, breast tenderness, injection site reactions (pain, redness, swelling).
* **Less Common but Serious:** Blood clots (thromboembolism), ovarian hyperstimulation syndrome (OHSS) (primarily in women undergoing fertility treatments), allergic reactions.  OHSS can be severe, requiring immediate medical attention.

Any unusual or concerning side effect warrants immediate consultation with a healthcare professional.


**How it Works:**

hCG mimics the action of luteinizing hormone (LH) and follicle-stimulating hormone (FSH).  In women,  it triggers the final maturation of the egg follicle and ovulation. In men, it stimulates Leydig cells in the testes to produce testosterone. The effects are achieved by binding to specific receptors in the ovaries and testes, initiating a cascade of hormonal events.

**Popular Brand Names (India):**

Brand names for hCG can vary significantly by region and manufacturer.  It is best to consult a pharmacist or healthcare provider for available brands within a specific area.  Generic formulations are also widely available.


**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** hCG should not be used in individuals with certain types of cancers, particularly those sensitive to hormones.  Pre-existing conditions affecting the cardiovascular system need careful consideration. Individuals with a history of blood clots should exercise caution.  Allergy to hCG is another clear contraindication.
* **Drug Interactions:**  Interactions with other medications are possible; complete disclosure of all medication usage to a physician is crucial before starting hCG treatment.
* **Vulnerable Groups:** Pregnancy and breastfeeding: The use of hCG during pregnancy requires careful assessment of benefits and risks by a physician.  Children should only receive hCG under strict medical supervision.  Older adults may require adjusted dosages.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long does it take to see results?** A: The time it takes to see results varies greatly depending on the condition being treated.  For ovulation induction, it might be within days. For testosterone increase in men, several weeks of treatment might be needed.
* **Q: How is hCG stored?** A: hCG injections should typically be stored according to the manufacturer's instructions, usually refrigerated.
* **Q: Is hCG safe?** A:  Like any medication, hCG carries risks and side effects.  It is safe when used under proper medical supervision and appropriate dosage.
* **Q: Can I buy hCG over the counter?** A: No, hCG is a prescription medication and should only be obtained and used under the guidance of a healthcare professional.
