**Usage:**

* **Hydroxypropyl methylcellulose (HPMC):**  This is a cellulose derivative commonly used as a thickening agent, film-forming agent, and binder in various pharmaceutical formulations.  It’s found in tablets, capsules, and topical preparations to improve drug delivery and texture.  It's also utilized in ophthalmic solutions to create a lubricating effect.

* **Glycerin:** A humectant (attracts and retains moisture), glycerin is widely used in pharmaceutical preparations to prevent dryness and improve the texture of creams, ointments, and liquid medications.  It also possesses some mild emollient properties.

* **Dextran:** A polysaccharide, dextran is primarily used as a plasma expander in cases of severe blood loss or shock. It increases blood volume, thereby improving blood pressure and circulation.  Certain dextran formulations may also have applications in other medical contexts.

In some formulations, these three ingredients might be combined to create a specific texture or delivery system (e.g., a viscous ophthalmic gel or a sustained-release tablet).  The specific use would depend entirely on the formulation itself.

**Side Effects:**

The side effects would vary greatly depending on the specific formulation and the context of use.  Possible side effects for each component individually are:

* **HPMC:** Generally well-tolerated.  Rarely, allergic reactions such as skin irritation (topical use) may occur.
* **Glycerin:**  High doses can cause diarrhea and headaches.
* **Dextran:**  Infrequent side effects include allergic reactions (rash, itching, difficulty breathing), bleeding, and kidney problems.


**How it Works:**

Each ingredient functions independently. HPMC acts primarily as a physical modifier of the drug formulation, affecting its texture and release properties. Glycerin works as a humectant and emollient. Dextran, in its plasma expander role, increases blood volume by drawing fluid from tissues into the bloodstream.

**Precautions:**

* **HPMC:** Generally safe, but individuals with known allergies to cellulose derivatives should avoid it.
* **Glycerin:**  Use caution in individuals with diabetes due to its potential to slightly raise blood sugar levels.  Avoid high doses.
* **Dextran:**  Requires careful monitoring, especially in individuals with kidney disease or a history of allergic reactions.  It should be administered under medical supervision.  Pregnancy and breastfeeding considerations should be discussed with a physician.
