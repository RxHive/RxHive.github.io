# Hydrochlorothiazide

**Generic Name: Hydrochlorothiazide**

**Usage:**

Hydrochlorothiazide is a thiazide diuretic, commonly known as a "water pill."  Its primary use is to treat high blood pressure (hypertension). By increasing the excretion of sodium and water from the body, it lowers blood volume and, consequently, blood pressure.  This makes it a cornerstone treatment for hypertension, often used alone or in combination with other blood pressure medications.

Beyond hypertension, hydrochlorothiazide also finds use in treating:

* **Fluid retention (edema):** This can be caused by various conditions, including heart failure, liver disease, and kidney disease.  Hydrochlorothiazide helps to reduce excess fluid buildup.
* **Nephrogenic diabetes insipidus:**  A rare condition characterized by the kidneys' inability to conserve water properly. Hydrochlorothiazide can help manage this condition.
* **Idiopathic hypercalciuria:**  A condition characterized by excessive calcium excretion in the urine.  Hydrochlorothiazide can help reduce this excretion.


**Dosage:**

Dosage varies significantly based on the condition being treated and the individual's response.  A healthcare professional will determine the appropriate dose.  Generally, for adults, the initial dose is often low, starting as low as 12.5 mg to 25 mg once daily. The dose can be adjusted based on blood pressure response and can range up to 50 mg or more daily.  Children’s dosages are significantly lower and are determined strictly by a physician based on weight and condition. Hydrochlorothiazide is typically available in tablet form. It's crucial to follow your doctor's prescription instructions carefully.


**Side Effects:**

Common side effects can include:

* Dizziness
* Lightheadedness
* Headaches
* Dehydration
* Muscle cramps
* Weakness
* Nausea
* Increased urination
* Increased thirst
* Low blood potassium (hypokalemia)


Less common but serious side effects may include:

* Allergic reactions (rash, hives, swelling)
* Low blood sodium (hyponatremia)
* Low blood magnesium (hypomagnesemia)
* Jaundice (yellowing of the skin and eyes)
* Pancreatitis (inflammation of the pancreas)


If you experience any adverse effects, especially those listed above, contact your doctor immediately.


**How it Works:**

Hydrochlorothiazide works primarily in the kidneys.  It inhibits the reabsorption of sodium and chloride ions in the early distal convoluted tubule, a part of the nephron (the functional unit of the kidney).  This increased excretion of sodium leads to increased water excretion (diuresis), reducing blood volume and subsequently blood pressure. The reduction in blood volume also has a secondary impact on blood pressure.


**Popular Brand Names (India):**

The availability of brand names varies regionally.  It is best to consult a pharmacist or doctor for a specific list of available brands in your area.  Generic hydrochlorothiazide is widely available and often more affordable.


**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:**  Hydrochlorothiazide should not be used by individuals with severe kidney disease, severe liver disease, or an allergy to thiazide diuretics.
* **Drug Interactions:**  Many medications can interact with hydrochlorothiazide, so it’s crucial to inform your doctor about all other medications, supplements, or herbal remedies you are taking.  This is especially important for medications affecting potassium levels.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  The use of hydrochlorothiazide during pregnancy and breastfeeding should be carefully considered and only prescribed by a physician after weighing the risks and benefits.
* **Older Adults:** Older adults are more susceptible to side effects and require careful monitoring.
* **Diabetes:**  Hydrochlorothiazide can affect blood sugar levels, requiring close monitoring in patients with diabetes.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: Can I stop taking hydrochlorothiazide suddenly?** A: No, abruptly stopping hydrochlorothiazide can cause a rebound increase in blood pressure.  Always follow your doctor's instructions for tapering off the medication.
* **Q: How should I store hydrochlorothiazide?** A: Store hydrochlorothiazide tablets in a cool, dry place, away from direct sunlight and moisture.
* **Q:  What if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it's almost time for your next dose. Do not double the dose.
* **Q: Is hydrochlorothiazide addictive?** A: No, hydrochlorothiazide is not an addictive medication.
