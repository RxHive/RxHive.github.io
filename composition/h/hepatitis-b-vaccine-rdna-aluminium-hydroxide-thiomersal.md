# Hepatitis B Vaccine rDNA + Aluminium Hydroxide + Thiomersal

**Generic Name:** Hepatitis B Vaccine rDNA + Aluminium Hydroxide + Thiomersal

**Usage:**

This vaccine is primarily used to prevent Hepatitis B infection.  Hepatitis B is a serious liver infection caused by the hepatitis B virus (HBV).  The infection can range from a short-term illness to a lifelong, chronic condition that can lead to liver cirrhosis, liver failure, and liver cancer.  This vaccine is highly effective in preventing infection. While not directly treating existing Hepatitis B infections,  vaccination is crucial for preventing new cases. There are no notable secondary uses for this specific formulation beyond preventing Hepatitis B.


**Dosage:**

This vaccine is administered via intramuscular injection.  The exact dosage and schedule vary depending on the specific vaccine product and the recipient's age.  Generally:

* **Infants:**  A three-dose series is typically recommended, starting at birth or shortly thereafter. The exact timing of the subsequent doses will be determined by a healthcare provider.
* **Children and Adults:**  A three-dose series is also standard, with injections given at specific intervals (e.g., 0, 1, and 6 months).  Again, a healthcare professional will guide on the precise schedule.

Weight and severity of a condition are not factors that alter the dosage of this preventive vaccine;  the schedule depends solely on age group guidelines.

**Side Effects:**

Common side effects are usually mild and temporary:

* Pain, redness, or swelling at the injection site.
* Fatigue
* Headache
* Muscle aches
* Fever

Less common, but more serious side effects are rare, but  include allergic reactions (ranging from hives to difficulty breathing).  

**Important:**  Seek immediate medical attention if you experience any severe allergic reaction or unusual symptoms after receiving the vaccine.


**How it Works:**

This vaccine uses recombinant DNA technology (rDNA). It contains inactive (non-infectious) parts of the Hepatitis B virus.  When injected, your body recognizes these viral components as foreign and triggers an immune response.  Your immune system creates antibodies that will protect you against future exposure to the actual Hepatitis B virus.  The aluminium hydroxide acts as an adjuvant, which enhances the immune response to the vaccine.  Thiomersal, a mercury-based preservative, was historically included in some formulations to prevent bacterial contamination, but its use is declining in many regions due to safety concerns. (Note: The presence or absence of Thiomersal should be confirmed with the specific vaccine product information).

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** Individuals with severe allergic reactions to any component of the vaccine (including yeast, aluminium, or thiomersal if present) should not receive it.
* **Interactions:** While generally safe, interactions with other medications are rare.  Inform your doctor of all medications you are taking.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  Generally considered safe during pregnancy and breastfeeding.  Discuss with your doctor to weigh benefits and potential risks.
* **Immunocompromised Individuals:**  The effectiveness of the vaccine may be reduced in individuals with weakened immune systems.  Consult with a healthcare provider.
* **Children:** The vaccine is routinely given to infants and children; adherence to the recommended schedule is key.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long does the vaccine protect me?** A:  The protection provided by the Hepatitis B vaccine typically lasts for many years, and in many cases, lifelong.  However, booster doses might be recommended in specific situations (like immunocompromised individuals). Your doctor will guide you on this.
* **Q:  Is the vaccine safe?** A: Extensive research and years of use show the Hepatitis B vaccine to be very safe and effective.  Like any vaccine, side effects are possible but usually mild.
* **Q:  How should I store the vaccine?** A:  Follow the storage instructions provided with your specific vaccine product. Typically this involves refrigeration at a specific temperature range.
* **Q:  What if I miss a dose?** A:  Consult your doctor to determine the best course of action to catch up on missed doses.  Don't just skip ahead to the next dose.
* **Q:  Can I get Hepatitis B even after getting vaccinated?** A:  While the vaccine is highly effective, it is not 100% effective.  A very small number of people who receive the vaccine might still get infected, though usually the infection will be milder.
