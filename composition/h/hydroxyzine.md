# Hydroxyzine

**Generic Name: Hydroxyzine**

**Usage:**

Hydroxyzine is primarily used as an antihistamine with anxiolytic (anxiety-reducing) properties.  Its main application is in the short-term management of anxiety symptoms, helping to alleviate feelings of nervousness, tension, and worry.  It's often prescribed for generalized anxiety disorder (GAD) and anxiety associated with stressful situations.  Additionally, hydroxyzine is also used to treat itching (pruritus) caused by allergic reactions, such as hives (urticaria) or skin rashes. It's also sometimes used to help pre-operatively reduce anxiety and as a sedative before medical procedures.  However, it’s crucial to remember that it’s not a long-term solution for chronic anxiety and shouldn't be used as such without a doctor's guidance.  Its use in treating anxiety is typically short-term, bridging the gap until more sustained treatment can be initiated.


**Dosage:**

Dosage varies considerably depending on the condition being treated, the patient's age and weight, and the specific formulation of hydroxyzine.  Always follow your doctor's prescribed dosage precisely.  This information is for general knowledge and should not be considered medical advice.

* **Adults (Anxiety):** Oral tablets or capsules are typically prescribed in doses ranging from 25-100mg per day, divided into several doses.  Higher doses might be used in specific situations but only under strict medical supervision.
* **Adults (Itching):** Dosage for allergic itching may also vary depending on severity.
* **Children:** Dosage for children is calculated based on weight and must be determined by a pediatrician. Liquid formulations are generally preferred for children.
* **Injection:**  Hydroxyzine is also available as an injection, typically administered by a healthcare professional in hospital settings, mainly for pre-operative sedation or acute anxiety management.  Dosage will be determined by a doctor based on individual needs and the situation.

Different formulations (tablets, capsules, syrup, injection) have varied absorption rates and therefore affect how quickly the medication works.  Your doctor will recommend the most suitable form for your needs.


**Side Effects:**

Common side effects:

* Drowsiness
* Dizziness
* Dry mouth
* Blurred vision
* Constipation
* Nausea
* Fatigue

Less common, but serious side effects:

* Irregular heartbeat
* Seizures
* Allergic reactions (rash, itching, swelling)
* Jaundice (yellowing of skin and eyes)
* Low blood pressure
* Difficulty breathing

If you experience any adverse effects, especially serious ones, contact your healthcare provider immediately.


**How it Works:**

Hydroxyzine primarily works by blocking the action of histamine, a chemical in the body that's involved in allergic reactions and anxiety.  By antagonizing histamine receptors (H1 receptors), it reduces itching and inflammation associated with allergies.  Its anxiolytic effect likely involves its interaction with other neurotransmitters in the brain, but the precise mechanism is not entirely understood. It influences the activity of certain receptors that modulate anxiety and can promote relaxation.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** Hydroxyzine should not be used by individuals with known hypersensitivity (allergy) to hydroxyzine or similar medications.
* **Drug Interactions:**  Hydroxyzine can interact with other medications, including sedatives, alcohol, and certain antidepressants.  Inform your doctor about all medications and supplements you are taking.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  Hydroxyzine should only be used during pregnancy or breastfeeding under strict medical supervision, weighing the potential benefits against the potential risks.
* **Elderly and Children:**  Elderly patients and children may be more sensitive to hydroxyzine's side effects, and their dosage should be carefully adjusted by a healthcare professional.
* **Driving and Operating Machinery:** Hydroxyzine can cause drowsiness, so avoid activities requiring alertness, such as driving or operating heavy machinery, until you know how the medication affects you.
* **Alcohol Consumption:**  Avoid consuming alcohol while taking hydroxyzine, as this can enhance drowsiness and other side effects.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long does it take hydroxyzine to work?** A: The onset of action varies depending on the formulation and the route of administration; it can range from minutes (injection) to several hours (oral).
* **Q: Can I stop taking hydroxyzine suddenly?** A:  It's generally recommended to avoid abruptly stopping hydroxyzine, especially if it's been taken for an extended period.  Your doctor should advise on the best way to gradually reduce your dosage.
* **Q: How should I store hydroxyzine?** A: Store hydroxyzine in a cool, dry place, away from direct sunlight and moisture.  Keep it out of the reach of children.
* **Q: Is hydroxyzine addictive?** A: Hydroxyzine has a low potential for physical dependence, although psychological dependence can occur with prolonged or misuse.
* **Q: Can I take hydroxyzine with other medications?** A: Always inform your doctor about all other medications you're taking before starting hydroxyzine.
