# Hydroxypropylmethylcellulose + Ofloxacin + Prednisolone

**Generic Name:** Hydroxypropylmethylcellulose + Ofloxacin + Prednisolone

**Usage:** This combination medication is primarily used to treat bacterial conjunctivitis (pink eye) that is accompanied by inflammation and swelling.  The individual components contribute as follows:  Ofloxacin is an antibiotic that targets and kills bacteria causing the infection. Prednisolone is a corticosteroid that reduces inflammation and swelling in the eye. Hydroxypropylmethylcellulose is a lubricating agent that helps to keep the eye moist and comfortable, easing dryness often associated with eye infections.  While primarily used for bacterial conjunctivitis, its use may extend to other eye conditions where bacterial infection and inflammation are present, but this should always be under the guidance of an ophthalmologist.  It's crucial to understand that this medication treats *bacterial* conjunctivitis; it's ineffective against viral or fungal causes.

**Dosage:** Dosage will be determined by an ophthalmologist and will vary depending on the severity of the infection and the patient's age. Typically, it's administered as eye drops.  The usual adult dosage is one or two drops in the affected eye(s) every 2-4 hours, as directed by a physician.  Children's dosages will be adjusted by the prescribing doctor, considering their age and weight.  This information is not a substitute for professional medical advice.  Never change the prescribed dosage without consulting your doctor.  The specific form (eye drops are most common) and the number of drops per dose and the frequency should be strictly followed according to the prescription.

**Side Effects:** Common side effects may include:

* Burning or stinging sensation upon application
* Temporary blurred vision
* Eye irritation
* Mild headache

Less common, but more serious side effects require immediate medical attention:

* Severe eye pain
* Increased eye pressure (glaucoma)
* Vision changes
* Allergic reactions (rash, swelling)
* Increased risk of infection with prolonged use

If you experience any adverse effects, contact your doctor or ophthalmologist immediately.

**How it Works:**  This combination medication works by targeting different aspects of the infection: Ofloxacin, the antibiotic, inhibits bacterial DNA replication, effectively killing the bacteria responsible for the infection. Prednisolone, the corticosteroid, reduces inflammation by suppressing the body's inflammatory response. Hydroxypropylmethylcellulose acts as a lubricant, improving comfort and aiding in the even distribution of the medication across the eye's surface.

**Precautions:**

* **Contraindications:** This medication is contraindicated in individuals with known hypersensitivity to any of its components (hydroxypropylmethylcellulose, ofloxacin, or prednisolone).  It should also be avoided in patients with certain viral or fungal eye infections.
* **Drug Interactions:**  Inform your doctor of all other medications you are taking, including over-the-counter drugs, herbal remedies, and supplements, as they may interact with this medication.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:** The use of this medication during pregnancy and breastfeeding requires careful consideration by a healthcare professional, weighing the benefits against potential risks.
* **Children and Elderly:**  Dosage adjustments are usually needed for children and the elderly.  Always follow the instructions from your doctor.
* **Contact Lens Use:**  It's advisable to remove contact lenses before application and wait at least 15 minutes before reinserting them.

**FAQs:**

* **Q: How long should I use this medication?**  A: The duration of treatment is determined by a doctor and depends on the severity of the infection and your response to the treatment. Do not stop using it prematurely.
* **Q: Can I use this medication for other eye problems?**  A: No, this medication is specifically for bacterial conjunctivitis with inflammation.  Use it only as prescribed for this condition.
* **Q: How should I store this medication?**  A: Store at room temperature, away from direct sunlight and moisture. Keep out of reach of children.
* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Apply the missed dose as soon as you remember. However, if it's almost time for the next dose, skip the missed dose and continue with your regular dosing schedule. Do not double the dose.
* **Q: Is it safe to drive after using this medication?** A: While the medication itself doesn't usually affect driving, temporary blurred vision can occur immediately after application. Avoid driving until your vision is clear.
